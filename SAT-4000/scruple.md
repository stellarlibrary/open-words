# ⚖️ scruple

🔊 /ˈskruːpəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A very small amount; a moral doubt or hesitation; a feeling of unease about the rightness of an action. (v.) To have qualms; to hesitate on moral grounds
**中文：** n. 踌躇，犹豫

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- qualm（疑虑）
- hesitation（犹豫）
- compunction（内疚）
- misgiving（担忧）
- reluctance（勉强）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **without scruple / have no scruples / moral scruples / scruple to do something**

👉 Example: A person of integrity would scruple to accept a bribe regardless of the amount offered.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ambitious executive had no **scruple** about exploiting confidential information to secure a competitive advantage in the marketplace.
   这位野心勃勃的高管毫不犹豫地利用机密信息在市场上获取竞争优势。

2. She did not **scruple** to voice her dissent publicly when the proposed legislation threatened fundamental civil liberties and freedoms.
   当拟议的立法威胁到基本公民自由和权利时，她毫不犹豫地公开表达了反对意见。

3. His persistent moral **scruples** ultimately prevented him from participating in the ethically questionable research project despite financial incentives.
   他持续的道德顾虑最终使他放弃了参与这项伦理上有问题的研究项目，尽管有经济激励。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "scruple" as a tiny "screw" poking your conscience — a small but persistent discomfort that makes you hesitate before acting.
把"scruple"想象成一颗小"螺丝钉"刺着你的良心——一种微小但持续的不适感，让你在行动前踌躇不前。
