# 🧐 suspicion

🔊 /səˈspɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A feeling of doubt or mistrust; the state of being suspected; a slight trace. (v.) To suspect or have doubts about.
**中文：** n. 怀疑，猜疑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- distrust（不信任）
- doubt（怀疑）
- mistrust（猜疑）
- skepticism（质疑）
- misgiving（疑虑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **under suspicion / above suspicion / arouse suspicion / on suspicion of / a suspicion of**

👉 Example: The manager fell under suspicion after financial irregularities were discovered in the quarterly reports.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The detective's **suspicion** was confirmed when forensic analysis revealed the defendant's fingerprints at the crime scene.
   当法医分析在犯罪现场发现了被告的指纹时，侦探的怀疑得到了证实。

2. There was a growing **suspicion** among faculty members that the administrator had misappropriated institutional research funds.
   教职员工中越来越怀疑该管理人员挪用了机构的研究经费。

3. Her calm demeanor did not arouse **suspicion**, even though she possessed critical information about the ongoing investigation.
   她冷静的举止并未引起怀疑，尽管她掌握着有关正在进行的调查的关键信息。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "suspicion" as "sus-spy-cion" — like a spy who is always watching with doubt, never fully trusting anyone.
把"suspicion"想成"sus-spy-cion"——就像一个间谍（spy）总是带着怀疑观察，从不完全信任任何人。
