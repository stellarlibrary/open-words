# 😩 jaded

🔊 /ˈdʒeɪdɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Tired, bored, or lacking enthusiasm, typically after having had too much of something
**中文：** adj. 厌倦的；疲倦不堪的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- weary（疲倦的）
- disillusioned（幻灭的）
- fatigued（疲惫的）
- blasé（漠不关心的）
- cynical（愤世嫉俗的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **jaded palate / jaded attitude / become jaded / feel jaded about**

👉 Example: After decades in politics, she had become thoroughly jaded about the promise of meaningful reform.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The once-passionate journalist had grown **jaded** after covering the same partisan conflicts for over fifteen consecutive years.
   这位曾经充满激情的记者在连续报道同样的党派冲突超过十五年后变得厌倦了。

2. Even the most **jaded** art critics were visibly moved by the emotional depth and technical mastery of the exhibition.
   即使是最麻木的艺术评论家也被展览的情感深度和精湛技艺明显打动。

3. Long hours and repetitive tasks can leave employees feeling **jaded**, ultimately reducing both morale and overall workplace productivity.
   长时间工作和重复性任务会让员工感到疲倦不堪，最终降低士气和整体工作效率。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "jaded" as coming from "jade" (an old, tired horse) — a jaded person is like an overworked horse, worn out and lacking spirit.
把"jaded"和"jade"（老马）联系起来——一个jaded的人就像一匹过度劳累的老马，精疲力竭、毫无生气。
