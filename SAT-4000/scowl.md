# 😠 scowl

🔊 /skaʊl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A frown; an angry or sullen look. (v.) To frown or glare angrily
**中文：** v. 皱眉头

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- frown（皱眉）
- glare（怒视）
- glower（怒目而视）
- grimace（做鬼脸）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **scowl at someone / scowl in disapproval / wear a scowl / scowl darkly**

👉 Example: The stern librarian would scowl at anyone who dared to speak above a whisper.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor fixed a disapproving **scowl** on the student who had arrived late to the examination for the third consecutive time.
   教授对连续第三次考试迟到的学生投以不满的怒容。

2. She could not help but **scowl** when the committee rejected her meticulously prepared proposal without offering any constructive feedback.
   当委员会没有提供任何建设性反馈就否决了她精心准备的提案时，她不禁皱起了眉头。

3. The guard continued to **scowl** at the visitors until they produced their identification badges and official authorization documents.
   保安一直怒视着来访者，直到他们出示了身份证件和官方授权文件。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "scowl" as "s-COWL" — imagine a dark cowl over someone's face, making them look angry and menacing.
把"scowl"想成"s-COWL"（兜帽）——想象一顶黑色兜帽遮住某人的脸，使他们看起来愤怒而阴沉。
