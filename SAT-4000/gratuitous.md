# 🚫 gratuitous

🔊 /ɡrəˈtuːɪtəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Given or done without good reason; uncalled for; provided free of charge
**中文：** adj. 无理由的，无端的；免费的，无偿的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unnecessary（不必要的）
- unwarranted（无根据的）
- unjustified（不合理的）
- complimentary（免费的）
- needless（不必要的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **gratuitous violence / gratuitous insult / gratuitous advice / gratuitous remark**

👉 Example: Critics condemned the film for its gratuitous violence, which added nothing to the narrative.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The editorial board criticized the senator's speech for its **gratuitous** personal attacks against political opponents rather than substantive policy debate.
   编辑委员会批评了该参议员的演讲，因为其中充满了对政治对手无端的人身攻击，而非实质性的政策辩论。

2. Many literary scholars argue that the novel's **gratuitous** depictions of suffering ultimately undermine its otherwise compelling moral message.
   许多文学学者认为，小说中无端的苦难描写最终削弱了其原本令人信服的道德信息。

3. The contract included a **gratuitous** clause that provided free legal consultation to all employees during the first year.
   合同中包含了一项无偿条款，为所有员工在第一年提供免费的法律咨询。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gratuitous" as sharing a root with "gratuity" (a tip) — something given freely, without being earned or necessary.
把"gratuitous"和"gratuity（小费）"联系起来——两者都有"免费给予"的意思，引申为"无端的、不必要的"。
