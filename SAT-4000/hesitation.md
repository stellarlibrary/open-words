# ⏸️ hesitation

🔊 /ˌhezɪˈteɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The act of pausing or delaying before saying or doing something due to uncertainty or reluctance
**中文：** n. 犹豫；踌躇；口吃；停顿

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- indecision（犹豫不决）
- reluctance（不情愿）
- doubt（怀疑）
- vacillation（摇摆不定）
- uncertainty（不确定）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **without hesitation / a moment of hesitation / show hesitation / after much hesitation**

👉 Example: She accepted the prestigious scholarship without hesitation, eager to begin her doctoral research.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. After a brief **hesitation**, the diplomat signed the treaty that would reshape international trade relations for decades.
   经过短暂的犹豫，这位外交官签署了将重塑数十年国际贸易关系的条约。

2. His **hesitation** during the critical negotiations revealed his lack of confidence in the terms being proposed by the opposition.
   他在关键谈判中的犹豫暴露了他对反方提出的条款缺乏信心。

3. The rescue team acted without **hesitation**, rushing into the burning building to save the trapped residents before dawn.
   救援队毫不犹豫地冲进燃烧的大楼，在天亮前营救被困居民。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "hesitation" as "he-sit-ation" — the situation where he just sits and cannot decide, stuck in a state of pause.
把"hesitation"想成"he-sit-ation"——他只是坐着无法决定的状态，陷入停顿之中。
