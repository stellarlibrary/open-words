# 📣 incite

🔊 /ɪnˈsaɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To encourage or stir up violent or unlawful behavior; to provoke or urge on.
**中文：** v. 煽动；激励；刺激

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- provoke（激怒）
- instigate（煽动）
- stir up（挑起）
- foment（煽动）
- agitate（鼓动）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **incite violence / incite a riot / incite hatred / incite rebellion**

👉 Example: The inflammatory speech was intended to incite the crowd into taking aggressive action against authorities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prosecutor argued that the defendant's remarks were deliberately crafted to **incite** violence among the gathered protesters.
   检察官认为被告的言论是故意策划的，旨在煽动聚集的抗议者实施暴力行为。

2. Historians debate whether the pamphlet was primarily intended to **incite** revolution or merely to raise public awareness.
   历史学家们争论这本小册子主要是为了煽动革命还是仅仅为了提高公众意识。

3. The editorial board cautioned that sensationalist media coverage could **incite** panic rather than promote informed civic discourse.
   编辑委员会警告说，耸人听闻的媒体报道可能会煽动恐慌，而不是促进有见地的公民讨论。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-CITE" — to cite someone in an argument fires them up. When you incite, you ignite emotions and spur people into action.
把"incite"想成"in-CITE"——在争论中引用某人会激怒他们。当你incite时，你点燃情绪并激励人们行动。
