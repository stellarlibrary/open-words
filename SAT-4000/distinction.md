# 🏅 distinction

🔊 /dɪˈstɪŋkʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A difference or contrast between similar things or people; excellence that sets someone apart; an honor or award
**中文：** n. 区别；差别；卓越；荣誉

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- difference（差异）
- contrast（对比）
- eminence（卓越）
- honor（荣誉）
- differentiation（区分）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **draw a distinction / make a distinction / without distinction / a person of distinction / with distinction**

👉 Example: The professor drew a clear distinction between correlation and causation in her statistical analysis lecture.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scholar drew a careful **distinction** between the theoretical framework and its practical application in contemporary policy settings.
   这位学者仔细区分了理论框架与其在当代政策环境中的实际应用。

2. She graduated with **distinction** from the university, earning the highest honors in her department for academic excellence.
   她以优异成绩从大学毕业，获得了所在系最高的学术荣誉。

3. The **distinction** between the two species is so subtle that only trained biologists can reliably identify them in the field.
   这两个物种之间的差别非常微妙，只有训练有素的生物学家才能在野外可靠地辨别它们。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-tinc-tion" as "distinct action" — the action of making things distinct or standing out distinctly from others.
把"distinction"想成"distinct（不同的）+ action（行为）"——使事物变得不同的行为，或从他人中脱颖而出。
