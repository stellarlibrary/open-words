# 🗣 misstate

🔊 /ˌmɪsˈsteɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To state wrongly or inaccurately; to give a false or erroneous account of something
**中文：** v. 说错；作虚伪叙述；谎报

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- misrepresent（歪曲）
- falsify（篡改）
- distort（歪曲）
- misinform（误导）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **misstate the facts / misstate a position / deliberately misstate / misstate earnings**

👉 Example: The executive was found to have misstated the company's quarterly earnings in official financial reports.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The witness appeared to **misstate** several key details during cross-examination, undermining the credibility of her entire testimony.
   该证人在交叉询问中似乎说错了几个关键细节，削弱了她整个证词的可信度。

2. The report was retracted after editors discovered that the author had **misstated** critical data from the original peer-reviewed study.
   在编辑发现作者错误引用了原始同行评审研究中的关键数据后，该报告被撤回。

3. Politicians who repeatedly **misstate** verifiable facts risk eroding public trust and damaging their long-term political credibility.
   反复说错可验证事实的政客有可能侵蚀公众信任并损害其长期政治信誉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mis-state" as "stating something in the wrong way" — you made a statement, but it missed the mark of truth.
把"misstate"想成"以错误的方式陈述（state）"——你做了一个陈述，但偏离了事实。
