# 🌑 oblivion

🔊 /əˈblɪviən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The state of being completely forgotten or unknown; the condition of being unaware or unconscious; official pardon or amnesty
**中文：** n. 遗忘；湮没；赦免

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- obscurity（湮没）
- anonymity（无名）
- forgetfulness（遗忘）
- nothingness（虚无）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fade into oblivion / sink into oblivion / consign to oblivion / rescue from oblivion**

👉 Example: Many once-famous artists have gradually faded into oblivion over the centuries.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient civilization was consigned to **oblivion** after its written records were destroyed in a devastating fire.
   这个古老的文明在其文字记录被毁灭性大火焚毁后陷入了湮没。

2. Without continued preservation efforts, these rare manuscripts will inevitably sink into **oblivion** and be lost forever.
   如果不持续进行保护工作，这些珍贵手稿将不可避免地被遗忘并永远消失。

3. The scholar dedicated her career to rescuing forgotten literary works from the depths of historical **oblivion**.
   这位学者毕生致力于从历史遗忘的深渊中拯救被忽略的文学作品。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ob-LIVION" as "obliterate + vision" — when something is obliterated from vision, it falls into oblivion.
把"oblivion"想成"obliterate + vision"——当某物从视野中被抹去，它就陷入了遗忘。
