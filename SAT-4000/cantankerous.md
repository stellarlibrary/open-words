# 😤 cantankerous

🔊 /kænˈtæŋkərəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Bad-tempered, argumentative, and uncooperative; difficult to deal with.
**中文：** adj. 脾气坏的；好吵架的；难相处的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- irritable（易怒的）
- quarrelsome（好争吵的）
- grouchy（脾气暴躁的）
- peevish（易怒的）
- cranky（暴躁的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cantankerous old man / cantankerous neighbor / cantankerous disposition / cantankerous attitude**

👉 Example: The cantankerous professor refused to accept any revisions to the department's longstanding curriculum.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **cantankerous** committee chairman rejected every proposal without offering constructive feedback or viable alternatives.
   这位脾气暴躁的委员会主席否决了每一项提案，却不提供建设性的反馈或可行的替代方案。

2. Despite his **cantankerous** reputation, the elderly scholar mentored several students who went on to achieve distinction.
   尽管这位年长学者以脾气坏著称，他指导的几位学生后来都取得了杰出成就。

3. Negotiations stalled because the **cantankerous** union representative refused to consider any compromise on working conditions.
   谈判陷入僵局，因为那位难缠的工会代表拒绝在工作条件上考虑任何妥协。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "can't-anchor-us" — a cantankerous person is so difficult that you can't anchor a stable relationship with them.
把"cantankerous"拆成"can't-anchor-us"——脾气坏的人太难相处，你无法与他们建立稳固的关系。
