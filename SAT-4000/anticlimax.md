# 📉 anticlimax

🔊 /ˌæntɪˈklaɪmæks/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A disappointing decline after a previous rise in tension, excitement, or importance; in rhetoric, a deliberate descent from a significant idea to a trivial one for effect.

**中文：** 突降法；虎头蛇尾（在紧张、兴奋或重要性上升之后令人失望的下降；在修辞中，故意从重要观点降到琐碎之事以产生效果）。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- letdown 失望
- disappointment 令人扫兴的事
- bathos 突降法
- comedown 扫兴的结局

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

Common collocations: **something of an anticlimax**, **a real anticlimax**, **prove to be an anticlimax**, **end in anticlimax**. Often used when expectations are built up but the result falls flat.

> Example: After months of anticipation, the finale proved to be a complete **anticlimax**.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The grand ceremony ended in an unexpected **anticlimax** when the keynote speaker failed to arrive on time.
   盛大的典礼以一个意想不到的虎头蛇尾告终，因为主讲嘉宾未能按时到场。

2. Critics described the novel's concluding chapter as a disappointing **anticlimax** after such a compelling narrative.
   评论家们将这部小说的结尾章节描述为引人入胜的叙事之后令人失望的虎头蛇尾。

3. The much-publicized product launch turned out to be an **anticlimax** that left investors feeling deeply underwhelmed.
   这场大肆宣传的产品发布会结果虎头蛇尾，让投资者深感失望。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Anti-" (against/opposite) + "climax" (peak). An **anticlimax** is the opposite of a climax — instead of reaching a thrilling peak, everything drops down to a disappointing low.

"anti-"（相反）+"climax"（高潮）。**Anticlimax** 就是高潮的反面——不是达到激动人心的顶点，而是一切跌落到令人失望的低谷。
