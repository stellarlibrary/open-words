# 😵 muddle

🔊 /ˈmʌdl/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To mix up in a confused or bungling manner; to confuse or bewilder; to make turbid or muddled. (n.) A state of confusion or disorder; a mess
**中文：** v. 混合；使糊涂；使咬字不清晰；使微醉 n. 糊涂；困惑；混浊状态

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- confuse（使困惑）
- jumble（混杂）
- bewilder（使迷惑）
- disorder（混乱）
- tangle（纠缠）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **muddle through / muddle up / in a muddle / muddle the issue**

👉 Example: Without a clear plan, the committee simply muddled through the crisis as best they could.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The inexperienced manager tended to **muddle** important directives, causing widespread confusion among the department staff.
   这位缺乏经验的经理常常把重要指令搞混，导致部门员工普遍困惑。

2. The students found themselves in a complete **muddle** after the professor abruptly changed the examination format.
   教授突然更改考试形式后，学生们发现自己陷入了完全的混乱。

3. Attempts to **muddle** through the complex negotiation without adequate preparation ultimately led to an unfavorable outcome.
   在没有充分准备的情况下试图勉强应对复杂谈判，最终导致了不利的结果。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "muddle" as "MUD-dle" — like stepping into mud, everything gets messy, mixed up, and confused.
把"muddle"想成"MUD-dle"——就像踩进泥巴里，一切都变得凌乱、混杂和糊涂。
