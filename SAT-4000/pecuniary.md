# 💰 pecuniary

🔊 /pɪˈkjuːniˌɛri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or consisting of money; involving a financial penalty or monetary matters
**中文：** adj. 金钱的；应罚款的；金钱上的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- financial（财务的）
- monetary（金钱的）
- fiscal（财政的）
- economic（经济的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **pecuniary interest / pecuniary gain / pecuniary loss / pecuniary penalty**

👉 Example: The judge imposed a pecuniary penalty rather than a custodial sentence for the minor regulatory violation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board member was required to disclose any **pecuniary** interest that might influence her decisions on corporate policy.
   该董事会成员须披露任何可能影响其公司政策决策的经济利益。

2. The lawsuit sought **pecuniary** damages to compensate the plaintiff for substantial financial losses incurred during the breach of contract.
   该诉讼要求金钱赔偿，以补偿原告在违约期间遭受的重大经济损失。

3. Scholars have debated whether **pecuniary** motivation alone is sufficient to drive meaningful innovation in scientific research.
   学者们一直在争论，仅靠金钱动机是否足以推动科学研究中有意义的创新。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pecuniary" as containing "money" hidden inside — it comes from Latin "pecunia" (money), which itself came from "pecus" (cattle), once used as currency.
把"pecuniary"想成里面藏着"money"——它来自拉丁语"pecunia"（钱），而这个词本身又来自"pecus"（牛），牛曾被用作货币。
