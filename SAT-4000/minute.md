# 🔍 minute

🔊 /maɪˈnjuːt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun, verb | 形容词, 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Extremely small; precise and detailed. (n.) A unit of time equal to sixty seconds; a brief note or summary; (pl.) an official record of a meeting. (v.) To record in writing.
**中文：** adj. 微小的；详细的 n. 分，分钟；笔记；会议记录 v. 记录，摘要

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- tiny（微小的）
- minuscule（极小的）
- detailed（详细的）
- meticulous（一丝不苟的）
- infinitesimal（极微小的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **minute detail / minute examination / minute differences / in minute detail / minute particles**

👉 Example: The detective conducted a minute examination of the crime scene, noting every tiny piece of evidence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The forensic analyst performed a **minute** inspection of the fabric fibers that ultimately proved crucial to solving the case.
   法医分析师对织物纤维进行了细致入微的检查，这最终对破案起到了关键作用。

2. Even the most **minute** discrepancies in the financial records were flagged during the comprehensive independent audit process.
   即使是财务记录中最微小的差异也在全面的独立审计过程中被标记出来。

3. The biologist used an electron microscope to observe the **minute** structures within the cell that are invisible to the naked eye.
   生物学家使用电子显微镜观察细胞内肉眼不可见的微小结构。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "minute" (my-NEWT) versus "minute" (MIN-it) — the adjective is pronounced differently and means something so small you need a microscope, not a clock, to measure it.
把形容词"minute"（发音 my-NEWT）和名词"minute"（发音 MIN-it）区分开——形容词意味着你需要显微镜而非时钟来衡量的极小事物。
