# 🦁 audacious

🔊 /ɔːˈdeɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** Showing a willingness to take bold risks; fearless and daring, sometimes to the point of recklessness or impudence.

**中文：** 无畏的；鲁莽的；大胆的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- bold（大胆的）
- daring（勇敢的）
- intrepid（无畏的）
- brazen（厚颜无耻的）
- fearless（无惧的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Audacious" can carry either a positive or negative connotation depending on context. When describing courage or innovation, it is admiring; when describing disrespect or recklessness, it implies disapproval. It is often paired with nouns like "plan," "move," or "attempt."

"audacious" 既可以表示褒义的"大胆创新"，也可以表示贬义的"鲁莽无礼"，需根据上下文判断。常与 plan、move、attempt 等名词搭配使用。

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **audacious** explorer ventured alone into uncharted territory despite all warnings.
   几位探险家不顾所有警告，**大胆地**独自进入了未知领域。

2. Her **audacious** proposal to restructure the entire department surprised the board members.
   她**大胆的**部门重组提案令董事会成员感到惊讶。

3. The diplomat made an **audacious** demand that no other negotiator had dared to raise.
   这位外交官提出了一个其他谈判者都不敢提的**大胆**要求。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "audacious" as "auda-" (like "audio," drawing attention) + "-cious" (like "spacious," large). Someone audacious makes a BIG, LOUD impression — they dare to be heard and seen.

联想记忆：把 "audacious" 拆成 "auda-"（像 audio，引人注意）+ "-cious"（像 spacious，广大的）。audacious 的人就是敢于"大声"表达、"大胆"行动的人。
