# 🔥 blaze

🔊 /bleɪz/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To mark a trail on tree bark; to announce publicly. (n.) A bright flame or fire; a brilliant display of light; an outburst of emotion.
**中文：** v. 在树皮上刻路标；公开宣布 n. 火焰，烈火；光辉；情感爆发；燃烧，冒火焰；照耀，发光

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flame（火焰）
- inferno（烈火）
- flare（闪耀）
- glow（光辉）
- proclaim（宣布）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **blaze a trail / blaze with anger / blaze of glory / in a blaze of light**

👉 Example: The pioneering scientist blazed a trail in the field of molecular genetics.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The explorers **blazed** a trail through the dense forest, marking each tree to guide those who followed.
   探险者们在茂密的森林中开辟了一条道路，在每棵树上做标记以引导后来者。

2. The building was engulfed in a fierce **blaze** that required dozens of firefighters to bring under control.
   那栋建筑被猛烈的大火吞噬，需要数十名消防员才能将其控制住。

3. Her eyes **blazed** with indignation when she discovered the extent of the committee's deliberate deception.
   当她发现委员会蓄意欺骗的程度时，她的眼中燃烧着愤怒。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "blaze" as a bright flame that lights the way — just as fire blazes to illuminate, a pioneer blazes a trail for others to follow.
把"blaze"想成一团照亮道路的明火——正如火焰燃烧照亮一切，先驱者也为后来者开辟道路。
