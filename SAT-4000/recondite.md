# 📚 recondite

🔊 /ˈrɛkəndaɪt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Dealing with very profound or difficult subject matter; little known or hidden from common understanding; abstruse and obscure
**中文：** adj. 深奥的；隐藏的；默默无闻的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- abstruse（深奥的）
- arcane（神秘的）
- esoteric（深奥的）
- obscure（晦涩的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **recondite knowledge / recondite subject / recondite theories / recondite scholarship**

👉 Example: The professor's recondite lecture on quantum mechanics left most of the audience thoroughly bewildered.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosopher's **recondite** arguments were so deeply layered that only a handful of specialists could fully comprehend them.
   这位哲学家深奥的论证层次如此之深，以至于只有少数专家能完全理解。

2. Medieval alchemists guarded their **recondite** formulas with elaborate codes and cryptic symbols to prevent unauthorized replication.
   中世纪的炼金术士用精心设计的密码和神秘符号来保护他们深奥的配方，以防止未经授权的复制。

3. The doctoral candidate chose a deliberately **recondite** topic that had received virtually no scholarly attention in the preceding decades.
   这位博士生选择了一个故意冷僻的课题，在过去几十年中几乎没有受到任何学术关注。

Think of "recondite" as "re-con-hide" — the knowledge is hidden away so deeply that you need to reconnoiter (scout) to find it.
把"recondite"想成"re-con-hide（深藏不露）"——知识隐藏得如此之深，你需要侦察一番才能找到它。
