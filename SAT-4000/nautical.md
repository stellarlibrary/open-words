# ⚓ nautical

🔊 /ˈnɔːtɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Relating to sailors, ships, or navigation at sea
**中文：** adj. 航海的，海上的；船员的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- maritime（海事的）
- marine（海洋的）
- seafaring（航海的）
- naval（海军的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **nautical mile / nautical chart / nautical terms / nautical experience**

👉 Example: The captain relied on nautical charts to navigate through the treacherous strait.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The museum's collection of **nautical** instruments includes compasses and sextants dating back to the sixteenth century.
   博物馆收藏的航海仪器包括可追溯到十六世纪的指南针和六分仪。

2. A **nautical** mile, which equals approximately 1.852 kilometers, serves as the standard unit of distance in maritime navigation.
   一海里约等于1.852公里，是海上航行中的标准距离单位。

3. The architect incorporated **nautical** design elements throughout the coastal resort to evoke the spirit of seafaring tradition.
   建筑师在整个海滨度假村中融入了航海设计元素，以唤起航海传统的精神。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "naut-" from Greek "nautes" meaning sailor — a nautical matter is a sailor's matter, anything related to the sea.
"naut-"来自希腊语"nautes"（水手）——nautical的事情就是水手的事情，与海洋有关的一切。
