# ⚖️ abrogate

🔊 /ˈæbrəɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To formally abolish or annul a law, agreement, or custom by an authoritative act
**中文：** v. 废除，取消（法令、协议等）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- abolish（废除）
- annul（宣告无效）
- repeal（撤销）
- revoke（废止）
- invalidate（使无效）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **abrogate a treaty / abrogate a law / abrogate responsibility / abrogate an agreement**

👉 Example: The new government moved swiftly to abrogate the outdated colonial legislation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Parliament voted overwhelmingly to **abrogate** the trade agreement that had governed commerce for decades.
   议会以压倒性多数投票废除了治理贸易数十年的贸易协定。

2. The president cannot unilaterally **abrogate** treaties without the formal consent of the legislative body.
   总统不能在没有立法机构正式同意的情况下单方面废除条约。

3. Legal scholars warned that **abrogating** the statute would create significant uncertainty in contract law.
   法学学者警告说，废除该法规将给合同法带来重大不确定性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Split it: "ab-" (away) + "rogate" (to propose/ask, like in "interrogate"). To abrogate is to take away what was once proposed as law.
拆分记忆："ab-"（离开）+"rogate"（提议/要求，如interrogate中的词根）。Abrogate就是把曾经提出的法律拿走。
