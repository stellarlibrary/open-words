# 🗡️ brigand

🔊 /ˈbrɪɡənd/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A member of a gang of bandits; a robber or outlaw, especially one operating in wild or isolated areas
**中文：** n. 强盗；土匪；盗贼；歹徒

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bandit（强盗）
- outlaw（亡命之徒）
- robber（抢劫犯）
- marauder（掠夺者）
- highwayman（拦路强盗）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a band of brigands / brigand attack / roving brigands / notorious brigand**

👉 Example: The mountain pass was known to be frequented by brigands who preyed on unsuspecting travelers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Medieval merchants often hired armed escorts to protect their caravans from **brigands** lurking along remote mountain passes.
   中世纪商人经常雇佣武装护卫来保护商队免受潜伏在偏僻山口的强盗袭击。

2. The governor dispatched a military force to suppress the **brigands** who had been terrorizing villages throughout the province.
   总督派遣了一支军事力量去镇压一直在全省各村庄制造恐慌的土匪。

3. Historical accounts describe the notorious **brigand** as a cunning strategist who evaded capture for over a decade.
   历史记载将这位臭名昭著的强盗描述为一个狡猾的策略家，他逃脱追捕长达十多年。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "brigand" as "brig-and" — a "brig" is a prison on a ship, and brigands are the criminals who belong there.
把"brigand"想成"brig-and"——"brig"是船上的监狱，而brigands就是应该被关在里面的罪犯。
