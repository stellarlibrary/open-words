# 💔 disillusion

🔊 /ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To free from false beliefs or idealistic expectations; to cause someone to realize the truth. (n.) The state of being disappointed by discovering reality differs from expectations
**中文：** v. 使醒悟；使觉醒 n. 幻灭

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disenchant（使清醒）
- undeceive（使醒悟）
- disappoint（使失望）
- enlighten（启发）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **disillusioned with / disillusioned by / a sense of disillusion / growing disillusion**

👉 Example: Many young voters became disillusioned with the political process after years of broken promises.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The documentary sought to **disillusion** the public about the supposed benefits of unregulated financial markets.
   这部纪录片旨在让公众认清不受监管的金融市场的所谓好处。

2. Years of corporate corruption gradually **disillusioned** even the most optimistic employees, eroding their trust in leadership.
   多年的企业腐败逐渐让最乐观的员工也醒悟过来，侵蚀了他们对领导层的信任。

3. A profound sense of **disillusion** swept through the academic community when the celebrated study was exposed as fraudulent.
   当这项备受赞誉的研究被揭露为造假时，一种深深的幻灭感席卷了学术界。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-illusion" as "removing an illusion" — when the illusion is removed, you see the harsh reality.
把"disillusion"想成"dis（去除）+ illusion（幻想）"——当幻想被去除，你看到的是残酷的现实。
