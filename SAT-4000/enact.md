# 📜 enact

🔊 /ɪˈnækt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make into law; to act out or perform a role or scene
**中文：** v. 颁布；制定法律

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- legislate（立法）
- decree（颁布）
- ratify（批准）
- promulgate（公布）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **enact a law / enact legislation / enact reforms / enact a policy**

👉 Example: Congress moved swiftly to enact legislation addressing the growing concerns over data privacy.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The parliament voted overwhelmingly to **enact** new environmental regulations aimed at reducing industrial carbon emissions nationwide.
   议会以压倒性多数投票通过了旨在减少全国工业碳排放的新环境法规。

2. Several states have recently **enacted** measures to strengthen protections for workers in the rapidly expanding gig economy.
   几个州最近制定了措施，以加强对快速扩张的零工经济中劳动者的保护。

3. The administration pledged to **enact** comprehensive healthcare reform within the first year of its term in office.
   政府承诺在其任期的第一年内颁布全面的医疗保健改革。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "en-act" as putting something "into action" — when a government enacts a law, it puts that rule into action.
把"enact"想成把某事"付诸行动"——当政府enact一项法律时，就是把该规则付诸行动。
