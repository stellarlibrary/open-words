# 🤷 irresolute

🔊 /ɪˈrɛzəluːt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Showing or feeling hesitancy; uncertain; unable to make firm decisions or commit to a course of action
**中文：** adj. 优柔寡断的；踌躇不定的；无决断的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- indecisive（优柔寡断的）
- hesitant（犹豫的）
- wavering（动摇的）
- vacillating（摇摆不定的）
- uncertain（不确定的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **irresolute leader / irresolute about / remain irresolute / appear irresolute**

👉 Example: The irresolute committee chair postponed the vote three times, unable to commit to advancing the proposal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **irresolute** governor failed to act decisively during the crisis, allowing the situation to deteriorate beyond any reasonable control.
   优柔寡断的州长在危机期间未能果断行动，导致局势恶化到无法合理控制的地步。

2. Investors grew frustrated with the **irresolute** board of directors, whose constant delays eroded confidence in the company's future.
   投资者对犹豫不决的董事会越来越失望，其不断的拖延削弱了对公司未来的信心。

3. An **irresolute** approach to foreign policy can undermine a nation's credibility and weaken its standing among international allies.
   在外交政策上犹豫不决的做法会损害一个国家的信誉，削弱其在国际盟友中的地位。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "irresolute" as "ir-resolute" — the prefix "ir-" means "not," so it means "not resolute," like a compass needle that never settles on one direction.
把"irresolute"想成"ir-resolute"——前缀"ir-"意为"不"，所以它意味着"不坚定的"，就像指南针的针永远无法固定在一个方向上。
