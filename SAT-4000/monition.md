# ⚠️ monition

🔊 /məˈnɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A warning or piece of advice; a formal notice from a court or ecclesiastical authority; an admonition
**中文：** n. 忠告；警告；法庭传票；宗教法庭的诫谕

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- warning（警告）
- admonition（训诫）
- caution（告诫）
- counsel（忠告）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **issue a monition / heed a monition / formal monition / solemn monition**

👉 Example: The judge issued a monition to the defendant regarding the consequences of failing to appear in court.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's **monition** about the dangers of academic dishonesty left a lasting impression on every student in the lecture hall.
   教授关于学术不诚实危害的忠告给讲堂里的每位学生留下了深刻印象。

2. Despite receiving a formal **monition** from the ecclesiastical court, the clergyman persisted in his controversial public statements.
   尽管收到了宗教法庭的正式诫谕，该牧师仍坚持发表有争议的公开声明。

3. The elder statesman delivered a grave **monition** to the assembly, urging caution before committing the nation to armed conflict.
   这位资深政治家向大会发出严肃警告，敦促在让国家卷入武装冲突之前务必谨慎。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "monition" as related to "admonition" — both come from Latin "monere" (to warn). A monition is a formal warning or counsel.
把"monition"与"admonition"（训诫）联系起来——两者都源自拉丁语"monere"（警告）。monition就是正式的警告或忠告。
