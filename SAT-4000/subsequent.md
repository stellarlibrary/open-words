# ⏭️ subsequent

🔊 /ˈsʌbsɪkwənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Coming after something in time; following
**中文：** adj. 随后的，后来的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- following（随后的）
- ensuing（接踵而来的）
- succeeding（后续的）
- later（之后的）
- consequent（随之而来的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **subsequent to / subsequent events / subsequent analysis / in subsequent years**

👉 Example: Subsequent investigations revealed that the initial findings had been based on incomplete data.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The initial experiment failed, but **subsequent** trials produced results that confirmed the researchers' original hypothesis.
   最初的实验失败了，但随后的试验产生了证实研究人员最初假设的结果。

2. **Subsequent** to the merger, the company restructured its management team to eliminate redundancies across departments.
   合并之后，公司重组了管理团队以消除各部门之间的冗余。

3. The author revised the manuscript extensively in **subsequent** editions to incorporate newly discovered archival materials.
   作者在随后的版本中大量修订了手稿，以纳入新发现的档案材料。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sub-sequent" as "following in sequence" — something that comes next in the sequence of events.
把"subsequent"想成"sub"（在后面）+"sequent"（顺序）——在事件顺序中紧随其后的事情。
