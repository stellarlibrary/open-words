# 👋 beck

🔊 /bɛk/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A gesture of beckoning, such as a nod or wave of the hand; (British dialect) a small stream or brook
**中文：** n. 招手；点头示意；（英）小河

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gesture（手势）
- nod（点头）
- signal（信号）
- summons（召唤）
- brook（小溪）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **at someone's beck and call / a beck of the hand / a mountain beck**

👉 Example: The servants were always at the master's beck and call, ready to fulfill any request instantly.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The wealthy aristocrat expected his entire household staff to remain at his **beck** and call throughout the day.
   这位富有的贵族期望他的全部家仆整天随时听候他的差遣。

2. With a slight **beck** of her hand, the chairwoman silenced the entire assembly and commanded immediate attention.
   女主席轻轻一招手，便使整个会场安静下来，立即引起了所有人的注意。

3. The hikers followed the winding **beck** through the Yorkshire valley until they reached the ancient stone bridge.
   徒步旅行者沿着蜿蜒的小溪穿过约克郡山谷，直到他们到达那座古老的石桥。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "beck" as the start of "beckon" — a beck is the gesture you make when you beckon someone to come closer.
把"beck"想成"beckon（招手）"的开头——beck就是你招手让人过来时做的那个手势。
