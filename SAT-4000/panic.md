# 😱 panic

🔊 /ˈpænɪk/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective, verb | 名词, 形容词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A sudden, uncontrollable fear or anxiety, often affecting a group of people. (adj.) Relating to or caused by panic. (v.) To feel or cause to feel sudden overwhelming fear
**中文：** n. 恐慌，惊慌 adj. 恐慌的；没有理由的 v. 使恐慌

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- alarm（惊恐）
- terror（恐惧）
- frenzy（狂乱）
- hysteria（歇斯底里）
- dread（恐惧）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **panic attack / panic buying / in a panic / cause panic / don't panic**

👉 Example: The sudden earthquake caused widespread panic among the residents of the densely populated coastal city.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Financial analysts warned that the sudden market downturn could trigger widespread **panic** among inexperienced investors holding volatile assets.
   金融分析师警告说，市场突然下跌可能引发持有波动性资产的缺乏经验的投资者的广泛恐慌。

2. The government urged citizens not to **panic** and assured them that emergency supplies were being distributed to all affected regions.
   政府敦促公民不要恐慌，并保证正在向所有受灾地区分发紧急物资。

3. A **panic** swept through the crowded theater when the fire alarm sounded, causing a dangerous stampede toward the exits.
   火灾警报响起时，一阵恐慌席卷了拥挤的剧院，导致人们危险地涌向出口。

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "panic" from the Greek god Pan, who would cause sudden, irrational fear in travelers passing through forests.
"panic"源自希腊神话中的潘神（Pan），他会让穿越森林的旅行者突然产生莫名的恐惧。
