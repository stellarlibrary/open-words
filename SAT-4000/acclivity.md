# ⛰️ acclivity

🔊 /əˈklɪvɪti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** An upward slope or incline of the ground; an ascending gradient.
**中文：** 向上的斜坡；上坡，上斜。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ascent（上坡）
- incline（斜坡）
- gradient（坡度）
- rise（升起处）
- upgrade（上坡路）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **steep/gentle/gradual acclivity**; opposite of "declivity" (下坡)

👉 Example: The hikers paused at the base of the steep acclivity to catch their breath.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The army struggled to advance its heavy artillery up the muddy **acclivity** during the siege.
   围城期间，军队艰难地将重型火炮推上泥泞的上坡。

2. A gentle **acclivity** led from the river valley to the elevated plateau where the village stood.
   一段平缓的上坡从河谷延伸到村庄所在的高地。

3. The geological survey revealed that the **acclivity** on the eastern ridge exceeded a thirty-degree angle.
   地质勘测显示，东侧山脊的上坡角度超过了三十度。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Acclivity" starts with "ac-" (upward) like "ascend" — picture climbing up a steep cliff (cliv- resembles "cliff").
acclivity 以"ac-"开头，类似"ascend（上升）"——想象攀登陡峭的悬崖（cliv- 形似 cliff）。
