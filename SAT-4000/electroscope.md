# 🔍 electroscope

🔊 /ɪˈlektrəskoʊp/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** An instrument used for detecting and measuring electric charge, typically consisting of a pair of thin metal leaves that diverge when charged
**中文：** n. 验电器

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- charge detector（电荷检测器）
- gold-leaf electroscope（金箔验电器）
- electrometer（静电计）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **gold-leaf electroscope / charge the electroscope / electroscope leaves / use an electroscope**

👉 Example: The physics teacher demonstrated static electricity by touching a charged rod to the electroscope.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The gold leaves of the **electroscope** diverged immediately when the charged glass rod was brought near the metal knob.
   当带电玻璃棒靠近金属球时，验电器的金箔立即张开。

2. Students used the **electroscope** to determine whether various materials had acquired a positive or negative static charge.
   学生们使用验电器来确定各种材料是否获得了正或负的静电荷。

3. The physicist calibrated the **electroscope** carefully before measuring the residual charge on the surface of the insulating material.
   物理学家在测量绝缘材料表面的残余电荷之前仔细校准了验电器。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "electro-scope" as "electric" + "scope (to see)" — an electroscope lets you see whether something has an electric charge.
把"electroscope"想成"electro（电）"加"scope（观察）"——验电器让你观察某物是否带有电荷。
