# 🚫 boycott

🔊 /ˈbɔɪˌkɑːt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To refuse to buy, use, or participate in something as a protest. (n.) A collective refusal to engage with a person, organization, or product
**中文：** v. 联合抵制；拒绝参加 n. 联合抵制

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- embargo（禁运）
- ban（禁止）
- shun（回避）
- blacklist（列入黑名单）
- ostracize（排斥）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **boycott a product / boycott an election / impose a boycott / call for a boycott**

👉 Example: Consumer groups organized a boycott of the corporation after revelations of unethical labor practices.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Several nations decided to **boycott** the international summit in protest against the host country's human rights violations.
   几个国家决定联合抵制这次国际峰会，以抗议东道国的人权侵犯行为。

2. The trade union called for a widespread **boycott** of the company's products until fair wages were guaranteed.
   工会号召广泛抵制该公司的产品，直到公平工资得到保障为止。

3. Activists urged consumers to **boycott** brands that failed to adopt environmentally sustainable manufacturing processes.
   活动人士敦促消费者抵制那些未能采用环保可持续生产工艺的品牌。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of Captain Charles Boycott, an Irish land agent who was shunned by his community — his name became synonymous with collective refusal.
想想查尔斯·博伊科特上尉，一位被社区集体排斥的爱尔兰土地代理人——他的名字成了集体抵制的代名词。
