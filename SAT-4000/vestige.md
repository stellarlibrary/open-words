# 🦴 vestige

🔊 /ˈvɛstɪdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A trace or remnant of something that once existed; a surviving evidence of a former condition; a degenerate or imperfectly developed organ
**中文：** n. 遗迹；残余；退化的器官

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- remnant（残余）
- trace（痕迹）
- relic（遗迹）
- residue（残留物）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vestige of / last vestige / without a vestige of / vestiges of the past**

👉 Example: The ancient ruins are the last vestige of a once-thriving civilization.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The crumbling fortress stood as the sole **vestige** of the medieval kingdom that had once dominated the entire region.
   这座摇摇欲坠的堡垒是曾经统治整个地区的中世纪王国的唯一**遗迹**。

2. Biologists consider the human appendix a **vestige** of an organ that served a digestive function in our distant ancestors.
   生物学家认为人类的阑尾是我们远古祖先体内曾发挥消化功能的器官的**残余**。

3. After the revolution, the new government sought to eliminate every **vestige** of the former regime's oppressive legal framework.
   革命后，新政府试图消除旧政权压迫性法律框架的每一丝**残余**。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vestige" as "vest-age" — an old vest from another age, a worn-out remnant of the past.
把"vestige"想成"vest-age"——来自另一个时代的旧背心，一件过去留下的破旧残余物。
