# 🕊️ absolution

🔊 /ˌæbsəˈluːʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Formal release from guilt, obligation, or punishment; in religion, a declaration of forgiveness of sins
**中文：** n. 赦免，免罪；（宗教中的）赦罪

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- forgiveness（宽恕）
- pardon（赦免）
- exoneration（免罪）
- acquittal（无罪释放）
- remission（赦免）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **grant absolution / seek absolution / receive absolution / absolution from sin / absolution of guilt**

👉 Example: The priest granted absolution to the penitent after a heartfelt confession.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court's ruling effectively granted **absolution** to the officials accused of misconduct during the crisis.
   法院的裁决实际上赦免了在危机期间被指控不当行为的官员。

2. He spent years seeking **absolution** for decisions that had caused irreparable harm to his community.
   他花了多年时间寻求对自己给社区造成不可挽回伤害之决定的赦免。

3. Public **absolution** from the committee did little to restore the disgraced minister's tarnished reputation.
   委员会的公开赦免对恢复这位失势部长受损的声誉收效甚微。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Connect "absolution" to "absolve" — both share the root "absolv-" meaning to set free. Absolution is the noun form: the act of setting someone free from guilt.
把"absolution"和"absolve"联系起来——两者共享词根"absolv-"，意为释放。Absolution是名词形式：将某人从罪责中释放的行为。
