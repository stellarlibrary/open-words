# ☢️ radioactive

🔊 /ˌreɪdioʊˈæktɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Emitting or relating to the emission of ionizing radiation or particles as a result of nuclear decay; figuratively, extremely controversial or dangerous.
**中文：** adj. 放射性的；有辐射的；放射引起的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- irradiated（受辐射的）
- contaminated（被污染的）
- nuclear（核的）
- toxic（有毒的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **radioactive material / radioactive waste / radioactive decay / radioactive isotope / radioactive contamination**

👉 Example: The facility was designed to safely store radioactive waste generated by the nuclear power plant.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Scientists discovered that the **radioactive** isotope could be used as a tracer to monitor blood flow in cardiac patients.
   科学家发现这种放射性同位素可以用作示踪剂来监测心脏病患者的血液流动。

2. The government mandated strict protocols for transporting **radioactive** materials across state borders to ensure public safety.
   政府强制要求跨州运输放射性材料时遵守严格的规程，以确保公共安全。

3. Prolonged exposure to **radioactive** elements in the abandoned mine posed a significant health risk to the surrounding community.
   长期接触废弃矿井中的放射性元素对周围社区构成了重大健康风险。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "radioactive" as "radio + active" — the atoms are so active that they broadcast energy like a radio station, sending out invisible waves in all directions.
把"radioactive"想成"radio（无线电）+ active（活跃的）"——原子如此活跃，就像广播电台一样向四面八方发送看不见的能量波。
