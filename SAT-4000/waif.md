# 🍂 waif

🔊 /weɪf/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A homeless, neglected, or abandoned person, especially a child; a thin or gaunt person who appears lost or helpless
**中文：** n. 流浪者；流浪儿；无主物；无家可归的人

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- stray（流浪者）
- orphan（孤儿）
- vagabond（流浪汉）
- foundling（弃儿）
- urchin（顽童）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **waifs and strays / homeless waif / little waif / a waif of a child**

👉 Example: The charity was established specifically to help waifs and strays find shelter and permanent homes.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The novel's protagonist is a young **waif** who navigates the harsh streets of Victorian London in search of safety and belonging.
   这部小说的主人公是一个年幼的流浪儿，在维多利亚时代伦敦的严酷街道上寻找安全和归属。

2. Volunteers at the shelter worked tirelessly to provide food and clothing to the **waifs** displaced by the devastating civil conflict.
   收容所的志愿者不知疲倦地为因毁灭性内战而流离失所的流浪者提供食物和衣物。

3. She appeared at the doorstep like a **waif**, thin and exhausted after wandering alone through the countryside for several days.
   她出现在门口时像个流浪儿，在乡间独自游荡数日后显得消瘦而疲惫。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "waif" as rhyming with "strafe" — a waif is someone who has been battered by life's hardships and left without a home.
把"waif"联想到一个被生活的艰辛打击、无家可归的人——一片在风中飘荡的落叶。
