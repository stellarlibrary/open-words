# 🌵 succulent

🔊 /ˈsʌkjələnt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Full of juice; tender, moist, and tasty. (n.) A plant with thick, fleshy tissues adapted to water storage
**中文：** adj. 多汁的，多水分的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- juicy（多汁的）
- luscious（甘美的）
- moist（湿润的）
- delectable（美味的）
- tender（嫩的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **succulent fruit / succulent steak / succulent plants / succulent flavor / succulent and tender**

👉 Example: The chef prepared a perfectly succulent roast that delighted every guest at the formal dinner.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The botanist cultivated a remarkable collection of **succulent** plants uniquely adapted to survive prolonged periods of extreme drought.
   植物学家培育了一系列独特的多汁植物，它们能够适应长时间的极端干旱。

2. The restaurant received critical acclaim for its **succulent** preparations of locally sourced seafood and seasonal organic produce.
   这家餐厅因其多汁鲜美的本地海鲜和时令有机食材的烹饪而广受好评。

3. After hours of slow roasting, the lamb emerged remarkably **succulent** and tender, exceeding the expectations of every diner.
   经过数小时的慢烤，羊肉变得异常多汁鲜嫩，超出了每位食客的期望。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "succulent" as "suc-cu-lent" — "suc" sounds like "suck," as in sucking juice from something so moist and flavorful it drips.
把"succulent"想成"suc-cu-lent"——"suc"听起来像"suck"（吸），就像从多汁美味、汁水四溢的东西中吸取汁液。
