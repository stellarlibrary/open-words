# ✨ furbish

🔊 /ˈfɜːrbɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To polish or renovate; to restore to good condition by rubbing or cleaning; to brighten or make new again
**中文：** v. 磨光；擦亮；恢复；更新

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- polish（磨光）
- burnish（擦亮）
- renovate（翻新）
- refurbish（整修）
- restore（恢复）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **furbish up / furbish the armor / furbish old furniture / furbish a reputation**

👉 Example: The antique dealer spent weeks furbishing the tarnished silver collection before the prestigious auction.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The museum curator hired skilled artisans to **furbish** the medieval armor so it could be properly displayed in the exhibition.
   博物馆馆长雇用了技艺精湛的工匠来擦亮中世纪盔甲，以便在展览中妥善展出。

2. Before the diplomatic reception, servants were instructed to **furbish** every piece of silverware until it gleamed under the chandeliers.
   在外交招待会之前，仆人们被指示将每件银器擦亮，直到它在枝形吊灯下闪闪发光。

3. The new administration attempted to **furbish** the institution's tarnished reputation through a series of transparent governance reforms.
   新一届政府试图通过一系列透明的治理改革来恢复该机构受损的声誉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "furbish" as "fur + brush" — you brush the fur to make it shine and look new again, just like furbishing restores luster.
把"furbish"想成"fur（毛皮）+ brush（刷子）"——你用刷子刷毛皮使其光亮如新，就像furbish恢复光泽一样。
