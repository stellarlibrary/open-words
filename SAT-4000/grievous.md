# 💔 grievous

🔊 /ˈɡriːvəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Causing great sorrow, pain, or suffering; very serious or severe
**中文：** adj. 令人忧伤的；痛苦的；剧烈的；严重的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- severe（严重的）
- grave（严肃的）
- dreadful（可怕的）
- painful（痛苦的）
- lamentable（可悲的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **grievous error / grievous harm / grievous bodily harm / grievous wound / grievous loss**

👉 Example: The commander acknowledged that the strategic miscalculation was a grievous error with lasting consequences.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The judge imposed a lengthy sentence on the defendant for inflicting **grievous** bodily harm upon the innocent victim.
   法官因被告对无辜受害者造成严重身体伤害而判处其长期监禁。

2. The diplomat's **grievous** misstatement during the summit threatened to unravel months of carefully negotiated agreements.
   这位外交官在峰会上的严重失言险些使数月精心谈判达成的协议化为泡影。

3. Historians regard the destruction of the ancient library as a **grievous** loss to human knowledge and cultural heritage.
   历史学家认为这座古代图书馆的毁灭是对人类知识和文化遗产的惨痛损失。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "grievous" as "grieve + ous" — full of grief, something so painful or serious that it makes you grieve.
把"grievous"想成"grieve + ous"——充满悲伤的，某件事情如此痛苦或严重以至于让你悲痛不已。
