# 🗣️ vilify

🔊 /ˈvɪlɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To speak or write about in an abusively disparaging manner; to defame or slander
**中文：** v. 诽谤，辱骂；贬低，轻视

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- defame（诽谤）
- slander（中伤）
- malign（恶意中伤）
- denigrate（贬低）
- disparage（蔑视）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vilify someone publicly / vilify a group / vilified by the press / unfairly vilified / vilify opponents**

👉 Example: The candidate was vilified by opponents who distorted her voting record to mislead the public.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The media campaign sought to **vilify** the whistleblower rather than address the serious allegations of corporate misconduct.
   媒体运动试图诽谤举报人，而非处理企业不当行为的严重指控。

2. Scientists who challenge popular assumptions are sometimes **vilified** by colleagues before their groundbreaking theories gain acceptance.
   挑战流行假设的科学家有时在其开创性理论获得认可之前会被同行诋毁。

3. It is unjust to **vilify** an entire community based on the criminal actions of a small number of individuals.
   基于少数人的犯罪行为来诽谤整个社区是不公正的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vilify" as making someone seem "vile" — to vilify someone is to paint them as vile and despicable in public.
把"vilify"想成让某人显得"vile"（卑鄙）——诽谤某人就是在公众面前把他们描绘成卑鄙可耻的人。
