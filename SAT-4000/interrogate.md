# 🔍 interrogate

🔊 /ɪnˈtɛrəˌɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To question someone thoroughly and aggressively, especially in a formal or official setting; to inquire closely
**中文：** v. 审问；质问；询问

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- question（询问）
- cross-examine（盘问）
- grill（严厉追问）
- probe（探查）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **interrogate a suspect / interrogate a witness / interrogate the data**

👉 Example: The detective began to interrogate the suspect about his whereabouts on the night of the crime.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The authorities decided to **interrogate** the detained individual for several hours to uncover the truth behind the incident.
   当局决定对被拘留者进行数小时的审讯，以揭开事件背后的真相。

2. Skilled journalists know how to **interrogate** assumptions that the public often takes for granted in political discourse.
   熟练的记者懂得如何质疑公众在政治话语中常常视为理所当然的假设。

3. The committee will **interrogate** the available evidence before reaching any conclusions about the proposed environmental regulations.
   委员会将在对拟议的环境法规作出任何结论之前仔细审查现有证据。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inter-rogate" as "inter" (between) + "rogate" (ask) — asking questions back and forth between people, like a formal questioning session.
把"interrogate"想成"inter"（之间）+ "rogate"（问）——在人与人之间来回提问，就像正式的审讯。
