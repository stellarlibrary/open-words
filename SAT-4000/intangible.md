# 🌫️ intangible

🔊 /ɪnˈtændʒɪbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Unable to be touched or grasped; not having a physical presence; difficult to define, understand, or quantify
**中文：** adj. 无形的，触摸不到的；难以理解的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- immaterial（非物质的）
- impalpable（触摸不到的）
- abstract（抽象的）
- ethereal（缥缈的）
- elusive（难以捉摸的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **intangible assets / intangible benefits / intangible qualities / intangible value**

👉 Example: Brand reputation is an intangible asset that significantly influences a company's market valuation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **intangible** benefits of a supportive work environment often outweigh the tangible rewards of higher compensation packages.
   支持性工作环境的无形好处往往超过更高薪酬的有形回报。

2. Accountants must carefully evaluate **intangible** assets such as intellectual property and brand equity during corporate acquisitions.
   会计师在企业收购过程中必须仔细评估知识产权和品牌价值等无形资产。

3. There was an **intangible** sense of tension in the courtroom that every observer could feel but none could articulate.
   法庭上弥漫着一种无形的紧张感，每个旁观者都能感觉到但无人能言表。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "intangible" as "in-tangible" — "in-" means "not" and "tangible" means "touchable," so intangible literally means "not touchable."
把"intangible"想成"in（不）+ tangible（可触摸的）"——字面意思就是"不可触摸的"，即无形的。
