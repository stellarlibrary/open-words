# 🌅 harbinger

🔊 /ˈhɑːrbɪndʒər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person or thing that announces or signals the approach of something; a forerunner. (v.) To signal the approach of
**中文：** n. 先驱；先兆；前兆 v. 预告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- forerunner（先驱）
- precursor（前兆）
- herald（预告者）
- omen（征兆）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **harbinger of doom / harbinger of spring / harbinger of change / a harbinger of things to come**

👉 Example: The first robin of the season is often regarded as a harbinger of spring.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Many economists viewed the sudden decline in consumer confidence as a **harbinger** of the severe recession that would soon follow.
   许多经济学家将消费者信心的突然下降视为即将到来的严重衰退的前兆。

2. The successful pilot program served as a **harbinger** of the sweeping educational reforms that transformed the entire school district.
   这一成功的试点项目成为了彻底改变整个学区的大规模教育改革的先兆。

3. Dark clouds gathering on the horizon proved to be an accurate **harbinger** of the devastating storm that struck the coastal region.
   地平线上聚集的乌云被证明是袭击沿海地区的毁灭性风暴的准确预兆。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "harbinger" as "harbor + bringer" — someone who brings news to the harbor before the main fleet arrives, announcing what is to come.
把"harbinger"想成"harbor + bringer"——一个在主舰队到达之前把消息带到港口的人，预告即将发生的事情。
