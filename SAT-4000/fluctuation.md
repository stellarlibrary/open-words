# 📈 fluctuation

🔊 /ˌflʌktʃuˈeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An irregular rising and falling in number or amount; a variation or instability in value, level, or state
**中文：** n. 起伏；波动；变动

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- variation（变化）
- oscillation（振荡）
- instability（不稳定）
- swing（摆动）
- shift（变动）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fluctuation in prices / fluctuation of temperature / market fluctuation / seasonal fluctuation**

👉 Example: The fluctuation in oil prices affected the global economy throughout the fiscal quarter.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **fluctuation** in currency exchange rates made international trade increasingly unpredictable for small businesses last year.
   货币汇率的**波动**使得去年国际贸易对小企业来说越来越难以预测。

2. Scientists observed a significant **fluctuation** in ocean temperatures that correlated with unusual weather patterns across the region.
   科学家观察到海洋温度的显著**波动**，这与该地区异常的天气模式相关。

3. The report documented the **fluctuation** of student enrollment over the past decade, revealing a gradual downward trend.
   报告记录了过去十年中学生入学人数的**起伏**，揭示了逐渐下降的趋势。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fluctuation" as "flux" + "uation" — like a continuous flow that keeps going up and down, never staying still.
把"fluctuation"想成"flux（流动）"加上"uation"——就像一股持续的水流，不断上下起伏，永不静止。
