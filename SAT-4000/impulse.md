# ⚡ impulse

🔊 /ˈɪmpʌls/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A sudden strong urge to act; a driving force or stimulus; a pulse of electrical energy or a change in momentum. (v.) To push forward; to impel
**中文：** n. 冲动；脉冲；刺激；神经冲动；推动力；冲量 v. 推动

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- urge（冲动）
- stimulus（刺激）
- drive（驱动力）
- inclination（倾向）
- compulsion（强迫）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **on impulse / impulse buying / resist an impulse / act on impulse / nerve impulse**

👉 Example: She bought the expensive painting on impulse, without considering her monthly budget.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher discovered that electrical **impulse** patterns in the brain change significantly during the transition from wakefulness to sleep.
   研究人员发现，大脑中的电脉冲模式在从清醒过渡到睡眠的过程中会发生显著变化。

2. Acting on **impulse** rather than careful deliberation often leads to decisions that individuals later come to regret profoundly.
   凭冲动而非深思熟虑行事往往会导致人们日后深感后悔的决定。

3. The initial **impulse** behind the reform movement was a desire to eliminate systemic inequalities within the educational framework.
   改革运动最初的推动力是消除教育体系中系统性不平等的愿望。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "impulse" as "im + pulse" — an inner pulse that suddenly pushes you to act before you can think it through.
把"impulse"想成"im（内在的）+ pulse（脉冲）"——一种内在的脉冲突然推动你在思考之前就行动。
