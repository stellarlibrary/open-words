# ✍️ punctuate

🔊 /ˈpʌŋktʃueɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To interrupt or insert at intervals; to emphasize or accentuate; to add punctuation marks to written text
**中文：** v. 加标点于；不时打断；强调

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- interrupt（打断）
- emphasize（强调）
- accentuate（突出）
- intersperse（穿插）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **punctuate a speech / punctuated by applause / punctuate with pauses / punctuated by interruptions**

👉 Example: The professor's lecture was punctuated by frequent questions from the engaged audience.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The otherwise calm negotiations were **punctuated** by sudden outbursts of disagreement between the two rival delegations.
   原本平静的谈判不时被两个对立代表团之间突然的争执所打断。

2. The composer chose to **punctuate** the symphony's quiet passages with dramatic bursts of percussion to heighten emotional impact.
   作曲家选择用戏剧性的打击乐爆发来点缀交响曲的安静乐段，以增强情感冲击力。

3. Students must learn to **punctuate** their essays correctly, as improper punctuation can significantly alter the intended meaning.
   学生必须学会正确地给文章加标点，因为不当的标点会显著改变原本的含义。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "punctu-ate" — related to "punctuation"; just as punctuation marks break up text, to punctuate means to break up or emphasize.
把"punctuate"和"punctuation（标点符号）"联系起来——正如标点符号分隔文本，punctuate意味着打断或强调。
