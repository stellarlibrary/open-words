# 🤔 quandary

🔊 /ˈkwɒndəri/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A state of uncertainty or perplexity, especially as requiring a choice between equally unfavorable options
**中文：** n. 困惑，窘境，进退两难

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dilemma（困境）
- predicament（窘境）
- perplexity（困惑）
- impasse（僵局）
- plight（困境）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in a quandary / moral quandary / ethical quandary / quandary over a decision**

👉 Example: The committee found itself in a quandary over whether to prioritize economic growth or environmental protection.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The medical ethics board faced a profound **quandary** when the experimental treatment showed promise but carried significant unknown risks.
   当实验性治疗显示出前景但带有重大未知风险时，医学伦理委员会面临着深刻的困境。

2. Policymakers remain in a **quandary** about how to balance individual privacy rights with the growing demands of national security.
   政策制定者在如何平衡个人隐私权与日益增长的国家安全需求之间仍然进退两难。

3. The scholar's **quandary** intensified as new archival evidence contradicted the central thesis of her previously published research.
   随着新的档案证据与她先前发表的研究的核心论点相矛盾，这位学者的困惑加深了。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "quan-dary" — "quan" sounds like "wander," and when you are in a quandary, your mind wanders between options, unable to decide.
把"quandary"想成"quan（像wander，徘徊）+ dary"——当你处于quandary时，你的思绪在各个选项之间徘徊，无法做出决定。
