# ⚠️ precarious

🔊 /prɪˈkɛriəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Not securely held or in position; dangerously likely to fall or collapse; dependent on chance or uncertain circumstances
**中文：** adj. 危险的；不确定的；不安全的；靠不住的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unstable（不稳定的）
- insecure（不安全的）
- perilous（危险的）
- uncertain（不确定的）
- risky（冒险的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **precarious position / precarious balance / precarious situation / precarious existence**

👉 Example: The company found itself in a precarious financial position after losing its largest client unexpectedly.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The mountaineers clung to a **precarious** ledge high above the valley, waiting anxiously for the rescue helicopter to arrive.
   登山者们紧贴在山谷上方高处一个危险的岩架上，焦急地等待救援直升机的到来。

2. The nation's economy remained in a **precarious** state, with rising inflation and declining exports threatening widespread financial instability.
   该国经济仍处于不稳定状态，不断上涨的通胀和下降的出口威胁着广泛的金融不稳定。

3. Freelance workers often face a **precarious** existence, lacking the job security and benefits that permanent employment typically provides.
   自由职业者常常面临不稳定的生活，缺乏固定工作通常提供的工作保障和福利。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-care-ious" — you need to "care" beforehand ("pre") because the situation is dangerous and uncertain.
把"precarious"想成"pre（提前）+care（小心）+ious"——你需要提前小心，因为情况是危险和不确定的。
