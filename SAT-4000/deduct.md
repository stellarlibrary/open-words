# 💰 deduct

🔊 /dɪˈdʌkt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To subtract or take away an amount from a total; to remove a portion, especially from a sum of money
**中文：** v. 扣除，减去；演绎

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- subtract（减去）
- withdraw（扣除）
- dock（克扣）
- remove（去除）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **deduct from salary / deduct taxes / deduct expenses / deduct points / deduct an amount**

👉 Example: The employer will deduct income tax directly from each employee's monthly paycheck automatically.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The accounting department will **deduct** the cost of employee benefits directly from the gross salary before issuing monthly payments.
   会计部门将在发放月薪之前直接从总工资中扣除员工福利费用。

2. Taxpayers are permitted to **deduct** certain qualified educational expenses when filing their annual federal income tax returns.
   纳税人在提交年度联邦所得税申报表时，可以扣除某些符合条件的教育费用。

3. The referee chose to **deduct** two points from the competitor's score for repeatedly violating the established competition rules.
   裁判选择因选手反复违反既定比赛规则而从其得分中扣除两分。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "deduct" as "de- + duct (lead away)" — to lead away a portion from the whole, like money being led out of your account.
把"deduct"想成"de- + duct（引走）"——从整体中引走一部分，就像钱从你的账户中被扣走。
