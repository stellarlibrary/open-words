# 🧹 rag

🔊 /ræɡ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A piece of old cloth, especially one torn from a larger piece; tattered clothing. (v.) To tease or mock someone in a playful or unkind way; to scold.
**中文：** n. 破布；碎屑；抹布；破旧衣服 v. 揶揄；戏弄；欺负；责骂

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- tatter（破布）
- scrap（碎片）
- tease（戏弄）
- taunt（嘲弄）
- mock（嘲笑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in rags / from rags to riches / rag doll / rag on someone / like a red rag to a bull**

👉 Example: The classic tale follows a young hero who rises from rags to astonishing riches through determination.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The impoverished children wore nothing but **rags**, yet their resilience and optimism inspired the visiting humanitarian workers.
   贫困的孩子们只穿着破烂的衣服，但他们的韧性和乐观精神激励了来访的人道主义工作者。

2. His classmates would constantly **rag** him about his unusual accent, though he eventually earned their respect through academic excellence.
   他的同学们经常因他不寻常的口音而戏弄他，尽管他最终通过优异的学业成绩赢得了他们的尊重。

3. The conservator carefully used a soft **rag** dampened with a solvent to remove centuries of accumulated grime from the painting.
   修复师小心地用蘸有溶剂的软布擦去画作上积累了几个世纪的污垢。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rag" as something so worn and torn it can only be used to wipe things — and when you "rag" someone, you treat them as roughly as an old rag.
把"rag"想成某种破旧不堪、只能用来擦东西的布——而当你"rag"某人时，你对待他们就像对待一块旧抹布一样粗暴。
