# 😣 inconvenient

🔊 /ˌɪnkənˈviːniənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Causing trouble, difficulty, or discomfort; not suited to one's needs or purposes
**中文：** adj. 不便的；打扰的；有困难的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- troublesome（麻烦的）
- awkward（不方便的）
- bothersome（令人烦恼的）
- untimely（不合时宜的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inconvenient time / inconvenient truth / inconvenient for someone / highly inconvenient**

👉 Example: The road closure at rush hour proved highly inconvenient for thousands of daily commuters.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden schedule change was deeply **inconvenient** for employees who had already arranged their travel plans.
   突如其来的日程变更对已经安排好出行计划的员工来说极为不便。

2. Politicians often choose to ignore **inconvenient** facts that contradict their carefully constructed public narratives.
   政客们常常选择忽视那些与他们精心构建的公共叙事相矛盾的不便事实。

3. The remote location of the research facility made regular supply deliveries logistically **inconvenient** and costly.
   研究设施偏远的位置使得定期物资运送在后勤上很不方便且成本高昂。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-convenient" as "not convenient" — the opposite of convenient means it causes you trouble or difficulty.
把"inconvenient"拆分为"in（不）+ convenient（方便的）"——不方便的，就是给你带来麻烦或困难的。
