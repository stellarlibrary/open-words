# 😶 diffident

🔊 /ˈdɪfɪdənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Lacking self-confidence; shy and hesitant in manner; modest to the point of being reserved
**中文：** adj. 羞怯的；缺乏自信的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- timid（胆小的）
- bashful（害羞的）
- reserved（矜持的）
- unassertive（不自信的）
- self-effacing（谦逊的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **diffident manner / diffident about / diffident smile / diffident approach**

👉 Example: Despite her remarkable talent, she remained diffident about accepting public praise.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **diffident** young scholar hesitated to share her groundbreaking research findings with the distinguished panel of experts.
   这位羞怯的年轻学者犹豫着是否要与杰出的专家小组分享她的突破性研究成果。

2. His **diffident** demeanor in meetings often concealed a brilliant analytical mind capable of extraordinary insight and creativity.
   他在会议上羞怯的举止常常掩盖了他杰出的分析头脑和非凡的洞察力与创造力。

3. A **diffident** approach to negotiation can undermine one's position, even when the underlying arguments are logically compelling.
   在谈判中缺乏自信的态度会削弱自己的立场，即使其论点在逻辑上令人信服。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "diff-ident" as "different from confident" — a diffident person is the opposite of confident, always shy and unsure.
把"diffident"想成"与confident不同"——一个diffident的人是自信的反面，总是害羞和不确定。
