# ⚖️ arbiter

🔊 /ˈɑːrbɪtər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A person who has the authority or power to judge or settle a dispute; one whose opinion or decision is considered authoritative
**中文：** n. 仲裁者；裁决人；权威人士

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- arbitrator（仲裁人）
- judge（裁判）
- mediator（调解人）
- referee（裁判员）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **arbiter of taste / arbiter of fashion / final arbiter / arbiter of disputes**

👉 Example: The Supreme Court serves as the final arbiter of constitutional questions in the United States.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The magazine has long been regarded as the ultimate **arbiter** of fashion trends among style-conscious professionals worldwide.
   该杂志长期以来一直被全球注重风格的专业人士视为时尚潮流的最终权威。

2. An independent **arbiter** was appointed to resolve the increasingly bitter commercial dispute between the two corporations.
   一位独立仲裁者被任命来解决两家公司之间日益激烈的商业纠纷。

3. Public opinion often becomes the de facto **arbiter** of political careers, determining which leaders rise and which fall.
   公众舆论往往成为政治生涯的实际裁决者，决定哪些领导人崛起，哪些衰落。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "arbiter" as someone who sits in an "arbor" (a sheltered place) and makes fair judgments from a position of calm authority.
把"arbiter"想象成坐在"arbor"（凉亭）中的人，从一个冷静权威的位置做出公正裁决。
