# ⚡ ion

🔊 /ˈaɪɑːn/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** An atom or molecule that has gained or lost one or more electrons, giving it a net positive or negative electrical charge
**中文：** n. 离子

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- charged particle（带电粒子）
- cation（阳离子）
- anion（阴离子）
- electrolyte（电解质）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **positive ion / negative ion / ion exchange / ion channel / hydrogen ion**

👉 Example: When sodium chloride dissolves in water, it separates into sodium and chloride ions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The chemistry professor explained how a hydrogen **ion** plays a crucial role in determining the acidity of a solution.
   化学教授解释了氢离子在决定溶液酸度方面发挥的关键作用。

2. Researchers discovered that calcium **ion** channels in nerve cells are essential for transmitting electrical signals throughout the body.
   研究人员发现，神经细胞中的钙离子通道对于在全身传递电信号至关重要。

3. The formation of a lithium **ion** occurs when a lithium atom loses one electron from its outermost energy level.
   当锂原子从其最外层能级失去一个电子时，就形成了锂离子。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "I-ON" — imagine an atom saying "I'm ON" because it has been charged up, like turning on a switch gives it electrical energy.
把"ion"想成"I-ON"——想象一个原子说"I'm ON"（我开了），因为它被充了电，就像打开开关给了它电能一样。
