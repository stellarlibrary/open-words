# 🚂 rail

🔊 /reɪl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A bar or series of bars fixed on upright supports, serving as a barrier or support; a steel track for trains. (v.) To complain or protest strongly and persistently about something.
**中文：** n. 铁轨；扶手；横杆；围栏 v. 责骂；抱怨

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bar（横杆）
- railing（栏杆）
- track（轨道）
- complain（抱怨）
- protest（抗议）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rail against / rail at / by rail / off the rails / rail system / go off the rails**

👉 Example: The senator continued to rail against the proposed legislation during every press conference.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Environmental activists **railed** against the proposed pipeline, arguing it would irreparably damage the region's fragile ecosystem.
   环保活动人士强烈抗议拟议的管道工程，认为它将对该地区脆弱的生态系统造成不可修复的损害。

2. The government invested billions in modernizing the national **rail** network to reduce dependence on automobile transportation.
   政府投资数十亿美元来现代化国家铁路网络，以减少对汽车运输的依赖。

3. Critics continued to **rail** at the administration's immigration policies, demanding comprehensive reform and greater transparency.
   批评者继续抨击政府的移民政策，要求全面改革和更大的透明度。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rail" in two ways: a train rail is a long bar that guides movement, and to "rail" against something is to bang loudly like a train on its tracks, voicing strong complaints.
从两个方面想"rail"：火车轨道是引导运动的长杆，而"rail against"某事就像火车在轨道上大声撞击一样，发出强烈的抱怨。
