# 💡 inkling

🔊 /ˈɪŋklɪŋ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A slight knowledge or suspicion; a hint or vague idea about something
**中文：** n. 暗示；略知；模糊概念；迹象

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- hint（暗示）
- clue（线索）
- suspicion（怀疑）
- notion（概念）
- glimmer（微光）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **have an inkling / no inkling of / the first inkling / give an inkling**

👉 Example: She had no inkling that her colleagues were planning a surprise celebration.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The investors had no **inkling** that the company was on the verge of bankruptcy until the official announcement was released.
   投资者毫不知情该公司即将破产，直到正式公告发布。

2. His subtle comments gave her the first **inkling** that the proposed restructuring would significantly affect her department's budget.
   他微妙的评论让她初步意识到拟议的重组将严重影响她部门的预算。

3. Without any **inkling** of the dangers that lay ahead, the expedition team ventured deep into the uncharted mountain territory.
   在对前方潜在的危险毫无察觉的情况下，探险队深入了未知的山区。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inkling" as a tiny "ink-ling" — just a small drop of ink on paper, representing only a faint trace or hint of the full picture.
把"inkling"想成一小点"ink（墨水）"——纸上一小滴墨水，代表全貌中仅有的一丝痕迹或暗示。
