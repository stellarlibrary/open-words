# 🎲 arbitrary

🔊 /ˈɑːrbɪtreri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Based on random choice or personal whim rather than any reason or system; exercised without restraint or regard for fairness
**中文：** adj. 任意的；武断的；专制的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- capricious（反复无常的）
- random（随机的）
- despotic（专制的）
- whimsical（任性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **arbitrary decision / arbitrary rule / arbitrary power / arbitrary deadline / seem arbitrary**

👉 Example: The manager's arbitrary decision to change the schedule angered many employees.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee criticized the **arbitrary** nature of the new regulations, which lacked any transparent or logical basis.
   委员会批评了新规定的武断性质，认为其缺乏任何透明或合理的依据。

2. Citizens protested against the **arbitrary** exercise of power by local officials who ignored established legal procedures entirely.
   市民抗议地方官员无视既定法律程序、专断行使权力的行为。

3. Setting an **arbitrary** cutoff score for admission may unfairly exclude otherwise qualified and deserving candidates from the program.
   设定一个武断的录取分数线可能会不公平地将本来合格且优秀的候选人排除在项目之外。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Connect "arbitrary" to "arbiter" — an arbiter who makes decisions without reason becomes arbitrary, ruling by whim instead of fairness.
将"arbitrary"与"arbiter"联系起来——一个不讲道理的仲裁者就变得arbitrary（武断的），凭心情而非公正来裁决。
