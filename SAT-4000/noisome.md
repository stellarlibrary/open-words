# 🤢 noisome

🔊 /ˈnɔɪsəm/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Extremely offensive to the senses, especially having a foul smell; harmful, noxious, or unwholesome
**中文：** adj. 恶臭的；有害的；令人讨厌的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fetid（恶臭的）
- noxious（有害的）
- putrid（腐臭的）
- malodorous（难闻的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **noisome odor / noisome fumes / noisome swamp / noisome conditions**

👉 Example: The noisome stench from the neglected landfill prompted residents to file formal complaints with the city.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **noisome** vapors rising from the stagnant pond made it impossible for anyone to remain nearby for long.
   从死水塘中升起的恶臭蒸气让任何人都无法在附近久留。

2. Health inspectors condemned the building after discovering **noisome** conditions that posed a serious risk to its occupants.
   卫生检查员在发现对居住者构成严重风险的有害环境后，下令封闭了该建筑。

3. Medieval cities were often plagued by **noisome** streets where refuse and sewage accumulated without any proper sanitation system.
   中世纪的城市经常饱受恶臭街道的困扰，垃圾和污水在没有适当卫生系统的情况下不断堆积。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "noi-some" as coming from "annoy" — something noisome is so foul and harmful that it greatly annoys your senses, especially your nose.
把"noisome"联想到"annoy（烦恼）"——noisome的东西臭到有害，极度刺激你的感官，尤其是鼻子。
