# 🤢 unsavory

🔊 /ʌnˈseɪvəri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Disagreeable to taste or smell; morally offensive or objectionable; having a bad reputation
**中文：** 难吃的；令人讨厌的；声名狼藉的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- distasteful（令人反感的）
- disreputable（名声不好的）
- objectionable（令人讨厌的）
- unpalatable（难以下咽的）
- repugnant（令人厌恶的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **unsavory character / unsavory reputation / unsavory business / unsavory details**

👉 Example: The journalist uncovered several unsavory details about the corporation's dealings with offshore entities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The investigation revealed that the executive had maintained connections with several **unsavory** figures in the criminal underworld.
   调查显示该高管与犯罪地下世界的几个声名狼藉的人物一直保持着联系。

2. The restaurant received numerous complaints about the **unsavory** flavor of its newly introduced seasonal menu items.
   这家餐厅收到了大量关于新推出的季节性菜品味道难吃的投诉。

3. Historians have documented the **unsavory** practices that were commonplace in early industrial factories throughout the region.
   历史学家记录了该地区早期工业工厂中普遍存在的令人不齿的做法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "un-savor-y" as "not savory" — something you cannot savor or enjoy, whether food that tastes bad or a person with a bad character.
把"unsavory"想成"un（不）+ savory（美味的）"——你无法品味或享受的东西，无论是难吃的食物还是品行不端的人。
