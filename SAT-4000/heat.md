# 🔥 heat

🔊 /hiːt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The quality of being hot; high temperature; intensity of feeling or pressure. (v.) To make or become warm or hot
**中文：** n. 热；高温；压力；激烈 v. 把…加热；使变热

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- warmth（温暖）
- intensity（强度）
- pressure（压力）
- fervor（热情）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in the heat of / heat up / take the heat / turn up the heat / heat wave**

👉 Example: In the heat of the debate, several delegates made remarks they later regretted.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The extreme **heat** of the summer months placed unprecedented strain on the region's electrical grid and water supply.
   夏季极端高温给该地区的电网和供水系统带来了前所未有的压力。

2. Political leaders began to **heat** up their rhetoric as the election campaign entered its final and most critical phase.
   随着竞选活动进入最后也是最关键的阶段，政治领导人开始升级言辞。

3. Scientists continue to study how rising ocean **heat** contributes to the increasing frequency and severity of tropical storms.
   科学家们继续研究海洋温度上升如何导致热带风暴频率和强度的增加。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "heat" as both literal fire and figurative pressure — when things "heat up," temperature and tension both rise.
把"heat"想成既有字面上的火热，也有比喻的压力——当事情"heat up（升温）"时，温度和紧张感都在上升。
