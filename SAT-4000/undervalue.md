# 💎 undervalue

🔊 /ˌʌndərˈvæljuː/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To estimate the worth of something as lower than its true value; to fail to appreciate the full importance of something
**中文：** v. 低估之价值；看轻

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- underestimate（低估）
- underrate（低估）
- depreciate（贬低）
- belittle（轻视）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **undervalue contributions / undervalue employees / undervalue assets / undervalue the importance**

👉 Example: Managers who consistently undervalue their team's contributions risk losing talented employees to competitors.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Investors who **undervalue** emerging technology stocks may miss significant opportunities for substantial long-term portfolio growth.
   低估新兴科技股的投资者可能会错过投资组合大幅长期增长的重要机会。

2. Society continues to **undervalue** the essential work performed by caregivers, teachers, and other public service professionals.
   社会仍然低估护理人员、教师和其他公共服务专业人员所从事的重要工作。

3. The appraiser was accused of deliberately attempting to **undervalue** the property to benefit the prospective buyer in negotiations.
   评估师被指控故意试图低估房产价值，以在谈判中使潜在买家受益。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "under-value" as placing a value under the real worth — you see something as worth less than it truly is.
把"undervalue"想成把价值定在真实价值"之下"——你认为某物不如其实际价值。
