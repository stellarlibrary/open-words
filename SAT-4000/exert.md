# 💪 exert

🔊 /ɪɡˈzɜːrt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To apply or bring to bear a force, influence, or quality; to make a physical or mental effort
**中文：** v. 运用，发挥；施加（影响、压力等）；尽（力）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- apply（施加）
- wield（运用）
- exercise（行使）
- employ（使用）
- exude（散发）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **exert influence / exert pressure / exert control / exert oneself / exert force**

👉 Example: The senator exerted considerable influence over the committee's final decision.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The international community must **exert** diplomatic pressure on the regime to ensure compliance with humanitarian standards.
   国际社会必须对该政权施加外交压力，以确保其遵守人道主义标准。

2. Gravitational forces **exert** a measurable pull on all objects possessing mass, regardless of their distance apart.
   引力对所有具有质量的物体施加可测量的拉力，无论它们之间的距离如何。

3. Students who **exert** themselves consistently throughout the semester tend to achieve significantly higher academic results.
   整个学期持续努力的学生往往会取得明显更高的学业成绩。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "exert" as "exit + hurt" — when you push effort outward, it can exhaust or even hurt you from the strain.
把"exert"想成"exit + hurt"——当你向外施加力量时，这种劳累甚至会让你感到疲痛。
