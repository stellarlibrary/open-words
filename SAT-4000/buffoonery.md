# 🎭 buffoonery

🔊 /bəˈfuːnəri/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Ridiculous or comical behavior; clowning or jesting intended to amuse others
**中文：** n. 滑稽；打诨；丑角行为

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- clowning（扮丑）
- tomfoolery（愚蠢行为）
- farce（闹剧）
- antics（滑稽动作）
- slapstick（打闹喜剧）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **engage in buffoonery / resort to buffoonery / sheer buffoonery**

👉 Example: The professor dismissed the student's constant buffoonery as a distraction from serious academic work.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician's **buffoonery** during the debate undermined his credibility and alienated many thoughtful voters.
   那位政客在辩论中的滑稽表现损害了他的信誉，疏远了许多深思熟虑的选民。

2. Despite the **buffoonery** of the court jester, the king remained somber and preoccupied with affairs of state.
   尽管宫廷小丑表演滑稽，国王依然神情严肃，专注于国家事务。

3. Critics argued that the film relied too heavily on crude **buffoonery** rather than intelligent humor or witty dialogue.
   评论家认为这部电影过于依赖粗俗的滑稽表演，而非巧妙的幽默或机智的对白。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "buffoon" + "-ery" — a buffoon is a clown, so buffoonery is the art of clowning around.
把"buffoonery"拆成"buffoon（小丑）+ -ery（行为）"——小丑的行为就是滑稽打诨。
