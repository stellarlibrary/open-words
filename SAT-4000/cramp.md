# 🔧 cramp

🔊 /kræmp/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A sudden, involuntary muscular contraction causing sharp pain. (v.) To restrict or inhibit the development or free movement of; to confine
**中文：** n. 痉挛；绞痛 v. 束缚；限制；用夹子夹紧

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- spasm（痉挛）
- constrain（限制）
- restrict（束缚）
- hinder（阻碍）
- confine（约束）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cramp one's style / muscle cramp / stomach cramps / cramp someone's efforts**

👉 Example: Insufficient funding continued to cramp the researchers' ability to pursue innovative experiments.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Severe budget cuts will inevitably **cramp** the university's capacity to attract world-class scholars and conduct vital research.
   严重的预算削减将不可避免地限制大学吸引世界级学者和开展重要研究的能力。

2. The athlete suffered a debilitating leg **cramp** during the final quarter, forcing her to withdraw from the competition.
   这位运动员在最后一节比赛中遭受了严重的腿部痉挛，迫使她退出比赛。

3. Excessive regulations can **cramp** economic growth by discouraging entrepreneurs from launching new ventures in emerging markets.
   过度的法规可能会通过阻止企业家在新兴市场开展新业务来限制经济增长。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cramp" as your muscles getting "clamped" — squeezed tight and restricted in movement.
把"cramp"想成肌肉被"clamp（夹住）"——被紧紧夹住，行动受限。
