# 😡 unconscionable

🔊 /ʌnˈkɒnʃənəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Not right or reasonable; shockingly unfair or unjust; exceeding the bounds of what is acceptable
**中文：** 不合理的；昧着良心的；过度的；不公正的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- outrageous（骇人的）
- unscrupulous（不择手段的）
- excessive（过分的）
- inexcusable（不可原谅的）
- egregious（极其恶劣的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **unconscionable delay / unconscionable behavior / unconscionable contract / unconscionable price**

👉 Example: The court declared the contract unconscionable because it imposed grossly unfair terms on the weaker party.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The landlord charged **unconscionable** rents during the housing crisis, exploiting desperate families who had no other options available.
   房东在住房危机期间收取过高的租金，剥削走投无路的家庭。

2. The judge ruled that the **unconscionable** terms of the agreement effectively stripped consumers of all their legal rights.
   法官裁定该协议中不合理的条款实际上剥夺了消费者的所有合法权利。

3. It would be **unconscionable** to reduce funding for public hospitals while millions of citizens still lack access to basic healthcare.
   在数百万公民仍然缺乏基本医疗保障的情况下削减公立医院资金是昧着良心的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "un-conscience-able" — something done without a conscience; so wrong that no reasonable person with a conscience would do it.
把"unconscionable"想成"un（无）+ conscience（良心）+ able"——没有良心才能做出的事，极不合理。
