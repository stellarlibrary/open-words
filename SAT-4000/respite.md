# 🛌 respite

🔊 /ˈrɛspɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A short period of rest or relief from something difficult or unpleasant; a temporary delay. (v.) To grant a reprieve or temporary relief
**中文：** n. 缓解；暂缓；暂时的休息 v. 使缓解；暂缓

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reprieve（缓刑；暂缓）
- relief（缓解）
- intermission（间歇）
- pause（暂停）
- recess（休息）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a brief respite / respite from / without respite / respite care / a moment of respite**

👉 Example: The ceasefire provided a temporary respite from the relentless bombardment of the besieged city.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The unexpected rainfall offered a welcome **respite** from the oppressive heat that had gripped the region for weeks.
   这场意外的降雨为困扰该地区数周的酷热带来了一次令人欣慰的缓解。

2. The exhausted medical staff worked without **respite** throughout the night to treat victims of the devastating earthquake.
   精疲力竭的医务人员整夜不休息地救治毁灭性地震的受害者。

3. The court agreed to **respite** the execution of the sentence pending a thorough review of newly discovered evidence.
   法院同意在对新发现的证据进行全面审查之前暂缓执行判决。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "res-PITE" — "rest a bit" — a respite is when you get to rest a bit from something difficult.
把"respite"想成"rest a bit"（休息一会儿）——respite就是从困难中暂时得到一点休息。
