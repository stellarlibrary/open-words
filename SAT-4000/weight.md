# ⚖️ weight

🔊 /weɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The heaviness of a person or object; a burden or responsibility; relative importance or influence. (v.) To make heavier; to assign importance or value to something
**中文：** n. 重量；重力；负担；砝码；重要性 v. 加重量于；使变重

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- heaviness（重量）
- burden（负担）
- significance（重要性）
- gravity（重力）
- load（载荷）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **carry weight / weight of evidence / weight a decision / give weight to / pull one's weight**

👉 Example: The professor's endorsement carries considerable weight in the academic selection committee.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee decided to give greater **weight** to empirical evidence rather than anecdotal testimony when formulating new policy guidelines.
   委员会决定在制定新政策指南时更加重视实证证据，而非轶事性证词。

2. The sheer **weight** of public opinion eventually compelled the legislators to reconsider their stance on the environmental regulation.
   公众舆论的巨大分量最终迫使立法者重新考虑他们对环境法规的立场。

3. Analysts must carefully **weight** each variable in the model to ensure the final projections reflect realistic economic conditions.
   分析师必须仔细权衡模型中的每个变量，以确保最终预测反映现实的经济状况。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "weight" as "wait" — when something has great weight, it makes you wait and think carefully before deciding.
把"weight"想成"wait（等待）"——当某事具有重大分量时，它会让你停下来仔细思考再做决定。
