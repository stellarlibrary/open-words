# 🔄 pervert

🔊 /pərˈvɜːrt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To distort or corrupt the original meaning or course of something; to lead astray morally. (n.) A person whose behavior deviates from what is considered normal
**中文：** v. 使堕落；诱惑；使反常；滥用 n. 堕落者；行为反常者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- corrupt（使腐化）
- distort（歪曲）
- debase（贬低）
- warp（使扭曲）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **pervert the course of justice / pervert the truth / pervert the meaning of**

👉 Example: The officials attempted to pervert the course of justice by concealing critical evidence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The propagandists sought to **pervert** historical facts in order to advance their own ideological narrative.
   宣传者试图歪曲历史事实，以推进他们自己的意识形态叙事。

2. Critics argued that the new regulations would **pervert** the original intent of the landmark legislation.
   批评者认为新法规会扭曲这项具有里程碑意义的立法的初衷。

3. Any attempt to **pervert** the judicial process undermines public confidence in the entire legal system.
   任何试图妨碍司法程序的行为都会损害公众对整个法律体系的信心。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "per-vert" as "per" (thoroughly) + "vert" (turn) — to thoroughly turn something away from its proper course, twisting it into something wrong.
把"pervert"想成"per"（完全地）+"vert"（转）——把某物完全从正确的方向扭转过来，变成错误的东西。
