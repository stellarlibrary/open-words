# 🔄 fungible

🔊 /ˈfʌndʒɪbəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Mutually interchangeable; capable of being substituted for something of equal value. (n.) A fungible item or commodity
**中文：** adj. 可互换的；代替的；可取代的 n. 代替物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- interchangeable（可互换的）
- replaceable（可替换的）
- substitutable（可替代的）
- equivalent（等价的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fungible assets / fungible goods / fungible commodities / non-fungible token**

👉 Example: Currency is fungible because one dollar bill can be freely exchanged for any other dollar bill of equal denomination.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. In contract law, commodities such as grain and oil are considered **fungible** because individual units are mutually interchangeable.
   在合同法中，谷物和石油等商品被视为可互换的，因为各个单位可以相互替代。

2. The economist argued that skilled labor is not entirely **fungible**, as each worker brings unique expertise and experience.
   经济学家认为，熟练劳动力并非完全可替代的，因为每位工人都带来独特的专业知识和经验。

3. The rise of non-**fungible** tokens demonstrated market demand for digital assets with verified uniqueness and individual provenance.
   非同质化代币的兴起表明了市场对具有验证唯一性和个体来源的数字资产的需求。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fungible" as "fun to jiggle around" — fungible items can be juggled and swapped without anyone noticing the difference.
把"fungible"想成"fun to jiggle around（随意交换很有趣）"——可互换的物品可以随意调换，没有人会注意到区别。
