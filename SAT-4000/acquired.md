# 🧬 acquired

🔊 /əˈkwaɪərd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Gained or developed through experience, learning, or external influence rather than being innate or inherited
**中文：** 后天的；已获得的；已成习惯的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- learned（习得的）
- developed（后天形成的）
- cultivated（培养的）
- gained（获得的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **acquired taste / acquired skill / acquired immunity / acquired trait / acquired knowledge**

👉 Example: Classical music is often considered an acquired taste that develops through repeated and attentive listening.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Many medical professionals regard dark chocolate as an **acquired** taste that requires gradual exposure to fully appreciate.
   许多医学专业人士认为黑巧克力是一种需要逐渐接触才能充分欣赏的后天养成的口味。

2. The biologist explained that **acquired** traits developed during an organism's lifetime cannot be passed to subsequent generations.
   生物学家解释说，生物体在其一生中发展出的后天特征不能遗传给后代。

3. Her extensive **acquired** knowledge of international trade law proved invaluable during the complex bilateral negotiations.
   她在国际贸易法方面丰富的后天知识在复杂的双边谈判中被证明是极其宝贵的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "acquired" as "a-choir-ed" — you were not born in the choir; you acquired the skill and earned your place through training.
把"acquired"想成"a-choir-ed"——你不是天生就在合唱团里的；你通过训练获得了技能并赢得了一席之地。
