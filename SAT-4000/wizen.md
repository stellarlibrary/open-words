# 🍂 wizen

🔊 /ˈwɪzən/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To become dry, shrunken, and wrinkled; to shrivel or wither. (adj.) Dried up, shrunken, or wrinkled in appearance
**中文：** v. 枯萎；凋谢；干瘪 adj. 枯萎的；皱缩的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- shrivel（枯萎）
- wither（凋谢）
- wrinkle（起皱）
- desiccate（使干燥）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **wizen face / wizened old man / wizened hands / wizened fruit / wizened appearance**

👉 Example: The archaeologist carefully examined the wizened remains of ancient grain discovered in the sealed burial chamber.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prolonged drought caused the once-thriving orchard's fruit to **wizen** and drop prematurely from the branches.
   长期干旱导致曾经繁茂的果园里的水果枯萎并过早地从树枝上掉落。

2. A **wizen** figure emerged from the shadowed doorway, her deeply lined face bearing testimony to decades of hardship.
   一个干瘪的身影从阴暗的门口走出，她布满深深皱纹的脸见证了数十年的艰辛。

3. Scholars noted how the **wizened** parchment scrolls had remarkably preserved their legible inscriptions despite centuries of storage.
   学者们注意到这些干皱的羊皮纸卷轴尽管存放了几个世纪，仍然奇迹般地保存着可辨认的铭文。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "wizen" as sounding like "wise-en" — a wise old person often has a wizened, wrinkled face from years of experience.
把"wizen"想成发音类似"wise（智慧的）+ -en"——智慧的老人通常有一张因岁月而布满皱纹的干瘪面孔。
