# ⚰️ obsequies

🔊 /ˈɑːbsɪkwiz/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Funeral rites or ceremonies; the observances or services held for a deceased person
**中文：** n. 葬礼，丧礼

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- funeral rites（葬礼仪式）
- burial ceremonies（安葬仪式）
- last rites（临终仪式）
- memorial services（追悼会）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **perform obsequies / solemn obsequies / attend the obsequies** — this is a formal, literary word almost always used in the plural

👉 Example: The obsequies for the fallen leader were attended by dignitaries from around the world.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The solemn **obsequies** for the distinguished statesman were conducted with full military honors at the national cathedral.
   这位杰出政治家的庄严葬礼在国家大教堂以全套军事礼仪举行。

2. Ancient Roman **obsequies** often included elaborate processions, eulogies, and ritual offerings to honor the memory of the departed.
   古罗马的葬礼通常包括精心组织的游行、悼词和祭祀仪式，以缅怀逝者。

3. The family requested that the **obsequies** remain private, allowing only close relatives and intimate friends to attend the ceremony.
   家属要求葬礼保持私密，只允许近亲和密友参加仪式。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "obsequies" as "OB-sequence" — a sequence of observances performed at a funeral to bid farewell to the deceased.
把"obsequies"想成"OB-sequence"——在葬礼上为逝者举行的一系列仪式。
