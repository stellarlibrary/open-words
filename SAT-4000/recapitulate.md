# 📋 recapitulate

🔊 /ˌriːkəˈpɪtʃʊleɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To summarize and restate the main points of something; to go over the key elements again briefly
**中文：** v. 扼要重述；概括；摘要

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- summarize（总结）
- reiterate（重申）
- review（回顾）
- outline（概述）
- recap（扼要重述）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **recapitulate the main points / recapitulate an argument / recapitulate the findings / briefly recapitulate**

👉 Example: Before concluding the lecture, the professor paused to recapitulate the three major themes discussed throughout the session.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The chairperson asked the secretary to **recapitulate** the key decisions made during the lengthy board meeting for absent members.
   主席请秘书为缺席成员扼要重述了冗长的董事会会议中做出的关键决定。

2. In the final chapter, the author skillfully **recapitulates** the central arguments presented across the preceding twelve chapters.
   在最后一章中，作者巧妙地概括了前十二章中提出的核心论点。

3. The defense attorney chose to **recapitulate** the strongest evidence in her closing statement to leave a lasting impression.
   辩护律师在结案陈词中选择重述最有力的证据，以留下深刻印象。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-cap-itulate" as "re-cap" — just like putting a cap back on, you are capping off a discussion by going over the main points again.
把"recapitulate"想成"re-cap"——就像重新盖上盖子，你通过重述要点来为讨论做总结收尾。
