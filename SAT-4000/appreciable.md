# 📏 appreciable

🔊 /əˈpriːʃəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Large or significant enough to be noticed, measured, or felt; considerable in amount or degree
**中文：** 可感知的；相当可观的；值得重视的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- noticeable（显著的）
- considerable（相当大的）
- significant（重要的）
- measurable（可衡量的）
- substantial（实质性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **an appreciable difference / an appreciable amount / an appreciable effect / appreciable improvement**

👉 Example: The new policy resulted in an appreciable reduction in carbon emissions across the industrial sector.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. There has been no **appreciable** change in the patient's condition despite three consecutive weeks of intensive medical treatment.
   尽管经过连续三周的强化治疗，患者的病情没有出现明显的变化。

2. The investment yielded an **appreciable** return that exceeded the expectations of even the most optimistic financial analysts.
   这项投资产生了可观的回报，甚至超出了最乐观的金融分析师的预期。

3. Scientists detected an **appreciable** increase in global ocean temperatures over the past decade through satellite monitoring data.
   科学家通过卫星监测数据发现过去十年全球海洋温度出现了明显的上升。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "appreciable" as "able to be appreciated" — if something is appreciable, it is large enough that you can actually notice and appreciate it.
把"appreciable"想成"able to be appreciated"——如果某物是appreciable的，说明它大到足以被注意到和感知到。
