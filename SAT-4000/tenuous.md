# 🕸️ tenuous

🔊 /ˈtɛnjuəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Very weak or slight; insubstantial; thin or slender in form; having little substance or significance.
**中文：** adj. 纤细的；稀薄的；贫乏的；脆弱的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flimsy（脆弱的）
- fragile（易碎的）
- insubstantial（薄弱的）
- slight（微小的）
- weak（虚弱的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tenuous connection / tenuous link / tenuous grasp / tenuous relationship / tenuous evidence**

👉 Example: The prosecution's case rested on a tenuous connection between the suspect and the crime scene.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scholar acknowledged that the **tenuous** evidence supporting the hypothesis required substantial additional research to be convincing.
   该学者承认，支持这一假说的薄弱证据需要大量额外研究才能令人信服。

2. Diplomatic relations between the neighboring countries remained **tenuous** despite several rounds of peace negotiations over the past decade.
   尽管过去十年间进行了多轮和平谈判，两个邻国之间的外交关系仍然脆弱。

3. The company maintained only a **tenuous** grip on its market share as competitors introduced innovative products at lower prices.
   随着竞争对手以更低的价格推出创新产品，该公司对市场份额的掌控变得十分薄弱。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tenuous" as "ten-u-ous" — something stretched so thin (like pulled into ten pieces) that it becomes fragile and weak.
把"tenuous"想成"ten（十）-u-ous"——某物被拉得很薄（仿佛被拉成十段），变得脆弱而虚弱。
