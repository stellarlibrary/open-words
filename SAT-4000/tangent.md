# 📐 tangent

🔊 /ˈtændʒənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Of or relating to a tangent; touching at a single point; diverging from a previous course. (n.) A line that touches a curve at a single point; the trigonometric function of an angle
**中文：** adj. 切线的，相切的；离题的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- divergent（离题的）
- digressive（偏离主题的）
- tangential（切线的）
- touching（接触的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **go off on a tangent / tangent line / tangent to a circle / tangent point**

👉 Example: The professor went off on a tangent and spent twenty minutes discussing an unrelated topic.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. During the lecture, the speaker went off on a **tangent** and discussed personal anecdotes unrelated to the main thesis.
   在演讲过程中，发言者偏离了主题，讲述了与主要论点无关的个人轶事。

2. The mathematician drew a **tangent** line to the curve at the point where the slope equaled zero.
   数学家在斜率等于零的点处画了一条曲线的切线。

3. His argument was merely **tangent** to the central issue and failed to address the core concerns of the committee.
   他的论点仅与核心问题相切，未能解决委员会的主要关切。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tangent" as "tan-gent" — a gentleman (gent) who barely touches the topic before wandering off in another direction.
把"tangent"想成"tan-gent"——一位绅士(gent)仅仅触碰话题就跑到另一个方向去了，就像切线只碰圆一个点。
