# ⚠️ impair

🔊 /ɪmˈpɛr/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To weaken, damage, or cause to diminish in quality, value, or strength
**中文：** v. 损害；损伤；削弱；减少

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- weaken（削弱）
- diminish（减少）
- undermine（损害）
- damage（损坏）
- compromise（危及）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **impair judgment / impair vision / impair ability / impair functioning**

👉 Example: Excessive alcohol consumption can severely impair your judgment and reaction time.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Sleep deprivation can significantly **impair** cognitive function, reducing a person's ability to concentrate and make decisions.
   睡眠不足会严重损害认知功能，降低人的注意力和决策能力。

2. The industrial pollution has **impaired** the water quality in surrounding communities to a dangerous and alarming degree.
   工业污染已将周围社区的水质损害到了危险且令人担忧的程度。

3. Chronic stress is known to **impair** the immune system, leaving individuals more vulnerable to infections and disease.
   众所周知，长期压力会削弱免疫系统，使人更容易受到感染和疾病的侵害。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-pair" as "not a pair" — when something is impaired, it is no longer in its proper pair or condition, so it is damaged.
把"impair"想成"im-pair（不再成对）"——当某物被impair时，它不再处于正常状态，即受到了损害。
