# 🔄 generalize

🔊 /ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make a broad or general statement by inferring from specific cases; to make widely applicable; to spread or extend
**中文：** v. 概括；推广；使一般化

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- extrapolate（推断）
- universalize（普遍化）
- broaden（扩展）
- abstract（抽象化）
- oversimplify（过度简化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **generalize from data / generalize findings / difficult to generalize / generalize a principle / generalize across contexts**

👉 Example: Researchers cautioned that it would be premature to generalize the results to the broader population.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. It is methodologically unsound to **generalize** conclusions drawn from a small sample to the entire population without further verification.
   从小样本中得出结论推广到整个群体而不加进一步验证，在方法论上是不严谨的。

2. The professor urged students not to **generalize** about cultural practices based solely on limited personal observations and anecdotal evidence.
   教授敦促学生不要仅凭有限的个人观察和轶事证据就对文化习俗做出概括。

3. The mathematical theorem was later **generalized** to apply to all higher-dimensional spaces, expanding its theoretical significance considerably.
   这一数学定理后来被推广应用于所有高维空间，大大扩展了其理论意义。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "general-ize" — to make something "general," taking a specific idea and applying it broadly to all cases.
把"generalize"拆分为"general-ize"——使某事物变得"general"（一般的），将一个具体的想法广泛应用于所有情况。
