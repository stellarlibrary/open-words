# 🕊️ emancipation

🔊 /ɪˌmænsɪˈpeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act or process of being set free from legal, social, or political restrictions; liberation from bondage or oppression
**中文：** n. 解放；释放

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- liberation（解放）
- freedom（自由）
- deliverance（解救）
- release（释放）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **emancipation of slaves / emancipation from oppression / political emancipation / women's emancipation**

👉 Example: The Emancipation Proclamation marked a turning point in American history.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **emancipation** of enslaved peoples in the nineteenth century represented one of the most significant social transformations in modern history.
   十九世纪被奴役人民的解放是现代历史上最重要的社会变革之一。

2. Many scholars argue that true **emancipation** requires not only legal freedom but also economic independence and access to education.
   许多学者认为，真正的解放不仅需要法律上的自由，还需要经济独立和受教育的机会。

3. The movement for women's **emancipation** gained considerable momentum during the early twentieth century across Europe and North America.
   争取妇女解放的运动在二十世纪初期在欧洲和北美获得了相当大的势头。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "emancipation" as the noun form of "emancipate" — it is the state of being freed, like the Emancipation Proclamation that freed enslaved people.
把"emancipation"想成"emancipate"（解放）的名词形式——它是被释放的状态，就像《解放宣言》解放了被奴役的人。
