# 🔓 discretionary

🔊 /dɪˈskreʃəˌneri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Available for use at the judgment or choice of the user; not fixed or mandatory but left to individual decision
**中文：** adj. 任意的，自由决定的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- optional（可选择的）
- elective（选择性的）
- voluntary（自愿的）
- unrestricted（不受限制的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **discretionary spending / discretionary income / discretionary power / discretionary funds**

👉 Example: After paying all essential bills, she had very little discretionary income left for entertainment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The central bank has **discretionary** authority to adjust interest rates in response to changing economic conditions without legislative approval.
   央行拥有自由裁量权，可以在无需立法批准的情况下根据经济状况的变化调整利率。

2. Rising inflation has significantly reduced the average household's **discretionary** spending on non-essential goods and leisure activities this year.
   不断上升的通货膨胀大幅减少了今年普通家庭在非必需品和休闲活动上的可自由支配支出。

3. The principal was granted **discretionary** power to implement new safety protocols at the school without seeking prior board approval.
   校长被授予自由裁量权，可以在不征求校董事会事先批准的情况下在学校实施新的安全协议。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "discretionary" as "discretion + ary" — having the quality of discretion, meaning you get to choose freely how to use it.
把"discretionary"想成"discretion + ary"——具有自由裁量的性质，意味着你可以自由选择如何使用。
