# 🐴 bridle

🔊 /ˈbraɪdl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The headgear used to control a horse, including reins and bit; a restraint. (v.) To put a bridle on; to restrain or control; to show resentment
**中文：** n. 缰绳；马勒；系带；约束物 v. 控制；给装马勒；上笼头；昂首；抑制

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- restrain（抑制）
- curb（约束）
- check（制止）
- harness（驾驭）
- rein（控制）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bridle one's temper / bridle at a remark / bridle one's emotions / put a bridle on**

👉 Example: She bridled at the suggestion that her research was not original.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The experienced diplomat managed to **bridle** his frustration during the prolonged and contentious negotiations with foreign delegates.
   这位经验丰富的外交官在与外国代表进行漫长而有争议的谈判中成功抑制了自己的不满。

2. Critics **bridled** at the new regulation, arguing that it imposed unnecessary restrictions on academic freedom and research.
   批评者对新法规表示不满，认为它对学术自由和研究施加了不必要的限制。

3. A well-fitted **bridle** allows the rider to communicate subtle commands to the horse with minimal physical effort.
   一副合适的马勒使骑手能够以最少的体力向马传达微妙的指令。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bridle" as "bride-le" — a bride holding the reins of her wedding, keeping everything under control.
把"bridle"想成"bride-le"——新娘握着婚礼的缰绳，控制一切事务井然有序。
