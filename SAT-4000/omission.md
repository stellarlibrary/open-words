# ✂️ omission

🔊 /oʊˈmɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act of leaving something out or excluding it; a failure to do something, especially something that one has a duty to do; something that has been left out or neglected
**中文：** n. 疏忽，遗漏；省略

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- exclusion（排除）
- oversight（疏忽）
- neglect（忽略）
- deletion（删除）
- gap（缺漏）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **glaring omission / serious omission / omission of details / sin of omission / omission from the list**

👉 Example: The omission of critical safety data from the report raised serious ethical concerns among reviewers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **omission** of several key variables from the study undermined the credibility of the researchers' final conclusions.
   研究中遗漏了几个关键变量，这削弱了研究人员最终结论的可信度。

2. Critics argued that the **omission** of minority perspectives from the curriculum represented a significant educational shortcoming.
   批评者认为，课程中省略少数群体的观点是一个重大的教育缺陷。

3. The lawyer contended that the **omission** of the defendant's medical records constituted a serious violation of due process.
   律师主张，遗漏被告的医疗记录构成对正当程序的严重违反。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "o-mission" as "oh, I missed it!" — you forgot to include something important, so it was an omission.
把"omission"想成"oh, mission（哦，任务）"——你遗漏了任务中的重要部分，所以就是一个疏忽。
