# 🚫 immoderate

🔊 /ɪˈmɒdərət/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Exceeding reasonable or normal limits; lacking moderation or restraint; excessive.
**中文：** adj. 无节制的；过度的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- excessive（过度的）
- extravagant（奢侈的）
- unreasonable（不合理的）
- intemperate（无节制的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **immoderate consumption / immoderate spending / immoderate behavior / immoderate demands**

👉 Example: The physician cautioned against immoderate consumption of processed foods high in sodium.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee criticized the executive's **immoderate** spending on personal luxuries funded entirely by corporate accounts.
   委员会批评该高管完全用公司账户资金在个人奢侈品上的过度消费。

2. Historians attribute the empire's decline in part to the ruling class's **immoderate** pursuit of wealth and political power.
   历史学家将帝国的衰落部分归因于统治阶级对财富和政治权力的无节制追求。

3. The professor warned that **immoderate** reliance on artificial intelligence could diminish critical thinking skills among university students.
   教授警告说，对人工智能的过度依赖可能会削弱大学生的批判性思维能力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-moderate" as "not moderate" — the opposite of being moderate or balanced, meaning excessive.
把"immoderate"想成"im（不）+ moderate（适度的）"——不适度的，所以是无节制的、过度的。
