# 🧠 intellect

🔊 /ˈɪntəlekt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The faculty of reasoning and understanding objectively; mental capacity or ability; a person of great intellectual ability
**中文：** n. 智力，理解力；知识分子；思维逻辑领悟力

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- intelligence（智力）
- mind（头脑）
- reasoning（推理力）
- brainpower（脑力）
- acumen（敏锐）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sharp intellect / superior intellect / public intellect / exercise one's intellect / a person of great intellect**

👉 Example: Her sharp intellect and analytical skills made her an invaluable asset to the research department.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor was renowned for possessing a formidable **intellect** that could unravel the most intricate philosophical arguments effortlessly.
   这位教授以拥有强大的智力而闻名，能够毫不费力地解开最错综复杂的哲学论证。

2. Many of the greatest **intellects** in human history made their most significant contributions before reaching the age of forty.
   人类历史上许多最伟大的知识分子在四十岁之前就做出了最重要的贡献。

3. The debate competition challenged students to demonstrate both their **intellect** and their ability to construct persuasive oral arguments.
   辩论比赛要求学生展示他们的智力以及构建有说服力的口头论证的能力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "intellect" as "intel-lect" — "intel" like intelligence gathering, and "lect" from Latin "legere" meaning "to choose or read." Your intellect is your ability to read, choose, and reason.
把"intellect"想成"intel（情报）+ lect（选择/阅读）"——你的intellect就是你阅读、选择和推理的能力。
