# 🌋 catastrophic

🔊 /ˌkætəˈstrɒfɪk/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Involving or causing sudden great damage, suffering, or destruction; extremely harmful or disastrous
**中文：** adj. 灾难的；悲惨的；毁灭性的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disastrous（灾难性的）
- devastating（毁灭性的）
- calamitous（灾难的）
- ruinous（毁灭性的）
- cataclysmic（剧变的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **catastrophic failure / catastrophic consequences / catastrophic event / catastrophic damage**

👉 Example: The engineers identified a design flaw that could lead to catastrophic failure of the entire structure.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Rising global temperatures may trigger **catastrophic** changes in weather patterns, threatening food supplies across multiple continents.
   全球气温上升可能引发灾难性的天气模式变化，威胁多个大洲的粮食供应。

2. The **catastrophic** collapse of the bridge during rush hour resulted in significant loss of life and widespread public outcry.
   这座桥在高峰时段的灾难性倒塌导致了重大人员伤亡和广泛的公众抗议。

3. Analysts concluded that the company's **catastrophic** mismanagement of funds led directly to its declaration of bankruptcy last quarter.
   分析师得出结论，该公司对资金的灾难性管理不善直接导致了上季度的破产声明。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "catastrophic" as "catastrophe" plus "-ic" — if a catastrophe is the event, catastrophic describes everything about it that is utterly destructive.
把"catastrophic"想成"catastrophe"加"-ic"——如果catastrophe是事件本身，catastrophic就是描述其彻底毁灭性的形容词。
