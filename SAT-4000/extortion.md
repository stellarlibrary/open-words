# 💰 extortion

🔊 /ɪkˈstɔːrʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The practice of obtaining something, especially money, through force, threats, or other coercive means
**中文：** n. 勒索；敲诈；强夺

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- blackmail（勒索）
- coercion（胁迫）
- shakedown（敲诈）
- intimidation（恐吓）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **extortion scheme / extortion racket / guilty of extortion / charges of extortion**

👉 Example: The businessman was arrested on charges of extortion after threatening his competitors.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The federal investigation uncovered a sophisticated **extortion** scheme targeting vulnerable small business owners across several states.
   联邦调查揭露了一个针对多个州脆弱小企业主的复杂勒索计划。

2. The corrupt official was convicted of **extortion** after demanding bribes from contractors seeking government building permits.
   这名腐败官员因向寻求政府建筑许可证的承包商索取贿赂而被判犯有敲诈罪。

3. Cybercriminals increasingly rely on digital **extortion**, encrypting victims' data and demanding cryptocurrency payments for its release.
   网络犯罪分子越来越依赖数字勒索，加密受害者的数据并要求支付加密货币以解锁。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "extortion" as "ex- (out) + tortion (twist)" — twisting something out of someone by force.
把"extortion"想成"ex-（出来）+ tortion（扭曲）"——通过强力从某人那里扭取东西。
