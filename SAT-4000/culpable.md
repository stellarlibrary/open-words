# ⚖️ culpable

🔊 /ˈkʌlpəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Deserving blame or censure; guilty of wrongdoing or negligence
**中文：** adj. 有罪的；该责备的；该谴责的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- blameworthy（该受责备的）
- guilty（有罪的）
- reprehensible（应受谴责的）
- liable（有责任的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **culpable negligence / culpable conduct / morally culpable / held culpable for**

👉 Example: The investigation found the manager culpable for the safety violations that led to the accident.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court determined that the corporation was **culpable** for the environmental contamination caused by its improper waste disposal.
   法院裁定该公司因不当废物处理而造成的环境污染负有责任。

2. Although he claimed ignorance, the board found the executive equally **culpable** in the financial fraud that damaged investors.
   尽管他声称不知情，董事会认为该高管在损害投资者的财务欺诈中同样有罪。

3. Historians continue to debate whether political leaders were **culpable** for failing to intervene during the humanitarian crisis.
   历史学家继续争论政治领导人是否因未能在人道主义危机中进行干预而应受谴责。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "culp-able" as "culprit + able" — someone who is able to be identified as the culprit is culpable.
把"culpable"想成"culprit（罪犯）+ able（能够）"——能够被认定为罪犯的人就是有罪的。
