# 😤 aggravation

🔊 /ˌæɡrəˈveɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The act of making something worse or more serious; a source of annoyance or irritation; the state of being aggravated
**中文：** 加剧；激怒；更恶化

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- exacerbation（加剧）
- irritation（恼怒）
- annoyance（烦恼）
- provocation（激怒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cause aggravation / source of aggravation / unnecessary aggravation / constant aggravation**

👉 Example: The endless bureaucratic delays became a constant source of aggravation for the research team.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The repeated construction noise outside the library became a significant **aggravation** for students preparing for their final examinations.
   图书馆外反复出现的施工噪音成了准备期末考试的学生们极大的困扰。

2. Medical professionals warned that continued neglect of the wound would lead to further **aggravation** of the underlying infection.
   医疗专业人员警告说，持续忽视伤口将导致潜在感染的进一步恶化。

3. The new regulations, rather than simplifying the process, introduced additional **aggravation** for small business owners across the entire region.
   新法规非但没有简化流程，反而给整个地区的小企业主带来了更多的麻烦。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "aggravation" as "aggravate" + "-tion" — it is the noun form of aggravate, the result of making things worse or being annoyed.
把"aggravation"想成"aggravate"（加重）+"-tion"——它是aggravate的名词形式，指使事情变得更糟或被激怒的结果。
