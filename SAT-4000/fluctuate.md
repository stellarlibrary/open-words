# 📈 fluctuate

🔊 /ˈflʌktʃueɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To rise and fall irregularly; to vary or change continuously; to waver or oscillate between states or values
**中文：** v. 波动；涨落；动摇；起伏不定

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- oscillate（振荡）
- vary（变化）
- waver（动摇）
- vacillate（犹豫不定）
- shift（变动）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fluctuate between / fluctuate widely / prices fluctuate / temperatures fluctuate / fluctuate dramatically**

👉 Example: Stock prices tend to fluctuate significantly during periods of economic uncertainty and market volatility.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Global oil prices continued to **fluctuate** throughout the quarter, creating considerable uncertainty for energy-dependent industries worldwide.
   全球石油价格在整个季度持续波动，给全球依赖能源的行业造成了相当大的不确定性。

2. The patient's blood pressure would **fluctuate** dramatically throughout the day, prompting physicians to adjust the medication dosage accordingly.
   患者的血压在一天中剧烈波动，促使医生相应地调整药物剂量。

3. Public opinion on the proposed legislation continued to **fluctuate** as new evidence and competing arguments emerged in the media.
   随着新证据和对立论点在媒体上出现，公众对拟议立法的看法持续摇摆不定。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fluctuate" as "flu-ctu-ate" — like ocean waves (fluctus means wave in Latin) that constantly rise and fall in an unpredictable rhythm.
把"fluctuate"想成来自拉丁语"fluctus"（波浪）——就像海浪不断起伏涨落，波动不止。
