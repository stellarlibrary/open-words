# 🤺 antagonist

🔊 /ænˈtæɡənɪst/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone or something; an adversary; in literature, the character who opposes the protagonist
**中文：** 敌手；对手；对抗者；（文学中的）反面角色

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- adversary（对手）
- opponent（反对者）
- rival（竞争对手）
- foe（敌人）
- nemesis（宿敌）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **main antagonist / chief antagonist / antagonist in the story / political antagonist / antagonist of the hero**

👉 Example: In the novel, the antagonist relentlessly pursued the protagonist across three continents in a battle of wits.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The literary critic argued that the most compelling **antagonist** is one whose motivations are understandable and morally ambiguous.
   文学评论家认为，最引人注目的反面角色是那些动机可以理解且道德上模棱两可的角色。

2. Throughout the negotiations, he served as the primary **antagonist** to the proposed environmental regulations favored by the majority.
   在整个谈判过程中，他是多数人支持的环境法规提案的主要反对者。

3. The pharmaceutical researcher studied how a receptor **antagonist** could block specific chemical signals responsible for chronic inflammation.
   药物研究人员研究了受体拮抗剂如何阻断导致慢性炎症的特定化学信号。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "antagonist" as "anti- (against) + agonist (actor)" — the antagonist is the actor who works against the main character.
把"antagonist"想成"anti-（反对）+ agonist（行动者）"——antagonist就是与主角对抗的那个人。
