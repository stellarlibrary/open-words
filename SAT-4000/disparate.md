# 🔀 disparate

🔊 /ˈdɪspərət/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Fundamentally different or distinct in kind; entirely dissimilar
**中文：** adj. 全异的；完全不同的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dissimilar（不同的）
- divergent（相异的）
- contrasting（对比鲜明的）
- heterogeneous（异质的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **disparate groups / disparate views / disparate elements / disparate outcomes**

👉 Example: The committee struggled to reconcile the disparate opinions of its diverse members.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researchers attempted to synthesize **disparate** data sources into a coherent and unified analytical framework.
   研究人员试图将全然不同的数据来源整合为一个连贯统一的分析框架。

2. Students from **disparate** cultural backgrounds brought unique perspectives that enriched the classroom discussion significantly.
   来自迥然不同文化背景的学生带来了独特视角，极大地丰富了课堂讨论。

3. The treaty sought to bridge **disparate** economic interests between the developing and industrialized nations involved.
   该条约旨在弥合参与其中的发展中国家与工业化国家之间截然不同的经济利益。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-par-ate" as "dis" (apart) + "par" (equal) — things that are not on par with each other are disparate, completely different.
把"disparate"想成"dis"（分开）+"par"（相等）——彼此不相等的事物就是全异的、完全不同的。
