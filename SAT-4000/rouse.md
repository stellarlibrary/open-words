# ⏰ rouse

🔊 /raʊz/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To wake up; to stir up or excite; to startle from cover. (n.) An awakening; a rallying
**中文：** v. 惊起；唤起，唤醒

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- awaken（唤醒）
- stir（激起）
- provoke（激发）
- galvanize（使振奋）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rouse from sleep / rouse suspicion / rouse the crowd / rouse oneself**

👉 Example: The passionate speech was enough to rouse the audience from their complacency and inspire action.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The thunderous alarm managed to **rouse** the entire household from deep slumber during the early morning emergency evacuation.
   震耳欲聋的警报声在清晨紧急疏散时成功地将全家人从沉睡中唤醒。

2. The political leader's fervent rhetoric was designed to **rouse** public indignation against the proposed austerity measures.
   这位政治领导人慷慨激昂的言辞旨在唤起公众对拟议的紧缩措施的愤怒。

3. The hunting dogs were trained to **rouse** pheasants from the dense undergrowth so the hunters could take aim.
   猎犬被训练从茂密的灌木丛中惊起野鸡，以便猎人瞄准射击。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rouse" as sounding like "arouse" — both mean to wake up or stir into action, like a rousing alarm clock.
把"rouse"想成听起来像"arouse"——两者都有唤醒或激起行动的意思，就像一个令人振奋的闹钟。
