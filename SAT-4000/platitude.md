# 🗣️ platitude

🔊 /ˈplætɪtjuːd/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A remark or statement that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful; a trite, meaningless, or unoriginal saying
**中文：** n. 陈词滥调；平凡；陈腐

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cliche（陈词滥调）
- banality（平庸）
- truism（老生常谈）
- commonplace（老生常谈）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **utter platitudes / mere platitude / empty platitudes / resort to platitudes**

👉 Example: The politician's speech was filled with platitudes but offered no concrete solutions to the crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics dismissed the commencement address as little more than a collection of **platitudes** lacking any genuine insight or original perspective.
   批评者认为毕业典礼致辞不过是一堆陈词滥调，缺乏任何真正的见解或独到的观点。

2. Rather than offering meaningful condolences, he resorted to empty **platitudes** that failed to comfort the grieving family in their difficult time.
   他没有提供有意义的慰问，而是诉诸空洞的陈词滥调，未能在困难时期安慰悲伤的家人。

3. The self-help book was criticized for recycling familiar **platitudes** instead of presenting evidence-based strategies for personal development and growth.
   这本自助书籍因循环使用熟悉的陈词滥调而受到批评，而没有提出基于证据的个人发展和成长策略。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "platitude" as "flat-itude" — a flat, dull statement with no depth or originality, as boring as a flat surface with nothing on it.
把"platitude"想成"flat-itude"——一个平淡、无聊的陈述，没有深度或原创性，就像一个空无一物的平面一样乏味。
