# 🩸 vein

🔊 /veɪn/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart; a distinctive quality or style; a streak or stripe of a different color; a thin layer of mineral in rock. (v.) To mark with or form veins
**中文：** n. 静脉；血管；叶脉；岩脉；纹理；性情 v. 使成脉络

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- vessel（血管）
- streak（条纹）
- seam（矿层）
- strain（特质）
- tone（语调）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in a similar vein / vein of humor / vein of gold / run through one's veins**

👉 Example: In a similar vein, the second study confirmed that environmental factors significantly influence gene expression.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The geologist discovered a rich **vein** of copper ore running through the sedimentary rock formation beneath the hillside.
   地质学家发现一条丰富的铜矿脉贯穿山坡下方的沉积岩层。

2. The author continued in a philosophical **vein**, exploring themes of identity and purpose throughout the remainder of the essay.
   作者以哲学的笔调继续写作，在文章的其余部分探讨了身份和目的的主题。

3. The nurse carefully located a suitable **vein** in the patient's forearm before administering the intravenous medication.
   护士在给患者静脉注射药物之前，仔细地在其前臂找到了一条合适的静脉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vein" as a channel that carries something — blood in your body, minerals in rock, or a thread of thought in writing.
把"vein"想成一条输送某物的通道——身体里的血液、岩石里的矿物质、或写作中的一条思路。
