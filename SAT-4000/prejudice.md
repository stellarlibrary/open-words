# ⚖️ prejudice

🔊 /ˈprɛdʒʊdɪs/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience; harm or injury resulting from an action. (v.) To cause someone to have a biased view; to impair or damage
**中文：** n. 偏见，成见；损害，侵害 v. 使抱偏见；影响某人；损害

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bias（偏见）
- discrimination（歧视）
- partiality（偏颇）
- preconception（先入之见）
- intolerance（不宽容）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prejudice against / without prejudice / racial prejudice / prejudice someone's chances**

👉 Example: The judge dismissed the case without prejudice, allowing the plaintiff to refile at a later date.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Deep-seated **prejudice** against minority groups continues to undermine efforts toward achieving genuine social equality in many societies.
   对少数群体根深蒂固的偏见继续破坏着许多社会实现真正社会平等的努力。

2. The defense attorney argued that extensive media coverage had **prejudiced** the jury and compromised the defendant's right to a fair trial.
   辩护律师辩称广泛的媒体报道已使陪审团产生偏见，损害了被告获得公正审判的权利。

3. Releasing the confidential report prematurely could **prejudice** the outcome of the ongoing investigation and compromise witness safety.
   过早发布机密报告可能会损害正在进行的调查结果，并危及证人安全。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-judice" as "pre-judge" — to judge someone before knowing the facts, which is exactly what prejudice means.
把"prejudice"想成"pre（之前）+ judice（判断）"——在了解事实之前就做出判断，这正是偏见的含义。
