# 🙏 mendicant

🔊 /ˈmɛndɪkənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Given to begging; relating to or characteristic of a beggar or religious order that relies on alms. (n.) A beggar; a member of a religious order who lives by charitable donations.
**中文：** adj. 行乞的；托钵修道会的 n. 乞丐；托钵僧

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- beggar（乞丐）
- panhandler（街头乞讨者）
- pauper（穷人）
- supplicant（恳求者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mendicant friar / mendicant order / mendicant monks / mendicant existence**

👉 Example: The mendicant friars traveled from village to village, relying entirely on the charity of local communities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. During the Middle Ages, **mendicant** orders such as the Franciscans played a vital role in providing education to the poor.
   中世纪时期，方济各会等托钵修会在为穷人提供教育方面发挥了重要作用。

2. The anthropologist studied the **mendicant** communities living on the outskirts of the ancient city's commercial districts.
   这位人类学家研究了生活在古城商业区外围的乞讨群体。

3. After losing his fortune, the once-wealthy merchant was reduced to a **mendicant** existence on the streets of the capital.
   在失去财产后，这位曾经富有的商人沦落到在首都街头以乞讨为生的境地。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mendi-cant" as "mend I can't" — a mendicant is someone so poor they can't even mend their own clothes, so they must beg for help.
把"mendicant"想成"mend I can't（我无法修补）"——mendicant是一个穷到连衣服都补不起的人，只能靠乞讨求助。
