# 💥 scathe

🔊 /skeɪð/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To harm or injure; to damage severely. (n.) Harm, injury, or damage
**中文：** v. 损伤，损害，伤害

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- damage（损害）
- harm（伤害）
- injure（损伤）
- devastate（毁坏）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **scathing criticism / scathing remark / scathing review / without scathe**

👉 Example: The film critic delivered a scathing review that questioned every aspect of the director's creative choices.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator's reputation was severely **scathed** by the investigative report that exposed years of undisclosed financial conflicts of interest.
   参议员的声誉因揭露其多年未公开的经济利益冲突的调查报告而受到严重损害。

2. The editorial board published a **scathing** assessment of the administration's failure to address the mounting environmental crisis effectively.
   编辑委员会发表了对政府未能有效应对日益加剧的环境危机的尖锐评估。

3. Although the hurricane threatened to **scathe** the entire coastal region, timely evacuations prevented any significant loss of human life.
   尽管飓风威胁要重创整个沿海地区，但及时的疏散防止了任何重大的人员伤亡。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "scathe" as "s + cathe" — like a catheter that pierces through, scathe means to pierce someone with harm or cutting words.
把"scathe"想成"s + cathe"——就像导管刺穿一样，scathe意味着用伤害或尖刻的话语刺痛某人。
