# ⚡ abrupt

🔊 /əˈbrʌpt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Sudden and unexpected; curt or brusque in manner; extremely steep
**中文：** adj. 突然的，意外的；唐突的，鲁莽的；陡峭的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sudden（突然的）
- brusque（唐突的）
- curt（简慢的）
- steep（陡峭的）
- unexpected（意外的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **abrupt change / abrupt departure / abrupt manner / abrupt end / abrupt halt**

👉 Example: The CEO's abrupt resignation shocked both investors and employees alike.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **abrupt** cancellation of the summit left diplomats scrambling to salvage months of careful negotiations.
   峰会的突然取消让外交官们手忙脚乱地试图挽救数月的精心谈判。

2. Her **abrupt** tone during the interview suggested she had little patience for repetitive questions.
   她在采访中唐突的语气表明她对重复的问题毫无耐心。

3. The trail made an **abrupt** descent along the cliff face, challenging even the most experienced hikers.
   小径沿着悬崖面陡然下降，即使最有经验的徒步者也觉得很有挑战。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ab-rupt" as "ab-ruptly broken off" — from Latin "abruptus" meaning broken off. Something abrupt feels like it was suddenly broken or cut short.
把"ab-rupt"想成"突然断裂"——来自拉丁语"abruptus"，意为折断。Abrupt的事物感觉像是突然被打断或截断。
