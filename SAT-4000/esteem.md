# 🌟 esteem

🔊 /ɪˈstiːm/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Respect and admiration for someone. (v.) To regard highly; to consider or deem
**中文：** n. 尊敬，敬重 v. 尊敬，敬重；认为；考虑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- respect（尊重）
- admiration（钦佩）
- regard（敬意）
- reverence（崇敬）
- veneration（尊崇）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **hold in high esteem / self-esteem / esteem someone as / highly esteemed**

👉 Example: The professor is held in high esteem by students and colleagues throughout the academic community.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The retiring judge was held in the highest **esteem** by members of the legal profession for her unwavering integrity.
   这位即将退休的法官因其坚定不移的正直品格而受到法律界同仁的最高敬重。

2. Many historians **esteem** the discovery of penicillin as one of the most significant medical breakthroughs of the century.
   许多历史学家认为青霉素的发现是本世纪最重要的医学突破之一。

3. Building healthy self-**esteem** in adolescents requires consistent encouragement and constructive feedback from parents and educators alike.
   培养青少年健康的自尊需要来自家长和教育者的持续鼓励和建设性反馈。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "esteem" as "e-STEAM" — like steam rising upward, esteem is the feeling of looking up to someone with respect.
把"esteem"想成"e-STEAM"——就像蒸汽向上升腾，esteem是仰望某人、对其表示敬意的感觉。
