# 🔥 seditious

🔊 /sɪˈdɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Inflammatory; tending to disturb public order; inciting rebellion
**中文：** adj. 煽动的；煽动叛变的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- subversive（颠覆性的）
- incendiary（煽动性的）
- rebellious（叛逆的）
- inflammatory（煽动的）
- treasonous（叛国的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **seditious speech / seditious material / seditious activities / seditious intent**

👉 Example: The authorities confiscated pamphlets deemed seditious because they encouraged citizens to revolt against the state.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court determined that the pamphlets contained **seditious** material designed to undermine public confidence in the government.
   法院认定这些小册子包含煽动性材料，旨在破坏公众对政府的信心。

2. Distributing **seditious** literature was punishable by imprisonment under the colonial administration's repressive legal framework.
   在殖民政府的高压法律框架下，散发煽动性文献可被判处监禁。

3. The professor argued that labeling peaceful criticism as **seditious** effectively silences democratic discourse and stifles civil liberties.
   这位教授认为，将和平批评贴上煽动性标签实际上压制了民主话语并扼杀了公民自由。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sedit-ious" — it comes from "sedition" plus "-ous" (full of). Someone seditious is full of sedition, always stirring up trouble.
把"seditious"想成"sedition（煽动）"加上"-ous（充满的）"——煽动性的人充满了煽动意图，总是在挑起事端。
