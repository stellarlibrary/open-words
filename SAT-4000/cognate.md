# 🌿 cognate

🔊 /ˈkɒɡneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having the same origin or related in nature; descended from the same language or root. (n.) A word or language that shares a common ancestor with another
**中文：** adj. 同源的；同类的 n. 同族；同根词

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- related（相关的）
- allied（同类的）
- kindred（同源的）
- analogous（类似的）
- akin（同族的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cognate with / cognate languages / cognate words / cognate forms**

👉 Example: The English word "mother" is cognate with the German word "Mutter," both derived from Proto-Indo-European.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Linguists have demonstrated that the Spanish word "noche" is **cognate** with the French word "nuit," both deriving from Latin.
   语言学家已经证明西班牙语单词"noche"与法语单词"nuit"同源，两者均来自拉丁语。

2. The researcher identified several **cognate** disciplines that could contribute valuable perspectives to the interdisciplinary study.
   研究人员确定了几个同类学科，它们可以为跨学科研究提供有价值的视角。

3. Many English and Dutch words are **cognate**, reflecting the shared Germanic heritage of both languages over centuries.
   许多英语和荷兰语单词是同源的，反映了两种语言数百年来共同的日耳曼语系传承。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cognate" as "co-g-nate" — "co" means together and "nate" sounds like "natal" (birth), so cognate words are "born together" from the same source.
把"cognate"想成"co（共同）-nate（出生）"——同源词就是从同一个来源"一起诞生"的词。
