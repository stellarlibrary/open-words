# 🔧 reform

🔊 /rɪˈfɔːrm/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make changes in order to improve; to correct or amend what is defective or corrupt. (n.) The improvement or amendment of what is wrong or unsatisfactory
**中文：** v. 改进；改革 n. 改进；改革

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- improve（改进）
- amend（修正）
- overhaul（彻底改革）
- rectify（纠正）
- transformation（变革）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reform the system / political reform / reform measures / reform movement / sweeping reforms**

👉 Example: The newly elected government pledged to reform the outdated healthcare system within its first term.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The legislature passed a series of sweeping **reforms** designed to increase transparency and reduce corruption within government agencies.
   立法机构通过了一系列旨在提高政府机构透明度和减少腐败的全面改革。

2. Educators argued that the country must **reform** its standardized testing practices to better reflect genuine student learning outcomes.
   教育工作者认为，国家必须改革其标准化考试做法，以更好地反映真实的学生学习成果。

3. The economic **reform** program successfully reduced inflation rates and attracted significant levels of foreign direct investment to the region.
   经济改革计划成功降低了通货膨胀率，并吸引了大量外国直接投资进入该地区。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-form" as forming something again — reshaping a broken system into a better version of itself.
把"reform"想成"re-form"（重新塑造）——将一个有问题的体系重新塑造成更好的版本。
