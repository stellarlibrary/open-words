# 🧩 conundrum

🔊 /kəˈnʌndrəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A confusing and difficult problem or question; a riddle whose answer involves a pun or unexpected twist
**中文：** n. 难题；谜语

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- puzzle（谜题）
- riddle（谜语）
- enigma（难以理解的事）
- dilemma（困境）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **face a conundrum / pose a conundrum / moral conundrum / ethical conundrum**

👉 Example: The committee faced a serious conundrum when both candidates proved equally qualified for the position.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ethical **conundrum** of balancing individual privacy rights with national security has challenged legislators for decades.
   在个人隐私权与国家安全之间取得平衡这一伦理难题已经困扰立法者数十年之久。

2. Scientists confronted a perplexing **conundrum** when experimental results consistently defied the predictions of their theoretical model.
   当实验结果持续违背理论模型的预测时，科学家们面临了一个令人困惑的难题。

3. The philosopher presented a fascinating **conundrum** that forced the audience to question their fundamental assumptions about free will.
   这位哲学家提出了一个引人入胜的谜题，迫使听众质疑他们关于自由意志的基本假设。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con-un-drum" as "can no one drum up an answer?" — a problem so hard that nobody can come up with a solution.
把"conundrum"想成"can no one drum up an answer?"——一个如此困难的问题，没人能想出答案。
