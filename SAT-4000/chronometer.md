# ⏱ chronometer

🔊 /krəˈnɑːmɪtər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A highly precise timepiece, especially one used for navigation at sea or scientific measurement
**中文：** n. 精密计时表；航海经线仪

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- timepiece（计时器）
- horologe（时钟）
- clock（钟表）
- watch（手表）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **marine chronometer / precision chronometer / calibrate a chronometer**

👉 Example: The captain relied on a marine chronometer to determine the ship's longitude during the voyage.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The maritime museum displayed a rare eighteenth-century **chronometer** that had guided explorers across uncharted oceans.
   海事博物馆展出了一枚罕见的十八世纪精密计时表，它曾引导探险家穿越未知的海洋。

2. Scientists employed an atomic **chronometer** to measure time intervals with extraordinary precision during the experiment.
   科学家们在实验中使用原子精密计时器以极高的精度测量时间间隔。

3. Without a reliable **chronometer**, early sailors found it nearly impossible to calculate their exact position at sea.
   没有可靠的精密计时表，早期的水手几乎不可能计算出他们在海上的确切位置。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "chrono" (time, as in "chronicle") + "meter" (measure) — a chronometer is a device that measures time with great accuracy.
把"chronometer"拆成"chrono"（时间，如"编年史"）+"meter"（测量）——精密测量时间的仪器。
