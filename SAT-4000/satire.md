# 🎭 satire

🔊 /ˈsætaɪər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The use of humor, irony, or exaggeration to criticize or expose people's vices, foolishness, or societal issues; a literary work employing such techniques
**中文：** n. 讽刺文学，讽刺

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- parody（模仿讽刺）
- lampoon（讽刺文章）
- caricature（讽刺画）
- burlesque（滑稽模仿）
- mockery（嘲弄）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **political satire / biting satire / social satire / satire on / satire of**

👉 Example: The television show is a brilliant satire on modern consumer culture and corporate greed.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The novel is a sharp **satire** of bureaucratic inefficiency, highlighting the absurdity of governmental procedures through dark humor.
   这部小说是对官僚低效的尖锐讽刺，通过黑色幽默突出了政府程序的荒谬性。

2. Political **satire** has long served as a powerful vehicle for challenging authority and holding public officials accountable.
   政治讽刺长期以来一直是挑战权威和追究公职人员责任的有力工具。

3. The playwright's **satire** was so subtle that many audience members failed to recognize the criticism embedded within the comedy.
   这位剧作家的讽刺非常微妙，以至于许多观众未能认识到喜剧中蕴含的批评。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "satire" as "sat-ire" — when you sit (sat) in ire (anger), you write satire to channel your frustration into clever, humorous criticism.
把"satire"想成"sat（坐）+ ire（愤怒）"——当你愤怒地坐着时，你会写讽刺作品来将挫败感转化为巧妙的幽默批评。
