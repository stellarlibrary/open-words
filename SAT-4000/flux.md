# 🌊 flux

🔊 /flʌks/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A continuous flow or movement; a state of constant change or instability; the rate of flow of a fluid or energy
**中文：** n. 流；流出；流动；涨潮；变迁；不稳定

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- change（变化）
- instability（不稳定）
- fluctuation（波动）
- transition（过渡）
- movement（流动）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in a state of flux / constant flux / magnetic flux / flux of change**

👉 Example: The company's organizational structure has been in a state of flux since the merger was announced.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The housing market remains in a state of **flux**, with prices rising in some regions while declining sharply in others.
   房地产市场仍处于**变动**之中，部分地区价格上涨，而其他地区则大幅下跌。

2. During periods of political **flux**, citizens often turn to established institutions for a sense of stability and continuity.
   在政治**动荡**时期，公民往往求助于既有制度以获得稳定感和连续性。

3. The physicist calculated the magnetic **flux** through the coil to determine the induced voltage in the experimental circuit.
   物理学家计算了通过线圈的磁**通量**，以确定实验电路中的感应电压。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "flux" as related to "flow" — everything is in flux when it keeps flowing and changing, never settling into a fixed state.
把"flux"和"flow（流动）"联系起来——一切都在变动中，不断流动和变化，永远不会固定下来。
