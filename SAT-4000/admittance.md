# 🚪 admittance

🔊 /ədˈmɪtəns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The right or permission to enter a place; the act of being allowed entry or access.
**中文：** 进入；入场权；通道

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- entry（进入）
- access（通道）
- entrance（入口）
- admission（准入）
- ingress（进入权）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **gain admittance**, **deny/refuse admittance**, **no admittance**

👉 Example: The sign at the gate read "No admittance without proper identification."

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Security personnel denied **admittance** to anyone who could not present a valid invitation.
   安保人员拒绝任何无法出示有效邀请函的人入场。

2. The researchers gained **admittance** to the restricted archives only after obtaining special clearance.
   研究人员在获得特别许可后才获准进入限制性档案室。

3. The theater's strict policy on **admittance** prevented latecomers from entering during the first act.
   剧院严格的入场政策禁止迟到者在第一幕期间进入。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "admit" + "-tance" — the state of being admitted, like a door that either grants or blocks your way in.
联想"admit"（允许进入）+"-tance"（状态后缀）——被允许进入的状态，就像一扇门决定你能否通过。
