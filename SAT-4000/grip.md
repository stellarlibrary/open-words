# ✊ grip

🔊 /ɡrɪp/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A firm hold or grasp; control or power over something. (v.) To take and hold firmly; to seize tightly
**中文：** n. 紧握；柄；控制力 v. 紧握；夹紧；吸引住

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- grasp（抓紧）
- clutch（紧握）
- hold（握住）
- clasp（扣紧）
- seize（抓住）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **grip on / lose one's grip / come to grips with / in the grip of / grip tightly**

👉 Example: The thriller gripped audiences worldwide with its suspenseful plot and unexpected twists.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The nation remained in the **grip** of an economic recession that showed no signs of easing in the foreseeable future.
   这个国家仍然处于经济衰退的控制之中，在可预见的未来没有缓解的迹象。

2. She **gripped** the podium tightly as she delivered her impassioned speech to the assembled delegates at the conference.
   她在会议上向与会代表发表慷慨激昂的演讲时紧紧握住了讲台。

3. Students must come to **grips** with fundamental mathematical concepts before attempting to solve advanced theoretical problems.
   学生必须先掌握基本的数学概念，然后才能尝试解决高级理论问题。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "grip" as the sound your hand makes when you squeeze something tightly — a short, sharp word for a tight hold.
把"grip"想成你紧紧抓住某物时手发出的声音——一个简短有力的词，代表紧握。
