# 📝 falsify

🔊 /ˈfɔːlsɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To alter or forge information so as to mislead; to tamper with or misrepresent; to prove something to be false
**中文：** v. 伪造；篡改；歪曲；证明……是虚假的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- forge（伪造）
- fabricate（捏造）
- distort（歪曲）
- tamper with（篡改）
- misrepresent（歪曲）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **falsify records / falsify data / falsify documents / falsify evidence**

👉 Example: The accountant was convicted of attempting to falsify the company's financial records.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher was dismissed from the university after colleagues discovered she had **falsified** critical experimental data in her publications.
   这位研究员在同事发现她在论文中伪造了关键实验数据后被大学解雇。

2. Any attempt to **falsify** official government documents constitutes a serious criminal offense punishable by substantial imprisonment.
   任何伪造官方政府文件的企图都构成严重的刑事犯罪，可处以大量监禁。

3. New archaeological findings effectively **falsified** the long-held theory regarding the origins of early human settlement in the region.
   新的考古发现有效地推翻了关于该地区早期人类定居起源的长期理论。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "false + ify" — to make something false, to turn truth into falsehood through deliberate alteration.
把"falsify"想成"false（虚假的）+ ify（使……）"——使某物变成假的，通过故意篡改把真相变成谎言。
