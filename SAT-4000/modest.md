# 🙇 modest

🔊 /ˈmɒdɪst/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Unassuming in one's estimation of abilities; moderate in size, amount, or extent; decorous in manner and conduct
**中文：** adj. 谦虚的；谦逊的；适度的；端庄的；羞怯的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- humble（谦逊的）
- unassuming（不张扬的）
- moderate（适度的）
- reserved（矜持的）
- unpretentious（朴实的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **modest proposal / modest income / modest improvement / modest about / remain modest**

👉 Example: Despite her remarkable achievements, the scientist remained genuinely modest about her contributions to the field.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher made a **modest** proposal to improve the existing methodology rather than overhaul the entire experimental framework.
   研究人员提出了一个适度的建议来改进现有方法，而非彻底改革整个实验框架。

2. Despite winning numerous prestigious awards, the celebrated author remained remarkably **modest** about her literary accomplishments.
   尽管赢得了众多声望卓著的奖项，这位著名作家对自己的文学成就仍然非常谦虚。

3. The company reported a **modest** increase in quarterly revenue, suggesting that the new marketing strategy was beginning to work.
   公司报告季度收入出现了适度增长，表明新的营销策略开始发挥作用。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "modest" as "mode-est" — someone who stays in the standard mode, the most ordinary setting, never boasting or showing off.
把"modest"想成"mode-est"——一个保持在标准模式、最普通设定的人，从不吹嘘或炫耀。
