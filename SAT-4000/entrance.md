# 🚪 entrance

🔊 /ˈentrəns/ (n.) /ɪnˈtræns/ (v.)<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A door, gate, or opening used for entering a place; the act of entering. (v.) To fill with wonder and delight; to cast a spell over
**中文：** n. 入口；进入 v. 使出神；使着迷

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- entry（入口）
- gateway（大门）
- doorway（门口）
- enchant（使着迷）
- captivate（迷住）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **entrance to / main entrance / entrance fee / entrance exam / entranced by**

👉 Example: The grand entrance to the cathedral featured intricate stone carvings dating back to the medieval period.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Security personnel were stationed at every **entrance** to the diplomatic summit to verify credentials of all attending delegates.
   安保人员驻守在外交峰会的每个入口处，核实所有与会代表的身份证件。

2. The magician's breathtaking illusions **entranced** the entire audience, leaving them in a state of speechless astonishment throughout the show.
   魔术师令人叹为观止的幻术令全场观众着迷，使他们在整场演出中惊叹不已。

3. Her dramatic **entrance** onto the political stage captivated the nation and reshaped the trajectory of the upcoming election.
   她在政治舞台上的戏剧性登场吸引了全国的目光，并重塑了即将到来的选举走向。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "en-trance" — as a noun, it is where you "enter"; as a verb, it puts you in a "trance" of wonder.
把"entrance"想成"enter（进入）"的名词形式是入口；作动词时像让人进入"trance（恍惚状态）"，即使人出神。
