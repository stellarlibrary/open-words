# ⚖️ justify

🔊 /ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To demonstrate or prove to be right, just, or reasonable; to provide adequate grounds for an action or decision
**中文：** v. 证明…是正当的；为…辩护；证明合法

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- vindicate（证明正当）
- defend（辩护）
- validate（证实）
- warrant（保证）
- rationalize（合理化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **justify a decision / justify the cost / justify oneself / hard to justify**

👉 Example: The manager had to justify the budget increase to the board of directors.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator attempted to **justify** her controversial vote by citing the potential long-term economic benefits of the legislation.
   这位参议员试图通过引用该立法的潜在长期经济利益来为她有争议的投票辩护。

2. No amount of statistical manipulation can **justify** the deliberate exclusion of contradictory evidence from the research findings.
   任何程度的统计操纵都无法为在研究结论中故意排除矛盾证据提供正当理由。

3. The architect struggled to **justify** the enormous expense of the renovation when a simpler design would have sufficed.
   当一个更简单的设计就足够时，建筑师很难为翻修的巨额费用提供正当理由。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "justify" as making something "just" — when you justify an action, you show that it is just and fair.
把"justify"想成使某事变得"just"（正当的）——当你justify一个行为时，你在证明它是正当和公平的。
