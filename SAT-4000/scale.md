# ⚖️ scale

🔊 /skeɪl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The relative size or extent of something; a ratio of distances; thin overlapping plates on fish or reptiles; a graduated range of values; a weighing instrument. (v.) To measure or compare; to climb up; to peel off in flakes; to form mineral deposits
**中文：** n. 鳞片

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- proportion（比例）
- magnitude（规模）
- extent（范围）
- scope（范围）
- gradient（梯度）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **on a large scale / scale up / scale down / tip the scales / economies of scale**

👉 Example: The company plans to scale up production to meet growing international demand.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researchers conducted the experiment on a massive **scale**, involving thousands of participants across twelve different countries.
   研究人员在大规模范围内开展了这项实验，涉及十二个不同国家的数千名参与者。

2. Urban planners must carefully **scale** infrastructure projects to accommodate projected population growth over the next decade.
   城市规划者必须仔细衡量基础设施项目的规模，以适应未来十年预计的人口增长。

3. The architect used a precise **scale** of one to five hundred to render the blueprint of the proposed civic center.
   建筑师使用一比五百的精确比例尺来绘制拟建市民中心的蓝图。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "scale" as a fish's armor — each tiny scale overlaps the next, just like numbers on a graduated scale overlap in sequence from small to large.
把"scale"想成鱼的铠甲——每片小鳞片依次叠放，就像刻度尺上的数字从小到大依次排列。
