# 🌍 mundane

🔊 /mʌnˈdeɪn/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Lacking interest or excitement; dull and ordinary; relating to the earthly world rather than the spiritual realm
**中文：** adj. 世俗的，平凡的；世界的，宇宙的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ordinary（普通的）
- prosaic（平淡的）
- banal（平庸的）
- commonplace（平凡的）
- tedious（乏味的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mundane tasks / mundane affairs / mundane existence / mundane reality**

👉 Example: The philosopher urged his students to look beyond mundane concerns and contemplate higher truths.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. After years of extraordinary adventures abroad, she found it difficult to adjust to the **mundane** routines of suburban life.
   在国外经历了多年非凡的冒险之后，她发现自己很难适应郊区生活的平凡日常。

2. The professor argued that great literature often transforms **mundane** experiences into profound reflections on the human condition.
   教授认为，伟大的文学作品常常将平凡的经历转化为对人类处境的深刻反思。

3. Many employees reported that the most **mundane** aspects of their work were gradually being automated by artificial intelligence.
   许多员工表示，他们工作中最平淡乏味的部分正逐渐被人工智能所取代。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mundane" as related to "mundo" (world in Spanish) — things of this world are ordinary and earthly, not heavenly or exciting.
把"mundane"联想到西班牙语"mundo（世界）"——属于这个世界的事物是普通的、世俗的，而非神圣或令人激动的。
