# 🔓 disclose

🔊 /dɪsˈkloʊz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make known or reveal something previously secret or unknown; to expose to view
**中文：** v. 公开；揭露

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reveal（揭示）
- divulge（泄露）
- expose（暴露）
- unveil（公开）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **disclose information / disclose details / disclose the truth / refuse to disclose / publicly disclose**

👉 Example: Federal regulations require companies to disclose all potential risks to investors before issuing new securities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist refused to **disclose** the identity of her confidential source despite intense pressure from law enforcement authorities.
   尽管受到执法部门的巨大压力，这位记者仍拒绝透露其机密消息来源的身份。

2. Companies listed on the stock exchange are legally obligated to **disclose** their financial statements to the public each quarter.
   上市公司在法律上有义务每季度向公众公开其财务报表。

3. The investigation **disclosed** a network of corruption that extended far beyond what officials had initially suspected or anticipated.
   调查揭露了一个腐败网络，其范围远远超出了官员们最初的怀疑和预料。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-close" as the opposite of "close" — to disclose is to open up what was closed and hidden, bringing secrets into the light.
把"disclose"拆成"dis-close"——与"关闭"相反，就是打开原本隐藏的东西，让秘密曝光。
