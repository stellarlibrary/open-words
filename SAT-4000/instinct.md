# 🧬 instinct

🔊 /ˈɪnstɪŋkt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An innate, typically fixed pattern of behavior; a natural or intuitive way of acting or thinking. (adj.) Filled or charged with a quality
**中文：** n. 本能；直觉；天性 adj. 充满着的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- intuition（直觉）
- impulse（冲动）
- inclination（倾向）
- tendency（天性）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **by instinct / survival instinct / natural instinct / trust your instincts / gut instinct**

👉 Example: The experienced diplomat relied on her political instincts to navigate the delicate negotiations successfully.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher argued that maternal **instinct** in primates is shaped by both genetic predisposition and early social environment.
   研究人员认为，灵长类动物的母性本能是由遗传倾向和早期社会环境共同塑造的。

2. His **instinct** for recognizing profitable investments made him one of the most successful venture capitalists of his generation.
   他识别盈利投资的直觉使他成为同代最成功的风险投资家之一。

3. Survival **instincts** compelled the stranded hikers to seek shelter immediately when the violent storm approached the mountain ridge.
   求生本能促使被困的徒步者在猛烈风暴逼近山脊时立即寻找避难所。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "instinct" as "in-stinct" — something "stinked in" to you from birth, a deeply embedded natural response you cannot remove.
把"instinct"想成"in-stinct"——从出生起就深深烙印在你身上的东西，一种无法去除的深层自然反应。
