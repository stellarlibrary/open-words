# 🖊️ underscore

🔊 /ˌʌndərˈskɔːr/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To emphasize or stress the importance of something; to draw a line beneath a word or phrase. (n.) The character "_" used in writing or computing
**中文：** v. 强调；划线于…下 n. 下划线

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- emphasize（强调）
- highlight（突出）
- accentuate（着重）
- stress（强调）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **underscore the importance / underscore the need / underscore the fact / underscore a point**

👉 Example: The recent data underscores the urgent need for comprehensive reform in the education sector.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The chairman's resignation served to **underscore** the severity of the financial crisis facing the corporation.
   董事长的辞职进一步凸显了公司所面临的金融危机的严重性。

2. Several independent studies **underscore** the critical link between adequate sleep and long-term cognitive performance.
   多项独立研究强调了充足睡眠与长期认知能力之间的关键联系。

3. The professor used a red pen to **underscore** the most significant passages in the manuscript before returning it.
   教授在归还手稿之前用红笔在最重要的段落下面划了线。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "under-score" as drawing a score (line) under something important — when you underline something, you emphasize it.
把"underscore"想成在重要内容"下面"画一条"线"——当你在某物下面画线时，就是在强调它。
