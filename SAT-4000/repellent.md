# :no_entry_sign: repellent

:speaker: /rɪˈpɛlənt/<br>
:label: *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## :books: Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Causing disgust or distaste; able to repel or resist a particular substance. (n.) A substance used to keep away insects or to resist water
**中文：** adj. 令人厌恶的；排斥的；防水的 n. 防护剂；驱虫剂

---

## :link: Synonyms | 近义词
- repulsive（令人反感的）
- revolting（令人厌恶的）
- distasteful（讨厌的）
- deterrent（威慑物）

---

## :pushpin: Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **insect repellent / water-repellent / morally repellent / find something repellent**

:point_right: Example: Hikers should always carry insect repellent when venturing into densely wooded wilderness areas.

---

## :memo: Examples | 例句

1. The committee found the proposal morally **repellent** because it disregarded the fundamental rights of vulnerable communities.
   委员会认为该提案在道德上令人厌恶，因为它无视了弱势群体的基本权利。

2. Scientists developed a new water-**repellent** material that could revolutionize the manufacturing of outdoor protective clothing.
   科学家们开发了一种新型防水材料，可能彻底改变户外防护服装的制造业。

3. The dictator's **repellent** policies of mass surveillance and censorship drew widespread international condemnation and sanctions.
   独裁者令人厌恶的大规模监控和审查政策引发了国际社会的广泛谴责和制裁。

---

## :jigsaw: Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "repellent" as "repel" + "ent" — something that actively repels, whether it pushes away bugs or pushes away people with disgust.
把"repellent"想成"repel（排斥）"+"ent（的）"——某种主动排斥的东西，无论是驱赶虫子还是让人反感。
