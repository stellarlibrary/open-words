# 🌸 euphemism

🔊 /ˈjuːfəmɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A mild or indirect word or expression used in place of one considered too harsh, blunt, or offensive
**中文：** n. 委婉语；委婉的说法

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- understatement（轻描淡写）
- polite term（礼貌用语）
- substitution（替代语）
- indirect expression（间接表达）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a euphemism for / use a euphemism / resort to euphemism**

👉 Example: The phrase "passed away" is a common euphemism for "died" in everyday English conversation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Politicians frequently employ **euphemism** to soften the impact of unpopular decisions on an increasingly skeptical electorate.
   政客们经常使用委婉语来减轻不受欢迎的决定对日益持怀疑态度的选民的影响。

2. The term "downsizing" is a corporate **euphemism** that obscures the reality of widespread employee layoffs and terminations.
   "缩减规模"是一种企业委婉语，掩盖了大规模裁员和解雇的现实。

3. Literary scholars argue that excessive **euphemism** in historical texts can distort our understanding of past social injustices.
   文学学者认为，历史文本中过多的委婉语会扭曲我们对过去社会不公的理解。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "eu-phem-ism" as "eu" (good) + "phem" (speak) — speaking in a good or pleasant way to avoid offense.
把"euphemism"想成"eu"（好的）+ "phem"（说）——用好听的方式说话以避免冒犯。
