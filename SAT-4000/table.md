# 🗂️ table

🔊 /ˈteɪbəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb, adjective | 名词, 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A piece of furniture with a flat top; an arrangement of data in rows and columns. (v.) To present formally for discussion; to postpone consideration of. (adj.) Of or relating to a table
**中文：** v. 搁置；把…列入议事日程 n. 桌子，台子；一桌人；表格

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- postpone（推迟）
- shelve（搁置）
- defer（延缓）
- chart（图表）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **table a motion / table a discussion / table of contents / set the table**

👉 Example: The committee voted to table the proposal until more evidence could be gathered and reviewed.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The chairperson decided to **table** the controversial amendment until the next legislative session for further deliberation.
   主席决定将这项有争议的修正案搁置到下一届立法会议进行进一步审议。

2. The data presented in the **table** clearly demonstrated a significant correlation between education levels and income.
   表格中呈现的数据清楚地表明了教育水平与收入之间存在显著相关性。

3. Several members argued that the board should **table** the budget discussion rather than rush into an uninformed decision.
   几位成员认为，董事会应搁置预算讨论，而不是仓促做出不明智的决定。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "table" as putting something on the table — in American English, to table means to set aside for later, like leaving papers on a table.
把"table"想成把东西放在桌上——在美式英语中，table意味着搁置以后再处理，就像把文件留在桌上一样。
