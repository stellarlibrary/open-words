# 😒 invidious

🔊 /ɪnˈvɪdiəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Likely to arouse resentment or anger in others; unfairly discriminating or showing prejudice; unpleasant and likely to cause ill will
**中文：** adj. 易招嫉妒的；引起反感的；不公平的；诽谤的；有害的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- odious（令人憎恶的）
- objectionable（令人反感的）
- discriminatory（歧视性的）
- resentful（令人不满的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **invidious comparison / invidious distinction / invidious position / invidious task / invidious discrimination**

👉 Example: Drawing invidious comparisons between siblings can damage their self-esteem and their relationship.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The manager was placed in the **invidious** position of having to choose which loyal employees would be laid off during restructuring.
   经理被置于一个令人反感的境地，不得不选择在重组期间解雇哪些忠诚的员工。

2. Scholars have criticized the **invidious** distinctions drawn between students based solely on standardized test scores and socioeconomic background.
   学者们批评了仅根据标准化考试成绩和社会经济背景对学生做出的不公平区分。

3. The report exposed **invidious** hiring practices that systematically disadvantaged qualified applicants from underrepresented minority communities.
   该报告揭露了系统性地使来自少数族裔社区的合格申请人处于不利地位的歧视性招聘做法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "invidious" from Latin "invidia" meaning "envy" — invidious actions provoke envy, resentment, and ill will in others.
把"invidious"与拉丁语"invidia（嫉妒）"联系起来——invidious的行为会在他人心中引发嫉妒、怨恨和敌意。
