# 🏗️ premise

🔊 /ˈprɛmɪs/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A proposition from which a conclusion is drawn; (pl.) a building and its grounds. (v.) To state as a premise; to set forth beforehand
**中文：** n. 前提；假设；房屋和地基；经营场所 v. 引出；预先提出；作为…的前提

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- assumption（假设）
- proposition（命题）
- hypothesis（假说）
- presupposition（预设）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **basic premise / on the premises / false premise / premise an argument on**

👉 Example: The entire theory is premised on the assumption that markets behave rationally under normal conditions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher challenged the fundamental **premise** of the study, arguing that its underlying assumptions were deeply flawed.
   研究人员质疑了该研究的基本前提，认为其基础假设存在严重缺陷。

2. Unauthorized personnel are strictly prohibited from entering the **premises** without prior written approval from the security director.
   未经安全主管事先书面批准，未授权人员严禁进入该场所。

3. Her argument was **premised** on decades of empirical research conducted across multiple disciplines and cultural contexts.
   她的论点以数十年跨多个学科和文化背景的实证研究为前提。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "premise" as "pre-mise" (pre = before, mise = put) — something put forward before the conclusion.
把"premise"想成"pre（之前）+ mise（放置）"——在结论之前放置的东西，即前提。
