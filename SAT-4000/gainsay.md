# ⚔️ gainsay

🔊 /ˌɡeɪnˈseɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To deny, contradict, or speak against; to declare to be untrue or invalid
**中文：** v. 反驳；否定；否认；反对

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- contradict（反驳）
- deny（否认）
- dispute（争论）
- refute（驳斥）
- challenge（质疑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cannot gainsay / dare not gainsay / gainsay the evidence / gainsay the truth**

👉 Example: No one could gainsay the effectiveness of the new treatment after reviewing the clinical data.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Even the most vocal critics could not **gainsay** the overwhelming evidence presented by the research committee.
   即使是最直言不讳的批评者也无法否认研究委员会提出的压倒性证据。

2. It would be foolish to **gainsay** the contributions that early pioneers made to the development of modern science.
   否认早期先驱者对现代科学发展所做的贡献将是愚蠢的。

3. The senator attempted to **gainsay** the findings of the independent audit, but the data was thoroughly documented.
   这位参议员试图反驳独立审计的结论，但数据记录非常详尽。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gainsay" as "gain-say" — to say something against what others have gained or established; to go against what has been said.
把"gainsay"想成"gain-say"——对别人已经获得或确立的东西说反话；反对已经说过的话。
