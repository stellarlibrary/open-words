# 🔥 inflammable

🔊 /ɪnˈflæməbəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Easily set on fire; easily excited or angered. (n.) A substance that is easily ignited
**中文：** adj. 易燃的；易怒的；易激动的 n. 易燃物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flammable（易燃的）
- combustible（可燃的）
- ignitable（可点燃的）
- volatile（易挥发的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inflammable materials / highly inflammable / inflammable substances / inflammable liquids**

👉 Example: Workers must store inflammable chemicals in specially designed containers to prevent accidental ignition.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The safety inspector warned that storing **inflammable** materials near the furnace created an extremely hazardous condition for all workers.
   安全检查员警告说，在熔炉附近存放易燃材料会对所有工人造成极其危险的状况。

2. Despite the prefix "in-," the word **inflammable** means the same as flammable, a fact that often confuses non-native speakers.
   尽管有前缀"in-"，但"inflammable"与"flammable"意思相同，这一事实常常令非母语者困惑。

3. The laboratory protocol requires that all **inflammable** solvents be kept in fireproof cabinets with proper ventilation at all times.
   实验室规程要求所有易燃溶剂必须始终存放在通风良好的防火柜中。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-flame-able" — something able to burst INTO flames. The "in-" here means "into," not "not," so inflammable means it CAN catch fire.
把"inflammable"想成"in（进入）-flame（火焰）-able（能够）"——能够着火的东西。这里的"in-"表示"进入"而非"不"。
