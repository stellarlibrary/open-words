# 🏰 rampart

🔊 /ˈræmpɑːrt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A defensive wall or embankment raised as a fortification; a protective barrier or bulwark
**中文：** n. 壁垒；垒；城墙；防御土墙

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bulwark（堡垒）
- fortification（防御工事）
- barricade（路障）
- bastion（壁垒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **behind the ramparts / stone rampart / rampart against / serve as a rampart**

👉 Example: The ancient stone ramparts surrounding the city had withstood countless sieges over the centuries.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The medieval **ramparts** surrounding the old town have been remarkably well preserved and now attract thousands of tourists annually.
   围绕老城的中世纪城墙保存得非常完好，现在每年吸引数千名游客。

2. Strong democratic institutions serve as a crucial **rampart** against the erosion of civil liberties in modern societies.
   强大的民主制度是现代社会中抵御公民自由遭受侵蚀的重要壁垒。

3. Archaeologists discovered the remains of an earthen **rampart** that once protected the settlement from invasion during the Iron Age.
   考古学家发现了一道土质壁垒的遗迹，它曾在铁器时代保护该定居点免受入侵。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rampart" as "ram-part" — the part of a fortress that stops a battering ram, a strong defensive wall.
把"rampart"想成"ram-part"——堡垒中阻挡攻城槌的部分，一道坚固的防御墙。
