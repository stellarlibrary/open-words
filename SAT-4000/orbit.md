# 🪐 orbit

🔊 /ˈɔːrbɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The curved path of a celestial object or spacecraft around a star, planet, or moon; a sphere of influence. (v.) To move in orbit around something
**中文：** n. 轨道；眼眶；势力范围；生活常规 v. 盘旋；绕轨道运行

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- trajectory（轨迹）
- path（路径）
- circuit（环绕）
- revolve（旋转）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **orbit around / in orbit / orbital path / put into orbit / orbit the Earth**

👉 Example: The International Space Station continues to orbit the Earth approximately every ninety minutes.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The satellite was successfully launched into **orbit** around Mars to collect atmospheric data for the international research mission.
   卫星成功发射进入火星轨道，为国际研究任务收集大气数据。

2. The moon takes approximately twenty-seven days to complete a single **orbit** around the Earth in its elliptical path.
   月球大约需要二十七天沿其椭圆路径完成一次绕地球的轨道运行。

3. Several emerging nations have entered the **orbit** of major economic powers through strategic trade agreements and diplomatic alliances.
   几个新兴国家通过战略贸易协定和外交联盟进入了主要经济大国的势力范围。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "orbit" as "orb-it" — an "orb" (sphere) follows "it" (a path), just like planets follow curved paths around the sun.
把"orbit"想成"orb-it"——一个"orb（球体）"沿着"it（路径）"运动，就像行星沿着弯曲的路径绕太阳运行。
