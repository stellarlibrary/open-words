# 📚 didactic

🔊 /daɪˈdæktɪk/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Intended to teach or instruct; having the manner of a teacher, especially in a moralistic or preachy way
**中文：** adj. 说教的；教诲的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- instructive（有教育意义的）
- pedagogical（教学的）
- moralistic（说教的）
- preachy（爱说教的）
- edifying（有启发性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **didactic purpose / didactic tone / didactic literature / didactic approach / didactic style**

👉 Example: The professor's didactic approach made complex philosophical concepts accessible to undergraduate students.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics dismissed the novel as overly **didactic**, arguing that its moral lessons overshadowed the quality of its storytelling.
   评论家认为这部小说过于说教，其道德教训盖过了叙事本身的质量。

2. The museum's **didactic** exhibits were carefully designed to educate visitors about the region's geological history.
   博物馆的教育性展览经过精心设计，旨在教育参观者了解该地区的地质历史。

3. Ancient fables served a **didactic** function by conveying practical wisdom and ethical principles through simple narratives.
   古代寓言通过简单的叙事传达实用的智慧和道德原则，发挥着教诲功能。

---

Think of "didactic" as related to "did act" — a teacher who "did act" out lessons, always instructing and moralizing.
把"didactic"想成与"did act"相关——一个"确实在表演"课程的老师，总是在教导和说教。
