# 🕰️ outlive

🔊 /ˌaʊtˈlɪv/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To live longer than another person; to survive beyond; to endure past the end of something
**中文：** v. 比…活得长；比…经久；经受住；渡过…而存在

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- outlast（比…长久）
- survive（幸存）
- endure（持久）
- transcend（超越）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **outlive one's usefulness / outlive a spouse / outlive expectations / outlive one's reputation**

👉 Example: The professor's groundbreaking theories have outlived him and continue to influence modern research.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Many of the composer's symphonies have **outlived** their creator by centuries and remain beloved masterpieces today.
   这位作曲家的许多交响曲流传至今已数百年，仍然是深受喜爱的杰作。

2. The institution had **outlived** its original purpose and was reorganized to address contemporary social challenges.
   这个机构已经超出了其最初的使命，被重组以应对当代社会挑战。

3. She **outlived** her husband by nearly twenty years, dedicating her remaining time to philanthropic endeavors.
   她比丈夫多活了将近二十年，将余生奉献给了慈善事业。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "out + live" — to live out beyond someone else, to keep living after others are gone.
把"outlive"拆成"out（超过）+ live（活）"——活得比别人更久，在他人离去后仍然继续生活。
