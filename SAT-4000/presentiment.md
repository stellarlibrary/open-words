# 🌫️ presentiment

🔊 /prɪˈzɛntɪmənt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An intuitive feeling or vague sense that something is about to happen, especially something unpleasant; a foreboding
**中文：** n. 预感；不祥之感；预觉

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- premonition（预感）
- foreboding（不祥预感）
- intuition（直觉）
- apprehension（忧虑）
- misgiving（疑虑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a presentiment of danger / a dark presentiment / have a presentiment / a presentiment that**

👉 Example: He could not shake the presentiment that the expedition would end in unforeseen disaster.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Despite the reassuring reports, the general had a strong **presentiment** that the enemy was preparing an ambush along the northern border.
   尽管报告令人安心，将军仍有一种强烈的预感，敌人正在北部边境准备伏击。

2. She dismissed her **presentiment** of failure as mere anxiety, only to discover later that her instincts had been entirely correct.
   她将自己对失败的预感当作单纯的焦虑而不予理会，后来才发现她的直觉完全正确。

3. The memoir recounted the author's haunting **presentiment** that the peaceful era was drawing to a close before the war began.
   这部回忆录讲述了作者在战争开始前萦绕心头的预感——那个和平时代即将结束。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-sentiment" as "a feeling before the event" — an emotional sense that arrives before something actually takes place.
把"presentiment"想成"pre-sentiment"——事件发生之前的"情感"，一种提前到来的预感。
