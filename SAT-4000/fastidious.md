# 🔍 fastidious

🔊 /fæˈstɪdiəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Very attentive to accuracy and detail; excessively particular or demanding; hard to please
**中文：** adj. 挑剔的；苛求的；难取悦的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- meticulous（一丝不苟的）
- finicky（过分讲究的）
- exacting（苛求的）
- punctilious（拘泥细节的）
- scrupulous（严谨的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fastidious about / fastidious attention to detail / fastidious in one's habits / fastidious taste**

👉 Example: The editor was fastidious about grammar and would not tolerate even the slightest error.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **fastidious** curator spent months verifying the provenance of each artifact before approving the museum exhibition.
   这位挑剔的策展人花了数月时间核实每件文物的来源，然后才批准博物馆展览。

2. Her **fastidious** attention to laboratory protocols ensured that every experiment yielded consistently reliable and reproducible results.
   她对实验室规程的严格关注确保了每次实验都能产出一致、可靠且可重复的结果。

3. Critics praised the architect for his **fastidious** approach to design, where every structural element served both form and function.
   评论家称赞这位建筑师一丝不苟的设计方法，其中每个结构元素都兼顾了形式与功能。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fast-tidious" as someone who is "fast to find the tidiest" flaw — always quick to spot imperfections because they are so picky.
把"fastidious"想成"fast to find the tidiest（最快发现最细微瑕疵的人）"——总是能迅速发现不完美之处，因为他们非常挑剔。
