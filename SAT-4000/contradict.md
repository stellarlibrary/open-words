# ⚔️ contradict

🔊 /ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To assert the opposite of a statement; to deny the truth of; to be inconsistent or in conflict with
**中文：** v. 反驳；否定；与……矛盾；与……抵触

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- deny（否认）
- refute（反驳）
- dispute（争论）
- negate（否定）
- oppose（反对）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **contradict a statement / contradict oneself / contradict the evidence / directly contradict**

👉 Example: The new evidence appeared to directly contradict the conclusions of the original investigation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The witness's testimony seemed to **contradict** the physical evidence that had been carefully collected from the crime scene.
   证人的证词似乎与从犯罪现场仔细收集的物证相矛盾。

2. Several recent studies **contradict** the long-held assumption that dietary fat is the primary cause of cardiovascular disease.
   几项最新研究与长期以来认为膳食脂肪是心血管疾病主要原因的观点相矛盾。

3. The politician was criticized for repeatedly **contradicting** his own earlier statements during the nationally televised debate last evening.
   这位政客因在昨晚的全国电视辩论中反复否定自己先前的发言而受到批评。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "contra-dict" as "against what was said" — contra means against, and dict means speak, so to contradict is to speak against.
把"contradict"想成"contra（反对）+ dict（说）"——反驳就是说出相反的话。
