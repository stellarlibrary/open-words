# 🐂 bullock

🔊 /ˈbʊlək/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A young or castrated bull, often used as a draft animal for heavy labor
**中文：** n. 小公牛；阉牛

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- steer（阉牛）
- ox（公牛）
- calf（小牛）
- heifer（小母牛）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bullock cart / a pair of bullocks / yoke a bullock / bullock team**

👉 Example: The farmer relied on a sturdy bullock to pull the heavy plow through the muddy fields.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. In many rural regions of South Asia, the **bullock** cart remains the primary mode of transporting agricultural goods to market.
   在南亚许多农村地区，牛车仍然是将农产品运往市场的主要交通方式。

2. The historian described how **bullock** teams were indispensable for hauling supplies across vast colonial territories in the nineteenth century.
   历史学家描述了十九世纪牛队在广阔殖民地领土上运输物资时不可或缺的作用。

3. A strong **bullock** was selected from the herd to serve as the lead draft animal during the upcoming harvest season.
   从牛群中挑选了一头强壮的阉牛，作为即将到来的收获季的领头挽畜。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bull" + "-ock" (a diminutive suffix) — a bullock is essentially a small or young bull.
把"bullock"想成"bull（公牛）+ -ock（小的后缀）"——bullock就是小公牛或被阉割的牛。
