# 😏 ridicule

🔊 /ˈrɪdɪkjuːl/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To mock or make fun of someone or something contemptuously. (n.) Contemptuous laughter or mockery directed at someone
**中文：** v. 嘲笑，奚落

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- mock（嘲笑）
- deride（嘲弄）
- scorn（蔑视）
- taunt（奚落）
- lampoon（讽刺）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ridicule someone for / subject to ridicule / hold up to ridicule / an object of ridicule**

👉 Example: The opposition party sought to ridicule the government's proposal during the parliamentary debate.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Colleagues openly **ridiculed** his unconventional theory, yet subsequent experiments vindicated his hypothesis beyond any reasonable doubt.
   同事们公开嘲笑他的非传统理论，然而后续实验无可置疑地证实了他的假说。

2. The editorial **ridiculed** the senator's contradictory statements, exposing a pattern of inconsistency throughout her entire political career.
   社论嘲弄了这位参议员自相矛盾的言论，揭露了她整个政治生涯中的不一致模式。

3. Fear of public **ridicule** prevented many innovative thinkers from sharing their groundbreaking ideas with the broader scientific community.
   对公众嘲笑的恐惧阻止了许多创新思想家与更广泛的科学界分享他们的突破性想法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ridicule" as "ridiculous" minus the "-ous" — when you find something ridiculous, you ridicule it by laughing at it mockingly.
把"ridicule"想成"ridiculous"去掉"-ous"——当你觉得某事荒谬时，你就会嘲笑它。
