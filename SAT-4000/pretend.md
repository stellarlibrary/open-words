# 🎪 pretend

🔊 /prɪˈtɛnd/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To act as if something is true when it is not; to feign or simulate. (adj.) Imaginary; not real
**中文：** v. 假装；伪装；佯装 adj. 假装的；虚假的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- feign（假装）
- simulate（模拟）
- dissemble（掩饰）
- bluff（虚张声势）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **pretend to / pretend that / pretend ignorance / pretend not to notice**

👉 Example: The diplomat chose to pretend that nothing unusual had occurred during the tense negotiations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The witness appeared to **pretend** that she had no prior knowledge of the financial transactions under investigation.
   证人似乎假装她事先不知道正在接受调查的金融交易。

2. Children often **pretend** to be explorers or scientists, which fosters their creativity and cognitive development from an early age.
   孩子们经常假装自己是探险家或科学家，这从小就培养了他们的创造力和认知发展。

3. The politician attempted to **pretend** that the controversial remarks had been taken out of context by the media.
   这位政治家试图假装那些有争议的言论是被媒体断章取义的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-tend" as "tending to something before it is real" — you tend to a false version of reality, acting it out before the truth arrives.
把"pretend"想成"pre-tend"——在事情变为真实之前去"tend（照料）"，即在真相出现之前上演一个虚假的版本。
