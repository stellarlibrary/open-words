# ⚡ capacitor

🔊 /kəˈpæsɪtər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An electronic component that stores electrical energy in an electric field, consisting of two conductors separated by an insulating material.
**中文：** n. 电容器

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- condenser（电容器，旧称）
- storage device（储能装置）
- electrical component（电子元件）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **charge a capacitor / discharge a capacitor / capacitor bank / ceramic capacitor / electrolytic capacitor**

👉 Example: The engineer replaced the faulty capacitor on the circuit board to restore normal operation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **capacitor** in the power supply failed, causing the entire system to shut down without warning.
   电源中的电容器故障，导致整个系统在没有警告的情况下关闭。

2. Engineers designed a high-capacity **capacitor** bank to smooth voltage fluctuations in the renewable energy grid.
   工程师设计了一个大容量电容器组，以平滑可再生能源电网中的电压波动。

3. Students learned that a **capacitor** stores energy in an electric field between two parallel conducting plates.
   学生们了解到电容器在两个平行导体板之间的电场中储存能量。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "capacity to store" — a capacitor has the capacity to store electrical charge, just like a tiny rechargeable battery.
"capacitor"含有"capacity（容量）"——电容器有储存电荷的容量，就像一个微型可充电电池。
