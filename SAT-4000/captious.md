# 🔍 captious

🔊 /ˈkæpʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Tending to find fault or raise petty objections; excessively critical over trivial matters
**中文：** adj. 挑剔的；吹毛求疵的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fault-finding（挑剔的）
- hypercritical（过分苛求的）
- carping（吹毛求疵的）
- nitpicking（鸡蛋里挑骨头的）
- censorious（苛评的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **captious critic / captious remarks / captious objections / captious attitude**

👉 Example: The professor's captious remarks on minor formatting errors frustrated the otherwise diligent student.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **captious** reviewer focused entirely on trivial stylistic flaws while ignoring the manuscript's substantial intellectual contributions.
   这位吹毛求疵的审稿人完全集中在细枝末节的风格瑕疵上，却忽视了稿件实质性的学术贡献。

2. Board members grew impatient with the **captious** objections that prolonged every meeting without advancing any meaningful agenda.
   董事会成员对那些延长每次会议却不推进任何实质议程的吹毛求疵的异议越来越不耐烦。

3. A truly effective leader avoids **captious** criticism and instead offers constructive feedback that inspires genuine professional growth.
   一位真正有效的领导者会避免挑剔的批评，转而提供能激发真正职业成长的建设性反馈。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "captious" as "cap + tious" — someone who tries to "capture" every tiny mistake, always looking to catch others in error.
把"captious"想成"cap(ture) + tious"——一个试图"捕捉"每一个小错误的人，总是想抓住别人的失误。
