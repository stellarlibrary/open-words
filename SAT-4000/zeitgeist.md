# 🌐 zeitgeist

🔊 /ˈzaɪtɡaɪst/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The defining spirit or mood of a particular period of history as shown by the ideas and beliefs of the time
**中文：** 时代精神；时代思潮

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- spirit of the age（时代精神）
- ethos（社会风气）
- climate（风气）
- tenor（基调）
- mood（氛围）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **capture the zeitgeist / reflect the zeitgeist / cultural zeitgeist / the zeitgeist of an era**

👉 Example: The novel brilliantly captured the zeitgeist of postwar America with its themes of disillusionment and hope.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The artist's provocative installations perfectly captured the **zeitgeist** of a generation grappling with rapid technological transformation.
   这位艺术家的挑衅性装置作品完美地捕捉了一代人应对快速技术变革的时代精神。

2. Scholars argue that the revolutionary pamphlet both reflected and actively shaped the political **zeitgeist** of the eighteenth century.
   学者们认为，这本革命小册子既反映了也积极塑造了十八世纪的政治时代思潮。

3. Understanding the cultural **zeitgeist** is essential for any historian attempting to interpret the social movements of a given period.
   理解文化时代精神对于任何试图解读特定时期社会运动的历史学家来说都是至关重要的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "zeitgeist" as German for "time-ghost" — "Zeit" (time) + "Geist" (spirit/ghost) — the invisible spirit that haunts a particular era.
把"zeitgeist"想成德语中的"时间幽灵"——"Zeit"（时间）+"Geist"（精神/幽灵）——萦绕在特定时代的无形精神。
