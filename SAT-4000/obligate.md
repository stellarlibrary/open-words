# 📜 obligate

🔊 /ˈɑːblɪɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To bind or compel someone legally or morally; to place under an obligation. (adj.) Required or essential by nature.
**中文：** v. 使负义务；强使，强迫 adj. 有责任的，有义务的；必需的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- compel（强迫）
- require（要求）
- bind（约束）
- mandate（命令）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **obligate someone to do something / feel obligated / legally obligated / morally obligated**

👉 Example: The contract obligates both parties to fulfill their respective duties within the specified deadline.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Federal regulations **obligate** pharmaceutical companies to disclose all known side effects of their medications.
   联邦法规要求制药公司披露其药品所有已知的副作用。

2. The scholarship agreement **obligated** the student to maintain a minimum grade point average throughout the program.
   奖学金协议要求该学生在整个学习期间保持最低平均绩点。

3. A sense of duty **obligated** the physician to remain at the hospital long after her scheduled shift had ended.
   一种责任感促使这位医生在预定班次结束后很久仍留在医院。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "OBLIG-ate" — it contains "oblig" like "obligation" — to obligate someone is to place an obligation upon them.
想想"OBLIG-ate"——它包含"oblig"，就像"obligation"（义务）——obligate就是把义务强加在某人身上。
