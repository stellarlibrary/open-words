# 😣 mawkish

🔊 /ˈmɔːkɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Sentimental in a feeble or sickly way; having a faint, sickly flavor; excessively or falsely emotional
**中文：** adj. 自作多情的；令人作呕的；淡而无味的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sentimental（多愁善感的）
- maudlin（感伤的）
- cloying（令人腻烦的）
- saccharine（过分甜蜜的）
- nauseating（令人作呕的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mawkish sentimentality / mawkish tone / mawkish display of emotion**

👉 Example: The critic dismissed the novel's ending as a mawkish attempt to manipulate readers' emotions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The audience found the film's **mawkish** sentimentality unconvincing, as the emotional scenes felt forced and artificial.
   观众觉得这部电影自作多情的感伤令人难以信服，因为情感场景显得刻意且虚假。

2. Literary scholars criticized the poet's later works for their **mawkish** tone, which lacked the subtlety of his earlier compositions.
   文学学者批评这位诗人后期作品的矫情语调，缺乏其早期作品的细腻之感。

3. The politician's **mawkish** appeal to patriotism failed to resonate with voters who demanded substantive policy proposals instead.
   那位政治家自作多情的爱国呼吁未能引起选民共鸣，他们要求的是实质性的政策方案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mawk-ish" as "mock-ish" — the emotion is so exaggerated it seems like a mockery of real feeling, making you feel sick.
把"mawkish"想成"mock-ish"——情感如此夸张，仿佛是对真实感情的嘲弄，令人作呕。
