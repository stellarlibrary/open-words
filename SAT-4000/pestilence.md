# ☠️ pestilence

🔊 /ˈpestɪləns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A fatal epidemic disease, especially bubonic plague; something that is destructive or harmful
**中文：** n. 瘟疫（尤指鼠疫）；有害的事物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- plague（瘟疫）
- epidemic（流行病）
- scourge（灾祸）
- contagion（传染病）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **spread pestilence / pestilence and famine / pestilence swept through / a moral pestilence**

👉 Example: Medieval chronicles frequently describe how pestilence swept through entire kingdoms without warning.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **pestilence** that ravaged Europe in the fourteenth century fundamentally altered the continent's social and economic structure.
   十四世纪肆虐欧洲的瘟疫从根本上改变了该大陆的社会和经济结构。

2. Historians regard the recurring waves of **pestilence** as a decisive factor in the decline of several ancient civilizations.
   历史学家认为反复发生的瘟疫是几个古代文明衰落的决定性因素。

3. The author used **pestilence** as a metaphor for the moral corruption spreading unchecked throughout the fictional society.
   作者用瘟疫作为隐喻，描写在虚构社会中不受控制地蔓延的道德腐败。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pest-ilence" — a "pest" is something harmful, and "pestilence" is the ultimate harmful thing: a deadly plague.
把"pestilence"想成"pest"（害虫、有害之物）+"ilence"——"pest"是有害的东西，而"pestilence"就是最致命的有害之物：瘟疫。
