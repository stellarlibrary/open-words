# 🦴 ossify

🔊 /ˈɑːsɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To turn into bone; to become hardened or rigid; to become set in a conventional or unchanging pattern.
**中文：** v. 骨化；硬化；僵化

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- harden（硬化）
- petrify（石化）
- solidify（固化）
- stiffen（使僵硬）
- calcify（钙化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ossify into / ossified thinking / ossified institutions / ossified traditions**

👉 Example: Without reform, the bureaucracy will ossify into an inflexible and inefficient structure.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The cartilage in an infant's skeleton gradually **ossifies** into solid bone during the first several years of development.
   婴儿骨骼中的软骨在出生后最初几年的发育过程中逐渐骨化为坚实的骨骼。

2. Critics warned that the institution's traditions had **ossified** to the point where meaningful innovation was virtually impossible.
   批评者警告说，该机构的传统已经僵化到几乎不可能进行有意义创新的程度。

3. Political ideologies tend to **ossify** over time, leaving little room for nuanced debate or flexible policy adaptation.
   政治意识形态往往随着时间推移而僵化，几乎没有留下细致辩论或灵活调整政策的空间。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ossi-" from Latin "os" (bone) + "-fy" (to make) — ossify literally means "to make into bone," and figuratively means to become rigid and unchanging.
把"ossify"拆成"ossi-"（拉丁语"os"，骨头）+"-fy"（使成为）——字面意思是"使成为骨头"，引申为变得僵硬不变。
