# 🌱 foster

🔊 /ˈfɑːstər/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To encourage the development of something; to bring up or nurture a child not one's own. (adj.) Involving or relating to temporary care of a child by someone other than the biological parents
**中文：** v. 培养；促进；收养，养育 adj. 收养的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- nurture（培育）
- cultivate（培养）
- promote（促进）
- encourage（鼓励）
- nourish（滋养）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **foster growth / foster cooperation / foster care / foster parents / foster a sense of**

👉 Example: The program was designed to foster creativity and critical thinking in young students.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The university's mentorship program aims to **foster** intellectual curiosity and independent research skills among first-generation college students.
   该大学的导师计划旨在培养第一代大学生的求知欲和独立研究能力。

2. International organizations work tirelessly to **foster** diplomatic relations between nations that have a long history of political conflict.
   国际组织不懈努力，促进长期存在政治冲突的国家之间的外交关系。

3. Growing up in a loving **foster** home provided the child with the stability and emotional support necessary for healthy development.
   在一个充满爱的寄养家庭中长大，为这个孩子提供了健康成长所必需的稳定性和情感支持。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "foster" as "fos-ter" — like a "forest" where seeds are nurtured and grow. To foster is to help something grow and develop.
把"foster"想成"forest"（森林）——森林里种子被培育并成长。Foster就是帮助某事物成长和发展。
