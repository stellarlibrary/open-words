# 🔍 abstruse

🔊 /əbˈstruːs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Difficult to understand; obscure and recondite
**中文：** adj. 深奥的；难懂的；奥妙的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- obscure（晦涩的）
- esoteric（深奥的）
- recondite（难解的）
- arcane（神秘难懂的）
- enigmatic（费解的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **abstruse theory / abstruse language / abstruse reasoning / highly abstruse**

👉 Example: The philosopher's abstruse writings required multiple readings before they could be understood.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's lecture on quantum mechanics was so **abstruse** that most students left deeply confused.
   教授关于量子力学的讲座过于深奥，大多数学生听后一头雾水。

2. Critics accused the author of deliberately using **abstruse** language to appear more intellectually sophisticated.
   评论家指责该作者故意使用晦涩难懂的语言来显得更有学术深度。

3. The legal document contained several **abstruse** clauses that even experienced attorneys found difficult to interpret.
   该法律文件包含几项深奥的条款，即使是经验丰富的律师也难以解读。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "abstruse" as "ab-TRUCE" — there is no truce (peace) when reading something abstruse; your brain keeps struggling to understand.
把"abstruse"想成"ab-TRUCE"——读深奥的东西时大脑无法"休战"(truce)，一直在费力理解。
