# 🌸 sensitive

🔊 /ˈsɛnsɪtɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Quick to detect or respond to slight changes, signals, or influences; easily affected emotionally. (n.) A person with heightened perceptive abilities
**中文：** adj. 敏感的；灵敏的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- delicate（敏感的）
- susceptible（易受影响的）
- responsive（灵敏的）
- perceptive（敏锐的）
- touchy（过敏的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sensitive to [stimulus] / sensitive issue / sensitive information / highly sensitive / politically sensitive**

👉 Example: The committee discussed the politically sensitive issue behind closed doors to avoid public controversy.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The new instrument is extremely **sensitive** to minute fluctuations in atmospheric pressure, enabling meteorologists to predict weather changes with greater accuracy.
   这台新仪器对大气压力的微小波动极其灵敏，使气象学家能够更准确地预测天气变化。

2. Diplomatic negotiations require handling **sensitive** topics with considerable tact and discretion to avoid escalating tensions between the parties involved.
   外交谈判需要以相当的策略和审慎来处理敏感话题，以避免加剧相关各方之间的紧张关系。

3. Children are often more **sensitive** to environmental toxins than adults because their developing organ systems are particularly vulnerable to chemical exposure.
   儿童通常比成人对环境毒素更敏感，因为他们正在发育的器官系统特别容易受到化学物质暴露的影响。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sensitive" as having keen "senses" — like a cat's whiskers that detect the slightest vibration, a sensitive person or device picks up what others miss.
把"sensitive"想成有敏锐的"感官"——就像猫的胡须能感知最轻微的振动，敏感的人或设备能捕捉到别人错过的东西。
