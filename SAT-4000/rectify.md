# 🔧 rectify

🔊 /ˈrektɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To correct something that is wrong or inaccurate; to put right; to purify or refine
**中文：** v. 改正；纠正；整顿；精馏；整流

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- correct（纠正）
- remedy（补救）
- amend（修正）
- redress（矫正）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rectify a mistake / rectify an error / rectify the situation / rectify a problem**

👉 Example: The company took immediate steps to rectify the billing errors that had affected hundreds of customers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The engineering team worked overnight to **rectify** the critical software flaw before the product launch date arrived.
   工程团队连夜工作，在产品发布日期到来之前纠正了关键的软件缺陷。

2. The administration promised to **rectify** the inequitable distribution of resources among the district's public schools.
   政府承诺纠正该地区公立学校之间资源分配不公平的问题。

3. Congress introduced new legislation intended to **rectify** the longstanding procedural deficiencies within the regulatory framework.
   国会提出了新立法，旨在纠正监管框架中长期存在的程序缺陷。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rect-ify" — "rect" sounds like "right," so to rectify means to make things right again.
把"rectify"想成"rect(=right，正确的) + ify(使…)"——使事情重新变正确，即纠正。
