# 🌪️ tumultuous

🔊 /tjuːˈmʌltʃuəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Making a loud, confused noise; uproarious; excited, confused, or disorderly; marked by turbulence or upheaval
**中文：** 吵闹的；骚乱的；狂暴的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- turbulent（动荡的）
- tempestuous（狂暴的）
- chaotic（混乱的）
- stormy（暴风雨般的）
- boisterous（喧闹的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tumultuous applause / tumultuous period / tumultuous relationship / tumultuous times**

👉 Example: The band received a tumultuous ovation from the audience after their final encore.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The nation endured a **tumultuous** decade of civil unrest, economic collapse, and sweeping political reform throughout the region.
   这个国家经历了十年的动荡，包括内乱、经济崩溃以及整个地区的全面政治改革。

2. The shareholders gave the departing CEO a **tumultuous** farewell, reflecting both deep admiration and lingering controversy.
   股东们给卸任的首席执行官送上了热烈的告别，反映出深深的敬佩和挥之不去的争议。

3. After a **tumultuous** debate in parliament, the legislation narrowly passed with a margin of only three decisive votes.
   在议会经过激烈的辩论后，该立法以仅三票的微弱优势勉强通过。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tumultuous" as "tumult + uous" — full of tumult, meaning full of noise, chaos, and wild commotion.
把"tumultuous"想成"tumult + uous"——充满了tumult（骚动），意味着充满了噪音、混乱和狂暴的骚动。
