# 🎯 diversion

🔊 /daɪˈvɜːrʒən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The act of turning something aside from its course; an activity that diverts the mind from tedious concerns; a distraction or amusement
**中文：** n. 转移；转向；消遣

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- distraction（分心）
- detour（绕道）
- recreation（消遣）
- amusement（娱乐）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **create a diversion / diversion of funds / diversion from / welcome diversion**

👉 Example: The magician's assistant created a clever diversion so the audience would not notice the trick.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The unauthorized **diversion** of public funds for private use resulted in a major political scandal and multiple criminal indictments.
   将公共资金擅自转移作私人使用导致了一场重大政治丑闻和多项刑事起诉。

2. Reading literary fiction serves as a welcome **diversion** from the relentless pressures of modern professional and academic life.
   阅读文学小说是从现代职业和学术生活的无尽压力中获得的一种受欢迎的消遣。

3. The military commander ordered a strategic **diversion** to draw enemy forces away from the primary objective of the operation.
   军事指挥官下令进行战略转移，以将敌军从行动的主要目标上引开。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "di-version" as "different version" — a diversion takes you to a different version of where you were heading.
把"diversion"想成"di(不同的)-version(版本)"——转移让你走向原来方向的一个不同版本。
