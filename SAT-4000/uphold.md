# ⚖️ uphold

🔊 /ʌpˈhoʊld/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To support, maintain, or defend something such as a law, principle, or decision; to confirm or endorse
**中文：** v. 支撑；维护；赞成；举起；坚持

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sustain（维持）
- maintain（维护）
- defend（捍卫）
- affirm（确认）
- endorse（支持）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **uphold the law / uphold a decision / uphold a principle / uphold standards / uphold rights**

👉 Example: The Supreme Court voted to uphold the lower court's ruling on the constitutionality of the legislation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The appellate court chose to **uphold** the original verdict, affirming that the defendant's constitutional rights had not been violated.
   上诉法院选择维持原判，确认被告的宪法权利未受到侵犯。

2. Educators have a fundamental responsibility to **uphold** academic integrity and ensure fair evaluation of all students.
   教育工作者有一项基本责任，即维护学术诚信并确保对所有学生进行公正评估。

3. The organization pledged to **uphold** its commitment to environmental sustainability throughout every phase of its operations.
   该组织承诺在其运营的每个阶段都坚持对环境可持续性的承诺。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "up-hold" — holding something up high so it does not fall, just as you hold up laws and principles to keep them standing.
把"uphold"想成"向上托住"——把某物高高托起使其不倒塌，就像你维护法律和原则使其屹立不倒。
