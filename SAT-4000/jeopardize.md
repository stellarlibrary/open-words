# ⚠️ jeopardize

🔊 /ˈdʒɛpərdaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To put something or someone into a situation in which there is a danger of loss, harm, or failure; to endanger
**中文：** v. 危害；使陷入危险；使受危困

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- endanger（危及）
- imperil（使陷入危险）
- threaten（威胁）
- compromise（损害）
- risk（冒险）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **jeopardize safety / jeopardize one's career / jeopardize the outcome / jeopardize relations**

👉 Example: Leaking confidential information could seriously jeopardize the ongoing negotiations between the two nations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scandal threatened to **jeopardize** the senator's reelection campaign and permanently damage her political reputation among constituents.
   这起丑闻可能危及参议员的连任竞选，并永久损害她在选民中的政治声誉。

2. Failure to comply with environmental regulations could **jeopardize** the company's operating license and result in substantial financial penalties.
   不遵守环境法规可能危及公司的经营许可证，并导致巨额经济处罚。

3. The researchers warned that continued deforestation would **jeopardize** the survival of numerous endangered species throughout the tropical region.
   研究人员警告说，持续的森林砍伐将危及整个热带地区众多濒危物种的生存。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "jeopardize" as related to "Jeopardy" (the game show) — when you're in jeopardy, you're at risk of losing everything, just like a wrong answer on the show.
把"jeopardize"和"Jeopardy"（电视节目《危险边缘》）联系起来——当你处于jeopardy中，你面临失去一切的风险，就像节目中答错了一样。
