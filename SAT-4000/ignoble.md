# 😤 ignoble

🔊 /ɪɡˈnoʊbəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Not honorable in character or purpose; deserving of shame; base or morally low
**中文：** adj. 不光彩的；卑鄙的；可耻的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dishonorable（不光彩的）
- despicable（卑劣的）
- base（低下的）
- contemptible（可鄙的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ignoble motives / ignoble conduct / ignoble act / ignoble defeat / ignoble pursuit**

👉 Example: The senator's ignoble attempt to conceal the scandal only deepened the public's distrust of political leaders.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The historian revealed that the celebrated general had been driven by **ignoble** motives of personal enrichment rather than patriotic duty.
   历史学家揭示，这位著名将军的真正动机是卑鄙的个人敛财，而非爱国责任。

2. The committee condemned the corporation's **ignoble** decision to prioritize short-term profits over the long-term welfare of its workers.
   委员会谴责该公司将短期利润置于员工长期福祉之上的可耻决定。

3. Despite his **ignoble** origins in the criminal underworld, the character ultimately redeemed himself through acts of genuine selflessness and courage.
   尽管这个角色出身于不光彩的犯罪黑社会，他最终通过真正的无私和勇敢行为实现了自我救赎。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ig-noble" — the prefix "ig-" negates "noble," so ignoble means the opposite of noble: without honor or dignity.
把"ignoble"拆成"ig（否定前缀）+ noble（高尚的）"——ignoble就是不高尚的，即卑鄙的、不光彩的。
