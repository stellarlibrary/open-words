# 🖤 abomination

🔊 /əˌbɒmɪˈneɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A thing that causes disgust or hatred; intense loathing or disapproval; something morally revolting
**中文：** n. 厌恶；憎恨；令人厌恶的事物；可憎的行为

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- atrocity（暴行）
- outrage（暴行）
- disgrace（耻辱）
- monstrosity（怪物）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **an abomination to / regard as an abomination / moral abomination / absolute abomination**

👉 Example: The senator called the proposed legislation an abomination that would erode civil liberties.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Historians regard the forced displacement of indigenous populations as an **abomination** against fundamental human rights.
   历史学家将强制驱逐原住民视为对基本人权的严重践踏。

2. The architectural critic dismissed the new skyscraper as an **abomination** that ruined the city's historic skyline.
   这位建筑评论家将新摩天大楼斥为破坏城市历史天际线的丑陋之物。

3. Child labor in hazardous industries remains an **abomination** that the international community must urgently address.
   危险行业中的童工问题仍然是国际社会必须紧急解决的可憎现象。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Abomination" = "abominate" + "-ion" (noun form). If you abominate something, the thing itself is an abomination.
"Abomination" = "abominate" + "-ion"（名词形式）。如果你abominate某事物，那个事物本身就是一个abomination。
