# 🌫️ obfuscate

🔊 /ˈɑːbfʌskeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make something unclear, obscure, or unintelligible; to bewilder or confuse deliberately.
**中文：** v. 使模糊；使混乱；使迷惑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- obscure（使模糊）
- confuse（使混乱）
- muddle（使困惑）
- bewilder（使迷惑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **obfuscate the truth / obfuscate the issue / deliberately obfuscate / obfuscate meaning**

👉 Example: The politician attempted to obfuscate the issue by introducing irrelevant details into the debate.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corporation's spokesperson tried to **obfuscate** the financial losses by burying key data in footnotes.
   公司发言人试图通过将关键数据隐藏在脚注中来混淆财务亏损情况。

2. Legal jargon can **obfuscate** the true meaning of a contract, making it difficult for ordinary citizens to understand.
   法律术语可能会使合同的真正含义变得模糊，让普通公民难以理解。

3. The author intentionally chose to **obfuscate** the narrative timeline, challenging readers to piece together the story.
   作者故意使叙事时间线变得模糊，挑战读者自行拼凑故事。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ob-FUS-cate" — "fus" sounds like "fuzz" — to obfuscate is to make things fuzzy and unclear.
把"obfuscate"中的"fus"想成"fuzz"（模糊）——obfuscate就是让事物变得模糊不清。
