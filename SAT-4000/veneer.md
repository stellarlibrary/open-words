# 🎭 veneer

🔊 /vəˈnɪr/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A thin decorative covering of fine wood; an attractive but deceptive outward appearance. (v.) To cover with a thin layer of fine material; to disguise with a superficially attractive appearance
**中文：** n. 薄板，单板；外表，虚饰 v. 胶合；虚饰；给…镶以饰片

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- facade（表面）
- guise（伪装）
- pretense（伪装）
- overlay（覆盖层）
- semblance（外观）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **veneer of respectability / veneer of civilization / thin veneer / beneath the veneer / strip away the veneer**

👉 Example: Beneath the veneer of politeness, there was a palpable tension between the two rival delegations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician maintained a **veneer** of composure during the press conference despite facing increasingly hostile questions from reporters.
   这位政治家在新闻发布会上保持着镇定的外表，尽管面对记者越来越尖锐的提问。

2. The antique dealer noticed that the walnut **veneer** on the cabinet had begun to crack and peel after decades of exposure to humidity.
   古董商注意到橱柜上的胡桃木贴面在数十年的潮湿环境下已开始开裂和剥落。

3. The documentary stripped away the **veneer** of corporate philanthropy to reveal the exploitative labor practices hidden beneath the surface.
   这部纪录片揭去了企业慈善的虚饰外衣，揭露了隐藏在表面之下的剥削性劳动行为。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "veneer" as "v-near" — the surface is very near to you, but what lies beneath may be entirely different from what appears on top.
把"veneer"想成"v-near（非常近）"——表面离你很近，但底下的东西可能与表面看到的完全不同。
