# 🏔️ precipice

🔊 /ˈpresɪpɪs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A very steep rock face or cliff; a dangerous or precarious situation.
**中文：** n. 悬崖；绝壁；险境；危急的处境

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cliff（悬崖）
- bluff（峭壁）
- brink（边缘）
- crag（险峰）
- escarpment（陡坡）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **on the precipice of / stand at the precipice / teetering on the precipice / the edge of a precipice**

👉 Example: The company stood on the precipice of bankruptcy after years of mismanagement and declining revenue.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The hikers paused at the edge of the **precipice**, gazing down at the valley hundreds of meters below.
   徒步旅行者们在悬崖边停下脚步，俯瞰数百米下方的山谷。

2. Many economists warned that the nation was teetering on the **precipice** of a severe financial crisis.
   许多经济学家警告说，该国正处于严重金融危机的边缘。

3. The negotiators understood that one misstep could push the fragile peace agreement over the **precipice** into collapse.
   谈判人员清楚，一个失误就可能使脆弱的和平协议坠入深渊，走向崩溃。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "preci-PICE" — picture a "piece" of rock breaking off a steep cliff, leaving a sheer drop.
把"precipice"想成"preci（陡峭）+ pice（碎片）"——一块岩石从陡峭的悬崖上破碎脱落。
