# 🔮 augur

🔊 /ˈɔːɡər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词，动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** (n.) A person who foretells events, especially a religious official in ancient Rome who interpreted omens. (v.) To portend or serve as an omen of a future outcome; to predict.

**中文：** (n.) 预言者 (v.) 预言；是……的预兆

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- prophet（预言家）
- foretell（预言）
- portend（预示）
- presage（预兆）
- foreshadow（预示）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

The phrase "augur well/ill (for)" is extremely common and means "to be a good/bad sign for something." Do not confuse "augur" (prophet/predict) with "auger" (a drilling tool) — they are homophones with entirely different meanings.

短语 "augur well/ill (for)" 非常常用，意为"对某事来说是好/坏兆头"。不要混淆 "augur"（预言者/预言）和 "auger"（螺旋钻）——它们是同音异义词。

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The strong early sales figures **augur** well for the company's annual revenue projections.
   强劲的早期销售数据对公司的年度收入预测来说是个好**预兆**。

2. In ancient Rome, an **augur** would interpret the flight patterns of birds before battles.
   在古罗马，**占卜师**会在战斗前解读鸟类的飞行模式。

3. The sudden resignation of two senior officials does not **augur** well for political stability.
   两位高级官员的突然辞职对政治稳定来说并非好**兆头**。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "augur" as "Au-GUR-u" — like a guru with a golden (Au = gold) vision of the future. An augur is a wise figure who *predicts* what lies ahead.

把 "augur" 想成 "Au-GUR-u"——像一位拥有黄金般（Au = 金）未来远见的"大师"（guru）。augur 就是一位能*预测*未来的智者。
