# 😤 gall

🔊 /ɡɔːl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Bile; bitterness of feeling; impudent boldness or audacity; something that causes irritation. (v.) To irritate, annoy, or vex greatly
**中文：** n. 胆汁；怨恨；苦味；厚颜无耻 v. 烦恼；使恼怒

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- audacity（厚颜无耻）
- bitterness（怨恨）
- nerve（胆量）
- irritate（激怒）
- vex（使恼怒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **have the gall to / full of gall / gall and wormwood / it galls me**

👉 Example: He had the gall to demand a promotion after repeatedly arriving late to work.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee was astonished that the contractor had the **gall** to submit an invoice for work that was never completed.
   委员会对承包商竟然厚颜无耻地为从未完成的工作提交发票感到震惊。

2. It **galled** the veteran employees to see a newly hired manager receive preferential treatment without any proven track record.
   看到新聘的经理在没有任何业绩证明的情况下获得优待，这让资深员工们深感恼火。

3. The bitter **gall** of defeat lingered long after the championship match, motivating the team to train even harder.
   失败的苦涩怨恨在锦标赛结束后久久不散，激励着球队更加努力训练。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gall" as related to "gall bladder" — the organ that holds bile, which is literally bitter; figuratively, gall means bitterness or bold nerve.
把"gall"联想到"gall bladder"（胆囊）——储存胆汁的器官，胆汁本身是苦的；引申义就是怨恨或厚颜无耻的胆量。
