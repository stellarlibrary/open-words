# 💧 transpire

🔊 /trænˈspaɪər/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To occur or happen; to become known or revealed; (in biology) to give off water vapor through the surface of leaves or skin
**中文：** v. 发生；蒸发；泄露

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- occur（发生）
- happen（发生）
- emerge（显现）
- evaporate（蒸发）
- unfold（展开）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **it transpired that / what transpired / events that transpired / transpire through leaves**

👉 Example: It later transpired that the company had been concealing its financial losses for years.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. It eventually **transpired** that the diplomat had been secretly negotiating a separate agreement without official authorization.
   最终真相大白，这位外交官一直在未经官方授权的情况下秘密谈判一项独立协议。

2. The events that **transpired** during the closed-door session were never formally disclosed to the general public.
   闭门会议期间发生的事件从未正式向公众披露。

3. Plants **transpire** water through tiny pores called stomata, a process essential for nutrient transport and temperature regulation.
   植物通过名为气孔的微小孔隙蒸发水分，这一过程对养分运输和温度调节至关重要。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tran-spire" as "breathe (spire) across (trans)" — like secrets breathing out into the open, or water vapor breathing through leaves.
把"transpire"想成"trans（穿过）+ spire（呼吸）"——就像秘密被泄露出来，或水蒸气从叶片中呼出。
