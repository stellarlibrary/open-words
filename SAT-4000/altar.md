# 🕯️ altar

🔊 /ˈɔːltər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A table or flat-topped structure used as the focus for a religious ritual, especially for making offerings or sacrifices to a deity.
**中文：** 祭坛；圣坛

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- shrine（神龛）
- sanctuary（圣所）
- chancel（圣坛所在处）
- communion table（圣餐台）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **at the altar, approach the altar, altar of sacrifice, lead to the altar, wedding altar**
👉 Example: The congregation gathered solemnly around the altar for the evening prayer service.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient **altar** discovered by archaeologists contained remnants of ceremonial offerings dating back two thousand years.
   考古学家发现的古代祭坛上有可追溯到两千年前的祭祀遗迹。

2. The bride and groom exchanged their vows before a beautifully decorated **altar** adorned with white lilies.
   新娘和新郎在装饰着白色百合花的美丽圣坛前交换了誓言。

3. Many civilizations have sacrificed their finest possessions upon the **altar** of their most revered deities.
   许多文明都在他们最崇敬的神灵的祭坛上献上了最珍贵的祭品。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "alt(high) + ar" — an altar is a raised, high structure where sacred rituals take place.
把 altar 想象成 "alt（高的，如 altitude）+ ar"——祭坛是举行神圣仪式的高台。
