# ⏳ finite

🔊 /ˈfaɪnaɪt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having definite limits or bounds; not infinite; restricted in size, amount, or extent
**中文：** adj. 有限的；限定的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- limited（有限的）
- bounded（有界的）
- restricted（受限的）
- measurable（可衡量的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **finite resources / finite number / finite amount / finite set / finite lifespan**

👉 Example: The Earth's finite resources demand that industries adopt sustainable practices for long-term survival.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Economists warn that the world's **finite** supply of fossil fuels necessitates an urgent transition to renewable energy sources.
   经济学家警告说，世界有限的化石燃料供应要求紧急过渡到可再生能源。

2. The mathematician demonstrated that only a **finite** number of solutions exist for the given set of polynomial equations.
   数学家证明了给定多项式方程组只存在有限个解。

3. Human attention is a **finite** resource, and effective communication requires conveying essential information concisely and clearly.
   人类的注意力是一种有限的资源，有效的沟通需要简洁明了地传达关键信息。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "finite" as "fin-ite" — "fin" means end in French, so finite means something that has an end or limit.
把"finite"想成"fin-ite"——"fin"在法语中意为"结束"，所以finite表示有终点或限度的事物。
