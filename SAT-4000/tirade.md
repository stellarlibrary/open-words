# 🗯️ tirade

🔊 /taɪˈreɪd/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A long, angry, or vehement speech of criticism or accusation; a prolonged outburst of denunciation
**中文：** n. 激烈的长篇演说；长篇的抨击性讲话

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- diatribe（抨击）
- harangue（长篇大论）
- rant（咆哮）
- invective（谩骂）
- denunciation（谴责）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **launch into a tirade / deliver a tirade / angry tirade / tirade against / lengthy tirade**

👉 Example: The senator launched into a tirade against the proposed legislation during the heated debate.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The manager's unexpected **tirade** against the entire department left employees stunned and demoralized for several days.
   经理对整个部门的突然长篇痛斥让员工们震惊且士气低落了好几天。

2. Critics noted that the politician's **tirade** lacked substantive arguments and relied entirely on emotional appeals and hyperbole.
   批评人士指出，这位政客的激烈演说缺乏实质性论据，完全依赖情感诉求和夸张手法。

3. The editorial was essentially a **tirade** against corporate greed, accusing major firms of exploiting vulnerable communities.
   这篇社论本质上是对企业贪婪的长篇抨击，指控大公司剥削弱势群体。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tirade" as "tired" rearranged — after listening to someone's long, angry tirade, you feel completely tired and exhausted.
把"tirade"想成"tired（疲倦的）"的变形——在听完某人冗长而愤怒的长篇大论后，你会感到完全疲惫不堪。
