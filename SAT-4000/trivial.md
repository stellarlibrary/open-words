# 🪶 trivial

🔊 /ˈtrɪviəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Of little value or importance; insignificant; concerned with unimportant matters
**中文：** adj. 不重要的；琐碎的；微不足道的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- insignificant（微不足道的）
- petty（琐碎的）
- trifling（微小的）
- negligible（可忽略的）
- minor（次要的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **trivial matter / trivial detail / far from trivial / seem trivial**

👉 Example: The committee decided not to waste time on trivial issues when urgent matters demanded attention.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher argued that what appeared to be a **trivial** detail in the data actually held the key to understanding the phenomenon.
   研究人员认为，数据中看似琐碎的细节实际上是理解这一现象的关键。

2. In the context of global climate change, disputes over municipal parking regulations seem remarkably **trivial** and unworthy of debate.
   在全球气候变化的背景下，关于市政停车规定的争论显得格外微不足道，不值得讨论。

3. The philosopher contended that no question about human existence should be dismissed as **trivial**, regardless of how simple it appears.
   这位哲学家主张，关于人类存在的任何问题都不应被视为琐碎的，无论它看起来多么简单。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "trivia" — trivia games deal with small, unimportant facts, so trivial means something of little importance.
想想"trivia（冷知识问答）"——冷知识游戏涉及细小、不重要的事实，所以trivial就是不重要的、琐碎的。
