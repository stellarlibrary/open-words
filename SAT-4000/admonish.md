# ⚠️ admonish

🔊 /ədˈmɒnɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To warn or reprimand someone firmly but not harshly; to counsel against something; to urge earnestly.
**中文：** 告诫；劝告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- warn（警告）
- caution（劝诫）
- reprimand（训斥）
- rebuke（斥责）
- counsel（劝告）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **admonish someone for/about something**, **admonish someone to do something**

👉 Example: The professor admonished the students for neglecting their academic responsibilities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The supervisor **admonished** the team for failing to follow established safety protocols during the drill.
   主管告诫团队在演习中未能遵守既定的安全规程。

2. Her mentor gently **admonished** her to consider the long-term consequences before making a decision.
   她的导师温和地劝告她在做决定之前考虑长远后果。

3. The editorial **admonished** public officials for their slow response to the escalating environmental crisis.
   社论谴责了公职人员对日益严重的环境危机反应迟缓。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ad-" (to) + "monish" (from Latin monere, to warn) — to direct a warning toward someone.
联想"ad-"（向）+"monish"（源自拉丁语monere，警告）——向某人发出警告。
