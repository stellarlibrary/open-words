# 💨 dishevel

🔊 /dɪˈʃɛvəl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make untidy or disordered, especially hair or clothing; to cause to appear unkempt
**中文：** v. 使蓬乱；弄乱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- tousle（弄乱）
- rumple（弄皱）
- disorder（使紊乱）
- mess up（搞乱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **disheveled hair / disheveled appearance / disheveled clothing / wind-disheveled**

👉 Example: The strong coastal winds disheveled her carefully styled hair within seconds of stepping outside.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The overnight storm had **disheveled** the garden's meticulously arranged flower beds beyond any immediate recognition.
   一夜暴风雨将花园里精心布置的花坛弄得面目全非。

2. Hours of intensive fieldwork had thoroughly **disheveled** the archaeologist's clothing, leaving her covered in dust and debris.
   数小时的密集田野调查彻底弄乱了这位考古学家的衣着，使她满身灰尘和碎屑。

3. The professor arrived at the lecture hall looking **disheveled**, having spent the entire night revising his research manuscript.
   这位教授到达演讲厅时看起来蓬头垢面，因为他整晚都在修改研究手稿。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-hevel" — "dis" means apart, and "hevel" sounds like "hairevel (level)" — your hair loses its level and goes all over the place.
把"dishevel"想成"dis（散开）+ hevel（像 hair level，头发的整齐度）"——头发失去整齐度，变得乱七八糟。
