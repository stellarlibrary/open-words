# 😐 mediocre

🔊 /ˌmiːdiˈoʊkər/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Of only moderate quality; not very good; ordinary and undistinguished
**中文：** adj. 普通的；平凡的；中等的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- average（平均的）
- ordinary（普通的）
- undistinguished（平凡的）
- lackluster（平淡无奇的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mediocre performance / mediocre results / mediocre quality / mediocre work**

👉 Example: The student's mediocre grades did not reflect his true intellectual potential.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Despite considerable investment, the company delivered only **mediocre** returns that disappointed its shareholders and analysts.
   尽管投入了大量资金，公司仅取得了平庸的回报，令股东和分析师感到失望。

2. The committee refused to accept **mediocre** research proposals, insisting that only exceptional work would receive funding.
   委员会拒绝接受平庸的研究提案，坚持只有优秀的工作才能获得资助。

3. A culture that tolerates **mediocre** standards will inevitably struggle to compete in the global marketplace.
   一种容忍平庸标准的文化，在全球市场竞争中必然会举步维艰。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mediocre" as "medium + okay" — something that is just medium and merely okay, neither good nor bad.
把"mediocre"想成"medium（中等）+ okay（还行）"——不好不坏，只是中等水平。
