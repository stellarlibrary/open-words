# ✨ embellish

🔊 /ɪmˈbɛlɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make something more attractive by adding decorative details; to enhance a story with fictitious or exaggerated details
**中文：** v. 修饰；装饰；润色

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- adorn（装饰）
- decorate（修饰）
- embroidery（润色）
- ornament（点缀）
- beautify（美化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **embellish a story / embellish the truth / embellish with details / embellish a narrative**

👉 Example: The author chose to embellish the historical account with vivid, imaginative descriptions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The architect decided to **embellish** the facade of the building with intricate marble carvings and gilded ornaments.
   建筑师决定用精美的大理石雕刻和镀金装饰来修饰建筑的外立面。

2. Critics accused the politician of **embellishing** his wartime record to gain public sympathy and electoral advantage.
   批评者指责该政客润色自己的战时经历以博取公众同情和选举优势。

3. The narrator tended to **embellish** every anecdote with colorful details that blurred the line between fact and fiction.
   叙述者倾向于给每个轶事添加丰富多彩的细节，模糊了事实与虚构之间的界限。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "em-BELL-ish" — you add a BELL to something plain to make it more decorative and attention-grabbing.
把"embellish"想成"em-BELL-ish"——你给平淡的东西加上一个铃铛（BELL），让它更加华丽引人注目。
