# 🗣️ suasion

🔊 /ˈsweɪʒən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Persuasion; the act of advising or urging someone earnestly
**中文：** n. 说服；劝告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- persuasion（说服）
- exhortation（劝告）
- urging（敦促）
- inducement（劝诱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **moral suasion / gentle suasion / through suasion / suasion rather than coercion**

👉 Example: The central bank relied on moral suasion to encourage commercial banks to lower their interest rates.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat relied on careful **suasion** rather than threats to bring the conflicting parties to a peaceful agreement.
   这位外交官依靠谨慎的劝说而非威胁来促使冲突各方达成和平协议。

2. Through persistent moral **suasion**, the community leader convinced local businesses to adopt more environmentally sustainable practices.
   通过持续的道德劝导，社区领袖说服当地企业采取更加环保可持续的做法。

3. The committee employed **suasion** as its primary strategy, understanding that voluntary compliance would prove more durable than mandates.
   委员会以劝说作为主要策略，认识到自愿遵从比强制命令更为持久。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "suasion" as related to "persuasion" — just remove the "per" prefix, and you get the root meaning of convincing someone through gentle urging.
把"suasion"想成和"persuasion"有关——去掉前缀"per"，就得到了通过温和劝导说服他人的核心含义。
