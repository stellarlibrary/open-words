# 😤 repugnant

🔊 /rɪˈpʌɡnənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Extremely distasteful or unacceptable; arousing strong feelings of disgust or aversion; incompatible or inconsistent with something
**中文：** 令人讨厌的；矛盾的；敌对的；冲突的；不一致的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- abhorrent（令人憎恶的）
- revolting（令人反感的）
- loathsome（令人厌恶的）
- odious（可憎的）
- repulsive（令人排斥的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **morally repugnant / repugnant to someone / find something repugnant / repugnant behavior**

👉 Example: The committee found the defendant's actions morally repugnant and deserving of the harshest penalty.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosopher argued that slavery is morally **repugnant** and fundamentally incompatible with any civilized system of governance.
   这位哲学家认为奴隶制在道德上是令人厌恶的，与任何文明的治理体系从根本上不相容。

2. Many voters found the candidate's inflammatory rhetoric deeply **repugnant** and refused to support the campaign any further.
   许多选民认为该候选人的煽动性言论极其令人反感，并拒绝进一步支持其竞选活动。

3. The court ruled that the proposed legislation was **repugnant** to the constitutional guarantee of equal protection under the law.
   法院裁定，拟议的立法与宪法规定的法律面前人人平等的保障相矛盾。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "repugnant" as "re-pug-nant" — imagine a pug dog wrinkling its nose in disgust, turning away from something truly revolting.
把"repugnant"想成"re-pug-nant"——想象一只哈巴狗皱起鼻子，厌恶地转过头去，远离真正令人恶心的东西。
