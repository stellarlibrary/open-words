# 📜 verbose

🔊 /vɜːrˈboʊs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Using or expressed in more words than are needed; long-winded and wordy
**中文：** adj. 冗长的；啰嗦的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- wordy（冗长的）
- long-winded（啰嗦的）
- prolix（冗长的）
- loquacious（多话的）
- garrulous（唠叨的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **verbose writing / verbose explanation / verbose style / overly verbose**

👉 Example: The editor asked the author to revise the verbose manuscript into a more concise version.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's **verbose** lectures often exceeded the allotted time, leaving students exhausted and struggling to identify key points.
   教授冗长的讲座经常超出规定时间，让学生精疲力竭且难以找到要点。

2. Academic reviewers criticized the paper for its **verbose** methodology section, recommending substantial cuts for clarity and brevity.
   学术审稿人批评该论文方法论部分过于冗长，建议大幅删减以提高清晰度和简洁性。

3. Effective legal writing avoids **verbose** phrasing and instead favors precise, unambiguous language that conveys meaning efficiently.
   有效的法律写作避免冗长的措辞，而是倾向于使用精确、明确的语言来高效传达含义。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "verbose" as "verb-ose" — overloaded with verbs and words, like someone who uses far too many words to say something simple.
把"verbose"想成"verb-ose"——满载着动词和词汇，就像某人用了太多词来表达简单的意思。
