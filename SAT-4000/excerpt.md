# 📜 excerpt

🔊 /ˈɛksɜːrpt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A short extract or passage taken from a text, film, or piece of music. (v.) To take a passage or segment from a larger work
**中文：** n. 摘录；选录；节录 v. 引用；摘录

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- extract（摘录）
- passage（段落）
- selection（选段）
- quotation（引文）
- clip（片段）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **an excerpt from / read an excerpt / publish an excerpt / brief excerpt**

👉 Example: The professor read an excerpt from the novel to illustrate the author's distinctive narrative style.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The literary journal published an **excerpt** from the forthcoming novel, generating considerable anticipation among avid readers.
   这本文学期刊刊登了即将出版的小说的节选，在热心读者中引起了极大的期待。

2. During the lecture, the historian **excerpted** several key passages from primary sources to support her central argument.
   在讲座中，这位历史学家从原始资料中摘录了几段关键段落来支持她的核心论点。

3. The documentary featured an **excerpt** from the president's inaugural address that resonated deeply with the viewing audience.
   这部纪录片播放了总统就职演说的一段节选，引起了观众的深刻共鸣。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-cerpt" as "ex-carpt" — you carve out (extract) a piece from a larger body of work, like carving a sculpture.
把"excerpt"想成"ex-carpt"——你从一部大作品中雕刻出（摘取）一部分，就像雕刻雕塑一样。
