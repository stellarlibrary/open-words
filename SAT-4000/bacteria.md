# 🦠 bacteria

🔊 /bækˈtɪəriə/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** Microscopic single-celled organisms that exist in vast numbers in almost every environment on Earth; some cause disease while others are beneficial.

**中文：** 细菌（bacterium 的复数形式）。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- germs（病菌）
- microbes（微生物）
- microorganisms（微生物）
- pathogens（病原体）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Bacteria" is the plural form of "bacterium." In formal scientific writing, use "bacteria" with plural verbs (e.g., "bacteria are"). In everyday English, "bacteria" is sometimes treated as singular, but this is considered incorrect in academic contexts.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Harmful **bacteria** in contaminated water supplies remain one of the leading causes of infectious disease worldwide.
   （受污染水源中的有害**细菌**仍然是全球传染病的主要原因之一。）

2. Researchers discovered that certain **bacteria** in the human gut play a vital role in maintaining overall health.
   （研究人员发现人体肠道中的某些**细菌**在维持整体健康方面起着至关重要的作用。）

3. The laboratory technician carefully cultured the **bacteria** on agar plates to identify the specific strain causing infection.
   （实验室技术人员在琼脂培养板上仔细培养**细菌**，以确定导致感染的特定菌株。）

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Bacteria" contains "act" in the middle — think of bacteria as tiny actors that "act" inside your body, some playing helpful roles and others causing trouble.

"Bacteria"中间包含"act（行动）"——想象细菌是在你体内"行动"的小演员，有些扮演有益的角色，有些则制造麻烦。
