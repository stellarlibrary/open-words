# 😩 drudgery

🔊 /ˈdrʌdʒəri/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Hard, tedious, and menial work; exhausting labor that is dull and unrewarding
**中文：** n. 苦工；苦差事；单调乏味的工作

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- toil（辛苦劳作）
- grind（苦差事）
- labor（劳动）
- drudge work（苦活）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sheer drudgery / escape the drudgery / daily drudgery / drudgery of housework**

👉 Example: Many workers sought to escape the drudgery of repetitive factory labor through education.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The invention of automated machinery liberated countless laborers from the mindless **drudgery** of repetitive manual tasks.
   自动化机械的发明将无数劳动者从重复性体力劳动的枯燥苦差中解放出来。

2. Despite the intellectual rewards of academic research, much of the preliminary fieldwork involved sheer **drudgery** and tedium.
   尽管学术研究有智力上的回报，但许多初步的实地工作充满了纯粹的苦差和单调。

3. The young apprentice endured years of **drudgery** before finally earning the opportunity to practice the craft independently.
   年轻的学徒忍受了多年的苦差事，才终于获得了独立从事这门手艺的机会。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "drudgery" as "drudge + ery" — a "drudge" is someone who does hard boring work, so "drudgery" is the state of doing such work.
把"drudgery"想成"drudge + ery"——"drudge"是做苦力的人，所以"drudgery"就是做这种苦力活的状态。
