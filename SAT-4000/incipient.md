# 🌱 incipient

🔊 /ɪnˈsɪpiənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Beginning to happen or develop; in an initial or early stage.
**中文：** adj. 初期的；初始的；起初的；发端的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- nascent（新生的）
- budding（萌芽的）
- emerging（新兴的）
- embryonic（初期的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **incipient stages / incipient signs / incipient disease / incipient rebellion**

👉 Example: The doctor detected incipient signs of the disease before any visible symptoms had appeared.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Early intervention proved critical in halting the **incipient** spread of the epidemic throughout the densely populated region.
   早期干预对于阻止疫情在人口稠密地区的初期传播至关重要。

2. The professor identified **incipient** trends in the data that would later become significant patterns in the research.
   教授在数据中识别出初期趋势，这些趋势后来成为研究中的重要模式。

3. Political analysts warned that the **incipient** unrest in the province could escalate into widespread civil conflict if ignored.
   政治分析人士警告说，如果忽视该省初期的动荡，可能会升级为大范围的内乱。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-CIPI-ent" — "cipi" sounds like "sip." When you take the first sip of tea, that is the incipient moment of drinking.
把"incipient"想成"in-CIPI-ent"——"cipi"听起来像"sip（啜饮）"。当你喝第一口茶时，那就是饮茶的初始时刻。
