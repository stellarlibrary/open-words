# 🔄 paradox

🔊 /ˈpærəˌdɒks/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A seemingly contradictory statement that may nonetheless be true; a self-contradictory person, thing, or situation
**中文：** n. 悖论，反论；似非而是的论点；自相矛盾的人或事

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- contradiction（矛盾）
- enigma（谜）
- anomaly（反常）
- inconsistency（不一致）
- puzzle（难题）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **paradox of / present a paradox / resolve a paradox / apparent paradox**

👉 Example: The paradox of thrift suggests that individual saving can lead to collective economic decline.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **paradox** of modern technology is that devices designed to connect people often contribute to social isolation.
   现代技术的悖论在于，旨在连接人们的设备往往反而加剧了社会孤立。

2. Historians continue to debate the **paradox** of a nation that proclaimed liberty while simultaneously permitting the institution of slavery.
   历史学家们继续争论一个宣扬自由却同时容许奴隶制度存在的国家的悖论。

3. The philosopher presented an intriguing **paradox** that challenged the audience to reconsider their fundamental assumptions about free will.
   这位哲学家提出了一个引人入胜的悖论，促使听众重新审视他们关于自由意志的基本假设。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "para-dox" as "pair of docs" — imagine two doctors giving opposite diagnoses that somehow are both correct.
把"paradox"想成"pair of docs"（一对医生）——想象两位医生给出相反的诊断，但两个诊断竟然都是正确的。
