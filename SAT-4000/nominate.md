# 🗳️ nominate

🔊 /ˈnɒmɪneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To formally propose or recommend someone for a position, office, honor, or award; to appoint or designate for a duty or role
**中文：** v. 提名；推荐；任命；指定

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- propose（提议）
- designate（指定）
- appoint（任命）
- recommend（推荐）
- select（选定）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **nominate someone for / nominate a candidate / nominate for an award / be nominated as**

👉 Example: The committee will nominate three candidates for the prestigious research fellowship this spring.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board voted unanimously to **nominate** Dr. Chen as the new chairperson of the international research committee.
   董事会一致投票提名陈博士为国际研究委员会的新主席。

2. Her groundbreaking documentary was **nominated** for multiple awards at several prominent international film festivals last year.
   她具有开创性的纪录片去年在多个著名国际电影节上获得了多项提名。

3. Each department may **nominate** up to two employees for the annual excellence in leadership recognition program.
   每个部门最多可以提名两名员工参加年度卓越领导力表彰计划。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "nomin-ate" — "nomin" comes from Latin "nomen" meaning "name," so to nominate is to name someone for a position or honor.
把"nominate"中的"nomin"联想到拉丁语"nomen（名字）"——提名就是为某个职位或荣誉"点名"某人。
