# 📜 annalist

🔊 /ˈænəlɪst/<br>
🏷️ *n. a writer of annals; a chronicler | 名词：编年史作者；纪年表编者*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A person who writes annals; a historian who records events in chronological order, year by year.
**中文：** 编写编年史的人；按年代顺序逐年记录事件的历史学家。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- chronicler（编年史家）
- historian（历史学家）
- recorder（记录者）
- archivist（档案管理员）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **court annalist, official annalist, medieval annalist, distinguished annalist**
👉 Example: The royal annalist meticulously documented every significant event that occurred during the king's reign.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The medieval **annalist** recorded each plague, famine, and battle with remarkable precision and scholarly detachment.
   这位中世纪的编年史作者以非凡的精确性和学术超然记录了每一次瘟疫、饥荒和战斗。

2. As the court's official **annalist**, he bore the solemn responsibility of preserving the dynasty's historical legacy.
   作为宫廷的官方编年史作者，他肩负着保存王朝历史遗产的庄重责任。

3. Modern historians rely heavily on the work of ancient **annalists** to reconstruct the political landscape of past civilizations.
   现代历史学家在很大程度上依赖古代编年史作者的工作来重建过去文明的政治格局。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Annalist" = "annual" + "-ist" (one who does) — an annalist is one who records events annually, writing the story of each year.
"Annalist" = "annual"（每年的）+ "-ist"（从事...的人）——编年史作者就是逐年记录事件的人，书写每一年的故事。
