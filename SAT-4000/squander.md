# 💸 squander

🔊 /ˈskwɒndər/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To waste something, especially money or time, in a reckless or foolish manner. (n.) An act or instance of squandering; wasteful expenditure
**中文：** v. 浪费 n. 浪费

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- waste（浪费）
- dissipate（挥霍）
- fritter away（消耗殆尽）
- lavish（挥霍）
- misuse（滥用）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **squander money / squander an opportunity / squander resources / squander one's talent**

👉 Example: The young heir managed to squander his entire inheritance within a single year.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corporation chose to **squander** millions of dollars on an ill-conceived marketing campaign that yielded no measurable results.
   该公司选择将数百万美元浪费在一场策划不当、毫无成效的营销活动上。

2. Critics argued that the government had **squander**ed a rare opportunity to reform the outdated educational system.
   批评人士认为政府浪费了一次改革过时教育体制的难得机会。

3. Rather than invest wisely, he continued to **squander** his considerable fortune on frivolous and extravagant pursuits.
   他没有明智地投资，反而继续将大量财富挥霍在轻浮奢侈的追求上。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "squander" as "square-wander" — imagine money wandering away in all directions from a square, scattering and wasting everywhere.
把"squander"想成"square-wander"——想象金钱从广场四处游荡散落，到处浪费。
