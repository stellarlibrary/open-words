# 🧲 induce

🔊 /ɪnˈdjuːs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To persuade or lead someone to do something; to bring about or cause; to produce by electromagnetic induction
**中文：** v. 劝诱；促使；引起，导致；感应

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- persuade（说服）
- provoke（引发）
- elicit（引出）
- prompt（促使）
- stimulate（刺激）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **induce labor / induce a response / induce sleep / induce someone to do something**

👉 Example: The pharmaceutical company developed a new compound designed to induce rapid healing in damaged tissues.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher used a carefully calibrated magnetic field to **induce** an electrical current in the experimental coil apparatus.
   研究人员使用精心校准的磁场在实验线圈装置中感应出电流。

2. Financial incentives alone were insufficient to **induce** qualified candidates to relocate to the remote rural hospital for permanent positions.
   仅靠经济激励不足以诱导合格的候选人搬迁到偏远的乡村医院担任永久职位。

3. Prolonged exposure to extreme stress can **induce** a variety of physiological disorders, including hypertension and chronic insomnia.
   长期暴露于极端压力下可以引起多种生理疾病，包括高血压和慢性失眠。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-DUCE" as "lead in" — the Latin root "ducere" means "to lead," so to induce is to lead someone into doing something.
把"induce"想成"in(进入)+duce(引导)"——拉丁词根"ducere"意为"引导"，所以induce就是引导某人去做某事。
