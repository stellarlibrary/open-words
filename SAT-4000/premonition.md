# 🔮 premonition

🔊 /ˌprɛməˈnɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A strong feeling that something is about to happen, especially something unpleasant; a forewarning or presentiment
**中文：** n. 预感；前兆；预先的警告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- foreboding（预感）
- presentiment（预感）
- forewarning（预先警告）
- omen（征兆）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a premonition of danger / have a premonition / a dark premonition / a premonition that…**

👉 Example: She had an unsettling premonition that something terrible was about to unfold during the expedition.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The captain reported a strange **premonition** of disaster just hours before the vessel encountered the catastrophic storm.
   船长报告说在船只遭遇灾难性风暴前几个小时，他有一种奇怪的不祥预感。

2. Despite having no rational basis for her **premonition**, she decided to cancel her travel arrangements for that weekend.
   尽管她的预感没有合理的依据，她还是决定取消那个周末的旅行安排。

3. Historians now view the economist's warning as a remarkable **premonition** of the financial crisis that would devastate global markets.
   历史学家现在将这位经济学家的警告视为对后来重创全球市场的金融危机的非凡预见。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-monition" as "pre-monitor" — like an internal monitor warning you before something happens.
把"premonition"想成"pre（之前）+ monition（警告）"——事情发生之前的内心警告，即预感。
