# 🌊 depth

🔊 /dɛpθ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The distance from the top or surface to the bottom of something; profoundness of thought, feeling, or meaning
**中文：** n. 深度；深处；深奥；深刻

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- profundity（深刻）
- deepness（深度）
- intensity（强度）
- extent（程度）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in depth / depth of knowledge / depth of field / out of one's depth / the depths of despair**

👉 Example: The researcher examined the issue in great depth before publishing her comprehensive analysis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The submarine descended to an unprecedented **depth** of three thousand meters beneath the surface of the Atlantic Ocean.
   该潜水艇下潜至大西洋海面以下三千米的空前深度。

2. Her doctoral thesis demonstrated remarkable **depth** of understanding in the field of quantum mechanics and theoretical physics.
   她的博士论文展现了在量子力学和理论物理学领域非凡的理解深度。

3. The economic crisis revealed the true **depth** of structural inequality that had persisted within the nation for decades.
   经济危机揭示了数十年来国内结构性不平等的真正严重程度。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "depth" as "deep + th" — just as "width" comes from "wide," depth comes from "deep," measuring how far down something goes.
把"depth"想成"deep（深的）+ th（名词后缀）"——就像width来自wide一样，depth来自deep，衡量某物有多深。
