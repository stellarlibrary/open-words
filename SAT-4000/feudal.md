# 🏰 feudal

🔊 /ˈfjuːdəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or resembling the feudal system of medieval Europe, characterized by lords, vassals, and serfs
**中文：** adj. 封建制度的；封建的；领地的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- medieval（中世纪的）
- manorial（庄园的）
- aristocratic（贵族的）
- hierarchical（等级制的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **feudal system / feudal society / feudal lord / feudal era / feudal structure**

👉 Example: The feudal system gradually collapsed as commerce and urbanization transformed European society.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **feudal** system required peasants to work the land in exchange for military protection from their overlords.
   封建制度要求农民耕种土地，以换取领主的军事保护。

2. Historians argue that many developing nations still retain **feudal** power structures despite their modern democratic constitutions.
   历史学家认为，许多发展中国家尽管拥有现代民主宪法，仍保留着封建权力结构。

3. The revolution sought to dismantle the **feudal** aristocracy and redistribute land ownership among the common citizens.
   这场革命旨在瓦解封建贵族制度，将土地所有权重新分配给普通公民。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "feudal" as "feud-al" — feuds between lords and vassals were common in the feudal system of medieval times.
把"feudal"想成"feud-al"——在中世纪的封建制度中，领主和附庸之间的争斗（feud）非常常见。
