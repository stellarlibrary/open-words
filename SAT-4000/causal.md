# 🔗 causal

🔊 /ˈkɔːzəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or acting as a cause; involving or constituting a cause-and-effect relationship
**中文：** adj. 因果关系的；有原因的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- causative（引起的）
- contributory（促成的）
- determinative（决定性的）
- instrumental（起作用的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **causal relationship / causal link / causal factor / causal connection / causal inference**

👉 Example: The researchers sought to establish a causal link between air pollution and respiratory disease.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The study failed to demonstrate a direct **causal** relationship between the dietary supplement and the observed improvements in cognitive function.
   该研究未能证明膳食补充剂与观察到的认知功能改善之间存在直接因果关系。

2. Epidemiologists must carefully distinguish between mere correlation and genuine **causal** connections when analyzing public health data.
   流行病学家在分析公共卫生数据时必须仔细区分单纯的相关性和真正的因果联系。

3. The committee identified several **causal** factors that contributed to the structural failure of the bridge during the earthquake.
   委员会确定了几个导致桥梁在地震中结构失效的因果因素。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "causal" as "cause-al" — it is simply the adjective form of "cause," describing anything that has to do with causes and their effects.
把"causal"想成"cause-al"——它就是"cause"（原因）的形容词形式，描述任何与因果关系有关的事物。
