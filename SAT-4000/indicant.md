# 🔎 indicant

🔊 /ˈɪndɪkənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Serving to indicate or point out. (n.) Something that indicates; a sign, token, or indicator of a particular condition
**中文：** adj. 指示的；表示的 n. 指示物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- indicator（指标）
- sign（标志）
- marker（标记物）
- signal（信号）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **indicant of change / key indicant / reliable indicant / indicant factor**

👉 Example: Rising consumer confidence is often viewed as an indicant of forthcoming economic recovery.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden decline in biodiversity was a clear **indicant** of severe environmental degradation occurring throughout the entire region.
   生物多样性的突然下降是整个地区正在发生严重环境退化的明显指示。

2. Researchers identified elevated cortisol levels as a reliable **indicant** of chronic stress in participants across all demographic groups.
   研究人员确定皮质醇水平升高是所有人口统计组参与者慢性压力的可靠指标。

3. The professor explained that voter turnout rates serve as an important **indicant** of civic engagement within democratic societies.
   教授解释说，投票率是民主社会中公民参与度的重要指示。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "indic-ant" — like an ant that indicates the way, pointing its tiny antennae toward something important to show the direction.
把"indicant"想成"指示的蚂蚁（ant）"——就像一只蚂蚁用它的小触角指向重要的东西，为你指明方向。
