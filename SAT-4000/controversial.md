# 🔥 controversial

🔊 /ˌkɒntrəˈvɜːrʃəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Giving rise to public disagreement or heated debate; likely to provoke strong and opposing opinions
**中文：** adj. 有争议的；有争论的；引起争论的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- contentious（有争议的）
- debatable（可争论的）
- divisive（引起分裂的）
- disputed（有争议的）
- polarizing（两极化的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **controversial issue / controversial decision / highly controversial / controversial figure / controversial topic**

👉 Example: The board's controversial decision to close the community center sparked immediate public outrage.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scientist's **controversial** theory challenged decades of established research and ignited a fierce debate within the academic community.
   这位科学家有争议的理论挑战了数十年来的既有研究，在学术界引发了激烈辩论。

2. The government passed a **controversial** law that many civil liberties organizations argued would severely restrict freedom of expression.
   政府通过了一项有争议的法律，许多公民自由组织认为该法律将严重限制言论自由。

3. The museum's decision to display the **controversial** artwork generated intense discussion about the boundaries of artistic expression.
   博物馆展出这件有争议的艺术品的决定引发了关于艺术表达界限的激烈讨论。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "contro-versy-al" as "against-turning" — a controversial topic turns people against each other, creating opposing sides.
把"controversial"想成"使人们相互对立"——有争议的话题让人们彼此对立，形成对立的阵营。
