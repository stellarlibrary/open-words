# 🌤️ forecast

🔊 /ˈfɔːrkæst/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A prediction or estimate of future events, especially weather or economic trends. (v.) To predict or estimate a future event or trend
**中文：** n. 预测；预报 v. 预报；预测

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- predict（预测）
- projection（预估）
- prognosis（预后）
- anticipate（预期）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **weather forecast / forecast growth / economic forecast / forecast demand / sales forecast**

👉 Example: Analysts forecast a steady increase in demand for renewable energy over the next decade.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The meteorological agency **forecast** heavy rainfall across the eastern seaboard, prompting authorities to issue evacuation advisories.
   气象部门预报东海岸将有大范围强降雨，促使当局发布了疏散通知。

2. Financial analysts revised their **forecast** for quarterly earnings downward after the unexpected disruption in global supply chains.
   在全球供应链意外中断后，财务分析师下调了季度收益预测。

3. It is exceedingly difficult to **forecast** technological breakthroughs, as innovation often emerges from unpredictable and disparate sources.
   预测技术突破极为困难，因为创新往往来自不可预见的、各种不同的来源。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fore + cast" — to cast your thoughts forward in time, predicting what will happen ahead.
把"forecast"想成"fore + cast"——把你的思维投射到未来，预测前方将发生什么。
