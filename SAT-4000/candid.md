# 💬 candid

🔊 /ˈkændɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Truthful and straightforward; frank and honest in expression; free from bias or prejudice.
**中文：** adj. 坦白的；率直的；公正的；无偏见的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- frank（坦率的）
- forthright（直率的）
- outspoken（直言不讳的）
- sincere（真诚的）
- unbiased（无偏见的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **candid opinion / candid conversation / candid assessment / candid photograph / be candid about**

👉 Example: The manager appreciated her candid feedback on the shortcomings of the new proposal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat offered a remarkably **candid** assessment of the failed negotiations, acknowledging mistakes made by both parties.
   那位外交官对失败的谈判做出了极为坦率的评估，承认了双方犯下的错误。

2. In her memoir, the author provides a **candid** account of her struggles with poverty and discrimination.
   在她的回忆录中，作者对自己与贫困和歧视的抗争做出了坦诚的叙述。

3. The committee requested a **candid** evaluation of the program's effectiveness before allocating additional funding resources.
   委员会要求在分配额外资金资源之前对该项目的有效性进行公正的评估。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "candid" as related to "candle" — a candle illuminates everything honestly, hiding nothing in the dark, just as a candid person reveals the truth openly.
把"candid"联想为与"candle（蜡烛）"有关——蜡烛诚实地照亮一切，不在黑暗中隐藏任何东西，正如一个坦率的人公开揭示真相。
