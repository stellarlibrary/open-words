# 😞 distressed

🔊 /dɪˈstrɛst/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Suffering from anxiety, sorrow, or pain; experiencing great mental or emotional suffering
**中文：** adj. 痛苦的；忧虑的；哀伤的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- anguished（痛苦的）
- troubled（忧虑的）
- afflicted（受折磨的）
- grief-stricken（悲痛欲绝的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **distressed by / distressed about / visibly distressed / emotionally distressed**

👉 Example: The students were visibly distressed after learning that the scholarship program had been discontinued.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The community was deeply **distressed** by the government's decision to demolish the historic library without public consultation.
   社区居民对政府在未经公众协商的情况下拆除历史图书馆的决定深感痛苦。

2. Researchers found that financially **distressed** households were significantly more likely to experience chronic health problems over time.
   研究人员发现，经济困难的家庭随着时间推移更容易出现慢性健康问题。

3. The **distressed** wildlife population struggled to survive as industrial pollution continued to contaminate their natural habitat.
   随着工业污染持续侵蚀自然栖息地，处于困境中的野生动物种群艰难求生。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-stressed" — when stress takes over completely, you become distressed, overwhelmed by pressure and sorrow.
把"distressed"想成"dis-stressed"——当压力完全占据你时，你变得痛苦不堪，被压力和悲伤淹没。
