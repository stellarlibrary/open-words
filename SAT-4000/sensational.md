# 💥 sensational

🔊 /sɛnˈseɪʃənəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Causing great public excitement or interest; deliberately provocative or exaggerated to arouse strong reactions
**中文：** adj. 轰动性的，引起哗然的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- spectacular（轰动的）
- dramatic（戏剧性的）
- shocking（令人震惊的）
- startling（惊人的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sensational news / sensational headline / sensational discovery / sensational performance**

👉 Example: The tabloid published a sensational story that provoked widespread controversy among readers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist was criticized for writing **sensational** headlines that distorted the facts in order to attract more readers to the publication.
   这位记者因撰写歪曲事实的轰动性标题以吸引更多读者而受到批评。

2. The **sensational** archaeological discovery of an intact ancient tomb generated intense media coverage and scholarly debate across the globe.
   这一轰动性的考古发现——一座完好无损的古墓——在全球引发了密集的媒体报道和学术辩论。

3. Critics argued that **sensational** reporting on criminal cases often undermines public trust in the integrity of the judicial process.
   批评人士认为，对刑事案件的耸人听闻的报道往往会损害公众对司法公正性的信任。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sensational" as "sensation + al" — something that creates a sensation, like a headline so dramatic it makes everyone stop and take notice.
把"sensational"想成"sensation + al"——能制造轰动的事物，就像一个戏剧性的标题让所有人都停下来关注。
