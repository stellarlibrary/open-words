# 📉 derogate

🔊 /ˈdɛrəɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To detract from; to disparage or belittle; to deviate from a standard or expectation
**中文：** v. 减损；贬损；毁损

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disparage（贬低）
- belittle（轻视）
- diminish（减损）
- detract（贬低）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **derogate from / derogate someone's authority / derogate a right**

👉 Example: Such behavior would derogate from the dignity of the judicial office.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee warned that the proposed amendment would **derogate** from the fundamental rights enshrined in the constitutional framework.
   委员会警告说，拟议的修正案将减损宪法框架中所保障的基本权利。

2. His persistent refusal to follow established procedures served to **derogate** his professional standing among senior colleagues and administrators.
   他一再拒绝遵循既定程序，这损害了他在资深同事和管理层中的职业声望。

3. No subsidiary regulation should **derogate** from the overarching principles established by international treaties and binding legal agreements.
   任何附属法规都不应减损国际条约和具有约束力的法律协议所确立的总体原则。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-rogue-ate" — to treat someone like a rogue is to derogate them, stripping away their respectability and reputation.
把"derogate"想成"de-rogue-ate（把人当流氓对待）"——像对待流氓一样对待某人就是贬损他们，剥夺其体面和声誉。
