# 🤏 modicum

🔊 /ˈmɒdɪkəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A small quantity or limited amount of something, especially something desirable or valuable
**中文：** n. 少量，一点点

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- iota（一点点）
- smidgen（少许）
- trace（微量）
- shred（一丝）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a modicum of respect / a modicum of truth / a modicum of effort / a modicum of decency**

👉 Example: Even a modicum of preparation would have prevented the catastrophic failure of the project.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor expected students to demonstrate at least a **modicum** of intellectual curiosity during seminar discussions.
   教授期望学生在研讨会讨论中至少表现出一点点求知欲。

2. With only a **modicum** of evidence, the prosecution struggled to convince the jury of the defendant's guilt.
   仅凭少量证据，控方难以说服陪审团相信被告有罪。

3. The negotiators maintained a **modicum** of civility despite the increasingly hostile atmosphere surrounding the trade dispute.
   尽管贸易争端的气氛日趋紧张，谈判代表们仍保持了起码的礼貌。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "modi-cum" as "modify + crumb" — just a crumb-sized modification, a tiny amount of something.
把"modicum"想成"modify + crumb"——只有面包屑大小的改动，即非常少量的东西。
