# 😏 disdain

🔊 /dɪsˈdeɪn/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The feeling that someone or something is unworthy of respect or consideration; contempt. (v.) To regard or treat with haughty contempt
**中文：** n. 轻蔑，鄙视 v. 鄙弃，不屑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- contempt（蔑视）
- scorn（轻蔑）
- derision（嘲笑）
- disregard（漠视）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **disdain for / look upon with disdain / treat with disdain / disdain to do something**

👉 Example: The aristocrat looked upon the common laborers with open disdain and refused to acknowledge them.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The literary critic expressed open **disdain** for the bestselling novel, dismissing it as shallow entertainment unworthy of serious analysis.
   这位文学评论家公开表达了对这本畅销小说的蔑视，认为它是不值得严肃分析的肤浅娱乐。

2. She **disdained** the offer of compromise, believing that any concession would undermine her principles and weaken her negotiating position.
   她不屑于接受妥协的提议，认为任何让步都会损害她的原则并削弱她的谈判地位。

3. The professor's visible **disdain** for poorly researched arguments motivated students to prepare more thoroughly before presenting in class.
   教授对研究不充分论点的明显鄙视激励学生在课堂上发言前做更充分的准备。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-dain" — "dis" means to disrespect, and "dain" sounds like "deign" (屈尊), so disdain means you do not even deign to respect someone.
把"disdain"想成"dis-dain"——"dis"表示不尊重，"dain"听起来像"deign"（屈尊），所以disdain就是连尊重别人都不屑。
