# 🏰 beleaguer

🔊 /bɪˈliːɡər/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To besiege; to surround with military forces; to harass or beset with difficulties
**中文：** v. 围攻；围困；使苦恼

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- besiege（围攻）
- harass（骚扰）
- plague（困扰）
- torment（折磨）
- pester（烦扰）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **beleaguered city / beleaguered leader / beleaguered by problems / beleaguered troops**

👉 Example: The beleaguered mayor faced mounting criticism from all sides during the prolonged fiscal crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **beleaguered** garrison held its position for three months despite dwindling supplies and relentless enemy bombardment.
   被围困的守军在物资日渐减少和敌军持续轰炸的情况下坚守了三个月。

2. Financial scandals continued to **beleaguer** the corporation, eroding investor confidence and depressing its share price.
   金融丑闻持续困扰着这家公司，侵蚀了投资者信心并压低了其股价。

3. The **beleaguered** prime minister struggled to maintain public support amid escalating protests and parliamentary opposition.
   这位困境中的首相在不断升级的抗议和议会反对中努力维持公众支持。

---

Think of "be-leaguer" — like a league of enemies surrounding you, leaving you beleaguered and under siege.
把"beleaguer"想成"be-leaguer"——好像一群(league)敌人围住你，让你陷入围困和困扰。
