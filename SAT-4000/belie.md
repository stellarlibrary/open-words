# 🎭 belie

🔊 /bɪˈlaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To give a false impression of; to contradict or show to be false; to disguise or conceal
**中文：** v. 掩饰；与…不符；证明…虚假错误；使失望

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- contradict（与…矛盾）
- misrepresent（歪曲）
- disguise（掩饰）
- negate（否定）
- conceal（隐藏）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **belie one's age / belie the truth / belie expectations / belie appearances**

👉 Example: Her calm demeanor belied the intense anxiety she was experiencing before the interview.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The nation's impressive economic statistics **belied** the widespread poverty that persisted in its rural communities.
   该国令人印象深刻的经济数据掩盖了其农村地区普遍存在的贫困状况。

2. His youthful appearance **belied** his sixty years of age, frequently causing colleagues to underestimate his experience.
   他年轻的外表与他六十岁的年龄不相称，常常导致同事低估他的经验。

3. The company's optimistic public statements **belied** the serious internal divisions threatening its long-term viability.
   该公司乐观的公开声明掩饰了威胁其长期生存能力的严重内部分歧。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "be-lie" — it contains "lie," so to belie is to present a lie, giving a false impression that contradicts the truth.
把"belie"想成"be-lie"——里面包含"lie（谎言）"，所以belie就是呈现一种虚假的表象，与事实不符。
