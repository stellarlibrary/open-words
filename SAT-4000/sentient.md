# 🧠 sentient

🔊 /ˈsenʃənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Able to perceive or feel things; having sensation or consciousness. (n.) A being that has awareness or feeling
**中文：** adj. 有感觉力的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conscious（有意识的）
- aware（觉察的）
- perceptive（有感知力的）
- feeling（有感觉的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sentient being / sentient creature / sentient life / sentient species / fully sentient**

👉 Example: The ethicist contended that all sentient beings deserve moral consideration regardless of their species.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Philosophers have long debated whether artificial intelligence could ever become truly **sentient** and possess genuine self-awareness.
   哲学家们长期争论人工智能是否能真正具有感知力并拥有真正的自我意识。

2. Animal rights advocates argue that all **sentient** creatures have an inherent right to be free from unnecessary suffering.
   动物权利倡导者认为，所有有感觉力的生物都有免受不必要痛苦的固有权利。

3. The discovery of complex neural networks in octopuses suggests they are far more **sentient** than scientists previously believed.
   章鱼体内复杂神经网络的发现表明它们的感知能力远超科学家此前的认知。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sentient" as "sent-I-ent" — "I was sent the ability to feel," meaning you are endowed with sensation and awareness.
把"sentient"想成"sent-I-ent"——"我被赋予了感知的能力"，意味着你拥有感觉和意识。
