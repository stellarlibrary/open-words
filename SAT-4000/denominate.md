# 🏷️ denominate

🔊 /dɪˈnɒmɪneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To give a name or title to; to designate or classify, especially in terms of a unit of value
**中文：** v. 为…命名；以…为单位

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- designate（指定）
- name（命名）
- label（标注）
- classify（分类）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **denominated in dollars / denominated in euros / denominate a currency / denominate a value**

👉 Example: The bonds were denominated in Japanese yen to attract international investors.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. International trade agreements often require that transactions be **denominated** in a mutually accepted and stable reserve currency.
   国际贸易协定通常要求交易以双方认可的稳定储备货币计价。

2. The central bank decided to **denominate** the new series of banknotes in smaller units for everyday convenience.
   中央银行决定将新系列纸币以较小的面额命名，以方便日常使用。

3. Scholars **denominated** the newly discovered mineral after the region where it was first identified by geological surveys.
   学者们以地质调查中首次发现该矿物的地区为其命名。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-nominate" as "to give a nominal name" — denominate means to assign a name or denomination to something.
把"denominate"想成"给一个nominal（名义上的）name（名字）"——denominate就是给某物指定一个名称或面额。
