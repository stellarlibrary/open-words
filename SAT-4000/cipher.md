# 🔐 cipher

🔊 /ˈsaɪfər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A secret code or system of writing; a person of no importance; the number zero. (v.) To encode a message; to calculate arithmetically
**中文：** n. 密码；暗号；零；无足轻重的人 v. 使用密码；计算

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- code（密码）
- cryptograph（密码文）
- zero（零）
- encode（加密）
- nobody（无名小卒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **break a cipher / cipher text / cipher key / a mere cipher**

👉 Example: The intelligence agency spent months trying to break the enemy's cipher during the war.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The intelligence officer employed an elaborate **cipher** to transmit classified information securely across enemy territory.
   情报官员使用了一种复杂的密码，以便在敌方领土上安全传输机密信息。

2. Without the corresponding key, the intercepted message remained an indecipherable **cipher** to the analysts on duty.
   没有相应的密钥，截获的信息对值班分析人员来说仍是一个无法破解的密码。

3. In the autocratic regime, the figurehead president was little more than a **cipher** for the military generals' authority.
   在那个专制政权中，名义上的总统不过是军事将领们权力的傀儡而已。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cipher" as related to "zero" (from Arabic "sifr" meaning empty) — a cipher can mean zero, a nobody, or a secret code that looks like nothing to outsiders.
"cipher"与"zero"有关（源自阿拉伯语"sifr"，意为空）——cipher可以指零、无名小卒，或者在外人看来毫无意义的密码。
