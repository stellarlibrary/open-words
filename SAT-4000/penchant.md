# 💫 penchant

🔊 /ˈpentʃənt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A strong or habitual liking for something; a tendency or inclination toward a particular thing
**中文：** n. 嗜好；倾向；趣味

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- inclination（倾向）
- fondness（喜好）
- predilection（偏好）
- proclivity（癖好）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a penchant for / have a penchant for / strong penchant**

👉 Example: The author has a well-known penchant for writing stories with unexpected endings.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor has a notable **penchant** for incorporating literary references into her otherwise technical scientific lectures.
   这位教授有一个显著的嗜好，喜欢在她原本技术性的科学讲座中融入文学典故。

2. His **penchant** for extravagant spending eventually led to serious financial difficulties and mounting personal debt.
   他挥霍无度的嗜好最终导致了严重的财务困难和不断增加的个人债务。

3. The diplomat's **penchant** for candid remarks often created awkward situations during delicate international negotiations.
   这位外交官直言不讳的倾向经常在微妙的国际谈判中造成尴尬局面。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pen-chant" — someone who "chants" about their "pen" has a strong liking for it. A penchant is a passionate preference.
把"penchant"想成"pen（笔）+ chant（吟唱）"——为一支笔吟唱的人一定对它有强烈的嗜好。
