# 📋 order

🔊 /ˈɔːrdər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A command or instruction; a sequence or arrangement; a formal request to purchase something. (v.) To give a command; to arrange systematically; to request a purchase
**中文：** n. 命令；顺序；规则；定单 v. 命令；整理；定购

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- command（命令）
- sequence（顺序）
- arrangement（排列）
- directive（指令）
- instruction（指示）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in order to / out of order / place an order / order of magnitude / law and order**

👉 Example: The manager placed an order for additional supplies to meet the growing demand.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee issued a formal **order** requiring all departments to submit their annual budget proposals by the end of March.
   委员会发布了一项正式命令，要求所有部门在三月底前提交年度预算提案。

2. The professor emphasized the importance of presenting arguments in a logical **order** to strengthen the overall thesis.
   教授强调了按逻辑顺序呈现论点对于加强整体论文的重要性。

3. The institution decided to **order** new laboratory equipment after the previous instruments proved insufficient for advanced research.
   在之前的仪器被证明不足以支持高级研究后，该机构决定订购新的实验室设备。

---

Think of "order" as "or-der" — "or there" will be chaos! Without order, everything falls apart.
把"order"想成"or-der"——"否则那里"就会混乱！没有秩序，一切都会崩溃。
