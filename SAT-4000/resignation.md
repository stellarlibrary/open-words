# 📝 resignation

🔊 /ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act of leaving a job or position voluntarily; passive acceptance of something undesirable but inevitable
**中文：** n. 辞职，让位，退职；屈从，服从，听任

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- departure（离职）
- abdication（放弃）
- acquiescence（默从）
- submission（顺从）
- acceptance（接受）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tender / submit / hand in one's resignation / resignation from a position / with resignation**

👉 Example: She submitted her resignation after discovering the company's unethical accounting practices.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The minister's **resignation** came as a surprise to colleagues who believed he still held considerable influence within the cabinet.
   部长的辞职令同僚们感到意外，他们认为他在内阁中仍拥有相当大的影响力。

2. There was a quiet **resignation** in her voice as she acknowledged that the funding proposal would not be approved this year.
   当她承认资助提案今年不会获批时，她的声音中带着一种平静的顺从。

3. Following the scandal, the board demanded the immediate **resignation** of the chief executive officer and two senior directors.
   丑闻发生后，董事会要求首席执行官和两名高级董事立即辞职。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-sign-ation" as "re-signing away" your position — you sign the paper again to officially give up your role.
把"resignation"想成"re-sign-ation"——重新签字交出你的职位，正式放弃你的角色。
