# 🔄 rationalize

🔊 /ˈræʃənəlaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To attempt to explain or justify behavior or attitudes with logical reasons, even if inappropriate; to make a process more efficient or logical
**中文：** v. 使……合理化；使……有理化；为……找借口

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- justify（为……辩护）
- excuse（为……找借口）
- explain away（解释掉）
- vindicate（证明……正当）
- streamline（使合理化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rationalize behavior / rationalize a decision / rationalize operations / rationalize away**

👉 Example: He tried to rationalize his poor performance by blaming external circumstances beyond his control.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Students often **rationalize** their procrastination by convincing themselves that they perform better under last-minute pressure.
   学生经常为自己的拖延找借口，说服自己在最后一刻的压力下表现更好。

2. The board voted to **rationalize** the company's supply chain operations in order to reduce costs and improve overall efficiency.
   董事会投票决定合理化公司的供应链运营，以降低成本并提高整体效率。

3. It is intellectually dishonest to **rationalize** unethical conduct by appealing to vague notions of the greater public good.
   通过诉诸模糊的公共利益概念来为不道德行为找借口，在学术上是不诚实的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rational-ize" — to make something sound "rational" even when it may not be, like dressing up a bad excuse in logical clothing.
把"rationalize"想成"rational（理性的）+ ize（使……化）"——把不合理的事情穿上理性的外衣。
