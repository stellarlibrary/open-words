# 🔮 inevitable

🔊 /ɪnˈevɪtəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Certain to happen; unavoidable; impossible to prevent or escape
**中文：** adj. 必然的；不可避免的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unavoidable（不可避免的）
- inescapable（逃不掉的）
- certain（必然的）
- predetermined（预先注定的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inevitable consequence / inevitable outcome / inevitable decline / seem inevitable / virtually inevitable**

👉 Example: Given the current trajectory, economic recession appears to be an inevitable consequence of these policies.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **inevitable** collapse of the aging infrastructure forced the city council to approve emergency funding for repairs.
   老化基础设施的**不可避免**的坍塌迫使市议会批准了紧急维修资金。

2. Many historians argue that the conflict was not **inevitable** but rather the result of deliberate political miscalculations.
   许多历史学家认为，这场冲突并非**不可避免**，而是蓄意的政治误判造成的。

3. Technological disruption is an **inevitable** feature of modern economies that requires adaptive regulatory frameworks and policies.
   技术颠覆是现代经济**不可避免**的特征，需要适应性的监管框架和政策。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-evit-able" as "not evitable (avoidable)" — something you cannot evade or escape, no matter how hard you try.
把"inevitable"拆成"in-evit-able"，即"不可逃避的"——无论你多努力都无法逃避的事情。
