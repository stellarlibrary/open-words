# 🌑 eclipse

🔊 /ɪˈklɪps/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The obscuring of one celestial body by another; a loss of significance or prominence. (v.) To obscure or surpass in importance or achievement
**中文：** n. 日食；月食；黯然失色 v. 使黯然失色；遮蔽；超越

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- overshadow（使相形见绌）
- surpass（超越）
- outshine（使逊色）
- obscure（遮蔽）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **eclipse the achievements of / solar eclipse / lunar eclipse / eclipse in popularity**

👉 Example: The young scientist's groundbreaking discovery eclipsed the work of her more experienced colleagues.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The rapid growth of digital media has **eclipsed** traditional print journalism in both reach and cultural influence.
   数字媒体的迅速发展在覆盖面和文化影响力上都使传统纸质新闻业黯然失色。

2. A total solar **eclipse** visible across the continent drew millions of spectators and generated unprecedented scientific interest.
   一场横跨整个大陆可见的日全食吸引了数百万观众，并引发了前所未有的科学兴趣。

3. Her remarkable achievements in international diplomacy **eclipsed** those of every predecessor who had held the position.
   她在国际外交方面的卓越成就超越了此前每一位担任该职位的前任。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of a solar eclipse where the moon blocks the sun — just as one thing "eclipses" another by overshadowing it completely.
想想日食时月亮遮住了太阳——就像一件事"eclipse"另一件事，完全将其遮蔽。
