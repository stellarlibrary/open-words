# 👑 successor

🔊 /səkˈsɛsər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person or thing that succeeds another; one who follows and takes the place of another, especially in an office or position
**中文：** n. 继承人，继任者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- heir（继承人）
- replacement（接替者）
- inheritor（继承者）
- descendant（后继者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **appoint a successor / name a successor / successor to the throne / worthy successor / immediate successor**

👉 Example: The retiring dean named her most accomplished colleague as her successor to lead the department.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board conducted a nationwide search to identify a suitable **successor** for the retiring chief executive of the organization.
   董事会在全国范围内进行搜寻，以确定该组织即将退休的首席执行官的合适继任者。

2. Many historians consider the younger statesman a worthy **successor** to the political legacy established by his distinguished predecessor.
   许多历史学家认为这位年轻的政治家是其杰出前任所建立的政治遗产的合格继承人。

3. The new software platform was designed as a **successor** to the outdated system that had served the institution for decades.
   新的软件平台被设计为已服务该机构数十年的过时系统的后继产品。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "successor" as "success-or" — the one who comes after a success, stepping into the role to continue the legacy.
把"successor"想成"success-or"——在成功之后到来的人，接手角色以延续遗产。
