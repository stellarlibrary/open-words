# 🗣️ jargon

🔊 /ˈdʒɑːrɡən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Special words or expressions used by a particular profession or group that are difficult for others to understand; technical terminology
**中文：** n. 行话，术语；专业用语

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- terminology（术语）
- lingo（行话）
- parlance（说法）
- vernacular（方言）
- argot（黑话）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **technical jargon / legal jargon / medical jargon / filled with jargon / avoid jargon**

👉 Example: The report was so filled with scientific jargon that most readers could barely comprehend a single paragraph.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The physician attempted to explain the diagnosis without relying on medical **jargon** so that the patient could fully understand.
   医生试图在不使用医学术语的情况下解释诊断结果，以便患者能够完全理解。

2. Effective academic writing should minimize unnecessary **jargon** and present complex ideas in clear, accessible language for broader audiences.
   有效的学术写作应尽量减少不必要的术语，用清晰易懂的语言向更广泛的读者呈现复杂的观点。

3. New employees often struggle to comprehend the corporate **jargon** that permeates internal memos, meetings, and official communications.
   新员工经常难以理解充斥在内部备忘录、会议和官方通讯中的企业行话。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "jargon" as "jar-gone" — the meaning has "gone into a jar," sealed away so that only insiders can open it and understand.
把"jargon"想成"jar-gone"——意思"跑进了罐子里"，被密封起来，只有圈内人才能打开并理解。
