# ⚡ discharge

🔊 /dɪsˈtʃɑːrdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To dismiss from employment or service; to unload cargo; to release or emit a substance; to allow to leave (e.g., a hospital). (n.) The act of releasing or emitting; official permission to leave
**中文：** v. 解雇；卸下；放出；准许离开；出院 n. 排放；准许离开

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- release（释放）
- dismiss（解雇）
- emit（排放）
- unload（卸下）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **discharge a patient / discharge duties / discharge waste / honorable discharge**

👉 Example: The hospital will discharge the patient once all follow-up tests confirm a full recovery.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The factory was fined for illegally **discharge**-ing toxic chemicals into the river, violating multiple environmental protection regulations.
   该工厂因非法向河流排放有毒化学物质、违反多项环保法规而被罚款。

2. After completing his military service with distinction, the soldier received an honorable **discharge** from the armed forces.
   在出色完成兵役后，这名士兵获得了光荣退伍的准许。

3. The physician decided to **discharge** the recovering patient on the condition that she attend weekly follow-up appointments.
   医生决定让正在康复的患者出院，条件是她每周参加随访预约。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-charge" — to remove a "charge" (responsibility, load, or electrical charge); releasing what was held or contained.
把"discharge"想成"去除(dis)+负荷(charge)"——移除责任、货物或电荷，即释放、排出或解雇。
