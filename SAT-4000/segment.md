# 🧩 segment

🔊 /ˈsɛɡmənt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A piece or section cut off or separated from something; a distinct part of something. (v.) To divide into separate parts or sections
**中文：** n. 弓形；部分

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- section（部分）
- portion（片段）
- fragment（碎片）
- division（分割）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **market segment / segment of the population / line segment / segment into groups**

👉 Example: The marketing team divided their audience into distinct segments based on demographic and behavioral data.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corporation restructured its operations by dividing the business into three distinct **segments** focused on different markets.
   该公司通过将业务划分为三个专注于不同市场的独立部分来重组其运营。

2. A significant **segment** of the electorate expressed deep dissatisfaction with both candidates in the national survey.
   在全国调查中，相当一部分选民对两位候选人都表示了深切的不满。

3. The engineer carefully **segmented** the data pipeline to isolate potential points of failure within the complex system.
   工程师仔细地将数据管道分割开来，以隔离复杂系统中潜在的故障点。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "seg-ment" as "separate-meant" — a segment is something meant to be separated from the whole.
把"segment"想成"separate-meant"——部分就是从整体中分离出来的东西。
