# ⚔️ inimical

🔊 /ɪˈnɪmɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Tending to obstruct or cause harm; hostile or unfriendly; adverse in tendency or effect
**中文：** adj. 敌意的；有害的；不利的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- hostile（敌对的）
- adverse（不利的）
- detrimental（有害的）
- antagonistic（对抗的）
- unfavorable（不利的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inimical to progress / inimical to growth / inimical to the interests of / inimical forces**

👉 Example: Corruption is fundamentally inimical to the development of a fair and just society.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board concluded that the proposed merger was **inimical** to the long-term interests of shareholders and employees alike.
   董事会得出结论，拟议中的合并不利于股东和员工的长期利益。

2. Prolonged exposure to polluted air creates conditions **inimical** to healthy respiratory function in both children and adults.
   长期暴露在受污染的空气中会造成不利于儿童和成人健康呼吸功能的环境。

3. The ambassador warned that such aggressive trade policies were **inimical** to maintaining peaceful diplomatic relations between the nations.
   大使警告说，这种激进的贸易政策不利于维护两国之间的和平外交关系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inimical" as related to "enemy" — both come from the Latin "inimicus" meaning hostile, so inimical describes something enemy-like.
把"inimical"与"enemy（敌人）"联系起来——两者都源自拉丁语"inimicus"，意为敌对的，所以inimical描述的是类似敌人的东西。
