# 😵 bewilder

🔊 /bɪˈwɪldər/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To confuse or perplex someone completely; to cause someone to feel disoriented or at a loss
**中文：** 使迷惑；使不知所措；使昏乱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- confuse（使困惑）
- perplex（使茫然）
- baffle（使困惑）
- mystify（使迷惑）
- disorient（使迷失方向）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bewilder someone / bewildered by something / utterly bewildered / a bewildering array of**

👉 Example: The sheer complexity of the legal document bewildered even the experienced attorneys.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's abstract theoretical arguments **bewildered** the majority of undergraduate students attending the advanced seminar.
   教授抽象的理论论证使参加高级研讨会的大多数本科生感到困惑不已。

2. Rapid technological advancements continue to **bewilder** consumers who struggle to keep pace with constant innovation.
   快速的技术进步继续使那些难以跟上持续创新步伐的消费者感到不知所措。

3. The contradictory witness testimonies **bewildered** the jury, making it exceedingly difficult to reach a unanimous verdict.
   相互矛盾的证人证词使陪审团迷惑不解，极难达成一致裁决。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "be-wilder" — to make someone feel as if they are lost in the wilderness, completely disoriented and confused.
把"bewilder"想成"be-wilder"——让某人感觉像在荒野中迷失一样，完全迷失方向和困惑。
