# 📉 scarcity

🔊 /ˈskɛrsɪti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The state of being in short supply; a lack or insufficiency of something
**中文：** n. 缺乏

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- shortage（短缺）
- deficiency（不足）
- dearth（缺乏）
- paucity（少量）
- insufficiency（不足）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **scarcity of resources / water scarcity / food scarcity / scarcity value / in times of scarcity**

👉 Example: The scarcity of clean drinking water remains one of the most urgent global challenges.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **scarcity** of affordable housing in major urban centers has forced many working families to relocate to distant suburban areas.
   主要城市中心经济适用房的匮乏迫使许多工薪家庭搬迁到遥远的郊区。

2. Economists argue that **scarcity** is the fundamental problem that drives all human decision-making regarding the allocation of limited resources.
   经济学家认为，稀缺是驱动人类在有限资源分配方面做出所有决策的根本问题。

3. The **scarcity** of qualified medical professionals in rural communities has led to significant disparities in healthcare access and outcomes.
   农村社区合格医疗专业人员的匮乏导致了医疗保健获取和结果方面的显著差距。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "scarcity" as "scare + city" — imagine a city so scared because everything is running out. When resources are scarce, people panic.
把"scarcity"想成"scare + city"——想象一个因为一切都快用完而恐慌的城市。当资源稀缺时，人们就会恐慌。
