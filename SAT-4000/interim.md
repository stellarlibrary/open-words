# ⏳ interim

🔊 /ˈɪntərɪm/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Temporary; serving during an intervening period. (n.) The intervening time; a transitional period
**中文：** adj. 临时的，暂时的；中间的 n. 过渡时期，中间时期

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- temporary（临时的）
- provisional（暂定的）
- transitional（过渡的）
- stopgap（权宜的）
- makeshift（临时凑合的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **interim report / interim government / interim measures / in the interim**

👉 Example: The board appointed an interim director while conducting a formal search for a permanent replacement.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The university appointed an **interim** president to oversee administrative affairs until a permanent candidate could be selected.
   大学任命了一位临时校长来监管行政事务，直到选出正式候选人。

2. In the **interim** between the two academic semesters, students were encouraged to pursue independent research projects abroad.
   在两个学期之间的过渡期内，学生们被鼓励到海外开展独立研究项目。

3. The government issued **interim** regulations to address the crisis while lawmakers debated a comprehensive legislative solution.
   政府在立法者讨论全面立法解决方案期间发布了临时法规以应对危机。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-ter-im" as "in the time in-between" — interim describes something that fills the gap between two permanent states.
把"interim"想成"in the time in-between"——interim描述的是填补两个永久状态之间空隙的事物。
