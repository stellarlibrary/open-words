# 🌆 gloaming

🔊 /ˈɡloʊmɪŋ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The time of day immediately following sunset; twilight or dusk
**中文：** n. 黄昏；薄暮；夕幕

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- twilight（黄昏）
- dusk（薄暮）
- nightfall（傍晚）
- eventide（黄昏时分）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in the gloaming / the gloaming hour / as the gloaming settled**

👉 Example: The couple walked hand in hand through the meadow in the soft gloaming light.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient castle appeared particularly majestic during the **gloaming**, its towers silhouetted against the fading sky.
   古堡在黄昏时分显得格外雄伟，塔楼在渐暗的天空中形成剪影。

2. Farmers traditionally returned from the fields in the **gloaming**, their long day of labor finally drawing to a close.
   农民们传统上在薄暮时分从田间归来，漫长的劳作终于接近尾声。

3. The poet captured the melancholy beauty of the **gloaming** in verses that resonated with readers for generations.
   诗人用诗句捕捉了黄昏的忧郁之美，引起了数代读者的共鸣。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gloaming" as "glowing + morning in reverse" — the sky glows softly, but it is the opposite of morning; it is evening.
把"gloaming"想成"glowing（发光）+ morning（早晨）的反面"——天空柔和地发光，但不是清晨，而是黄昏。
