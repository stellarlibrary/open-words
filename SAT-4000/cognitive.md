# 🧩 cognitive

🔊 /ˈkɒɡnɪtɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Relating to the mental processes of perception, memory, judgment, and reasoning; pertaining to cognition
**中文：** adj. 认知的；认识的；有感知的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- mental（心理的）
- intellectual（智力的）
- cerebral（大脑的）
- perceptual（感知的）
- rational（理性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cognitive ability / cognitive development / cognitive function / cognitive dissonance**

👉 Example: Research suggests that regular exercise can enhance cognitive function and delay age-related mental decline.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The study revealed that prolonged sleep deprivation significantly impairs **cognitive** performance in both attention and decision-making tasks.
   研究表明，长期睡眠不足会显著损害注意力和决策任务中的认知表现。

2. Early childhood education plays a critical role in **cognitive** development, shaping the foundation for lifelong learning abilities.
   幼儿教育在认知发展中起着关键作用，为终身学习能力奠定基础。

3. Psychologists introduced **cognitive** behavioral therapy as an effective treatment for patients suffering from chronic anxiety disorders.
   心理学家引入了认知行为疗法，作为治疗慢性焦虑症患者的有效方法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cognitive" as containing "cognit-" from Latin "cognoscere" (to know) — cognitive abilities are your "knowing" abilities, the brain's tools for understanding the world.
把"cognitive"想成包含拉丁语"cognoscere"（认识）——认知能力就是你"认识"世界的能力，是大脑理解世界的工具。
