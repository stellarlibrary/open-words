# 🖼️ impression

🔊 /ɪmˈpreʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** An idea, feeling, or opinion about something, especially one formed without conscious thought; an effect produced on someone; a mark or indentation made by pressing
**中文：** n. 印象；效果，影响；压痕，印记；感想

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- perception（感知）
- feeling（感觉）
- notion（概念）
- imprint（印记）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **make an impression / first impression / under the impression / leave an impression on**

👉 Example: The candidate's confident demeanor made a strong impression on all members of the interview panel.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's inaugural lecture left a lasting **impression** on the students, inspiring many to pursue advanced studies in the field.
   教授的就职演讲给学生们留下了深刻印象，激励许多人在该领域继续深造。

2. Fossil **impressions** preserved in the ancient rock layers provide invaluable evidence about organisms that existed millions of years ago.
   保存在古老岩层中的化石印痕为数百万年前存在的生物提供了宝贵的证据。

3. She was under the **impression** that the deadline had been extended, only to discover her application was submitted far too late.
   她以为截止日期已经延长，结果却发现她的申请提交得太晚了。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "IM-PRESS-ION" — when something "presses into" your mind, it leaves an impression, just like a stamp pressed into wax.
把"impression"想成"im（进入）+ press（按压）"——当某物按压进你的脑海中，就留下了印象，就像印章按入蜡封一样。
