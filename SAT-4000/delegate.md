# 🤝 delegate

🔊 /ˈdelɪɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To entrust a task or responsibility to another person; to appoint as a representative. (n.) A person designated to act for or represent another or others
**中文：** v. 委派…为代表；授权 n. 代表

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- representative（代表）
- envoy（使节）
- assign（委派）
- entrust（委托）
- deputy（副手）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **delegate authority / delegate responsibility / delegate tasks / a delegate to the conference / delegate from**

👉 Example: Effective leaders know when to delegate tasks to subordinates rather than attempting to manage everything alone.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The manager decided to **delegate** the supervision of daily operations to her most experienced and trusted team members.
   经理决定将日常运营的监督工作委派给她最有经验和信任的团队成员。

2. Each country sent a **delegate** to the international summit to negotiate the terms of the new trade agreement.
   每个国家都派出了一名代表参加国际峰会，以谈判新贸易协定的条款。

3. Learning to **delegate** effectively is essential for executives who wish to avoid burnout and maintain organizational productivity.
   学会有效地授权对于希望避免倦怠并保持组织生产力的高管来说至关重要。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-leg-ate" as "de- + leg (law/choose) + -ate" — to legally choose someone to act on your behalf, like sending a leg of your team forward.
把"delegate"想成"de- + leg（法律/选择）+ -ate"——合法地选择某人代表你行事，就像派出团队的一员向前走。
