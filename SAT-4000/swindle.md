# 🎭 swindle

🔊 /ˈswɪndl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A fraud; a scam. (v.) To cheat; to obtain by deception
**中文：** v. 诈骗

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- defraud（欺诈）
- cheat（欺骗）
- scam（诈骗）
- dupe（欺骗）
- fleece（敲诈）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **swindle someone out of / a massive swindle / swindle money from / fall victim to a swindle**

👉 Example: The con artist managed to swindle elderly investors out of their life savings through a fake charity scheme.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Federal investigators uncovered an elaborate **swindle** that defrauded thousands of investors of approximately fifty million dollars.
   联邦调查人员揭露了一个精心策划的骗局，使数千名投资者被骗走约五千万美元。

2. The corporation attempted to **swindle** local landowners by deliberately misrepresenting the environmental impact of the proposed development.
   该公司试图通过故意歪曲拟议开发项目的环境影响来欺诈当地土地所有者。

3. Historians regard the scheme as one of the most audacious financial **swindles** ever perpetrated upon an unsuspecting public.
   历史学家认为这一阴谋是对毫无防备的公众实施的最大胆的金融骗局之一。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "swindle" as "s-win-dle" — the swindler tries to "win" your money through a dishonest deal.
把"swindle"想成"s-win-dle"——骗子试图通过不诚实的交易来"赢得"你的钱。
