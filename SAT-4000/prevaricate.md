# 🐍 prevaricate

🔊 /prɪˈværɪkeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To speak or act in an evasive way; to avoid giving a direct or honest answer; to equivocate
**中文：** v. 搪塞；支吾其辞；闪烁其辞；说谎

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- equivocate（含糊其辞）
- hedge（闪避）
- evade（回避）
- dissemble（掩饰）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prevaricate about / prevaricate on / stop prevaricating / tend to prevaricate**

👉 Example: The senator continued to prevaricate when asked directly about his involvement in the financial scandal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The executive chose to **prevaricate** during the congressional hearing rather than provide straightforward answers about the company's practices.
   这位高管在国会听证会上选择搪塞，而不是就公司的做法提供直截了当的回答。

2. When confronted with evidence of academic dishonesty, the student began to **prevaricate**, offering vague and contradictory explanations.
   当面对学术不端的证据时，这名学生开始支吾其辞，给出模糊且自相矛盾的解释。

3. Diplomats are often trained to **prevaricate** skillfully, revealing minimal information while appearing to engage in open dialogue.
   外交官通常接受过巧妙搪塞的训练，在看似进行公开对话的同时透露最少的信息。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-VARI-cate" as "varying" your story — when you prevaricate, you keep varying and changing what you say to avoid the truth.
把"prevaricate"想成"pre-VARI-cate"——当你搪塞时，你不断"vary（变换）"你的说辞来回避真相。
