# 🏛️ tenure

🔊 /ˈtɛnjər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The holding of an office or position; the period during which something is held; guaranteed permanent employment. (v.) To grant a permanent position to.
**中文：** n. 任期；占有；终身职位 v. 授予终身职位

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- incumbency（任期）
- term（期限）
- occupation（占有）
- holding（持有）
- permanence（永久性）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tenure as / tenure at / tenure track / grant tenure / during one's tenure**

👉 Example: During her tenure as department chair, the university expanded its research programs considerably.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor was granted **tenure** after publishing extensively in peer-reviewed journals and demonstrating exceptional dedication to teaching.
   这位教授在同行评审期刊上广泛发表论文并展现出对教学的非凡奉献后，被授予了终身职位。

2. During his **tenure** as chief executive, the corporation underwent a dramatic transformation in both strategy and corporate culture.
   在他担任首席执行官的任期内，该公司在战略和企业文化方面都经历了巨大转变。

3. The committee voted unanimously to **tenure** the young researcher whose groundbreaking work had attracted international recognition and funding.
   委员会一致投票决定授予这位年轻研究员终身职位，其开创性工作已获得国际认可和资助。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tenure" as "ten + sure" — after ten years of hard work, you are sure to keep your position permanently.
把"tenure"想成"ten（十）+ sure（确定）"——经过十年的努力工作，你肯定能永久保住你的职位。
