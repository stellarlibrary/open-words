# ⚠️ sedition

🔊 /sɪˈdɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An uprising; inflammatory speech or conduct; disturbance of public order
**中文：** n. 煽动，暴乱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- insurrection（起义）
- rebellion（叛乱）
- subversion（颠覆）
- mutiny（兵变）
- agitation（煽动）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **charged with sedition / acts of sedition / sedition laws / incite sedition**

👉 Example: The activist was charged with sedition after publicly urging citizens to resist the government's decrees.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government passed strict **sedition** laws to suppress dissent and prevent organized opposition from gaining popular support.
   政府通过了严格的煽动法来压制异议，防止有组织的反对派获得民众支持。

2. Several journalists were accused of **sedition** for publishing articles that criticized the military regime's authoritarian policies.
   几名记者因发表批评军事政权专制政策的文章而被指控犯有煽动罪。

3. Throughout history, those who advocated for reform were frequently branded with charges of **sedition** by ruling authorities.
   纵观历史，那些倡导改革的人经常被当权者冠以煽动叛乱的罪名。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sed-ition" — sounds like "said it on" (the streets). Sedition is when dangerous words are said to incite public unrest.
把"sedition"想成"said it on（在街上说）"——煽动就是说出危险的话来激起公众骚乱。
