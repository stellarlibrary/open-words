# ✅ appropriate

🔊 /əˈproʊpriət/ (adj.) · /əˈproʊpriˌeɪt/ (v.)<br>
🏷️ *adjective, verb | 形容词，动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** (adj.) Suitable or proper in the circumstances. (v.) To take possession of something, especially without permission; to set aside for a specific purpose.

**中文：** (adj.) 适当的；恰当的；合适的。(v.) 占用；挪用；拨出（款项等）。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- suitable（合适的）
- fitting（恰当的）
- proper（适宜的）
- allocate（拨出，动词义）
- seize（占用，动词义）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Collocations:** appropriate for, appropriate to, appropriate funds, appropriate behavior, culturally appropriate, deem appropriate

**Example:** The committee voted to appropriate additional funds for the education initiative.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee determined that formal attire was the most **appropriate** dress code for the ceremony.
   （委员会认定正式着装是该典礼最合适的着装要求。）

2. The government decided to **appropriate** significant funds for rebuilding infrastructure damaged by the recent disaster.
   （政府决定拨出大量资金用于重建近期灾害中受损的基础设施。）

3. It is never **appropriate** to use confidential information for personal advantage in professional settings.
   （在职业环境中，利用机密信息谋取个人利益永远是不恰当的。）

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Appropriate" = "ap-" (to) + "propri-" (own, proper). As an adjective, it means "proper"; as a verb, it means "to make one's own" — to take or allocate.

"Appropriate" = "ap-"（朝向）+ "propri-"（自己的、适当的）。作形容词意为"适当的"；作动词意为"据为己有"——占用或拨出。
