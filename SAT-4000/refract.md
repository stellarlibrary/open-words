# 🌈 refract

🔊 /rɪˈfrækt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause light, sound, or other waves to change direction when passing from one medium to another; to bend or deflect
**中文：** v. 使折射

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bend（弯折）
- deflect（偏转）
- diffract（衍射）
- divert（使偏转）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **refract light / refract through / refracted by / refract a beam**

👉 Example: A prism can refract white light into a brilliant spectrum of individual colors.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The glass lens was carefully designed to **refract** incoming light rays and focus them precisely onto the sensor surface.
   该玻璃透镜经过精心设计，将入射光线折射并精确聚焦到传感器表面上。

2. Water droplets suspended in the atmosphere **refract** sunlight at specific angles, producing the characteristic arc of a rainbow.
   悬浮在大气中的水滴以特定角度折射阳光，产生彩虹的特征弧形。

3. The researcher demonstrated how different crystalline structures **refract** electromagnetic waves in measurably distinct and predictable patterns.
   研究人员演示了不同的晶体结构如何以可测量的、独特的和可预测的模式折射电磁波。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-fract" as "re-fracture" — light gets "fractured" or broken into a new direction when it passes through a different medium.
把"refract"想成"re-fracture"（重新折断）——光在通过不同介质时被"折断"到新的方向。
