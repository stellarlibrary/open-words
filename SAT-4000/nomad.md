# 🏕️ nomad

🔊 /ˈnoʊmæd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who moves from place to place without a permanent home, especially as a way of life; a wanderer. (adj.) Relating to a nomadic lifestyle
**中文：** n. 游牧民；流浪者 adj. 游牧的；流浪的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- wanderer（流浪者）
- rover（漫游者）
- itinerant（巡回者）
- drifter（漂泊者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **desert nomad / digital nomad / nomad lifestyle / nomad tribes**

👉 Example: Many young professionals now embrace the digital nomad lifestyle, working remotely from various countries.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **nomad** tribes of Central Asia have traversed the vast steppes with their herds for countless generations.
   中亚的游牧部落带着他们的畜群穿越广袤的草原已有无数代人。

2. As a modern **nomad**, she carried only a laptop and a backpack while traveling across three continents last year.
   作为一名现代流浪者，她去年仅带着一台笔记本电脑和一个背包便游历了三大洲。

3. Archaeological evidence suggests that early **nomad** societies developed sophisticated trade networks spanning thousands of miles.
   考古证据表明，早期游牧社会建立了横跨数千英里的复杂贸易网络。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "no-mad" as "no ma'am, I don't have a fixed address" — a nomad has no permanent home and keeps moving.
把"nomad"想成"no ma'am（不，女士）"——游牧民没有固定地址，一直在迁移。
