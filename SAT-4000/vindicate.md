# ⚖️ vindicate

🔊 /ˈvɪndɪkeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To clear someone of blame or suspicion; to show or prove to be right, reasonable, or justified
**中文：** v. 为…辩护；证明…无辜；证明…正确

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- exonerate（证明无罪）
- justify（证明正当）
- absolve（赦免）
- acquit（宣判无罪）
- defend（辩护）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vindicate someone's honor / vindicate a decision / feel vindicated / vindicate one's rights / fully vindicated**

👉 Example: The new evidence completely vindicated the defendant, proving his innocence beyond any doubt.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The Supreme Court ruling served to **vindicate** the constitutional rights of citizens who had been subjected to unlawful surveillance.
   最高法院的裁决为遭受非法监控的公民的宪法权利进行了维护。

2. After years of criticism, the researcher felt **vindicated** when independent studies confirmed the accuracy of her original findings.
   经过多年批评后，当独立研究证实了她原始发现的准确性时，这位研究人员感到得到了证明。

3. The investigation's final report was expected to **vindicate** the officials who had been falsely accused of financial misconduct.
   调查的最终报告预计将为那些被错误指控财务不当行为的官员证明清白。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vindicate" as "win-dicate" — when you are vindicated, you win your case and your innocence is declared for all to see.
把"vindicate"想成"win-dicate"——当你被证明无辜时，你赢得了你的案件，你的清白被公之于众。
