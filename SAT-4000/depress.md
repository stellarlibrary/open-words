# 😞 depress

🔊 /dɪˈprɛs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make someone feel sad or dispirited; to push down or lower; to reduce the level of economic activity or prices
**中文：** v. 使沮丧；压抑；降低；使萧条

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dishearten（使沮丧）
- suppress（压制）
- dampen（抑制）
- lower（降低）
- discourage（使气馁）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **depress the economy / depress prices / depress a lever / depress someone's spirits**

👉 Example: The prolonged recession continued to depress wages and consumer spending across the region.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Persistent unemployment and stagnant wages tend to **depress** consumer confidence and overall economic growth significantly.
   持续的失业和停滞的工资往往会严重压低消费者信心和整体经济增长。

2. The physician explained that chronic isolation can **depress** the immune system, making individuals more susceptible to illness.
   医生解释说，长期的孤立会抑制免疫系统，使人更容易患病。

3. To activate the emergency brake, firmly **depress** the pedal until you feel resistance and the vehicle comes to a stop.
   要启动紧急制动，请用力踩下踏板，直到感觉到阻力且车辆完全停止。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-press" as "press down" — whether pressing down emotions, prices, or a physical button, it all means pushing something lower.
把"depress"想成"de + press（按压）"——无论是压低情绪、价格还是按下按钮，都意味着向下推压。
