# 📋 term

🔊 /tɜːrm/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A word or phrase used to describe something; a fixed period of time; a condition or stipulation; a mathematical quantity. (v.) To call by a particular name or designation.
**中文：** n. 术语；学期；期限；条款；项 v. 把…叫做

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- expression（表达）
- period（期间）
- condition（条件）
- designation（名称）
- stipulation（规定）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in terms of / long-term / short-term / term paper / terms and conditions / come to terms with**

👉 Example: In terms of academic performance, the university has consistently ranked among the top institutions worldwide.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The contract clearly outlined the **terms** under which both parties agreed to share intellectual property rights and financial responsibilities.
   合同清楚地列出了双方同意分享知识产权和财务责任的条款。

2. Scientists coined a new **term** to describe the phenomenon observed when atmospheric pressure drops rapidly before a major storm system develops.
   科学家们创造了一个新术语来描述在主要风暴系统形成前大气压急剧下降时观察到的现象。

3. The president served two consecutive **terms** in office, during which significant reforms in education and healthcare were successfully implemented.
   总统连续任职两届，在此期间成功实施了教育和医疗方面的重大改革。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "term" as a "terminal point" — every term has a defined beginning and end, whether it is a school term, a prison term, or a contract term.
把"term"想成"terminal point（终端点）"——每个term都有明确的开始和结束，无论是学期、刑期还是合同期限。
