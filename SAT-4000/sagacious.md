# 🦉 sagacious

🔊 /səˈɡeɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Wise, shrewd, and far-sighted; having keen discernment and sound judgment
**中文：** adj. 有洞察力的，有远见的；精明的，敏锐的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- wise（睿智的）
- shrewd（精明的）
- astute（敏锐的）
- discerning（有洞察力的）
- perspicacious（敏锐的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sagacious leader / sagacious advice / sagacious decision / sagacious mind**

👉 Example: The sagacious investor foresaw the market downturn and adjusted her portfolio accordingly.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **sagacious** elder provided counsel that steered the community through a period of unprecedented crisis.
   这位睿智的长者提供的建议引导社区度过了一段前所未有的危机时期。

2. Her **sagacious** interpretation of the data allowed the research team to identify a critical pattern others had overlooked.
   她对数据的敏锐解读使研究团队发现了其他人忽略的关键模式。

3. A **sagacious** diplomat recognizes when compromise serves the greater interest better than rigid adherence to principle.
   一位精明的外交官明白何时妥协比固守原则更能服务于大局。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "saga-cious" — a person who is sagacious has the wisdom of someone who has lived through many sagas and learned from them.
把"sagacious"想成"saga（传奇）+ cious"——一个睿智的人就像经历过许多传奇故事并从中汲取经验的人。
