# 🚧 hinder

🔊 /ˈhɪndər/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To create difficulty for someone or something, resulting in delay or obstruction; to impede progress. (adj.) Situated at the back; rear
**中文：** v. 阻止；妨碍；阻碍 adj. 后面的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- impede（妨碍）
- obstruct（阻挡）
- hamper（阻碍）
- inhibit（抑制）
- thwart（阻挠）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **hinder progress / hinder development / hinder growth / hinder efforts / hinder someone from doing something**

👉 Example: Bureaucratic regulations often hinder small businesses from expanding their operations efficiently.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Severe budget constraints continued to **hinder** the department's ability to recruit qualified researchers and maintain essential laboratory equipment throughout the year.
   严重的预算限制继续阻碍该部门全年招募合格研究人员和维护必要实验室设备的能力。

2. The committee warned that excessive government intervention could **hinder** innovation and ultimately slow down technological advancement in the private sector.
   委员会警告说，过度的政府干预可能会阻碍创新，并最终减缓私营部门的技术进步。

3. Poor infrastructure in rural regions significantly **hindered** the delivery of essential medical supplies to communities most desperately in need of assistance.
   农村地区糟糕的基础设施严重阻碍了向最迫切需要援助的社区运送基本医疗物资。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "hinder" sounding like "behind" — something that holds you behind, keeps you from moving forward, blocking your path.
"hinder"听起来像"behind"（在后面）——某样东西把你拖在后面，阻止你前进，挡住你的路。
