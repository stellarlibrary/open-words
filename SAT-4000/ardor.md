# 🔥 ardor

🔊 /ˈɑːrdər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Intense passion, enthusiasm, or zeal; a burning warmth of feeling or emotion
**中文：** n. 热情；狂热；灼热

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- passion（热情）
- fervor（热忱）
- zeal（热心）
- enthusiasm（热情）
- intensity（强烈）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **with great ardor / youthful ardor / revolutionary ardor / diminish one's ardor / patriotic ardor**

👉 Example: The young activists pursued their cause with an ardor that impressed even their most skeptical critics.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The poet wrote with such **ardor** that his verses captured the raw emotional turbulence of the revolutionary period.
   这位诗人以如此炽热的激情写作，他的诗句捕捉到了革命时期原始的情感动荡。

2. Her **ardor** for social justice never diminished, even after decades of setbacks and institutional resistance to reform.
   她对社会正义的热情从未减退，即使在经历了数十年的挫折和制度性改革阻力之后。

3. The general's fiery speech rekindled the **ardor** of the weary soldiers and restored their determination to continue fighting.
   将军慷慨激昂的演讲重新点燃了疲惫士兵们的热情，恢复了他们继续战斗的决心。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ardor" — it comes from Latin "ardere" meaning "to burn." Ardor is the burning fire of passion and enthusiasm inside someone.
"Ardor"来自拉丁语"ardere"，意思是"燃烧"。Ardor就是一个人内心燃烧着的激情和热忱之火。
