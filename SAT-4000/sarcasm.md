# 😏 sarcasm

🔊 /ˈsɑːrkæzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The use of irony to mock or convey contempt; a sharp, bitter, or cutting expression or remark
**中文：** n. 挖苦，讽刺

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- irony（反讽）
- mockery（嘲弄）
- derision（嘲笑）
- ridicule（讥笑）
- cynicism（讽世）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dripping with sarcasm / a note of sarcasm / biting sarcasm / heavy sarcasm**

👉 Example: Her voice was dripping with sarcasm when she congratulated him on his "brilliant" decision.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's biting **sarcasm** discouraged students from asking questions they feared might be considered too elementary.
   教授尖刻的讽刺使学生们不敢提出他们担心会被认为太基础的问题。

2. His reply was laced with **sarcasm**, suggesting that he did not genuinely appreciate the committee's unsolicited recommendations.
   他的回答充满了挖苦，暗示他并不真正感激委员会主动提出的建议。

3. Political commentators often rely on **sarcasm** to expose the contradictions and hypocrisy embedded in official government statements.
   政治评论员经常依靠讽刺来揭露官方政府声明中隐含的矛盾和虚伪。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sarc-asm" — from Greek "sarkazein" meaning to tear flesh. Sarcasm tears at someone with sharp, mocking words that cut like a knife.
把"sarcasm"想成来自希腊语"sarkazein"（撕裂肉体）——讽刺就是用尖锐的嘲弄之词撕裂对方，像刀一样锋利。
