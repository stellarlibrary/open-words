# 🏅 accredit

🔊 /əˈkrɛd.ɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To officially authorize, recognize, or attribute something to someone; to send with credentials.
**中文：** 授权；信任；委派；归因于

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- authorize（授权）
- certify（认证）
- endorse（认可）
- attribute（归因于）
- commission（委任）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **accredit someone to (a post); accredit something to someone; accredited by (an organization)**

👉 Example: The university is accredited by the national board of education.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government **accredited** the ambassador to the United Nations to represent national interests abroad.
   政府委派该大使驻联合国，代表国家利益。

2. Only programs **accredited** by the professional council are eligible to confer recognized degrees.
   只有经过专业委员会认证的项目才有资格授予被认可的学位。

3. Historians **accredit** the invention of the printing press to Johannes Gutenberg in the fifteenth century.
   历史学家将活字印刷术的发明归功于十五世纪的约翰内斯·古腾堡。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think "ac-CREDIT" — to give someone credit or credentials, officially recognizing their authority or achievement.
accredit 中有 credit（信用），给予某人"信用"就是"授权、认证"的意思。
