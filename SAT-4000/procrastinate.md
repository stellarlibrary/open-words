# ⏳ procrastinate

🔊 /prəˈkræstɪneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To delay or postpone action; to put off doing something, especially out of laziness or habitual carelessness
**中文：** v. 耽搁，延迟；拖延

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- delay（延迟）
- postpone（推迟）
- defer（拖延）
- stall（拖住）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **procrastinate on / stop procrastinating / tend to procrastinate / procrastinate until**

👉 Example: Students who procrastinate on their assignments often produce work of noticeably lower quality.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Many students tend to **procrastinate** on research papers until the deadline is only a few days away.
   许多学生往往拖延研究论文，直到截止日期只剩几天。

2. The board members chose to **procrastinate** on the critical decision, hoping that market conditions would eventually improve.
   董事会成员选择拖延这一关键决策，希望市场状况最终会改善。

3. If you continually **procrastinate** instead of addressing urgent problems, the consequences will inevitably become far more severe.
   如果你持续拖延而不解决紧迫问题，后果将不可避免地变得更加严重。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pro-crastin-ate" as "for (pro) tomorrow (crastinus)" — you keep pushing tasks to tomorrow, always delaying.
把"procrastinate"想成"pro（为了）+ crastinus（明天）"——总是把事情推到明天，不断拖延。
