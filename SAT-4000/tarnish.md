# 🪞 tarnish

🔊 /ˈtɑːrnɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Dullness of color or luster; a stain or blemish on reputation. (v.) To make or become less bright; to damage or spoil a reputation
**中文：** v. 失去光泽

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- blemish（玷污）
- sully（损害）
- stain（污染）
- taint（败坏）
- diminish（减损）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tarnish a reputation / tarnish an image / tarnish one's legacy / tarnished silver**

👉 Example: The scandal threatened to tarnish the senator's previously unblemished political career.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corruption allegations **tarnished** the organization's reputation and led to a significant decline in public donations.
   腐败指控玷污了该组织的声誉，导致公众捐款大幅下降。

2. Exposure to humid air will gradually **tarnish** silver jewelry, leaving a dull and discolored surface over time.
   暴露在潮湿空气中会逐渐使银首饰失去光泽，随着时间推移留下暗淡变色的表面。

3. No amount of subsequent philanthropy could fully **tarnish** the legacy of exploitation upon which the fortune was built.
   再多的后续慈善事业也无法完全抹去建立在剥削基础上的财富遗产的污点。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tar-nish" — tar is dark and sticky, so when something is "tar-nished," it is covered in darkness and loses its shine.
把"tarnish"想成"tar-nish"——焦油(tar)是黑暗而粘稠的，当某物被"tarnish"时，就像被焦油覆盖，失去了光泽。
