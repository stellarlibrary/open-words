# 🔄 inveterate

🔊 /ɪnˈvɛtərɪt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having a particular habit, activity, or interest that is long-established and unlikely to change; deeply rooted and firmly established
**中文：** adj. 根深蒂固的；积习的；成癖的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- habitual（习惯性的）
- chronic（长期的）
- ingrained（根深蒂固的）
- deep-rooted（根深蒂固的）
- incorrigible（不可救药的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inveterate liar / inveterate gambler / inveterate habit / inveterate optimist / inveterate hostility**

👉 Example: He was an inveterate collector of rare manuscripts and first editions from around the world.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prosecutor portrayed the defendant as an **inveterate** fraudster who had deceived investors for over two decades without remorse.
   检察官将被告描绘成一个根深蒂固的骗子，二十多年来一直毫无悔意地欺骗投资者。

2. Despite numerous attempts at reform, the **inveterate** corruption within the bureaucracy continued to undermine public trust in government.
   尽管进行了多次改革尝试，官僚机构中根深蒂固的腐败仍在继续削弱公众对政府的信任。

3. An **inveterate** traveler since her youth, she had visited over sixty countries before reaching the age of forty.
   她从年轻时起就是一个积习难改的旅行者，在四十岁之前已经访问了六十多个国家。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inveterate" as "in + veteran" — like a veteran soldier, an inveterate habit has been there so long it is deeply entrenched.
把"inveterate"想成"in + veteran（老兵）"——就像一个久经沙场的老兵，inveterate的习惯根深蒂固，难以改变。
