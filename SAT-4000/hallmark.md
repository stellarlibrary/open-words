# 🏅 hallmark

🔊 /ˈhɔːlmɑːrk/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A distinguishing characteristic or feature; an official mark stamped on precious metals to certify quality
**中文：** n. 特点；品质证明；检验标记

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- trademark（标志）
- characteristic（特征）
- emblem（象征）
- indicator（标志）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **the hallmark of / a hallmark feature / bear the hallmark of / hallmark of excellence**

👉 Example: Precision and clarity are the hallmarks of effective scientific writing.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The meticulous attention to detail has long been considered the **hallmark** of this prestigious architectural firm.
   对细节的精心关注长期以来一直被视为这家著名建筑事务所的标志。

2. Innovation and adaptability are the **hallmarks** of companies that consistently outperform their competitors in global markets.
   创新和适应能力是那些在全球市场上持续超越竞争对手的公司的显著特点。

3. The professor noted that rigorous methodology is the essential **hallmark** of credible and reproducible academic research.
   这位教授指出，严谨的方法论是可信且可重复的学术研究的基本特征。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "hallmark" as a "mark in the hall" — a stamp or seal displayed in a grand hall to prove quality and authenticity.
把"hallmark"想成"大厅里的标记"——展示在大厅中的印章或标志，用来证明品质和真实性。
