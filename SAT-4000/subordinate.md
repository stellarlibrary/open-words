# 👤 subordinate

🔊 /səˈbɔːrdɪnət/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective, verb | 名词, 形容词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person under the authority or control of another. (adj.) Lower in rank or position; of lesser importance. (v.) To place in a lower rank or to make subservient
**中文：** adj. 次要的，从属的；下级的 n. 部属，下级

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- inferior（下级的）
- secondary（次要的）
- underling（下属）
- subservient（从属的）
- ancillary（辅助的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **subordinate to / subordinate role / subordinate clause / subordinate position**

👉 Example: In a well-run organization, subordinate employees often defer to senior management on strategic decisions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The manager instructed her **subordinate** to prepare the quarterly financial report before the Friday deadline.
   经理指示她的下属在周五截止日期前准备好季度财务报告。

2. Personal interests must be **subordinate** to the collective welfare of the institution during times of crisis.
   在危机时期，个人利益必须从属于机构的集体利益。

3. The committee voted to **subordinate** aesthetic considerations to practical safety requirements in the building design.
   委员会投票决定在建筑设计中将美学考虑置于实际安全要求之下。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sub-ordinate" as "sub" (below) + "ordinate" (order) — someone placed below in the order of rank.
把"subordinate"想成"sub"（在下面）+"ordinate"（等级）——在等级排列中被放在下面的人。
