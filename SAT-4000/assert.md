# 💪 assert

🔊 /əˈsɜːrt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To state or declare something firmly and confidently; to defend or maintain one's rights or position.
**中文：** v. 维护，坚持；断言；主张

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- declare（宣称）
- affirm（肯定）
- maintain（坚持）
- contend（主张）
- insist（坚持）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use **assert** when stating something with confidence or defending a position. Common collocations: "assert one's rights," "assert authority," "assert that..." followed by a clause.

👉 Example: The lawyer asserted that her client was innocent of all charges.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The defendant continued to **assert** his innocence despite the overwhelming circumstantial evidence presented against him in court.
   尽管法庭上提出了压倒性的间接证据，被告仍继续断言自己是无辜的。

2. Citizens must learn to **assert** their constitutional rights when faced with unjust treatment by governmental authorities.
   公民在面对政府当局的不公正对待时，必须学会维护自己的宪法权利。

3. The research team **asserted** that their experimental findings would fundamentally transform modern approaches to renewable energy.
   研究团队断言，他们的实验发现将从根本上改变现代可再生能源的研究方法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "assert" as "a + s(trong) + sert(ain)" — to assert is to be strong and certain about what you say. You insert your strong opinion firmly.
把"assert"想成"a + s(trong) + sert(ain)"——assert就是对你所说的话坚定而确信。你坚定地插入你的强烈观点。
