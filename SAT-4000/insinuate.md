# 🌀 insinuate

🔊 /ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To suggest or hint at something indirectly and often unpleasantly; to maneuver oneself into a position gradually and subtly
**中文：** v. 暗示；含沙射影地说；使逐渐而巧妙地取得；使迂回地潜入

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- imply（暗示）
- hint（暗指）
- suggest（暗示）
- intimate（暗示）
- allude（影射）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **insinuate that / insinuate oneself into / insinuate something about**

👉 Example: The article seemed to insinuate that the official had accepted bribes, though it never stated so directly.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The opposition leader attempted to **insinuate** that the administration had deliberately concealed critical information from the public.
   反对派领袖试图含沙射影地暗示政府故意向公众隐瞒了关键信息。

2. Over several months, the ambitious advisor managed to **insinuate** himself into the inner circle of the company's executive board.
   在几个月的时间里，这位雄心勃勃的顾问设法巧妙地挤进了公司董事会的核心圈子。

3. The reviewer appeared to **insinuate** that the author had borrowed ideas without proper attribution in the published manuscript.
   评审人似乎在暗示作者在已发表的手稿中借用了他人的观点而未适当注明出处。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in + sinuate" (from Latin "sinuare," to curve) — to curve your way in, sliding into a place or planting an idea indirectly.
把"insinuate"想成"in + sinuate"（源自拉丁语"sinuare"，弯曲）——弯弯绕绕地潜入，迂回地进入或间接地暗示。
