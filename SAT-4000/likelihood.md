# 🎯 likelihood

🔊 /ˈlaɪklihʊd/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The state or fact of something being probable; the chance that something will happen.
**中文：** n. 可能性，可能

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- probability（概率）
- chance（机会）
- possibility（可能性）
- prospect（前景）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in all likelihood / the likelihood of / increase the likelihood / reduce the likelihood**

👉 Example: In all likelihood, the proposed legislation will be approved by the committee next month.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Statistical models indicate that the **likelihood** of a significant economic downturn has increased substantially over the past quarter.
   统计模型表明，过去一个季度发生严重经济衰退的可能性大幅增加。

2. The implementation of stricter safety protocols will greatly reduce the **likelihood** of workplace accidents in industrial facilities.
   实施更严格的安全规程将大大降低工业设施中发生工作场所事故的可能性。

3. In all **likelihood**, the archaeological team will uncover additional artifacts when excavation resumes in the spring season.
   在所有可能性中，考古队在春季恢复发掘时很可能会发现更多文物。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "likely + hood" — the "hood" (state) of being "likely," meaning how probable something is.
把"likelihood"拆分为"likely（可能的）+ hood（状态）"——处于"可能"的状态，即某事发生的概率。
