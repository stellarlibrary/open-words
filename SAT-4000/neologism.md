# 🆕 neologism

🔊 /niˈɒlədʒɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A newly coined word or expression; a new meaning assigned to an existing word
**中文：** n. 新词；新语；新义；旧词新义

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- coinage（新造词）
- new term（新术语）
- innovation（创新用语）
- nonce word（临时造词）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **coin a neologism / linguistic neologism / introduce a neologism / adopt a neologism**

👉 Example: The rapid evolution of technology has produced countless neologisms that quickly enter everyday language.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The word "selfie" is a modern **neologism** that originated from social media culture and entered formal dictionaries remarkably quickly.
   "自拍"一词是一个源自社交媒体文化的现代新词，极快地被正式词典收录。

2. Shakespeare was a prolific inventor of **neologisms**, contributing hundreds of words that remain in common usage today.
   莎士比亚是一位多产的新词创造者，贡献了数百个至今仍在常用的词汇。

3. Linguists carefully study **neologisms** to understand how cultural shifts and technological advances reshape human communication patterns.
   语言学家仔细研究新词，以了解文化变迁和技术进步如何重塑人类的交流方式。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "neo" (new) + "log" (word, from Greek logos) + "ism" — a new word that becomes part of the language system.
把"neologism"拆分为"neo"(新) + "log"(词，源自希腊语logos) + "ism"——一个成为语言系统一部分的新词。
