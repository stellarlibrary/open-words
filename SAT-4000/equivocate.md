# 🌀 equivocate

🔊 /ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To use ambiguous or unclear language in order to avoid committing to a position or to mislead
**中文：** v. 说模棱两可的话；含糊其词

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- hedge（回避）
- prevaricate（支吾搪塞）
- waffle（含糊其词）
- dodge（闪避）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **equivocate on / equivocate about / refuse to equivocate / continue to equivocate**

👉 Example: The executive continued to equivocate when asked directly about the company's financial losses.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician chose to **equivocate** rather than provide a straightforward answer to the journalist's pointed question about tax reform.
   这位政治家选择含糊其词，而不是对记者关于税制改革的尖锐问题给出直接回答。

2. When pressed about the safety data, the pharmaceutical spokesperson began to **equivocate**, raising serious concerns among regulatory officials.
   当被追问安全数据时，制药公司发言人开始说模棱两可的话，引起了监管官员的严重关切。

3. A trustworthy leader does not **equivocate** on matters of principle but communicates decisions with clarity and unwavering conviction.
   一位值得信赖的领导者不会在原则问题上含糊其词，而是以清晰和坚定的信念传达决策。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "equivocate" as speaking with "equal voices" — saying one thing and its opposite at the same time, so nobody knows what you really mean.
把"equivocate"想成用"相等的声音"说话——同时说一件事和它的反面，这样没人知道你到底是什么意思。
