# 🔍 appraise

🔊 /əˈpreɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To assess the value, quality, or nature of something; to evaluate or estimate the worth of property, goods, or performance
**中文：** 评价，鉴定；估价

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- evaluate（评估）
- assess（评定）
- estimate（估算）
- gauge（衡量）
- valuate（估价）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **appraise the value / appraise a property / appraise performance / appraise the situation**

👉 Example: The insurance company sent an expert to appraise the damage caused by the flood.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The auction house hired a specialist to **appraise** the rare painting before presenting it to prospective international buyers.
   拍卖行聘请了一位专家在向潜在的国际买家展示这幅珍稀画作之前对其进行鉴定。

2. Managers are required to **appraise** employee performance annually using standardized criteria approved by the human resources department.
   管理人员须每年使用人力资源部门批准的标准化指标来评估员工绩效。

3. The jeweler carefully **appraised** the antique necklace and confirmed that its gemstones were authentic and historically significant.
   珠宝商仔细鉴定了这条古董项链，确认其宝石是真品且具有历史意义。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "appraise" as "a-PRAISE after evaluation" — before you can praise something, you must first appraise its true value.
把"appraise"想成"a-PRAISE after evaluation"——在赞美某物之前，你必须先评估它的真正价值。
