# 🚀 exceed

🔊 /ɪkˈsiːd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To go beyond a set limit, measure, or expectation; to be greater than or superior to
**中文：** v. 超过，胜过

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- surpass（超越）
- outstrip（超过）
- transcend（超越）
- outdo（胜过）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **exceed expectations / exceed the limit / exceed the budget / exceed in quality**

👉 Example: The company's annual revenue exceeded analysts' predictions by a substantial margin.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The student's final thesis **exceeded** all expectations, earning the highest distinction awarded by the department in over a decade.
   该学生的毕业论文超出了所有人的期望，获得了该系十多年来授予的最高荣誉。

2. Environmental regulators imposed heavy fines on factories whose carbon emissions **exceeded** the limits established by federal legislation.
   环境监管机构对碳排放超过联邦立法规定限额的工厂处以重罚。

3. The costs of the infrastructure project **exceeded** the original budget estimate by nearly forty percent due to unforeseen geological challenges.
   由于未预见的地质挑战，该基础设施项目的成本超出最初预算估计近百分之四十。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-CEED" — "ceed" comes from Latin "cedere" meaning to go, so exceed means to go beyond the expected boundary.
把"exceed"想成"ex-CEED"——"ceed"来自拉丁语"cedere"，意为前进，所以exceed就是超越预期的边界。
