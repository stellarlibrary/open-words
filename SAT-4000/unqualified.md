# 🚫 unqualified

🔊 /ˌʌnˈkwɒlɪfaɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Not having the necessary skills, knowledge, or experience for a particular job or activity; absolute; without reservation or limitation
**中文：** 不合格的；无条件的；绝对的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- incompetent（不称职的）
- ineligible（不合格的）
- absolute（绝对的）
- unconditional（无条件的）
- unreserved（无保留的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **unqualified success / unqualified approval / unqualified candidate / unqualified support**

👉 Example: The new product launch was an unqualified success, exceeding all projected sales targets.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee deemed the applicant **unqualified** for the research position due to insufficient laboratory experience and credentials.
   委员会认为该申请人因实验室经验和资质不足而不具备担任该研究职位的资格。

2. Critics hailed the documentary as an **unqualified** triumph that redefined the boundaries of investigative journalism.
   评论家称赞这部纪录片是一次彻底的成功，重新定义了调查新闻的边界。

3. The board offered its **unqualified** endorsement of the proposal after reviewing the comprehensive financial projections.
   董事会在审查了全面的财务预测后，对该提案给予了无条件的支持。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "un-qualified" as "not qualified" — without the qualities or qualifications needed, or without any qualifying conditions attached.
把"unqualified"想成"un（没有）+ qualified（合格的）"——没有所需的资质，或没有附加任何限定条件。
