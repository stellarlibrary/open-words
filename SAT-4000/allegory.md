# 📖 allegory

🔊 /ˈæləˌɡɔːri/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A story, poem, or picture that reveals a hidden moral or political meaning through symbolic characters and events
**中文：** 寓言；讽喻

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- parable（寓言）
- fable（寓言故事）
- metaphor（隐喻）
- symbolism（象征手法）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **political allegory / religious allegory / allegory of/for something / an allegory about**

👉 Example: George Orwell's Animal Farm is widely regarded as an allegory for the Russian Revolution.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor explained that the novel functions as an **allegory** for the devastating consequences of unchecked political ambition.
   教授解释说，这部小说是对不受约束的政治野心所带来的毁灭性后果的寓言。

2. Many scholars interpret Plato's Cave as an **allegory** illustrating the distinction between illusion and genuine philosophical enlightenment.
   许多学者将柏拉图的洞穴比喻解读为阐释幻象与真正哲学启蒙之间区别的寓言。

3. The filmmaker crafted a powerful **allegory** about social inequality that resonated deeply with audiences around the world.
   这位电影制作人创作了一部关于社会不平等的有力寓言，深深引起了全世界观众的共鸣。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "allegory" as "all-a-gory story" — a story where all the gory details actually stand for something deeper.
把"allegory"想成"all-a-gory story"——一个所有血腥细节实际上代表着更深层含义的故事。
