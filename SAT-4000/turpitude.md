# 😈 turpitude

🔊 /ˈtɜːrpɪtjuːd/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Inherent baseness or vileness of character; a depraved or wicked act
**中文：** n. 卑鄙；奸恶；堕落

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- depravity（堕落）
- wickedness（邪恶）
- vileness（卑劣）
- baseness（卑鄙）
- iniquity（不公正）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **moral turpitude / acts of turpitude / turpitude of character**

👉 Example: The attorney was disbarred for conduct involving moral turpitude.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee concluded that the official's behavior constituted **turpitude** unbecoming of a public servant in office.
   委员会认定该官员的行为构成了不配公职人员身份的卑劣行径。

2. Immigration law permits the denial of visas to individuals convicted of crimes involving moral **turpitude**.
   移民法允许拒绝向犯有涉及道德败坏罪行的个人发放签证。

3. The historian documented the sheer **turpitude** of the regime, which had systematically oppressed its own citizens for decades.
   历史学家记录了该政权的极端邪恶，它数十年来系统性地压迫本国公民。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "turp-itude" as "turp" sounds like "turd" — something foul and base, just like turpitude describes moral filth.
把"turpitude"想成"turp"听起来像"turd"（污秽之物）——正如turpitude描述的是道德上的卑劣肮脏。
