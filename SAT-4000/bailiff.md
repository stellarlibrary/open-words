# ⚖️ bailiff

🔊 /ˈbeɪlɪf/<br>
🏷️ *n. | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** An officer of the court who maintains order, serves legal documents, and executes court orders or judgments.

**中文：** 法警；执行官；法庭事务官，负责维持法庭秩序、送达法律文件和执行法院命令。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- marshal（法警）
- constable（治安官）
- sheriff（警长）
- officer（执法官）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Bailiff" specifically refers to a court official responsible for maintaining order in the courtroom and carrying out legal duties such as serving writs and escorting defendants. In British English, it can also refer to a landlord's agent who collects rent or manages an estate.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **bailiff** commanded everyone in the courtroom to rise as the judge entered through the side door.
   法警命令法庭里的所有人在法官从侧门进入时起立。

2. A **bailiff** was dispatched to serve the eviction notice upon the tenant who had defaulted on payments.
   一名执行官被派去向拖欠付款的租户送达驱逐通知。

3. The presiding judge instructed the **bailiff** to remove the disruptive spectator from the gallery immediately.
   主审法官指示法警立即将扰乱秩序的旁听者从旁听席带走。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "bail" + "iff" — the **bailiff** is the one who handles **bail** matters in court. If (iff) you need bail, the bailiff is involved!

联想"bail（保释）"+"iff"——**法警**就是在法庭上处理**保释**事务的人。如果你需要保释，法警就会参与其中！
