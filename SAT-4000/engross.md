# 🔍 engross

🔊 /ɪnˈɡroʊs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To absorb all the attention or interest of someone completely; to occupy exclusively or monopolize
**中文：** v. 使全神贯注；吸引；独占

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- absorb（吸引）
- captivate（使着迷）
- preoccupy（使全神贯注）
- immerse（使沉浸）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **engrossed in a book / engrossed in work / engrossed in thought / completely engrossed**

👉 Example: The students were so engrossed in the experiment that they lost track of time entirely.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The detective became so **engrossed** in the cold case files that she worked through the entire night without a single break.
   侦探对这些悬案档案如此全神贯注，以至于整夜工作没有休息片刻。

2. The novel's intricate plot and compelling characters **engrossed** readers worldwide, earning unanimous critical acclaim upon publication.
   这部小说错综复杂的情节和引人入胜的角色吸引了全世界的读者，出版后获得了一致的好评。

3. Scholars were thoroughly **engrossed** in the newly discovered manuscript, analyzing every passage for historical and linguistic significance.
   学者们全神贯注于新发现的手稿，分析每一段的历史和语言学意义。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "engross" as "en-GROSS" — something so large (gross) in your mind that it takes over all your attention and focus.
把"engross"想成"en-GROSS"——在你脑海中某事物如此巨大（gross），以至于占据了你所有的注意力。
