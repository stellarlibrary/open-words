# 🐺 feral

🔊 /ˈfɪrəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Existing in a wild or untamed state, especially of an animal that was once domesticated; resembling a wild beast; savage or ferocious
**中文：** adj. 野生的；凶猛的；阴郁的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- wild（野生的）
- untamed（未驯服的）
- savage（凶猛的）
- undomesticated（未驯化的）
- feral（野性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **feral cat / feral animal / feral dog / feral instinct / go feral / feral population**

👉 Example: The island was overrun by feral goats that had escaped from farms generations ago.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The city allocated additional funds to address the growing population of **feral** cats inhabiting abandoned buildings across the district.
   该市拨出额外资金来解决该地区废弃建筑中不断增长的野猫数量问题。

2. After months of living alone in the wilderness, the castaway developed an almost **feral** intensity in his eyes and movements.
   在荒野中独自生活数月后，这位漂流者的眼神和动作中展现出近乎野性的凌厉。

3. Ecologists warned that **feral** species introduced to the fragile island ecosystem could drive several native birds to extinction.
   生态学家警告说，引入脆弱岛屿生态系统的野生物种可能导致数种本地鸟类灭绝。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "feral" as "fear-al" — a feral animal inspires fear because it is wild, untamed, and potentially dangerous.
把"feral"想成"fear-al（令人恐惧的）"——野生动物令人恐惧，因为它们未被驯服，可能很危险。
