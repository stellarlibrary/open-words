# 🔙 reactionary

🔊 /riˈækʃəˌnɛri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Opposing political or social progress or reform; extremely conservative. (n.) A person who holds such views
**中文：** adj. 保守的，反动的 n. 反动分子；反动派

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conservative（保守的）
- traditionalist（传统主义者）
- ultraconservative（极端保守的）
- right-wing（右翼的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reactionary views / reactionary forces / reactionary politics / reactionary stance**

👉 Example: The senator's reactionary stance on education reform drew sharp criticism from progressive advocacy groups.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **reactionary** faction within the parliament consistently blocked legislation aimed at modernizing the nation's outdated labor laws.
   议会中的反动派系一直阻挠旨在使国家过时劳动法现代化的立法。

2. Critics dismissed his proposals as dangerously **reactionary**, arguing that they would reverse decades of hard-won social progress.
   批评者认为他的提议是危险的倒退，声称这些提议将逆转数十年来艰难取得的社会进步。

3. The political commentator warned that **reactionary** movements often gain momentum during periods of rapid economic and cultural change.
   这位政治评论家警告说，反动运动往往在经济和文化快速变革时期获得势头。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "reactionary" as someone who "reacts" against change — they want to pull society backward rather than move it forward.
把"reactionary"想成一个对变革"react（反应）"的人——他们想把社会拉回过去，而不是推动社会前进。
