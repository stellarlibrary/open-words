# 🧲 compelling

🔊 /kəmˈpelɪŋ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Evoking strong interest, attention, or admiration in a powerfully irresistible way; forceful and convincing
**中文：** adj. 引人注目的；令人信服的；强制的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- persuasive（有说服力的）
- captivating（迷人的）
- convincing（令人信服的）
- riveting（引人入胜的）
- forceful（有力的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **compelling evidence / compelling argument / compelling reason / compelling narrative / compelling case**

👉 Example: The prosecutor presented compelling evidence that left little room for reasonable doubt.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The documentary presented a **compelling** narrative about the environmental consequences of unchecked industrial expansion in developing nations.
   这部纪录片呈现了一个关于发展中国家不受控制的工业扩张所带来的环境后果的引人注目的叙事。

2. Without **compelling** evidence to support the hypothesis, the scientific community remained deeply skeptical of the extraordinary claims.
   由于缺乏令人信服的证据来支持该假设，科学界对这些非凡的声明仍然深表怀疑。

3. Her **compelling** oratory style transformed even the most mundane policy discussions into engaging and memorable public addresses.
   她引人入胜的演讲风格将最平凡的政策讨论都变成了引人注目且令人难忘的公开演说。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "compelling" as "compel + ling" — something so powerful it compels you to pay attention, pulling you in like a magnet.
把"compelling"想成"compel（强迫）+ ling"——某件事如此有力，以至于强迫你注意，像磁铁一样把你吸引过来。
