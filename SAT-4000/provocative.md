# 🔥 provocative

🔊 /prəˈvɑːkətɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Causing anger, irritation, or strong reaction, especially deliberately; tending to provoke or stimulate. (n.) Something that serves as a provocation or stimulus
**中文：** adj. 挑衅的；刺激的；惹人讨厌的 n. 刺激物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- inflammatory（煽动性的）
- inciting（煽动的）
- stimulating（刺激的）
- contentious（有争议的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **provocative remarks / provocative behavior / provocative question / intellectually provocative**

👉 Example: The professor posed a provocative question that challenged the students to reconsider their fundamental assumptions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat's **provocative** statements escalated tensions between the neighboring countries, drawing sharp criticism from the international community.
   该外交官的挑衅性言论加剧了邻国之间的紧张局势，招致国际社会的严厉批评。

2. Her **provocative** thesis argued that traditional educational methods systematically suppress creative thinking in young learners across all disciplines.
   她那具有挑战性的论点认为，传统教育方法系统性地压制了各学科年轻学习者的创造性思维。

3. The exhibition featured **provocative** artwork that deliberately confronted viewers with uncomfortable social realities and demanded deeper moral reflection.
   该展览展出了具有刺激性的艺术品，故意让观众面对令人不安的社会现实并要求更深层次的道德反思。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "provoc-ative" as "pro-voke-active" — something provocative actively provokes a reaction from others.
把"provocative"想成"pro（向前）+ voc（叫喊）+ ative"——具有挑衅性的东西会主动激起他人的反应。
