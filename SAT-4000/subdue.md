# ⚔️ subdue

🔊 /səbˈdjuː/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To conquer and bring into subjection; to restrain or suppress; to soften or reduce in intensity
**中文：** v. 征服；抑制

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conquer（征服）
- suppress（压制）
- quell（镇压）
- restrain（抑制）
- overcome（克服）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **subdue an enemy / subdue emotions / subdue a rebellion / subdued lighting**

👉 Example: The commander devised a strategy to subdue the insurgent forces without causing unnecessary civilian casualties.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The peacekeeping forces were deployed to **subdue** the escalating conflict and restore stability to the war-torn region.
   维和部队被派遣去平息不断升级的冲突，恢复饱受战争蹂躏地区的稳定。

2. She struggled to **subdue** her anxiety before delivering the keynote address at the prestigious international academic conference.
   她在著名的国际学术会议上发表主旨演讲前努力抑制自己的焦虑。

3. The new legislation was designed to **subdue** speculative trading practices that had destabilized the financial markets for years.
   新立法旨在抑制多年来扰乱金融市场稳定的投机交易行为。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "subdue" as "sub + due" — to bring something under what is due or proper control, like subduing a wild force into submission.
把"subdue"想成"sub + due"——将某事物置于应有的控制之下，就像制服一股野蛮力量使其屈服。
