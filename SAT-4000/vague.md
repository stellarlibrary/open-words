# 🌫️ vague

🔊 /veɪɡ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Of uncertain, indefinite, or unclear character or meaning; not clearly expressed or defined
**中文：** adj. 模糊的；含糊的；不明确的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ambiguous（模棱两可的）
- unclear（不清楚的）
- indefinite（不明确的）
- nebulous（朦胧的）
- hazy（模糊的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vague idea / vague notion / vague description / vague memory / vague about something**

👉 Example: The witness could only provide a vague description of the suspect's appearance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The contract contained several **vague** provisions that allowed either party to interpret the terms in their own favor.
   该合同包含几项模糊的条款，允许任何一方按照自己有利的方式解释这些条款。

2. She had only a **vague** recollection of the events that had transpired during her early childhood in the countryside.
   她对童年早期在乡下发生的事情只有模糊的记忆。

3. The professor urged students to avoid **vague** generalizations and instead support their arguments with specific, verifiable evidence.
   教授敦促学生避免含糊的概括，而是用具体的、可验证的证据来支持他们的论点。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vague" as sounding like "fog" with a "v" — when there is fog, everything appears vague and unclear.
把"vague"想成带"v"的"fog"（雾）——当有雾的时候，一切都显得模糊不清。
