# 💭 whim

🔊 /wɪm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A sudden desire or change of mind, especially one that is unusual or unexplained; a capricious notion or fancy
**中文：** n. 奇想；一时的兴致；怪念头；幻想

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- caprice（任性）
- fancy（幻想）
- impulse（冲动）
- notion（念头）
- vagary（奇想）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **on a whim / at the whim of / every whim / passing whim / indulge a whim**

👉 Example: She decided on a whim to enroll in the advanced philosophy course offered that semester.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The director changed the entire production schedule on a **whim**, leaving the cast and crew scrambling to adapt to new arrangements.
   导演一时兴起改变了整个制作日程，让演员和工作人员手忙脚乱地适应新安排。

2. Effective governance cannot be subject to the personal **whims** of a single authority figure without proper institutional oversight.
   有效的治理不能在没有适当制度监督的情况下受制于某个权威人物的个人心血来潮。

3. The philanthropist donated a substantial sum on a **whim** after reading a compelling article about underfunded rural education programs.
   这位慈善家在读到一篇关于资金不足的农村教育项目的文章后，一时兴起捐赠了一笔可观的款项。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "whim" as a "whimsical wind" — it blows suddenly and unpredictably, just like a spontaneous idea that appears without warning.
把"whim"想成"whimsical wind（异想天开的风）"——它突然且不可预测地吹来，就像一个毫无预兆的突发念头。
