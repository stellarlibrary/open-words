# 🎤 declaim

🔊 /dɪˈkleɪm/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To speak passionately and at length; to deliver a speech in a rhetorical or impassioned manner; to recite aloud
**中文：** v. 慷慨陈词；演讲；朗读

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- orate（演说）
- proclaim（宣告）
- harangue（长篇大论地演说）
- recite（朗诵）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **declaim against injustice / declaim passionately / declaim poetry / declaim from the podium**

👉 Example: The senator rose to declaim against the proposed legislation with fervor and conviction.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The activist stood before the crowd to **declaim** against the government's failure to protect the rights of marginalized communities.
   这位活动家站在人群面前，慷慨陈词反对政府未能保护边缘化群体的权利。

2. During the ceremony, the professor was invited to **declaim** a passage from a celebrated work of classical literature.
   在典礼上，教授被邀请朗诵一段著名古典文学作品中的段落。

3. The opposition leader chose to **declaim** passionately in parliament, arguing that the new policy would undermine economic stability.
   反对党领袖选择在议会中慷慨陈词，认为新政策将破坏经济稳定。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "declaim" as "de- + claim" — to loudly and dramatically make a claim, as if proclaiming something to the world.
把"declaim"想成"de- + claim"——大声而戏剧性地提出主张，好像在向全世界宣告。
