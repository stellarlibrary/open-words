# 🚪 vacate

🔊 /vəˈkeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To leave a place or position, making it available for others; to give up occupancy of a premises; to resign from an office
**中文：** 空出，腾出；辞职

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- evacuate（撤离）
- abandon（离开）
- relinquish（放弃）
- leave（离开）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vacate the premises / vacate a position / vacate a seat / vacate a judgment / vacate the room**

👉 Example: Tenants were given thirty days to vacate the building before renovation work commenced.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court ordered the tenants to **vacate** the premises within sixty days following the landlord's successful breach-of-contract claim.
   法院命令租户在房东成功提出违约索赔后六十天内腾出房屋。

2. The director announced her decision to **vacate** her position, citing irreconcilable differences with the board of trustees.
   主任宣布辞去职务，理由是与董事会存在不可调和的分歧。

3. The appellate court moved to **vacate** the original ruling after discovering that key evidence had been improperly excluded.
   上诉法院在发现关键证据被不当排除后决定撤销原判。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vacate" as related to "vacant" — when you vacate a place, you make it vacant (empty).
把"vacate"和"vacant（空的）"联系起来——当你vacate一个地方时，你让它变成vacant（空的）。
