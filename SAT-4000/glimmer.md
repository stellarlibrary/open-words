# 🌟 glimmer

🔊 /ˈɡlɪmər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A faint or unsteady light; a faint sign or trace. (v.) To shine faintly with a wavering light
**中文：** n. 微光；闪光；少许 v. 闪烁

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flicker（闪烁）
- shimmer（微光）
- sparkle（闪光）
- gleam（微光）
- twinkle（闪烁）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a glimmer of hope / a glimmer of light / a faint glimmer / glimmer in the distance**

👉 Example: Even in the darkest times, there remained a glimmer of hope for peaceful resolution.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The negotiators detected a **glimmer** of hope when both parties agreed to resume diplomatic discussions after months of silence.
   当双方同意在数月沉默后恢复外交讨论时，谈判者看到了一线希望。

2. A faint **glimmer** of light from the distant lighthouse guided the sailors safely through the treacherous coastal waters.
   远处灯塔发出的微弱光芒引导水手们安全通过了危险的沿海水域。

3. The abandoned cathedral's stained glass windows continued to **glimmer** softly whenever the late afternoon sunlight filtered through.
   每当午后的阳光透过时，废弃大教堂的彩色玻璃窗仍会发出柔和的微光。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "glimmer" as "glimpse + shimmer" — a glimmer is like catching a shimmering glimpse of faint, wavering light.
把"glimmer"想成"glimpse（一瞥）+ shimmer（微光）"——glimmer就像捕捉到微弱闪烁光芒的一瞥。
