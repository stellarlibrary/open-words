# 🚫 invalid

🔊 /ɪnˈvælɪd/ (adj. 无效的) /ˈɪnvəlɪd/ (n. 病人)<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Not legally or officially acceptable; not valid. Also: weak or disabled by illness or injury. (n.) A person made weak or disabled by illness or injury
**中文：** adj. 无效的；无根据的；有病的，伤残的 n. 病人；病弱者；伤残人

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- void（无效的）
- null（无效的）
- unfounded（无根据的）
- infirm（体弱的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **invalid argument / invalid claim / invalid passport / render invalid / declared invalid**

👉 Example: The court ruled that the contract was invalid because it had been signed under duress.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor dismissed the student's thesis as **invalid** because it relied entirely on outdated and discredited sources.
   教授驳回了学生的论文，认为其无效，因为它完全依赖于过时和不可信的来源。

2. Her passport was declared **invalid** by immigration authorities, preventing her from boarding the international flight to London.
   她的护照被移民当局宣布无效，导致她无法登上飞往伦敦的国际航班。

3. The experiment's results were rendered **invalid** when researchers discovered a critical flaw in the original methodology.
   当研究人员发现原始方法中存在一个关键缺陷时，实验结果被判定为无效。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-valid" as "not valid" — the prefix "in-" negates "valid," making something without force or legal standing.
把"invalid"想成"in（不）+ valid（有效的）"——前缀"in-"否定了"valid"，使某物没有效力或法律地位。
