# 🚫 discredit

🔊 /dɪsˈkredɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause someone or something to seem untrustworthy; to damage the reputation of. (n.) Loss of reputation or respect; the state of being doubted
**中文：** v. 不信；败坏声誉 n. 怀疑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disgrace（使丢脸）
- defame（诽谤）
- undermine（削弱）
- dishonor（使蒙羞）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **discredit a theory / discredit a witness / bring discredit upon / fall into discredit**

👉 Example: The scandal threatened to discredit the entire research team and their published findings.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The defense attorney attempted to **discredit** the key witness by revealing inconsistencies in her previous sworn testimony.
   辩护律师试图通过揭示关键证人先前宣誓证词中的不一致之处来使其失去可信度。

2. New evidence has emerged that could **discredit** the widely accepted theory of how the ancient civilization came to collapse.
   新的证据已经出现，可能会推翻关于这个古代文明如何走向崩溃的广泛接受的理论。

3. The fraudulent data brought great **discredit** upon the university's research department and led to several retractions from major journals.
   这些伪造数据给该大学的研究部门带来了极大的耻辱，并导致多篇论文从主要期刊撤回。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-credit" — to take away someone's "credit" (信誉), leaving them with no credibility or trustworthiness.
把"discredit"想成"dis-credit"——夺走某人的"credit"（信誉），使其失去可信度和信任。
