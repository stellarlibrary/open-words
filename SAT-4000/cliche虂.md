# 🔄 cliché

🔊 /kliːˈʃeɪ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A phrase, idea, or expression that has been overused to the point of losing its original meaning or impact; a hackneyed saying
**中文：** n. 陈词滥调；老生常谈；陈腐的思想

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- platitude（陈词滥调）
- banality（老生常谈）
- truism（老套的话）
- commonplace（老生常谈）
- bromide（陈腐之语）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tired cliché / avoid clichés / overused cliché / become a cliché / resort to clichés**

👉 Example: The professor warned students to avoid relying on clichés in their academic essays.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The critic dismissed the novel as a collection of predictable **clichés** lacking any genuine originality or depth.
   评论家认为这部小说不过是一堆可预见的陈词滥调，缺乏任何真正的原创性或深度。

2. Although "time heals all wounds" is a **cliché**, many people still find comfort in its familiar reassurance.
   虽然"时间会治愈一切伤痛"是句陈词滥调，但许多人仍从其熟悉的安慰中获得慰藉。

3. The politician's speech was filled with empty **clichés** that failed to address the substantive concerns of voters.
   这位政治家的演讲充满了空洞的陈词滥调，未能解决选民的实质性关切。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cliché" as the sound of a printing press clicking repeatedly — the same phrase stamped over and over until it becomes worn out and meaningless.
把"cliché"想象成印刷机反复咔嗒作响的声音——同样的短语一遍又一遍地印刷，直到磨损殆尽、毫无意义。
