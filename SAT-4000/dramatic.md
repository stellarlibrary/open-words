# 🎭 dramatic

🔊 /drəˈmætɪk/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Relating to drama or the theater; strikingly impressive or sudden in effect or change
**中文：** adj. 戏剧的；急剧的；引人注目的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- striking（引人注目的）
- remarkable（显著的）
- theatrical（戏剧性的）
- spectacular（壮观的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dramatic change / dramatic increase / dramatic effect / dramatic improvement**

👉 Example: The new policy led to a dramatic reduction in carbon emissions within just two years.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The country experienced a **dramatic** shift in public opinion after the investigative report revealed widespread government corruption.
   在调查报告揭露大范围政府腐败后，该国公众舆论发生了急剧转变。

2. Advances in renewable energy technology have produced **dramatic** improvements in solar panel efficiency over the past decade.
   可再生能源技术的进步在过去十年中使太阳能电池板效率获得了显著提升。

3. The architect's **dramatic** design for the concert hall won international acclaim for its bold geometric forms.
   建筑师为音乐厅设计的引人注目的方案因其大胆的几何造型而赢得了国际赞誉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dramatic" as coming from "drama" — just like a drama on stage, something dramatic captures attention with powerful impact.
把"dramatic"想成来自"drama（戏剧）"——就像舞台上的戏剧一样，戏剧性的事物以强大的冲击力吸引注意力。
