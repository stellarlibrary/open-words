# :scroll: repeal

:speaker: /rɪˈpiːl/<br>
:label: *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## :books: Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To revoke or annul a law or act of parliament. (n.) The act of revoking or annulling a law or regulation
**中文：** v. 废除；撤销；废止 n. 废除；撤销

---

## :link: Synonyms | 近义词
- revoke（撤销）
- annul（废除）
- rescind（废止）
- abolish（取消）
- nullify（使无效）

---

## :pushpin: Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **repeal a law / repeal legislation / repeal an act / call for the repeal of**

:point_right: Example: Activists campaigned vigorously to repeal the outdated statute that restricted civil liberties.

---

## :memo: Examples | 例句

1. The legislature voted overwhelmingly to **repeal** the controversial tax measure that had burdened small businesses for decades.
   立法机关以压倒性多数投票废除了这项数十年来给小企业带来负担的争议性税收措施。

2. The **repeal** of the restrictive trade policy opened new avenues for international commerce and economic growth.
   废除限制性贸易政策为国际商务和经济增长开辟了新途径。

3. Scholars argued that the failure to **repeal** the obsolete regulation would continue to hinder scientific research and innovation.
   学者们认为，未能废除这项过时的法规将继续阻碍科学研究和创新。

---

## :jigsaw: Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-peal" as "peeling back" a law — you strip it away layer by layer until it no longer exists.
把"repeal"想成"re-peal（重新剥开）"——你把一项法律一层一层地剥掉，直到它不复存在。
