# 🪶 trifle

🔊 /ˈtraɪfl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A thing of little value or importance; a small amount. (v.) To treat without seriousness or respect; to waste time idly.
**中文：** n. 琐事；少量；微不足道的东西 v. 开玩笑；闲混；轻视

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- triviality（琐事）
- bagatelle（小事）
- pittance（少量）
- dally（闲混）
- toy with（玩弄）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a mere trifle / trifle with someone / not to be trifled with / a trifle nervous**

👉 Example: The seasoned diplomat warned the young ambassador that the foreign minister was not a person to be trifled with.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senior professor dismissed the minor formatting error as a **trifle** and urged the student to focus on substantive research.
   资深教授将这个小格式错误视为琐事，并敦促学生专注于实质性研究。

2. One should never **trifle** with matters of international diplomacy, as even small missteps can escalate into serious conflicts.
   人们绝不应在国际外交事务上掉以轻心，因为即使小失误也可能升级为严重冲突。

3. She felt a **trifle** uneasy about presenting her unconventional findings before the distinguished panel of senior scholars.
   在向资深学者组成的杰出评审团展示她非常规的研究发现之前，她感到有些不安。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "trifle" as "tri-fle" — something so small it would take only a "trifling" effort to flick away, like a tiny speck.
把"trifle"想成"tri-fle"——小到只需轻轻一弹（fle=flick）就能弹走的东西，微不足道。
