# 😤 chagrin

🔊 /ʃəˈɡrɪn/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A feeling of distress, annoyance, or humiliation caused by failure, disappointment, or embarrassment. (v.) To cause someone to feel annoyed or humiliated
**中文：** n. 懊恼；委屈；气愤 v. 使懊恼；使失望

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- mortification（屈辱）
- vexation（烦恼）
- humiliation（羞辱）
- dismay（沮丧）
- exasperation（恼怒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **to one's chagrin / much to the chagrin of / chagrined by / feel chagrin**

👉 Example: Much to her chagrin, she discovered that the scholarship had been awarded to a less qualified candidate.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. To the **chagrin** of the senior researchers, the prestigious journal rejected their manuscript without providing substantive peer-review comments.
   令资深研究人员懊恼的是，这家知名期刊拒绝了他们的稿件，且未提供实质性的同行评审意见。

2. The ambassador was visibly **chagrined** when diplomatic negotiations collapsed abruptly due to irreconcilable differences between the delegations.
   当外交谈判因代表团之间不可调和的分歧而突然破裂时，大使明显感到懊恼。

3. Much to the **chagrin** of the organizing committee, the keynote speaker cancelled her appearance merely two hours before the ceremony.
   令组委会十分气愤的是，主题演讲人在典礼前仅两个小时取消了出席。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "chagrin" as "sha-GRIN" — imagine someone who cannot grin (smile) because they are too annoyed and embarrassed by their failure.
把"chagrin"想成"sha-GRIN"——想象一个人因为失败而懊恼和尴尬到无法露出笑容（grin）。
