# 👋 dismiss

🔊 /dɪsˈmɪs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To send away or allow to leave; to remove from employment; to treat as unworthy of serious consideration
**中文：** v. 解散；让离开；解雇，免职；不予理会

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- discharge（解雇）
- disregard（忽视）
- reject（拒绝）
- terminate（终止雇用）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dismiss an idea / dismiss from employment / dismiss a case / dismiss concerns**

👉 Example: The judge decided to dismiss the case due to insufficient evidence presented by the prosecution.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board voted unanimously to **dismiss** the chief executive officer following revelations of repeated financial misconduct.
   董事会一致投票决定解雇首席执行官，此前曝出了其多次财务不当行为。

2. Scholars cautioned against the tendency to **dismiss** indigenous knowledge systems as inferior to Western scientific methodologies.
   学者们告诫不要将土著知识体系贬低为不如西方科学方法论。

3. The appellate court chose to **dismiss** the lawsuit after determining that the plaintiff lacked proper legal standing.
   上诉法院在认定原告缺乏适当的法律地位后选择驳回了该诉讼。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-miss" as "dis (away) + miss (send)" — you send someone away, whether from a job, a class, or your attention.
把"dismiss"想成"dis（离开）+ miss（发送）"——把某人打发走，无论是解雇、解散还是不予理会。
