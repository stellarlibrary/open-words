# 🎭 stagy

🔊 /ˈsteɪdʒi/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Excessively theatrical or artificial in style or manner; affectedly dramatic
**中文：** adj. 做作的，不自然的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- theatrical（戏剧性的）
- affected（做作的）
- melodramatic（夸张的）
- artificial（不自然的）
- mannered（矫揉造作的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **stagy performance / stagy gestures / stagy manner / stagy delivery / stagy behavior**

👉 Example: The politician's stagy display of emotion failed to convince the skeptical audience.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The director criticized the actor's **stagy** performance, urging him to adopt a more natural and understated approach.
   导演批评了这位演员做作的表演，要求他采用更自然、更低调的方式。

2. Her **stagy** gestures during the presentation distracted the audience from the substance of her research findings.
   她在演讲过程中做作的手势分散了观众对其研究成果实质内容的注意力。

3. The novel's dialogue was criticized as **stagy** and unconvincing, lacking the authenticity of natural human conversation.
   小说的对话被批评为做作且缺乏说服力，没有自然人类对话的真实感。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "stagy" as "stage-y" — behaving as if one is always on a stage, overly dramatic and unnatural in everyday situations.
把"stagy"想成"stage-y"——行为举止好像一直在舞台上，在日常生活中过于戏剧化和不自然。
