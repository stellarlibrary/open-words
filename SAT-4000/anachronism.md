# ⏳ anachronism

🔊 /əˈnækrənɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A thing belonging to a period other than the one in which it exists; a person or custom regarded as outdated or old-fashioned
**中文：** 时代错误；不合潮流的人或物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- relic（遗物）
- holdover（残留物）
- throwback（返祖现象）
- archaism（古语；过时事物）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **historical anachronism / a glaring anachronism / an anachronism in modern society / seem like an anachronism**

👉 Example: The horse-drawn carriage in the futuristic film was a glaring anachronism that critics immediately noticed.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The wristwatch worn by the actor in the medieval period drama was an obvious **anachronism** that distracted attentive viewers.
   演员在中世纪古装剧中佩戴的手表是一个明显的时代错误，让细心的观众分心。

2. Many argue that the monarchy has become an **anachronism** in an era of democratic governance and popular sovereignty.
   许多人认为，在民主治理和人民主权的时代，君主制已经成为不合潮流的事物。

3. The professor identified several **anachronisms** in the student's essay, including references to technologies that did not yet exist.
   教授在学生的论文中发现了几处时代错误，包括对当时尚不存在的技术的引用。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Break it into "ana- (against) + chron (time) + -ism" — something that goes against its proper time, placed in the wrong era.
把"anachronism"拆分为"ana-（相反）+ chron（时间）+ -ism"——与其应有的时间相悖的事物，被放在了错误的时代。
