# ⛓️ coercion

🔊 /koʊˈɜːrʒən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The practice of compelling someone to act through force, threats, or intimidation; the use of pressure to achieve compliance
**中文：** n. 强制；强迫；威压；高压政治

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- compulsion（强制）
- intimidation（恐吓）
- duress（胁迫）
- pressure（压力）
- force（武力）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **under coercion / act of coercion / political coercion / economic coercion / free from coercion**

👉 Example: The treaty was signed under coercion and therefore lacked genuine consent from the weaker nation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The confession was ultimately deemed inadmissible in court because it had been obtained through psychological **coercion** and duress.
   该供词最终被法院认定为不可采纳，因为它是通过心理胁迫和强制手段获得的。

2. Political analysts warned that economic **coercion** could destabilize fragile democracies and undermine international diplomatic relationships significantly.
   政治分析人士警告说，经济胁迫可能会破坏脆弱的民主体制并严重损害国际外交关系。

3. The organization advocated for legislation that would protect vulnerable workers from workplace **coercion** by unscrupulous employers.
   该组织倡导立法保护弱势工人免受不道德雇主的职场强迫。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "coercion" as "coerce" + "-ion" (the act of coercing) — it is the noun form of forcing someone against their will.
把"coercion"想成"coerce"（强制）+ "-ion"（行为）——它是强迫某人违背意愿的名词形式。
