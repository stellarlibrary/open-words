# ✊ commit

🔊 /kəˈmɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To carry out or perpetrate a crime or wrongful act; to entrust or hand over for safekeeping; to pledge or bind oneself to a course of action
**中文：** v. 犯（罪）；把…交托给，提交；使…承担义务

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- perpetrate（犯罪）
- entrust（委托）
- pledge（承诺）
- dedicate（致力于）
- consign（交付）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **commit a crime / commit to a plan / commit oneself / commit resources to**

👉 Example: The organization decided to commit significant resources to addressing climate change.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The defendant was found guilty of having **committed** multiple acts of fraud against vulnerable elderly investors over several years.
   被告被判犯有多项针对弱势老年投资者的长达数年的欺诈罪行。

2. The university chose to **commit** substantial funding to the development of its new interdisciplinary research center.
   该大学决定投入大量资金用于新跨学科研究中心的建设。

3. Before signing the agreement, she wanted to fully understand what she would **commit** herself to over the next decade.
   在签署协议之前，她想充分了解自己在未来十年中将承担什么义务。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "commit" as "com-mit" (together + send) — you send yourself together with a promise, binding yourself to an action or cause.
把"commit"想成"com-mit"（一起+送出）——你把自己和承诺一起送出去，将自己与某个行动或事业绑定。
