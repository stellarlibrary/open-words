# 🍃 slight

🔊 /slaɪt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, verb, noun | 形容词, 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Small in degree; not considerable or important; thin and delicate. (v.) To treat with disrespect or indifference; to snub. (n.) An act of disrespect or discourtesy
**中文：** adj. 轻微的，微小的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- minor（轻微的）
- trivial（微不足道的）
- negligible（可忽略的）
- snub（怠慢）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **slight difference / slight improvement / slight delay / feel slighted**

👉 Example: There was only a slight difference between the two proposals submitted by the competing firms.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The doctor noted a **slight** improvement in the patient's condition after the new treatment regimen was administered.
   医生注意到在实施新的治疗方案后，患者的病情有了轻微的改善。

2. The ambassador felt deeply **slighted** when the host nation failed to send a representative to the welcoming ceremony.
   当东道国未能派代表参加欢迎仪式时，大使感到深受怠慢。

3. Even a **slight** miscalculation in the trajectory could cause the spacecraft to miss its target by thousands of kilometers.
   即使轨道上的微小计算失误也可能导致航天器偏离目标数千公里。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "slight" as "s-light" — something so light and small it barely registers, like a feather's weight.
把"slight"想成"s-light（轻的）"——轻到几乎感觉不到，就像一根羽毛的重量。
