# 📎 redundant

🔊 /rɪˈdʌndənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Exceeding what is necessary or normal; superfluous; no longer needed for a job; repetitively verbose
**中文：** adj. 多余的，过剩的；被解雇的；冗长的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- superfluous（多余的）
- excessive（过多的）
- unnecessary（不必要的）
- surplus（过剩的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **redundant information / make redundant / redundant workers / redundant systems**

👉 Example: The editor removed several redundant paragraphs that added no new information to the manuscript.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee determined that several administrative positions had become **redundant** following the comprehensive organizational restructure last quarter.
   委员会确定，在上季度全面的组织重组之后，几个行政职位已变得多余。

2. Engineers installed **redundant** safety mechanisms in the reactor to ensure continued operation even if the primary system failed.
   工程师在反应堆中安装了冗余安全机制，以确保即使主系统故障也能继续运行。

3. The professor criticized the student's essay for containing **redundant** phrases that weakened the overall clarity of the argument.
   教授批评学生的论文包含冗余的短语，削弱了论点的整体清晰度。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-dundant" as "done-done-ant" — something done more than once is redundant, like saying the same thing twice.
把"redundant"想成"re-dundant"（重复做的）——做了不止一次的事情就是多余的，就像同一件事说两遍。
