# 🧒 urchin

🔊 /ˈɜːrtʃɪn/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A mischievous or raggedly dressed young child, especially one who is poor or homeless; a sea urchin
**中文：** n. 顽童，淘气鬼；（尤指贫穷的）小孩；海胆

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ragamuffin（衣衫褴褛的孩子）
- waif（流浪儿）
- scamp（淘气鬼）
- rascal（小淘气）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **street urchin / sea urchin / little urchin / ragged urchin**

👉 Example: The story follows a group of street urchins struggling to survive in Victorian-era London.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The Victorian novel depicted the lives of impoverished **urchins** who wandered the foggy streets of London begging for food.
   这部维多利亚时代的小说描绘了在伦敦雾蒙蒙的街头流浪乞讨的贫穷顽童们的生活。

2. Marine biologists discovered a new species of sea **urchin** thriving in the deep coral ecosystems of the Pacific Ocean.
   海洋生物学家在太平洋深海珊瑚生态系统中发现了一种新的海胆物种。

3. The little **urchin** darted through the crowded marketplace, skillfully evading the merchant who had caught him stealing fruit.
   那个小顽童飞快地穿过拥挤的集市，巧妙地躲开了抓到他偷水果的商贩。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "urchin" as a small, prickly creature — just like a sea urchin is spiny, a street urchin is scrappy and tough.
把"urchin"想成一种小而带刺的生物——就像海胆浑身是刺一样，街头小顽童也是泼辣顽强的。
