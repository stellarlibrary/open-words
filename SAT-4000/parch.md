# ☀️ parch

🔊 /pɑːrtʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make extremely dry, especially by exposure to heat; to scorch or roast with dry heat
**中文：** v. 烤，烘；使干透；焦干

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dehydrate（使脱水）
- desiccate（使干燥）
- scorch（烤焦）
- wither（枯萎）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **parched earth / parched throat / parched lips / sun-parched fields**

👉 Example: The relentless summer heat had parched the once-fertile farmland beyond recognition.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Months of relentless drought had **parched** the agricultural fields, leaving the soil cracked and utterly devoid of moisture.
   数月无情的干旱烤干了农田，使土壤龟裂并完全失去了水分。

2. The travelers arrived at the oasis with **parched** throats, desperate for water after their arduous trek through the desert.
   旅行者们到达绿洲时喉咙干渴，在穿越沙漠的艰苦跋涉后急需饮水。

3. The intense midday sun **parched** the exposed vegetation, transforming the lush green landscape into a barren brown expanse.
   正午猛烈的太阳晒干了暴露的植被，将郁郁葱葱的绿色景观变成了一片荒芜的棕色旷野。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "parch" as sounding like "patch" — imagine a dry patch of scorched earth where nothing can grow.
把"parch"想成发音类似"patch（一块地）"——想象一块被烈日烤焦的干旱土地，寸草不生。
