# 🃏 witling

🔊 /ˈwɪtlɪŋ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who attempts to be witty but lacks genuine cleverness; one who makes feeble jokes or pretentious remarks
**中文：** n. 玩弄小聪明的人；自以为机智的人

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pretender（装腔作势者）
- wiseacre（自作聪明的人）
- smartaleck（自以为是的人）
- jester（小丑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mere witling / a tiresome witling / dismissed as a witling / witling at court**

👉 Example: The literary critic dismissed the young author as a mere witling whose attempts at humor fell entirely flat.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The salon was populated by self-important **witlings** whose forced epigrams impressed no one of genuine intellectual substance.
   这个沙龙里满是自命不凡的耍小聪明者，他们勉强的警句没有给任何真正有学识的人留下印象。

2. A true satirist differs fundamentally from a **witling** in possessing both moral purpose and authentic comedic talent.
   真正的讽刺家与耍小聪明者有本质区别，前者兼具道德目的和真正的喜剧才能。

3. The essayist cautioned aspiring writers against becoming mere **witlings** who sacrifice depth of thought for superficial cleverness.
   这位散文家告诫有抱负的作家不要沦为只追求表面聪明而牺牲思想深度的耍小聪明者。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "wit-ling" as a "small wit" — the suffix "-ling" means something small or inferior, so a witling has only a little wit.
把"witling"想成"wit（机智）+ -ling（小的）"——只有一点点小聪明的人，自以为幽默却并不高明。
