# 💉 inject

🔊 /ɪnˈdʒekt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To introduce a substance into the body with a syringe; to introduce or add something new into a situation or process
**中文：** v. 注入；注射；引入

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- infuse（注入）
- introduce（引入）
- administer（施用）
- insert（插入）
- instill（灌输）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inject capital / inject enthusiasm / inject a drug / inject into the bloodstream**

👉 Example: The government plans to inject billions of dollars into the struggling economy.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The central bank announced plans to **inject** substantial liquidity into the financial markets to stabilize falling asset prices.
   中央银行宣布计划向金融市场注入大量流动性，以稳定不断下跌的资产价格。

2. The new director managed to **inject** fresh creativity and energy into a department that had grown stagnant over the years.
   新任主管成功地为多年来陷入停滞的部门注入了新的创造力和活力。

3. Physicians must carefully **inject** the vaccine into the deltoid muscle to ensure proper immune response in the patient.
   医生必须将疫苗仔细注射到三角肌中，以确保患者产生适当的免疫反应。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-ject" as "in + throw" — from Latin "jacere" meaning to throw, so inject literally means to throw something in.
把"inject"想成"in（进入）+ ject（投掷）"——源自拉丁语"jacere"意为投掷，所以inject字面意思是把东西投入。
