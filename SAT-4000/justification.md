# 📋 justification

🔊 /ˌdʒʌstɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act of showing something to be right, reasonable, or warranted; a good reason or explanation for an action or belief
**中文：** n. 理由；辩护；认为有理；认为正当

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rationale（理由）
- defense（辩护）
- vindication（证明正当）
- grounds（根据）
- explanation（解释）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **justification for / moral justification / provide justification / without justification**

👉 Example: The administration struggled to provide adequate justification for the sudden policy reversal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The company offered no convincing **justification** for the abrupt cancellation of the long-anticipated community development project.
   该公司没有为突然取消这一期待已久的社区发展项目提供令人信服的理由。

2. Historical scholars continue to debate the **justification** for military intervention during the colonial period of the eighteenth century.
   历史学者们继续争论十八世纪殖民时期军事干预的正当性。

3. Without proper **justification**, the allocation of additional funding to the failing department was met with strong institutional opposition.
   在没有适当理由的情况下，向这个失败的部门追加资金遭到了机构内部的强烈反对。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "justification" as "just-if-I-cation" — you need it just if someone questions your action; it is the reason that makes your decision just.
把"justification"想成"just-if-I-cation"——只有当有人质疑你的行为时你才需要它；它是使你的决定正当的理由。
