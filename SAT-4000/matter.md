# 🔬 matter

🔊 /ˈmætər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Physical substance that occupies space and has mass; a subject or situation under consideration. (v.) To be of importance or significance
**中文：** n. 物质；事件 v. 有关系；要紧

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- substance（物质）
- issue（问题；事务）
- concern（关注；事项）
- significance（重要性）
- material（物质；材料）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a matter of time / no matter what / subject matter / matter of fact / dark matter / it doesn't matter**

👉 Example: The professor explained that dark matter constitutes approximately twenty-seven percent of the known universe.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Physicists have long studied how **matter** and energy interact at the subatomic level to understand the fundamental laws of nature.
   物理学家长期以来一直在亚原子层面研究物质和能量如何相互作用，以理解自然的基本规律。

2. The committee determined that the **matter** required immediate attention and scheduled an emergency session to address the growing crisis.
   委员会认定此事需要立即处理，并安排了一次紧急会议来应对日益严重的危机。

3. Every vote will **matter** in this closely contested election where the margin of victory could be fewer than a hundred ballots.
   在这场竞争激烈的选举中，每一票都至关重要，因为胜负差距可能不到一百张选票。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "matter" as what "matters" — everything physical is made of matter, and everything important matters; both are central to our world.
把"matter"想成重要的东西——一切有形之物都由物质（matter）构成，一切重要之事都要紧（matter）；两者都是我们世界的核心。
