# ☀️ exposure

🔊 /ɪkˈspoʊʒər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The state of being exposed to something harmful or unpleasant; the revelation of something secret; the experience of encountering something new
**中文：** n. 暴露；曝光；揭露；陈列

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- revelation（揭露）
- disclosure（披露）
- vulnerability（暴露）
- contact（接触）
- publicity（曝光）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **exposure to sunlight / media exposure / risk exposure / exposure of corruption**

👉 Example: Prolonged exposure to ultraviolet radiation significantly increases the risk of developing skin cancer.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The investigative journalist's **exposure** of the financial scandal led to multiple resignations among senior government officials.
   调查记者对财务丑闻的揭露导致多名高级政府官员辞职。

2. Early **exposure** to foreign languages has been shown to enhance cognitive flexibility and improve academic performance in children.
   早期接触外语已被证明能增强儿童的认知灵活性并提高学业表现。

3. The company minimized its financial **exposure** by diversifying its investment portfolio across several different international markets.
   该公司通过在多个国际市场分散投资组合来最大限度地降低其财务风险敞口。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-posure" as "position outside" — when something is exposed, it is placed out in the open where everyone can see it, with no cover or protection.
把"exposure"想成"放在外面的位置"——当某物被暴露时，它被放在了公开的地方，人人都能看到，没有任何遮盖或保护。
