# 📜 mandate

🔊 /ˈmændeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An official order or authorization to carry out a policy; the authority granted by an electorate. (v.) To authorize or require by law or decree
**中文：** n. 授权；命令，指令 v. 授权；托管

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- decree（法令）
- directive（指令）
- authorization（授权）
- edict（敕令）
- command（命令）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mandate a policy / electoral mandate / federal mandate / mandate compliance**

👉 Example: The court issued a mandate requiring all public institutions to comply with the new accessibility standards.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The newly elected president claimed a strong **mandate** from the voters to reform the nation's healthcare system.
   新当选的总统声称获得了选民的强力授权来改革国家的医疗保健制度。

2. Federal regulators moved to **mandate** stricter safety protocols for all commercial airlines operating within domestic airspace.
   联邦监管机构着手强制要求所有在国内空域运营的商业航空公司执行更严格的安全规程。

3. The university's **mandate** to promote academic integrity led to the establishment of a comprehensive honor code system.
   该大学促进学术诚信的指令促使其建立了一套全面的荣誉准则制度。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "man-date" as a "man" giving a "date" (deadline) — someone in authority telling you when something must be done, like an official order.
把"mandate"想成"man"（人）给出"date"（日期）——一个有权威的人告诉你某事必须在什么时候完成，就像一道正式命令。
