# ⚖️ precedent

🔊 /ˈpresɪdənt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An earlier event or action regarded as an example or guide for similar circumstances. (adj.) Preceding in time, order, or importance.
**中文：** n. 先例；前例；范例；惯例 adj. 在前的；在先的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- example（范例）
- model（典范）
- standard（标准）
- prototype（原型）
- antecedent（先例）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **set a precedent / establish a precedent / without precedent / legal precedent**

👉 Example: The landmark ruling set a precedent that would influence judicial decisions for decades to come.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The Supreme Court's decision established a binding **precedent** that lower courts must follow in all subsequent cases.
   最高法院的裁决确立了一个具有约束力的先例，下级法院在所有后续案件中必须遵循。

2. There is no historical **precedent** for such a rapid and comprehensive transformation of an entire economic system.
   对于如此迅速和全面地改造整个经济体系，历史上没有先例。

3. The committee warned that approving the proposal would set a dangerous **precedent** for future policy negotiations.
   委员会警告说，批准该提案将为未来的政策谈判开创一个危险的先例。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-cede-nt" — something that "cedes" (goes) before, becoming an example for what follows.
把"precedent"想成"pre（前）+ cede（走）"——走在前面的事物，成为后来者的范例。
