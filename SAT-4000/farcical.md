# 😂 farcical

🔊 /ˈfɑːrsɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Resembling or characteristic of a farce; absurdly ridiculous or laughably inadequate
**中文：** adj. 滑稽的，闹剧的；引人发笑的；荒谬可笑的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- absurd（荒谬的）
- ludicrous（荒唐可笑的）
- ridiculous（可笑的）
- preposterous（荒谬的）
- comical（滑稽的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **farcical situation / farcical proceedings / farcical attempt / farcical performance**

👉 Example: The trial became farcical when key witnesses contradicted their own earlier testimony.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The parliamentary debate descended into **farcical** proceedings when opposing members began shouting over one another without any semblance of order.
   议会辩论沦为闹剧，反对派成员开始互相叫喊，毫无秩序可言。

2. Critics described the government's **farcical** attempt at reform as little more than a superficial gesture designed to appease voters.
   批评者将政府荒谬可笑的改革尝试描述为仅仅是为了安抚选民的表面姿态。

3. The investigation became increasingly **farcical** as crucial evidence was repeatedly mishandled by the authorities responsible for the case.
   随着关键证据被负责此案的当局反复处理不当，调查变得越来越荒唐可笑。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "farce + ical" — a farce is a comedic play full of absurd situations, so farcical means resembling such a ridiculous performance.
把"farcical"想成"farce（闹剧）+ ical"——闹剧是充满荒谬情节的喜剧表演，所以farcical就是像闹剧一样荒唐可笑的。
