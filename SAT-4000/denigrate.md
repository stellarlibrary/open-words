# 🗣️ denigrate

🔊 /ˈdenɪɡreɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To criticize unfairly; to disparage or belittle the reputation of someone or something
**中文：** v. 贬低；诋毁；使变黑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disparage（贬低）
- belittle（轻视）
- defame（诽谤）
- malign（诬蔑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **denigrate someone's reputation / denigrate achievements / denigrate the efforts of**

👉 Example: The critic sought to denigrate the artist's work by questioning her originality.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Political opponents attempted to **denigrate** the senator's record by distorting her voting history on key legislative issues.
   政治对手试图通过歪曲她在关键立法问题上的投票记录来诋毁这位参议员的政绩。

2. It is unprofessional to **denigrate** a colleague's contributions during a public presentation, regardless of personal disagreements.
   无论个人分歧如何，在公开演讲中贬低同事的贡献是不专业的行为。

3. The scholar warned against the tendency to **denigrate** traditional knowledge systems in favor of modern scientific frameworks.
   这位学者告诫人们不要为了推崇现代科学框架而贬低传统的知识体系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "deni-grate" as "deny + great" — when you denigrate someone, you deny that they are great.
把"denigrate"想成"deny（否认）+ great（伟大）"——当你诋毁某人时，你就是在否认他们的伟大。
