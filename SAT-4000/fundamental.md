# 🧱 fundamental

🔊 /ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Forming a necessary base or core; of central importance. (n.) A basic principle, rule, or law that serves as the groundwork of a system
**中文：** adj. 基础的；基本的；根本的 n. 基本原则；基本原理

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- essential（基本的）
- cardinal（首要的）
- foundational（基础的）
- elementary（基础的）
- rudimentary（初步的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fundamental principle / fundamental right / fundamental change / fundamental to success**

👉 Example: A respect for evidence is fundamental to the practice of rigorous scientific inquiry.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor emphasized that understanding **fundamental** concepts in mathematics is essential before attempting advanced theoretical coursework.
   教授强调，在尝试高级理论课程之前，理解数学的基本概念至关重要。

2. Access to clean drinking water is considered a **fundamental** human right by international law and humanitarian organizations worldwide.
   获得清洁饮用水被国际法和全球人道主义组织视为一项基本人权。

3. The discovery of DNA structure represented a **fundamental** shift in biology that transformed medicine, genetics, and forensic science.
   DNA结构的发现代表了生物学的根本性转变，改变了医学、遗传学和法医科学。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fundamental" as "fund-a-mental foundation" — the mental foundation you fund first before building anything else.
把"fundamental"想成"fund（资助）+ a mental foundation（思想基础）"——在建造任何东西之前，你首先投入的思想基础。
