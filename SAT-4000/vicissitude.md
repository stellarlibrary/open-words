# 🔄 vicissitude

🔊 /vɪˈsɪsɪtjuːd/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A change of circumstances or fortune, typically one that is unwelcome or unpleasant; the quality of being changeable
**中文：** n. 变迁；盛衰；变化无常；兴衰

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fluctuation（波动）
- upheaval（剧变）
- mutability（变化无常）
- reversal（逆转）
- alternation（交替）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vicissitudes of life / vicissitudes of fortune / endure vicissitudes / through many vicissitudes**

👉 Example: The family endured the vicissitudes of war, emerging stronger despite years of hardship and loss.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The historian's memoir documented the **vicissitudes** of a nation that had survived centuries of conquest and revolution.
   这位历史学家的回忆录记录了一个经历了数百年征服与革命的国家的兴衰变迁。

2. Through all the **vicissitudes** of his long career, the diplomat maintained an unwavering commitment to peaceful negotiation.
   在漫长职业生涯的所有变迁中，这位外交官始终坚持对和平谈判的不懈承诺。

3. The ancient temple has withstood the **vicissitudes** of time, remaining a powerful symbol of cultural resilience and faith.
   这座古老的寺庙经受住了时间的变迁，至今仍是文化韧性与信仰的有力象征。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vicissitude" as containing "vice" (change, turn) — life takes turns, bringing alternating periods of fortune and misfortune.
把"vicissitude"想成包含"vice"（变化、转折）——生活轮流带来幸运与不幸的交替时期。
