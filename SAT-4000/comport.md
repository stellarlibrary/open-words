# 🎭 comport

🔊 /kəmˈpɔːrt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To conduct or behave oneself in a specified manner; to be in agreement or accord with
**中文：** v. 举止；行为；表现；与……相符

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- behave（行为）
- conduct（举止）
- acquit（表现）
- deport（举止）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **comport oneself / comport with expectations / comport with standards / comport with dignity**

👉 Example: The diplomat was expected to comport herself with grace and poise at the international summit.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. All representatives are required to **comport** themselves with the utmost professionalism during official diplomatic engagements and state functions.
   所有代表在正式外交活动和国家职能活动中都必须以最高的专业水准举止得体。

2. The committee found that the proposed regulations did not **comport** with the constitutional provisions protecting individual civil liberties.
   委员会发现，拟议的法规与保护个人公民自由权的宪法条款不相符。

3. Students who fail to **comport** themselves according to the university's code of conduct may face disciplinary action or suspension.
   未能按照大学行为准则规范自身举止的学生可能面临纪律处分或停学。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "comport" as "com-PORT" — how you "carry" (Latin portare = to carry) yourself in company, i.e., your behavior.
把"comport"想成"随身携带"——拉丁语词根portare意为"携带"，举止就是你随身携带的行为方式。
