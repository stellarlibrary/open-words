# 👶 birthright

🔊 /ˈbɜːrθraɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A right or privilege that one is entitled to by birth; especially the rights of the firstborn
**中文：** n. 与生俱来的权利；长子继承权

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- inheritance（继承权）
- patrimony（世袭财产）
- prerogative（特权）
- entitlement（应有的权利）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sell one's birthright / claim one's birthright / deny someone's birthright / a fundamental birthright**

👉 Example: Freedom of expression is often regarded as a birthright in democratic societies.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The constitution affirms that liberty and the pursuit of happiness are the **birthright** of every citizen.
   宪法确认自由和追求幸福是每个公民与生俱来的权利。

2. In the biblical narrative, Esau famously sold his **birthright** to his younger brother Jacob for a bowl of stew.
   在圣经故事中，以扫为了一碗红豆汤将自己的长子继承权卖给了弟弟雅各。

3. Many philosophers argue that access to education should be considered a universal **birthright** rather than a privilege.
   许多哲学家认为，受教育的机会应被视为一种与生俱来的权利，而非一种特权。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "birth" + "right" — a right you receive simply by being born into this world.
把"birthright"拆成"birth"（出生）+"right"（权利）——一出生就拥有的权利。
