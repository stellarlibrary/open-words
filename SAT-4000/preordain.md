# ✨ preordain

🔊 /ˌpriːɔːrˈdeɪn/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To decide or determine an outcome in advance; to ordain or decree beforehand, as by divine will or fate
**中文：** v. 预先决定；注定；命中注定

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- predestine（注定）
- foreordain（预先注定）
- predetermine（预先决定）
- destine（命定）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **preordained outcome / preordained fate / preordained destiny / preordained to succeed/fail**

👉 Example: Many theologians debated whether human destiny is preordained or shaped by individual free will.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosopher argued that nothing in human history is **preordained** and that societies are shaped by deliberate collective choices.
   这位哲学家认为，人类历史中没有什么是注定的，社会是由深思熟虑的集体选择塑造的。

2. Critics dismissed the election results as a **preordained** conclusion, citing the overwhelming imbalance of resources between candidates.
   批评人士将选举结果视为预先注定的结局，理由是候选人之间资源的巨大不平衡。

3. The novel explores whether the protagonist's tragic downfall was **preordained** by fate or caused by his own moral failures.
   这部小说探讨了主人公的悲剧性衰落是命中注定的，还是由其自身的道德缺失造成的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-ordain" as "ordered in advance" — like placing an order before the event, meaning the outcome was already decided.
把"preordain"想成"pre（预先）+ ordain（命令、注定）"——预先下达的命令，即注定的结果。
