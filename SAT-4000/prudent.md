# 🧠 prudent

🔊 /ˈpruːdənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Acting with or showing care and thought for the future; exercising good judgment and caution in practical matters
**中文：** adj. 谨慎的；精明的；节俭的；深谋远虑的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cautious（谨慎的）
- judicious（明智的）
- sensible（合理的）
- wise（睿智的）
- circumspect（审慎的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prudent decision / prudent approach / prudent investor / it would be prudent to**

👉 Example: A prudent investor diversifies their portfolio to minimize exposure to financial risk.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee deemed it **prudent** to postpone the expansion plan until more reliable economic forecasts became available.
   委员会认为在获得更可靠的经济预测之前推迟扩张计划是谨慎的做法。

2. Financial advisors consistently recommend that young professionals adopt a **prudent** approach to saving and long-term investment.
   财务顾问一贯建议年轻专业人士对储蓄和长期投资采取审慎的态度。

3. The governor's **prudent** management of state resources during the crisis earned widespread praise from constituents and analysts.
   州长在危机期间对州资源的审慎管理赢得了选民和分析人士的广泛赞誉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "prudent" as "prune + dent" — a prudent person prunes unnecessary risks to prevent dents in their plans.
把"prudent"想成"prune + dent"——一个谨慎的人会修剪不必要的风险，以防止计划出现凹痕。
