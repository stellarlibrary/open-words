# 😳 abash

🔊 /əˈbæʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make someone feel embarrassed, self-conscious, or ill at ease
**中文：** v. 使困窘；使羞愧；使局促不安

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- embarrass（使尴尬）
- disconcert（使不安）
- fluster（使慌乱）
- mortify（使难堪）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **abashed by / abashed at / feel abashed / completely abashed**

👉 Example: The young intern was visibly abashed by the senior executive's harsh public criticism.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The unexpected question from the journalist clearly **abashed** the spokesperson during the live press conference.
   记者出其不意的提问明显让新闻发言人在现场新闻发布会上感到窘迫。

2. Nothing could **abash** the seasoned diplomat, who remained composed even under the most intense scrutiny.
   没有什么能让这位经验丰富的外交官感到难堪，即使在最严格的审视下他依然镇定自若。

3. He was deeply **abashed** when his factual errors were exposed before the entire academic committee.
   当他的事实错误在整个学术委员会面前被揭露时，他深感羞愧。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "a-bash" — getting bashed (hit) emotionally makes you feel embarrassed and ashamed.
想想"a-bash"——在情感上被bash（打击）会让你感到尴尬和羞愧。
