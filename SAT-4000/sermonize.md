# 🎤 sermonize

🔊 /ˈsɜːrmənaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To deliver a sermon; to lecture someone about moral or ethical matters in a tedious or preachy manner
**中文：** v. 讲道，布道；对……说教

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- preach（说教）
- moralize（教训）
- lecture（训诫）
- pontificate（自以为是地说教）
- admonish（劝诫）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sermonize about / sermonize on / sermonize to someone / constantly sermonize**

👉 Example: He had a tendency to sermonize about the virtues of hard work whenever he met young people.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician would **sermonize** endlessly about fiscal responsibility while simultaneously approving enormous government expenditures.
   这位政客会无休止地说教财政责任，同时却批准巨额政府开支。

2. Rather than **sermonize** about the importance of education, the mentor chose to lead by personal example.
   这位导师没有对教育的重要性进行说教，而是选择以身作则。

3. Critics accused the novelist of using her characters merely to **sermonize** about social injustice rather than telling compelling stories.
   批评者指责这位小说家仅仅利用她的角色来说教社会不公，而非讲述引人入胜的故事。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sermonize" as "sermon + ize" — to make a sermon out of everything, lecturing people as if you were a preacher at a pulpit.
把"sermonize"想成"sermon + ize"——把一切都变成布道，像站在讲坛上的传教士一样对人说教。
