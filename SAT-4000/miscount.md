# 🔢 miscount

🔊 /ˌmɪsˈkaʊnt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To count incorrectly; to make an error in calculating a number. (n.) An incorrect count or tally
**中文：** v. 数错；算错 n. 数错；误算

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- miscalculate（算错）
- err（出错）
- misjudge（误判）
- overcount（多算）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **miscount the votes / miscount the ballots / a serious miscount / miscount the inventory**

👉 Example: Officials were forced to recount after discovering a significant miscount in the election results.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Election officials acknowledged that they had **miscounted** several hundred ballots, prompting an immediate and thorough recount of all votes.
   选举官员承认他们数错了几百张选票，促使立即对所有选票进行彻底重新计票。

2. The accountant's **miscount** of the quarterly inventory led to a significant discrepancy in the company's annual financial report.
   会计对季度库存的误算导致公司年度财务报告出现重大差异。

3. Researchers discovered a **miscount** in the original data set that had fundamentally undermined the validity of the published study.
   研究人员发现原始数据集中存在计算错误，从根本上破坏了已发表研究的有效性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mis-count" — "mis" means wrongly, "count" means to tally numbers. A miscount is simply counting wrong.
把"miscount"拆成"mis（错误地）+ count（计数）"——就是数错了，计算出了差错。
