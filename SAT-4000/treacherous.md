# 🐍 treacherous

🔊 /ˈtretʃərəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Guilty of or involving betrayal or deception; hazardous because of presenting hidden or unpredictable dangers
**中文：** adj. 奸诈的，叛逆的；危险的；不可靠的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- perfidious（背信弃义的）
- deceitful（欺诈的）
- treasonous（叛逆的）
- perilous（危险的）
- hazardous（有害的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **treacherous conditions / treacherous waters / treacherous path / treacherous ally**

👉 Example: The mountaineers had to navigate treacherous terrain before reaching the summit.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat's **treacherous** betrayal of state secrets compromised national security and endangered countless operatives abroad.
   这位外交官对国家机密的背叛危及了国家安全，并使海外无数特工陷入危险。

2. Sailors have long feared the **treacherous** currents near the strait, which have claimed dozens of vessels.
   水手们长期以来一直惧怕海峡附近危险的水流，那里已经吞噬了数十艘船只。

3. The seemingly calm surface of the frozen lake concealed **treacherous** ice that could give way without warning.
   冰冻湖面看似平静的表面下隐藏着危险的冰层，随时可能毫无预兆地坍塌。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "treacherous" as "treason + erous" — someone full of treason is treacherous, always ready to betray.
把"treacherous"想成"treason + erous"——一个充满叛逆之心的人就是奸诈的，随时准备背叛。
