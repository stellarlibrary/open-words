# 🌫️ ambiguous

🔊 /æmˈbɪɡjuəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Open to more than one interpretation; not having a clear or definite meaning; uncertain or doubtful in nature
**中文：** 模糊不清的；引起歧义的；不明确的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- vague（模糊的）
- equivocal（含糊的）
- unclear（不清楚的）
- enigmatic（令人费解的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ambiguous statement / ambiguous language / morally ambiguous / deliberately ambiguous**

👉 Example: The contract contained several ambiguous clauses that led to a prolonged legal dispute.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician's **ambiguous** response left journalists uncertain about the administration's actual position on the issue.
   这位政治家模糊不清的回答让记者们对政府在该问题上的实际立场感到不确定。

2. The novel's **ambiguous** ending has sparked decades of scholarly debate about the protagonist's ultimate fate.
   这部小说模糊的结局引发了学术界数十年来关于主人公最终命运的争论。

3. Legal experts advised the company to revise the **ambiguous** wording before the agreement was formally executed.
   法律专家建议公司在协议正式签署之前修改其中含糊不清的措辞。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ambi-" (both ways) + "-guous" (driving) — the word drives you in both directions because it can mean two things.
把"ambiguous"想成"ambi-"（两边）+ "-guous"（驱动）——这个词把你往两个方向驱动，因为它可以有两种含义。
