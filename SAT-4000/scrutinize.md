# 🧐 scrutinize

🔊 /ˈskruːtɪnaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To examine closely and carefully; to inspect thoroughly
**中文：** v. 细察

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- examine（检查）
- inspect（审查）
- analyze（分析）
- investigate（调查）
- probe（探究）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **scrutinize the evidence / scrutinize a document / scrutinize closely / scrutinize every detail**

👉 Example: The auditors were hired to scrutinize the company's financial statements for any irregularities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The Senate committee convened to **scrutinize** the proposed budget allocation before granting final approval for the fiscal year expenditure.
   参议院委员会召开会议细察拟议的预算分配，然后才对本财年支出给予最终批准。

2. Immigration officers are trained to **scrutinize** travel documents carefully in order to detect forgeries and prevent unauthorized border crossings.
   移民官员接受过培训，会仔细审查旅行证件，以发现伪造文件并防止非法越境。

3. The literary critic chose to **scrutinize** the author's use of symbolism throughout the novel, revealing previously overlooked thematic patterns.
   这位文学评论家选择细查作者在整部小说中对象征手法的运用，揭示了之前被忽视的主题模式。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "scrutinize" as "scrut-in-eyes" — putting something right in your eyes to look at it as closely as possible.
把"scrutinize"想成"scrut-in-eyes"——把东西放到眼前，尽可能仔细地观察。
