# ⚖️ redress

🔊 /rɪˈdrɛs/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To remedy or set right; to correct or compensate for a wrong. (n.) Remedy, compensation, or relief from a grievance
**中文：** v. 救济；赔偿；纠正 n. 救济；赔偿；矫正

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- remedy（补救）
- rectify（纠正）
- compensate（赔偿）
- amend（修正）
- restitution（赔偿）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **redress a grievance / redress the balance / seek redress / redress an injustice**

👉 Example: The new legislation was designed to redress decades of systemic inequality in the education system.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court ordered the corporation to **redress** the environmental damage caused by years of unregulated chemical waste disposal.
   法院命令该公司纠正多年无管制化学废物排放造成的环境损害。

2. Citizens petitioned the government to provide adequate **redress** for families displaced by the controversial infrastructure development project.
   公民请愿政府为因有争议的基础设施开发项目而流离失所的家庭提供适当的赔偿。

3. The organization worked tirelessly to **redress** the historical injustices suffered by marginalized communities across the entire region.
   该组织不知疲倦地致力于纠正整个地区边缘化社区所遭受的历史不公正。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-dress" — to "dress" a wound again, meaning to fix or correct something that has gone wrong.
把"redress"想成"re-dress"——重新"包扎"伤口，意味着修复或纠正出了问题的事情。
