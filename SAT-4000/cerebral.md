# 🧠 cerebral

🔊 /səˈriːbrəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Of or relating to the brain or cerebrum; intellectual rather than emotional or physical
**中文：** adj. 脑的，大脑的；理智的，用脑的（而非情感的或体力的）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- intellectual（智力的）
- cognitive（认知的）
- analytical（分析的）
- scholarly（学术的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cerebral cortex / cerebral activity / cerebral approach / cerebral palsy**

👉 Example: His cerebral approach to problem-solving often yielded innovative solutions that others overlooked.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's lectures were highly **cerebral**, demanding intense concentration and advanced analytical reasoning from every student.
   这位教授的讲座极具思辨性，要求每位学生高度集中注意力并运用高级分析推理。

2. Critics praised the film for its **cerebral** narrative structure, which challenged audiences to interpret multiple layers of meaning.
   评论家称赞这部电影富有理性的叙事结构，它要求观众解读多层次的含义。

3. A **cerebral** hemorrhage occurs when a blood vessel within the brain ruptures, causing potentially life-threatening internal bleeding.
   脑出血发生在大脑内的血管破裂时，可能导致危及生命的内部出血。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cerebral" as coming from "cerebrum" (the largest part of the brain) — anything "cerebral" relates to the brain or deep thinking, as opposed to gut feelings.
把"cerebral"想成来自"cerebrum"（大脑最大的部分）——任何"cerebral"的事物都与大脑或深度思考有关，而非直觉。
