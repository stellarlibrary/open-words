# 🛋️ indolence

🔊 /ˈɪndələns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Avoidance of activity or exertion; habitual laziness; a disposition to remain idle or inactive
**中文：** n. 懒散；懒惰；怠惰

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- laziness（懒惰）
- sloth（怠惰）
- lethargy（无精打采）
- idleness（闲散）
- torpor（迟钝）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sink into indolence / life of indolence / overcome indolence / bred by indolence**

👉 Example: The philosopher warned that prolonged indolence would erode both the intellect and the moral character.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The once-promising scholar succumbed to **indolence** and abandoned his doctoral research after years of declining academic motivation.
   这位曾经前途光明的学者屈服于懒惰，在多年学术动力下降之后放弃了他的博士研究。

2. Critics attributed the empire's gradual decline to the ruling class's **indolence** and their refusal to address systemic economic problems.
   批评者将帝国的逐渐衰落归因于统治阶层的怠惰以及他们拒绝解决系统性经济问题。

3. The headmaster delivered a stern lecture on the dangers of **indolence**, urging students to cultivate discipline and intellectual curiosity.
   校长就懒惰的危害发表了严厉演讲，敦促学生培养自律精神和求知欲。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-dolence" as "in-dull-ence" — indulging in dullness, doing nothing, just lying around being lazy and idle.
把"indolence"想成"in-dull-ence"——沉溺于无聊之中，什么都不做，只是懒洋洋地躺着无所事事。
