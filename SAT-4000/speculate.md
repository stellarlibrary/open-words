# 🤔 speculate

🔊 /ˈspekjʊleɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To form a theory or conjecture without firm evidence; to invest in stocks or ventures hoping for large gains but with risk of loss.
**中文：** v. 推测，思索；做投机买卖

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conjecture（推测）
- theorize（推论）
- hypothesize（假设）
- guess（猜测）
- gamble（投机）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **speculate about / speculate on / speculate that / speculate in the market / widely speculated**

👉 Example: Analysts began to speculate about the company's future after its unexpected leadership change.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Scientists can only **speculate** about the origins of the universe based on the limited observational evidence available.
   科学家只能根据有限的观测证据来推测宇宙的起源。

2. Investors who **speculate** in volatile markets often face significant financial losses alongside potential gains.
   在波动市场中做投机买卖的投资者常常面临重大经济损失和潜在收益并存的局面。

3. The media continued to **speculate** that the prime minister would announce her resignation before the end of the week.
   媒体继续推测首相将在本周结束前宣布辞职。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "specul-ate" from Latin "speculari" meaning "to observe" — when you speculate, you observe clues and guess at the truth.
把"speculate"联想到拉丁语"speculari"（观察）——当你speculate时，你观察线索并猜测真相。
