# 💪 confront

🔊 /kənˈfrʌnt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To face or encounter something difficult or hostile; to challenge someone directly; to compare or bring face to face
**中文：** v. 使面临，使遭遇；对抗，面对

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- face（面对）
- encounter（遭遇）
- challenge（挑战）
- oppose（对抗）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **confront a problem / confront the truth / confront someone about / be confronted with**

👉 Example: The committee must confront the issue of declining enrollment before it becomes irreversible.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The newly elected president was immediately **confronted** with an economic crisis that demanded swift and decisive action.
   新当选的总统立即面临一场需要迅速果断行动的经济危机。

2. Rather than avoiding the controversy, the researcher chose to **confront** the methodological flaws in the widely cited study.
   研究者没有回避争议，而是选择正面面对这项被广泛引用的研究中的方法论缺陷。

3. Environmental activists **confronted** the corporation's executives during the annual meeting about their pollution of local waterways.
   环保活动人士在年度会议上就企业污染当地水道一事当面质问了公司高管。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con-FRONT" — you stand in FRONT of a challenge, facing it directly and head-on.
把"confront"想成"con-FRONT"——你站在挑战的前面（FRONT），直面它。
