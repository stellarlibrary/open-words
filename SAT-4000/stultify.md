# 🚫 stultify

🔊 /ˈstʌltɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To render useless or ineffective; to make someone appear foolish or absurd; to negate or nullify
**中文：** v. 使显得愚笨；使变无效，使成为徒劳

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- negate（否定）
- nullify（使无效）
- thwart（阻碍）
- stifle（抑制）
- undermine（削弱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **stultify creativity / stultify efforts / stultify growth / stultifying routine**

👉 Example: The rigid bureaucratic procedures served only to stultify innovation within the organization.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Excessive regulation can **stultify** economic growth by discouraging entrepreneurs from pursuing innovative business ventures and investments.
   过度的监管会通过阻碍企业家追求创新商业投资来使经济增长变得无效。

2. The oppressive censorship laws effectively **stultified** artistic expression, leaving an entire generation of writers unable to publish freely.
   压迫性的审查法律实际上扼杀了艺术表达，使整整一代作家无法自由出版。

3. Critics argued that the repetitive curriculum would **stultify** students rather than encourage the critical thinking skills they desperately needed.
   批评者认为，这种重复的课程会使学生变得愚钝，而不是培养他们迫切需要的批判性思维能力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "stult-ify" as related to "stultus" (Latin for foolish) — to stultify is to make something foolish, useless, or void.
把"stultify"想成与"stultus"（拉丁语"愚蠢"）有关——stultify就是使某事变得愚蠢、无用或无效。
