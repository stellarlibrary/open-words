# 🛡️ safeguard

🔊 /ˈseɪfɡɑːrd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A measure taken to protect or prevent something; a protective device or provision. (v.) To protect or ensure the safety of something
**中文：** v. 维护，保护，捍卫 n. 安全装置，安全措施

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- protect（保护）
- shield（屏蔽）
- preserve（维护）
- defend（防卫）
- precaution（预防措施）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **safeguard against / safeguard interests / safeguard the environment / safeguard democracy**

👉 Example: The new legislation was designed to safeguard consumers against deceptive and fraudulent business practices.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. International organizations have implemented multiple **safeguards** to prevent the proliferation of nuclear weapons among hostile nations.
   国际组织已实施多项安全措施，以防止核武器在敌对国家间扩散。

2. The regulatory framework was established to **safeguard** the financial system from the kind of reckless speculation that caused the crisis.
   该监管框架的建立旨在保护金融体系免受导致危机的那种鲁莽投机行为的影响。

3. Parents must take deliberate steps to **safeguard** their children's personal information in an increasingly digital world.
   在日益数字化的世界中，父母必须采取刻意的措施来保护子女的个人信息。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "safe-guard" — literally a "guard" that keeps things "safe." A safeguard is anything that guards your safety.
把"safeguard"直接拆解为"safe（安全）+guard（守卫）"——守护安全的人或措施，即保护手段。
