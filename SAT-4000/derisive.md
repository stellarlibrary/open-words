# 😤 derisive

🔊 /dɪˈraɪsɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Expressing contempt or ridicule; mocking and scornful
**中文：** adj. 嘲笑的；嘲弄的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- mocking（嘲弄的）
- scornful（轻蔑的）
- contemptuous（蔑视的）
- sneering（讥笑的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **derisive laughter / derisive tone / derisive comment / derisive snort**

👉 Example: His proposal was met with derisive laughter from the entire committee.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The audience responded with **derisive** laughter when the speaker presented data that clearly contradicted well-established scientific findings.
   当演讲者展示明显与公认科学发现相矛盾的数据时，观众发出了嘲弄的笑声。

2. Her **derisive** tone during the debate alienated potential allies who might otherwise have supported her legislative proposal.
   她在辩论中嘲讽的语气疏远了原本可能支持她立法提案的潜在盟友。

3. The editorial adopted a **derisive** stance toward the administration's environmental policy, calling it woefully inadequate and superficial.
   社论对政府的环境政策采取了嘲弄的立场，称其严重不足且流于表面。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "derisive" as "de-risk-ive" reversed — a derisive person takes no risk in hiding their contempt; they openly mock others.
把"derisive"想成与"de-risk-ive"相反——一个嘲弄的人毫不掩饰他们的蔑视，公开嘲笑别人。
