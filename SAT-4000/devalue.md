# 📉 devalue

🔊 /diːˈvæljuː/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To reduce the worth or importance of something; to lower the official value of a currency relative to other currencies
**中文：** v. 使贬值；降低……的价值

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- depreciate（贬值）
- diminish（减少）
- degrade（降低）
- undervalue（低估）
- cheapen（贬低）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **devalue the currency / devalue someone's contribution / devalue the importance of**

👉 Example: The central bank decided to devalue the national currency in response to the economic crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government was forced to **devalue** its currency by fifteen percent to address the mounting trade deficit.
   政府被迫将其货币贬值百分之十五，以应对不断增加的贸易逆差。

2. Critics accused the new policy of **devaluing** the contributions of experienced teachers in the public school system.
   批评者指责新政策贬低了公立学校系统中经验丰富的教师的贡献。

3. Flooding the market with counterfeit goods will inevitably **devalue** authentic products and erode consumer trust.
   市场上充斥假冒商品将不可避免地降低正品的价值并侵蚀消费者信任。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-value" as literally "removing value" — when you devalue something, you strip away its worth or importance.
把"devalue"想成"de(去除) + value(价值)"——当你devalue某物时，你剥夺了它的价值或重要性。
