# ⏳ reprieve

🔊 /rɪˈpriːv/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To postpone or cancel a punishment; to provide temporary relief from danger or trouble. (n.) A temporary delay of punishment; a respite
**中文：** v. 缓期执行；暂时解救；暂减 n. 暂缓；缓刑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- respite（暂缓）
- pardon（赦免）
- postponement（推迟）
- clemency（宽恕）
- relief（缓解）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **grant a reprieve / temporary reprieve / reprieve from punishment / last-minute reprieve**

👉 Example: The governor granted the condemned prisoner a last-minute reprieve pending further review of the evidence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court granted a temporary **reprieve** to the defendant, allowing additional time for new evidence to be examined.
   法院给予被告临时缓刑，允许更多时间审查新证据。

2. The unexpected rainfall provided a welcome **reprieve** from the relentless drought that had devastated agricultural regions for months.
   意外的降雨为已经数月摧毁农业地区的持续干旱带来了一次令人欣慰的缓解。

3. Diplomatic negotiations offered a brief **reprieve** from hostilities, though neither side expected the ceasefire to endure indefinitely.
   外交谈判为敌对行动提供了短暂的喘息，尽管双方都不指望停火能无限期持续。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-prieve" — "prieve" sounds like "relieve." A reprieve relieves you from punishment, at least temporarily.
把"reprieve"想成"re"+"prieve"——"prieve"听起来像"relieve"（缓解）。Reprieve就是暂时解除你的惩罚。
