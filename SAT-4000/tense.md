# 😰 tense

🔊 /tɛns/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, verb, noun | 形容词, 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Feeling or showing nervousness; stretched tight. (v.) To become tight or rigid. (n.) A form of a verb indicating the time of an action.
**中文：** adj. 紧张的；拉紧的 v. 变得紧张 n. 时态

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- anxious（焦虑的）
- strained（紧绷的）
- rigid（僵硬的）
- nervous（紧张的）
- taut（绷紧的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tense atmosphere / tense moment / tense muscles / tense up / past tense**

👉 Example: The atmosphere in the courtroom grew increasingly tense as the verdict was about to be announced.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Negotiations between the two nations became extremely **tense** after the diplomatic envoy was unexpectedly recalled from the embassy.
   两国之间的谈判在外交特使被意外召回后变得极其紧张。

2. The athlete began to **tense** every muscle in her body moments before the starting signal echoed through the stadium.
   运动员在起跑信号响彻体育场前的瞬间开始绷紧全身每一块肌肉。

3. Students must learn to identify the correct **tense** of a verb to construct grammatically accurate sentences in formal writing.
   学生必须学会辨别动词的正确时态，以便在正式写作中构造语法准确的句子。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tense" as a "tent" stretched tight — when a tent is pulled tight, it is tense, just like your muscles when you are nervous.
把"tense"想成一个被拉紧的"tent（帐篷）"——帐篷被拉紧时就是tense的状态，就像你紧张时的肌肉一样。
