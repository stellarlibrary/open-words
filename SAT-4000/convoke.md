# 📢 convoke

🔊 /kənˈvoʊk/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To call together or summon people to a formal assembly or meeting, especially by authority
**中文：** v. 召集；召集…开会

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- summon（召集）
- assemble（集合）
- convene（召开）
- muster（召集）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **convoke a meeting / convoke an assembly / convoke a session / convoke Parliament**

👉 Example: The president convoked an emergency session of Parliament to address the escalating national crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The university chancellor decided to **convoke** an extraordinary faculty meeting to discuss the proposed changes to the academic curriculum.
   大学校长决定召集一次特别教职员工会议，讨论对学术课程的拟议修改。

2. International leaders agreed to **convoke** a global summit on climate change to establish binding environmental protection agreements.
   国际领导人同意召集一次全球气候变化峰会，以制定具有约束力的环境保护协议。

3. The chairman exercised his authority to **convoke** the board of directors for an urgent review of the organization's financial situation.
   主席行使权力召集董事会，对该组织的财务状况进行紧急审查。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con-voke" as "call voices together" — the root "voc" means voice, so convoke means to call all voices together for a meeting.
把"convoke"想成"call voices together"——词根"voc"意为声音，所以convoke意为召集所有声音来开会。
