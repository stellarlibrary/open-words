# 🫣 bawdy

🔊 /ˈbɔːdi/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Humorously indecent or vulgar; dealing with sexual matters in a comical or lewd way
**中文：** adj. 猥亵的；下流的；好色的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ribald（下流的）
- lewd（猥亵的）
- vulgar（粗俗的）
- indecent（不雅的）
- risque（有伤风化的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bawdy humor / bawdy joke / bawdy language / bawdy comedy / bawdy song**

👉 Example: The playwright was famous for writing bawdy comedies that simultaneously entertained and scandalized audiences.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Shakespeare's plays frequently contain **bawdy** humor that modern audiences may not immediately recognize beneath the archaic language.
   莎士比亚的戏剧中经常包含猥亵的幽默，现代观众可能不会立刻在古老的语言中识别出来。

2. The professor warned students that the medieval text contained **bawdy** passages reflecting the social norms of that era.
   教授警告学生，这篇中世纪文本中包含反映那个时代社会规范的下流段落。

3. Critics praised the film for its clever use of **bawdy** wit, noting that it never descended into gratuitous vulgarity.
   评论家称赞这部电影巧妙地运用了粗俗的机智，指出它从未沦为无端的低俗。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bawdy" as "body" — bawdy humor is all about the body and its indecent, physical comedy.
把"bawdy"想成"body"（身体）——猥亵的幽默都与身体和不雅的肢体喜剧有关。
