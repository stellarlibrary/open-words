# ⚠️ imperil

🔊 /ɪmˈpɛrəl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To put into danger; to expose to risk of harm, destruction, or loss
**中文：** v. 危及；使陷于危险；使处于危险；危害

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- endanger（危及）
- jeopardize（危害）
- threaten（威胁）
- risk（使冒险）
- hazard（使遭受危险）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **imperil lives / imperil the future / imperil national security / imperil the environment**

👉 Example: Reckless deforestation practices continue to imperil countless species in the tropical rainforest.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The proposed budget cuts would seriously **imperil** public health programs that serve millions of vulnerable citizens nationwide.
   拟议的预算削减将严重危及为全国数百万弱势群体服务的公共卫生项目。

2. Rising sea levels increasingly **imperil** low-lying island nations, threatening both their economies and their very long-term existence.
   海平面上升日益危及低洼岛国，威胁着它们的经济和长期存续。

3. The diplomatic scandal threatened to **imperil** years of carefully negotiated trade agreements between the two allied nations.
   这一外交丑闻威胁着两个盟国之间多年来精心谈判达成的贸易协定。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-peril" as "in peril" — to imperil someone is to put them in peril, placing them directly into a dangerous situation.
把"imperil"想成"im（进入）+ peril（危险）"——让某人进入危险之中，也就是使其处于危险。
