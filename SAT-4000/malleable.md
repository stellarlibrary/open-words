# 🔨 malleable

🔊 /ˈmæliəbəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (Of a metal or other material) Able to be hammered or pressed into shape without breaking. (Of a person) Easily influenced; adaptable and pliable
**中文：** adj. 可锻的；可塑的；有延展性的；易适应的；易受影响的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pliable（柔韧的）
- ductile（可延展的）
- impressionable（易受影响的）
- adaptable（适应性强的）
- flexible（灵活的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **malleable material / malleable personality / malleable mind / highly malleable / malleable enough to**

👉 Example: Gold is one of the most malleable metals, capable of being hammered into extremely thin sheets.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sculptor selected copper for the installation because the metal was sufficiently **malleable** to be shaped into intricate organic forms.
   雕塑家选择铜来制作这件装置作品，因为这种金属有足够的延展性，可以被塑造成复杂的有机形态。

2. Young children have remarkably **malleable** minds, which is precisely why early education plays such a critical developmental role.
   幼儿的思维具有极强的可塑性，这正是早期教育在发展中扮演如此关键角色的原因。

3. The committee feared that the newly appointed director was too **malleable** and would simply yield to pressure from corporate lobbyists.
   委员会担心新任命的主任太容易受影响，会轻易屈服于企业游说者的压力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "malle-able" as "mallet-able" — something you can hit with a mallet and reshape, like soft metal or a flexible mind.
把"malleable"想成"mallet-able"（可用木槌敲打的）——可以用木槌敲打并重新塑形的东西，就像软金属或灵活的头脑。
