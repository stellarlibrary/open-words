# 😤 antagonize

🔊 /ænˈtæɡənaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To cause someone to become hostile or angry; to provoke opposition or enmity in someone
**中文：** 使…敌对；使…对抗；激怒；引起…的敌意

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- provoke（激怒）
- irritate（使恼怒）
- alienate（疏远）
- aggravate（使恶化）
- offend（冒犯）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **antagonize the opposition / antagonize the public / deliberately antagonize / antagonize one's allies**

👉 Example: His confrontational remarks served only to antagonize the committee members who had previously been sympathetic.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator's inflammatory rhetoric threatened to **antagonize** moderate voters whose support was essential for the upcoming election.
   这位参议员的煽动性言论有可能激怒温和派选民，而他们的支持对即将到来的选举至关重要。

2. Experienced diplomats understand that it is unwise to **antagonize** a negotiating partner before the terms of agreement have been settled.
   经验丰富的外交官明白，在协议条款敲定之前激怒谈判伙伴是不明智的。

3. The new manager's abrasive leadership style continued to **antagonize** the staff, resulting in widespread dissatisfaction and high turnover.
   新经理粗暴的领导风格持续引起员工的敌意，导致普遍不满和高离职率。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "antagonize" as "making an antagonist out of someone" — when you antagonize people, you turn them into your opponents.
把"antagonize"想成"把某人变成antagonist（对手）"——当你antagonize别人时，你就把他们变成了你的敌人。
