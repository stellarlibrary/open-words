# ✨ scintillate

🔊 /ˈsɪntɪleɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To emit sparks; to sparkle or flash
**中文：** v. 发出火花

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sparkle（闪耀）
- glitter（闪烁）
- flash（闪光）
- shimmer（微光闪烁）
- twinkle（闪烁）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **scintillate with wit / scintillating conversation / scintillating performance**

👉 Example: The professor's scintillating lecture captivated every student in the auditorium.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diamonds on the chandelier began to **scintillate** under the ballroom lights, casting brilliant reflections across the ceiling.
   枝形吊灯上的钻石在舞厅灯光下开始闪烁，在天花板上投射出耀眼的光芒。

2. Her **scintillating** remarks during the debate demonstrated a sharp intellect and an impressive command of the subject matter.
   她在辩论中精彩的言辞展现了敏锐的才智和对该主题令人印象深刻的掌握。

3. The night sky appeared to **scintillate** with thousands of stars visible far from the city's intrusive artificial illumination.
   远离城市人工照明的干扰，夜空中数千颗星星似乎闪闪发光。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "scintillate" as containing "tin" — imagine a piece of tin foil catching light and sparkling brightly in the sun.
把"scintillate"想成含有"tin"（锡）——想象一片锡箔在阳光下闪闪发光。
