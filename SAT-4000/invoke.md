# 🙏 invoke

🔊 /ɪnˈvoʊk/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To call upon for aid or support; to appeal to as an authority; to bring about or cause; to summon through incantation
**中文：** v. 调用；祈求；引起；恳求；唤起

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- summon（召唤）
- appeal to（诉诸）
- call upon（呼吁）
- evoke（唤起）
- cite（引用）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **invoke a law / invoke the right / invoke a spirit / invoke the name of**

👉 Example: The defense attorney chose to invoke the Fifth Amendment to protect the client from self-incrimination.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator decided to **invoke** an obscure parliamentary rule to delay the vote on the controversial legislation.
   这位参议员决定援引一项鲜为人知的议会规则来推迟对这项有争议立法的投票。

2. The artist's haunting paintings seemed to **invoke** a deep sense of melancholy and longing in every viewer.
   这位艺术家令人难忘的画作似乎唤起了每位观者内心深处的忧郁和渴望。

3. Environmental activists frequently **invoke** scientific evidence to support their demands for stronger climate protection policies.
   环保活动家经常引用科学证据来支持他们对更强气候保护政策的要求。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-VOKE" — "voke" sounds like "voice," so to invoke is to use your voice to call upon something or someone for help.
把"invoke"想成"in-VOKE"——"voke"听起来像"voice"（声音），所以invoke就是用你的声音呼唤某事或某人来帮助。
