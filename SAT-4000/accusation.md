# ⚖️ accusation

🔊 /ˌæk.juːˈzeɪ.ʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A formal charge or claim that someone has done something wrong or illegal.
**中文：** 控告；指控；谴责

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- allegation（指控）
- indictment（起诉）
- charge（指控）
- impeachment（弹劾）
- denunciation（谴责）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **make/bring/face/deny an accusation; accusation of/against**

👉 Example: He vehemently denied the accusation of misconduct.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician faced a serious **accusation** of corruption that threatened to end his long career in office.
   这位政治家面临严重的腐败指控，这可能终结他漫长的从政生涯。

2. The committee launched a formal investigation after multiple **accusations** of academic fraud surfaced at the university.
   在多起学术欺诈的指控浮出水面后，委员会对该大学启动了正式调查。

3. Without credible evidence, an **accusation** remains merely an unsubstantiated claim in any court of law.
   没有可信的证据，指控在任何法庭上都只是一个未经证实的说法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think "ac-CUS-ation" — someone points a finger at the "accused," making a formal accusation in a courtroom.
accusation 来自 accuse（控告）+ -ation（名词后缀），"控告"的行为或结果就是"指控"。
