# 🔬 precursor

🔊 /prɪˈkɜːrsər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person or thing that comes before another of the same kind; a forerunner or harbinger that indicates what is to follow
**中文：** n. 先驱；前导；先兆

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- forerunner（先驱）
- harbinger（预兆）
- predecessor（前任）
- herald（先驱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **precursor to / precursor of / an important precursor / a direct precursor**

👉 Example: The early experiments served as a precursor to the groundbreaking discoveries that followed.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The invention of the telegraph was a significant **precursor** to modern telecommunications and digital networking systems.
   电报的发明是现代电信和数字网络系统的重要先驱。

2. Rising unemployment rates are often considered a **precursor** to broader economic downturns and financial instability.
   失业率上升往往被视为更大范围经济衰退和金融不稳定的前兆。

3. The philosopher's radical ideas served as a **precursor** to the revolutionary social movements of the following century.
   这位哲学家的激进思想是下一个世纪革命性社会运动的先导。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-cursor" as "before the cursor" — the cursor moves forward, but something came before it, leading the way.
把"precursor"想成"pre（前）+ cursor（光标）"——光标向前移动，但有东西在它之前引领方向。
