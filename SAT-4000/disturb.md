# 😣 disturb

🔊 /dɪˈstɜːrb/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To interrupt or interfere with; to cause anxiety or unease; to disrupt the normal condition of something
**中文：** v. 打扰；妨碍；使不安；扰乱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disrupt（扰乱）
- unsettle（使不安）
- agitate（使焦虑）
- perturb（使烦恼）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **disturb the peace / disturb someone's sleep / do not disturb / deeply disturbing**

👉 Example: The loud construction noise continued to disturb the residents throughout the entire afternoon.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor asked students not to **disturb** the delicate laboratory equipment during the course of their experiments.
   教授要求学生在实验过程中不要触碰精密的实验室设备。

2. New evidence has begun to **disturb** the long-held assumptions that scholars maintained about the origins of the civilization.
   新的证据开始动摇学者们长期以来对该文明起源所持有的假设。

3. The government's unexpected policy reversal managed to **disturb** the stability of international financial markets for several weeks.
   政府出人意料的政策逆转在数周内扰乱了国际金融市场的稳定。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-turb" as "dis-turbine" — like throwing something into a turbine that disrupts its smooth spinning.
把"disturb"想成"dis-turb(ine)"——就像往涡轮机里扔了东西，打乱了它平稳的运转。
