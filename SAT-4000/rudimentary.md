# 📐 rudimentary

🔊 /ˌruːdɪˈmɛntəri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Basic; elementary; involving or limited to basic principles; undeveloped or vestigial
**中文：** adj. 基础的；未发展的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- basic（基本的）
- elementary（初级的）
- primitive（原始的）
- fundamental（基础的）
- embryonic（未发展的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rudimentary knowledge / rudimentary skills / rudimentary understanding / rudimentary form**

👉 Example: Students must acquire a rudimentary understanding of statistics before enrolling in advanced courses.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The archaeological team discovered **rudimentary** stone tools that provided valuable insight into early human civilization and survival techniques.
   考古团队发现了原始的石器工具，为了解早期人类文明和生存技术提供了宝贵的见解。

2. His **rudimentary** grasp of the foreign language was insufficient for conducting the complex diplomatic negotiations required by his post.
   他对外语的基础掌握不足以进行其职位所要求的复杂外交谈判。

3. The prototype was still in a **rudimentary** stage of development and required extensive refinement before commercial production could begin.
   该原型仍处于初步开发阶段，在商业化生产之前还需要大量的改进完善。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rudimentary" as "rude + elementary" — something so basic it seems almost rude in its simplicity, like a rough first draft.
把"rudimentary"想成"rude（粗糙的）+ elementary（初级的）"——基础到几乎显得粗糙的东西，就像一份粗略的初稿。
