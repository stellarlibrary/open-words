# 😩 tantalize

🔊 /ˈtæntəlaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To tease or torment by presenting something desirable but keeping it out of reach; to excite expectations that are repeatedly disappointed
**中文：** v. 挑逗，惹弄

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- tease（逗弄）
- torment（折磨）
- tempt（诱惑）
- entice（引诱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tantalize someone with something / tantalize the audience / tantalizing aroma / tantalizing prospect**

👉 Example: The advertisement tantalized consumers with images of luxurious destinations they could barely afford.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The publisher **tantalized** readers with cryptic excerpts from the forthcoming novel months before its official release date.
   出版商在小说正式发行前几个月就用神秘的摘录来吊读者的胃口。

2. The aroma of freshly baked bread **tantalized** the hungry students as they walked past the bakery on their way to class.
   新鲜出炉的面包香气惹弄着饥饿的学生们，他们正路过面包店去上课。

3. Researchers were **tantalized** by preliminary findings that suggested a breakthrough, only to encounter insurmountable obstacles later.
   研究人员被暗示突破性进展的初步发现所吸引，结果后来遇到了不可逾越的障碍。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "Tantalus" from Greek mythology — he was condemned to stand in water that receded when he tried to drink, forever tantalized.
想想希腊神话中的"坦塔洛斯(Tantalus)"——他被罚站在水中，每当他想喝水时水就退去，永远被吊着胃口。
