# 🌱 naive

🔊 /naɪˈiːv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment; innocent and unsophisticated
**中文：** adj. 天真的；幼稚的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- innocent（天真的）
- gullible（易受骗的）
- unsophisticated（单纯的）
- ingenuous（坦率天真的）
- credulous（轻信的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **naive assumption / naive belief / politically naive / naive about something**

👉 Example: It would be naive to assume that international negotiations will proceed without significant obstacles.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee dismissed the proposal as **naive**, arguing that it failed to account for the complexities of global trade policy.
   委员会认为该提案过于**天真**，认为它未能考虑到全球贸易政策的复杂性。

2. Critics described the administration's foreign policy approach as dangerously **naive** in its assumptions about diplomatic goodwill.
   批评者认为政府的外交政策方法在对外交善意的假设上是危险地**幼稚**的。

3. The **naive** optimism of early investors was quickly tempered by the harsh realities of a volatile and unpredictable market.
   早期投资者**天真的**乐观很快被动荡且不可预测的市场的严酷现实所打击。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "naive" as "na-I've" — "na, I've never experienced that!" — someone who lacks experience and is innocent about the world.
把"naive"想成"na-I've"——"不，我从没经历过！"——一个缺乏经验、对世界一无所知的天真的人。
