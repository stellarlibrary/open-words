# 🔀 rearrange

🔊 /ˌriːəˈreɪndʒ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To change the position, order, or arrangement of something; to organize differently
**中文：** v. 重新排列；重新整理；重新安排

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reorganize（重新组织）
- reorder（重新排序）
- reshuffle（重新洗牌）
- restructure（重新构建）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rearrange the furniture / rearrange a schedule / rearrange the order / rearrange priorities**

👉 Example: She decided to rearrange the furniture in her office to create a more productive working environment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee voted to **rearrange** the agenda so that the most pressing budgetary matters would be addressed before any other items.
   委员会投票决定重新安排议程，以便在处理其他事项之前优先解决最紧迫的预算问题。

2. Scientists had to **rearrange** the entire experimental protocol after discovering a fundamental flaw in their original methodology and assumptions.
   科学家们在发现原始方法论和假设中的根本缺陷后，不得不重新调整整个实验方案。

3. The editor chose to **rearrange** the chapters of the manuscript to improve narrative coherence and strengthen the overall argument presented.
   编辑选择重新排列手稿的章节，以改善叙事的连贯性并加强所呈现的整体论点。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-arrange" as "arrange again" — the prefix "re-" means to do it over, so rearrange means to put things in a new order or position.
把"rearrange"拆成"re（再次）+ arrange（安排）"——前缀"re-"表示重新做一次，所以rearrange就是重新安排、换个顺序摆放。
