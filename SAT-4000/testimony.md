# ⚖️ testimony

🔊 /ˈtestɪmoʊni/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A formal written or spoken statement, especially one given in a court of law; evidence or proof provided in support of a fact or claim
**中文：** 证词，证言；证据；证明

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- evidence（证据）
- statement（陈述）
- deposition（宣誓证词）
- attestation（证明）
- declaration（声明）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **give testimony / sworn testimony / expert testimony / eyewitness testimony / testimony before Congress**

👉 Example: The witness provided compelling testimony that significantly influenced the jury's final verdict in the case.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The detective's **testimony** proved crucial in establishing the timeline of events that led to the suspect's eventual conviction.
   侦探的证词在确定导致嫌疑人最终定罪的事件时间线方面起到了关键作用。

2. Several expert witnesses were called to provide scientific **testimony** regarding the environmental impact of the proposed industrial facility.
   几位专家证人被传唤就拟建工业设施的环境影响提供科学证词。

3. Her moving **testimony** before the congressional committee inspired widespread legislative reform on matters of public health policy.
   她在国会委员会面前感人的证词激发了公共卫生政策方面的广泛立法改革。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "testi-mony" as coming from "testis" (Latin for "witness") — testimony is what a witness provides in court.
把"testimony"的词根"testis"想成拉丁语中的"证人"——testimony就是证人在法庭上提供的内容。
