# 🔀 conditional

🔊 /kənˈdɪʃənəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词，名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** (adj.) Subject to or dependent on certain conditions being met; not absolute. (n.) A grammatical construction expressing a condition, such as an "if" clause.

**中文：** (adj.) 有条件的；假定的。(n.) 条件句。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- contingent（取决于…的）
- dependent（依赖的）
- provisional（暂定的）
- qualified（有限制条件的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Collocation:** conditional on / conditional approval / conditional statement

**Example:** The university granted **conditional** admission pending the submission of final examination results.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board issued **conditional** approval for the project, requiring the applicant to secure additional funding first.
   董事会对该项目发出了有条件的批准，要求申请人首先获得额外资金。

2. In formal logic, a **conditional** statement asserts that one proposition necessarily follows from another given premise.
   在形式逻辑中，条件语句断言一个命题必然由另一个给定前提推导而出。

3. Her acceptance into the graduate program was **conditional** upon maintaining a minimum grade point average this semester.
   她被研究生项目录取的条件是本学期保持最低平均绩点。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

**English:** "Condition" + "-al" — relating to a condition. If-then logic: something happens only IF a condition is met.

**中文：** "condition"（条件）+ "-al"（与…相关的）——与条件相关的。就像"如果…那么…"的逻辑：只有满足条件才会发生，即"有条件的"。
