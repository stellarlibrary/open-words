# 💢 collide

🔊 /kəˈlaɪd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To come into violent contact; to crash together; to conflict or be in opposition
**中文：** v. 碰撞；相撞；抵触，冲突

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- crash（碰撞）
- clash（冲突）
- smash（猛撞）
- conflict（抵触）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **collide with / collide head-on / interests collide / worlds collide**

👉 Example: The two vehicles collided at the intersection due to a malfunctioning traffic signal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The asteroid is projected to **collide** with the planet's atmosphere, though scientists expect it to disintegrate before reaching the surface.
   预计这颗小行星将与行星大气层碰撞，但科学家们预期它会在到达地表之前解体。

2. When differing political ideologies **collide** in public discourse, the resulting debates can either strengthen or undermine democratic institutions.
   当不同的政治意识形态在公共话语中发生冲突时，由此产生的辩论可能加强或削弱民主制度。

3. Two freight trains **collided** on the main rail line, causing extensive damage and disrupting commercial transport for several days.
   两列货运列车在主铁路线上相撞，造成严重损坏并中断了数天的商业运输。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "col-lide" — things coming together ("col-") and sliding ("lide") into each other with great force.
把"collide"想成"col-lide"——物体聚到一起("col-")并猛力滑向("lide")彼此，发生碰撞。
