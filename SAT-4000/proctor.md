# 👁️ proctor

🔊 /ˈprɒktər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who supervises students during an examination; an agent or representative. (v.) To supervise or monitor
**中文：** n. 代理人；学监 v. 监督

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- supervisor（监督者）
- invigilator（监考人）
- monitor（监控者）
- overseer（监管人）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **proctor an exam / exam proctor / proctor a test / serve as proctor**

👉 Example: The university assigned a proctor to supervise the final examination in the auditorium.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor agreed to **proctor** the standardized examination for over two hundred students in the main hall.
   教授同意在大厅为两百多名学生监督这场标准化考试。

2. Each **proctor** was responsible for ensuring that no unauthorized materials were brought into the testing room.
   每位监考人负责确保没有未经授权的材料被带入考场。

3. The department hired additional staff to **proctor** the midterm exams across multiple classrooms on the same day.
   该系聘请了额外的工作人员在同一天的多个教室监督期中考试。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "proctor" as "protector of the test" — the proctor protects the integrity of an exam by watching over students.
把"proctor"想成"protector（保护者）"的缩写——监考人保护考试的公正性。
