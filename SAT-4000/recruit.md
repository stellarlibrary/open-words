# 🎖️ recruit

🔊 /rɪˈkruːt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A newly enlisted member of the armed forces or a new member of an organization. (v.) To enlist or enroll new members; to seek out and hire qualified individuals
**中文：** v. 招募，招收 n. 新兵，新成员

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- enlist（征募）
- enroll（招收）
- conscript（征召）
- novice（新手）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **recruit new members / recruit employees / army recruit / recruit talent / recruit for a position**

👉 Example: The company launched an ambitious campaign to recruit top engineering talent from leading universities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The university actively seeks to **recruit** outstanding scholars from around the world to strengthen its faculty and research programs.
   该大学积极从世界各地招募杰出学者，以加强其师资队伍和研究项目。

2. Every new **recruit** must complete an intensive orientation program before being assigned to a specific operational unit.
   每位新成员在被分配到具体操作单位之前，必须完成一项密集的入职培训计划。

3. The nonprofit organization managed to **recruit** over two hundred dedicated volunteers for the annual community outreach initiative.
   该非营利组织成功为年度社区外展活动招募了两百多名敬业的志愿者。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "recruit" as "re-crew-it" — you are crewing up again, bringing in fresh new members to fill out the team.
把"recruit"想成"re-crew-it"——重新组建团队，招募新鲜成员来充实队伍。
