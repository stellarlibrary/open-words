# 🔬 qualitative

🔊 /ˈkwɒlɪtətɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or concerned with quality or character rather than quantity or number; based on descriptions rather than measurements
**中文：** adj. 性质上的，定性的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- descriptive（描述性的）
- subjective（主观的）
- non-numerical（非数量的）
- interpretive（解释性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **qualitative research / qualitative analysis / qualitative data / qualitative difference**

👉 Example: The study employed qualitative methods such as interviews and focus groups to explore participant experiences.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sociologist relied on **qualitative** interviews rather than statistical surveys to understand the lived experiences of marginalized communities.
   社会学家依靠定性访谈而非统计调查来了解边缘化群体的生活经历。

2. There is a **qualitative** difference between memorizing facts for an examination and truly comprehending the underlying theoretical principles.
   为考试死记硬背事实与真正理解基本理论原则之间存在质的差异。

3. The peer reviewers recommended that the authors supplement their **qualitative** findings with quantitative data to strengthen the overall analysis.
   同行评审人员建议作者用定量数据补充其定性研究结果，以加强整体分析。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "qualit-ative" — it contains "quality," so qualitative analysis focuses on the quality and nature of things, not the quantity.
把"qualitative"想成包含"quality（质量）"——定性分析关注的是事物的性质和特征，而非数量。
