# ❌ nullify

🔊 /ˈnʌlɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make legally void or invalid; to cancel out; to render ineffective or useless
**中文：** v. 使无效，作废；取消

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- invalidate（使无效）
- annul（废除）
- negate（否定）
- void（使作废）
- revoke（撤销）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **nullify a contract / nullify the effects / nullify a law / nullify an agreement**

👉 Example: The Supreme Court voted to nullify the executive order on constitutional grounds.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The appellate court decided to **nullify** the lower court's ruling due to significant procedural errors during the trial.
   上诉法院因审判过程中存在重大程序错误，决定撤销下级法院的裁决。

2. Introducing a new variable into the experiment could potentially **nullify** months of carefully collected and analyzed data.
   在实验中引入新变量可能会使数月精心收集和分析的数据变得无效。

3. The ceasefire agreement was effectively **nullified** when both parties resumed military operations along the disputed border region.
   当双方在争议边境地区恢复军事行动时，停火协议实际上被废除了。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "null-ify" — to make something "null" means to reduce it to nothing, to zero out its value or effect.
把"nullify"想成"null-ify"——使某物变为"null（零）"，意味着将其价值或效力归零。
