# 🎯 incentive

🔊 /ɪnˈsɛntɪv/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Something that motivates or encourages someone to do something; a reward or stimulus. (adj.) Serving to encourage or motivate
**中文：** n. 动机；刺激；鼓励 adj. 激励的；刺激的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- motivation（动力）
- stimulus（刺激）
- inducement（诱因）
- encouragement（鼓励）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **incentive to do something / financial incentive / tax incentive / provide an incentive / strong incentive**

👉 Example: The company introduced a generous bonus structure as an incentive to boost employee productivity.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government offered substantial tax **incentives** to corporations willing to invest in renewable energy infrastructure and research.
   政府向愿意投资可再生能源基础设施和研究的企业提供了大量税收激励。

2. Without a meaningful **incentive** to participate, researchers found that survey response rates dropped below statistically acceptable thresholds.
   没有有意义的参与激励，研究人员发现调查回复率降至统计学可接受的阈值以下。

3. Scholars argue that economic **incentives** alone are insufficient to address the complex challenges of environmental conservation.
   学者们认为，仅靠经济激励不足以解决环境保护方面的复杂挑战。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-CENT-ive" — imagine someone placing "cents" (coins) in front of you to motivate you; money is the classic incentive.
把"incentive"想成"in-CENT（分币）-ive"——想象有人在你面前放置硬币来激励你；金钱是最经典的激励方式。
