# 🏴 preemption

🔊 /priˈɛmpʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act of preempting; the right to purchase something before others; the act of seizing or taking precedence
**中文：** n. 优先购买；强制收购；抢先占有；取代

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- appropriation（占有）
- precedence（优先）
- forestalling（预先阻止）
- seizure（夺取）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **right of preemption / preemption of land / federal preemption / preemption doctrine**

👉 Example: The doctrine of federal preemption ensures that national laws override conflicting state legislation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The settlers exercised their right of **preemption** to purchase the land before it was offered to the general public.
   定居者行使了他们的优先购买权，在土地向公众出售之前将其购入。

2. Federal **preemption** of state environmental regulations has become a contentious issue in recent constitutional debates.
   联邦对州环境法规的优先取代权已成为近期宪法辩论中一个有争议的问题。

3. The board approved the **preemption** of the broadcast schedule to air an emergency announcement regarding public safety.
   董事会批准抢占广播时间表，以播出一则有关公共安全的紧急公告。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "preemption" as "pre-emption" — taking (emption, from Latin "emptio" meaning purchase) before others, like having first dibs.
把"preemption"想成"pre（之前）+ emption（购买）"——在别人之前购买，就像拥有优先选择权。
