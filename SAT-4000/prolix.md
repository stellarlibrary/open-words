# 📜 prolix

🔊 /proʊˈlɪks/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Using or containing too many words; tediously lengthy in speech or writing
**中文：** adj. 冗长的；说话啰嗦的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- verbose（冗长的）
- wordy（啰嗦的）
- long-winded（冗长乏味的）
- garrulous（唠叨的）
- loquacious（话多的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prolix speech / prolix writing / prolix style / prolix explanation**

👉 Example: The editor advised the author to trim the prolix passages that weakened the overall narrative.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's **prolix** lectures often extended well beyond the scheduled time, exhausting even the most attentive students.
   教授冗长的讲座经常远远超出预定时间，即使是最专注的学生也感到疲惫。

2. Critics dismissed the politician's **prolix** speech as lacking substance, noting it contained many words but few ideas.
   评论家们认为该政客冗长的演讲缺乏实质内容，指出其言辞繁多却鲜有观点。

3. Academic journals increasingly reject **prolix** manuscripts, favoring concise and well-structured arguments supported by clear evidence.
   学术期刊越来越多地拒绝冗长的稿件，更青睐简洁且结构良好、有明确证据支持的论证。

把"prolix"想成"pro-LICKS"——一个说话时不停"舔嘴唇"的人，滔滔不绝，说个没完。
