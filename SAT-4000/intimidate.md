# 😨 intimidate

🔊 /ɪnˈtɪmɪˌdeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To frighten or threaten someone, especially to make them do what one wants; to overawe through force of personality or superior display
**中文：** vt. 恐吓；威胁；胁迫

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- threaten（威胁）
- bully（恃强凌弱）
- coerce（强迫）
- menace（恐吓）
- daunt（使畏惧）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **intimidate someone into doing something / intimidate a witness / feel intimidated by / intimidating presence**

👉 Example: The defense attorney accused the prosecution of attempting to intimidate key witnesses before the trial.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corporation attempted to **intimidate** smaller competitors by filing numerous frivolous lawsuits designed to drain their limited financial resources.
   该公司试图通过提起大量无理诉讼来恐吓较小的竞争对手，以耗尽其有限的财务资源。

2. Many first-generation college students feel **intimidated** by the complex admissions process and the unfamiliar academic environment they must navigate.
   许多第一代大学生对复杂的录取流程和他们必须适应的陌生学术环境感到畏惧。

3. The authoritarian regime routinely sought to **intimidate** journalists who published articles critical of government policies and corruption.
   这个专制政权经常试图恐吓那些发表批评政府政策和腐败文章的记者。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-TIMID-ate" — it contains the word "timid" (fearful). To intimidate is to make someone timid or fearful through threats.
把"intimidate"想成含有"timid"（胆怯的）——恐吓就是通过威胁使某人变得胆怯或害怕。
