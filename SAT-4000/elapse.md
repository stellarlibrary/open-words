# ⏳ elapse

🔊 /ɪˈlæps/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (Of time) to pass or go by; to slip away gradually
**中文：** vi. 消逝；流逝；（时间）过去

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pass（过去）
- expire（届满）
- transpire（流逝）
- slip by（悄然流逝）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **time elapses / hours elapse / years elapsed / before much time had elapsed**

👉 Example: Several months elapsed before the committee finally reached a consensus on the proposed reform.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Nearly three decades had **elapsed** since the treaty was signed, yet its provisions remained largely unimplemented by member states.
   条约签署后近三十年已经过去，但其条款在很大程度上仍未被成员国执行。

2. The researchers allowed forty-eight hours to **elapse** between experimental trials to minimize the influence of residual effects.
   研究人员让实验试验之间间隔四十八小时，以尽量减少残余效应的影响。

3. Considerable time had **elapsed** before investigators discovered the critical flaw in the original structural engineering analysis.
   在调查人员发现原始结构工程分析中的关键缺陷之前，已经过去了相当长的时间。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "e-LAPSE" — time has a "lapse" as it slips away; elapse means time passing by, moment by moment.
把"elapse"想成"e-LAPSE"——时间在"lapse"（流逝）中悄然溜走；elapse意为时间一点一点地过去。
