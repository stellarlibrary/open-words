# 🏰 impregnable

🔊 /ɪmˈpreɡnəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Unable to be captured, overcome, or broken into; strong enough to resist or withstand any attack; unassailable
**中文：** adj. 无法攻取的；不受影响的；坚不可摧的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- invincible（不可战胜的）
- unassailable（无法攻破的）
- indestructible（坚不可摧的）
- invulnerable（无懈可击的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **impregnable fortress / impregnable defense / impregnable position / seemingly impregnable**

👉 Example: The medieval castle's thick stone walls made it virtually impregnable against enemy sieges.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient mountain fortress was considered **impregnable** due to its elevated position and the sheer cliffs surrounding it on all sides.
   这座古老的山地堡垒因其居高临下的位置和四周的陡峭悬崖而被认为坚不可摧。

2. The corporation built an **impregnable** legal defense by hiring the nation's most experienced attorneys to protect its intellectual property.
   该公司通过聘请全国最有经验的律师来保护其知识产权，构建了无懈可击的法律防线。

3. What once seemed like an **impregnable** lead in the polls gradually eroded as new scandals diminished the candidate's public credibility.
   曾经看似不可动摇的民调领先优势随着新丑闻削弱了候选人的公信力而逐渐消失。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "IM-PREGNABLE" — "im-" means "not" and "pregnable" relates to being taken or seized, so impregnable means "cannot be taken or seized."
把"impregnable"拆分为"im（不能）+ pregnable（被攻取的）"——意思是"不能被攻取的"，坚不可摧。
