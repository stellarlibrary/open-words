# 🗡️ malign

🔊 /məˈlaɪn/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To speak about someone in a spitefully critical manner; to slander. (adj.) Evil in nature or effect; malevolent
**中文：** v. 诽谤；污蔑 adj. 恶意的；恶性的；恶毒的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- slander（诽谤）
- defame（诋毁）
- vilify（诋毁）
- disparage（贬低）
- denigrate（抹黑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **malign someone's character / malign influence / malign intent / unfairly maligned**

👉 Example: The whistleblower was maligned by executives who sought to discredit her testimony.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Political opponents sought to **malign** the senator's reputation by circulating fabricated documents to investigative journalists.
   政治对手试图通过向调查记者散发伪造文件来诽谤这位参议员的名誉。

2. Historians have often **maligned** the emperor, but recent archaeological discoveries suggest a more nuanced and complex legacy.
   历史学家经常诋毁这位皇帝，但最近的考古发现表明其遗产更为复杂和微妙。

3. The report identified several **malign** foreign actors who had attempted to interfere with the integrity of democratic elections.
   报告确定了几个恶意的外国行为者，他们试图干扰民主选举的公正性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "malign" as "mal" (bad) + "sign" — someone who puts a bad sign on you, speaking ill of your character or casting an evil influence.
把"malign"想成"mal"（坏的）+"sign"（标记）——某人给你贴上坏标签，说你坏话或施加恶意影响。
