# 📋 brief

🔊 /briːf/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun, verb | 形容词, 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Short in duration or length; concise. (n.) A summary or set of instructions; a legal document. (v.) To give essential information or instructions to someone
**中文：** adj. 简短的，简洁的；短暂的，草率的 n. 摘要，简报，大纲 v. 简短介绍

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- concise（简洁的）
- succinct（简明的）
- short（短暂的）
- summary（摘要）
- fleeting（转瞬即逝的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a brief overview / brief someone on / in brief / a legal brief / brief remarks**

👉 Example: The director gave a brief overview of the project before the detailed discussion began.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor delivered a **brief** lecture summarizing the key principles of thermodynamics before the laboratory session commenced.
   教授在实验课开始前做了一个简短的讲座，总结了热力学的关键原理。

2. The intelligence officer was asked to **brief** the committee on recent developments concerning national security threats abroad.
   情报官员被要求向委员会简要介绍有关海外国家安全威胁的最新动态。

3. The attorney submitted a legal **brief** outlining the constitutional arguments in support of the defendant's right to appeal.
   律师提交了一份法律摘要，概述了支持被告上诉权利的宪法论点。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "brief" as a "brief-case" — a briefcase carries only the essential documents, keeping things short and concise.
把"brief"想成"brief-case"（公文包）——公文包只装重要文件，保持简短精练。
