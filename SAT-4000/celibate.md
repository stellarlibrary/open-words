# 🕊️ celibate

🔊 /ˈselɪbət/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who abstains from marriage and sexual relations. (adj.) Abstaining from marriage and sexual relations, especially for religious reasons
**中文：** n. 独身者，独身主义者 adj. 独身的，禁欲的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unmarried（未婚的）
- chaste（贞洁的）
- abstinent（禁欲的）
- single（单身的）
- continent（自制的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **remain celibate / celibate lifestyle / celibate clergy / confirmed celibate**

👉 Example: The monks remain celibate throughout their lives as part of their spiritual commitment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The young priest chose to remain **celibate** in accordance with the long-standing doctrinal requirements of his religious denomination.
   这位年轻的牧师选择保持独身，以符合其宗教教派长期以来的教义要求。

2. Historical records suggest that certain philosophical schools required their adherents to live as **celibates** devoted entirely to intellectual pursuits.
   历史记录表明，某些哲学流派要求其追随者作为独身者，完全投身于学术追求。

3. The debate over whether clergy should be **celibate** has persisted for centuries within various branches of organized religion worldwide.
   关于神职人员是否应该独身的争论在世界各地有组织的宗教的各个分支中持续了数个世纪。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "celibate" as "cell-i-bate" — a person who "abates" (reduces) their desires and lives in a solitary "cell" like a monk.
把"celibate"想成"cell-i-bate"——一个"abate（减少）"自己欲望、像僧侣一样住在孤独的"cell（小房间）"里的人。
