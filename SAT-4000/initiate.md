# 🚀 initiate

🔊 /ɪˈnɪʃieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause a process or action to begin; to introduce someone to a new activity or skill. (n.) A person who has been newly introduced to an organization or field
**中文：** v. 开始，创始；发起；使初步了解 n. 新加入者；新入会者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- commence（开始）
- launch（发起）
- inaugurate（开创）
- instigate（发动）
- novice（新手）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **initiate a program / initiate proceedings / initiate a discussion / initiate reform**

👉 Example: The president moved to initiate sweeping reforms across the healthcare system.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The university decided to **initiate** a comprehensive review of its admissions policies to promote greater equity and access.
   该大学决定发起一项全面审查其招生政策的计划，以促进更大的公平和开放。

2. Several environmental organizations collaborated to **initiate** legal proceedings against the corporation responsible for the toxic spill.
   几个环保组织合作发起了针对造成有毒泄漏的公司的法律诉讼。

3. As a newly **initiated** member of the research team, she was eager to contribute her expertise to the ongoing study.
   作为研究团队的新成员，她渴望将自己的专业知识贡献给正在进行的研究。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "initiate" as containing "init" — just like "initialize" in computing, it means to start something from the beginning.
把"initiate"想成包含"init"——就像计算机中的"initialize（初始化）"一样，意思是从头开始某事。
