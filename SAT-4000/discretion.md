# 🎯 discretion

🔊 /dɪˈskreʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The quality of behaving or speaking in a careful, prudent way; the freedom to decide what should be done in a particular situation
**中文：** n. 判断；慎重，谨慎；自由裁量权

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- prudence（审慎）
- judgment（判断力）
- caution（谨慎）
- tact（圆滑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **at one's discretion / use discretion / exercise discretion / with discretion**

👉 Example: The allocation of emergency funds is left entirely at the director's discretion.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The judge exercised considerable **discretion** in determining the appropriate sentence for the first-time offender given the mitigating circumstances.
   鉴于减轻处罚的情节，法官在确定对初犯的适当刑罚时行使了相当大的自由裁量权。

2. Employees are expected to use **discretion** when handling sensitive client information to maintain the firm's reputation for confidentiality.
   员工在处理敏感的客户信息时应谨慎行事，以维护公司在保密方面的声誉。

3. The scholarship committee has full **discretion** over the selection process and is not required to disclose the criteria to applicants.
   奖学金委员会对遴选过程拥有完全的自由裁量权，无需向申请人披露评选标准。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "discretion" as the noun form of "discreet" — it is the quality of being careful and having the power to make wise decisions.
把"discretion"看作"discreet"的名词形式——它既指谨慎的品质，也指做出明智决定的权力。
