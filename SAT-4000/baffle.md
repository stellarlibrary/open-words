# 🤔 baffle

🔊 /ˈbæfl/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词，名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** (v.) To bewilder or perplex someone completely; to hinder or impede progress; (n.) a device used to restrain or regulate the flow of gas, liquid, light, or sound.

**中文：** （动词）使……困惑；阻碍；（名词）挡板。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- bewilder（使困惑）
- perplex（使费解）
- confound（使迷惑）
- mystify（使迷惑不解）
- puzzle（使困惑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Baffle" as a verb suggests a deep level of confusion — more intense than simply "confuse." The adjective form "baffling" is very common (e.g., "a baffling mystery"). The noun form "baffle" is mainly used in engineering and technical contexts.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The mysterious disappearance of the ancient manuscript continued to **baffle** scholars and historians for several decades.
   （那份古代手稿的神秘消失持续**困扰**了学者和历史学家数十年之久。）

2. Engineers installed a sound **baffle** inside the auditorium to reduce unwanted echoes and improve overall acoustic quality.
   （工程师在礼堂内安装了隔音**挡板**，以减少不必要的回声并提高整体音质。）

3. The defendant's contradictory testimony **baffled** the jury, making it nearly impossible to reach a unanimous verdict.
   （被告相互矛盾的证词**使**陪审团**困惑不已**，几乎不可能达成一致裁决。）

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Baffle" sounds like "battle" — imagine your brain having a battle trying to understand something extremely confusing. When you are baffled, your mind is at war with the puzzle.

"Baffle"听起来像"battle（战斗）"——想象你的大脑在试图理解极其令人困惑的事物时进行的一场战斗。当你被baffle时，你的大脑在与谜题作战。
