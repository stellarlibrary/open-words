# 🍷 inebriate

🔊 /ɪˈniːbrieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective, noun | 动词, 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make drunk; to exhilarate or stupefy. (adj.) Intoxicated. (n.) A habitual drunkard
**中文：** v. 灌醉；使陶醉 adj. 酒醉的 n. 酒鬼

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- intoxicate（使醉）
- stupefy（使昏迷）
- besot（使沉迷）
- exhilarate（使兴奋）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inebriated state / inebriated by success / inebriate with power / confirmed inebriate**

👉 Example: The heady atmosphere of victory seemed to inebriate the entire team with a dangerous overconfidence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The potent combination of flattery and unchecked authority can **inebriate** even the most disciplined leaders into making reckless decisions.
   奉承和不受约束的权力的强效组合可以使即使是最自律的领导人也陶醉其中，做出鲁莽的决定。

2. Witnesses testified that the defendant was visibly **inebriated** and therefore incapable of making rational judgments at the time of signing.
   证人作证说被告明显处于醉酒状态，因此在签字时无法做出理性判断。

3. The nineteenth-century reformers established temperance societies specifically to rehabilitate **inebriates** and discourage excessive alcohol consumption.
   十九世纪的改革者专门建立了禁酒协会，以帮助酒鬼康复并劝阻过度饮酒。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-EBRI-ate" — the Latin "ebrius" means "drunk," so to inebriate is to make someone drunk or intoxicated with excitement.
把"inebriate"想成"in(使)+ebri(醉)+ate(动词后缀)"——拉丁语"ebrius"意为"醉的"，所以inebriate就是灌醉或使人陶醉。
