# 🦅 arrogate

🔊 /ˈærəɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To claim or seize something without justification; to assume or appropriate presumptuously
**中文：** v. 冒称；霸占

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- usurp（篡夺）
- seize（夺取）
- appropriate（侵占）
- commandeer（强占）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **arrogate power / arrogate authority / arrogate to oneself / arrogate rights**

👉 Example: The general attempted to arrogate supreme authority during the political crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The executive tried to **arrogate** decision-making authority that had traditionally belonged to the board of directors.
   这位高管试图霸占传统上属于董事会的决策权。

2. No individual should be permitted to **arrogate** the rights of an entire community for personal political advantage.
   不应允许任何个人为了个人政治利益而冒称整个社区的权利。

3. The newly appointed governor moved swiftly to **arrogate** legislative powers that exceeded the scope of the office.
   新任命的州长迅速行动，霸占了超出其职权范围的立法权力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "arrogate" as "arrogant + gate" — an arrogant person who crashes through the gate to seize what is not theirs.
把"arrogate"想成"arrogant（傲慢的）+ gate（门）"——一个傲慢的人闯过大门，霸占不属于自己的东西。
