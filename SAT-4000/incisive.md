# 🔪 incisive

🔊 /ɪnˈsaɪsɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Intelligently analytical and clear-thinking; sharp and penetrating in perception or expression.
**中文：** adj. 深刻的；敏锐的；锋利的；尖锐的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- penetrating（敏锐的）
- acute（深刻的）
- sharp（尖锐的）
- trenchant（犀利的）
- perceptive（有洞察力的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **incisive analysis / incisive criticism / incisive mind / incisive remark**

👉 Example: Her incisive analysis of the economic crisis earned widespread respect among policy experts.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist was celebrated for her **incisive** reporting that exposed corruption at the highest levels of government.
   这位记者因其深刻的报道揭露了最高层政府的腐败行为而受到赞扬。

2. His **incisive** questioning during the debate forced the candidate to address substantive policy issues directly and honestly.
   他在辩论中尖锐的提问迫使候选人直接而诚实地回应实质性政策问题。

3. The committee valued her **incisive** intellect, which consistently identified flaws in proposals that others had overlooked entirely.
   委员会重视她敏锐的才智，她总能找出其他人完全忽略的提案中的缺陷。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-CIS-ive" — "cis" comes from Latin "caedere" meaning "to cut." An incisive comment cuts right to the heart of the matter.
把"incisive"想成"in-CIS-ive"——"cis"来自拉丁语"caedere"，意为"切割"。一个incisive的评论能直切问题的核心。
