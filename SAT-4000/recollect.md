# 💭 recollect

🔊 /ˌrɛkəˈlɛkt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To remember something; to bring back to the level of conscious awareness; to recall past experiences or information
**中文：** v. 回忆；想起；记起

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- recall（回忆）
- remember（记起）
- reminisce（追忆）
- retrieve（检索）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **recollect clearly / recollect the events / as far as I can recollect / recollect from memory**

👉 Example: She struggled to recollect the details of the conversation that took place years earlier.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The elderly witness could not **recollect** the precise sequence of events that occurred on the night of the incident.
   这位年迈的目击者无法回忆起事件发生当晚的确切事件顺序。

2. Historians attempted to **recollect** the oral traditions of the indigenous community before they faded from collective memory entirely.
   历史学家试图在土著社区的口头传统完全从集体记忆中消失之前将其追忆记录下来。

3. As far as the professor could **recollect**, no student had ever submitted such an exceptionally original and compelling thesis.
   据教授回忆，从未有学生提交过如此格外原创且有说服力的论文。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-collect" as "collecting again" — you collect scattered memories again, gathering them back into your mind.
把"recollect"想成"re-collect（重新收集）"——你重新收集散落的记忆，将它们重新汇聚到脑海中。
