# 🔽 downplay

🔊 /ˈdaʊnpleɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make something appear less important or significant than it actually is; to minimize
**中文：** v. 不予重视；轻描淡写；淡化

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- minimize（最小化）
- understate（低估陈述）
- trivialize（轻视）
- diminish（减少）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **downplay the significance / downplay the risk / downplay the impact / downplay concerns**

👉 Example: Officials attempted to downplay the severity of the crisis to prevent public panic.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corporation attempted to **downplay** the environmental impact of its manufacturing operations during the regulatory hearing.
   该公司在监管听证会上试图淡化其制造业务对环境的影响。

2. Critics accused the administration of trying to **downplay** the severity of the economic recession affecting working families.
   批评人士指责政府试图轻描淡写影响工薪家庭的经济衰退的严重性。

3. The researcher refused to **downplay** her findings even though they contradicted the prevailing scientific consensus.
   这位研究人员拒绝淡化她的发现，尽管这些发现与主流科学共识相矛盾。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "downplay" as "play it down" — you push something down to make it seem smaller or less important.
把"downplay"想成"play it down（把它往下压）"——你把某事往下压以使其看起来更小或更不重要。
