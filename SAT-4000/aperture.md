# 📷 aperture

🔊 /ˈæp.ɚ.tʃɚ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** An opening, hole, or gap; specifically in optics, the adjustable opening in a camera lens through which light passes to reach the film or sensor.

**中文：** 孔，穴；光圈，孔径

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- opening（开口）
- hole（孔）
- gap（缝隙）
- orifice（孔口）
- slit（狭缝）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

Common collocations: **aperture setting**, **lens aperture**, **wide/narrow aperture**, **aperture size**, **through the aperture**.

> The photographer adjusted the **aperture** to allow more light into the camera.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scientist observed the specimen through a narrow **aperture** in the protective shielding apparatus.
   科学家通过防护装置上的一个狭窄孔径观察标本。

2. A wider **aperture** permits greater light transmission but reduces the overall depth of field.
   更大的光圈允许更多光线透过，但会降低整体景深。

3. The ancient fortress featured small **apertures** in its walls designed for defensive observation purposes.
   这座古老的堡垒墙壁上设有用于防御观察的小孔。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Aperture" comes from Latin *apertura* (an opening), from *aperire* (to open). Think of it as "a-PURE-TOUR" — on a pure tour of a cave, you look through an **aperture** (opening) to see inside.

词源记忆：aperture 来自拉丁语 apertura（开口），源于 aperire（打开）。可以联想为"打开（aper-）的结构（-ture）"，即一个开口或孔。
