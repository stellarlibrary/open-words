# 🚫 averse

🔊 /əˈvɜːrs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** Having a strong feeling of opposition, reluctance, or distaste toward something; unwilling or disinclined.

**中文：** 反对的；不愿意的；嫌恶的。表示对某事有强烈的反感、不情愿或厌恶。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- reluctant （不情愿的）
- opposed （反对的）
- disinclined （不愿意的）
- loath （勉强的，不乐意的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Averse" is typically followed by the preposition "to." Do not confuse it with "adverse," which means harmful or unfavorable. "Averse" describes a personal feeling, while "adverse" describes external conditions.

"averse"通常后接介词"to"。不要将它与"adverse"混淆，后者表示有害的或不利的。"averse"描述的是个人感受，而"adverse"描述的是外部条件。

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. She was not **averse** to taking calculated risks when the potential rewards justified the effort.
   *当潜在回报值得付出时，她并不**反对**承担经过深思熟虑的风险。*

2. The committee members appeared **averse** to any proposal that would significantly increase the annual budget.
   *委员会成员似乎**不愿意**接受任何会大幅增加年度预算的提案。*

3. Although he was generally **averse** to public speaking, he delivered a compelling address at the conference.
   *尽管他通常**不喜欢**公开演讲，但他在会议上发表了一篇引人注目的讲话。*

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "a-VERSE" — you turn away ("a-" away) from a bad "verse" of poetry because you dislike it. Being averse means turning away from something you oppose.

联想"a-VERSE"——你因为不喜欢一首糟糕的诗"verse"而转身离开（"a-"表示远离）。averse就是对你反对的事物转身离开。
