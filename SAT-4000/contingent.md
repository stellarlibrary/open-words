# 🔀 contingent

🔊 /kənˈtɪndʒənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Dependent on certain conditions; possible but not certain. (n.) A group of people sharing a common feature, sent as part of a larger body
**中文：** adj. 因情况而异的；不一定的；可能发生的 n. 分遣队；代表团

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conditional（有条件的）
- dependent（依赖的）
- provisional（临时的）
- delegation（代表团）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **contingent on / contingent upon / a contingent of soldiers / the largest contingent**

👉 Example: The final approval of the merger was contingent upon regulatory clearance from both governments.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The university's funding for the research project was **contingent** on the successful completion of the preliminary feasibility study.
   大学对该研究项目的资助取决于初步可行性研究的成功完成。

2. A large **contingent** of international observers arrived to monitor the fairness and transparency of the national elections.
   一大批国际观察员抵达以监督全国选举的公平性和透明度。

3. Her acceptance of the position was **contingent** upon receiving a salary package that matched her extensive professional experience.
   她接受该职位的条件是获得与其丰富专业经验相匹配的薪酬方案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "contingent" as "it can go either way, touching on chance" — the outcome depends on conditions that have not yet been met.
把"contingent"想成"取决于偶然条件"——结果取决于尚未满足的条件。
