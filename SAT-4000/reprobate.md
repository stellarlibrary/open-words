# 😈 reprobate

🔊 /ˈrɛprəbeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective, noun | 动词, 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To express strong disapproval of; to condemn. (adj.) Morally unprincipled; wicked. (n.) An unprincipled or immoral person; a scoundrel
**中文：** v. 非难；拒绝 adj. 邪恶的 n. 恶棍

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- scoundrel（恶棍）
- degenerate（堕落的人）
- villain（恶人）
- condemn（谴责）
- miscreant（恶徒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **moral reprobate / old reprobate / reprobate behavior / reprobate conduct / utterly reprobate**

👉 Example: The court dismissed the testimony of the known reprobate, citing his long history of dishonesty.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The community regarded the corrupt official as a complete **reprobate** who had betrayed every principle of public trust.
   社区将这位腐败官员视为一个彻底的恶棍，他背叛了公众信任的每一条原则。

2. Religious leaders in the seventeenth century would openly **reprobate** any conduct deemed contrary to established moral doctrine.
   十七世纪的宗教领袖会公开谴责任何被认为违反既定道德教义的行为。

3. Despite his **reprobate** reputation, the aging rogue displayed an unexpected act of generosity that surprised the entire village.
   尽管名声败坏，这位年迈的无赖却表现出一种出人意料的慷慨之举，令整个村庄惊讶。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-prob-ate" as "re" (again) + "prob" (prove, test) + "ate" — someone who has been tested and proven bad again and again, a confirmed villain.
把"reprobate"想成"re"（再次）+"prob"（证明、检验）——一个被反复检验并证明是坏人的家伙，一个确认的恶棍。
