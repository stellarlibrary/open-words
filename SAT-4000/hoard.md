# 💰 hoard

🔊 /hɔːrd/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To accumulate and store away a large quantity of something, often secretly. (n.) A secret stock or store of something valuable
**中文：** v. 贮藏；囤积；积蓄 n. 贮藏物；储藏物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- stockpile（囤积）
- amass（积累）
- cache（隐藏物）
- stash（藏匿物）
- accumulate（积聚）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **hoard supplies / hoard wealth / hoard resources / a hoard of treasure**

👉 Example: During the crisis, some consumers began to hoard essential supplies like water and canned goods.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Archaeologists unearthed a remarkable **hoard** of Roman gold coins buried beneath the foundations of an ancient villa.
   考古学家在一座古代别墅的地基下发掘出了一批不同寻常的罗马金币贮藏物。

2. Governments imposed strict regulations to prevent merchants from **hoarding** essential medical supplies during the pandemic.
   政府实施了严格的法规，以防止商人在疫情期间囤积基本医疗物资。

3. The squirrel's instinct to **hoard** acorns each autumn ensures its survival through the long and harsh winter months.
   松鼠每年秋天贮藏橡果的本能确保了它能在漫长严酷的冬季存活下来。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "hoard" as hiding things on a "board" — you pile everything onto a board and hide it away for safekeeping.
把"hoard"想成把东西藏在"board"（板子）上——你把所有东西堆在板子上藏起来，以备不时之需。
