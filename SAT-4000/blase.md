# 🥱 blase

🔊 /blɑːˈzeɪ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Unimpressed or indifferent to something because of overfamiliarity; bored with pleasure or luxury
**中文：** adj. 厌倦的；玩腻了的；对享乐不感兴趣的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- indifferent（冷漠的）
- nonchalant（漫不经心的）
- jaded（厌倦的）
- apathetic（无动于衷的）
- world-weary（厌世的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **blase attitude / blase about something / seem blase / appear blase / become blase**

👉 Example: After years of international travel, she had grown blase about visiting new countries.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The wealthy socialite appeared utterly **blase** about the extravagant gala, having attended hundreds of similar events before.
   这位富有的社交名媛对这场奢华晚宴显得完全无动于衷，因为她之前已参加过数百场类似的活动。

2. Teenagers often adopt a **blase** demeanor to conceal their genuine excitement or vulnerability from their peers.
   青少年经常装出一副漫不经心的样子，以掩饰他们在同龄人面前的真实兴奋或脆弱。

3. The art critic's **blase** response to the groundbreaking exhibition surprised colleagues who expected enthusiastic praise.
   这位艺术评论家对这场开创性展览的冷淡反应让期待热情赞誉的同行们感到惊讶。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "blase" as "blaze" that has burned out — the fire of excitement is gone, leaving only indifference and boredom.
把"blase"想成"blaze"（火焰）已经熄灭——兴奋的火焰已经消失，只剩下冷漠和厌倦。
