# 📊 deficit

🔊 /ˈdɛfɪsɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The amount by which something falls short of what is required; an excess of expenditure over income or revenue
**中文：** n. 赤字，逆差；不足额

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- shortfall（不足）
- shortage（短缺）
- deficiency（缺乏）
- arrears（欠款）
- shortcoming（不足之处）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **budget deficit / trade deficit / attention deficit / deficit spending / run a deficit**

👉 Example: The national budget deficit has grown to an alarming level over the past fiscal year.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The country's trade **deficit** widened significantly as imports continued to outpace exports throughout the fiscal quarter.
   随着进口在整个财季持续超过出口，该国的贸易逆差显著扩大。

2. The school district faces a severe funding **deficit** that threatens to eliminate several essential academic programs.
   该学区面临严重的资金不足，可能导致几个重要学术项目被取消。

3. Economists warn that persistent government **deficit** spending could lead to rising inflation and long-term financial instability.
   经济学家警告说，政府持续的赤字支出可能导致通货膨胀上升和长期金融不稳定。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "deficit" as "de-" (away from) + "ficit" (doing/making, from Latin facere) — what is made falls away from what is needed, leaving a gap.
把"deficit"想成"de-"（偏离）+ "ficit"（做/制造）——做出来的不够所需的，留下了一个缺口，即赤字。
