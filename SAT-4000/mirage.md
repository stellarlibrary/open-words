# 🏜️ mirage

🔊 /mɪˈrɑːʒ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An optical illusion caused by atmospheric conditions; something that appears real but is not; a vain hope or dream
**中文：** n. 海市蜃楼；幻想，妄想

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- illusion（幻觉）
- hallucination（幻象）
- fantasy（幻想）
- delusion（妄想）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a mirage of / chase a mirage / prove to be a mirage / political mirage**

👉 Example: The promise of quick wealth turned out to be nothing more than a mirage.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The shimmering lake on the horizon was merely a **mirage** caused by the intense desert heat and atmospheric refraction.
   地平线上波光粼粼的湖泊不过是沙漠酷热和大气折射造成的海市蜃楼。

2. Economic prosperity without structural reform remains a **mirage** that policymakers continue to pursue in vain.
   没有结构性改革的经济繁荣仍然是政策制定者徒劳追求的幻想。

3. The candidate's campaign promises proved to be a **mirage**, dissolving quickly once the realities of governance set in.
   该候选人的竞选承诺被证明是一场幻想，一旦面对执政现实便迅速化为乌有。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mirage" as "my rage" — you feel rage when you discover that what you saw was just an illusion, not real.
把"mirage"想成"my rage"——当你发现看到的只是幻觉而非真实时，你会感到愤怒。
