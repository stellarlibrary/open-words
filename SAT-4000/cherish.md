# 💖 cherish

🔊 /ˈtʃɛrɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To hold dear; to feel or show great love and affection for; to keep a hope or memory alive in one's mind
**中文：** v. 珍爱；怀抱；珍视

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- treasure（珍视）
- adore（崇爱）
- value（重视）
- nurture（培育）
- revere（敬重）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cherish the memory / cherish a hope / cherish the moment / cherish one's freedom**

👉 Example: She will always cherish the memories of her childhood spent in the countryside.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor **cherished** every opportunity to mentor young scholars and guide them toward academic excellence.
   这位教授珍爱每一个指导年轻学者走向学术卓越的机会。

2. Communities that **cherish** their cultural heritage tend to maintain stronger social bonds across generations.
   珍视文化遗产的社区往往能在各代人之间保持更强的社会纽带。

3. The diplomat continued to **cherish** the hope that peaceful negotiations would ultimately resolve the conflict.
   这位外交官继续怀抱着和平谈判最终会解决冲突的希望。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cher-ish" — "cher" sounds like the French word for "dear" (cher), so to cherish is to hold something dear.
把"cherish"中的"cher"联想到法语中的"cher（亲爱的）"——珍爱就是把某物视为珍贵的。
