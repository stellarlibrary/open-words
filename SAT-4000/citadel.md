# 🏰 citadel

🔊 /ˈsɪtədəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A fortress, typically on high ground, protecting or dominating a city; a stronghold or place of refuge.
**中文：** 城堡；堡垒；大本营；根据地；避难处。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fortress（堡垒）
- stronghold（据点）
- bastion（棱堡）
- fortification（防御工事）
- refuge（避难所）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a citadel of freedom / the ancient citadel / storm the citadel / within the citadel**

👉 Example: The medieval citadel stood atop the hill, its walls still intact after centuries of warfare.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The invading forces laid siege to the **citadel** for three months before the garrison finally surrendered due to starvation.
   入侵军队围攻城堡长达三个月，驻军最终因饥饿而投降。

2. The university has long been regarded as a **citadel** of intellectual freedom and independent scholarly inquiry.
   这所大学长期以来被视为知识自由和独立学术探究的大本营。

3. Archaeologists discovered the ruins of an ancient **citadel** that once served as the regional capital of a powerful empire.
   考古学家发现了一座古代城堡的遗迹，它曾是一个强大帝国的地区首府。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cit-" (city) + "-adel" — a citadel is the fortified heart of a city, the last place of defense where citizens take refuge.
把"citadel"联想到"cit-（城市）"——城堡是城市的坚固核心，是市民最后避难的防御之地。
