# 🏰 entrench

🔊 /ɪnˈtrentʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To establish firmly so that change is difficult; to dig trenches for defensive purposes
**中文：** v. 确立，牢固；用壕沟围住；以壕沟防护

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- establish（确立）
- embed（嵌入）
- fortify（加固）
- ingrain（根深蒂固）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **entrench a position / deeply entrenched / entrenched beliefs / entrenched interests**

👉 Example: Years of tradition have entrenched these customs so deeply that reform seems nearly impossible.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Decades of bureaucratic inertia have **entrenched** outdated regulations that now hinder economic growth and innovation.
   数十年的官僚惰性已经使过时的法规根深蒂固，如今阻碍了经济增长和创新。

2. The military commander ordered his troops to **entrench** their positions along the northern ridge before dawn.
   军事指挥官命令部队在黎明前沿北部山脊构筑防御工事。

3. Systemic inequality becomes **entrenched** when institutions fail to address the root causes of social disparity.
   当制度未能解决社会不平等的根本原因时，系统性的不平等就会变得根深蒂固。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "en-TRENCH" — when you dig a trench around something, you make it firmly fixed and hard to remove.
把"entrench"想成"在周围挖壕沟(trench)"——当你在某物周围挖了壕沟，它就牢牢固定住，难以移除。
