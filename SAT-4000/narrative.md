# 📖 narrative

🔊 /ˈnærətɪv/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A spoken or written account of connected events; a story or tale. (adj.) Of or relating to narration; in the form of storytelling
**中文：** n. 叙述；记叙文；故事 adj. 叙述的；有故事性的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- story（故事）
- account（叙述）
- chronicle（编年史）
- tale（故事）
- report（报告）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **narrative structure / personal narrative / dominant narrative / narrative technique / control the narrative**

👉 Example: The political candidate sought to control the narrative surrounding her controversial policy proposals.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher constructed a compelling **narrative** that connected disparate historical events into a coherent analytical framework.
   研究者构建了一个引人注目的**叙述**，将不同的历史事件连接成一个连贯的分析框架。

2. The dominant **narrative** in mainstream media often oversimplifies the complex socioeconomic factors that contribute to inequality.
   主流媒体中的主导**叙事**往往过度简化了导致不平等的复杂社会经济因素。

3. The professor employed a **narrative** approach to teaching history, using personal stories to illuminate broader societal transformations.
   教授采用**叙事**方法教授历史，用个人故事来阐明更广泛的社会变革。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "narrative" as "narrate + ive" — adding "-ive" turns the act of narrating into the story itself or something related to storytelling.
把"narrative"想成"narrate + ive"——加上"-ive"将叙述的行为变成了故事本身或与讲故事相关的事物。
