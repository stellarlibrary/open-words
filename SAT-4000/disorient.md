# 🧭 disorient

🔊 /dɪsˈɔːriɛnt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause someone to lose their sense of direction or feel confused about their position or surroundings
**中文：** v. 使迷惑；使失去方向感

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- confuse（使困惑）
- bewilder（使迷惑）
- perplex（使困惑）
- disorientate（使迷失方向）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **disorient a person / disoriented by the darkness / feel disoriented / completely disoriented**

👉 Example: The dense fog disoriented the hikers, causing them to wander far from the marked trail.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden shift in time zones can significantly **disorient** international travelers, disrupting their circadian rhythms for several days.
   时区的突然变化会严重使国际旅行者失去方向感，扰乱他们数天的生物节律。

2. The labyrinthine layout of the ancient city was designed to **disorient** invading forces and provide defenders with strategic advantages.
   这座古城的迷宫般布局旨在使入侵部队迷失方向，为防御者提供战略优势。

3. Patients recovering from general anesthesia often feel **disoriented** and require careful monitoring until full cognitive awareness returns.
   从全身麻醉中恢复的患者通常会感到迷糊，需要仔细监护直到完全恢复认知意识。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-orient" as "losing your orient (direction)" — the word "orient" originally meant "east," so losing your orient means you cannot find which way is which.
把"disorient"想成"dis（失去）+ orient（方向）"——"orient"原意是"东方"，失去方向就意味着分不清东南西北。
