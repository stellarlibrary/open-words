# 🧷 prim

🔊 /prɪm/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, verb | 形容词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Feeling or showing disapproval of anything regarded as improper; stiffly correct and neat. (v.) To make prim; to purse or give a demure expression to
**中文：** adj. 拘谨的；整洁的；呆板的 v. 使显得一本正经

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- prudish（过分拘谨的）
- demure（端庄的）
- strait-laced（古板的）
- proper（正经的）
- fastidious（挑剔的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prim and proper / prim expression / prim appearance / prim one's lips**

👉 Example: She sat with prim posture throughout the formal dinner, never resting her elbows on the table.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **prim** librarian insisted on absolute silence and would glare at anyone who dared to whisper in her domain.
   那位拘谨的图书管理员坚持要求绝对安静，对任何敢在她管辖范围内窃窃私语的人怒目而视。

2. She **primmed** her lips in disapproval when the children tracked mud across the freshly cleaned marble floor.
   当孩子们在刚刚清洁过的大理石地板上踩出泥印时，她不满地抿紧了嘴唇。

3. Despite his **prim** exterior, the diplomat possessed a sharp wit that occasionally surfaced during informal conversations.
   尽管外表拘谨，这位外交官却拥有敏锐的机智，偶尔在非正式交谈中流露出来。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "prim" as related to "prime" — someone prim always wants to look their prime best, overly neat and proper at all times.
把"prim"联想到"prime（最好的）"——一个拘谨的人总是想看起来最体面，时刻保持过分整洁和正经。
