# 🧪 colloid

🔊 /ˈkɒl.ɔɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词，名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** (adj.) Of or relating to a colloid; glue-like in texture. (n.) A substance consisting of microscopically dispersed particles suspended throughout another substance.

**中文：** （形容词）胶质的。（名词）胶体；由微观分散粒子悬浮在另一种物质中组成的物质。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- suspension（悬浮液）
- emulsion（乳状液）
- gel（凝胶）
- dispersion（分散体）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Collocation:** "colloidal solution / colloidal particles" — commonly used in scientific and technical contexts.

**Example:** The chemist prepared a colloid by dispersing fine metallic particles in a liquid medium.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Milk is a common example of a **colloid** in which fat droplets are evenly dispersed throughout a water-based solution.
   牛奶是**胶体**的一个常见例子，其中脂肪液滴均匀分散在水基溶液中。

2. The researcher analyzed the stability of the **colloid** to determine how long the suspended particles would remain uniformly distributed.
   研究人员分析了**胶体**的稳定性，以确定悬浮粒子能均匀分布多长时间。

3. Industrial applications of **colloid** chemistry include the production of paints, adhesives, and pharmaceutical formulations.
   **胶体**化学的工业应用包括油漆、粘合剂和药物制剂的生产。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

**English:** "Colloid" comes from Greek *kolla* (glue) + *-oid* (resembling) — something resembling glue, a thick, sticky substance with particles suspended in it.

**中文：** "Colloid"来自希腊语 *kolla*（胶水）+ *-oid*（类似）——类似胶水的东西，即含有悬浮粒子的黏稠物质，也就是"胶体"。
