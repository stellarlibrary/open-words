# 🚶 perambulate

🔊 /pəˈræmbjʊleɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To walk through or around, especially for inspection or pleasure; to stroll or traverse
**中文：** v. 巡行；巡视；漫步

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- stroll（漫步）
- traverse（穿越）
- promenade（散步）
- wander（徘徊）
- patrol（巡逻）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **perambulate the grounds / perambulate the streets / perambulate the boundary / perambulate leisurely**

👉 Example: The inspector chose to perambulate the entire estate before submitting his official assessment report.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The security guard was required to **perambulate** the campus grounds every two hours throughout the night shift.
   保安被要求在夜班期间每两小时巡视一次校园。

2. Victorian scholars would often **perambulate** along the riverbank while discussing matters of philosophy and literature.
   维多利亚时代的学者们常常在河岸漫步，同时讨论哲学和文学问题。

3. The committee decided to **perambulate** the parish boundaries in accordance with the centuries-old ceremonial tradition.
   委员会决定按照数百年的仪式传统巡视教区边界。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "perambulate" as "per-amble-ate" — to amble (walk slowly) per (through) a place, taking it all in.
把"perambulate"想成"per-amble-ate"——在一个地方慢慢走过(amble through)，边走边看。
