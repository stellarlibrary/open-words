# 🕳️ devoid

🔊 /dɪˈvɔɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Entirely lacking or free from; completely without a particular quality or substance
**中文：** adj. 缺乏的；全无的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- lacking（缺乏的）
- bereft（丧失的）
- empty（空的）
- destitute（缺乏的）
- void（空的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **devoid of emotion / devoid of meaning / devoid of hope / devoid of any**

👉 Example: The abandoned warehouse was devoid of any furnishings or signs of recent habitation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The critic dismissed the novel as being entirely **devoid** of originality, calling it a pale imitation of earlier works.
   评论家认为这部小说完全缺乏原创性，称其为早期作品的拙劣模仿。

2. The defendant's testimony appeared **devoid** of genuine remorse, which ultimately influenced the severity of the sentence.
   被告的证词似乎全无真诚的悔意，这最终影响了量刑的严重程度。

3. The barren landscape stretched for miles, **devoid** of vegetation and seemingly incapable of sustaining any form of life.
   荒芜的景观绵延数英里，完全没有植被，似乎无法维持任何形式的生命。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-void" as literally "of the void" — something devoid of a quality has a void, an empty space where that quality should be.
把"devoid"想成"de + void(空白)"——devoid of某种品质意味着那里有一个空白，该有的品质完全不存在。
