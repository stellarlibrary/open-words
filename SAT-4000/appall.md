# 😱 appall

🔊 /əˈpɔːl/<br>
🏷️ *v. 使胆寒；使惊骇*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** To greatly dismay or horrify; to fill with shock, dread, or a strong sense of revulsion.

**中文：** 使惊骇；使胆寒；使人感到极度震惊或恐惧。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- horrify 使恐惧
- dismay 使惊愕
- shock 使震惊
- terrify 使害怕
- outrage 使愤慨

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Common collocations:** appall someone, appalled by/at something, be appalled to learn, deeply appalled

**Example:** "Appall" is often used in the passive voice (e.g., "I was appalled by the conditions"). It conveys a stronger emotional reaction than "surprise" or "disappoint."

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The graphic images of the disaster **appalled** the viewers and prompted immediate calls for humanitarian aid.
   灾难的惨烈画面**使观众震惊**，并立即引发了人道主义援助的呼声。

2. Citizens were deeply **appalled** by the government's failure to address the widespread environmental contamination.
   公民对政府未能解决大范围环境污染问题深感**震惊**。

3. The sheer magnitude of corporate corruption **appalled** even the most experienced investigative journalists.
   企业腐败的惊人规模甚至**使**最有经验的调查记者也**感到胆寒**。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Appall" sounds like "a pall" — a pall is a dark cloth draped over a coffin. Something that appalls you casts a dark shadow over your spirit.

谐音联想：appall 听起来像"a pall"（棺罩）——令人 appall 的事就像给心灵蒙上一层黑布，使人胆寒。
