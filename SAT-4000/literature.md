# 📕 literature

🔊 /ˈlɪtərətʃər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Written works, especially those regarded as having artistic merit; the body of writings on a particular subject
**中文：** n. 文学；文学作品；文献；著作

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- writings（著作）
- publications（出版物）
- texts（文本）
- letters（文学）
- corpus（文集）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **English literature / scientific literature / literature review / body of literature / classical literature**

👉 Example: The researcher conducted an extensive literature review before designing the experimental methodology.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The university's **literature** department offers courses spanning ancient Greek drama to contemporary postcolonial fiction and poetry.
   该大学的文学系开设了从古希腊戏剧到当代后殖民小说和诗歌的课程。

2. A thorough review of the existing **literature** revealed significant gaps in research on early childhood cognitive development.
   对现有文献的全面回顾揭示了早期儿童认知发展研究中的重大空白。

3. Nobel Prize winners in **literature** are recognized for their outstanding contributions to the art of written expression worldwide.
   诺贝尔文学奖获得者因其对全球书面表达艺术的杰出贡献而获得认可。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "literature" as "liter-ature" — from Latin "littera" meaning "letter." Literature is the grand collection of all that has been written.
把"literature"想成源自拉丁语"littera（字母）"——文学就是所有书面作品的宏大集合。
