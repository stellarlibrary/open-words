# ♾️ perpetuate

🔊 /pərˈpetʃueɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make something continue indefinitely; to preserve from oblivion or extinction; to cause to last or endure
**中文：** v. 使不朽；使长存；使永久化；保持

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sustain（维持）
- preserve（保存）
- maintain（保持）
- immortalize（使不朽）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **perpetuate a myth / perpetuate stereotypes / perpetuate a cycle / perpetuate the memory**

👉 Example: The documentary aimed to perpetuate the memory of those who sacrificed their lives for freedom.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics argued that the media coverage served only to **perpetuate** harmful stereotypes about marginalized communities across the country.
   批评者认为，媒体报道只是在全国范围内延续对边缘化社区的有害刻板印象。

2. The foundation was established to **perpetuate** the legacy of the Nobel laureate through annual scholarships and research grants.
   该基金会的成立旨在通过年度奖学金和研究资助来延续这位诺贝尔奖获得者的遗产。

3. Failing to address systemic inequality will inevitably **perpetuate** the cycle of poverty that has persisted for generations.
   未能解决系统性不平等将不可避免地延续已经持续了几代人的贫困循环。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "perpetu-ate" — it contains "perpetual," meaning everlasting. To perpetuate is to make something perpetual, to keep it going forever.
把"perpetuate"想成"able to make perpetual（永久的）"——使某事物永久化，让它永远持续下去。
