# ⏸️ abeyance

🔊 /əˈbeɪəns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A state of temporary suspension or inactivity; a condition of being shelved or put on hold
**中文：** n. 中止，搁置；暂时无效；归属待定

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- suspension（暂停）
- dormancy（休眠）
- postponement（推迟）
- intermission（中断）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in abeyance / held in abeyance / fall into abeyance / keep in abeyance**

👉 Example: The reform proposal has been held in abeyance until the new administration takes office.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The controversial policy was placed in **abeyance** pending a thorough review by the legislative committee.
   这项有争议的政策在立法委员会进行全面审查之前被暂时搁置。

2. Construction of the new bridge has fallen into **abeyance** due to a prolonged dispute over funding.
   由于资金问题的长期争议，新桥的建设已经中止。

3. The court ordered that the inheritance claim be held in **abeyance** until all legal heirs were identified.
   法院命令在确认所有合法继承人之前，将遗产索赔暂时搁置。

---

"Abeyance" sounds like "a bay" — imagine a ship resting in a bay, waiting and inactive, not sailing anywhere.
"Abeyance"听起来像"a bay"（一个海湾）——想象一艘船停在海湾里，静止等待，哪儿也不去。
