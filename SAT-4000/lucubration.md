# 📚 lucubration

🔊 /ˌluːkjʊˈbreɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Study or work carried out late into the night; a piece of writing produced by laborious study, especially one that is overly elaborate or pedantic
**中文：** n. 刻苦钻研；苦心而成的著作

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- study（研究）
- scholarship（学问）
- meditation（沉思）
- treatise（论文）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **scholarly lucubration / midnight lucubration / years of lucubration / product of lucubration**

👉 Example: The professor's latest book was the product of decades of patient lucubration in the university archives.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosopher's dense treatise was clearly the result of years of painstaking **lucubration** in the quiet solitude of his study.
   这位哲学家晦涩的论著显然是他在书房的安静独处中多年刻苦钻研的成果。

2. Her doctoral dissertation represented the culmination of five years of intense **lucubration** on medieval European trade networks.
   她的博士论文是她对中世纪欧洲贸易网络五年深入钻研的结晶。

3. Critics dismissed his latest publication as mere pedantic **lucubration** that lacked practical relevance to contemporary scientific discourse.
   评论家们将他最新的出版物斥为纯粹的学究式钻研，缺乏与当代科学话语的实际关联。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "luc-ubration" — "luc" relates to Latin "lucubrare" meaning "to work by lamplight," picturing a scholar studying late at night by candlelight.
把"lucubration"想成"luc（灯光）+ ubration"——"luc"与拉丁语"lucubrare（在灯光下工作）"有关，想象一位学者深夜在烛光下刻苦钻研。
