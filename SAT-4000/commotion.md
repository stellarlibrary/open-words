# 🌪️ commotion

🔊 /kəˈmoʊʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A state of confused and noisy disturbance; a civil uprising or public disorder; a scene of turmoil and agitation
**中文：** n. 骚动；暴乱；喧闹；混乱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- turmoil（骚动）
- uproar（喧嚣）
- disturbance（骚乱）
- tumult（混乱）
- agitation（激动）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cause a commotion / create a commotion / a commotion in the crowd / amid the commotion**

👉 Example: The unexpected announcement caused a great commotion among the assembled delegates.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden fire alarm triggered a **commotion** in the crowded auditorium as hundreds of attendees rushed toward the emergency exits.
   突然响起的火警在拥挤的礼堂引起了骚动，数百名与会者冲向紧急出口。

2. The controversial judicial ruling created a widespread **commotion** across the nation, prompting demonstrations in several major cities.
   这项有争议的司法裁决在全国引发了广泛的骚动，在几个主要城市引发了示威活动。

3. Amid all the **commotion** surrounding the scandal, the company's board of directors quietly convened to discuss the matter privately.
   在围绕丑闻的一片骚动中，公司董事会悄悄召开会议私下讨论此事。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "commotion" as "common motion" — when everyone is in common motion at once, running around chaotically, that is commotion.
把"commotion"想成"common motion"——当所有人同时都在混乱地奔跑，那就是骚动。
