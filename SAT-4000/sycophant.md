# 🦊 sycophant

🔊 /ˈsɪkəfænt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A flatterer; a person who seeks favor through excessive praise. (adj.) Obsequious; fawning
**中文：** n. 拍马屁的人，谄媚者，奉承者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flatterer（奉承者）
- toady（谄媚者）
- fawner（献媚者）
- bootlicker（拍马屁者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mere sycophant / surround oneself with sycophants / sycophant behavior / political sycophant**

👉 Example: The emperor surrounded himself with sycophants who told him only what he wished to hear.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The executive's inner circle consisted largely of **sycophants** who never challenged any of his increasingly questionable decisions.
   这位高管的核心圈子主要由谄媚者组成，他们从不质疑他日益可疑的决定。

2. Effective leadership requires advisors who offer honest counsel rather than **sycophants** who merely echo popular sentiment.
   有效的领导力需要提供诚实建议的顾问，而不是仅仅附和主流意见的奉承者。

3. The journalist's article exposed several prominent **sycophants** in the administration who had privately expressed views contrary to their public praise.
   记者的文章揭露了政府中几位知名的谄媚者，他们私下表达的观点与公开的赞美截然相反。

Think of "syco-phant" as "sick of ants" — imagine ants crawling around a picnic, constantly buzzing and clinging, just like a sycophant clings to power.
把"sycophant"想成"sick of ants（讨厌蚂蚁）"——想象蚂蚁围着野餐不停地嗡嗡叫和攀附，就像谄媚者攀附权力一样。
