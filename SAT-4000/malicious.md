# 🐍 malicious

🔊 /məˈlɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Intending or intended to do harm; characterized by malice or ill will
**中文：** adj. 恶意的；恶毒的；蓄意的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- spiteful（恶意的）
- vindictive（报复性的）
- malevolent（恶毒的）
- vicious（恶毒的）
- pernicious（有害的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **malicious intent / malicious gossip / malicious software / malicious prosecution / malicious rumor**

👉 Example: The IT department identified malicious software that had been secretly installed on the company network.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The investigation revealed that **malicious** rumors had been deliberately spread to undermine the candidate's reputation before the election.
   调查显示，恶意谣言是在选举前被蓄意散布以损害候选人声誉的。

2. Cybersecurity experts warned that **malicious** actors were increasingly targeting hospitals and critical infrastructure with ransomware attacks.
   网络安全专家警告说，恶意行为者正日益以勒索软件攻击瞄准医院和关键基础设施。

3. The judge dismissed the lawsuit, ruling that it constituted **malicious** prosecution intended solely to harass the defendant.
   法官驳回了这起诉讼，裁定其构成恶意起诉，目的仅在于骚扰被告。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "malicious" as "malice" + "-ious" — full of malice. If someone is malicious, they are overflowing with the desire to cause harm.
把"malicious"想成"malice"（恶意）+"-ious"（充满的）——充满恶意的。一个malicious的人就是满怀伤害他人之心的人。
