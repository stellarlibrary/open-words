# 😢 maudlin

🔊 /ˈmɔːdlɪn/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Self-pityingly or tearfully sentimental; weakly and effusively emotional, often in an exaggerated or embarrassing way
**中文：** adj. 感情脆弱的；容易流泪的；过于伤感的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sentimental（多愁善感的）
- mawkish（令人作呕的伤感）
- lachrymose（爱哭的）
- weepy（爱哭的）
- bathetic（故作伤感的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **maudlin sentimentality / maudlin tears / maudlin speech / become maudlin / grow maudlin after drinking**

👉 Example: After a few glasses of wine, he became maudlin and began reminiscing tearfully about his childhood.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The critic dismissed the film as **maudlin** and manipulative, arguing that it relied on cheap emotional tricks rather than genuine storytelling.
   评论家认为这部电影过于伤感且有操控性，认为它依赖廉价的情感技巧而非真正的叙事。

2. Her **maudlin** recollections of the past grew increasingly tiresome to colleagues who preferred practical discussions over emotional outbursts.
   她对过去伤感的回忆越来越让那些喜欢实际讨论而非情感宣泄的同事感到厌烦。

3. The politician's **maudlin** appeal for sympathy during the debate was widely seen as insincere and failed to persuade skeptical voters.
   这位政客在辩论中感情脆弱地博取同情的表现被普遍认为不真诚，未能说服持怀疑态度的选民。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "maudlin" as coming from Mary Magdalene, who was often depicted weeping — so maudlin means excessively tearful and sentimental.
"Maudlin"源自抹大拉的玛丽亚（Mary Magdalene），她常被描绘为泪流满面的形象——因此maudlin意为过于伤感和多愁善感。
