# ⏳ impend

🔊 /ɪmˈpɛnd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To be about to happen; to loom or be imminent, especially something threatening or momentous
**中文：** v. 迫近；即将发生

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- loom（逼近）
- approach（临近）
- threaten（威胁）
- be imminent（即将来临）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **impending danger / impending crisis / impending doom / impending deadline**

👉 Example: A sense of impending disaster hung over the coastal city as the hurricane approached.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The financial analysts warned that a severe economic recession was **impending** and urged immediate government intervention.
   金融分析师警告说，一场严重的经济衰退即将来临，并敦促政府立即干预。

2. As the deadline **impended**, the research team worked through the night to finalize their comprehensive grant proposal.
   随着截止日期迫近，研究团队通宵工作以完成他们全面的拨款提案。

3. Dark storm clouds gathered on the horizon, signaling that a violent tempest was clearly **impending** over the region.
   乌云在地平线上聚集，预示着一场猛烈的暴风雨即将降临该地区。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-pend" as "I'm pending" — something pending is about to happen, hanging overhead and ready to arrive.
把"impend"想成"I'm pending"——某件事悬而未决，即将发生，就像悬在头顶上一样。
