# 👨‍⚖️ legislator

🔊 /ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪtər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who makes laws; a member of a legislative body such as a parliament or congress.
**中文：** n. 立法者；立法委员；议员

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- lawmaker（立法者）
- senator（参议员）
- representative（代表）
- congressman（国会议员）
- parliamentarian（议员）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **state legislator / federal legislator / elected legislator / legislator proposed / legislator voted**

👉 Example: The legislator introduced a bipartisan bill aimed at reforming the criminal justice system.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The veteran **legislator** spent three decades championing affordable healthcare and public education reform in the senate.
   这位资深立法者在参议院花了三十年时间倡导可负担的医疗保健和公共教育改革。

2. Every **legislator** bears a solemn responsibility to represent the interests and concerns of their constituents faithfully.
   每位立法者都肩负着忠实代表选民利益和关切的庄严责任。

3. The newly elected **legislator** pledged to prioritize transparency and accountability throughout her entire term in office.
   这位新当选的立法者承诺在其整个任期内优先考虑透明度和问责制。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "legislator" as "legislate + or" — the "-or" suffix means "one who does," so a legislator is one who legislates, or makes laws.
把"legislator"想成"legislate + or"——后缀"-or"表示"做……的人"，所以legislator就是制定法律的人。
