# 📉 subside

🔊 /səbˈsaɪd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To become less intense or severe; to sink to a lower level; to settle or deposit
**中文：** v. 下沉；沉淀；平息；减退

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- diminish（减少）
- abate（减弱）
- recede（消退）
- wane（衰退）
- ebb（退潮）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **subside gradually / pain subsides / floodwaters subside / anger subsides**

👉 Example: The doctor assured the patient that the swelling would subside within a few days of treatment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Public outrage over the scandal began to **subside** only after the government announced a comprehensive reform package.
   直到政府宣布了一项全面的改革方案，公众对丑闻的愤怒才开始平息。

2. The floodwaters took several weeks to **subside**, leaving behind extensive damage to homes and infrastructure.
   洪水花了几周时间才退去，留下了对房屋和基础设施的大面积破坏。

3. As the initial excitement **subsided**, the committee began a more sober evaluation of the project's feasibility.
   随着最初的兴奋消退，委员会开始对项目的可行性进行更加冷静的评估。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sub-side" as "sinking to the side and below" — like water levels going down or emotions settling.
把"subside"想成"sub"（向下）+"side"（落下）——就像水位下降或情绪平复一样。
