# 💎 veritable

🔊 /ˈvɛrɪtəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Used for emphasis to describe something as genuine, real, or true in the fullest sense
**中文：** adj. 真正的；名副其实的；十足的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- genuine（真正的）
- authentic（真实的）
- undeniable（不可否认的）
- absolute（绝对的）
- bona fide（真正的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a veritable feast / a veritable treasure / a veritable paradise / a veritable flood**

👉 Example: The library was a veritable treasure trove of rare manuscripts and first editions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The tropical rainforest is a **veritable** pharmacy of natural compounds that scientists have only begun to catalog and study.
   热带雨林是一座名副其实的天然化合物宝库，科学家们才刚刚开始编目和研究。

2. After the merger, the corporation became a **veritable** empire spanning six continents and employing over two hundred thousand workers.
   合并后，该公司成为一个名副其实的帝国，横跨六大洲，雇佣了超过二十万名员工。

3. The annual conference attracted a **veritable** who's who of leading scholars, policy makers, and influential industry executives.
   年度会议吸引了一批名副其实的顶级学者、政策制定者和有影响力的行业高管。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "verit-able" — from Latin "veritas" meaning truth. Something veritable is "able to be verified as true," hence truly genuine.
把"veritable"想成"verit-able"——来自拉丁语"veritas"意为真理。veritable就是"能够被证实为真的"，因此是真正的。
