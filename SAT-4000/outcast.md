# 🚷 outcast

🔊 /ˈaʊtkæst/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who has been rejected or excluded from a group or society. (adj.) Cast out; homeless; rejected
**中文：** n. 流浪的人；被驱逐的人 adj. 被遗弃的；无家可归的；被逐出的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pariah（贱民）
- exile（流亡者）
- outlaw（被放逐者）
- reject（被拒者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **social outcast / become an outcast / treat someone as an outcast / outcast from society**

👉 Example: After the scandal, he became a social outcast, shunned by former friends and colleagues.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The protagonist of the novel is an **outcast** who struggles to find acceptance in a deeply prejudiced society.
   这部小说的主人公是一个在充满偏见的社会中努力寻求接纳的被逐者。

2. Throughout history, many brilliant thinkers were treated as **outcasts** before their ideas gained recognition.
   纵观历史，许多杰出的思想家在其思想获得认可之前都被视为异类。

3. The refugee community felt like **outcasts** in their new country, struggling with language barriers and discrimination.
   难民群体在新国家感觉自己像被遗弃的人，挣扎于语言障碍和歧视之中。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "out + cast" — someone who is cast out of the group, thrown outside the circle of belonging.
把"outcast"拆成"out（外面）+ cast（扔）"——一个被扔到群体之外、被排斥在归属圈子之外的人。
