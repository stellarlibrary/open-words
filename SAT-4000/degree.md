# 🎓 degree

🔊 /dɪˈɡriː/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The extent, measure, or scope of an action, condition, or relation; a unit of measurement for temperature or angles; an academic title conferred by a university
**中文：** n. 程度，等级；度；度数；学位

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- extent（程度）
- level（水平）
- measure（尺度）
- qualification（资格）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **to a certain degree / by degrees / degree of difficulty / bachelor's degree / to some degree**

👉 Example: The success of the project depends to a large degree on the cooperation of all team members.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee acknowledged that the proposed reforms would, to a significant **degree**, reshape the institution's governance structure.
   委员会承认，拟议的改革将在很大程度上重塑该机构的治理结构。

2. She earned a master's **degree** in environmental science before pursuing a career in sustainable urban planning and design.
   她在攻读可持续城市规划与设计职业之前获得了环境科学硕士学位。

3. The temperature dropped by several **degrees** overnight, causing widespread frost damage to the region's agricultural crops.
   气温在一夜之间下降了好几度，对该地区的农作物造成了大范围的霜冻损害。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "degree" as steps on a ladder — each step is a different level or measure, whether of temperature, angle, or academic achievement.
把"degree"想成梯子上的台阶——每一阶都是不同的水平或度量，无论是温度、角度还是学术成就。
