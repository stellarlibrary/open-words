# ⏳ imminent

🔊 /ˈɪmɪnənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** About to happen very soon; impending and often threatening or unavoidable.
**中文：** adj. 即将来临的；迫近的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- impending（即将发生的）
- looming（逼近的）
- forthcoming（即将到来的）
- approaching（临近的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **imminent danger / imminent threat / imminent collapse / imminent arrival**

👉 Example: Meteorologists warned that an imminent hurricane posed serious risks to coastal communities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The intelligence report indicated that an **imminent** security threat required the immediate evacuation of all government personnel.
   情报报告表明，一个迫在眉睫的安全威胁要求所有政府人员立即撤离。

2. Economists cautioned that the **imminent** recession would disproportionately affect small businesses and lower-income households across the nation.
   经济学家警告说，即将到来的经济衰退将对全国的小企业和低收入家庭产生不成比例的影响。

3. The structural engineers confirmed that the **imminent** collapse of the aging bridge necessitated its immediate closure to all traffic.
   结构工程师确认，这座老化桥梁即将坍塌，必须立即对所有交通关闭。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-min-ent" as "in minutes" — something that will happen in just minutes, so it is very close.
把"imminent"想成"in minutes（几分钟之内）"——马上就要发生的事，所以是即将来临的。
