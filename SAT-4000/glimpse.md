# 👁️ glimpse

🔊 /ɡlɪmps/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A momentary or partial view of something. (v.) To see something briefly or partially
**中文：** n. 一瞥，一看 v. 瞥见

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- glance（一瞥）
- peek（偷看）
- sight（看见）
- flash（一闪）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **catch a glimpse of / a brief glimpse / a rare glimpse / glimpse into / glimpse the future**

👉 Example: The biography offers a rare glimpse into the private life of the renowned physicist.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The released documents provided a rare **glimpse** into the classified diplomatic negotiations that shaped the postwar international order.
   公开的文件让人们难得地一窥塑造战后国际秩序的机密外交谈判。

2. Visitors to the archaeological site can **glimpse** the remains of a civilization that flourished over three thousand years ago.
   考古遗址的参观者可以瞥见三千多年前繁荣文明的遗迹。

3. The preliminary research findings offer a tantalizing **glimpse** of how gene therapy could revolutionize the treatment of hereditary diseases.
   初步研究结果让人们得以一窥基因疗法如何可能彻底改变遗传疾病的治疗方式。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "glimpse" as "gli-mpse (limps)" — your vision limps past something so quickly that you only get a brief, incomplete view.
把"glimpse"想成"gli-mpse（limps，跛行）"——你的视线像跛行一样快速掠过某物，只获得短暂而不完整的一瞥。
