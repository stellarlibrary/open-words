# 📯 herald

🔊 /ˈhɛrəld/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A sign that something important is about to happen; a messenger or forerunner. (v.) To signal or announce the approach of something significant
**中文：** n. 预兆；征兆；先驱；传令官 v. 预示…的来临；传达；通告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- harbinger（先驱）
- precursor（前兆）
- forerunner（先行者）
- announce（宣告）
- signal（预示）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **herald a new era / herald the arrival of / herald change / herald as a breakthrough**

👉 Example: Many scientists herald the discovery as a turning point in the fight against infectious diseases.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The invention of the printing press is widely considered to **herald** the beginning of the modern information age.
   印刷术的发明被广泛认为预示了现代信息时代的开始。

2. Economists viewed the sudden rise in consumer spending as a **herald** of broader economic recovery across the region.
   经济学家将消费者支出的突然增长视为该地区更广泛经济复苏的预兆。

3. The first robin of spring has traditionally served as a **herald** of warmer weather in many Northern Hemisphere cultures.
   在北半球的许多文化中，春天的第一只知更鸟传统上被视为温暖天气的先兆。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of a "herald" as a royal messenger blowing a trumpet to announce the king's arrival — it signals something important is coming.
把"herald"想成一个皇家信使吹响号角宣布国王驾到——它预示着重要的事情即将到来。
