# 🤦 blunder

🔊 /ˈblʌndər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To move clumsily or blindly; to make a serious, careless mistake. (n.) A grave error or mistake caused by ignorance or carelessness.
**中文：** v. 跌跌撞撞地走；犯大错，做错 n. 大错，愚蠢的错误

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gaffe（失态）
- blunder（失误）
- fumble（笨拙行事）
- stumble（跌跌撞撞）
- miscalculation（误判）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **make a blunder / costly blunder / blunder into / tactical blunder / blunder through**

👉 Example: The diplomat's inadvertent blunder during negotiations nearly derailed the entire peace process.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The general's strategic **blunder** resulted in the needless loss of thousands of soldiers during the ill-conceived military campaign.
   将军的战略**失误**导致在这场考虑不周的军事行动中数千名士兵无谓牺牲。

2. The exhausted hiker **blundered** through the dense undergrowth in darkness, unable to locate the marked trail back to camp.
   精疲力竭的徒步者在黑暗中**跌跌撞撞地**穿过茂密的灌木丛，找不到回营地的标记路线。

3. A single administrative **blunder** in the financial report cost the corporation millions of dollars in regulatory penalties and fines.
   财务报告中一个行政**大错**使该公司损失了数百万美元的监管处罚和罚款。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "blunder" as "blind + under" — stumbling blindly under pressure, making careless and costly mistakes.
把"blunder"想成"blind + under"——在压力下盲目行事，犯下粗心且代价高昂的错误。
