# 🐑 gullible

🔊 /ˈɡʌləbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Easily persuaded to believe something; readily deceived or cheated
**中文：** adj. 易受骗的；轻信的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- credulous（轻信的）
- naive（天真的）
- trusting（信任的）
- unsuspecting（无戒备的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **gullible person / gullible consumers / gullible enough to / too gullible**

👉 Example: Scammers specifically target gullible individuals who lack experience with online financial transactions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The fraudulent investment company preyed on **gullible** retirees who believed the promise of extraordinarily high guaranteed returns.
   这家欺诈性投资公司以轻信的退休人员为目标，他们相信了超高保证回报的承诺。

2. Critics argued that only the most **gullible** voters would accept such transparently misleading claims without scrutiny.
   批评者认为，只有最容易受骗的选民才会不加审视地接受如此明显误导性的说法。

3. A healthy degree of skepticism prevents even well-intentioned people from becoming **gullible** targets of sophisticated scams.
   适度的怀疑精神能防止即使是好心的人成为复杂骗局的易骗目标。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gullible" as related to "gull" (a seabird) — gulls will swallow almost anything you throw at them, just like gullible people swallow any story.
把"gullible"和"gull"（海鸥）联系起来——海鸥几乎会吞下你扔给它的任何东西，就像容易受骗的人会相信任何故事。
