# 💰 loot

🔊 /luːt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Goods taken from an enemy in war or stolen by thieves; plunder. (v.) To rob or steal, especially during a war or riot
**中文：** n. 战利品；抢劫 v. 掠夺，抢劫，劫掠

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- plunder（掠夺）
- pillage（抢劫）
- ransack（洗劫）
- spoils（战利品）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **loot and plunder / loot a store / war loot / loot from / stolen loot**

👉 Example: Rioters began to loot shops along the main street after the power outage plunged the city into darkness.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Archaeologists recovered ancient **loot** that had been buried by raiders centuries ago beneath the ruins of a medieval fortress.
   考古学家在一座中世纪堡垒的废墟下发现了数百年前被劫掠者埋藏的古代战利品。

2. Opportunistic criminals attempted to **loot** commercial establishments during the widespread civil unrest that followed the controversial verdict.
   在有争议的判决引发的大范围社会动荡期间，投机犯罪分子试图洗劫商业机构。

3. The museum exhibition displayed **loot** confiscated from illegal trafficking networks that had systematically plundered cultural heritage sites worldwide.
   博物馆展览展出了从非法贩运网络中没收的赃物，这些网络曾系统性地掠夺世界各地的文化遗产。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "loot" sounding like "lute" — imagine a thief grabbing a valuable lute (instrument) as plunder from a castle.
把"loot"想成听起来像"lute（鲁特琴）"——想象一个小偷从城堡里抢走一把贵重的鲁特琴作为战利品。
