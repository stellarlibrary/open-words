# 🏥 cancer

🔊 /ˈkænsər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A serious disease caused by uncontrolled division of abnormal cells in the body; figuratively, something evil or destructive that spreads.
**中文：** n. 癌症；恶性肿瘤；弊病

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- malignancy（恶性肿瘤）
- tumor（肿瘤）
- carcinoma（癌）
- blight（祸害）
- scourge（灾祸）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cancer patient / cancer research / lung cancer / cancer treatment / a cancer on society**

👉 Example: Corruption is often described as a cancer that erodes the foundations of democratic governance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Advances in immunotherapy have significantly improved survival rates for patients diagnosed with previously untreatable forms of **cancer**.
   免疫疗法的进步显著提高了被诊断患有先前无法治疗的癌症患者的存活率。

2. The professor argued that systemic inequality functions as a **cancer** within society, slowly undermining social cohesion.
   那位教授认为，系统性不平等如同社会中的毒瘤，缓慢地破坏着社会凝聚力。

3. Early detection remains the most effective strategy in combating **cancer**, according to leading oncological researchers worldwide.
   根据全球顶尖肿瘤学研究人员的观点，早期发现仍然是对抗癌症最有效的策略。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cancer" as coming from Latin "cancer" meaning "crab" — like a crab's claws spreading outward, cancer cells spread uncontrollably through the body.
"Cancer"源自拉丁语"cancer"，意为"螃蟹"——就像螃蟹的爪子向外伸展，癌细胞不可控地在体内扩散。
