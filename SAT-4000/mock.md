# 🎭 mock

🔊 /mɒk/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb, adjective | 名词, 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To ridicule or laugh at with contempt. (adj.) Imitation; simulated; not genuine. (n.) A practice examination or trial
**中文：** v. 嘲笑；讥笑 adj. 仿制的；假装的；模拟的 n. 模拟考试

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ridicule（嘲笑）
- deride（嘲弄）
- scoff（嘲笑）
- taunt（讥讽）
- imitation（仿制的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mock someone / mock trial / mock exam / mock surprise / make a mockery of**

👉 Example: The students took a mock examination to prepare themselves for the actual final test next month.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The arrogant critic continued to **mock** the young artist's unconventional style, dismissing her innovative work as amateurish.
   这位傲慢的评论家继续嘲笑这位年轻艺术家的非传统风格，将她的创新作品贬为业余水平。

2. The law school organized a **mock** trial to give students practical courtroom experience before their professional internships.
   法学院组织了一场模拟审判，让学生在专业实习前获得实际的法庭经验。

3. She expressed **mock** astonishment at the announcement, though everyone could see she had anticipated the outcome all along.
   她对这一宣布表现出假装的惊讶，尽管每个人都能看出她早已预料到这个结果。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mock" as "making a copy to knock" — you make a fake copy (imitation) and use it to knock someone down (ridicule).
把"mock"想成"making a copy to knock"——你制作一个假的副本（仿制），并用它来打击别人（嘲笑）。
