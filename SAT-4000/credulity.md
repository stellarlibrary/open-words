# 😯 credulity

🔊 /krɪˈdjuːlɪti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A tendency to be too ready to believe that something is real or true; excessive willingness to accept things without proper evidence
**中文：** n. 轻信；易受骗

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gullibility（易受骗）
- naivety（天真）
- trustfulness（轻信）
- ingenuousness（坦率）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **strain credulity / test one's credulity / exploit someone's credulity / beyond credulity**

👉 Example: The con artist exploited the elderly victims' credulity to steal their life savings.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The outrageous claims in the advertisement strained **credulity**, yet thousands of consumers still purchased the fraudulent product.
   广告中离谱的声称令人难以置信，但仍有数千名消费者购买了这一欺诈产品。

2. Historians must guard against **credulity** when evaluating primary sources that may contain deliberate bias or exaggeration.
   历史学家在评估可能包含蓄意偏见或夸大的第一手资料时，必须警惕轻信。

3. The scheme's success depended entirely on the **credulity** of investors who failed to conduct proper due diligence.
   该骗局的成功完全依赖于未能进行适当尽职调查的投资者的轻信。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "credulity" as "cred-u-lity" — you give too much "credit" to everything you hear, making you gullible.
把"credulity"想成"cred-u-lity"——你对听到的一切给予过多"credit（信任）"，因此容易上当受骗。
