# 💬 quibble

🔊 /ˈkwɪbəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A minor objection or criticism; an evasion of the point. (v.) To argue about trivial matters; to find fault with petty details
**中文：** n. 谬论；双关语；遁辞；吹毛求疵的意见 v. 诡辩；挑剔

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cavil（吹毛求疵）
- nitpick（挑剔）
- objection（异议）
- complaint（抱怨）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **quibble about / quibble over / quibble with / minor quibble**

👉 Example: The editor had only a minor quibble with the manuscript before approving it for publication.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee members continued to **quibble** over insignificant details while the major budgetary issues remained unresolved.
   委员会成员们继续在无关紧要的细节上争论不休，而重大预算问题仍未解决。

2. Her only **quibble** with the otherwise excellent proposal concerned the timeline for implementation of the third phase.
   她对这份总体优秀的提案唯一的挑剔之处在于第三阶段的实施时间表。

3. Rather than addressing the substantive arguments, the critic chose to **quibble** about minor factual inaccuracies in the report.
   批评者没有回应实质性论点，而是选择对报告中的小事实错误吹毛求疵。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "quibble" as "quick babble" — someone who babbles quickly about trivial things is quibbling over unimportant details.
把"quibble"想成"quick babble"——一个人快速喋喋不休地说些琐事，就是在为不重要的细节吹毛求疵。
