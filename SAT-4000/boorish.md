# 🐗 boorish

🔊 /ˈbʊrɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Rough and bad-mannered; crude; lacking refinement or social grace; uncouth
**中文：** adj. 粗野的；粗鲁的；粗鄙的；笨拙的；乡土气的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- uncouth（粗鲁的）
- vulgar（粗俗的）
- crude（粗野的）
- loutish（粗暴的）
- uncivilized（不文明的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **boorish behavior / boorish manners / boorish remarks / boorish attitude**

👉 Example: His boorish behavior at the formal dinner embarrassed everyone seated at the table.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ambassador's **boorish** conduct at the diplomatic reception drew sharp criticism from foreign officials and journalists alike.
   大使在外交招待会上粗鲁的举止招致了外国官员和记者的严厉批评。

2. Despite his considerable intellect, his **boorish** manners prevented him from establishing meaningful professional relationships with his colleagues.
   尽管他才智过人，但粗鄙的举止使他无法与同事建立有意义的职业关系。

3. The committee condemned the senator's **boorish** remarks as wholly inappropriate for a public figure addressing a diverse audience.
   委员会谴责这位参议员粗鲁的言论，认为作为面对多元受众的公众人物，此类言辞完全不当。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "boor-ish" as behaving like a "boor" (a rude peasant) — someone with no manners, like a wild boar crashing through a china shop.
把"boorish"想成表现得像个"boor"（粗鲁的乡人）——一个毫无礼貌的人，就像一头野猪闯进瓷器店。
