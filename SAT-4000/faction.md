# ⚔️ faction

🔊 /ˈfækʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A small dissenting group within a larger organization, especially in politics; internal conflict or dissension within a group
**中文：** 派别；小集团；内讧；派系纷争

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- clique（小圈子）
- sect（派系）
- splinter group（分裂组织）
- bloc（集团）
- caucus（核心小组）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **political faction / rival faction / opposing faction / faction within the party**

👉 Example: A radical faction within the party threatened to derail the proposed legislation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. A powerful **faction** within the ruling party openly challenged the prime minister's authority on critical economic reform measures.
   执政党内一个强大的派系公开挑战首相在关键经济改革措施上的权威。

2. The rebel **faction** refused to participate in peace negotiations until their demands for regional autonomy were formally acknowledged.
   反叛派系拒绝参加和平谈判，直到他们关于地区自治的要求得到正式承认。

3. Historians attribute the empire's decline to persistent infighting among rival **factions** competing for control of the imperial court.
   历史学家将帝国的衰落归因于争夺朝廷控制权的敌对派系之间持续不断的内斗。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "faction" as "fraction" — a faction is a fraction or piece that breaks away from the larger whole, creating division.
把"faction"想成"fraction"(部分)——一个faction就是从整体中分裂出来的一小部分，制造分裂。
