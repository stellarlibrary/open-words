# 👣 misstep

🔊 /ˌmɪsˈstɛp/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A wrong step; a mistake or blunder in conduct. (v.) To step wrongly; to make an error in judgment
**中文：** n. 失足；过失；失策 v. 失足；走上歧途

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- blunder（失误）
- error（错误）
- gaffe（过失）
- stumble（绊倒）
- slip（失误）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **make a misstep / a costly misstep / a political misstep / one misstep away**

👉 Example: A single political misstep can permanently damage a candidate's reputation during an election campaign.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat's **misstep** during the press conference caused significant tension between the two allied nations.
   这位外交官在新闻发布会上的失言导致两个盟国之间关系严重紧张。

2. One careless **misstep** on the narrow mountain trail could result in a dangerous and potentially fatal fall.
   在狭窄的山间小径上一个不慎的失足可能导致危险甚至致命的坠落。

3. The executive acknowledged that the failed merger was a strategic **misstep** that cost the company millions in revenue.
   这位高管承认，失败的合并是一个战略失误，使公司损失了数百万收入。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mis-step" as literally a "missed step" — when you miss a step on the stairs, you stumble and make an error.
把"misstep"想成"missed step"——当你在楼梯上踩空一步时，你会绊倒，犯下一个错误。
