# 🦊 disingenuous

🔊 /ˌdɪsɪnˈdʒɛnjuəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Not candid or sincere, typically by pretending to know less or be less aware than one actually is; insincere
**中文：** adj. 虚伪的；不诚实的；不真诚的，无诚意的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- insincere（不真诚的）
- deceitful（欺骗的）
- duplicitous（口是心非的）
- dishonest（不诚实的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a disingenuous argument / disingenuous claim / it would be disingenuous to / somewhat disingenuous**

👉 Example: It would be disingenuous to claim that the policy change was motivated purely by concern for the public.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics argued that the corporation's public apology was entirely **disingenuous**, designed merely to appease shareholders rather than address harm.
   批评者认为该公司的公开道歉完全是虚伪的，只是为了安抚股东而非解决问题。

2. The senator's **disingenuous** portrayal of the legislation misled voters about its true implications for environmental regulation.
   这位参议员对该立法不真诚的描述误导了选民对其环境监管真正影响的理解。

3. It would be **disingenuous** to suggest that economic growth alone can resolve deeply entrenched systemic inequalities.
   声称仅靠经济增长就能解决根深蒂固的系统性不平等是不诚实的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-ingenuous" as the opposite of "ingenuous (genuine/innocent)" — someone who is disingenuous is the opposite of genuine, meaning they are fake and insincere.
把"disingenuous"想成"dis（相反）+ ingenuous（真诚的）"——与真诚相反，就是虚伪的、不真诚的。
