# 🎭 defraud

🔊 /dɪˈfrɔːd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To illegally obtain money or property from someone by deception; to cheat or swindle
**中文：** v. 欺骗，诈骗

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- swindle（诈骗）
- cheat（欺骗）
- deceive（蒙骗）
- embezzle（挪用）
- fleece（敲诈）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **defraud someone of something / defraud investors / defraud the government / intent to defraud**

👉 Example: The executive was charged with conspiring to defraud investors of millions of dollars.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The investment manager was convicted of scheming to **defraud** thousands of elderly clients of their retirement savings.
   这位投资经理因密谋诈骗数千名老年客户的退休储蓄而被定罪。

2. Federal prosecutors alleged that the corporation attempted to **defraud** the government through falsified tax documents.
   联邦检察官指控该公司试图通过伪造税务文件欺骗政府。

3. The online scam was designed to **defraud** unsuspecting consumers by offering counterfeit products at seemingly legitimate prices.
   这个网络骗局旨在通过以看似合理的价格提供假冒产品来欺骗毫无防备的消费者。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-fraud" as literally containing the word "fraud" — to defraud is to commit fraud against someone, taking what is theirs through deception.
把"defraud"想成包含"fraud（欺诈）"这个词——defraud就是对某人实施欺诈，通过欺骗手段夺取他们的东西。
