# 🎭 caprice

🔊 /kəˈpriːs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A sudden, unpredictable change of mind or behavior; a whimsical or fanciful notion; a lively musical composition
**中文：** n. 任性，反复无常；随想曲，怪想

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- whim（一时兴起）
- fancy（幻想）
- vagary（奇想）
- impulse（冲动）
- fickleness（善变）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a mere caprice / act on caprice / the caprice of fate / subject to caprice**

👉 Example: The director's decision seemed driven by caprice rather than any strategic rationale.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The monarch's **caprice** led to sudden policy reversals that bewildered even the most loyal advisors.
   君主的反复无常导致了突然的政策逆转，甚至让最忠诚的顾问都感到困惑。

2. Financial markets are often subject to the **caprice** of investor sentiment and unpredictable global events.
   金融市场往往受到投资者情绪的反复无常和不可预测的全球事件的影响。

3. She dismissed his romantic overtures as nothing more than a passing **caprice** unworthy of serious attention.
   她把他的殷勤示好不过看作一时兴起的念头，不值得认真对待。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "caprice" as "cap + rice" — imagine someone capriciously throwing a cap full of rice in the air on a whim.
把"caprice"想成"cap + rice"——想象一个人任性地把一帽子米抛向空中，完全出于一时兴起。
