# 🌿 quagmire

🔊 /ˈkwæɡmaɪər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A soft, wet area of ground that gives way underfoot; a difficult or precarious situation from which it is hard to escape
**中文：** n. 沼泽，沼泽地；无法脱身的困境

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- swamp（沼泽）
- morass（泥沼）
- predicament（困境）
- bog（沼地）
- dilemma（困境）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **political quagmire / legal quagmire / trapped in a quagmire / quagmire of debt**

👉 Example: The prolonged conflict became a quagmire from which neither side could easily withdraw.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The nation found itself mired in a diplomatic **quagmire** that threatened to destabilize the entire region for decades.
   这个国家发现自己陷入了一场外交泥潭，威胁着整个地区数十年的稳定。

2. Heavy rainfall transformed the construction site into a treacherous **quagmire** that halted all building operations indefinitely.
   暴雨将工地变成了危险的沼泽地，无限期地停止了所有建筑施工。

3. The company's executives recognized that their regulatory **quagmire** would require significant legal expertise to navigate successfully.
   公司高管意识到，要成功应对监管困境，需要大量的法律专业知识。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "quag-mire" — "quag" sounds like "quake" (shaking ground) and "mire" means mud, so it is shaky, muddy ground you cannot escape.
把"quagmire"想成"quag（像quake，摇晃的地面）+ mire（泥沼）"——摇晃的烂泥地，让人无法脱身。
