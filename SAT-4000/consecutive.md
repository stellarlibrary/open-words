# 🔢 consecutive

🔊 /kənˈsekjʊtɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Following continuously in unbroken or logical sequence; successive without interruption.
**中文：** adj. 连贯的；连续不断的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- successive（连续的）
- sequential（按顺序的）
- uninterrupted（不间断的）
- continuous（持续的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **consecutive days / consecutive years / consecutive wins / for the third consecutive time**

👉 Example: The team achieved victories in five consecutive matches, setting a new record for the league this season.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The company reported record profits for the fourth **consecutive** quarter, exceeding all analyst expectations for the fiscal year.
   该公司连续第四个季度报告了创纪录的利润，超出了分析师对本财年的所有预期。

2. After twelve **consecutive** hours of negotiations, the diplomats finally reached a preliminary agreement on the trade dispute.
   经过连续十二小时的谈判，外交官们终于就贸易争端达成了初步协议。

3. The researcher observed that the organism reproduced successfully for twenty **consecutive** generations under controlled laboratory conditions.
   研究人员观察到，该有机体在受控的实验室条件下连续二十代成功繁殖。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con-secut-ive" as "following (secut) together (con)" — consecutive events follow one after another without any gap in between.
把"consecutive"想成"一起(con)跟随(secut)"——连续的事件一个接一个地发生，中间没有间隔。
