# 📚 tutorship

🔊 /ˈtjuːtərʃɪp/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The office, function, or work of a tutor; the period during which one serves as a tutor
**中文：** n. 辅导；教师职位；导师身份

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- mentorship（导师关系）
- instruction（教学）
- teaching（教导）
- coaching（辅导）
- preceptorship（指导者职位）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **hold a tutorship / under one's tutorship / academic tutorship**

👉 Example: She accepted a tutorship at the university to support her doctoral studies.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. After earning his doctorate, he secured a **tutorship** at a prestigious university to continue his academic career.
   获得博士学位后，他在一所知名大学获得了教师职位以继续他的学术生涯。

2. The young scholar's progress improved remarkably during the two-year **tutorship** with a leading expert in the field.
   在与该领域顶尖专家进行两年辅导期间，这位年轻学者取得了显著进步。

3. The foundation offers a funded **tutorship** program designed to prepare talented students for competitive graduate admissions.
   该基金会提供一个资助性辅导项目，旨在帮助有才华的学生准备竞争激烈的研究生入学。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tutor-ship" as a "ship" captained by a "tutor" — a vessel of knowledge guiding students on their learning voyage.
把"tutorship"想成由"tutor（导师）"驾驶的"ship（船）"——一艘知识之船，引导学生踏上学习之旅。
