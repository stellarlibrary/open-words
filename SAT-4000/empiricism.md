# 🧪 empiricism

🔊 /ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The theory that all knowledge is derived from sensory experience rather than innate ideas or pure reason
**中文：** n. 经验主义；经验论

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pragmatism（实用主义）
- experimentation（实验法）
- positivism（实证主义）
- observation（观察法）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **philosophical empiricism / British empiricism / radical empiricism / grounded in empiricism**

👉 Example: The scientist's approach was firmly grounded in empiricism, relying on observable data rather than speculation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosophical tradition of **empiricism** holds that genuine knowledge must originate from direct sensory experience and observation.
   经验主义的哲学传统认为，真正的知识必须来源于直接的感官经验和观察。

2. John Locke's contribution to **empiricism** profoundly shaped modern epistemology and the development of scientific methodology.
   约翰·洛克对经验主义的贡献深刻地塑造了现代认识论和科学方法论的发展。

3. Critics of pure **empiricism** argue that certain mathematical and logical truths exist independently of sensory experience.
   纯粹经验主义的批评者认为，某些数学和逻辑真理独立于感官经验而存在。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "empiricism" as rooted in "empirical" — what you can experience and test with your senses is what counts as real knowledge.
把"empiricism"看作源于"empirical"——你能用感官体验和检验的东西才算真正的知识。
