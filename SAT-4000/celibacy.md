# 🕊️ celibacy

🔊 /ˈselɪbəsi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The state of abstaining from marriage and sexual relations, typically for religious reasons; the condition of being unmarried
**中文：** n. 独身，独身生活；禁欲

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- abstinence（禁欲）
- chastity（贞洁）
- continence（节制）
- singlehood（单身状态）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vow of celibacy / practice celibacy / lifelong celibacy / enforced celibacy**

👉 Example: Members of the religious order take a vow of celibacy upon entering the monastery.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The monastic tradition requires a strict vow of **celibacy**, which candidates must willingly accept before being admitted to the order.
   修道传统要求严格的独身誓言，候选人在被接纳入会之前必须自愿接受。

2. Scholars have long debated whether mandatory **celibacy** among clergy contributes to or detracts from their spiritual effectiveness and moral authority.
   学者们长期以来一直在争论神职人员的强制独身是促进还是削弱了他们的精神效力和道德权威。

3. In certain ancient cultures, **celibacy** was regarded as a pathway to heightened spiritual awareness and deeper philosophical understanding.
   在某些古代文化中，独身被视为通往更高精神觉悟和更深哲学理解的途径。

Think of "celibacy" as "cell-i-bacy" — like living alone in a "cell" (small room), choosing a solitary life of privacy and restraint.
把"celibacy"想成"cell-i-bacy"——就像独自住在一间"cell（小房间）"里，选择孤独、隐私和自我约束的生活。
