# 👁️ witness

🔊 /ˈwɪtnəs/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who sees an event; one who gives testimony; evidence or proof. (v.) To observe an event firsthand; to testify or give evidence
**中文：** n. 目击者，证人；证据 v. 目击，目睹；作证，证明

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- observer（观察者）
- spectator（旁观者）
- testify（作证）
- attest（证明）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **witness an event / bear witness to / witness a signature / eyewitness / call as a witness**

👉 Example: Several bystanders were called upon to witness the signing of the historic international agreement.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist was among the few foreign correspondents to **witness** the peaceful transfer of power in the newly formed nation.
   这位记者是少数亲眼目睹这个新成立国家和平权力交接的外国记者之一。

2. Archaeological discoveries in the region bear **witness** to a sophisticated civilization that flourished over three thousand years ago.
   该地区的考古发现见证了一个三千多年前繁荣的先进文明。

3. The prosecution's key **witness** provided compelling testimony that proved decisive in securing the unanimous guilty verdict.
   控方的关键证人提供了令人信服的证词，对获得一致有罪裁决起了决定性作用。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "wit-ness" as having the "wit to know" — a witness has the knowledge of what happened because they saw it with their own eyes.
把"witness"想成"wit（知道）+ -ness（状态）"——知情的状态，因为亲眼所见所以知道发生了什么。
