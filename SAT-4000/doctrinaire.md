# 📕 doctrinaire

🔊 /ˌdɑːktrɪˈnɛr/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Seeking to impose a doctrine rigidly without regard for practical considerations. (n.) A person who dogmatically applies theory without flexibility
**中文：** adj. 教条主义的；空谈理论的 n. 教条主义者；空谈家

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dogmatic（教条的）
- rigid（僵化的）
- inflexible（不灵活的）
- ideological（意识形态的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **doctrinaire approach / doctrinaire views / doctrinaire ideology / doctrinaire adherence**

👉 Example: The senator's doctrinaire stance on fiscal policy left little room for the pragmatic compromises needed to pass legislation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The administration's **doctrinaire** approach to economic reform ignored the practical realities faced by small business owners across the region.
   政府对经济改革的教条主义做法忽视了该地区小企业主面临的实际情况。

2. Critics dismissed the professor as a **doctrinaire** who clung stubbornly to outdated theories despite overwhelming contrary empirical evidence.
   批评者认为这位教授是一个教条主义者，尽管有大量相反的实证证据，他仍固执地坚持过时的理论。

3. A **doctrinaire** insistence on ideological purity often prevents political parties from building the broad coalitions necessary for governance.
   对意识形态纯洁性的教条主义坚持往往阻碍政党建立治理所必需的广泛联盟。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "doctrinaire" as "doctrine + air" — someone full of hot air about their doctrine, rigidly preaching theory without practical sense.
把"doctrinaire"想成"doctrine(教条)+aire(空气)"——一个满口教条的人，只会僵化地宣讲理论而不顾实际。
