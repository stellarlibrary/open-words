# 🗣️ vocative

🔊 /ˈvɑːkətɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or denoting a grammatical case used in addressing a person or thing. (n.) The vocative case or a word in the vocative case
**中文：** adj. 呼唤的，呼格的 n. 呼格

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- appellative（称呼的）
- addressing（称呼的）
- invocatory（祈求的）
- salutatory（致辞的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vocative case / vocative form / vocative comma / vocative particle / vocative expression**

👉 Example: In the sentence "Friends, Romans, countrymen," each noun functions as a vocative address.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Latin students must memorize the **vocative** endings for each declension in order to properly address persons in classical compositions.
   拉丁语学生必须记住每种变格的呼格词尾，才能在经典作文中正确称呼他人。

2. The **vocative** comma is an essential punctuation mark that separates a direct address from the remainder of the sentence.
   呼格逗号是一个必不可少的标点符号，用于将直接称呼与句子的其余部分分隔开。

3. Linguists studying ancient Greek texts pay close attention to **vocative** constructions as indicators of social hierarchy and register.
   研究古希腊文本的语言学家密切关注呼格结构，将其作为社会等级和语域的指标。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vocative" as "voice + ative" — it is the grammatical case you use when raising your voice to call someone directly.
把"vocative"想成"voice（声音）+ ative"——这是你在提高嗓门直接呼唤某人时使用的语法格。
