# 💸 prodigal

🔊 /ˈprɒdɪɡəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Spending money or resources freely and recklessly; wasteful; lavishly generous. (n.) A person who spends money in a recklessly extravagant way
**中文：** adj. 挥霍的；十分慷慨的；浪费的 n. 浪子；挥霍者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- extravagant（奢侈的）
- wasteful（浪费的）
- lavish（挥霍的）
- spendthrift（挥霍者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prodigal son / prodigal spending / prodigal lifestyle / prodigal with money**

👉 Example: The prodigal heir squandered his family fortune within just a few years of inheriting it.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **prodigal** spending habits of the young executive eventually led to the company's severe financial difficulties.
   这位年轻高管挥霍的消费习惯最终导致了公司严重的财务困难。

2. Like the biblical **prodigal** son, he returned home humbled after years of reckless living and poor decisions.
   像圣经中的浪子一样，他在多年挥霍放纵和错误决定之后谦卑地回到了家。

3. The government was criticized for its **prodigal** allocation of public funds on unnecessary infrastructure projects.
   政府因在不必要的基础设施项目上挥霍公共资金而受到批评。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "prodigal" as related to "prodigy gone wrong" — a prodigy has extraordinary gifts, but a prodigal wastes them extravagantly.
把"prodigal"和"prodigy（天才）"联系起来——天才有非凡的天赋，而浪子则把一切挥霍殆尽。
