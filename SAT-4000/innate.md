# 💒 innate

🔊 /ɪˈneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Inborn; existing from birth rather than acquired; belonging to the essential nature of something
**中文：** adj. 先天的；固有的；与生俱来的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- inborn（天生的）
- inherent（固有的）
- intrinsic（内在的）
- natural（自然的）
- congenital（先天的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **innate ability / innate talent / innate sense of / innate capacity / innate tendency**

👉 Example: Many linguists argue that humans possess an innate capacity for language acquisition.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher argued that human beings possess an **innate** sense of fairness that manifests even in early childhood development.
   研究人员认为，人类拥有一种先天的公平感，这种公平感在儿童早期发展中就已显现。

2. Her **innate** musical talent became evident when she began composing original melodies at the remarkably young age of four.
   她与生俱来的音乐天赋在她年仅四岁就开始创作原创旋律时便显而易见。

3. Philosophers have long debated whether moral principles are **innate** or gradually acquired through socialization and cultural experience.
   哲学家们长期以来一直在争论道德原则是先天的，还是通过社会化和文化体验逐渐获得的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-nate" as "in nature" — something innate is already in your nature from birth, not learned or taught.
把"innate"想成"in nature（在本性中）"——先天的东西从出生起就在你的本性中，不是后天学习或教导的。
