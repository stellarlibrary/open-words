# 🔌 breakdown

🔊 /ˈbreɪkdaʊn/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A mechanical failure; a collapse of mental or physical health; a detailed analysis or classification of something into components; the process of decomposition
**中文：** n. 故障；崩溃；分解；分类；击穿；衰弱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- failure（故障）
- collapse（崩溃）
- malfunction（失灵）
- analysis（分析）
- decomposition（分解）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **nervous breakdown / mechanical breakdown / breakdown of communication / a detailed breakdown**

👉 Example: The report included a breakdown of expenditures by department for the fiscal year.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The unexpected **breakdown** of the central cooling system forced the factory to suspend all production operations for three days.
   中央冷却系统的意外故障迫使工厂暂停了所有生产运营三天。

2. The analyst provided a comprehensive **breakdown** of quarterly revenue figures, categorizing each source of income for the board.
   分析师提供了季度收入数据的全面分类，为董事会逐一归类了每个收入来源。

3. Prolonged exposure to extreme workplace stress ultimately led to her complete emotional **breakdown** and subsequent hospitalization for recovery.
   长期暴露在极端的工作压力下最终导致了她的情绪完全崩溃，随后住院接受康复治疗。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "breakdown" literally — when something "breaks down," it stops working or falls apart into smaller pieces.
把"breakdown"直接拆开理解——当某物"breaks down"时，它停止运转或分解成更小的部分。
