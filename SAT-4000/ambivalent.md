# ⚖️ ambivalent

🔊 /æmˈbɪvələnt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Having mixed or contradictory feelings about something or someone; uncertain or unable to decide
**中文：** 矛盾的；好恶相克的；举棋不定的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conflicted（内心矛盾的）
- uncertain（不确定的）
- indecisive（犹豫不决的）
- torn（左右为难的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ambivalent about / ambivalent toward / feel ambivalent / remain ambivalent**

👉 Example: Many voters felt ambivalent about the candidate, admiring her policies but questioning her experience.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee members were deeply **ambivalent** about the merger, recognizing both its potential benefits and significant risks.
   委员会成员对此次合并深感矛盾，既认识到其潜在好处，也意识到重大风险。

2. She remained **ambivalent** toward the job offer, torn between the higher salary and the longer commute it required.
   她对这份工作机会仍感到矛盾，在更高的薪水和更长的通勤时间之间左右为难。

3. Public opinion was largely **ambivalent** regarding the new policy, with surveys revealing an almost even split in support.
   公众对这项新政策基本上持矛盾态度，调查显示支持率几乎平分。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ambi-" (both) + "valent" (value) — you value BOTH sides equally, so you cannot decide which way to go.
把"ambivalent"拆成"ambi-"（两个）+ "valent"（价值）——你对两边都同样看重，所以无法决定走哪条路。
