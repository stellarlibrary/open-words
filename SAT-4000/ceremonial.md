# 🎭 ceremonial

🔊 /ˌserəˈmoʊniəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or used in formal ceremonies or rituals. (n.) A set of formal acts or procedures prescribed for a ceremony
**中文：** adj. 仪式的；礼仪的 n. 仪式；礼节

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ritual（仪式的）
- formal（正式的）
- solemn（庄严的）
- liturgical（礼拜仪式的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ceremonial occasion / ceremonial dress / ceremonial duties / ceremonial role**

👉 Example: The ambassador wore ceremonial dress for the state dinner hosted by the foreign minister.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The head of state performed **ceremonial** duties at the inauguration, symbolizing the peaceful transfer of democratic power.
   国家元首在就职典礼上履行了仪式性职责，象征着民主权力的和平交接。

2. Ancient civilizations used **ceremonial** objects crafted from precious metals and stones to honor their deities during sacred festivals.
   古代文明使用由贵金属和宝石制成的礼仪器物在神圣节日期间祭祀神灵。

3. The university's **ceremonial** proceedings included academic processions, formal addresses, and the conferring of honorary degrees.
   大学的典礼仪式包括学术游行、正式致辞和授予荣誉学位。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ceremonial" as "ceremony + -al" — simply the adjective form of "ceremony," describing anything connected to formal rites and rituals.
把"ceremonial"想成"ceremony（仪式）+ -al"——就是"ceremony"的形容词形式，描述与正式典礼和仪式有关的一切。
