# 🗑️ clutter

🔊 /ˈklʌtər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A collection of things lying about in an untidy state; disorder and confusion. (v.) To fill or cover with scattered or disordered things that impede movement or reduce effectiveness
**中文：** n. 杂乱；混乱；喧嚣 v. 使凌乱；使混乱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disorder（混乱）
- mess（杂乱）
- jumble（杂乱的一堆）
- disarray（混乱）
- litter（杂物）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **clutter up / reduce clutter / clear the clutter / cluttered with / mental clutter**

👉 Example: The researcher's desk was cluttered with stacks of journals, manuscript drafts, and reference books.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The accumulation of unnecessary **clutter** in the laboratory significantly impeded the researchers' ability to work efficiently.
   实验室中不必要杂物的堆积严重妨碍了研究人员高效工作的能力。

2. Digital notifications constantly **clutter** our attention, making sustained concentration on complex tasks increasingly difficult.
   数字通知不断占据我们的注意力，使得对复杂任务的持续专注变得越来越困难。

3. The committee recommended policies to **clutter** bureaucratic procedures less and streamline administrative operations instead.
   委员会建议制定政策以减少官僚程序的繁杂，转而精简行政运作。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "clutter" as "cluster of litter" — when litter clusters together, it creates clutter, a messy and disorganized heap.
把"clutter"想成"cluster of litter（一堆垃圾）"——当垃圾聚集在一起时，就形成了clutter，一堆杂乱无章的东西。
