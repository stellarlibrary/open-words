# 🩹 stanch

🔊 /stɔːntʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To stop the flow of blood or other liquid; to put an end to something. (adj.) Strong and loyal; watertight; firm in one's principles
**中文：** v. 止（血）；平息 adj. 坚固的；忠实的；防水的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- stem（止住）
- halt（停止）
- check（阻止）
- staunch（忠诚的）
- curb（遏制）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **stanch the bleeding / stanch the flow / stanch the wound / stanch the tide**

👉 Example: The medic applied pressure to stanch the bleeding from the soldier's wound.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The paramedic used a sterile bandage to **stanch** the flow of blood from the deep laceration on his arm.
   急救人员用无菌绷带止住了他手臂深处伤口的出血。

2. The central bank introduced emergency measures to **stanch** the rapid outflow of foreign capital from the economy.
   中央银行出台紧急措施以遏制外国资本从经济体中的快速外流。

3. Despite their efforts, the engineers could not **stanch** the leak that threatened to flood the lower compartments.
   尽管做出了努力，工程师们仍无法止住威胁淹没下层舱室的泄漏。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "stanch" as "stand-unch" — you stand firm and unmoving to stop the flow, just as you press firmly on a wound to stanch the bleeding.
把"stanch"想成"stand-unch"——你坚定不移地站住来阻止流动，就像你用力按住伤口止血一样。
