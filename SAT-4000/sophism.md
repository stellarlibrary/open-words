# 🎭 sophism

🔊 /ˈsɑːfɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A clever but false argument intended to deceive; a piece of deceptive reasoning
**中文：** n. 诡辩；谬论

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fallacy（谬论）
- casuistry（诡辩术）
- specious reasoning（似是而非的推理）
- deception（欺骗）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **expose a sophism / resort to sophism / mere sophism / intellectual sophism**

👉 Example: The philosopher warned his students to recognize sophism disguised as legitimate logical reasoning.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The defense attorney's argument was dismissed by the judge as mere **sophism** lacking any genuine evidentiary or legal foundation.
   辩护律师的论点被法官驳回，认为不过是缺乏任何真正证据或法律依据的诡辩。

2. Critical thinking courses teach students to identify **sophism** and distinguish superficially persuasive claims from truly valid arguments.
   批判性思维课程教授学生识别诡辩，并将表面上有说服力的主张与真正有效的论证区分开来。

3. The editorial accused the politician of relying on **sophism** rather than substantive evidence to justify the controversial new policy.
   这篇社论指责该政客依靠诡辩而非实质性证据来为这项有争议的新政策辩护。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of the ancient Greek "Sophists" who were known for clever but misleading arguments — "sophism" is named after their deceptive rhetorical style.
想想古希腊的"智者派"（Sophists），他们以巧妙但具有误导性的论证而闻名——"sophism"正是以他们诡辩的修辞风格命名的。
