# 🔔 resonance

🔊 /ˈrɛzənəns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The quality of being deep, full, and reverberating; the power to evoke lasting images, memories, or emotions; vibration produced in response to an external stimulus
**中文：** n. 共鸣，回声，反响；共振

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reverberation（回响）
- echo（回声）
- vibration（振动）
- significance（意义）
- impact（影响）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **emotional resonance / cultural resonance / have resonance with / magnetic resonance / resonance of a message**

👉 Example: The documentary had deep emotional resonance with audiences who had experienced similar hardships.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The poet's words carried a profound **resonance** that transcended cultural boundaries and spoke to universal experiences of loss and hope.
   这位诗人的文字承载着深刻的共鸣，超越了文化界限，诉说着关于失去与希望的普遍经历。

2. Physicists used magnetic **resonance** imaging technology to examine the internal structure of the specimen without causing any physical damage.
   物理学家利用磁共振成像技术检查了标本的内部结构，且未造成任何物理损伤。

3. The historical novel achieved particular **resonance** among younger readers who recognized parallels between the story and contemporary social issues.
   这部历史小说在年轻读者中引起了特别的反响，他们发现了故事与当代社会问题之间的相似之处。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "resonance" as "reason-ance" — when something resonates, it gives you a reason to feel moved, like a bell that keeps ringing.
把"resonance"想成"reason-ance"——当某事物引起共鸣时，它给了你感动的理由，就像一口钟持续鸣响。
