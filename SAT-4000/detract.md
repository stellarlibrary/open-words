# 🔀 detract

🔊 /dɪˈtrækt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To take away from the value, quality, or importance of something; to divert or distract attention
**中文：** v. 减损；贬低；转移（注意力），使分心

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- diminish（减少）
- undermine（削弱）
- divert（转移）
- distract（使分心）
- belittle（贬低）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **detract from something / detract attention / detract from the value**

👉 Example: The minor errors in the report did not detract from its overall significance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The constant noise from the construction site greatly **detracted** from the students' ability to concentrate during lectures.
   建筑工地持续不断的噪音极大地分散了学生在课堂上集中注意力的能力。

2. Critics argued that the proposed amendments would **detract** from the original intent and integrity of the legislation.
   批评者认为拟议的修正案会减损该立法的原始意图和完整性。

3. The author's excessive use of jargon **detracted** from the clarity and accessibility of the academic paper.
   作者过度使用术语减损了该学术论文的清晰度和可读性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-tract" as "pulling away from a track" — when you detract, you pull attention or value away from the main path.
把"detract"想成"de(离开) + tract(轨道)"——当你detract时，你把注意力或价值从主要轨道上拉走了。
