# ⚖️ castigate

🔊 /ˈkæstɪɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To reprimand or criticize severely; to punish harshly in order to correct
**中文：** v. 严惩；苛评；严厉批评；矫正

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rebuke（斥责）
- chastise（惩罚）
- reprimand（训斥）
- berate（痛斥）
- censure（谴责）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **castigate someone for something / publicly castigate / severely castigate**

👉 Example: The editorial castigated the administration for its failure to address the environmental crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator **castigated** his colleagues for their unwillingness to support the long-overdue reform legislation.
   这位参议员严厉批评了他的同事们不愿支持早该推行的改革立法。

2. Literary critics **castigated** the novel for its superficial treatment of complex historical events and cultural issues.
   文学评论家苛评了这部小说对复杂历史事件和文化问题的肤浅处理。

3. The headmaster **castigated** the students who had violated the code of conduct during the official ceremony.
   校长严厉惩罚了在正式典礼期间违反行为准则的学生。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "casti-gate" as a "gate of casting out" — when you castigate someone, you cast harsh words at them through the gate of criticism.
把"castigate"想成"驱逐之门"（casti-gate）——当你castigate某人时，你通过批评之门向他们投掷严厉的话语。
