# 🌟 distinguished

🔊 /dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Successful, authoritative, and commanding great respect; dignified and noble in appearance or manner
**中文：** adj. 著名的；卓越的；杰出的；高贵的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- eminent（杰出的）
- renowned（著名的）
- illustrious（显赫的）
- prestigious（有声望的）
- notable（知名的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **distinguished scholar / distinguished career / distinguished service / distinguished guest / distinguished appearance**

👉 Example: The distinguished professor was invited to deliver the keynote address at the international conference.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **distinguished** scientist received the Nobel Prize for her groundbreaking contributions to the field of molecular genetics.
   这位杰出的科学家因其在分子遗传学领域的开创性贡献而获得诺贝尔奖。

2. After a long and **distinguished** career in public service, the ambassador retired to write her memoirs and lecture at universities.
   在漫长而卓越的公共服务生涯之后，这位大使退休后撰写回忆录并在大学讲学。

3. The panel of **distinguished** experts convened to evaluate the feasibility of the proposed infrastructure development project.
   一个由杰出专家组成的小组召集会议，评估拟议基础设施开发项目的可行性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "distinguish-ed" as "one who has been distinguished" — someone who stands out so remarkably that they have been set apart from others.
把"distinguished"想成"被distinguish（区分）出来的人"——某人如此出众，以至于从人群中脱颖而出。
