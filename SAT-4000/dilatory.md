# 🐌 dilatory

🔊 /ˈdɪləˌtɔːri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Tending to delay or procrastinate; slow to act; intended to cause delay
**中文：** adj. 拖拉的；缓慢的；旨在拖延的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- tardy（迟缓的）
- sluggish（懈怠的）
- procrastinating（拖延的）
- dawdling（磨蹭的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dilatory tactics / dilatory behavior / dilatory response / dilatory approach**

👉 Example: The defense attorney employed dilatory tactics to postpone the trial as long as possible.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The judge reprimanded the attorney for using **dilatory** tactics designed to delay the proceedings and exhaust the plaintiff's resources.
   法官斥责了律师使用拖延战术，旨在推迟诉讼程序并耗尽原告的资源。

2. His **dilatory** approach to completing assignments consistently resulted in missed deadlines and diminished academic performance throughout the semester.
   他拖拉的作业完成方式导致整个学期不断错过截止日期，学业成绩也随之下降。

3. The committee criticized the government's **dilatory** response to the environmental crisis, arguing that immediate action was urgently required.
   委员会批评了政府对环境危机的缓慢回应，认为迫切需要立即采取行动。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dilatory" as "delay-tory" — someone with a dilatory nature is always in "delay" territory, putting things off.
把"dilatory"想成"delay-tory"——具有拖拉性格的人总是在"delay"（拖延）的领地里，不断推迟事情。
