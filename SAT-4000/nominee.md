# 🏅 nominee

🔊 /ˌnɒmɪˈniː/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A person who has been formally proposed or named as a candidate for a position, office, honor, or award
**中文：** n. 被提名的人；被任命者；代名人

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- candidate（候选人）
- contender（竞争者）
- appointee（被任命者）
- designee（被指定者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **presidential nominee / award nominee / nominee for / the leading nominee**

👉 Example: All nominees for the scholarship must submit their applications before the end of this month.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **nominee** for the Supreme Court position underwent weeks of rigorous questioning during the Senate confirmation hearings.
   最高法院职位的被提名人在参议院确认听证会上接受了数周的严格质询。

2. Each **nominee** at the ceremony delivered a brief acceptance speech thanking colleagues who supported their groundbreaking research.
   典礼上每位被提名者都发表了简短的感言，感谢支持他们开创性研究的同事。

3. The party's **nominee** pledged to prioritize education reform and environmental sustainability throughout the upcoming campaign season.
   该党的提名人承诺在即将到来的竞选季中优先推进教育改革和环境可持续发展。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "nomin-ee" — "nomin" means name, and "-ee" is the person who receives the action, so a nominee is the person who gets named.
把"nominee"拆成"nomin（命名）+ ee（被动者）"——nominee就是"被点名的人"。
