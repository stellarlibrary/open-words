# 📊 comparative

🔊 /kəmˈpærətɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Measured or judged by comparison; relative rather than absolute; involving systematic comparison. (n.) The comparative degree of an adjective or adverb
**中文：** adj. 比较的；相当的；相对的 n. 比较级

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- relative（相对的）
- proportional（成比例的）
- approximate（近似的）
- analogous（类似的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **comparative study / comparative analysis / comparative advantage / comparative literature**

👉 Example: The professor assigned a comparative analysis of two contrasting political systems.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The university offers a distinguished program in **comparative** literature that examines texts across multiple cultural traditions.
   该大学开设了一个杰出的比较文学项目，研究跨越多种文化传统的文本。

2. After years of conflict the region now enjoys **comparative** peace, though underlying tensions have not fully dissolved.
   经过多年冲突，该地区现在享有相对的和平，尽管潜在的紧张局势尚未完全消除。

3. Economists often cite **comparative** advantage as the fundamental principle governing international trade between nations.
   经济学家经常引用比较优势作为国际贸易的基本原则。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "comparative" as "compare + ative" — relating to comparison, like a comparative study that puts two things side by side to find differences.
把"comparative"想成"compare（比较）+ ative（与…相关的）"——与比较有关的，就像比较研究将两个事物并排放置以发现差异。
