# 🔥 audacity

🔊 /ɔːˈdæsɪti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** The willingness to take bold risks; impudent boldness or daring, often implying a shocking lack of respect or shame.

**中文：** 大胆；厚颜无耻

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- boldness（大胆）
- nerve（胆量）
- impudence（厚颜无耻）
- temerity（鲁莽）
- effrontery（放肆）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Audacity" often appears in the phrase "have the audacity to (do something)," implying that someone has done something shockingly bold or disrespectful. It leans more negative than "audacious" in everyday usage.

"audacity" 常用于短语 "have the audacity to（竟然敢……）"，暗示某人做了令人震惊的大胆或无礼之事。在日常用法中，其贬义色彩比 "audacious" 更浓。

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The intern had the **audacity** to challenge the senior partner's strategy in public.
   那位实习生竟然**厚颜无耻地**在公开场合质疑高级合伙人的策略。

2. Her **audacity** in negotiating a higher salary impressed the entire hiring committee.
   她在薪资谈判中的**大胆**给整个招聘委员会留下了深刻印象。

3. Critics admired the artist's creative **audacity** in blending classical and modern styles.
   评论家们赞赏这位艺术家将古典与现代风格融合的创作**胆识**。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Audacity" shares the root "audac-" with "audacious." Picture someone on a stage with a microphone (audio) saying something so bold that the audience gasps — that is pure audacity.

"audacity" 与 "audacious" 共享词根 "audac-"。想象一个人拿着麦克风（audio）在台上说了一句大胆到令全场倒吸一口气的话——这就是 audacity。
