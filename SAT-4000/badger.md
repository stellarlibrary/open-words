# 🦡 badger

🔊 /ˈbædʒər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词，动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** (n.) A sturdy burrowing mammal with a black-and-white striped face; (v.) to repeatedly and annoyingly ask someone to do something; to pester or harass.

**中文：** （名词）獾；（动词）纠缠不休；烦扰。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- pester（纠缠）
- harass（骚扰）
- hound（追逼）
- nag（唠叨）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

As a verb, "badger" implies persistent, annoying pressure. It is often followed by "into" (badger someone into doing something) or "about" (badger someone about a topic). The verb usage derives from the old cruel sport of badger-baiting.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The relentless journalist continued to **badger** the politician with probing questions about the financial scandal.
   （那位执着的记者继续就财务丑闻用尖锐的问题**纠缠**那位政治家。）

2. Children often **badger** their parents into buying toys by repeating their requests with tireless determination.
   （孩子们经常以不知疲倦的决心反复提出要求，**缠着**父母给他们买玩具。）

3. The nocturnal **badger** emerged from its underground burrow at dusk to forage for food across the meadow.
   （夜行性的**獾**在黄昏时分从地下洞穴中出来，在草地上觅食。）

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Picture a badger (the animal) stubbornly digging and digging at the earth — that same stubborn, relentless persistence is what it means to "badger" someone with constant requests.

想象一只獾（动物）固执地不停挖土——那种顽固、不懈的坚持就是"badger"某人（不断纠缠）的意思。
