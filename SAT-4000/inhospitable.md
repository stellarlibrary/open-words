# 🏜️ inhospitable

🔊 /ˌɪnhɒˈspɪtəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Unwelcoming or unfriendly toward visitors; providing no shelter or sustenance; harsh and difficult to live in
**中文：** adj. 荒凉的；冷淡的，不好客的；不适居留的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unwelcoming（不友好的）
- barren（荒芜的）
- desolate（荒凉的）
- hostile（不利的）
- forbidding（令人生畏的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inhospitable terrain / inhospitable climate / inhospitable environment / inhospitable to life**

👉 Example: The Arctic tundra remains one of the most inhospitable environments on Earth.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Early explorers found the desert landscape utterly **inhospitable**, with no reliable sources of water or shelter.
   早期探险者发现沙漠地貌极其荒凉，没有可靠的水源或庇护所。

2. The committee described the regulatory framework as **inhospitable** to innovation and foreign investment in the sector.
   委员会称该监管框架不利于该行业的创新和外国投资。

3. Despite the **inhospitable** conditions of deep ocean trenches, scientists have discovered thriving ecosystems near hydrothermal vents.
   尽管深海海沟条件极其恶劣，科学家们仍在热液喷口附近发现了繁荣的生态系统。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-hospitable" as "not hospitable" — the prefix "in-" negates "hospitable," meaning a place or person that offers no hospitality.
把"inhospitable"想成"in（不）+ hospitable（好客的）"——前缀"in-"否定了"hospitable"，意思是不好客的或不宜居住的。
