# 🗣️ squabble

🔊 /ˈskwɒbl/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To quarrel noisily over a trivial matter; to engage in a petty argument. (n.) A noisy quarrel or dispute about something unimportant
**中文：** v. 争论

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- quarrel（争吵）
- bicker（拌嘴）
- wrangle（争辩）
- dispute（争论）
- spat（口角）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **squabble over / squabble about / petty squabble / family squabble**

👉 Example: The siblings would often squabble over trivial matters such as television remote control.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee members continued to **squabble** over minor procedural details while the critical deadline rapidly approached.
   委员会成员继续为次要的程序细节争吵不休，而关键的截止日期正在迅速逼近。

2. Political analysts noted that the prolonged **squabble** between the two factions had severely weakened the party's credibility.
   政治分析人士指出，两个派系之间旷日持久的争吵严重削弱了该党的信誉。

3. The neighbors began to **squabble** about the property boundary, escalating what had been a perfectly amicable relationship.
   邻居们开始为财产边界争吵，使原本完全友好的关系急剧恶化。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "squabble" as "squawk-babble" — people squawking and babbling at each other in a noisy, petty argument.
把"squabble"想成"squawk-babble"——人们像鸟一样嘎嘎叫着互相唠叨，进行一场吵闹而琐碎的争吵。
