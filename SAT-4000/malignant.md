# 💀 malignant

🔊 /məˈlɪɡnənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (Medical) Tending to invade normal tissue or to recur after removal; cancerous. (General) Disposed to cause harm, suffering, or distress; deeply malevolent
**中文：** adj. 恶性的；致命的；有害的；恶意的；恶毒的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cancerous（癌性的）
- virulent（剧毒的）
- pernicious（有害的）
- lethal（致命的）
- malevolent（恶毒的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **malignant tumor / malignant growth / malignant influence / malignant intent**

👉 Example: The doctor confirmed that the growth was malignant and required immediate treatment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Pathology results confirmed that the tissue sample was **malignant**, prompting the medical team to schedule surgery without delay.
   病理结果证实组织样本为恶性，促使医疗团队立即安排手术。

2. The senator described corruption within the institution as a **malignant** force that threatened the foundations of democratic governance.
   这位参议员将该机构内部的腐败描述为一种恶性力量，威胁着民主治理的基础。

3. If left untreated, **malignant** cells can metastasize rapidly and compromise the function of vital organs throughout the body.
   如果不加以治疗，恶性细胞可以迅速转移并损害全身重要器官的功能。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "malignant" as "mal" (bad) + "ignant" (like ignite) — something bad that ignites and spreads, just like a cancerous tumor or a harmful influence.
把"malignant"想成"mal"（坏的）+"ignant"（像ignite点燃）——坏的东西被点燃并蔓延，就像癌性肿瘤或有害影响一样。
