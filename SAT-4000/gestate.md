# 🤰 gestate

🔊 /ˈdʒɛsteɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To carry offspring in the womb during pregnancy; to develop slowly in the mind over a period of time
**中文：** v. 孕育；怀孕；酝酿（思想或计划）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- incubate（孵化）
- develop（发展）
- conceive（构想）
- nurture（培育）
- brew（酝酿）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **gestate an idea / gestate for months / gestate a plan / the project gestated**

👉 Example: The novel gestated in the author's mind for nearly a decade before she began writing.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ambitious policy proposal had **gestated** within the think tank for several years before being formally presented.
   这项雄心勃勃的政策提案在智库内酝酿了数年之后才被正式提出。

2. Elephants **gestate** their offspring for approximately twenty-two months, the longest period among all land mammals.
   大象孕育后代的时间约为二十二个月，是所有陆地哺乳动物中最长的。

3. The revolutionary theory continued to **gestate** quietly in academic circles before transforming the entire scientific discipline.
   这一革命性理论在学术界悄然酝酿，随后彻底改变了整个科学学科。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gestate" as related to "gestation" — like a baby gestating in the womb, ideas also need time to grow before birth.
把"gestate"和"gestation"（妊娠期）联系起来——就像婴儿在子宫中孕育一样，想法也需要时间来成长。
