# ⏸️ moratorium

🔊 /ˌmɔːrəˈtɔːriəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A temporary prohibition or suspension of an activity; a legally authorized period of delay in the performance of an obligation
**中文：** n. 暂停；中止；延期偿付

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- suspension（暂停）
- halt（中止）
- freeze（冻结）
- postponement（延期）
- ban（禁令）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **impose a moratorium / declare a moratorium / a moratorium on / lift a moratorium**

👉 Example: The government imposed a moratorium on new construction projects near the protected wetlands.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The international community called for an immediate **moratorium** on nuclear weapons testing to promote global peace and stability.
   国际社会呼吁立即暂停核武器试验，以促进全球和平与稳定。

2. A five-year **moratorium** on commercial fishing in the region was enacted to allow the depleted fish populations to recover.
   该地区颁布了为期五年的商业捕鱼禁令，以使枯竭的鱼类种群得以恢复。

3. The university declared a **moratorium** on tuition increases while a committee reviewed the institution's long-term financial strategy.
   大学宣布暂停上调学费，同时由一个委员会审查该机构的长期财务战略。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "moratorium" as containing "mora" (delay in Latin) — a moratorium is an official delay or pause on something.
"moratorium"中包含拉丁语"mora"（延迟的意思）——moratorium就是对某事的正式暂停或延期。
