# 📜 canon

🔊 /ˈkænən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A general rule, standard, or principle by which something is judged; a collection of sacred books accepted as genuine; the recognized body of works of a particular author or field.
**中文：** n. 标准；教规；正典圣经；真作集；经典

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- standard（标准）
- criterion（准则）
- doctrine（教义）
- scripture（经典）
- code（法规）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **literary canon / Western canon / canon law / canon of works / established canon**

👉 Example: The committee debated whether the newly discovered manuscript should be included in the author's official canon.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. University literature departments continue to debate which works should be included in the Western literary **canon** of essential texts.
   大学文学系继续争论哪些作品应被纳入西方文学经典的必读书目中。

2. The theologian devoted decades to studying **canon** law and its influence on medieval European governance and social institutions.
   那位神学家花了数十年时间研究教规及其对中世纪欧洲治理和社会制度的影响。

3. Scholars recently authenticated a painting that will now be added to the recognized **canon** of the Renaissance master's works.
   学者们最近鉴定了一幅画作，该画现在将被纳入这位文艺复兴大师公认的真作集中。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "canon" as a "cannon" of rules — imagine a cannon firing out official rules and standards that everyone must follow. Drop one "n" and you get the standard itself.
把"canon"想成规则的"cannon（大炮）"——想象一门大炮发射出每个人都必须遵守的官方规则和标准。去掉一个"n"就得到了标准本身。
