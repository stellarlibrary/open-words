# 📊 indicator

🔊 /ˈɪndɪkeɪtər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A thing that indicates the state or level of something; a device providing specific information; a chemical substance used to show a condition
**中文：** n. 指示器；指示剂；指示符；压力计

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gauge（量表）
- measure（衡量标准）
- signal（信号）
- barometer（晴雨表）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **key indicator / economic indicator / leading indicator / indicator of success**

👉 Example: The unemployment rate is widely regarded as a key indicator of overall economic health.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Gross domestic product remains the most commonly cited **indicator** of a nation's economic performance and overall productivity.
   国内生产总值仍然是衡量一个国家经济表现和整体生产力的最常引用的指标。

2. In the chemistry laboratory, students used litmus paper as an **indicator** to determine whether the solution was acidic or alkaline.
   在化学实验室里，学生们使用石蕊试纸作为指示剂来确定溶液是酸性还是碱性的。

3. The rising frequency of extreme weather events serves as a powerful **indicator** that climate change is accelerating at an alarming rate.
   极端天气事件频率的上升是气候变化正在以惊人速度加速的有力指标。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "indicate + or" — an indicator is something that indicates or points to a particular condition, like a dashboard gauge showing you the status.
把"indicator"想成"indicate（指示）+ or"——indicator就是指示或指向某种特定状况的东西，就像仪表盘上的表盘显示状态一样。
