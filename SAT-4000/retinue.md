# 👥 retinue

🔊 /ˈretɪnjuː/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A group of advisers, attendants, or assistants accompanying an important person; an entourage
**中文：** n. 随行人员；扈从；随员

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- entourage（随行人员）
- escort（护卫队）
- cortege（随行队伍）
- attendants（侍从）
- suite（随从）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a retinue of advisers / royal retinue / large retinue / travel with a retinue**

👉 Example: The ambassador arrived at the summit with an impressive retinue of diplomats and security personnel.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The medieval monarch never traveled without a vast **retinue** of knights, servants, and trusted political counselors.
   这位中世纪君主出行时总是带着大量的骑士、仆人和受信赖的政治顾问随行。

2. The celebrity's **retinue** included personal stylists, bodyguards, and a dedicated team of public relations specialists.
   这位名人的随行人员包括私人造型师、保镖和一支专业的公关团队。

3. Historical accounts describe the emperor's **retinue** as numbering in the thousands during formal ceremonial processions.
   历史记载描述皇帝在正式典礼巡游中的扈从人数多达数千人。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "RETAIN-ue" — a retinue is a group of people you "retain" or keep around you as attendants and advisers.
把"retinue"想成"RETAIN-ue"——随行人员就是你"retain"（留住）在身边的侍从和顾问。
