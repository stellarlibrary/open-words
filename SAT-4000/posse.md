# 🤠 posse

🔊 /ˈpɑːsi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A group of people summoned by a sheriff to enforce the law; a body of armed citizens organized to maintain order; informally, a group of associates
**中文：** n. 一队；民防团；地方武装团队

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- militia（民兵）
- squad（小队）
- band（一帮）
- contingent（分遣队）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **form a posse / posse of deputies / sheriff's posse / gather a posse**

👉 Example: The sheriff organized a posse to search the surrounding countryside for the escaped prisoner.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The frontier sheriff quickly assembled a **posse** of armed volunteers to pursue the outlaws who had robbed the stagecoach.
   边境治安官迅速召集了一队武装志愿者去追捕抢劫了驿马车的歹徒。

2. In nineteenth-century America, forming a **posse** was a common legal mechanism for maintaining order in remote territories.
   在十九世纪的美国，组建民防团是维持偏远地区秩序的一种常见法律手段。

3. The governor authorized the local magistrate to raise a **posse** in response to the growing threat of civil unrest.
   州长授权地方法官组建一支武装队伍，以应对日益增长的社会动荡威胁。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "posse" as "posse-ssion of power" — a posse possesses the power of the law as a group enforcing order.
把"posse"想成"posse-ssion of power"——一支民防团作为执法团体掌握着法律的力量。
