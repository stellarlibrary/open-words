# 😩 frustrate

🔊 /frʌˈstreɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To prevent a plan or action from progressing or succeeding; to cause feelings of disappointment or discouragement
**中文：** 挫败；阻挠；使感到灰心；阻止

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- thwart（阻挠）
- foil（挫败）
- hinder（妨碍）
- discourage（使灰心）
- stymie（阻碍）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **frustrate efforts / frustrate plans / frustrate opponents / deeply frustrated**

👉 Example: Repeated bureaucratic delays continued to frustrate the team's efforts to launch the humanitarian project.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The constant regulatory obstacles served to **frustrate** the company's ambitious expansion plans into emerging international markets.
   持续的监管障碍挫败了该公司向新兴国际市场积极扩张的计划。

2. Poor communication between departments continued to **frustrate** progress on the critical cross-functional initiative throughout the fiscal year.
   部门之间糟糕的沟通在整个财政年度中持续阻碍了这一关键跨部门计划的进展。

3. The detective was deeply **frustrated** by the lack of credible witnesses willing to testify in the high-profile criminal case.
   侦探对缺乏愿意在这起备受瞩目的刑事案件中作证的可靠证人感到非常灰心。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "frus-TRATE" as "fuss + rate" — when things keep going wrong at a high rate, you fuss and feel frustrated.
把"frustrate"想成"fuss（烦恼）+ rate（频率）"——当事情以很高的频率出错时，你就会烦恼并感到灰心。
