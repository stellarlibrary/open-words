# 🧊 polar

🔊 /ˈpoʊlər/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or situated near the North or South Pole; of or relating to magnetic poles; completely opposite in character or nature. (n.) A polar curve or line in mathematics.
**中文：** adj. 极地的；两极的；正好相反的 n. 极面；极线

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- arctic（北极的）
- opposite（相反的）
- diametrical（截然对立的）
- glacial（冰川的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **polar region / polar bear / polar opposite / polar ice cap / polar vortex**

👉 Example: The two candidates held polar opposite views on the issue of immigration reform.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Scientists are closely monitoring **polar** ice caps because their accelerated melting poses a severe threat to global sea levels.
   科学家们正在密切监测**极地**冰盖，因为其加速融化对全球海平面构成严重威胁。

2. The two philosophers represented **polar** extremes of thought, one advocating pure rationalism and the other embracing radical empiricism.
   这两位哲学家代表了思想的**两极**极端，一位倡导纯粹理性主义，另一位拥抱激进经验主义。

3. Expeditions to **polar** regions require meticulous preparation owing to the extreme cold and unpredictable weather conditions encountered there.
   前往**极地**地区的探险需要周密准备，因为那里会遇到极端寒冷和不可预测的天气状况。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "polar" as "pole-ar" — directly relating to the poles of the Earth, where everything is at the extreme end, completely opposite.
把"polar"想成"pole-ar"——直接与地球两极相关，一切都在极端的两端，完全相反。
