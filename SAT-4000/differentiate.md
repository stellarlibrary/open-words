# 🔬 differentiate

🔊 /ˌdɪfəˈrenʃieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To recognize or identify the differences between two or more things; to make something distinct or different
**中文：** v. 区分，区别；使不同

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- distinguish（区分）
- discern（辨别）
- discriminate（区别）
- separate（分开）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **differentiate between A and B / differentiate A from B / differentiate oneself**

👉 Example: Scientists must differentiate between correlation and causation when interpreting experimental data.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor asked students to **differentiate** between the two philosophical schools of thought in their essays.
   教授要求学生在论文中区分这两个哲学流派。

2. A strong personal brand can help professionals **differentiate** themselves in an increasingly competitive job market.
   强有力的个人品牌可以帮助专业人士在日益激烈的就业市场中脱颖而出。

3. Advanced microscopy techniques allow researchers to **differentiate** healthy cells from those affected by the disease.
   先进的显微镜技术使研究人员能够区分健康细胞和受疾病影响的细胞。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "differ-enti-ate" as "to make the difference entity clear" — you actively find what makes things differ.
把"differentiate"想成"让不同的实体清晰"——你主动找出事物之间的不同之处。
