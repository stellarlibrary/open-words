# ⚛️ neutron

🔊 /ˈnjuːtrɒn/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A subatomic particle with no electric charge, found in the nucleus of every atom except hydrogen
**中文：** n. 中子

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- nucleon（核子）
- subatomic particle（亚原子粒子）
- nuclear particle（核粒子）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **neutron star / neutron bomb / neutron beam / free neutron / thermal neutron**

👉 Example: Scientists used a neutron beam to probe the internal structure of the crystalline material.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **neutron** carries no electrical charge, which allows it to penetrate atomic nuclei without electromagnetic repulsion.
   中子不带电荷，这使它能够在不受电磁排斥的情况下穿透原子核。

2. A **neutron** star is formed when a massive star collapses, compressing its core to extraordinary density.
   当大质量恒星坍缩时会形成中子星，其核心被压缩到极高的密度。

3. In nuclear fission, a **neutron** strikes a uranium atom, splitting it and releasing tremendous amounts of energy.
   在核裂变中，一个中子撞击铀原子，使其裂变并释放出巨大的能量。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "neut-ron" — "neutral" particle, because a neutron has no charge, remaining neutral inside the atom's nucleus.
把"neutron"想成"neutral（中性的）"粒子——因为中子没有电荷，在原子核内保持中性。
