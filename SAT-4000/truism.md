# 💬 truism

🔊 /ˈtruːɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A statement that is obviously true and says nothing new or interesting; a self-evident truth or cliche
**中文：** 自明之理；老生常谈

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- platitude（陈词滥调）
- cliche（老套的话）
- axiom（公理）
- banality（平庸之语）
- commonplace（老生常谈）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mere truism / obvious truism / old truism / repeat a truism**

👉 Example: The professor dismissed the student's argument as nothing more than a truism dressed up in academic jargon.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. It is a **truism** that practice makes perfect, yet few people apply this principle with genuine consistency.
   "熟能生巧"是一句老生常谈，但很少有人能真正持之以恒地运用这一原则。

2. The politician's speech was filled with **truisms** that offered no substantive solutions to the nation's economic crisis.
   那位政客的演讲充满了老生常谈，对国家的经济危机没有提出任何实质性解决方案。

3. While the observation may seem like a **truism**, its implications for modern educational reform remain profoundly significant.
   尽管这一观察看似自明之理，但它对现代教育改革的影响仍然意义深远。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "truism" as "true + ism" — a belief or statement so obviously true that it barely needs saying.
把"truism"想成"true + ism"——一种如此显而易见的真理，以至于几乎不需要说出来。
