# ⏳ period

🔊 /ˈpɪriəd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A length or portion of time; a punctuation mark indicating the end of a sentence; a recurring interval or cycle. (adj.) Belonging to or characteristic of a past historical era
**中文：** n. 周期；期间；时期；句号 adj. 某一时代的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- era（时代）
- epoch（纪元）
- interval（间隔）
- phase（阶段）
- duration（持续时间）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **period of time / historical period / period furniture / grace period / transition period**

👉 Example: The Romantic period in literature produced some of the most celebrated poetry in the English language.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The Renaissance **period** witnessed an extraordinary flourishing of art, science, and intellectual inquiry throughout Western Europe.
   文艺复兴时期见证了整个西欧艺术、科学和学术探究的非凡繁荣。

2. Economists predict a prolonged **period** of slow growth following the recent financial downturn that affected global markets severely.
   经济学家预测，在最近严重影响全球市场的金融衰退之后，将出现一段漫长的缓慢增长期。

3. The museum's new exhibit features authentic **period** costumes that accurately represent fashion trends from the eighteenth century.
   博物馆的新展览展出了真实的时代服饰，准确地再现了十八世纪的时尚潮流。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "period" as a "dot in time" — just as a period ends a sentence, it also marks the end or boundary of a span of time.
把"period"想成"时间中的句号"——正如句号结束一个句子，它也标志着一段时间的终结或边界。
