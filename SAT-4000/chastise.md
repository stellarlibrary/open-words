# 🔨 chastise

🔊 /tʃæˈstaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To punish severely; to reprimand or criticize harshly for wrongdoing
**中文：** v. 严惩；惩罚；责骂

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- punish（惩罚）
- reprimand（训斥）
- rebuke（斥责）
- castigate（严厉批评）
- scold（责骂）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **chastise someone for / publicly chastise / chastise harshly / chastise severely**

👉 Example: The editor chastised the journalist for publishing unverified claims in the article.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee voted unanimously to **chastise** the official for his repeated violations of established ethical guidelines and protocols.
   委员会一致投票谴责这位官员多次违反既定的伦理准则和规程。

2. Parents who **chastise** their children excessively may inadvertently foster resentment rather than encourage genuine behavioral improvement.
   过度惩罚孩子的父母可能无意中激起怨恨，而不是培养真正的行为改善。

3. The literary critic **chastise**d the novelist publicly for producing a work that lacked originality and intellectual depth.
   文学评论家公开责骂这位小说家创作了一部缺乏原创性和思想深度的作品。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "chast-ise" as related to "chaste" — to chastise is to punish someone to make them pure or corrected, restoring moral order.
把"chastise"和"chaste（纯洁的）"联系起来——惩罚某人使其纯洁或改正，恢复道德秩序。
