# ⚛️ atom

🔊 /ˈæt.əm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The smallest unit of a chemical element that retains its identity; an extremely small amount of something.
**中文：** 原子；极小的东西；微量

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- particle（粒子）
- molecule（分子）
- speck（微粒）
- iota（极少量）
- trace（微量）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **atom of truth/sense/evidence**, **split the atom**, **atom bomb/energy**

👉 Example: There was not an atom of evidence to support the defendant's claim of innocence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Scientists successfully split the **atom** in the early twentieth century, forever changing the course of human history.
   科学家在二十世纪初成功分裂了原子，永远改变了人类历史的进程。

2. The professor explained that every **atom** consists of a nucleus surrounded by a cloud of orbiting electrons.
   教授解释说，每个原子都由一个被电子云环绕的原子核组成。

3. There was not a single **atom** of doubt in her mind when she accepted the prestigious research position.
   当她接受这个享有盛誉的研究职位时，她心中没有一丝一毫的疑虑。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"ATOM" comes from Greek "atomos" meaning "indivisible" — the ancient Greeks believed it was the smallest, uncuttable unit of matter.
"ATOM"源自希腊语"atomos"，意为"不可分割的"——古希腊人认为它是最小的、不可切割的物质单位。
