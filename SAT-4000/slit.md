# 🔪 slit

🔊 /slɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make a long, narrow cut or opening in something. (n.) A long, narrow cut, opening, or gap
**中文：** n. 狭缝

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- slot（狭槽）
- gash（裂口）
- incision（切口）
- fissure（裂缝）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **slit open / narrow slit / slit in the curtain / slit an envelope**

👉 Example: She carefully slit the envelope open with a letter opener to avoid tearing the contents.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scientist observed the light pattern that formed when a laser beam passed through a narrow **slit** in the barrier.
   科学家观察了激光束穿过屏障上狭缝时形成的光线图案。

2. The tailor **slit** the fabric along the marked line to create the precise opening required for the garment's design.
   裁缝沿着标记线将布料裁开，以创造出服装设计所需的精确开口。

3. Through a thin **slit** in the shuttered window, the detective could observe the suspect's movements inside the building.
   透过百叶窗上的一条狭缝，侦探可以观察到嫌疑人在建筑物内的动向。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "slit" as "s-lit" — a narrow opening that lets just a sliver of light through, like a "lit" gap.
把"slit"想成"s-lit（点亮的）"——一条只让一线光透过的狭缝，就像一个"发光的"缝隙。
