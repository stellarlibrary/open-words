# 😤 resent

🔊 /rɪˈzɛnt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To feel bitter indignation or ill will toward someone or something perceived as unfair or offensive
**中文：** v. 怨恨；愤恨；厌恶

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- begrudge（妒忌）
- deplore（痛恨）
- loathe（憎恶）
- bristle at（对...恼怒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **resent someone for / resent being told / deeply resent / bitterly resent**

👉 Example: Many employees resent being overlooked for promotion despite their consistent and dedicated performance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. She began to **resent** her colleague's rapid promotion, feeling that her own contributions had been consistently undervalued.
   她开始怨恨同事的快速晋升，觉得自己的贡献一直被低估。

2. Citizens openly **resent** the proposed tax increase, arguing that the burden falls disproportionately on middle-class households.
   市民们公开愤恨拟议的增税方案，认为负担不成比例地落在了中产阶级家庭身上。

3. The author deeply **resent**ed the unauthorized adaptation of his novel, which distorted his original thematic intentions entirely.
   这位作者深深怨恨对他小说的未经授权的改编，这完全歪曲了他最初的主题意图。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "resent" as "re-sent" — when someone re-sends you the same annoying message, you resent them for it.
把"resent"想成"re-sent"——当某人重复发给你同一条烦人的消息时，你会因此怨恨他们。
