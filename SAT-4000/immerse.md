# 🌊 immerse

🔊 /ɪˈmɜːrs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To plunge or dip into a liquid; to involve oneself deeply in an activity, subject, or experience.
**中文：** v. 沉浸；使浸没；使陷入

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- submerge（浸没）
- engross（使全神贯注）
- absorb（吸引；使专心）
- engulf（吞没）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **immerse oneself in / immerse in water / immerse in study / immerse in a culture**

👉 Example: The best way to learn a foreign language is to immerse yourself in a country where it is spoken.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scholars chose to **immerse** themselves in archival research for an entire year before publishing their findings.
   学者们选择在发表研究成果之前，用整整一年时间沉浸于档案研究之中。

2. To fully appreciate the complexity of the novel, one must **immerse** oneself in its rich historical and cultural context.
   要充分领会这部小说的复杂性，必须将自己沉浸在其丰富的历史和文化背景中。

3. The conservation team carefully **immersed** the fragile artifacts in a chemical solution designed to halt further deterioration.
   保护团队小心地将脆弱的文物浸入一种旨在阻止进一步劣化的化学溶液中。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-merse" as "into the merge" — you merge into something completely, like diving into water or deep study.
把"immerse"想成"im（进入）+ merse（浸）"——完全浸入其中，就像潜入水中或深入学习一样。
