# 📢 exaggerate

🔊 /ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To represent something as being larger, greater, better, or worse than it actually is; to overstate or magnify beyond the truth
**中文：** v. 夸大，夸张；使扩大，使增大

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- overstate（夸大）
- embellish（润色）
- amplify（放大）
- magnify（夸大）
- inflate（膨胀）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **exaggerate the importance / exaggerate the effects / greatly exaggerated / exaggerate a claim**

👉 Example: The media was accused of exaggerating the threat to increase public anxiety and viewership.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics argued that the report significantly **exaggerated** the environmental risks associated with the proposed construction of the new dam.
   批评者认为，该报告严重夸大了拟建新大坝所带来的环境风险。

2. Politicians often **exaggerate** their accomplishments during election campaigns to create a more favorable impression among potential voters.
   政客们在竞选活动中经常夸大自己的成就，以在潜在选民中留下更好的印象。

3. It would be difficult to **exaggerate** the profound impact that the invention of the printing press had on European civilization.
   要夸大印刷术的发明对欧洲文明产生的深远影响是很困难的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-AGGER-ate" — "agger" sounds like "bigger," so to exaggerate is to make something sound bigger or greater than it really is.
把"exaggerate"想成"ex-AGGER-ate"——"agger"听起来像"bigger（更大）"，所以exaggerate就是把事情说得比实际更大更夸张。
