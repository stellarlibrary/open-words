# ⚖️ arraign

🔊 /əˈreɪn/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To call before a court to answer charges; to accuse or criticize severely
**中文：** v. 传讯，控告；责难

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- indict（起诉）
- accuse（控告）
- prosecute（检控）
- censure（谴责）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **arraign someone on charges / arraign before the court / arraign for a crime**

👉 Example: The suspect was arraigned on multiple charges of fraud and embezzlement.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The defendant was **arraigned** before the federal court on charges of conspiracy and money laundering last Monday.
   被告于上周一因共谋和洗钱罪被联邦法院传讯。

2. Several prominent journalists **arraigned** the administration for its failure to address the growing humanitarian crisis.
   数位知名记者严厉责难政府未能应对日益加剧的人道主义危机。

3. After a lengthy investigation, the grand jury decided to **arraign** the corporate executives for securities fraud.
   经过漫长的调查，大陪审团决定以证券欺诈罪传讯这些公司高管。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "arraign" sounding like "a rain" of accusations — when you arraign someone, you shower them with charges.
"arraign"听起来像"a rain（一场雨）"——传讯某人就像是向他们倾泻一场指控之雨。
