# 🕵️ cabal

🔊 /kəˈbæl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A secret political group or faction; a plot or conspiracy, especially a political one; (v.) to form a secret group or plot
**中文：** n. 阴谋小集团；阴谋（尤指政治上的） v. 策划阴谋

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conspiracy（阴谋）
- clique（小集团）
- faction（派系）
- intrigue（密谋）
- plot（阴谋）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a secret cabal / a cabal of advisors / political cabal / cabal against someone**

👉 Example: A shadowy cabal of disgruntled officials conspired to undermine the newly elected government.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Historians revealed that a powerful **cabal** within the royal court had secretly orchestrated the overthrow of the reigning monarch.
   历史学家揭示，宫廷内一个强大的阴谋集团曾秘密策划了推翻在位君主的行动。

2. The senator accused her political opponents of forming a **cabal** designed to discredit her legislative reform proposals.
   这位参议员指控其政治对手组成阴谋集团，意图诋毁她的立法改革提案。

3. Throughout the turbulent decade, a **cabal** of wealthy industrialists wielded disproportionate influence over national economic policy.
   在动荡的十年间，一群富有的工业家组成的小集团对国家经济政策施加了不成比例的影响。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cabal" sounding like "cable" — a secret cabal is like a hidden cable connecting conspirators behind the scenes.
想想"cabal"听起来像"cable"（电缆）——一个秘密的cabal就像一条隐藏的电缆，在幕后将阴谋者连接在一起。
