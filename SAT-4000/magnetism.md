# 🧲 magnetism

🔊 /ˈmæɡnɪˌtɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A physical phenomenon produced by moving electric charges, resulting in attractive or repulsive forces; the branch of physics studying magnetic fields; personal charm or allure.
**中文：** n. 磁性；磁力；磁学；吸引力，魅力

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- attraction（吸引力）
- allure（魅力）
- charisma（个人魅力）
- charm（吸引力）
- appeal（感染力）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **personal magnetism / animal magnetism / the magnetism of / magnetism and electricity**

👉 Example: Her personal magnetism made her the natural leader of every group she joined.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The physicist devoted her career to studying **magnetism** and its fundamental relationship to electric current.
   这位物理学家毕生致力于研究磁学及其与电流的基本关系。

2. The young politician possessed a natural **magnetism** that drew enormous crowds to every campaign rally.
   这位年轻政治家拥有一种天然的魅力，每次竞选集会都能吸引大批人群。

3. Early navigators relied on the **magnetism** of lodestones to orient their compasses during long ocean voyages.
   早期航海者在漫长的海上航行中依靠天然磁石的磁力来校准指南针。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "magnet" + "-ism" (the quality of) — magnetism is the invisible force that pulls things together, whether iron to a magnet or people to a charismatic leader.
把"magnetism"想成"magnet"（磁铁）+"-ism"（特性）——磁力是一种无形的力量，无论是铁被磁铁吸引，还是人们被魅力领袖吸引。
