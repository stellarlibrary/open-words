# ⚡ charge

🔊 /tʃɑːrdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A fee for a service; an electric charge; responsibility or control; a formal accusation. (v.) To supply with electrical energy; to accuse formally; to demand payment
**中文：** n. 费用；电荷；掌管；控告 v. 使充电；指责；索费；控告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fee（费用）
- accusation（指控）
- indict（控告）
- cost（花费）
- allege（指称）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **charge with / in charge of / free of charge / charge a fee / press charges**

👉 Example: The manager is in charge of overseeing all daily operations at the facility.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prosecutor decided to **charge** the suspect with multiple counts of fraud after reviewing the financial evidence carefully.
   检察官在仔细审查财务证据后决定以多项欺诈罪指控嫌疑人。

2. The hotel does not **charge** guests for the continental breakfast that is served each morning in the dining hall.
   酒店不向客人收取每天早晨在餐厅提供的欧式早餐的费用。

3. The committee placed her in **charge** of the international conference, trusting her organizational skills and diplomatic experience.
   委员会让她负责这次国际会议，信任她的组织能力和外交经验。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "charge" like charging a battery — you fill it with energy, just as charges fill responsibilities or costs onto someone.
把"charge"想成给电池充电——你给它注入能量，就像把责任或费用加到某人身上。
