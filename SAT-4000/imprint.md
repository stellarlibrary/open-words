# 🖋️ imprint

🔊 /ɪmˈprɪnt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A mark or impression made by pressing; a lasting effect or influence; publisher information on a book. (v.) To stamp or press a mark onto something; to fix firmly in the mind
**中文：** n. 印记；痕迹；特征；版本说明 v. 加特征；刻上记号；铭记

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- stamp（印记）
- impression（印象）
- engrave（铭刻）
- mark（痕迹）
- inscribe（刻写）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **imprint on one's memory / leave an imprint / imprint a pattern / publisher's imprint**

👉 Example: The traumatic event left a lasting imprint on her memory that she could never fully erase.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The early childhood experiences **imprint** themselves on the developing brain and shape behavioral patterns throughout adulthood.
   早期童年经历会铭刻在发育中的大脑上，并塑造贯穿成年期的行为模式。

2. Archaeological excavations revealed the **imprint** of ancient footprints preserved in the volcanic sediment for thousands of years.
   考古发掘揭示了保存在火山沉积物中数千年的古代脚印印记。

3. The professor's rigorous intellectual standards left a permanent **imprint** on every student who studied under his guidance.
   这位教授严格的学术标准给每一位在他指导下学习的学生留下了永久的印记。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-print" as "in + print" — when something is printed into you, it leaves a lasting mark or impression.
把"imprint"想成"im（进入）+ print（印刷）"——当某物被印入你心中，它就留下了持久的印记。
