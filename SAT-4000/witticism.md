# 💬 witticism

🔊 /ˈwɪtɪsɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A witty remark or saying; a clever and humorous observation or comment
**中文：** n. 妙语；俏皮话；名言

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- quip（俏皮话）
- bon mot（妙语）
- epigram（警句）
- jest（笑话）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **deliver a witticism / sharp witticism / famous witticism / well-timed witticism / a string of witticisms**

👉 Example: The keynote speaker delighted the audience with a series of clever witticisms throughout her address.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Oscar Wilde remains celebrated for his brilliant **witticisms** that simultaneously entertained and exposed the hypocrisy of Victorian society.
   奥斯卡·王尔德至今仍因其既能娱乐大众又能揭露维多利亚社会虚伪的精彩妙语而闻名。

2. The professor's well-timed **witticism** about statistical probability lightened the mood of the otherwise dense academic lecture.
   教授关于统计概率的恰到好处的妙语缓解了这场原本枯燥的学术讲座的气氛。

3. Political commentators frequently employ sharp **witticisms** to make complex policy debates more accessible and engaging for general audiences.
   政治评论员经常使用犀利的俏皮话使复杂的政策辩论对普通观众更易理解和更具吸引力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "witticism" as "wit + criticism" — a witty form of criticism, a clever remark that often carries a pointed observation.
把"witticism"想成"wit（机智）+ -icism（像criticism批评）"——一种机智的表达方式，既幽默又带有深意的妙语。
