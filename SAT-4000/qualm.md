# 😟 qualm

🔊 /kwɑːm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A feeling of doubt, uneasiness, or anxiety, especially about one's own conduct; a misgiving or pang of conscience
**中文：** n. 疑虑；不安

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- misgiving（疑虑）
- scruple（顾虑）
- apprehension（忧虑）
- uneasiness（不安）
- hesitation（犹豫）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **have qualms about / without qualms / moral qualms / qualms of conscience**

👉 Example: She had no qualms about reporting the ethical violation to the oversight committee.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The executive expressed serious **qualms** about approving the merger without first conducting a thorough environmental impact assessment.
   这位高管对在未进行全面环境影响评估的情况下批准合并表示严重的疑虑。

2. Despite initial **qualms**, the research team proceeded with the controversial experiment after receiving full ethical board approval.
   尽管最初有所不安，研究团队在获得伦理委员会的全面批准后继续进行了这项有争议的实验。

3. The journalist felt no **qualms** about publishing the investigation, knowing that transparency served the greater public interest.
   记者对发表这篇调查报道毫无顾虑，因为她知道透明度符合更大的公众利益。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "qualm" as sounding like "calm" — when you have qualms, you are the opposite of calm; you feel uneasy and doubtful.
把"qualm"想成听起来像"calm（平静）"——当你有qualms时，你正好与平静相反，感到不安和疑虑。
