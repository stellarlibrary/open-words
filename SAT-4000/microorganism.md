# 🦠 microorganism

🔊 /ˌmaɪkroʊˈɔːrɡənɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A microscopic organism, especially a bacterium, virus, or fungus, that is too small to be seen with the naked eye.
**中文：** n. 微生物；微小动植物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- microbe（微生物）
- bacterium（细菌）
- germ（病菌）
- pathogen（病原体）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **harmful microorganism / beneficial microorganism / single-celled microorganism / soil microorganism**

👉 Example: Scientists study microorganisms to understand how they interact with their environments at the cellular level.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The discovery of penicillin revolutionized medicine by demonstrating that certain **microorganism** byproducts could effectively combat infections.
   青霉素的发现通过证明某些微生物副产品可以有效对抗感染，彻底改变了医学。

2. Researchers identified a previously unknown **microorganism** in deep-sea hydrothermal vents that thrives under extreme temperature conditions.
   研究人员在深海热液喷口中发现了一种此前未知的微生物，它在极端温度条件下茁壮成长。

3. Each **microorganism** in the human gut plays a distinct role in digestion, immunity, and overall metabolic health.
   人体肠道中的每种微生物在消化、免疫和整体代谢健康中都发挥着独特的作用。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "micro-organism" — literally a "tiny living thing." The prefix "micro" means small, so a microorganism is simply an organism too small to see.
把"microorganism"想成"micro"（微小的）+"organism"（生物体）——字面意思就是"微小的生物"，小到肉眼看不见。
