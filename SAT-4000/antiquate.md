# ⏳ antiquate

🔊 /ˈæntɪkweɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** To make obsolete or outdated; to cause something to fall out of use or become old-fashioned by replacing it with something newer or more effective.

**中文：** 废弃；使过时。使某事物因被更新或更有效的事物取代而不再使用。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- obsolesce（变得过时）
- outdate（使过时）
- supersede（取代）
- displace（替代）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Common collocations:** antiquate a system, antiquate a method, antiquated technology, antiquated practices

**Example:** Rapid technological progress will inevitably **antiquate** many of the tools we rely on today.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The introduction of digital photography effectively **antiquated** the traditional film development process within a single decade.
   数码摄影的引入在短短十年内就有效地淘汰了传统的胶片冲洗工艺。

2. Policymakers warned that failing to modernize infrastructure would **antiquate** the nation's transportation network beyond repair.
   政策制定者警告说，如果不对基础设施进行现代化改造，国家的交通网络将过时到无法修复的地步。

3. The new regulatory framework was designed to **antiquate** outdated compliance procedures that had burdened the industry.
   新的监管框架旨在废除那些给行业带来负担的过时合规程序。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "antique" + "-ate" (to make). To antiquate something is to make it an antique — to render it old and obsolete.

联想："antique"（古董）+ "-ate"（使…）。antiquate 就是使某物变成"古董"——让它变得陈旧过时。
