# 😈 immoral

🔊 /ɪˈmɒrəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Not conforming to accepted standards of morality; morally wrong or wicked in conduct or character.
**中文：** adj. 不道德的；邪恶的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unethical（不道德的）
- corrupt（腐败的）
- wicked（邪恶的）
- depraved（堕落的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **immoral behavior / immoral act / immoral conduct / morally immoral**

👉 Example: The philosopher argued that profiting from others' suffering is fundamentally immoral.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court ruled that the corporation's deliberate concealment of toxic waste data constituted an **immoral** disregard for public health.
   法院裁定，该公司故意隐瞒有毒废物数据构成对公众健康的不道德漠视。

2. Many scholars consider the exploitation of child labor in industrialized nations during the nineteenth century profoundly **immoral**.
   许多学者认为十九世纪工业化国家对童工的剥削是极其不道德的。

3. The journalist exposed the **immoral** practices of the financial institution, which had systematically defrauded thousands of elderly clients.
   记者揭露了该金融机构的不道德行为，该机构系统性地欺骗了数千名老年客户。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-moral" as "not moral" — the prefix "im-" negates "moral," making it the opposite of morally right.
把"immoral"想成"im（不）+ moral（道德的）"——不道德的，与道德相悖的行为。
