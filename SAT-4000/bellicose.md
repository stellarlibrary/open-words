# ⚔️ bellicose

🔊 /ˈbɛlɪkoʊs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Demonstrating aggression and willingness to fight; inclined toward warfare or hostility
**中文：** adj. 好战的；好斗的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- belligerent（好战的）
- pugnacious（好斗的）
- combative（好争斗的）
- quarrelsome（爱争吵的）
- militant（好战的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bellicose rhetoric / bellicose attitude / bellicose nation / bellicose tone**

👉 Example: The ambassador's bellicose rhetoric alarmed neighboring countries and threatened diplomatic relations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator's **bellicose** remarks during the debate heightened tensions and undermined prospects for a peaceful resolution.
   那位参议员在辩论中好战的言论加剧了紧张局势，削弱了和平解决的前景。

2. Historians attribute the empire's decline in part to its increasingly **bellicose** foreign policy and relentless military campaigns.
   历史学家将帝国的衰落部分归因于其日益好战的外交政策和无休止的军事行动。

3. Despite his **bellicose** posture, the general privately advocated for diplomatic negotiations over armed conflict.
   尽管这位将军摆出好战姿态，但他私下里主张通过外交谈判而非武装冲突来解决问题。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "belli-" from Latin "bellum" (war) + "-cose" — bellicose literally means "war-like" or inclined to fight.
"Belli-"来自拉丁语"bellum"（战争）+"cose"——bellicose字面意思就是"好战的"。
