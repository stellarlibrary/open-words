# 💀 moribund

🔊 /ˈmɔːrɪbʌnd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) At the point of death; in terminal decline, lacking vitality or vigor. (n.) A person who is dying
**中文：** adj. 垂死的；停滞不前的 n. 垂死的人

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dying（垂死的）
- declining（衰退的）
- stagnant（停滞的）
- waning（衰落的）
- expiring（奄奄一息的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **moribund economy / moribund industry / moribund institution / moribund patient**

👉 Example: The once-thriving manufacturing sector had become moribund after decades of neglect and foreign competition.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **moribund** textile industry in the region finally collapsed when cheaper imports flooded the domestic market without restriction.
   该地区奄奄一息的纺织业在廉价进口商品不受限制地涌入国内市场后最终崩溃。

2. Critics described the political party as **moribund**, noting its inability to attract younger voters or adapt to modern concerns.
   批评人士称该政党已是日薄西山，指出其无法吸引年轻选民或适应现代社会关切。

3. The physician determined that the **moribund** patient required immediate palliative care rather than further aggressive medical intervention.
   医生判断这位垂危的病人需要立即接受姑息治疗，而非进一步的积极医疗干预。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "moribund" as "mori" (Latin mori = to die) + "bund" (bound) — moribund means bound for death, on the verge of dying.
"moribund"包含拉丁语"mori"（死亡）+ "bund"（束缚）——意思是被死亡束缚，即将走向消亡。
