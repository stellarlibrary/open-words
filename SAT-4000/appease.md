# 🕊️ appease

🔊 /əˈpiːz/<br>
🏷️ *v. 使平息；安抚；满足*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** To bring to a state of peace or calm; to pacify or soothe, especially by acceding to demands; to satisfy or relieve a feeling such as hunger or curiosity.

**中文：** 使平息；安抚；姑息；满足（欲望、需求等）。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- placate 安抚
- pacify 使平静
- mollify 平息
- conciliate 调和
- soothe 抚慰

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Common collocations:** appease someone's anger, appease one's hunger, appease the crowd, policy of appeasement, appease critics

**Example:** "Appease" often carries a negative connotation of giving in to unreasonable demands (as in political appeasement). However, it can also simply mean to satisfy or calm.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government offered several policy concessions in an attempt to **appease** the increasingly frustrated public.
   政府提出了若干政策让步，试图**安抚**日益不满的公众。

2. Historians widely criticize the strategy of **appeasing** aggressive regimes, as it often emboldens further expansion.
   历史学家普遍批评**姑息**侵略性政权的策略，因为它往往助长进一步的扩张。

3. She ate a small meal to **appease** her hunger before the long and demanding evening rehearsal began.
   她在漫长而紧张的晚间排练开始前吃了一小顿饭来**满足**饥饿感。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Appease" contains "peace" — to appease someone is to bring them peace by calming them down or giving them what they want.

拆词联想：appease 中包含"peace"（和平）——安抚某人就是给他们带来"和平"，让他们平息下来。
