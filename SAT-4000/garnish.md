# 🍽️ garnish

🔊 /ˈɡɑːrnɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To decorate or embellish something, especially food; to add a decorative or flavorful touch. (n.) A decorative addition to food or drink; an embellishment
**中文：** v. 装饰；点缀；为食物加配菜 n. 配菜；装饰品；装饰物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- decorate（装饰）
- adorn（点缀）
- embellish（修饰）
- ornament（装点）
- trim（装饰）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **garnish with / garnish a dish / garnish a plate / as a garnish / fresh garnish**

👉 Example: The chef garnished the entrée with a sprig of rosemary and a drizzle of balsamic reduction.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The pastry chef carefully **garnished** each dessert plate with edible flowers and a delicate dusting of powdered sugar.
   糕点师仔细地用食用花卉和精致的糖粉装饰每一盘甜点。

2. Skilled writers know how to **garnish** their prose with vivid metaphors without overwhelming the reader with excessive ornamentation.
   熟练的作家知道如何用生动的比喻来装饰文章，而不会用过多的修饰令读者感到负担。

3. A simple lemon wedge served as the perfect **garnish** for the elegantly prepared seafood dish at the formal banquet.
   一片简单的柠檬角作为完美的配菜，点缀了正式宴会上精心准备的海鲜佳肴。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "garnish" as the finishing touch that makes a dish look "gorgeous" — both words start with "gar" and relate to making something visually appealing.
把"garnish"想成让菜肴看起来"gorgeous"（华丽）的最后一笔——两个词都以"gar"开头，都与让事物视觉上更吸引人有关。
