# 🏢 monopoly

🔊 /məˈnɒpəli/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Exclusive control or possession of the supply of a commodity or service; a company or group that has such control; the exclusive right to trade in a particular market
**中文：** n. 垄断；垄断者；专卖权

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dominance（支配地位）
- oligopoly（寡头垄断）
- cartel（卡特尔）
- exclusivity（独占性）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **hold a monopoly / break a monopoly / monopoly on / state monopoly / natural monopoly**

👉 Example: The corporation held a virtual monopoly on the telecommunications industry for nearly two decades.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government enacted antitrust legislation to dismantle the corporation's **monopoly** over the nation's entire railroad network.
   政府颁布了反垄断法，以拆除该公司对全国铁路网络的垄断。

2. Critics argued that no single entity should possess a **monopoly** on access to essential public resources like clean water.
   批评人士认为，任何单一实体都不应垄断清洁水等基本公共资源的使用权。

3. The technology firm's **monopoly** on the operating system market attracted intense scrutiny from international regulatory authorities worldwide.
   该科技公司对操作系统市场的垄断引起了全球国际监管机构的严格审查。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mono" (one) + "poly" (sell) — a monopoly means "one seller," only one company controls the entire market.
把"monopoly"拆成"mono"（一个）+"poly"（卖）——垄断就是"一个卖家"，只有一家公司控制整个市场。
