# 🖊️ satirize

🔊 /ˈsætəraɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To attack or ridicule with satire; to use humor, irony, or exaggeration to criticize or mock
**中文：** v. 讽刺

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- lampoon（讽刺）
- mock（嘲笑）
- parody（戏仿）
- ridicule（嘲弄）
- caricature（讽刺性描绘）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **satirize a politician / satirize society / satirize the establishment / satirize hypocrisy**

👉 Example: The comedian satirized the government's contradictory policies in a widely shared television sketch.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The cartoonist **satirized** the administration's handling of the crisis by depicting officials as blindfolded navigators steering a sinking ship.
   这位漫画家通过将官员描绘成蒙着眼睛驾驶沉船的领航员来讽刺政府对危机的处理。

2. Many Enlightenment writers sought to **satirize** the corruption of the clergy and the rigid hierarchy of feudal institutions.
   许多启蒙运动作家试图讽刺神职人员的腐败和封建制度的僵化等级制度。

3. The playwright's decision to **satirize** corporate greed resonated deeply with audiences frustrated by rising economic inequality.
   这位剧作家讽刺企业贪婪的决定与对经济不平等加剧感到沮丧的观众产生了深刻共鸣。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "satirize" as "satire + ize" — to turn something into the subject of satire. When you satirize, you make someone or something the target of sharp, witty criticism.
把"satirize"想成"satire（讽刺）+ ize（使…化）"——将某事物变成讽刺的对象。当你satirize时，你就是用尖锐的机智批评来针对某人或某事。
