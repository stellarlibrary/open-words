# 🌊 profound

🔊 /prəˈfaʊnd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Very deep or intense; having great insight or understanding; far-reaching in significance or effect
**中文：** adj. 深厚的；意义深远的；渊博的；造诣深的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- deep（深刻的）
- intense（强烈的）
- far-reaching（深远的）
- insightful（有洞察力的）
- momentous（重大的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **profound impact / profound effect / profound understanding / profound change / profound silence**

👉 Example: The discovery of antibiotics had a profound impact on modern medicine and global public health.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosopher's writings had a **profound** influence on subsequent generations of scholars who studied ethics and moral reasoning.
   这位哲学家的著作对后来研究伦理学和道德推理的几代学者产生了深远的影响。

2. Witnessing the devastating aftermath of the natural disaster left a **profound** impression that permanently altered her career aspirations.
   目睹自然灾害的毁灭性后果给她留下了深刻的印象，永久地改变了她的职业抱负。

3. The research team's **profound** understanding of molecular biology enabled them to develop a revolutionary approach to gene therapy.
   研究团队对分子生物学的渊博理解使他们能够开发出一种革命性的基因治疗方法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pro-found" as "professionally found" — something so deep it takes a professional to find the meaning at the very bottom.
把"profound"想成"pro（专业地）+ found（发现）"——某物如此深邃，需要专业人士才能发现最深处的含义。
