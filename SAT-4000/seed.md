# 🌱 seed

🔊 /siːd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A flowering plant's unit of reproduction; an origin or beginning; offspring. (v.) To plant seeds; to produce seeds; to mature
**中文：** n. 种子

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- kernel（种子）
- origin（起源）
- germ（萌芽）
- source（根源）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **seed of doubt / plant a seed / seed money / go to seed**

👉 Example: The professor's lecture planted the seed of curiosity that would shape her entire academic career.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The agricultural researchers developed a new **seed** variety capable of withstanding prolonged drought conditions in arid regions.
   农业研究人员开发了一种新的种子品种，能够在干旱地区承受长期干旱条件。

2. His careless remark planted the **seed** of suspicion that eventually undermined trust among the committee members.
   他不经意的言论埋下了怀疑的种子，最终破坏了委员会成员之间的信任。

3. The investors provided essential **seed** funding to transform the innovative concept into a commercially viable technology.
   投资者提供了必要的种子资金，将这一创新概念转化为具有商业可行性的技术。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "seed" as the tiny beginning from which something large grows — every great oak was once a small seed.
把"seed"想成万物生长的微小起点——每棵参天大橡树都曾是一粒小小的种子。
