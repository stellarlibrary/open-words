# 🎯 selective

🔊 /sɪˈlɛktɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or involving selection; tending to choose carefully; affecting some things and not others
**中文：** adj. 选择的，选择性的；精挑细选的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- discriminating（有鉴别力的）
- particular（挑剔的）
- choosy（讲究的）
- discerning（有辨别力的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **selective process / selective memory / selective attention / selective breeding**

👉 Example: The admissions committee employed a highly selective process to identify the most promising candidates.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prestigious university maintains an extremely **selective** admissions policy, accepting fewer than five percent of all applicants annually.
   这所著名大学保持着极为严格的精挑细选式招生政策，每年录取率不到百分之五。

2. Critics accused the historian of presenting a **selective** interpretation of events that conveniently supported her predetermined thesis.
   批评者指责这位历史学家对事件进行了选择性的解读，恰好支持了她预设的论点。

3. Through **selective** breeding over many generations, agricultural scientists developed crop varieties with significantly improved resistance to disease.
   通过多代选择性育种，农业科学家培育出了抗病性显著提高的作物品种。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "select-ive" as "select + active" — being actively engaged in selecting only the best from many options.
把"selective"想成"select + active"——积极地从众多选项中只挑选最好的。
