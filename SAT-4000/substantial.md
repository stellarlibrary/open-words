# 🏛️ substantial

🔊 /səbˈstænʃəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Of considerable importance, size, or worth; real and tangible; solidly built. (n.) The essential nature or material
**中文：** adj. 实质的，真实的；坚固的，结实的；富裕的，可观的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- considerable（相当大的）
- significant（重要的）
- ample（充足的）
- solid（坚固的）
- meaningful（有意义的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **substantial evidence / substantial amount / substantial progress / substantial investment**

👉 Example: The prosecution must present substantial evidence to convince the jury beyond a reasonable doubt.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The research team secured a **substantial** grant that enabled them to expand their study to include three additional countries.
   研究团队获得了一笔可观的资助，使他们能够将研究扩展到另外三个国家。

2. There has been **substantial** progress in reducing childhood mortality rates across sub-Saharan Africa over the past decade.
   在过去十年中，撒哈拉以南非洲在降低儿童死亡率方面取得了显著进展。

3. The architect designed a **substantial** structure capable of withstanding seismic activity common to the coastal region.
   建筑师设计了一座坚固的建筑，能够抵御该沿海地区常见的地震活动。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sub-stantial" as "standing with substance" — something substantial has real substance, weight, and significance.
把"substantial"想成"有实质地站立"——substantial的事物具有真正的实质、分量和重要性。
