# 👎 disrepute

🔊 /ˌdɪsrɪˈpjuːt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The state of being held in low esteem; a loss or lack of good reputation
**中文：** n. 不光彩；坏名声；丧失名誉

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disgrace（耻辱）
- infamy（恶名）
- dishonor（不名誉）
- ignominy（耻辱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fall into disrepute / bring into disrepute / in disrepute / of disrepute**

👉 Example: The once-respected institution gradually fell into disrepute after a series of financial scandals.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The fraudulent practices of several executives brought the entire corporation into **disrepute** among investors and regulators.
   几位高管的欺诈行为使整个公司在投资者和监管机构中声名狼藉。

2. The theory fell into **disrepute** after multiple independent studies failed to replicate the original experimental findings.
   在多项独立研究未能重复原始实验结果后，该理论声誉扫地。

3. Engaging in conduct that brings the profession into **disrepute** may result in suspension or permanent revocation of one's license.
   从事有损行业声誉的行为可能导致执照被暂停或永久吊销。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-repute" as "dis" (not) + "repute" (reputation) — disrepute is the state of having a damaged or negative reputation.
把"disrepute"想成"dis"（不）+"repute"（名声）——disrepute就是名声受损或名声不好的状态。
