# 🎙️ discourse

🔊 /ˈdɪskɔːrs/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Written or spoken communication or discussion; a formal treatment of a subject in speech or writing. (v.) To speak or write authoritatively about a topic
**中文：** n. 论述；谈话 v. 演说

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- discussion（讨论）
- treatise（论文）
- dialogue（对话）
- lecture（演讲）
- exposition（阐述）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **public discourse / political discourse / discourse on a topic / academic discourse / discourse analysis**

👉 Example: The professor delivered a lengthy discourse on the ethical implications of artificial intelligence in modern society.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The quality of public **discourse** has declined significantly as polarization and misinformation continue to dominate social media platforms.
   随着极端化和虚假信息持续主导社交媒体平台，公共话语的质量已显著下降。

2. The philosopher's most celebrated **discourse** on human freedom remains a foundational text in political theory courses at major universities.
   这位哲学家关于人类自由的最著名论述至今仍是各大学政治理论课程的基础文献。

3. Scholars gathered at the annual conference to **discourse** upon recent developments in climate science and their policy implications worldwide.
   学者们在年度会议上齐聚一堂，就气候科学的最新进展及其在全球的政策影响进行论述。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-course" — a course of discussion. A discourse is like running a course through a topic, covering it thoroughly in speech or writing.
把"discourse"想成"dis-course"——一场讨论的过程。就像在一个话题上跑完一个"course（课程/路线）"，通过演讲或写作全面覆盖。
