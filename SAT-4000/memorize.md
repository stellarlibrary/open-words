# 🧠 memorize

🔊 /ˈmɛməˌraɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To commit something to memory; to learn by heart so that it can be recalled accurately without reference.
**中文：** v. 记住，背熟；记忆

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- learn（学习）
- retain（记住）
- commit to memory（铭记）
- recall（回忆）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **memorize facts / memorize a speech / memorize vocabulary / memorize lines**

👉 Example: Students were required to memorize the periodic table before the chemistry examination.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The pianist spent weeks trying to **memorize** the entire concerto before her debut performance at the symphony hall.
   这位钢琴家花了数周时间背熟整首协奏曲，为她在交响乐厅的首演做准备。

2. Effective language learners do not simply **memorize** vocabulary lists but instead practice words in meaningful contexts.
   高效的语言学习者不会只是死记硬背词汇表，而是在有意义的语境中练习单词。

3. Ancient scholars relied on elaborate mnemonic devices to **memorize** lengthy philosophical texts and oral traditions.
   古代学者依靠精心设计的助记方法来背诵冗长的哲学文本和口头传统。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "memo-rize" as "making a memo rise up in your mind" — you create a mental memo that rises to the surface whenever you need it.
把"memorize"想成"让memo（备忘录）rise（升起）"——你在脑中创建一个备忘录，需要时它就会浮现出来。
