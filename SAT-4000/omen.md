# 🔮 omen

🔊 /ˈoʊmən/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A sign or warning of something that will happen in the future, especially something significant or momentous. (v.) To serve as a prophetic sign; to portend
**中文：** n. 预兆；征兆 v. 预示；有…的前兆；预告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- portent（前兆）
- harbinger（预兆）
- presage（预感）
- augury（占卜）
- forewarning（预先警告）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **good omen / bad omen / ill omen / omen of doom / take something as an omen**

👉 Example: Many ancient cultures regarded a solar eclipse as an omen of impending disaster or divine wrath.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The villagers interpreted the sudden appearance of a white deer as a favorable **omen** for the coming harvest season.
   村民们将白鹿的突然出现解读为即将到来的收获季节的好兆头。

2. Dark clouds gathering on the horizon seemed to **omen** a violent storm that would disrupt the outdoor ceremony.
   地平线上聚集的乌云似乎预示着一场猛烈的暴风雨将打断户外仪式。

3. The historian noted that the comet was widely regarded as an **omen** of political upheaval throughout medieval Europe.
   历史学家指出，这颗彗星在整个中世纪欧洲被广泛视为政治动荡的预兆。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "omen" as "oh, men!" — a surprised exclamation when people see a sign of something big about to happen.
把"omen"想成"oh, men!（哦，天哪！）"——当人们看到即将发生大事的征兆时发出的惊叹。
