# 🌀 embroil

🔊 /ɪmˈbrɔɪl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To involve someone deeply in an argument, conflict, or difficult situation; to throw into confusion or disorder
**中文：** v. 使卷入；使混乱；牵连

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- entangle（使纠缠）
- implicate（牵连）
- enmesh（使陷入）
- involve（使卷入）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **embroil in a dispute / embroiled in controversy / embroiled in scandal / embroil in conflict**

👉 Example: The leaked documents embroiled the administration in a prolonged and damaging political scandal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The territorial dispute **embroiled** several neighboring nations in a tense standoff that threatened regional stability for months.
   领土争端使几个邻国卷入了持续数月、威胁地区稳定的紧张对峙。

2. The corporation became **embroiled** in a complex lawsuit after allegations of systematic environmental violations surfaced publicly.
   在系统性环境违规指控被公开后，该公司卷入了一场复杂的诉讼。

3. The professor warned students not to become **embroiled** in academic dishonesty, as the consequences could permanently damage their careers.
   教授警告学生不要卷入学术不诚信行为，因为后果可能会永久损害他们的职业生涯。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "em-BROIL" — to be broiled (cooked) in a heated conflict, as if thrown into boiling water with no easy escape.
把"embroil"想成"em-BROIL"——被放在激烈的冲突中炙烤（BROIL），就像被扔进沸水中无法轻易脱身。
