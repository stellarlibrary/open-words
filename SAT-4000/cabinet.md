# 🗄️ cabinet

🔊 /ˈkæbɪnɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A body of advisors to a head of state; a piece of furniture with shelves or drawers for storage; (adj.) of or relating to a governmental cabinet; private or confidential
**中文：** n. 内阁；橱柜 adj. 内阁的；私下的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- council（委员会）
- cupboard（橱柜）
- ministry（内阁部门）
- closet（壁橱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cabinet meeting / cabinet minister / cabinet reshuffle / medicine cabinet / filing cabinet**

👉 Example: The prime minister convened an emergency cabinet meeting to address the escalating economic crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The president appointed several distinguished scholars to the **cabinet** in an effort to strengthen the administration's policy expertise.
   总统任命了几位杰出学者进入内阁，以加强政府的政策专业水平。

2. The antique **cabinet** displayed in the museum contained rare porcelain artifacts dating back to the eighteenth century.
   博物馆展出的古董橱柜中陈列着可追溯到十八世纪的稀有瓷器文物。

3. Following the electoral defeat, the outgoing leader conducted a thorough **cabinet** reshuffle before officially transferring power to the successor.
   选举失败后，即将卸任的领导人在正式移交权力给继任者之前进行了彻底的内阁改组。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cabin-et" — a small cabin or room. A cabinet is either a small enclosed storage space (furniture) or the small room where top advisors meet (government).
想想"cabin-et"——一个小cabin（小屋/房间）。Cabinet既是一个封闭的小储物空间（家具），也是高级顾问们开会的小房间（政府内阁）。
