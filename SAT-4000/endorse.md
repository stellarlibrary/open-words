# ✅ endorse

🔊 /ɪnˈdɔːrs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To sign on the back of a document; to declare one's approval or support of something publicly
**中文：** v. 背书；认可；赞同；在背面签名

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- approve（批准）
- support（支持）
- sanction（认可）
- ratify（批准）
- advocate（提倡）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **endorse a candidate / endorse a product / endorse a check / endorse a policy**

👉 Example: Several prominent scientists publicly endorsed the new climate initiative proposed by the international panel.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board of directors unanimously voted to **endorse** the proposed merger, citing significant long-term financial benefits for shareholders.
   董事会一致投票认可拟议的合并方案，并指出这将为股东带来重大的长期财务利益。

2. The celebrity agreed to **endorse** the product only after independent laboratory tests confirmed its safety and effectiveness.
   这位名人在独立实验室测试确认其安全性和有效性后才同意为该产品代言。

3. Please **endorse** the check on the back before depositing it, as the bank requires a valid signature for processing.
   请在支票背面签名后再存入，因为银行需要有效签名才能处理。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "en-dorse" as writing on the "dorsal (back)" side — to endorse originally meant to sign on the back of a document, and it grew to mean giving your backing or support.
把"endorse"想成在"dorsal（背部）"一面书写——endorse最初的意思是在文件背面签名，后来引申为表示支持或认可。
