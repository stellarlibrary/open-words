# 😤 exasperate

🔊 /ɪɡˈzæspəˌreɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To irritate or frustrate intensely; to make a situation or condition worse
**中文：** v. 激怒，使恼怒；加剧，恶化

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- irritate（激怒）
- infuriate（使大怒）
- aggravate（加剧）
- frustrate（使沮丧）
- provoke（激怒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **exasperated by / exasperated with / exasperated tone / become exasperated**

👉 Example: The teacher became exasperated with the students who repeatedly ignored the classroom rules.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The repeated bureaucratic delays **exasperated** the researchers who had been waiting months for approval to begin their clinical trials.
   反复的官僚延误激怒了已等待数月才获批开展临床试验的研究人员。

2. Voters grew increasingly **exasperated** with the administration's inability to address the rising cost of living and stagnant wages.
   选民对政府无力解决生活成本上升和工资停滞的问题越来越恼怒。

3. The diplomat's evasive answers during the press conference **exasperated** journalists seeking clarity on the emerging international crisis.
   这位外交官在新闻发布会上含糊其辞的回答激怒了寻求了解新兴国际危机详情的记者们。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-ASPER-ate" — "asper" means rough in Latin, so to exasperate is to make someone feel rough and irritated inside.
把"exasperate"想成"ex-ASPER-ate"——"asper"在拉丁语中意为粗糙，所以exasperate就是让人内心感到粗糙和恼怒。
