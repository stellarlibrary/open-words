# 😬 awkward

🔊 /ˈɔːkwərd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** Causing difficulty or embarrassment; lacking grace or skill in movement or manner; hard to deal with.

**中文：** 尴尬的；笨拙的；棘手的。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- clumsy（笨拙的）
- embarrassing（令人尴尬的）
- ungainly（不雅的）
- uncomfortable（不自在的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Awkward" can describe physical clumsiness (an awkward movement), social discomfort (an awkward silence), or a difficult situation (an awkward position). Context determines the specific shade of meaning.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **awkward** silence that followed his remark made everyone in the room visibly uncomfortable.
   （他的话之后那段**尴尬的**沉默让房间里的每个人都明显感到不自在。）

2. She made an **awkward** attempt to apologize, but her words only deepened the misunderstanding further.
   （她**笨拙地**试图道歉，但她的话只是进一步加深了误解。）

3. The committee found itself in an **awkward** position when both candidates presented equally compelling arguments.
   （当两位候选人提出同样有说服力的论点时，委员会发现自己处于**棘手的**境地。）

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "awk-ward" as walking in an "awk" (odd) direction — when you move oddly, you look clumsy and the situation becomes embarrassing.

联想"awk-ward"中的"awk"听起来像"哦克"，想象一个人走路姿势奇怪（odd），看起来笨拙，场面非常尴尬。
