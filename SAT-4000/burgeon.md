# 🌱 burgeon

🔊 /ˈbɜːrdʒən/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To begin to grow or flourish rapidly; to sprout. (n.) A bud or new growth
**中文：** vi. 萌芽；快速成长 n. 芽，嫩枝

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flourish（繁荣）
- proliferate（激增）
- thrive（蓬勃发展）
- sprout（发芽）
- blossom（开花，兴旺）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **burgeoning industry / burgeoning population / burgeon into / burgeoning demand**

👉 Example: The technology sector continued to burgeon as venture capital poured into artificial intelligence startups.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. International trade agreements helped the nation's export industry **burgeon** into one of the largest in the region.
   国际贸易协定帮助该国的出口产业迅速发展成为该地区最大的产业之一。

2. Public interest in renewable energy sources has **burgeoned** dramatically over the past decade due to environmental concerns.
   由于环境问题，公众对可再生能源的兴趣在过去十年中急剧增长。

3. New residential neighborhoods began to **burgeon** around the transit hub as the city expanded its rail infrastructure.
   随着城市扩展铁路基础设施，新的住宅区开始在交通枢纽周围迅速涌现。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "burgeon" as "bur-GROW-n" — the word sounds like it contains "grow," and that is exactly what it means: to grow rapidly.
把"burgeon"想成"bur-GROW-n"——这个词听起来像包含"grow"（生长），而这正是它的意思：快速成长。
