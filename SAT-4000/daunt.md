# 😨 daunt

🔊 /dɔːnt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make someone feel intimidated or discouraged; to diminish the courage of
**中文：** v. 使气馁；使畏缩；使沮丧；威吓

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- intimidate（恐吓）
- discourage（使气馁）
- dishearten（使沮丧）
- unnerve（使紧张）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **nothing can daunt / daunted by the challenge / refuse to be daunted / daunted by the prospect**

👉 Example: The sheer scale of the project did not daunt the determined young architect.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The complexity of the mathematical proof did not **daunt** the graduate student, who persisted through months of analysis.
   数学证明的复杂性并没有使这位研究生气馁，她坚持了数月的分析。

2. Many applicants were **daunted** by the rigorous selection process, yet the most resilient candidates ultimately prevailed.
   许多申请者被严格的选拔过程吓倒，但最有韧性的候选人最终脱颖而出。

3. The expedition leader refused to be **daunted** by the treacherous terrain that lay between the camp and the summit.
   探险队长不畏营地与山顶之间险恶的地形，毫不退缩。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "daunt" as rhyming with "haunt" — something that haunts you makes you feel scared and daunted.
把"daunt"想成和"haunt（困扰）"押韵——困扰你的事情让你感到恐惧和气馁。
