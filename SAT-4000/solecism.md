# 📕 solecism

🔊 /ˈsɑːlɪˌsɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A grammatical mistake in speech or writing; a breach of good manners or etiquette; an error or blunder
**中文：** n. 语法错误；失礼

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- blunder（过失）
- gaffe（失态）
- impropriety（不得体）
- barbarism（语病）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **commit a solecism / grammatical solecism / social solecism / obvious solecism**

👉 Example: The diplomat committed a serious social solecism by addressing the ambassador incorrectly at the state dinner.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor identified several **solecisms** in the student's essay and urged more careful attention to grammatical conventions.
   教授在学生的论文中发现了几处语法错误，并敦促更加注意语法规范。

2. Using informal slang in a formal academic paper would be considered a significant **solecism** by most scholarly reviewers.
   在正式学术论文中使用非正式俚语会被大多数学术评审视为严重的语法失当。

3. His failure to acknowledge the host upon arrival was regarded as an unforgivable **solecism** in diplomatic circles.
   他到达时未能向主人致意，在外交圈中被视为不可原谅的失礼行为。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "solecism" as from "Soloi," an ancient Greek colony whose inhabitants spoke a corrupted form of Greek — hence, a language error.
把"solecism"想成来自"Soloi"，一个古希腊殖民地，其居民说的是一种不标准的希腊语——因此指语言错误。
