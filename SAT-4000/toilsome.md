# 😓 toilsome

🔊 /ˈtɔɪlsəm/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Involving hard or fatiguing labor; arduous and demanding great effort or exertion
**中文：** adj. 辛苦的；劳苦的；费力的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- laborious（费力的）
- arduous（艰巨的）
- strenuous（繁重的）
- grueling（累人的）
- onerous（繁重的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **toilsome journey / toilsome labor / toilsome task / toilsome work / toilsome effort**

👉 Example: The migrants endured a toilsome journey across rugged terrain to reach the border safely.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The construction of the transcontinental railroad was a **toilsome** endeavor that claimed thousands of workers' lives.
   横贯大陆铁路的修建是一项艰苦的事业，夺走了数千名工人的生命。

2. Scholars engaged in the **toilsome** process of translating ancient manuscripts that had deteriorated significantly over centuries.
   学者们从事着翻译古代手稿的辛苦工作，这些手稿经过几个世纪已严重老化。

3. Despite the **toilsome** nature of agricultural work, many farmers find profound satisfaction in cultivating the land.
   尽管农业劳动十分辛苦，许多农民仍在耕种土地中找到深刻的满足感。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "toilsome" as "toil + some" — "toil" means hard work, so "toilsome" literally means full of toil, requiring great effort.
把"toilsome"想成"toil（辛劳）+ some（充满的）"——"toil"意为辛苦劳作，所以"toilsome"字面意思就是充满辛劳的，需要极大努力的。
