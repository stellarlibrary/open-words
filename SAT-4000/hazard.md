# ⚠️ hazard

🔊 /ˈhæzərd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A danger or risk; something that could cause harm. (v.) To venture to say or do something risky; to risk
**中文：** n. 危险，冒险；危害 v. 冒险；冒…的危险

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- peril（危险）
- risk（风险）
- threat（威胁）
- jeopardy（危难）
- danger（危险）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **health hazard / fire hazard / hazard a guess / occupational hazard / pose a hazard**

👉 Example: Improper storage of chemicals poses a serious health hazard to laboratory workers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee identified several environmental **hazards** that required immediate remediation before construction could proceed.
   委员会确定了几项需要在施工前立即整治的环境危害。

2. She would not **hazard** a guess about the outcome until all the experimental data had been thoroughly analyzed.
   在所有实验数据被彻底分析之前，她不愿冒险猜测结果。

3. Distracted driving remains one of the most significant **hazards** on modern roadways, contributing to thousands of accidents annually.
   分心驾驶仍然是现代道路上最重大的危险之一，每年导致数千起事故。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "hazard" as "has-hard" — anything that has hard consequences is a hazard you should avoid.
把"hazard"想成"has-hard（有困难的）"——任何带来严重后果的事物都是你应该避免的危险。
