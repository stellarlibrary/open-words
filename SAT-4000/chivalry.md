# 🛡️ chivalry

🔊 /ˈʃɪvəlri/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The medieval knightly system with its religious, moral, and social code; courteous behavior, especially of men toward women; gallantry
**中文：** n. 骑士精神；骑士制度

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gallantry（英勇）
- courtesy（礼貌）
- knighthood（骑士身份）
- valor（勇气）
- nobility（高尚）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **age of chivalry / code of chivalry / act of chivalry / chivalry is dead**

👉 Example: The medieval code of chivalry demanded that knights protect the weak and uphold justice.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The historian's new book examines how the ideals of **chivalry** shaped European social norms during the medieval period.
   这位历史学家的新书考察了骑士精神的理想如何在中世纪塑造了欧洲的社会规范。

2. Many literary scholars argue that Arthurian legends represent the highest expression of **chivalry** in Western literature.
   许多文学学者认为亚瑟王传说代表了西方文学中骑士精神的最高表达。

3. The young officer displayed remarkable **chivalry** by offering his seat and assistance to the elderly passengers aboard.
   这位年轻军官向年长乘客让座并提供帮助，展现了非凡的骑士精神。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "chival-ry" from French "chevalier" (knight on horseback) — chivalry is the code that knights on horses followed.
把"chivalry"追溯到法语"chevalier（骑士）"——骑士精神就是骑马的骑士所遵循的行为准则。
