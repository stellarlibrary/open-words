# 🤒 malinger

🔊 /məˈlɪŋɡər/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To pretend to be ill or otherwise incapacitated in order to avoid duty, work, or other responsibilities
**中文：** v. 装病以逃避职责

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- shirk（逃避）
- feign illness（装病）
- slack off（偷懒）
- dodge（躲避）
- goldbricking（磨洋工）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **malinger to avoid work / accused of malingering / suspected of malingering / malinger on duty**

👉 Example: The sergeant suspected several soldiers were malingering to avoid the dangerous patrol mission.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Military physicians were trained to distinguish between soldiers who were genuinely wounded and those who attempted to **malinger**.
   军医接受过训练，能够区分真正受伤的士兵和试图装病的人。

2. The manager suspected that the employee was **malingering** after noticing a pattern of absences coinciding with major project deadlines.
   经理注意到该员工的缺勤总是与重大项目截止日期重合，因此怀疑其在装病。

3. Insurance investigators were dispatched to determine whether the claimant was disabled or simply **malingering** to collect benefits fraudulently.
   保险调查员被派去确定索赔人是否残疾，还是仅仅在装病以欺诈性地领取福利。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "malinger" as "mal" (bad) + "linger" — someone who lingers around badly, staying in bed pretending to be sick to avoid responsibilities.
把"malinger"想成"mal"（坏的）+"linger"（逗留）——一个赖在床上不起，假装生病以逃避责任的人。
