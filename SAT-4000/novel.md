# 💡 novel

🔊 /ˈnɒvəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) New and original; not like anything seen before; unusual. (n.) A fictitious prose narrative of book length
**中文：** adj. 新奇的；新颖的；异常的 n. 小说

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- innovative（创新的）
- original（独创的）
- unprecedented（前所未有的）
- fresh（新鲜的）
- groundbreaking（开创性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a novel approach / novel idea / novel concept / novel solution / a bestselling novel**

👉 Example: The team proposed a novel approach to solving the persistent water contamination problem.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scientist introduced a **novel** method for detecting genetic mutations that significantly reduced the time required for diagnosis.
   这位科学家提出了一种检测基因突变的新颖方法，大大缩短了诊断所需的时间。

2. Her **novel** interpretation of the classical theory challenged long-held assumptions and generated considerable debate among scholars.
   她对经典理论的新颖解读挑战了长期以来的假设，并在学者中引发了相当大的争论。

3. The architect presented a **novel** design that integrated sustainable materials with advanced engineering to minimize environmental impact.
   这位建筑师展示了一种新颖的设计，将可持续材料与先进工程技术相结合以减少环境影响。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "novel" as "nov" meaning new (like "novice" or "Nova") — a novel idea is a brand-new, never-seen-before idea.
把"novel"想成"nov(新)"——就像"novice(新手)"和"Nova(新星)"一样，novel就是全新的、前所未见的。
