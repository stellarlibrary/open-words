# 🔧 refurbish

🔊 /ˌriːˈfɜːrbɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To renovate, restore, or brighten up; to make clean, bright, or fresh again by repairing or redecorating.
**中文：** v. 刷新；再磨光；翻新

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- renovate（翻新）
- restore（修复）
- overhaul（彻底检修）
- recondition（修整）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **refurbish a building / refurbish equipment / refurbish furniture / refurbished products**

👉 Example: The company plans to refurbish the entire office space before the new employees arrive.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The city council allocated substantial funds to **refurbish** the historic library, preserving its original architectural character.
   市议会拨出大量资金翻新这座历史悠久的图书馆，以保留其原有的建筑特色。

2. Many consumers prefer to purchase **refurbished** electronics because they offer significant savings without compromising on quality.
   许多消费者更喜欢购买翻新电子产品，因为它们在不影响质量的情况下能大幅节省开支。

3. The museum hired skilled artisans to carefully **refurbish** the Renaissance paintings that had deteriorated over several centuries.
   博物馆聘请了技艺精湛的工匠，仔细修复了几个世纪以来已经退化的文艺复兴时期画作。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "refurbish" as "re-FURBISH" — to "furbish" means to polish, so refurbish means to polish something up again, making it like new.
把"refurbish"想成"re-FURBISH"——"furbish"意为擦亮，所以refurbish就是重新擦亮，使之焕然一新。
