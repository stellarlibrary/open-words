# 🕳️ vacuity

🔊 /vəˈkjuːɪti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An empty space; a void; lack of thought or intelligence; intellectual emptiness
**中文：** n. 空虚；空白；思想贫乏

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- emptiness（空虚）
- void（空白）
- blankness（茫然）
- inanity（空洞）
- vacuousness（空虚）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **intellectual vacuity / vacuity of thought / utter vacuity / moral vacuity**

👉 Example: The critic condemned the intellectual vacuity of the popular television series.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor lamented the **vacuity** of the student's essay, which lacked any substantive argument or critical analysis.
   教授对学生文章的空洞感到遗憾，因为其中缺乏任何实质性的论证或批判性分析。

2. Behind the politician's eloquent rhetoric lay a profound **vacuity** that careful observers were quick to identify.
   在这位政客雄辩的辞令背后，隐藏着深刻的思想贫乏，细心的观察者很快就察觉到了。

3. The philosophical treatise explored the existential **vacuity** that modern individuals experience in an increasingly materialistic society.
   这篇哲学论文探讨了现代人在日益物质化的社会中所体验到的存在性空虚。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vacuity" as related to "vacuum" — both share the root "vacu-" meaning empty. A vacuity is an emptiness of the mind.
把"vacuity"想成和"vacuum"（真空）相关——两者共享词根"vacu-"，意思是空的。vacuity就是思想的空虚。
