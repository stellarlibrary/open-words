# 🐑 docile

🔊 /ˈdɑːsaɪl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Ready to accept control or instruction; submissive; easy to manage or handle
**中文：** adj. 温顺的；驯服的；听话的；易管教的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- submissive（顺从的）
- obedient（听话的）
- compliant（顺从的）
- tractable（易驾驭的）
- meek（温顺的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **docile animal / docile temperament / docile nature / surprisingly docile**

👉 Example: The once-wild horse became remarkably docile after months of patient and gentle training by the experienced handler.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The students appeared unusually **docile** during the examination period, sitting quietly and following every instruction without complaint.
   学生们在考试期间表现得异常温顺，安静地坐着，毫无怨言地遵循每一项指示。

2. Authoritarian regimes often seek to create a **docile** population through censorship, propaganda, and the suppression of dissent.
   专制政权往往通过审查、宣传和压制异见来塑造温顺的民众。

3. The veterinarian noted that the large breed was remarkably **docile** despite its intimidating size and powerful physical build.
   兽医指出，尽管这种大型犬体型令人生畏且体格强壮，但它们非常温顺。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "docile" as related to "doctor" — both come from Latin "docere" meaning to teach; a docile person is easy to teach.
把"docile"和"doctor"联系起来——两者都源自拉丁语"docere"（教导）；温顺的人容易被教导。
