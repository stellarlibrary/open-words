# 🎭 backstage

🔊 /ˌbækˈsteɪdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *adverb | 副词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** In or to the area behind the stage in a theater, not visible to the audience; by extension, in private or out of public view.

**中文：** 在后台；在幕后。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- behind the scenes（幕后）
- offstage（台下）
- in the wings（在侧幕）
- privately（私下地）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Backstage" can function as an adverb, adjective, or noun. Beyond its literal theatrical meaning, it is frequently used figuratively to describe secret or private activities, such as "backstage negotiations" or "backstage dealings."

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The director went **backstage** to congratulate the actors on their outstanding and emotionally captivating performance.
   （导演到**后台**祝贺演员们出色而富有情感感染力的表演。）

2. Much of the political decision-making occurred **backstage**, far from the scrutiny of the general public.
   （许多政治决策都是在**幕后**进行的，远离公众的审视。）

3. Journalists were granted rare access **backstage** to observe the elaborate preparations before the grand ceremony began.
   （记者们获得了罕见的**后台**参观机会，观察盛大典礼开始前的精心准备。）

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Simply break it down: "back" + "stage" = the back of the stage, where the audience cannot see. Whatever happens backstage is hidden from public view.

简单拆分："back（后面）" + "stage（舞台）" = 舞台的后面，观众看不到的地方。在后台发生的一切都是隐藏在公众视野之外的。
