# ⚡ oust

🔊 /aʊst/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To drive out or expel someone from a position or place; to deprive of or remove from a role of power.
**中文：** v. 驱逐；剥夺；取代

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- expel（驱逐）
- depose（罢免）
- overthrow（推翻）
- dislodge（赶走）
- supplant（取代）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **oust from power / oust a leader / oust from office / oust the government**

👉 Example: The board voted unanimously to oust the chief executive after a series of financial scandals.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The military coup successfully **ousted** the democratically elected president, plunging the nation into political turmoil.
   军事政变成功驱逐了民选总统，使国家陷入政治动荡。

2. Shareholders organized a campaign to **oust** the chairman, citing persistent mismanagement and declining corporate revenues.
   股东们发起运动罢免董事长，理由是持续的管理不善和公司收入下降。

3. The new technology eventually **ousted** traditional methods of manufacturing, transforming the entire industry within a single decade.
   新技术最终取代了传统的制造方法，在短短十年内改变了整个行业。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "oust" sounding like "out" + "st" — to oust someone is to force them OUT of their position, to push them out the door.
把"oust"联想成"out"（出去）——驱逐某人就是把他们赶出去，推出门外。
