# 🎓 valedictory

🔊 /ˌvælɪˈdɪktəri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or given as a farewell. (n.) A farewell speech, especially one delivered at a graduation ceremony
**中文：** adj. 告别的；辞别的 n. 告别辞；（尤指毕业典礼上的）告别演讲

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- farewell（告别的）
- parting（离别的）
- departing（辞行的）
- concluding（结束的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **valedictory speech / valedictory address / valedictory remarks / valedictory message**

👉 Example: The senior class president delivered a moving valedictory address at commencement.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The retiring professor offered a **valedictory** lecture that reflected on three decades of groundbreaking research in molecular biology.
   即将退休的教授发表了一场告别演讲，回顾了三十年来在分子生物学领域的开创性研究。

2. Her **valedictory** speech urged graduates to pursue knowledge with unwavering determination and intellectual curiosity throughout their careers.
   她的告别辞敦促毕业生在整个职业生涯中以坚定不移的决心和求知欲追求知识。

3. The ambassador's **valedictory** remarks emphasized the enduring diplomatic bonds forged between the two nations over the past decade.
   大使的告别致辞强调了过去十年两国之间建立的持久外交纽带。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vale-dictory" as "vale" (farewell in Latin) + "dictory" (speaking) — speaking farewell words at a ceremony.
把"valedictory"想成"vale"（拉丁语中的再见）+"dictory"（说话）——在仪式上说告别的话。
