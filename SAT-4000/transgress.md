# ⚠️ transgress

🔊 /trænzˈɡrɛs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To violate a law, rule, or code of conduct; to go beyond a boundary or limit; to commit an offense.
**中文：** 违反；侵犯；越界；犯罪。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- violate（违反）
- infringe（侵犯）
- trespass（侵入）
- offend（冒犯）
- breach（违背）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **transgress a law / transgress boundaries / transgress social norms / transgress moral codes**

👉 Example: Those who transgress the established regulations will face severe disciplinary consequences.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat was accused of having **transgressed** international law by authorizing covert surveillance operations on foreign soil.
   该外交官被指控通过授权在外国领土上进行秘密监视行动而违反了国际法。

2. Artists who deliberately **transgress** social norms often provoke meaningful public discourse about cultural values and traditions.
   故意违反社会规范的艺术家往往会引发关于文化价值观和传统的有意义的公共讨论。

3. The corporation **transgressed** environmental regulations by discharging untreated chemical waste into the nearby river system.
   该公司将未经处理的化学废物排入附近的河流系统，违反了环境法规。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "trans-gress" as "step (gress) across (trans)" a boundary — to transgress is to step across the line of what is allowed.
把"transgress"想成"跨过（trans）+ 步伐（gress）"——违规就是跨过允许的界限。
