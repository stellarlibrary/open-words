# 🔈 monotone

🔊 /ˈmɒnətəʊn/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective, verb | 名词, 形容词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A continuous sound that does not change in pitch or tone; a dull, tedious sameness of style or expression. (adj.) Without variation in tone or pitch. (v.) To speak or read in an unchanging tone
**中文：** n. 单调；单音调 adj. 单调的 v. 单调地读

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- drone（嗡嗡声）
- flatness（平淡）
- tedium（单调乏味）
- uniformity（一致性）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **speak in a monotone / dull monotone / monotone voice / monotone delivery**

👉 Example: The lecturer spoke in such a monotone that several students struggled to remain attentive.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor delivered the entire two-hour lecture in a flat **monotone**, causing half the audience to lose concentration entirely.
   教授用平淡的单调语气讲完了整整两个小时的课，导致一半听众完全无法集中注意力。

2. The robot's **monotone** voice lacked any emotional inflection, making it difficult to distinguish between urgent and routine announcements.
   机器人单调的声音缺乏任何情感变化，使人难以区分紧急通知和常规通知。

3. She deliberately adopted a calm **monotone** during negotiations to conceal her frustration and maintain an appearance of composure.
   她在谈判中刻意采用平静的单调语气，以掩饰自己的沮丧并保持沉着的外表。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mono" (one) + "tone" (sound) — a monotone is literally "one tone," a single unchanging sound with no variation.
把"monotone"拆成"mono"（一个）+"tone"（音调）——单调就是"一个音调"，毫无变化的声音。
