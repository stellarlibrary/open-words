# 😐 banal

🔊 /bəˈnɑːl/<br>
🏷️ *adj. | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** So lacking in originality as to be obvious and boring; trite, commonplace, and unimaginative.

**中文：** 陈腐的；平庸的；老套的；缺乏新意的，乏味的。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- trite（陈腐的）
- hackneyed（老套的）
- mundane（平凡的）
- commonplace（平淡无奇的）
- pedestrian（平庸的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Banal" is a somewhat formal word used to criticize something for being unoriginal or dull. It is often applied to remarks, ideas, conversations, or artistic works. Note the pronunciation: in American English it is commonly stressed on the second syllable (/bəˈnɑːl/ or /bəˈnæl/), not the first.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The critic dismissed the film as **banal** and predictable, offering nothing that audiences had not already seen countless times.
   评论家认为这部电影**平庸**且可预测，没有提供观众未曾多次见过的任何东西。

2. His speech was filled with **banal** platitudes about hard work and perseverance that failed to inspire the graduating class.
   他的演讲充满了关于努力工作和坚持不懈的**陈腐**陈词滥调，未能激励毕业班的学生。

3. What began as a promising philosophical discussion quickly devolved into a **banal** exchange of superficial opinions and cliches.
   原本很有前景的哲学讨论很快沦为**平庸的**肤浅意见和陈词滥调的交换。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Banal" sounds like "ba-NAL" — think "ban all" originality! Something banal has banned all creativity and freshness, leaving only dull, overused ideas.

"Banal"听起来像"ban all（禁止所有）"——想象禁止了所有的原创性！某样东西如果是banal的，就等于禁绝了一切创意和新鲜感，只剩下枯燥和老套的想法。
