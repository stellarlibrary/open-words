# 🎭 melodrama

🔊 /ˈmɛləˌdrɑːmə/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A dramatic work characterized by exaggerated emotions, sensational events, and stereotypical characters; any situation or behavior that is overly theatrical or emotionally exaggerated.
**中文：** n. 情节剧；音乐剧；耸人听闻的事件，闹剧

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- theatrics（夸张表演）
- histrionics（装腔作势）
- spectacle（戏剧性场面）
- farce（闹剧）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **pure melodrama / descend into melodrama / political melodrama / sheer melodrama**

👉 Example: The debate quickly descended into melodrama when the candidates started trading personal insults.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics dismissed the film as pure **melodrama**, arguing that its emotional scenes lacked subtlety and genuine depth.
   评论家将这部电影斥为纯粹的情节剧，认为其情感场面缺乏细腻和真实的深度。

2. The courtroom proceedings turned into a **melodrama** when the defendant collapsed dramatically upon hearing the verdict.
   当被告在听到判决后戏剧性地瘫倒时，法庭审理变成了一场闹剧。

3. Victorian literature often embraced **melodrama** as a narrative device to explore themes of morality and social injustice.
   维多利亚时代的文学常常以情节剧作为叙事手法来探讨道德和社会不公的主题。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "melo-drama" as "melody + drama" — a drama so exaggerated it needs background music to match its over-the-top emotions.
把"melodrama"想成"melody（旋律）+ drama（戏剧）"——一出夸张到需要配乐来衬托其过度情感的戏剧。
