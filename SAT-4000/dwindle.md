# 📉 dwindle

🔊 /ˈdwɪndl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To gradually become smaller, fewer, or less; to shrink or diminish progressively over time
**中文：** v. 减少；缩小；衰退

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- diminish（减少）
- shrink（缩小）
- wane（衰退）
- decline（下降）
- taper off（逐渐减少）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dwindle to nothing / dwindle away / dwindling resources / dwindling population**

👉 Example: The town's population has dwindled steadily over the past two decades as young people move away.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Public enthusiasm for the new policy began to **dwindle** as citizens realized its limited practical benefits.
   随着公民意识到该新政策的实际益处有限，公众的热情开始减退。

2. The river's water supply continued to **dwindle** throughout the prolonged drought, threatening regional agricultural production.
   在持续干旱期间，河流的水量不断减少，威胁着该地区的农业生产。

3. Financial reserves **dwindled** rapidly after the organization expanded operations without securing additional sources of funding.
   在该机构未获得额外资金来源就扩大运营后，财务储备迅速减少。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dwindle" as "d-wind-le" — like the wind slowly dying down, everything gradually becomes less and less.
把"dwindle"想成"d-wind-le"——就像风慢慢减弱，一切都逐渐变少。
