# 🎵 resonate

🔊 /ˈrɛzəˌneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To produce or be filled with a deep, reverberating sound; to evoke a feeling of shared emotion or belief in someone
**中文：** v. 共鸣，共振；引起反响

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reverberate（回荡）
- echo（回响）
- vibrate（振动）
- strike a chord（引起共鸣）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **resonate with someone / resonate deeply / resonate throughout / resonate among audiences / resonate across cultures**

👉 Example: The speaker's message about equality resonated with people from all walks of life.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's lecture on social inequality continued to **resonate** with students long after the semester had officially concluded.
   教授关于社会不平等的讲座在学期正式结束后很久仍在学生中引起共鸣。

2. Her passionate advocacy for education reform began to **resonate** throughout the nation as more citizens recognized the urgency of change.
   随着越来越多的公民认识到变革的紧迫性，她对教育改革的热情倡导开始在全国引起反响。

3. The chamber hall was designed so that every musical note would **resonate** clearly, creating an immersive auditory experience for the audience.
   这座室内音乐厅的设计使每个音符都能清晰共振，为听众创造沉浸式的听觉体验。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "resonate" as "re-so-nate" — a sound so powerful that it plays again (re) and so (so) naturally (nate), echoing on and on.
把"resonate"想成"re-so-nate"——一种如此强大的声音，自然而然地反复回响，余音绕梁。
