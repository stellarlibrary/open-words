# ⚖️ infringe

🔊 /ɪnˈfrɪndʒ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To actively break the terms of a law, agreement, or right; to encroach or trespass upon something
**中文：** v. 侵犯；违反；破坏

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- violate（违反）
- breach（违背）
- transgress（逾越）
- encroach（侵占）
- trespass（侵犯）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **infringe on/upon rights / infringe a patent / infringe copyright / infringe the law**

👉 Example: The new regulation may infringe upon the constitutional rights of ordinary citizens.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court ruled that the company's product did **infringe** upon the competitor's patented technology design.
   法院裁定该公司的产品确实侵犯了竞争对手的专利技术设计。

2. Critics argued that the surveillance program would **infringe** on the fundamental privacy rights of millions of citizens.
   批评人士认为该监控计划将侵犯数百万公民的基本隐私权。

3. Any attempt to **infringe** upon academic freedom undermines the integrity of educational institutions and scholarly research.
   任何侵犯学术自由的企图都会损害教育机构和学术研究的完整性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-FRINGE" — when you step into the fringe (edge) of someone's territory, you are infringing on their space.
把"infringe"想成"in + fringe（边缘）"——当你踏入别人领地的边缘时，你就在侵犯他们的空间。
