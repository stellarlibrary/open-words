# 🧪 gel

🔊 /dʒɛl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A semisolid, jellylike substance; a colloid. (v.) To form or become a gel; to solidify or take definite shape
**中文：** n. 凝胶；胶体 v. 胶化；成形

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- jelly（果冻状物）
- colloid（胶体）
- solidify（凝固）
- coagulate（凝结）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **hair gel / gel formation / gel electrophoresis / ideas begin to gel / plans gel**

👉 Example: After weeks of brainstorming, the team's strategy finally began to gel into a coherent plan.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The research team used **gel** electrophoresis to separate DNA fragments according to their molecular size and electrical charge.
   研究团队使用凝胶电泳根据分子大小和电荷分离DNA片段。

2. After several rounds of discussion, the committee's proposals began to **gel** into a unified and actionable policy framework.
   经过几轮讨论，委员会的提案开始凝聚成一个统一且可操作的政策框架。

3. The pharmaceutical company developed a new topical **gel** that delivers medication more effectively through the dermal layers of skin.
   这家制药公司开发了一种新型外用凝胶，能更有效地通过皮肤真皮层输送药物。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gel" as related to "jelly" — both are semisolid substances that hold their shape, and ideas "gel" when they solidify into form.
把"gel"与"jelly"（果冻）联系起来——两者都是能保持形状的半固体物质，想法"gel"就是它们凝固成形。
