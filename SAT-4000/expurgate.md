# ✂️ expurgate

🔊 /ˈɛkspərˌɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To remove objectionable or offensive material from a text or account; to censor or purify written content
**中文：** v. 删除，删去（不当内容）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- censor（审查删除）
- bowdlerize（删改）
- purge（清除）
- sanitize（净化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **expurgate a text / expurgate offensive passages / expurgated edition / expurgate from the record**

👉 Example: The editor was tasked with expurgating all profane language from the manuscript before publication.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The publisher decided to **expurgate** several controversial chapters from the novel to make it suitable for younger audiences.
   出版商决定删除小说中几个有争议的章节，使其适合年轻读者阅读。

2. Government officials sought to **expurgate** sensitive intelligence details from the report before releasing it to the public.
   政府官员试图在向公众发布报告之前删除其中敏感的情报细节。

3. Literary scholars have long debated whether it is ethical to **expurgate** classical works to conform to modern sensibilities.
   文学学者长期以来一直在争论，为迎合现代观念而删改古典作品是否合乎道德。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-purge-ate" — to purge something out, removing the impure or offensive parts from a text.
把"expurgate"想成"ex(出)+purge(清除)+ate"——把不纯的、令人反感的部分清除出去，就是删除。
