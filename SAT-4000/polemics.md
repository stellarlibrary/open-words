# 🗣️ polemics

🔊 /pəˈlemɪks/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The art or practice of vigorous argumentation or debate; contentious rhetoric used to defend a position or refute an opposing view
**中文：** n. 辩论术；论证法；论战

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- argumentation（论证）
- disputation（辩论）
- controversy（争论）
- rhetoric（修辞术）
- debate（辩论）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **engage in polemics / political polemics / sharp polemics / the art of polemics**

👉 Example: The professor warned students against confusing rigorous academic debate with mere polemics.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The editorial descended into bitter **polemics** rather than offering a balanced analysis of the economic policy under discussion.
   这篇社论沦为激烈的论战，而非对所讨论的经济政策提供平衡的分析。

2. Throughout history, religious **polemics** have shaped theological discourse and influenced the formation of distinct doctrinal traditions.
   纵观历史，宗教论战塑造了神学话语，并影响了不同教义传统的形成。

3. The candidate's speech was dismissed by analysts as empty **polemics** designed to inflame passions rather than address substantive issues.
   分析人士认为该候选人的演讲不过是空洞的论战，旨在煽动情绪而非解决实质问题。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "polemics" as "pole-mics" — two people at opposite poles, each holding microphones, arguing fiercely with each other.
把"polemics"想成"pole-mics（两极的麦克风）"——两个人站在两极，各持麦克风激烈辩论。
