# 😤 foolhardy

🔊 /ˈfuːlˌhɑːrdi/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Recklessly bold or rash; showing courage but lacking good judgment or caution
**中文：** adj. 有勇无谋的；蛮干的；鲁莽的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reckless（鲁莽的）
- rash（轻率的）
- imprudent（不明智的）
- audacious（大胆的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **foolhardy attempt / foolhardy decision / foolhardy venture / it would be foolhardy to...**

👉 Example: It would be foolhardy to climb the mountain without proper gear and training.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The explorer's **foolhardy** decision to cross the desert without adequate water supplies nearly cost him his life.
   探险家鲁莽地决定在没有足够水源的情况下穿越沙漠，几乎让他丧命。

2. Critics described the company's **foolhardy** expansion into untested markets as a grave strategic miscalculation.
   批评人士将该公司贸然向未经测试的市场扩张描述为严重的战略误判。

3. Only a **foolhardy** investor would commit substantial capital to a venture without conducting thorough due diligence.
   只有有勇无谋的投资者才会在没有进行充分尽职调查的情况下将大量资金投入一项风险投资。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fool + hardy" — a fool who is hardy (bold) but lacks wisdom, charging ahead without thinking.
把"foolhardy"想成"fool + hardy"——一个大胆的傻瓜，有勇气但缺乏智慧，不假思索地冲向前。
