# ⚡ bounce

🔊 /baʊns/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A rebound; springiness or elasticity; energy and liveliness. (v.) To spring back after hitting a surface; to cause something to rebound
**中文：** n. 跳；弹力；活力 v. （使）弹起，（使）反弹，（使）弹跳

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rebound（反弹）
- spring（弹起）
- ricochet（弹跳）
- leap（跳跃）
- recoil（回弹）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bounce back / bounce off / bounce an idea / bounce a check / put a bounce in one's step**

👉 Example: The economy is expected to bounce back strongly after the temporary downturn caused by the global crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The resilient economy managed to **bounce** back remarkably quickly after the severe recession that had gripped the nation for months.
   这个富有韧性的经济体在困扰全国数月的严重衰退之后设法实现了显著的快速反弹。

2. Scientists studied how light waves **bounce** off various surfaces to develop more efficient solar panel designs for renewable energy systems.
   科学家研究了光波如何从不同表面反弹，以开发更高效的可再生能源系统太阳能板设计。

3. The board members decided to **bounce** several innovative ideas off one another before presenting the final proposal to investors.
   董事会成员决定相互交流几个创新想法，然后再向投资者提交最终方案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bounce" as the sound a ball makes — "bounce, bounce" — when it hits the ground and springs back up with energy.
把"bounce"想成球落地时发出的声音——"弹，弹"——当球撞击地面又充满活力地弹起来时。
