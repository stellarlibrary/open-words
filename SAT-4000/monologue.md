# 🎭 monologue

🔊 /ˈmɒnəlɒɡ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A long speech by one person, especially in a play or performance; an extended, uninterrupted discourse delivered by a single speaker
**中文：** n. 独白；独角戏；长篇大论

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- soliloquy（自言自语）
- speech（演讲）
- discourse（论述）
- tirade（长篇大论）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **deliver a monologue / dramatic monologue / opening monologue / interior monologue**

👉 Example: The actor delivered a powerful monologue that captivated every member of the audience.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The playwright crafted a compelling **monologue** for the lead character that revealed her deepest fears and hidden motivations.
   剧作家为主角精心编写了一段引人入胜的独白，揭示了她最深层的恐惧和隐藏的动机。

2. The talk show host opened each episode with a humorous **monologue** addressing the most prominent news events of the week.
   脱口秀主持人在每集开头以幽默的独白评论当周最突出的新闻事件。

3. Critics praised the film's final **monologue** as a masterful piece of writing that encapsulated the story's central philosophical theme.
   评论家称赞该电影最后的独白是一段精妙的创作，浓缩了故事的核心哲学主题。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mono" (one) + "logue" (speech) — a monologue is a speech by one person, just one voice speaking alone on stage.
把"monologue"拆成"mono"（一个）+"logue"（说话）——独白就是"一个人的讲话"，舞台上只有一个声音。
