# 🚀 momentum

🔊 /moʊˈmɛntəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The force or speed of movement; the impetus gained by a moving object; in physics, the product of mass and velocity; figuratively, driving force or buildup of progress
**中文：** n. 势头；动量；动力；冲力

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- impetus（推动力）
- thrust（冲力）
- drive（驱动力）
- force（力量）
- inertia（惯性）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **gain momentum / lose momentum / build momentum / political momentum / angular momentum**

👉 Example: The reform movement gained considerable momentum after several influential organizations publicly endorsed the initiative.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The social justice campaign rapidly gained **momentum** as thousands of supporters joined the movement through organized demonstrations and online advocacy.
   随着数千名支持者通过组织示威和网络宣传加入运动，这场社会正义运动迅速获得了势头。

2. In classical mechanics, **momentum** is defined as the product of an object's mass and its velocity in a given direction.
   在经典力学中，动量被定义为物体质量与其在给定方向上速度的乘积。

3. The startup struggled to maintain its initial **momentum** after key investors withdrew their financial support during the economic downturn.
   在经济低迷期间关键投资者撤回资金支持后，这家初创企业难以维持其最初的发展势头。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "moment-um" as "moment + um (force)" — the force that builds up over moments of time, like a rolling boulder that becomes harder to stop.
把"momentum"想成"moment（时刻）+ um（力）"——随着时间推移积聚起来的力量，就像滚动的巨石越来越难以停止。
