# 🤝 colloquy

🔊 /ˈkɒl.ə.kwi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A formal conversation or dialogue; a written work structured as a dialogue between two or more speakers.

**中文：** 谈话，会话；以对话体写的文章。指正式的对话或讨论，也指以两人或多人对话形式构成的文学作品。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- dialogue（对话）
- conversation（交谈）
- discourse（论述）
- discussion（讨论）
- conference（会谈）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Collocation:** "engage in colloquy / a colloquy between / scholarly colloquy" — typically used in formal or literary contexts to describe a serious exchange of ideas.

**Example:** The senators engaged in a lengthy colloquy on the proposed amendments to the legislation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosophical **colloquy** between the two scholars was published as a seminal text in the field of ethical theory.
   两位学者之间的哲学**对话**作为伦理学领域的开创性文本出版。

2. During the legislative session, a spirited **colloquy** arose between the committee chair and several dissenting members.
   在立法会议期间，委员会主席与几位持异议的成员之间进行了一场激烈的**讨论**。

3. The Renaissance author composed an elegant **colloquy** that explored the competing virtues of contemplative and active life.
   这位文艺复兴时期的作者创作了一篇优雅的**对话体文章**，探讨了沉思生活与积极生活之间相互竞争的美德。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

**English:** From Latin *colloquium* — "col-" (together) + "loqui" (to speak). People speaking together, hence a formal conversation or dialogue.

**中文：** 来自拉丁语 *colloquium*——"col-"（一起）+ "loqui"（说话）。人们一起说话，即正式的"谈话、会话"。
