# 🌈 refraction

🔊 /rɪˈfrækʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The bending of light, sound, or other waves as they pass from one medium into another of different density; the change in direction of a propagating wave
**中文：** n. 折射；折光

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bending（弯折）
- deflection（偏转）
- diffraction（衍射）
- dispersion（色散）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **refraction of light / index of refraction / atmospheric refraction / angle of refraction**

👉 Example: The refraction of light through the prism revealed the full visible spectrum of wavelengths.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **refraction** of starlight through the Earth's atmosphere causes celestial objects near the horizon to appear slightly displaced.
   星光通过地球大气层的折射导致地平线附近的天体看起来略有偏移。

2. Students conducted an experiment measuring the index of **refraction** for various transparent materials including glass, water, and diamond.
   学生们进行了一项实验，测量了包括玻璃、水和钻石在内的各种透明材料的折射率。

3. The phenomenon of **refraction** explains why submerged objects in water appear to be at a different position than their actual location.
   折射现象解释了为什么水中浸没的物体看起来位于与其实际位置不同的地方。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "refraction" as "re-fraction" — light gets "fractioned" or split into a new angle, like a fraction breaking away from the original path.
把"refraction"想成"re-fraction"（重新分割）——光被"分割"成新的角度，就像从原来的路径中分离出来。
