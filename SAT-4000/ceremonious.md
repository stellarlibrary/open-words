# 🎭 ceremonious

🔊 /ˌserəˈmoʊniəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Excessively polite or formal; characterized by elaborate ceremony, pomp, and strict adherence to protocol
**中文：** adj. 隆重的；讲究仪式的；正式的；过分客气的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- formal（正式的）
- stately（庄严的）
- punctilious（一丝不苟的）
- solemn（隆重的）
- dignified（有尊严的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ceremonious manner / ceremonious greeting / ceremonious bow / ceremonious occasion**

👉 Example: The ambassador received each guest with a ceremonious bow and a carefully rehearsed greeting.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat's **ceremonious** manner impressed the foreign delegates, who appreciated his meticulous attention to protocol and etiquette.
   这位外交官隆重的举止给外国代表留下了深刻印象，他们欣赏他对礼仪规范的细致关注。

2. The royal court maintained a highly **ceremonious** atmosphere in which every gesture and word carried symbolic significance.
   王室宫廷保持着高度隆重的气氛，其中每一个手势和话语都具有象征意义。

3. Critics found his **ceremonious** speech to be excessively ornate and formal, lacking the sincerity the occasion demanded.
   评论家认为他讲究排场的演讲过于华丽和正式，缺乏这一场合所需要的真诚。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ceremonious" as "ceremony + -ous (full of)" — someone ceremonious is full of ceremony, excessively formal and elaborate in manner.
把"ceremonious"想成"ceremony（仪式）+ -ous（充满）"——一个ceremonious的人充满仪式感，行为举止过分正式和隆重。
