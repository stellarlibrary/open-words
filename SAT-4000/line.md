# 📏 line

🔊 /laɪn/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A long narrow mark or band; a route or course; a row of people or things. (v.) To arrange in a row; to mark with lines.
**中文：** n. 路线；排；绳；直线；线段 v. 排成一行；划线于

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- row（排）
- queue（队列）
- route（路线）
- stripe（条纹）
- boundary（界线）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **draw the line / line up / in line with / along the lines of / bottom line**

👉 Example: The committee's recommendations were entirely in line with the organization's strategic objectives.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government drew a clear **line** between acceptable regulatory oversight and excessive interference in private enterprise operations.
   政府在可接受的监管和对私营企业运营的过度干预之间划了一条明确的界线。

2. Hundreds of applicants formed a long **line** outside the admissions office, each hoping to secure a place in the program.
   数百名申请者在招生办公室外排起了长队，每人都希望在该项目中获得一席之地。

3. The professor asked students to **line** up their arguments logically before presenting their thesis in the final seminar.
   教授要求学生在最终研讨会上陈述论文之前将论点进行逻辑排列。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "line" as the simplest geometric shape — connect two points and you get a line, the foundation of all structure.
把"line"想成最简单的几何形状——连接两点就得到一条线，它是所有结构的基础。
