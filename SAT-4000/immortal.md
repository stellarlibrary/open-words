# ✨ immortal

🔊 /ɪˈmɔːrtl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Living forever; never dying or decaying. Deserving to be remembered forever. (n.) A being that lives forever, such as a god or deity.
**中文：** adj. 不朽的；不死的 n. 神仙；不朽的人物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- eternal（永恒的）
- everlasting（永久的）
- undying（不灭的）
- imperishable（不朽的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **immortal soul / immortal fame / immortal legacy / immortal words**

👉 Example: Shakespeare's plays have earned him immortal status in the canon of English literature.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient Greeks believed that the gods on Mount Olympus were **immortal** beings who governed every aspect of human existence.
   古希腊人相信奥林匹斯山上的众神是掌管人类生活方方面面的不朽存在。

2. Her contributions to the field of theoretical physics have secured her an **immortal** place in the history of modern science.
   她在理论物理学领域的贡献为她在现代科学史上赢得了不朽的地位。

3. The philosopher questioned whether an **immortal** existence would ultimately prove to be a blessing or an unbearable curse.
   这位哲学家质疑永生最终究竟是一种祝福还是一种难以忍受的诅咒。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-mortal" as "not mortal" — if you are not mortal (subject to death), then you live forever.
把"immortal"想成"im（不）+ mortal（会死的）"——不会死的，所以是不朽的、永生的。
