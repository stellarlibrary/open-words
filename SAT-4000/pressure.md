# 💨 pressure

🔊 /ˈprɛʃər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The force exerted on a surface; a feeling of stress or urgency. (v.) To persuade or coerce someone into doing something
**中文：** n. 压力；压迫；压强 v. 迫使；对……施加压力

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- stress（压力）
- coercion（强迫）
- tension（紧张）
- compulsion（强制）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **under pressure / put pressure on / peer pressure / pressure someone into**

👉 Example: The board put immense pressure on the CEO to resign after the financial scandal became public.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Students often experience intense academic **pressure** during college admissions season, which can affect their mental health.
   学生在大学申请季往往承受巨大的学业压力，这可能影响他们的心理健康。

2. The attorney attempted to **pressure** the witness into retracting her testimony before the case went to trial.
   律师试图迫使证人在案件开庭前撤回她的证词。

3. Atmospheric **pressure** decreases significantly at higher altitudes, making it more difficult for climbers to breathe normally.
   大气压强在较高海拔处会显著降低，使得登山者更难以正常呼吸。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "press-ure" as something that "presses" down on you — like a heavy weight pushing you to act or feel stressed.
把"pressure"想成有东西在"press（压）"着你——就像一块重物压在你身上，迫使你行动或感到紧张。
