# 🌵 prickle

🔊 /ˈprɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A small, sharp point or thorn on a plant; a tingling or stinging sensation on the skin. (v.) To prick or sting; to cause a tingling sensation
**中文：** n. 刺；刺痛；植物的皮刺 v. 针一般地刺；戳；使感到刺痛

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- thorn（刺）
- spine（棘刺）
- barb（倒钩）
- sting（刺痛）
- tingle（刺痛感）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prickle of fear / prickle on the skin / prickle with anxiety / feel a prickle**

👉 Example: She felt a prickle of unease as the stranger approached her in the dimly lit corridor.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. A sharp **prickle** of fear ran down her spine when she heard the unexpected noise echoing through the hallway.
   当她听到走廊里传来意外的声响时，一阵尖锐的恐惧刺痛感沿着脊背蔓延开来。

2. The rose stems were covered in tiny **prickles** that scratched her fingers as she carefully arranged the bouquet.
   玫瑰的茎上布满了细小的刺，当她小心地整理花束时，这些刺划伤了她的手指。

3. His skin began to **prickle** with discomfort as the harsh winter wind swept across the frozen landscape.
   当凛冽的寒风席卷冰冻的大地时，他的皮肤开始感到一阵刺痛的不适。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "prickle" as "prick" + "le" — a little prick, like a tiny thorn poking your skin and making it tingle.
把"prickle"想成"prick（刺）"+"le（小的）"——一个小小的刺，就像一根小刺扎进皮肤让你感到刺痛。
