# 🚀 eject

🔊 /ɪˈdʒɛkt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To force or throw out; to expel or discharge; to drive out from a place or position
**中文：** v. 喷射；排出；驱逐；弹出

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- expel（驱逐）
- discharge（排出）
- oust（赶出）
- evict（驱逐）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **eject from a vehicle / eject a disc / eject a passenger / eject someone from a venue**

👉 Example: The pilot was forced to eject from the aircraft moments before it crashed into the terrain.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The referee chose to **eject** the player from the match after a series of deliberate and unsportsmanlike fouls.
   裁判在该球员连续故意犯规和非体育行为后决定将其驱逐出比赛。

2. The volcanic eruption **ejected** massive quantities of ash and debris into the upper layers of the atmosphere.
   火山喷发将大量火山灰和碎片喷射到大气层上层。

3. Security personnel were instructed to **eject** any unauthorized individuals found within the restricted government facility.
   安保人员被指示驱逐在政府限制区域内发现的任何未经授权的人员。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "e-JECT" — "ject" means "to throw" in Latin (like project, inject); eject means to throw something out.
把"eject"想成"e-JECT"——"ject"在拉丁语中意为"扔"（如project、inject）；eject就是把东西扔出去。
