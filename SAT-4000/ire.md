# 🔥 ire

🔊 /ˈaɪər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Intense anger; wrath. (v.) To make angry; to enrage
**中文：** n. 忿怒；愤怒 vt. 使发怒

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- wrath（愤怒）
- fury（狂怒）
- indignation（愤慨）
- rage（暴怒）
- anger（怒气）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **draw the ire of / raise the ire of / provoke ire / public ire / righteous ire**

👉 Example: The politician's controversial remarks drew the ire of citizens and advocacy groups across the country.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The proposed budget cuts drew the **ire** of educators who argued that reduced funding would severely harm public schools.
   拟议的预算削减引起了教育工作者的愤怒，他们认为减少资金将严重损害公立学校。

2. The corporation's decision to raise prices during a national crisis provoked widespread public **ire** and calls for regulation.
   该公司在国家危机期间涨价的决定引发了广泛的公众愤怒和监管呼声。

3. His dismissive tone during the debate managed to **ire** even his most loyal supporters, who expected greater diplomacy.
   他在辩论中轻蔑的语气甚至激怒了他最忠实的支持者，他们期望他表现出更多的外交风度。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "IRE" — it looks and sounds like "fire" without the "f." When you feel ire, there is a fire of anger burning inside you.
把"ire"想成去掉"f"的"fire"（火）。当你感到ire时，内心有一团愤怒之火在燃烧。
