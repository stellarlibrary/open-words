# 🎖️ induct

🔊 /ɪnˈdʌkt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To formally admit someone to a position or organization; to enroll in military service; to introduce or initiate; to produce by induction
**中文：** v. 引导；使就职；征召入伍；感应

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- install（就任）
- initiate（引入）
- enlist（征召）
- inaugurate（就职）
- enroll（登记）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **induct into the Hall of Fame / induct into service / induct new members / induct into office**

👉 Example: The academy will induct three distinguished scientists into its honorary fellowship this autumn.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The organization plans to **induct** twelve exceptional athletes into the National Sports Hall of Fame during the annual ceremony.
   该组织计划在年度典礼上将十二位杰出运动员引入国家体育名人堂。

2. During the wartime mobilization, the government moved swiftly to **induct** thousands of young citizens into compulsory military service.
   在战时动员期间，政府迅速行动，将数千名年轻公民征召入伍。

3. The department chair personally **inducted** each new faculty member, outlining institutional protocols and academic expectations in thorough detail.
   系主任亲自引导每位新教职员工，详细介绍了机构规章制度和学术要求。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-DUCT" as "lead into a duct (channel)" — to induct someone is to channel them into a new role or organization.
把"induct"想成"in(进入)+duct(管道)"——把某人引入一个新角色或组织，就像引导他们进入一个通道。
