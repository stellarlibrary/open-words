# 💡 posit

🔊 /ˈpɑːzɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To put forward as a basis for argument; to assume as fact; to place or position. (n.) A hypothesis or assumption
**中文：** v. 安置；假定 n. 假设；设想

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- propose（提议）
- postulate（假设）
- hypothesize（假定）
- assume（假设）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **posit a theory / posit that / posit the existence of / posit an explanation**

👉 Example: The researcher posited that environmental factors play a more significant role than genetics in this case.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Several prominent scholars have **posited** that the decline of the empire was primarily caused by internal economic instability.
   几位著名学者假定该帝国的衰落主要是由内部经济不稳定造成的。

2. The philosopher **posited** the existence of innate moral principles that are universal across all human cultures.
   这位哲学家假设存在一种跨越所有人类文化的先天道德原则。

3. In her latest publication, the scientist **posited** a new framework for understanding the relationship between climate and migration.
   在她最新的出版物中，这位科学家提出了一个理解气候与移民关系的新框架。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "posit" as "position it" — when you posit something, you position an idea as a starting point for discussion.
把"posit"想成"position it"——当你posit某事时，你把一个想法摆放在讨论的起点上。
