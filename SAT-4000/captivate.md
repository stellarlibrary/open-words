# ✨ captivate

🔊 /ˈkæptɪveɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To attract and hold the attention or interest of someone; to charm or enchant irresistibly
**中文：** v. 迷住，迷惑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- enchant（使着迷）
- fascinate（迷住）
- mesmerize（使入迷）
- enthrall（使着迷）
- charm（吸引）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **captivate an audience / captivate the imagination / captivated by / completely captivated**

👉 Example: The keynote speaker captivated the audience with her compelling narrative and vivid illustrations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The documentary **captivated** millions of viewers worldwide with its breathtaking cinematography and powerful personal narratives.
   这部纪录片以其令人叹为观止的摄影和有力的个人叙事迷住了全世界数百万观众。

2. Renaissance art continues to **captivate** scholars who study the intricate techniques and symbolic depth of the masterworks.
   文艺复兴时期的艺术继续迷住那些研究杰作精湛技法和象征深度的学者们。

3. The young pianist **captivated** the entire concert hall with a performance of extraordinary emotional intensity and precision.
   这位年轻的钢琴家以非凡的情感强度和精准度的演出迷住了整个音乐厅。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "captivate" as "captive + ate" — when something captivates you, it takes you captive and devours your full attention.
把"captivate"想成"captive + ate"——当某事迷住你时，它把你变成了"俘虏"，吞噬了你全部的注意力。
