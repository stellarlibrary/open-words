# 🤓 pedant

🔊 /ˈpedənt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who is excessively concerned with minor details, rules, or formal accuracy, especially in teaching or scholarship
**中文：** n. 学究；书呆子；卖弄学问的人；空谈家

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- purist（纯粹主义者）
- perfectionist（完美主义者）
- doctrinaire（教条主义者）
- nitpicker（吹毛求疵者）
- stickler（一丝不苟的人）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **insufferable pedant / grammar pedant / intellectual pedant / dismissed as a pedant**

👉 Example: His colleagues considered him a tiresome pedant who would correct every trivial grammatical error in their reports.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The literary critic was dismissed as a mere **pedant** who focused on obscure stylistic details rather than the work's deeper meaning.
   这位文学评论家被视为一个只关注晦涩文体细节而忽视作品深层含义的学究。

2. Only a true **pedant** would interrupt a keynote address to quibble over the speaker's pronunciation of a single technical term.
   只有一个真正的书呆子才会打断主旨演讲，去纠结演讲者对一个技术术语的发音。

3. The professor, though brilliant in her field, was often regarded as a **pedant** for her insistence on rigid adherence to formatting rules.
   这位教授虽然在其领域才华横溢，但因坚持严格遵守格式规范而常被视为学究。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pedant" as a "ped-ant" — an ant obsessed with every tiny crumb, just as a pedant obsesses over every tiny detail.
把"pedant"想成"ped-ant"——一只对每一粒碎屑都执着的蚂蚁，就像学究对每一个细节都斤斤计较一样。
