# 🔮 anticipate

🔊 /ænˈtɪsɪpeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** To expect or predict something; to act in advance of something; to look forward to something or to deal with something before it happens.

**中文：** 预期，期望；预料；占先，提前处理。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- expect 期望
- foresee 预见
- predict 预测
- envisage 设想
- foretell 预言

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

Common collocations: **anticipate a problem**, **anticipate the needs of**, **widely anticipated**, **eagerly anticipated**, **anticipate that (+ clause)**. Often used in both formal and everyday contexts.

> Example: Analysts **anticipate** a significant rise in consumer spending this quarter.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government failed to **anticipate** the severity of the economic downturn and was unprepared for its consequences.
   政府未能预见到经济衰退的严重性，对其后果毫无准备。

2. Experienced managers learn to **anticipate** potential obstacles well before they disrupt ongoing operations.
   有经验的管理者会学会在潜在障碍干扰正常运营之前就提前预见到它们。

3. We eagerly **anticipate** the publication of her new novel, which critics have already praised enthusiastically.
   我们热切期待她新小说的出版，评论家们已经对其赞不绝口。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "anti-" (before) + "cip-" (take, from Latin *capere*). To **anticipate** is to "take action before" something happens — you grab hold of the future in advance.

联想"anti-"（在……之前）+"cip-"（抓取，源自拉丁语 *capere*）。**Anticipate** 就是"在事情发生之前就行动"——提前抓住未来。
