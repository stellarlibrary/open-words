# 💭 idealize

🔊 /aɪˈdiːəlaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To regard or represent as perfect or better than in reality; to form ideals
**中文：** v. 理想化；使理想化；使合乎理想

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- romanticize（浪漫化）
- glorify（美化）
- exalt（颂扬）
- embellish（润色）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **idealize the past / idealize a person / idealize a relationship / idealize childhood**

👉 Example: Many people tend to idealize their childhood memories, forgetting the difficulties they actually faced.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Students often **idealize** historical figures without fully examining the complex moral dilemmas those individuals actually confronted.
   学生们常常将历史人物理想化，而没有充分审视这些人实际面临的复杂道德困境。

2. The novelist was criticized for her tendency to **idealize** rural life and overlook the genuine hardships of agricultural communities.
   这位小说家因倾向于将乡村生活理想化、忽视农业社区的真实困难而受到批评。

3. Psychologists warn that people who **idealize** their romantic partners are more likely to experience profound disappointment later.
   心理学家警告说，那些将恋人理想化的人更可能在日后经历深深的失望。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ideal + ize" — to make something seem "ideal" or perfect, even when it is not.
把"idealize"拆成"ideal（理想的）+ ize（使……化）"——就是把事物变成理想的样子，即使它并非如此。
