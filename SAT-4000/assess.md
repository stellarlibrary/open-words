# 📊 assess

🔊 /əˈses/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To evaluate or estimate the nature, quality, ability, or value of something; to impose a tax or charge on something.
**中文：** v. 评定；估价；对…征税

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- evaluate（评估）
- appraise（鉴定）
- estimate（估计）
- gauge（衡量）
- judge（判断）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use **assess** when evaluating something systematically. Common collocations: "assess the situation," "assess the damage," "assess a tax," "assess performance." It implies careful, often formal evaluation.

👉 Example: The insurance company will assess the damage to the property before issuing payment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The teacher will **assess** each student's progress through a combination of written examinations and practical demonstrations.
   教师将通过笔试和实际操作相结合的方式来评定每位学生的学习进展。

2. Engineers were dispatched to **assess** the structural integrity of the aging bridge before allowing heavy vehicles to cross.
   工程师被派去评估这座老化桥梁的结构完整性，然后才允许重型车辆通行。

3. The government decided to **assess** a new environmental tax on corporations that exceeded established carbon emission thresholds.
   政府决定对超过既定碳排放标准的企业征收一项新的环境税。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Assess" has double "s" twice — think of it as "a + SS + e + SS" where SS stands for "Systematic Scoring." To assess is to systematically score or evaluate something.
"assess"有两组双"s"——把它想成"a + SS + e + SS"，其中SS代表"Systematic Scoring（系统评分）"。assess就是系统地评分或评估某事物。
