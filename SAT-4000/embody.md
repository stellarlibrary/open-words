# 🧬 embody

🔊 /ɪmˈbɑːdi/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To give a body or concrete form to an abstract idea; to represent or express something in a tangible or visible way
**中文：** v. 体现；使具体化；包含

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- represent（代表）
- exemplify（例证）
- personify（人格化）
- manifest（体现）
- incorporate（包含）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **embody the spirit of / embody principles / embody values / embody an ideal**

👉 Example: The constitution is meant to embody the fundamental values of a democratic society.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The monument was designed to **embody** the resilience and determination of a nation that had endured decades of hardship.
   这座纪念碑旨在体现一个经历了数十年艰辛的国家的韧性和决心。

2. Her leadership style **embodied** the principles of transparency and accountability that the organization had long advocated.
   她的领导风格体现了该组织长期倡导的透明和问责原则。

3. The new curriculum seeks to **embody** progressive educational philosophies that emphasize critical thinking and collaborative problem-solving.
   新课程旨在体现强调批判性思维和协作解决问题的进步教育理念。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "em-BODY" — to put an idea into a BODY, giving it physical form so people can see and understand it.
把"embody"想成"em-BODY"——把一个想法放进一个身体（BODY）里，赋予它实体形式让人们能看到和理解。
