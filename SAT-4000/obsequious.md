# 🙇 obsequious

🔊 /əbˈsiːkwiəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Excessively eager to please or obey; servilely compliant or deferential; fawning
**中文：** adj. 谄媚的；奉承的；顺从的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- servile（卑躬屈膝的）
- sycophantic（谄媚的）
- fawning（奉承的）
- subservient（顺从的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **obsequious manner / obsequious flattery / obsequious behavior / obsequious servant**

👉 Example: The obsequious assistant agreed with every word the director said without question.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **obsequious** courtier lavished excessive praise upon the monarch in hopes of securing a favorable political appointment.
   这位谄媚的朝臣对君主大加赞美，希望获得一个有利的政治任命。

2. Critics described the journalist's interview style as **obsequious**, noting that she never challenged the celebrity's contradictory statements.
   评论家形容这位记者的采访风格为阿谀奉承的，指出她从未质疑过名人自相矛盾的言论。

3. His **obsequious** demeanor toward wealthy patrons concealed a deeply calculating and ambitious nature beneath the surface.
   他对富有赞助人的谄媚态度掩盖了内心深处精于算计和野心勃勃的本性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "obsequious" as "OB-seek-ious" — someone who obsessively seeks approval, always bowing and flattering to please others.
把"obsequious"想成"OB-seek-ious"——一个痴迷地寻求认可的人，总是鞠躬奉承以取悦他人。
