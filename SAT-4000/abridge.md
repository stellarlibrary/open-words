# ✂️ abridge

🔊 /əˈbrɪdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To shorten or condense a written work without losing the essential meaning; to curtail or reduce
**中文：** v. 删节，缩短；节略，精简

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- condense（压缩）
- shorten（缩短）
- abbreviate（缩写）
- curtail（削减）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **abridge a book / abridge a text / abridged version / abridged edition**

👉 Example: The publisher decided to abridge the lengthy novel for younger readers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The editor was tasked with **abridging** the 800-page manuscript into a more accessible 300-page edition.
   编辑的任务是将800页的手稿删节成更易读的300页版本。

2. Critics argued that **abridging** the classic work would inevitably sacrifice much of its literary depth.
   批评者认为删节这部经典作品将不可避免地牺牲其文学深度。

3. The committee voted to **abridge** the original report, removing sections deemed redundant or outdated.
   委员会投票决定精简原始报告，删除被视为冗余或过时的部分。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "abridge" as "a bridge" — a bridge is a shortcut across a river, just as abridging is a shortcut through a long text.
把"abridge"想成"a bridge"（一座桥）——桥是跨越河流的捷径，正如abridge是穿越长篇文本的捷径。
