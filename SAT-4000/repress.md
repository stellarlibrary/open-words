# 🔒 repress

🔊 /rɪˈprɛs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To hold back or restrain by force; to suppress feelings, desires, or political opposition
**中文：** v. 抑制；镇压；约束；压制

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- suppress（压制）
- stifle（扼杀）
- restrain（抑制）
- subdue（制服）
- quell（镇压）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **repress emotions / repress dissent / repress a rebellion / repress memories / repress feelings**

👉 Example: Authoritarian regimes often repress any form of political dissent through intimidation and censorship.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government used military force to **repress** the growing wave of pro-democracy demonstrations throughout the capital city.
   政府动用军事力量镇压首都日益高涨的民主示威浪潮。

2. Psychologists warn that individuals who habitually **repress** their emotions may eventually suffer from chronic anxiety or depression.
   心理学家警告说，习惯性压抑情绪的人最终可能会患上慢性焦虑或抑郁症。

3. The regime sought to **repress** independent journalism by imposing strict censorship laws and imprisoning outspoken reporters.
   该政权试图通过实施严格的审查法律和监禁直言不讳的记者来压制独立新闻业。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-press" as pressing something down again and again — you keep pushing emotions or opposition down so they cannot rise.
把"repress"想成一再地"press"（按下）——你不断地把情绪或反对力量压下去，使它们无法抬头。
