# 🤝 coalition

🔊 /ˌkoʊəˈlɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A temporary alliance of distinct parties, persons, or states for joint action; a union or merger of separate entities
**中文：** n. 联合；结合，合并；联盟

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- alliance（联盟）
- confederation（联邦）
- union（联合）
- partnership（合作关系）
- bloc（集团）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **form a coalition / coalition government / coalition of forces / ruling coalition**

👉 Example: The opposition parties formed a coalition to challenge the incumbent government in the upcoming election.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Several environmental organizations formed a **coalition** to advocate for stricter pollution regulations at the federal level.
   几个环保组织组成了一个联盟，倡导在联邦层面实施更严格的污染法规。

2. The fragile **coalition** between the two parties collapsed after disagreements over economic policy became irreconcilable.
   两党之间脆弱的联盟在经济政策分歧变得不可调和后瓦解了。

3. A broad **coalition** of citizens, academics, and legislators united to oppose the proposed constitutional amendment.
   一个由公民、学者和立法者组成的广泛联盟团结起来反对拟议的宪法修正案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "co-" (together) + "alition" (like "a union") — people coming together to form a united front.
把"coalition"想成"co-"（一起）+ "alition"（像"a union"联合）——人们走到一起组成统一战线。
