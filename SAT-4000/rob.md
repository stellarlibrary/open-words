# 🦹 rob

🔊 /rɒb/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To take property unlawfully from someone by force or threat; to deprive someone of something
**中文：** v. 抢夺，抢掠；剥夺

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- steal（偷窃）
- plunder（掠夺）
- deprive（剥夺）
- pillage（洗劫）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rob someone of something / rob a bank / rob the cradle / rob Peter to pay Paul**

👉 Example: The chronic illness robbed her of the energy she once devoted to academic research and teaching.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prolonged economic recession effectively **robbed** millions of citizens of their savings and financial security.
   长期的经济衰退实际上剥夺了数百万公民的储蓄和经济保障。

2. Critics argued that the new policy would **rob** public schools of essential funding needed to maintain educational quality.
   批评者认为新政策将剥夺公立学校维持教育质量所必需的关键资金。

3. The invading forces attempted to **rob** the nation of its cultural heritage by destroying ancient monuments and artifacts.
   入侵军队试图通过摧毁古代纪念碑和文物来掠夺该国的文化遗产。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rob" as "robe" without the "e" — a thief in a robe sneaks in to rob you of your valuables.
把"rob"想成"robe（长袍）"去掉"e"——一个穿长袍的小偷悄悄进来抢夺你的贵重物品。
