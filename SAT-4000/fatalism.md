# 🌀 fatalism

🔊 /ˈfeɪtəlɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The belief that all events are predetermined and inevitable; a submissive attitude toward fate
**中文：** n. 宿命论

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- determinism（决定论）
- predestination（命运注定）
- resignation（听天由命）
- defeatism（失败主义）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sense of fatalism / embrace fatalism / a fatalism about / fatalism and resignation**

👉 Example: A deep sense of fatalism pervaded the community after years of recurring natural disasters.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosopher argued that **fatalism** undermines human motivation because it denies the significance of individual choice and effort.
   这位哲学家认为宿命论会削弱人类的动力，因为它否定了个人选择和努力的重要性。

2. A pervasive **fatalism** settled over the war-torn region, as civilians felt powerless to alter the course of events.
   一种无处不在的宿命论笼罩着这个饱受战争蹂躏的地区，平民感到无力改变事态的发展。

3. Critics of **fatalism** contend that belief in free will is essential for moral responsibility and meaningful social progress.
   宿命论的批评者认为，自由意志的信念对于道德责任和有意义的社会进步至关重要。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fatal-ism" — the belief that everything is "fatal" or fated, as if the outcome is already sealed and nothing can change it.
把"fatalism"想成"fatal（致命的）+ ism（主义）"——一切都是命中注定的，仿佛结局已经注定，无法改变。
