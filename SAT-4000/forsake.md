# 💔 forsake

🔊 /fərˈseɪk/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To abandon, renounce, or turn away from entirely; to give up something valued or cherished
**中文：** v. 放弃；断念；抛弃

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- abandon（遗弃）
- desert（抛弃）
- renounce（放弃）
- relinquish（放弃）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **forsake one's duty / forsake a friend / forsake old habits / forsake all others**

👉 Example: The knight vowed never to forsake his sworn oath of loyalty to the crown.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The disillusioned scholar chose to **forsake** a prestigious academic career in order to pursue humanitarian work in developing nations.
   这位幻灭的学者选择放弃享有盛誉的学术生涯，转而在发展中国家从事人道主义工作。

2. Communities that **forsake** sustainable practices in favor of short-term profit often face severe environmental consequences in later decades.
   为了短期利润而抛弃可持续实践的社区往往在后来几十年面临严重的环境后果。

3. He could not bring himself to **forsake** his childhood friends, even after achieving extraordinary wealth and international prominence.
   即使在获得非凡的财富和国际声望之后，他也无法让自己抛弃童年的朋友们。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "for-sake" — "for the sake of something new, you leave the old behind." When you forsake something, you give it up for the sake of moving on.
把"forsake"想成"for sake（为了某事）"——为了新事物，你抛下旧事物。当你forsake某事时，你为了继续前行而放弃它。
