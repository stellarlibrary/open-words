# 🏛️ legislative

🔊 /ˈlɛdʒɪslətɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or having the power to make laws; of or pertaining to a legislature. (n.) The legislative branch or body of government.
**中文：** adj. 立法的；有立法权的；立法机关的 n. 立法权；立法机关

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- lawmaking（立法的）
- parliamentary（议会的）
- congressional（国会的）
- statutory（法定的）
- juridical（司法的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **legislative body / legislative branch / legislative power / legislative process / legislative session**

👉 Example: The legislative branch serves as a critical check on executive authority in democratic systems of governance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **legislative** process required extensive debate and multiple committee reviews before the bill could reach the floor.
   立法过程需要广泛的辩论和多次委员会审查，法案才能进入全体投票阶段。

2. Several **legislative** reforms were introduced to address systemic inequalities that had persisted in the education sector for decades.
   若干立法改革被提出，以解决教育领域长期存在的系统性不平等问题。

3. The constitution grants the **legislative** assembly exclusive authority to approve the national budget and allocate public funds.
   宪法授予立法议会批准国家预算和分配公共资金的专属权力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "legislative" as "legislate + ive" — the "-ive" suffix means "relating to," so legislative means relating to the act of making laws.
把"legislative"想成"legislate + ive"——后缀"-ive"表示"与……有关的"，所以legislative就是与制定法律有关的。
