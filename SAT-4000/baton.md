# 🎶 baton

🔊 /bəˈtɒn/<br>
🏷️ *n. | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A thin stick used by a conductor to direct an orchestra or choir; a short stick passed between runners in a relay race; a truncheon or club carried by a police officer.

**中文：** 指挥棒；接力棒；警棍。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- wand （指挥棒；魔杖）
- rod （棒；杆）
- truncheon （警棍）
- staff （杖；棒）
- stick （棍棒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

The meaning of "baton" depends entirely on context. In music, it refers to the conductor's stick. In athletics, it is the stick passed in relay races — hence the idiom "pass the baton," meaning to hand over responsibility. In law enforcement, it refers to a police club. The word comes from French and may be pronounced /bəˈtɒn/ (British) or /bæˈtɑːn/ (American).

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The maestro raised his **baton** and the entire orchestra fell silent in anticipation.
   中文：指挥大师举起指挥棒，整个管弦乐队在期待中安静下来。

2. The sprinter fumbled the **baton** during the crucial exchange, costing the team precious seconds.
   中文：短跑运动员在关键交接时掉了接力棒，使团队损失了宝贵的几秒钟。

3. The officer drew his **baton** to maintain order as the crowd surged toward the entrance.
   中文：当人群涌向入口时，警察抽出警棍以维持秩序。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Baton" comes from French and sounds like "ba-TONG" — imagine a conductor going "ba-TONG, ba-TONG" as they wave their stick to keep the beat. The stick they wave is the baton!

谐音记忆："baton"来自法语，听起来像"ba-TONG"——想象一位指挥家挥动棒子打拍子时发出"ba-TONG, ba-TONG"的节奏声。他们挥动的那根棒子就是baton！
