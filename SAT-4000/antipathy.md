# 😤 antipathy

🔊 /ænˈtɪpəθi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A deep-seated feeling of aversion, dislike, or hostility toward someone or something.

**中文：** 反感；厌恶；憎恶。对某人或某事物的强烈反感或敌意。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- aversion（厌恶）
- hostility（敌意）
- animosity（仇恨）
- repugnance（反感）
- loathing（憎恶）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Common collocations:** deep antipathy, mutual antipathy, antipathy toward/for, natural antipathy, strong antipathy

**Example:** She harbored a lasting **antipathy** toward those who betrayed her trust.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator made no effort to conceal his profound **antipathy** toward the proposed trade agreement.
   这位参议员毫不掩饰他对拟议贸易协定的强烈反感。

2. A mutual **antipathy** between the two rival factions rendered diplomatic negotiations virtually impossible.
   两个敌对派系之间的相互敌意使外交谈判几乎不可能进行。

3. Her instinctive **antipathy** for dishonesty compelled her to expose the corruption within the organization.
   她对不诚实行为的本能厌恶促使她揭露了组织内部的腐败。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "anti-" (against) + "-pathy" (feeling). Antipathy is a feeling against someone — a strong dislike or hostility.

联想："anti-"（反对）+ "-pathy"（感情）。antipathy 就是对某人怀有反对的感情——强烈的反感或敌意。
