# 🤥 mendacious

🔊 /mɛnˈdeɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Not telling the truth; lying or deliberately deceitful; characterized by falsehood or untruthfulness.
**中文：** adj. 虚伪的；说谎的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- deceitful（欺骗的）
- dishonest（不诚实的）
- untruthful（不真实的）
- duplicitous（口是心非的）
- fraudulent（欺诈的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mendacious claims / mendacious account / mendacious politician / mendacious testimony**

👉 Example: The investigation revealed a series of mendacious statements made by the corporation's chief executive.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist exposed the **mendacious** propaganda campaign that had been designed to manipulate public opinion for years.
   记者揭露了这场旨在多年来操纵公众舆论的虚假宣传活动。

2. Historians regard the autobiography as largely **mendacious**, filled with fabricated achievements and embellished personal narratives.
   历史学家认为这本自传大部分内容都是虚假的，充斥着捏造的成就和美化的个人叙事。

3. The witness provided a **mendacious** account of events that directly contradicted the physical evidence collected at the scene.
   证人提供了一份虚假的事件陈述，与现场收集到的物证直接矛盾。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mend-acious" as "mending the truth" — a mendacious person tries to patch together lies to cover the real story.
把"mendacious"想成"mend（修补）+ acious"——一个mendacious的人试图把谎言拼凑起来，掩盖真实的故事。
