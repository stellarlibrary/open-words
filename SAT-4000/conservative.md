# 🏛️ conservative

🔊 /kənˈsɜːvətɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Averse to change or innovation; holding traditional values; cautious and moderate. (n.) A person who is averse to change and holds traditional values.
**中文：** adj. 保守的；谨慎的 n. 保守主义者；保守派

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- traditional（传统的）
- cautious（谨慎的）
- conventional（保守的）
- moderate（温和的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **conservative estimate / conservative approach / politically conservative / conservative values / fiscal conservative**

👉 Example: The analyst provided a conservative estimate of the project costs to ensure the budget would not be exceeded.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The financial advisor recommended a **conservative** investment strategy to minimize risk during the period of economic uncertainty.
   财务顾问建议采取保守的投资策略，以在经济不确定时期将风险降至最低。

2. As a fiscal **conservative**, the senator consistently opposed any legislation that would significantly increase government spending.
   作为一名财政保守主义者，这位参议员一贯反对任何会大幅增加政府支出的立法。

3. Even by the most **conservative** estimates, the restoration of the historic building will require several million dollars in funding.
   即使按最保守的估计，修复这座历史建筑也将需要数百万美元的资金。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "conserv-ative" as "one who conserves" — a conservative person wants to conserve and keep things the way they are.
把"conservative"想成"保持(conserve)现状的人"——保守的人希望保持事物原样不变。
