# 🌟 predestine

🔊 /prɪˈdestɪn/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To determine or decree in advance by divine will or fate; to destine or foreordain something beforehand
**中文：** v. 注定；预先确定

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- foreordain（预先注定）
- predetermine（预先决定）
- destine（命定）
- fate（注定）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **predestined to / predestined for / predestined by fate / seem predestined**

👉 Example: Many believed the young prodigy was predestined for greatness in the field of music.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Some theological traditions hold that every individual is **predestined** to follow a particular spiritual path from birth.
   一些神学传统认为，每个人从出生起就被注定要走一条特定的精神道路。

2. The historian argued that the collapse of the empire was not **predestined** but resulted from deliberate political choices.
   这位历史学家论证说，帝国的崩溃并非命中注定，而是出于刻意的政治选择。

3. Their remarkable meeting seemed **predestined**, as though invisible forces had conspired to bring them together at that moment.
   他们不同寻常的相遇似乎是命中注定，仿佛无形的力量促使他们在那一刻相聚。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-destine" as "pre (before) + destine (destiny)" — your destiny was decided before you even arrived.
把"predestine"想成"pre（之前）+ destine（命运）"——你的命运在你到来之前就已经决定了。
