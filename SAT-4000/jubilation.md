# 🎊 jubilation

🔊 /ˌdʒuːbɪˈleɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A feeling of great happiness, triumph, and celebration; the act of rejoicing exuberantly
**中文：** n. 喜欢；庆祝；欢呼；欢腾

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- exultation（欢欣）
- rejoicing（欢庆）
- elation（兴高采烈）
- celebration（庆祝）
- triumph（胜利的喜悦）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **scenes of jubilation / wild jubilation / cause for jubilation / sense of jubilation**

👉 Example: Scenes of jubilation erupted across the capital when the ceasefire agreement was announced.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. There was widespread **jubilation** among the scientific community when the decades-long space mission finally returned conclusive data.
   当长达数十年的太空任务终于传回确凿数据时，科学界一片欢腾。

2. The announcement of the peace accord was met with **jubilation** in cities throughout the war-torn region and beyond.
   和平协议的宣布在饱受战火蹂躏的地区及其他城市引发了欢庆。

3. A mood of quiet **jubilation** spread through the courtroom after the jury delivered a verdict of acquittal for the defendant.
   当陪审团宣布被告无罪的裁决后，法庭内弥漫着一种安静的欢欣。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "jubilation" as "jubilee + action" — the action and feeling you experience during a jubilee or grand celebration.
把"jubilation"想成"jubilee（庆典）+ action（行动）"——你在盛大庆典中体验到的行动和感受。
