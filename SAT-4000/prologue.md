# 📖 prologue

🔊 /ˈproʊlɔːɡ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An introductory section of a literary work or speech; an event or action that leads to another
**中文：** n. 开场白；序言

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- preface（前言）
- introduction（引言）
- foreword（序言）
- preamble（序文）
- prelude（前奏）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prologue to a novel / prologue to the story / serve as a prologue / write a prologue**

👉 Example: The author crafted an evocative prologue that immediately drew readers into the world of the story.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **prologue** of the novel established the historical context necessary for understanding the complex narrative that followed.
   这部小说的序言建立了理解后续复杂叙事所必需的历史背景。

2. Historians often regard the border skirmishes of 1913 as a **prologue** to the devastating global conflict that erupted shortly after.
   历史学家们常将1913年的边境冲突视为随后爆发的毁灭性全球冲突的序幕。

3. The playwright wrote a compelling **prologue** in which the narrator addressed the audience directly to set the scene.
   剧作家写了一段引人入胜的开场白，叙述者在其中直接面向观众交代场景。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pro-LOG" — "pro" means before, and "log" is like a written record; a prologue is the writing that comes before the main story.
把"prologue"想成"pro-LOG"——"pro"意为"之前"，"log"是记录；prologue就是正文之前的文字记录。
