# 🧿 prescient

🔊 /ˈprɛʃənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having or showing knowledge of events before they take place; possessing foresight
**中文：** adj. 预知的；有先见之明的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- prophetic（预言的）
- farsighted（有远见的）
- visionary（有远见卓识的）
- clairvoyant（有洞察力的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a prescient warning / prescient analysis / remarkably prescient / prescient observation**

👉 Example: Her prescient analysis of the geopolitical situation proved accurate when the predicted conflict erupted.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scientist's **prescient** warnings about climate change decades ago are now validated by overwhelming empirical evidence and data.
   这位科学家几十年前对气候变化的先见之明的警告如今已被大量实证证据和数据所证实。

2. In a remarkably **prescient** essay, the philosopher anticipated the ethical dilemmas that artificial intelligence would eventually raise.
   在一篇极具前瞻性的文章中，这位哲学家预见了人工智能最终将引发的伦理困境。

3. The investment firm's **prescient** decision to divest from fossil fuels protected its clients from significant financial losses last year.
   该投资公司有先见之明地决定从化石燃料中撤资，保护了客户免受去年的重大财务损失。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-scient" as "knowing before" (Latin scire = to know) — someone who knows things before they happen.
把"prescient"想成"pre-scient"（拉丁语scire意为"知道"）——在事情发生之前就知道的人。
