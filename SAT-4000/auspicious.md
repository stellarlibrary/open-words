# 🍀 auspicious

🔊 /ɔːˈspɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Conducive to success; indicating a favorable outcome; marked by good fortune or lucky circumstances.
**中文：** 吉兆的，吉利的；幸运的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- favorable（有利的）
- propitious（吉利的）
- fortunate（幸运的）
- promising（有前途的）
- opportune（适时的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **auspicious occasion/beginning/start**, **auspicious sign/omen**, **auspicious moment/day**

👉 Example: The couple chose an auspicious date for their wedding based on traditional customs.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The company's **auspicious** debut on the stock exchange exceeded all analyst expectations for opening-day performance.
   该公司在证券交易所的良好首秀超出了所有分析师对首日表现的预期。

2. Clear skies and mild weather provided an **auspicious** start to the long-awaited archaeological expedition.
   晴朗的天空和温和的天气为这次期待已久的考古探险提供了一个吉利的开端。

3. The diplomat regarded the successful preliminary negotiations as an **auspicious** sign for the formal peace talks.
   这位外交官将初步谈判的成功视为正式和平谈判的吉兆。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Auspicious" shares the root with "auspice" — from bird-watching omens in ancient Rome. If the birds flew right, it was an auspicious sign. Think: "au-SPECIAL-ous" — a special, lucky moment.
"Auspicious"与"auspice"同根——源自古罗马的鸟占。如果鸟飞向正确的方向，就是吉兆。联想："au-SPECIAL-ous"——一个特别的、幸运的时刻。
