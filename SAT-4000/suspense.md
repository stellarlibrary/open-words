# 😰 suspense

🔊 /səˈspɛns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A state of uncertainty, anxiety, or excited anticipation; a quality in a narrative that creates tension about the outcome.
**中文：** n. 焦虑，悬念，悬而不决

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- tension（紧张）
- anticipation（期待）
- uncertainty（不确定）
- anxiety（焦虑）
- apprehension（忧虑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in suspense / keep someone in suspense / build suspense / full of suspense**

👉 Example: The filmmaker skillfully builds suspense throughout the narrative, keeping audiences on the edge of their seats.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The jury deliberated for three days, keeping the entire courtroom in unbearable **suspense** about the verdict.
   陪审团审议了三天，使整个法庭对判决结果处于难以忍受的悬念之中。

2. The novelist masterfully creates **suspense** by withholding crucial information until the final chapter of the book.
   这位小说家巧妙地通过在最后一章才揭示关键信息来制造悬念。

3. Political **suspense** gripped the nation as election results remained too close to call throughout the evening.
   由于选举结果整晚都难分胜负，政治悬念笼罩了整个国家。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "suspense" as being "suspended" in a state of waiting — you are left hanging, not knowing what will happen next.
把"suspense"想成处于"悬挂"（suspended）的等待状态——你被吊在那里，不知道接下来会发生什么。
