# 🧬 inherent

🔊 /ɪnˈhɪərənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Existing as a natural or permanent quality of something; intrinsic and inseparable from the thing itself
**中文：** adj. 固有的；内在的；与生俱来的；天生的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- intrinsic（内在的）
- innate（天生的）
- inborn（与生俱来的）
- built-in（固有的）
- fundamental（根本的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inherent in / inherent risk / inherent quality / inherent flaw / inherent right**

👉 Example: There is an inherent risk in any investment that promises unusually high returns.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researchers acknowledged the **inherent** limitations of the experimental design before presenting their preliminary findings to reviewers.
   研究人员在向评审者展示初步结果之前承认了实验设计的固有局限性。

2. Freedom of expression is considered an **inherent** right in democratic societies, protected by constitutional law and tradition.
   言论自由被视为民主社会中一项固有权利，受宪法和传统保护。

3. The **inherent** complexity of climate systems makes accurate long-term weather prediction an extraordinarily challenging scientific undertaking.
   气候系统固有的复杂性使得准确的长期天气预测成为一项极具挑战性的科学工作。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-HERE-nt" — the quality is right in HERE, inside the thing itself, inseparable and permanent.
把"inherent"想成"in + HERE（这里）+ nt"——那个特质就在这里面，在事物自身内部，不可分离。
