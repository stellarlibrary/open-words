# 🚪 expulsion

🔊 /ɪkˈspʌlʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act of forcing someone to leave a place, organization, or institution; the process of driving out or ejecting
**中文：** n. 驱逐；开除；逐出

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ejection（驱逐）
- banishment（放逐）
- dismissal（开除）
- ouster（罢免）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **expulsion from school / expulsion from the country / face expulsion / order the expulsion of**

👉 Example: The student faced expulsion from the university for repeated violations of the academic honor code.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **expulsion** of several diplomats from the country heightened tensions between the two nations significantly.
   数名外交官被驱逐出境，使两国之间的紧张关系显著加剧。

2. The committee voted unanimously in favor of the **expulsion** of the member who had violated ethical standards.
   委员会一致投票赞成开除那位违反道德准则的成员。

3. Historians have documented the forced **expulsion** of indigenous populations from their ancestral territories during colonial expansion.
   历史学家记录了殖民扩张期间原住民被强制驱逐出祖先领地的过程。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-puls-ion" as "pushing out" — the Latin root "pellere" means to push, so expulsion is pushing someone out.
把"expulsion"想成"ex(向外)+puls(推)+ion"——把人向外推出去，就是驱逐、开除。
