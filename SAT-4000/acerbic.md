# 🌵 acerbic

🔊 /əˈsɜːrbɪk/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Sharp and forthright in speech or manner; having a sour or harsh taste.
**中文：** 尖刻的；酸的；辛辣的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- caustic（刻薄的）
- sardonic（讽刺的）
- biting（尖锐的）
- acrid（辛辣的）
- tart（尖酸的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **acerbic wit / humor / tongue / remark / criticism**

👉 Example: The critic was known for his acerbic reviews of contemporary literature.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's **acerbic** commentary on the student's thesis left the entire committee in uncomfortable silence.
   教授对学生论文的尖刻评论让整个委员会陷入了令人不安的沉默。

2. Her **acerbic** wit, though entertaining to audiences, often alienated colleagues who felt personally targeted.
   她尖刻的机智虽然令观众觉得有趣，但常常疏远了那些觉得被针对的同事。

3. The editorial delivered an **acerbic** critique of the government's failure to address rising inequality.
   这篇社论对政府未能解决日益加剧的不平等问题进行了尖锐的批评。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Acerbic" shares its root with "acerb" (Latin: sour). Picture biting into an unripe, sour fruit — that sharp, puckering sensation is acerbic.
"acerbic"源自拉丁语"acerbus（酸涩的）"。想象咬一口未熟的酸果子——那种尖锐、令人皱眉的感觉就是acerbic。
