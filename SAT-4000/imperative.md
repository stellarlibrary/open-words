# 🔺 imperative

🔊 /ɪmˈpɛrətɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Of vital importance; crucial; urgent; commanding or authoritative. (n.) An essential or urgent thing; a command; the imperative mood in grammar
**中文：** adj. 必要的；紧急的；命令的；强制的；祈使的 n. 必要的事；必须完成的事；命令；祈使语气

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- essential（必要的）
- urgent（紧急的）
- compulsory（强制的）
- crucial（关键的）
- mandatory（强制性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **moral imperative / imperative that (+ subjunctive) / imperative need / strategic imperative**

👉 Example: It is imperative that the committee address the safety concerns before approving the new construction project.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The surgeon stressed that it was **imperative** to operate within the next two hours to prevent permanent neurological damage.
   外科医生强调，必须在接下来的两小时内进行手术，以防止永久性神经损伤。

2. Addressing climate change has become a global **imperative** that demands unprecedented cooperation among all sovereign nations.
   应对气候变化已成为一项全球性的迫切任务，需要所有主权国家进行空前的合作。

3. The professor explained that the **imperative** mood in Latin expresses direct commands and urgent exhortations to the listener.
   教授解释说，拉丁语中的祈使语气表达直接的命令和对听者的紧急劝告。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "imperative" as related to "emperor" — an emperor issues commands that are absolutely necessary to obey, making them imperative.
把"imperative"联想到"emperor（皇帝）"——皇帝发出的命令是必须服从的，因此是"必要的、强制的"。
