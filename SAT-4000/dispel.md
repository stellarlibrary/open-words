# 💨 dispel

🔊 /dɪˈspɛl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To drive away or cause to vanish; to eliminate doubts, fears, or false beliefs
**中文：** v. 驱散；消除

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dissipate（驱散）
- banish（消除）
- allay（减轻）
- scatter（驱散）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dispel a myth / dispel doubts / dispel fears / dispel rumors / dispel misconceptions**

👉 Example: The professor's thorough explanation helped dispel the common misconceptions about the theory.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The official report was intended to **dispel** widespread rumors about the safety of the municipal water supply.
   这份官方报告旨在消除公众对市政供水安全性的广泛谣言。

2. New archaeological evidence helped **dispel** longstanding myths regarding the origins of the ancient civilization's decline.
   新的考古证据帮助消除了关于该古代文明衰落起源的长期误解。

3. The chairman's transparent disclosure of financial records served to **dispel** investor concerns about corporate governance.
   董事长对财务记录的透明公开有助于消除投资者对公司治理的担忧。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-pel" as "dis" (away) + "pel" (push, like "compel") — to push something away, to drive it off completely.
把"dispel"想成"dis"（离开）+"pel"（推动，如"compel"中的词根）——把某物推走，彻底驱散。
