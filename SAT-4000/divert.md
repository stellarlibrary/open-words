# 🔀 divert

🔊 /dɪˈvɜːrt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To turn aside from a course or direction; to distract or entertain; to redirect
**中文：** v. 转移；使欢娱；使转向

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- redirect（转向）
- deflect（使偏转）
- distract（分散注意力）
- amuse（使欢娱）
- reroute（改变路线）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **divert attention / divert traffic / divert resources / divert funds**

👉 Example: The magician used hand gestures to divert the audience's attention from his hidden trick.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. City officials decided to **divert** traffic away from the downtown area during the large-scale construction project.
   市政官员决定在大规模建设项目期间将交通从市中心转移开。

2. The government chose to **divert** substantial funds from defense spending toward public education and healthcare reform.
   政府选择将大量资金从国防开支转移到公共教育和医疗改革上。

3. Critics argued that the scandal was deliberately fabricated to **divert** public attention from the failing economic policies.
   批评人士认为这一丑闻是蓄意捏造的，目的是转移公众对失败经济政策的注意力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "di-vert" as "different vertical" — turning something to go in a different direction, away from its original path.
把"divert"想成"di(不同)+vert(转)"——把事物转向不同的方向，偏离原来的路径。
