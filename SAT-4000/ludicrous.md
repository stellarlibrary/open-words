# 🤡 ludicrous

🔊 /ˈluːdɪkrəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** So foolish, unreasonable, or out of place as to be amusing; utterly absurd or ridiculous
**中文：** adj. 滑稽的；荒唐的；可笑的；愚蠢的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- absurd（荒谬的）
- ridiculous（可笑的）
- preposterous（荒唐的）
- farcical（滑稽的）
- laughable（可笑的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ludicrous claim / ludicrous idea / ludicrous situation / utterly ludicrous / ludicrous suggestion**

👉 Example: The defendant's explanation for his whereabouts was so ludicrous that even the jury could not suppress their disbelief.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The proposal to build a bridge across the entire ocean was dismissed by engineers as utterly **ludicrous** and financially impossible.
   建造一座横跨整个海洋的桥梁的提议被工程师们斥为完全荒唐且在财务上不可能实现。

2. It would be **ludicrous** to suggest that a single policy change could resolve decades of deeply entrenched institutional inequality.
   认为单一的政策变化就能解决数十年根深蒂固的制度性不平等，这种想法是荒谬的。

3. The comedian's **ludicrous** impersonation of the pompous politician had the entire audience roaring with uncontrollable laughter throughout the evening.
   喜剧演员对那位自命不凡的政客的滑稽模仿让全场观众整晚都笑得前仰后合。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "lud-icrous" — "lud" comes from Latin "ludus" meaning "play" or "game," so ludicrous describes something so silly it seems like a joke.
把"ludicrous"想成"lud（游戏）+ icrous"——"lud"来自拉丁语"ludus（游戏）"，所以ludicrous形容的是荒唐得像笑话一样的事物。
