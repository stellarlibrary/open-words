# 🌀 compulsion

🔊 /kəmˈpʌlʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** An irresistible urge to behave in a certain way; the act of compelling or state of being compelled by force or pressure
**中文：** n. 强制；强迫；强烈欲望；冲动

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- urge（冲动）
- obsession（执念）
- coercion（强迫）
- impulse（冲动）
- drive（驱动力）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **feel a compulsion to / under compulsion / compulsion to act / irresistible compulsion**

👉 Example: She felt an overwhelming compulsion to check her email every few minutes during the workday.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The psychologist explained that the patient experienced a powerful **compulsion** to wash his hands repeatedly throughout the day.
   心理学家解释说，该患者每天都有反复洗手的强烈冲动。

2. No citizen should act under **compulsion** when exercising the fundamental right to vote in democratic elections.
   任何公民在行使民主选举的基本投票权时都不应受到强制。

3. Despite knowing the risks, he could not resist the **compulsion** to gamble away his remaining savings at the casino.
   尽管知道风险，他仍无法抵抗冲动，在赌场中输掉了剩余的积蓄。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "compel" + "sion" — compulsion is the noun form of compel. When you are compelled, you feel a compulsion.
把"compulsion"想成"compel（强迫）+ sion"——当你被迫做某事时，你感受到的就是compulsion（强迫感）。
