# 🎵 cantata

🔊 /kænˈtɑːtə/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A medium-length narrative or descriptive piece of music with vocal solos, chorus, and orchestral accompaniment, typically performed without staging.
**中文：** n. 清唱剧；康塔塔；大合唱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- oratorio（清唱剧）
- choral work（合唱作品）
- vocal composition（声乐作品）
- hymn（赞美诗）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **Bach cantata / church cantata / perform a cantata / sacred cantata / secular cantata**

👉 Example: The choir rehearsed a Bach cantata every week in preparation for the holiday concert season.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Johann Sebastian Bach composed over two hundred church **cantatas** during his tenure as music director in Leipzig.
   约翰·塞巴斯蒂安·巴赫在莱比锡担任音乐总监期间创作了两百多部教堂清唱剧。

2. The university ensemble performed a secular **cantata** that celebrated the arrival of spring with exuberant vocal passages.
   大学合奏团演出了一部庆祝春天到来的世俗康塔塔，其中充满了欢快的声乐段落。

3. Scholars consider the **cantata** an essential genre for understanding Baroque-era compositional techniques and religious aesthetics.
   学者们认为清唱剧是理解巴洛克时期作曲技法和宗教美学的重要体裁。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cantata" as coming from Italian "cantare" (to sing) — a cantata is a piece that is sung, not acted out on stage.
"cantata"源自意大利语"cantare（唱）"——清唱剧是用来"唱"的，而非在舞台上表演的。
