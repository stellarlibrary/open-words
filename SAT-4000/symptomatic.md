# 🩺 symptomatic

🔊 /ˌsɪmptəˈmætɪk/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Serving as a symptom or sign of something; exhibiting or involving medical symptoms
**中文：** adj. 症状的，征候的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- indicative（指示的）
- suggestive（暗示的）
- characteristic（特有的）
- diagnostic（诊断的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **symptomatic of / symptomatic treatment / symptomatic patient / symptomatic relief**

👉 Example: The rising dropout rate is symptomatic of deeper problems within the educational system.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The physician noted that the patient's persistent fatigue was **symptomatic** of an underlying thyroid disorder requiring further investigation.
   医生指出，患者持续的疲劳是潜在甲状腺疾病的症状，需要进一步检查。

2. Economists warned that the decline in consumer spending was **symptomatic** of a broader recession threatening the national economy.
   经济学家警告说，消费支出的下降是威胁国民经济的更大范围衰退的征兆。

3. The treatment plan focused on providing **symptomatic** relief while researchers continued to search for a permanent cure.
   治疗方案侧重于提供症状缓解，同时研究人员继续寻找永久性治愈方法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "symptom-atic" as "symptom-related" — if something is symptomatic, it relates to or shows symptoms of a larger issue.
把"symptomatic"想成"symptom（症状）+ atic（相关的）"——如果某事是symptomatic的，它与更大问题的症状有关。
