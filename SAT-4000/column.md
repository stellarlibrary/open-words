# 🏛️ column

🔊 /ˈkɒl.əm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A tall, vertical cylindrical structure used as a support or ornament in architecture; a vertical arrangement of items (such as numbers or text); a regular section in a newspaper or magazine written by a particular journalist.

**中文：** 圆柱；纵队，列；专栏。指建筑中用作支撑或装饰的高大圆柱形结构；也指项目的纵向排列；还指报纸或杂志中由特定记者撰写的固定版块。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- pillar（支柱）
- post（柱子）
- row（列）
- article（文章）
- editorial（社论）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Collocation:** "newspaper column / column of figures / marble column" — the meaning shifts depending on context: architecture, data arrangement, or journalism.

**Example:** The journalist's weekly column attracted a devoted readership eager for her incisive political commentary.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient Greek temple was supported by a series of massive Doric **columns** carved from locally quarried marble.
   这座古希腊神庙由一系列用当地开采的大理石雕刻而成的巨大多立克**圆柱**支撑。

2. The analyst organized the financial data into a clearly labeled **column** to facilitate accurate comparison across fiscal quarters.
   分析师将财务数据整理成标注清晰的**列**，以便于准确对比各财政季度的数据。

3. Her syndicated **column** on international affairs was widely read and frequently cited by policymakers and academic scholars.
   她关于国际事务的联合**专栏**被广泛阅读，并经常被政策制定者和学术学者引用。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

**English:** From Latin *columna* (pillar). Picture a tall pillar — it stands vertically like a column of numbers, and a newspaper "column" is a vertical strip of text on a page.

**中文：** 来自拉丁语 *columna*（柱子）。想象一根高大的柱子——它像数字的"列"一样垂直矗立，而报纸"专栏"则是页面上垂直的文字条。
