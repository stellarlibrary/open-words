# 📍 designate

🔊 /ˈdɛzɪɡneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To officially assign a name, status, or role; to appoint or indicate. (adj.) Appointed but not yet installed in office
**中文：** v. 指明；指出；任命；指定 adj. 指定的；已受委派的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- appoint（任命）
- assign（分配）
- nominate（提名）
- specify（指定）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **designate someone as / designate a leader / designated area / chairman designate**

👉 Example: The committee voted to designate the historic building as a protected cultural landmark.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board of directors decided to **designate** her as the interim chief executive officer effective immediately.
   董事会决定任命她为临时首席执行官，即刻生效。

2. Federal authorities have **designated** the wetland region as a protected habitat for endangered migratory bird species.
   联邦当局已将该湿地区域指定为濒危候鸟物种的保护栖息地。

3. The ambassador **designate** spent several weeks preparing for her diplomatic assignment before formally assuming the position.
   被指定的大使在正式就任之前花了数周时间准备她的外交任务。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "design + ate" — someone designed a plan and ate the responsibility by being designated for the role.
把"designate"想成"design + ate"——某人设计了一个计划，并通过被指定担任该角色而承担了责任。
