# 😏 deride

🔊 /dɪˈraɪd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To laugh at with contempt or scorn; to mock or ridicule
**中文：** v. 嘲笑；嘲弄

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- mock（嘲笑）
- ridicule（嘲弄）
- scorn（蔑视）
- taunt（奚落）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **deride someone for something / deride someone as something / publicly deride**

👉 Example: Critics derided the new policy as impractical and fundamentally flawed.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The academic community chose to **deride** his unconventional theory rather than engage with its underlying premises seriously.
   学术界选择嘲笑他的非传统理论，而不是认真审视其基本前提。

2. Political commentators **derided** the proposal as naive, arguing that it lacked any feasible implementation strategy whatsoever.
   政治评论员们嘲弄该提案天真幼稚，认为它完全缺乏可行的实施策略。

3. It is counterproductive to **deride** those who hold differing opinions instead of fostering constructive and meaningful dialogue.
   嘲笑持不同意见的人而不是促进建设性的有意义对话是适得其反的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-ride" as "ride down" — when you deride someone, you verbally ride them down with mockery and contempt.
把"deride"想成"de-ride（往下骑）"——当你嘲笑某人时，你用嘲弄和蔑视在言语上把他们踩在脚下。
