# 😈 knave

🔊 /neɪv/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A dishonest or unscrupulous man; a rogue or scoundrel; also the jack in a deck of playing cards
**中文：** n. 无赖；流氓；（纸牌中的）杰克

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rogue（无赖）
- scoundrel（恶棍）
- rascal（流氓）
- villain（坏人）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a cunning knave / a deceitful knave / knave of hearts**

👉 Example: The old merchant warned his apprentice never to trust a knave who speaks with honeyed words.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court jester was dismissed after the king discovered he was actually a **knave** conspiring with foreign agents.
   宫廷小丑在国王发现他实际上是一个与外国间谍勾结的无赖后被解雇了。

2. Shakespeare's plays frequently feature a clever **knave** whose treachery ultimately leads to his own undoing.
   莎士比亚的戏剧中经常出现一个聪明的流氓，其背叛最终导致了自身的毁灭。

3. The historian described the corrupt senator as a political **knave** who exploited public trust for personal gain.
   历史学家将这位腐败的参议员描述为一个利用公众信任谋取私利的政治无赖。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "knave" as sounding like "naive" reversed — a knave is the opposite of naive; he is cunning and dishonest.
把"knave"想成"naive"（天真）的反面——knave不是天真的人，而是狡猾不诚实的无赖。
