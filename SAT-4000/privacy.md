# 🔒 privacy

🔊 /ˈpraɪvəsi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The state of being free from public attention or intrusion; the condition of being secluded or undisturbed
**中文：** n. 隐私；秘密；隐居；隐居处

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- seclusion（隐居）
- solitude（独处）
- confidentiality（保密性）
- isolation（隔离）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **right to privacy / invasion of privacy / privacy policy / protect one's privacy**

👉 Example: Citizens have a fundamental right to privacy that must be safeguarded by law.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The new legislation aims to strengthen digital **privacy** protections for consumers who share personal data with technology companies.
   新立法旨在加强对与科技公司共享个人数据的消费者的数字隐私保护。

2. The celebrity filed a lawsuit alleging a serious invasion of **privacy** by the tabloid newspaper and its photographers.
   这位名人提起诉讼，指控小报及其摄影师严重侵犯了其隐私。

3. Many scholars argue that the erosion of **privacy** in the digital age poses unprecedented challenges to democratic societies.
   许多学者认为，数字时代隐私的侵蚀对民主社会构成了前所未有的挑战。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "privacy" as "private-cy" — the state of keeping things private, away from the eyes of others.
把"privacy"想成"private-cy"——保持事物私密的状态，远离他人的目光。
