# 🔍 negligible

🔊 /ˈnɛɡlɪdʒəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** So small, trifling, or unimportant as to be not worth considering; insignificant
**中文：** adj. 微不足道的；可以忽略的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- insignificant（无关紧要的）
- trivial（微不足道的）
- minimal（极小的）
- inconsequential（不重要的）
- trifling（微不足道的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **negligible impact / negligible amount / negligible difference / negligible risk**

👉 Example: The cost difference between the two options was negligible and did not affect the final decision.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The environmental impact of the proposed construction project was deemed **negligible** by the independent assessment committee.
   拟议建设项目的环境影响被独立评估委员会认定为可以忽略不计。

2. Researchers found that the statistical difference between the control group and the test group was **negligible**.
   研究人员发现对照组和实验组之间的统计差异微乎其微。

3. The additional fuel consumption resulting from the modified engine design proved to be **negligible** in extensive field tests.
   在广泛的实地测试中，改进发动机设计导致的额外燃油消耗被证明微不足道。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "negligible" as something you can "neglect" — if something is negligible, it is so small that you can safely neglect or ignore it.
把"negligible"想成你可以"neglect（忽略）"的东西——如果某事微不足道，它小到你可以安全地忽略它。
