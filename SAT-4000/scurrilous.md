# 😤 scurrilous

🔊 /ˈskʌrɪləs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Vulgar; using or given to coarse, abusive, or indecent language; grossly offensive
**中文：** adj. 说话粗鄙恶劣的；下流的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- defamatory（诽谤的）
- slanderous（中伤的）
- vulgar（粗俗的）
- abusive（辱骂的）
- libelous（诋毁的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **scurrilous attack / scurrilous remarks / scurrilous allegations / scurrilous language**

👉 Example: The newspaper published scurrilous allegations against the senator without any credible evidence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The political opponent launched a **scurrilous** campaign filled with baseless accusations designed to destroy the incumbent's reputation.
   政治对手发起了一场充满无端指控的恶毒运动，旨在摧毁现任者的声誉。

2. The court found the defendant liable for publishing **scurrilous** statements that deliberately misrepresented the plaintiff's character.
   法院认定被告因发布故意歪曲原告品格的恶毒言论而承担责任。

3. Academic discourse should remain civil and evidence-based rather than devolving into **scurrilous** personal attacks against colleagues.
   学术讨论应保持文明和以证据为基础，而不应沦为对同事的恶毒人身攻击。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "scurrilous" as "scur-rile-ous" — it sounds like "rile" which means to irritate, and scurrilous remarks are designed to rile people up with vulgar language.
把"scurrilous"想成包含"rile"（激怒）——粗鄙恶劣的言辞就是用下流的语言来激怒别人。
