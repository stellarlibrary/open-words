# 🏚️ squalid

🔊 /ˈskwɒlɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Extremely dirty, unclean, and unpleasant; morally degrading or sordid; showing neglect and poverty
**中文：** adj. 肮脏的；卑劣的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- filthy（肮脏的）
- sordid（卑鄙的）
- wretched（悲惨的）
- decrepit（破旧的）
- grimy（污秽的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **squalid conditions / squalid dwelling / squalid affair / squalid neighborhood**

👉 Example: The humanitarian report described the squalid conditions in which thousands of refugees were forced to live.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The investigative journalist documented the **squalid** living conditions endured by migrant workers in the overcrowded industrial dormitories.
   调查记者记录了外来务工人员在拥挤的工业宿舍中所忍受的肮脏生活条件。

2. The once-prosperous district had deteriorated into a **squalid** slum plagued by poverty, disease, and rampant criminal activity.
   这个曾经繁荣的地区已经沦为一个肮脏的贫民窟，饱受贫困、疾病和猖獗犯罪活动的困扰。

3. The novel exposed the **squalid** corruption that pervaded every level of the colonial administration during that turbulent era.
   这部小说揭露了在那个动荡时期弥漫于殖民政府各个层级的卑劣腐败。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "squalid" as "squall-id" — after a squall or violent storm, everything is left dirty, damaged, and in terrible condition.
把"squalid"想成"squall-id"——暴风雨过后，一切都变得肮脏、破损，处于糟糕的状态。
