# ⚔️ fray

🔊 /freɪ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A fight, battle, or heated argument. (v.) To wear away by rubbing; to become strained or tense
**中文：** n. 争论；打架 v. 磨损；使紧张

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unravel（磨损；拆散）
- wear（磨损）
- brawl（争吵；打架）
- skirmish（小冲突）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **enter the fray / nerves fray / edges fray / tempers fray**

👉 Example: After hours of heated negotiation, tempers began to fray on both sides of the table.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The constant friction caused the rope to **fray** at the edges, eventually making it too weak to support any weight.
   持续的摩擦导致绳子边缘磨损，最终使其无法承受任何重量。

2. Several prominent politicians entered the **fray** over the proposed legislation, each offering sharply different viewpoints on the matter.
   几位知名政客加入了关于拟议立法的争论，各自提出了截然不同的观点。

3. Diplomatic relations between the two countries began to **fray** as trade disputes intensified throughout the fiscal year.
   随着贸易争端在整个财政年度不断加剧，两国之间的外交关系开始变得紧张。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fray" rhyming with "decay" — things that fray are decaying, wearing out, falling apart at the edges.
把"fray"想成和"decay"（衰败）押韵——磨损的东西就是在衰败，在边缘处散开。
