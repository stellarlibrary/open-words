# 💪 bravado

🔊 /brəˈvɑːdoʊ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A bold manner or show of courage intended to impress or intimidate; a pretense of bravery
**中文：** n. 虚张声势；故作勇敢；装作有自信的样子

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bluster（虚张声势）
- swagger（大摇大摆）
- bravura（炫技）
- audacity（大胆）
- boastfulness（自夸）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sheer bravado / false bravado / with bravado / an act of bravado**

👉 Example: His confident speech was mere bravado, masking deep uncertainty about the outcome.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Behind his apparent **bravado**, the young soldier harbored profound fears about the dangers awaiting him on the battlefield.
   在他表面的虚张声势背后，这个年轻士兵对战场上等待他的危险怀有深深的恐惧。

2. The politician's **bravado** during the debate could not conceal her lack of substantive policy proposals from the audience.
   这位政治家在辩论中的虚张声势无法掩盖她在观众面前缺乏实质性政策提案的事实。

3. Critics dismissed his public **bravado** as a transparent attempt to distract from the company's deteriorating financial performance.
   批评者认为他公开的虚张声势不过是转移人们对公司财务状况恶化关注的拙劣企图。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bravado" as "brave + ado" — making a big fuss (ado) about being brave, but it is all just a show.
把"bravado"想成"brave + ado"——对勇敢大做文章（ado），但一切不过是做做样子。
