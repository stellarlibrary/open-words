# 🌀 disorder

🔊 /dɪsˈɔːrdər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A state of confusion, disarray, or lack of order; a medical condition involving a disturbance of function. (v.) To disturb the systematic arrangement of; to upset the normal condition
**中文：** n. 混乱；无序；失调 v. 使失调；使紊乱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- chaos（混乱）
- disarray（混乱）
- turmoil（动荡）
- confusion（困惑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mental disorder / in disorder / public disorder / disorder of the immune system**

👉 Example: The sudden evacuation threw the entire building into complete disorder.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Prolonged exposure to extreme stress can contribute to the development of serious psychological **disorders** requiring professional intervention.
   长期处于极端压力下可能导致严重的心理障碍，需要专业干预。

2. The unexpected resignation of the committee chair threw the entire legislative agenda into considerable **disorder**.
   委员会主席的意外辞职使整个立法议程陷入了相当大的混乱。

3. Civil **disorder** erupted throughout several provinces after the government announced sweeping austerity measures without public consultation.
   政府在未经公众协商的情况下宣布大规模紧缩措施后，多个省份爆发了社会骚乱。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-order" as "the opposite of order" — when order is removed, everything falls into chaos and confusion.
把"disorder"想成"dis（相反）+ order（秩序）"——当秩序被打破时，一切陷入混乱。
