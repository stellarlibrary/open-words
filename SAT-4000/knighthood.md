# ⚔️ knighthood

🔊 /ˈnaɪthʊd/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The rank, dignity, or status of a knight; the qualities associated with being a knight, such as chivalry and valor
**中文：** n. 骑士身份；骑士风格；侠义精神

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- chivalry（骑士精神）
- gallantry（英勇）
- valor（勇气）
- nobility（高贵）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **receive knighthood / confer knighthood / bestow knighthood / the age of knighthood**

👉 Example: The queen conferred knighthood upon the distinguished scientist for his contributions to medical research.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The celebrated author was awarded **knighthood** by the monarchy in recognition of her extraordinary contributions to literature.
   这位著名作家因其对文学的杰出贡献而被王室授予骑士身份。

2. Medieval chronicles describe the rituals of **knighthood** as elaborate ceremonies involving oaths of loyalty and sacred vows.
   中世纪编年史将骑士封授仪式描述为包含忠诚誓言和神圣誓约的精心典礼。

3. The professor's lecture explored how the ideals of **knighthood** influenced European social norms throughout the Middle Ages.
   教授的讲座探讨了骑士精神的理想如何在整个中世纪影响了欧洲的社会规范。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "knighthood" as "knight + hood" — the "hood" suffix means a state or condition, so knighthood is the state of being a knight.
把"knighthood"想成"knight（骑士）+ hood（状态）"——hood后缀表示一种状态，所以knighthood就是作为骑士的身份状态。
