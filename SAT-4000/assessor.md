# 👨‍⚖️ assessor

🔊 /əˈsesər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A person who evaluates or estimates the value of property for taxation purposes; an expert who provides advice or assists in making judgments.
**中文：** n. 评审员；估税员

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- evaluator（评估者）
- appraiser（鉴定人）
- examiner（审查员）
- inspector（检查员）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use **assessor** for a person whose job is to assess. In legal contexts, an assessor advises a judge; in taxation, an assessor determines property values. Common collocations: "tax assessor," "property assessor," "independent assessor."

👉 Example: The county tax assessor determined the current market value of the property.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The property **assessor** visited the newly renovated building to determine its updated market value for tax purposes.
   房产估税员参观了新翻修的大楼，以确定其用于征税的最新市场价值。

2. An independent **assessor** was appointed to evaluate the candidates' qualifications for the prestigious academic scholarship program.
   一名独立评审员被任命来评估候选人申请这项著名学术奖学金项目的资格。

3. The court appointed a qualified **assessor** to provide expert technical advice during the complex intellectual property trial.
   法院指定了一名合格的评审员，在复杂的知识产权审判中提供专业技术建议。

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Assessor" = "assess" + "-or" (person suffix). Just as a "professor" professes, an "assessor" assesses. Think of the person behind the evaluation clipboard.
"assessor"="assess"+"-or"（表示人的后缀）。就像"professor（教授）"是profess的人一样，"assessor"就是assess的人。想象一个拿着评估表格的人。
