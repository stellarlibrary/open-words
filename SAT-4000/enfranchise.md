# 🗳️ enfranchise

🔊 /ɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To grant the right to vote; to set free from bondage or legal disability; to liberate
**中文：** v. 给予选举权；解放；释放

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- emancipate（解放）
- liberate（使自由）
- empower（赋权）
- free（释放）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **enfranchise citizens / enfranchise women / enfranchise minorities / newly enfranchised voters**

👉 Example: The constitutional amendment enfranchised millions of citizens who had been denied the right to vote.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The landmark legislation sought to **enfranchise** all adult citizens regardless of race, gender, or socioeconomic background.
   这项具有里程碑意义的立法旨在赋予所有成年公民选举权，不论种族、性别或社会经济背景。

2. Historians credit the suffrage movement with **enfranchising** women and fundamentally transforming the democratic process in the early twentieth century.
   历史学家认为妇女选举权运动赋予了女性选举权，并在二十世纪初从根本上改变了民主进程。

3. The newly **enfranchised** population eagerly participated in the first free elections held after decades of authoritarian rule.
   新获得选举权的民众积极参与了在数十年独裁统治后举行的首次自由选举。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "enfranchise" as "en-FRANCHISE" — to grant someone a franchise or stake in the political system through voting rights.
把"enfranchise"想成"en-FRANCHISE"——通过投票权授予某人在政治体系中的特许权或参与权。
