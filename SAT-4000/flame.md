# 🔥 flame

🔊 /fleɪm/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The visible, burning gas that rises from a fire; intense passion or emotion. (v.) To burn with flames; to burst into flame
**中文：** n. 火焰；热情 v. 焚烧；燃烧

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- blaze（火焰）
- fire（火）
- inferno（烈火）
- passion（热情）
- burn（燃烧）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **burst into flames / fan the flames / flame of passion / old flame / go up in flames**

👉 Example: The candle's gentle flame illuminated the ancient manuscript resting on the scholar's desk.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The entire warehouse went up in **flames** before the firefighters could arrive and establish a perimeter around the building.
   整个仓库在消防员赶到并在建筑物周围建立警戒线之前就已被大火吞噬。

2. The poet's words **flamed** with passionate conviction, inspiring a generation of young writers to pursue literary excellence.
   诗人的文字燃烧着热切的信念，激励了一代年轻作家追求文学卓越。

3. A single **flame** of curiosity can ignite a lifelong dedication to scientific inquiry and intellectual exploration.
   一丝好奇的火焰就能点燃对科学探索和知识追求的毕生热忱。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "flame" as "f-lame" — fire is never lame; it is always bright, hot, and captivating, just like a burning flame.
把"flame"想成火焰跳动的画面——火焰永远不会"lame"（无趣），它总是明亮、炽热且引人注目。
