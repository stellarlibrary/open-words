# ✨ foreordain

🔊 /ˌfɔːrɔːrˈdeɪn/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To determine or appoint beforehand; to predestine or decree in advance
**中文：** v. 注定；预定命运；预先决定

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- predestine（命中注定）
- predetermine（预先决定）
- destine（注定）
- ordain（命令）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **foreordained by fate / foreordained outcome / foreordained to fail / foreordained destiny**

👉 Example: Many believed that the empire's collapse was foreordained by centuries of internal decay.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosopher argued that human destiny is not **foreordained** but shaped by the conscious choices individuals make daily.
   这位哲学家认为，人类的命运并非注定的，而是由个人每天做出的有意识的选择所塑造的。

2. Critics contended that the outcome of the trial was effectively **foreordained** by the overwhelming weight of physical evidence.
   批评人士认为，审判的结果实际上已被压倒性的物证所预先决定。

3. Theologians have long debated whether historical events are **foreordained** by divine will or arise from purely human agency.
   神学家们长期以来一直在争论历史事件究竟是由神的旨意注定的，还是纯粹出于人类的作为。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fore + ordain" — to ordain (officially decree) something before it happens, meaning it was already decided.
把"foreordain"想成"fore + ordain"——在事情发生之前就下达命令（正式决定），意味着早已注定。
