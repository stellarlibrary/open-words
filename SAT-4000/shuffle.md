# 🔀 shuffle

🔊 /ˈʃʌfəl/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To mix cards randomly; to shift responsibility; to walk dragging one's feet. (n.) The act of mixing cards; disorder; a dragging walk
**中文：** v. 搅乱；洗牌

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rearrange（重新排列）
- scramble（搅乱）
- drag（拖曳）
- mix（混合）
- shamble（蹒跚）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **shuffle the deck / shuffle one's feet / shuffle off responsibility / shuffle through papers**

👉 Example: The dealer began to shuffle the deck before distributing cards to each player at the table.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The bureaucrat attempted to **shuffle** responsibility onto subordinates rather than accept blame for the oversight.
   这位官僚试图把责任推卸给下属，而不是承担疏忽的过错。

2. Passengers began to **shuffle** slowly toward the exit as the announcement signaled the train's arrival at the station.
   当广播提示列车到站时，乘客们开始慢吞吞地向出口挪动。

3. The editor decided to **shuffle** the chapters to create a more compelling narrative structure for the manuscript.
   编辑决定重新调整章节顺序，为手稿打造更引人入胜的叙事结构。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "shuffle" as "shift + muffle" — you shift things around in a muffled, slow, disorderly way, like shuffling cards or feet.
把"shuffle"想成"shift（移动）+ muffle（含糊）"——你以含混、缓慢、无序的方式移动东西，就像洗牌或拖着脚走。
