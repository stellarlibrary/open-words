# 🏃 forerun

🔊 /fɔːrˈrʌn/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To serve as a sign or precursor of something to come; to run or go ahead of
**中文：** v. 预示；跑在…之前；预告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- precede（先于）
- foreshadow（预示）
- herald（预报）
- presage（预示）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **forerun a crisis / forerun change / forerun an era / signs that forerun**

👉 Example: Subtle shifts in public opinion often forerun major political transformations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The minor tremors that shook the region were believed to **forerun** a far more catastrophic seismic event.
   震撼该地区的轻微震动被认为是一次更具灾难性的地震事件的预兆。

2. Historians note that periods of intense artistic innovation frequently **forerun** sweeping social and political revolutions.
   历史学家注意到，艺术创新活跃的时期往往预示着大规模的社会和政治变革。

3. The rapid advances in artificial intelligence may **forerun** a fundamental transformation in the global labor market structure.
   人工智能的快速进步可能预示着全球劳动力市场结构的根本性变革。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fore + run" — something that runs before you, like a scout running ahead to signal what is coming.
把"forerun"想成"fore + run"——跑在你前面的东西，就像侦察兵跑在前方预告即将到来的事物。
