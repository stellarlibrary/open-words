# 🗣️ accost

🔊 /əˈkɔːst/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To approach and address someone boldly or aggressively, often in an unwelcome manner.
**中文：** 搭讪；勾引；（冒昧地）上前与…搭话

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- confront（对质）
- approach（接近）
- solicit（搭讪）
- waylay（拦住）
- buttonhole（拦住搭话）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **accost someone in/on (a place)**

👉 Example: A stranger accosted him on the street demanding money.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist **accosted** the senator outside the courthouse, demanding answers about the scandal.
   记者在法院外拦住了参议员，要求他就丑闻作出回答。

2. Visitors to the district are frequently **accosted** by aggressive street vendors selling counterfeit goods.
   来到这个地区的游客经常被兜售假货的强势街头小贩搭讪。

3. She was **accosted** by a stranger who insisted on showing her an unsolicited business proposal.
   一个陌生人拦住她，执意要向她展示一份不请自来的商业提案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think "a-COST" — someone approaches you and it "costs" you your peace; they accost you uninvited.
accost 听起来像"额，cost（花费）"，想象有人搭讪你，"花费"你的时间和安宁。
