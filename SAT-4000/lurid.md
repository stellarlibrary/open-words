# 😱 lurid

🔊 /ˈlʊərɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Shocking or sensational, especially in a gruesome or vivid way; unnaturally pale or ghastly; glowing with an unnatural reddish color
**中文：** 苍白的；可怕的；血红的；耸人听闻的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gruesome（可怕的）
- sensational（耸人听闻的）
- ghastly（骇人的）
- macabre（恐怖的）
- shocking（令人震惊的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **lurid details / lurid headlines / lurid tales / lurid description / lurid glow**

👉 Example: The tabloid newspaper was notorious for publishing lurid details about the private lives of celebrities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist was criticized for including **lurid** descriptions of the crime scene that served no legitimate informational purpose.
   这位记者因在报道中加入了毫无正当信息价值的耸人听闻的犯罪现场描述而遭到批评。

2. A **lurid** orange glow from the distant wildfire illuminated the night sky and filled the community with deep apprehension.
   远处野火发出的诡异橙红色光芒照亮了夜空，使整个社区充满了深深的忧虑。

3. The prosecution presented **lurid** evidence that visibly shocked the jury and fundamentally altered the trajectory of the trial.
   控方出示了令人震惊的证据，明显震惊了陪审团，从根本上改变了审判的走向。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "lurid" as "lure-id" — something so shocking it lures your attention like a sensational headline you cannot ignore.
把"lurid"想成"lure（引诱）+ id"——某事耸人听闻到像一个无法忽视的轰动标题一样吸引你的注意力。
