# 😈 unscrupulous

🔊 /ʌnˈskruːpjʊləs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Having or showing no moral principles; not honest or fair; willing to act in a dishonest way to get what one wants
**中文：** 肆无忌惮的；不讲道德的；不择手段的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unprincipled（无原则的）
- unethical（不道德的）
- dishonest（不诚实的）
- ruthless（无情的）
- unconscionable（不合理的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **unscrupulous businessman / unscrupulous tactics / unscrupulous behavior / unscrupulous dealer**

👉 Example: The unscrupulous landlord exploited vulnerable tenants by charging exorbitant fees for basic repairs.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The regulatory agency imposed severe penalties on the **unscrupulous** company that had falsified its environmental safety reports.
   监管机构对这家伪造环境安全报告的无良公司处以了严厉的处罚。

2. Consumers are advised to remain vigilant against **unscrupulous** vendors who sell counterfeit products at inflated prices.
   建议消费者对以虚高价格销售假冒产品的不法商贩保持警惕。

3. The documentary exposed how **unscrupulous** recruiters exploited migrant workers through deceptive employment contracts and hidden fees.
   这部纪录片揭露了不道德的招聘者如何通过欺骗性的雇佣合同和隐藏费用来剥削外来务工人员。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "un-scruple-ous" as "without scruples" — a scruple is a moral doubt, so someone unscrupulous has no moral doubts about doing wrong.
把"unscrupulous"想成"un（没有）+ scruple（良心不安）+ ous"——scruple是道德上的顾虑，所以unscrupulous的人做坏事毫无顾虑。
