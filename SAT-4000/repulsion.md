# 🧲 repulsion

🔊 /rɪˈpʌlʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A feeling of intense distaste or disgust; the force by which bodies or particles are driven apart or tend to repel each other
**中文：** 排斥；反感；厌恶；斥力；反驳

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- revulsion（厌恶）
- disgust（反感）
- aversion（嫌恶）
- loathing（憎恨）
- abhorrence（厌恶）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **feel repulsion / magnetic repulsion / a sense of repulsion / mutual repulsion**

👉 Example: She could not hide her repulsion at the sight of the unsanitary conditions in the facility.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scientist demonstrated that electromagnetic **repulsion** between like charges causes the particles to accelerate away from each other.
   科学家证明，同种电荷之间的电磁斥力使粒子彼此加速远离。

2. The audience reacted with visible **repulsion** when the documentary revealed the full extent of the environmental contamination.
   当纪录片揭示了环境污染的全部严重程度时，观众表现出明显的厌恶反应。

3. His initial **repulsion** toward the unfamiliar cuisine gradually transformed into genuine appreciation after living abroad for a year.
   他对陌生菜肴最初的反感在国外生活一年后逐渐转变为真正的欣赏。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "repulsion" as "re-pulsion" — a repeated pulse pushing things apart, like magnets of the same pole repelling each other.
把"repulsion"想成"re-pulsion"——反复的脉冲把东西推开，就像同极磁铁互相排斥一样。
