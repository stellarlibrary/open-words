# ⚖️ incriminate

🔊 /ɪnˈkrɪmɪneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make someone appear guilty of a crime or wrongdoing; to charge with or involve in a crime
**中文：** v. 控告；暗示有罪；使负罪

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- implicate（牵连）
- accuse（指控）
- indict（起诉）
- charge（控告）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **incriminate oneself / incriminating evidence / incriminate someone in / incriminating documents**

👉 Example: The recovered emails contained incriminating evidence that linked the executive to the financial fraud scheme.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The witness refused to testify on the grounds that her statements might **incriminate** her in the ongoing investigation.
   证人拒绝作证，理由是她的陈述可能会使她在正在进行的调查中被牵连入罪。

2. Forensic analysts discovered **incriminating** financial records that directly connected the official to the embezzlement operation.
   法医分析师发现了直接将该官员与挪用公款行动联系起来的有罪证据。

3. The Fifth Amendment protects citizens from being compelled to **incriminate** themselves during criminal judicial proceedings.
   第五修正案保护公民在刑事司法程序中不被强迫自证其罪。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-crimin-ate" as "put into crime" (crimin = crime in Latin) — to incriminate someone is to put them into a criminal situation.
把"incriminate"拆分为"in（进入）+ crimin（犯罪）+ ate"——让某人陷入犯罪之中，就是控告、使负罪。
