# 🌊 waver

🔊 /ˈweɪvər/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To become unsteady; to falter in resolve or determination; to fluctuate or sway. (n.) An act of wavering; hesitation
**中文：** v. 摇曳；踌躇；摆动；减弱；动摇 n. 动摇；踌躇；挥动者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- hesitate（犹豫）
- falter（动摇）
- vacillate（踌躇）
- fluctuate（波动）
- oscillate（摆动）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **waver in one's resolve / waver between options / never waver / waver on a decision**

👉 Example: The committee began to waver in its support for the controversial legislation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Despite mounting criticism from the media, the director refused to **waver** in her commitment to the ambitious project.
   尽管面临媒体越来越多的批评，这位主管拒绝在她对这个雄心勃勃项目的承诺上动摇。

2. Public confidence in the administration began to **waver** after a series of poorly handled economic crises emerged.
   在一系列处理不当的经济危机出现后，公众对政府的信心开始动摇。

3. The young scholar's determination never **wavered**, even when his peers abandoned the challenging line of research entirely.
   这位年轻学者的决心从未动摇，即使他的同行们完全放弃了这一具有挑战性的研究方向。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "waver" as a "wave" that moves back and forth — just like someone who wavers cannot stay firm in one position.
把"waver"想成"wave（波浪）"——波浪来回摆动，就像一个犹豫不决的人无法坚定立场一样。
