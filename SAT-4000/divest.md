# 🧹 divest

🔊 /daɪˈvɛst/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To strip or deprive of something, especially rights, property, or clothing; to rid oneself of
**中文：** v. 剥夺；使脱去；放弃

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- strip（剥夺）
- deprive（使丧失）
- dispossess（剥夺）
- relieve（解除）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **divest oneself of / divest from / divest of power / divest holdings**

👉 Example: The corporation announced plans to divest itself of all overseas subsidiaries by the end of the fiscal year.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board voted unanimously to **divest** the company of its underperforming assets in the international market.
   董事会一致投票决定剥离公司在国际市场上表现不佳的资产。

2. Several prominent universities pledged to **divest** from fossil fuel companies in response to growing environmental concerns.
   几所知名大学承诺从化石燃料公司撤资，以回应日益增长的环境问题。

3. The new legislation sought to **divest** the monarch of certain hereditary privileges that were deemed incompatible with democracy.
   新立法旨在剥夺君主某些被认为与民主不相容的世袭特权。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "divest" as the opposite of "invest" — if you invest to put something in, you divest to take something away.
把"divest"想成"invest（投资）"的反义——投资是投入，剥夺是拿走。
