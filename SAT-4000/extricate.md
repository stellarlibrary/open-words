# 🪢 extricate

🔊 /ˈekstrɪkeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To free someone or something from a constraint, difficulty, or entanglement; to disengage or release
**中文：** v. 使解脱；解救；使游离；救出；使脱离

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disentangle（解开）
- liberate（解放）
- release（释放）
- free（释放）
- rescue（营救）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **extricate oneself from / extricate from a situation / extricate from danger / extricate from debt**

👉 Example: The diplomat worked tirelessly to extricate himself from the political scandal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Emergency responders worked for hours to **extricate** the trapped passengers from the wreckage of the collapsed overpass.
   应急救援人员花了数小时从倒塌的天桥残骸中救出被困乘客。

2. The company struggled to **extricate** itself from the contractual obligations that had become financially unsustainable over time.
   该公司努力从已变得在财务上不可持续的合同义务中脱身。

3. Skilled negotiators managed to **extricate** the hostages from the dangerous standoff without any casualties or further escalation.
   熟练的谈判人员设法在没有伤亡或进一步升级的情况下将人质从危险的对峙中解救出来。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "extricate" as "ex- (out) + tric (tricks/tangles) + ate" — to work your way out of a tricky tangle.
把"extricate"想成"ex-（出来）+ tric（诡计/纠缠）+ ate"——从棘手的纠缠中找到出路。
