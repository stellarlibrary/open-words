# 😤 vulgar

🔊 /ˈvʌlɡər/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Lacking sophistication or good taste; crude and unrefined; relating to the common people; coarse and rude in manner or language
**中文：** adj. 粗俗的；庸俗的；普通的；通俗的；本土的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- crude（粗鲁的）
- coarse（粗俗的）
- uncouth（粗野的）
- boorish（粗鄙的）
- tasteless（低俗的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vulgar language / vulgar display / vulgar taste / vulgar humor / Vulgar Latin**

👉 Example: The critic dismissed the artwork as a vulgar display of wealth that lacked any genuine artistic merit.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator's **vulgar** remarks during the debate caused widespread outrage and prompted calls for a formal public apology.
   这位参议员在辩论中的粗俗言论引起了广泛愤怒，并引发了要求正式公开道歉的呼声。

2. Scholars of linguistics study **Vulgar** Latin as the spoken language from which the modern Romance languages ultimately evolved.
   语言学学者研究通俗拉丁语，因为它是现代罗曼语族最终演变而来的口语。

3. The once elegant neighborhood had been transformed by **vulgar** commercial developments that prioritized profit over aesthetic harmony.
   这个曾经优雅的社区已被粗俗的商业开发所改变，这些开发将利润置于美学和谐之上。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vulgar" as from Latin "vulgaris" meaning "of the common people" — what was common was considered lacking refinement.
把"vulgar"想成来自拉丁语"vulgaris"，意为"普通人的"——普通的东西被认为缺乏精致。
