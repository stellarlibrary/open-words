# 😤 tantrum

🔊 /ˈtæntrəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An uncontrolled outburst of anger and frustration, typically in a young child; a fit of bad temper
**中文：** n. 发脾气

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- outburst（爆发）
- fit（发作）
- rage（暴怒）
- meltdown（情绪崩溃）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **throw a tantrum / have a tantrum / temper tantrum / childish tantrum**

👉 Example: The toddler threw a tantrum in the grocery store when his mother refused to buy candy.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The executive threw a **tantrum** during the board meeting when the directors unanimously rejected his proposed acquisition strategy.
   当董事们一致否决他提出的收购策略时，这位高管在董事会上大发脾气。

2. Political analysts described the leader's abrupt withdrawal from negotiations as little more than a diplomatic **tantrum**.
   政治分析人士将这位领导人突然退出谈判描述为不过是一场外交上的发脾气。

3. Rather than throwing a **tantrum** over the unfavorable review, the author responded with a measured and articulate rebuttal.
   作者没有因不利的评论而大发脾气，而是以一种克制而清晰的方式进行了反驳。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tantrum" as "tan-drum" — imagine someone so angry they are beating a drum (drum) until they are tan from the exertion.
把"tantrum"想成"tan-drum"——想象一个人气得猛敲鼓(drum)，直到累得脸色发红(tan)。
