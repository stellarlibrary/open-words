# 🧪 liquefy

🔊 /ˈlɪkwɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make or become liquid; to convert a solid or gas into a liquid state; to dissolve
**中文：** v. 液化；溶解；使成为液体

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- melt（融化）
- dissolve（溶解）
- thaw（解冻）
- flux（熔化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **liquefy a gas / liquefy under heat / liquefy into a solution**

👉 Example: The intense heat caused the metal to liquefy and flow into the cast.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Scientists discovered that certain gases **liquefy** at extremely low temperatures and high atmospheric pressure conditions.
   科学家发现某些气体在极低温度和高大气压条件下会液化。

2. The volcanic eruption caused surrounding rocks to **liquefy** into molten lava that cascaded down the mountainside.
   火山爆发使周围的岩石液化为熔岩，沿着山坡倾泻而下。

3. Engineers developed a new process to **liquefy** natural gas efficiently for safer long-distance transportation overseas.
   工程师们开发了一种新工艺，可以高效地将天然气液化，以便更安全地进行海外长途运输。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "liqu-efy" as "liquid + make" — to make something into a liquid form.
把"liquefy"想成"liquid（液体）+ make（制造）"——使某物变成液体形态。
