# 🎭 spurious

🔊 /ˈspjʊəriəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Not genuine, authentic, or true; false or fake; based on false reasoning or information
**中文：** adj. 伪造的，假的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- counterfeit（伪造的）
- fraudulent（欺诈的）
- bogus（假的）
- fabricated（捏造的）
- phony（虚假的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **spurious claim / spurious correlation / spurious argument / spurious evidence**

👉 Example: The scientist warned against drawing conclusions from spurious correlations in the data.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist exposed the **spurious** documents that had been used to justify the corporation's controversial land acquisition.
   记者揭露了被用来为该公司有争议的土地收购辩护的伪造文件。

2. Statisticians cautioned that the **spurious** correlation between the two variables did not imply any genuine causal relationship.
   统计学家警告说，这两个变量之间的虚假相关性并不意味着存在任何真正的因果关系。

3. The defense attorney argued that the prosecution's case relied entirely upon **spurious** evidence and unreliable witness testimony.
   辩护律师辩称，控方的案件完全依赖于伪造的证据和不可靠的证人证词。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "spurious" as "spur-ious" — something that spurs suspicion because it is fake and not what it claims to be.
把"spurious"想成"spur-ious"——引起怀疑的东西，因为它是假的，并非它所声称的那样。
