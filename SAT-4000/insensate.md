# 🪨 insensate

🔊 /ɪnˈsenseɪt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Lacking physical sensation; unfeeling or indifferent; without sense or reason
**中文：** 无感觉的；无情的；无生命的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unfeeling（无情的）
- numb（麻木的）
- inanimate（无生命的）
- callous（冷酷的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **insensate matter / insensate cruelty / insensate rage / insensate to pain**

👉 Example: The philosopher argued that insensate matter cannot possess consciousness or moral agency.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The dictator displayed **insensate** cruelty toward the civilian population, ignoring all international pleas for humanitarian restraint.
   独裁者对平民表现出毫无人性的残忍，无视所有国际社会的人道主义呼吁。

2. After hours of exposure to the freezing temperatures, the mountaineer's fingers became completely **insensate** and unresponsive.
   在零下温度中暴露数小时后，登山者的手指完全失去了知觉，毫无反应。

3. Critics described the policy as **insensate** to the genuine suffering of communities affected by the industrial pollution crisis.
   批评者认为该政策对受工业污染危机影响的社区的真实苦难麻木不仁。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-sens-ate" — "in" (not) + "sens" (sense). Without sense or feeling — like a stone that cannot feel anything.
把"insensate"想成"in（没有）+ sens（感觉）+ ate"——没有感觉的，就像石头一样什么也感受不到。
