# 😤 disparage

🔊 /dɪˈspærɪdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To speak of or treat with contempt; to belittle or degrade
**中文：** v. 蔑视；贬损；毁谤

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- belittle（贬低）
- denigrate（诋毁）
- deprecate（轻视）
- malign（诽谤）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **disparage someone's efforts / disparage a reputation / disparage achievements**

👉 Example: Critics often disparage innovative ideas before fully understanding their potential impact.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator publicly **disparaged** his opponent's record, calling the legislative accomplishments insignificant and overrated.
   这位参议员公开贬损对手的政绩，称其立法成就微不足道且被过度吹捧。

2. It is unprofessional to **disparage** a colleague's contributions during a formal academic conference presentation.
   在正式的学术会议演讲中贬低同事的贡献是不专业的行为。

3. The editorial **disparaged** the new policy as poorly conceived, lacking both empirical evidence and strategic foresight.
   这篇社论抨击新政策构思拙劣，既缺乏实证依据又缺乏战略远见。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-parage" as "dis" (negative) + "parage" (sounds like "peerage," meaning nobility) — to strip someone of their noble standing is to disparage them.
把"disparage"想成"dis"（否定）+"parage"（听起来像"peerage"，意为贵族身份）——剥夺某人的尊贵地位就是蔑视他们。
