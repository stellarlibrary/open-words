# 📂 fold

🔊 /foʊld/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To bend something over on itself so that one part covers another; to collapse or cease operating. (n.) A crease or line made by folding; an enclosed area for sheep
**中文：** v. 对折；折叠；合拢；倒闭 n. 折痕；褶皱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- crease（折痕）
- bend（弯折）
- collapse（倒塌）
- wrap（包裹）
- pleat（褶）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fold in half / fold arms / fold a letter / fold under pressure / return to the fold**

👉 Example: She carefully folded the document and placed it inside the envelope before sealing it shut.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The geologist explained how tectonic forces **fold** layers of rock over millions of years, creating mountain ranges and deep valleys.
   地质学家解释了构造力如何在数百万年中**折叠**岩层，形成山脉和深谷。

2. Unable to sustain further financial losses, the struggling company was forced to **fold** after only two years of operation.
   由于无法承受进一步的财务损失，这家陷入困境的公司在运营仅两年后被迫**倒闭**。

3. The artist used each **fold** in the paper to create intricate origami sculptures that displayed extraordinary precision and craftsmanship.
   艺术家利用纸上的每一道**折痕**创作出精美的折纸雕塑，展现了非凡的精确性和工艺。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fold" as what you do with a letter — you fold it to fit inside an envelope, creating a crease where the paper bends.
把"fold"想成你对信纸做的事——你把它折叠起来放进信封，在纸弯曲的地方留下折痕。
