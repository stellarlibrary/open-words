# ⚖️ undue

🔊 /ʌnˈdjuː/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Exceeding what is appropriate or normal; excessive, unwarranted, or disproportionate
**中文：** adj. 过度的，过分的；不适当的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- excessive（过度的）
- unwarranted（不正当的）
- disproportionate（不成比例的）
- unreasonable（不合理的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **undue influence / undue pressure / undue hardship / undue burden / undue delay**

👉 Example: The court ruled that the contract was invalid because it was signed under undue influence from a third party.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee expressed concern that the proposed regulation would place an **undue** burden on small businesses throughout the region.
   委员会担忧拟议的法规会给该地区的小型企业带来过度的负担。

2. Judges must ensure that no single party exercises **undue** influence over the proceedings or the outcome of the trial.
   法官必须确保任何一方都不会对诉讼程序或审判结果施加不当影响。

3. The project was completed without **undue** delay, despite the unexpected challenges that arose during the construction phase.
   尽管在施工阶段出现了意想不到的困难，项目还是没有过度延误地完成了。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "un-due" as something not due or not deserved — the amount of pressure or influence is more than what the situation calls for.
把"undue"想成"不应有的"——施加的压力或影响超出了情况所需要的。
