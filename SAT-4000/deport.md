# 🚪 deport

🔊 /dɪˈpɔːrt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To expel a person from a country, typically for legal violations; to conduct or behave oneself in a specified manner
**中文：** v. 驱逐出境；举止，表现

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- expel（驱逐）
- banish（放逐）
- exile（流放）
- behave（举止）
- conduct（表现）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **deport someone from a country / deport oneself with dignity / deport illegal immigrants**

👉 Example: The authorities decided to deport the foreign nationals who had overstayed their visas.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The federal government moved to **deport** several individuals who had entered the country without proper documentation.
   联邦政府采取行动驱逐了数名未持有合法证件入境的人员。

2. Despite the mounting pressure, she managed to **deport** herself with remarkable grace and composure throughout the trial.
   尽管压力不断增大，她在整个审判过程中仍举止优雅、沉着冷静。

3. International law prohibits nations from **deporting** refugees to territories where their lives would be endangered.
   国际法禁止各国将难民驱逐到其生命可能受到威胁的地区。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-port" as "away from the port" — someone is sent away from the port of entry, expelled from the country.
把"deport"想成"de（离开）+ port（港口）"——某人被从入境口岸赶走，即被驱逐出境。
