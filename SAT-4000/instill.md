# 💧 instill

🔊 /ɪnˈstɪl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To gradually but firmly establish an idea, attitude, or habit in a person's mind; to introduce slowly, drop by drop
**中文：** v. 徐徐滴入；逐渐灌输

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- implant（灌输）
- inculcate（谆谆教导）
- infuse（注入）
- imbue（灌输）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **instill confidence / instill values / instill a sense of / instill discipline / instill fear**

👉 Example: Effective educators instill a genuine love of learning in their students from an early age.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The leadership program was designed to **instill** a strong sense of ethical responsibility in future corporate executives.
   该领导力项目旨在向未来的企业高管逐渐灌输强烈的道德责任感。

2. Her parents worked diligently to **instill** the values of perseverance and integrity throughout her formative childhood years.
   她的父母在她成长的童年岁月中努力灌输毅力和正直的价值观。

3. The military academy sought to **instill** unwavering discipline and a deep commitment to service in every new cadet.
   军事学院力求在每一位新学员心中逐步灌输坚定的纪律和对服务的深度承诺。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "instill" as "in-still" — pouring knowledge in slowly, like a still (distiller) that drips liquid one drop at a time.
把"instill"想成"in-still"——像蒸馏器一样一滴一滴地慢慢注入知识。
