# ⚔️ enmity

🔊 /ˈenmɪti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A state or feeling of active opposition or hostility; deep-seated hatred between parties
**中文：** n. 敌意；憎恨；仇恨

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- hostility（敌意）
- animosity（仇恨）
- antagonism（对抗）
- hatred（憎恶）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **enmity between / enmity toward / deep enmity / bitter enmity / long-standing enmity**

👉 Example: Centuries of enmity between the two nations made diplomatic reconciliation extremely difficult.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The bitter **enmity** between the rival political factions threatened to destabilize the entire region for generations.
   敌对政治派系之间的深仇大恨有可能使整个地区几代人都陷入动荡。

2. Despite years of personal **enmity**, the two senators agreed to collaborate on the crucial bipartisan legislation.
   尽管多年来彼此敌意深重，两位参议员还是同意就这项关键的两党立法进行合作。

3. The historian documented how religious **enmity** during the medieval period led to devastating conflicts across Europe.
   这位历史学家记录了中世纪时期的宗教仇恨如何导致了整个欧洲的毁灭性冲突。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "enmity" as coming from "enemy" — enmity is the feeling that exists between enemies, a state of deep hostility.
把"enmity"想成来自"enemy（敌人）"——enmity就是敌人之间存在的那种深深的敌意。
