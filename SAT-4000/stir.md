# 🌀 stir

🔊 /stɜːr/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A commotion or sensation. (v.) To move or mix something with a circular motion; to arouse or provoke feelings
**中文：** v. 搅拌，搅动；动，摇动；激动；轰动；煽动，鼓动

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- agitate（搅动）
- rouse（激起）
- provoke（激发）
- commotion（骚动）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **stir emotions / stir up trouble / stir controversy / cause a stir / stir the pot**

👉 Example: The controversial documentary stirred intense debate among scholars and the general public alike.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician's inflammatory speech was designed to **stir** nationalist sentiments and rally public support before the election.
   那位政客的煽动性演讲旨在激起民族主义情绪，并在选举前争取公众支持。

2. The discovery of ancient artifacts beneath the city center caused quite a **stir** among archaeologists and historians worldwide.
   在市中心地下发现古代文物在全球考古学家和历史学家中引起了不小的轰动。

3. The author's memoir **stirred** profound empathy in readers who had experienced similar hardships during their own formative years.
   这位作者的回忆录在那些成长过程中经历过类似困境的读者心中激起了深深的共鸣。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "stir" as stirring a pot — just as you move a spoon in circles, stirring emotions means moving people's feelings around.
把"stir"想成搅动一锅汤——就像你用勺子画圈搅拌一样，stirring emotions就是搅动人们的情感。
