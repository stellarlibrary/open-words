# 🏔️ hermit

🔊 /ˈhɜːrmɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A person who lives in seclusion, especially for religious contemplation; a recluse
**中文：** n. 隐士；隐居者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- recluse（隐居者）
- loner（独居者）
- solitary（独居之人）
- ascetic（苦行者）
- anchorite（隐修者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **live like a hermit / become a hermit / hermit lifestyle / hermit crab**

👉 Example: After retiring from public life, the former diplomat chose to live like a hermit in the mountains.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The medieval **hermit** spent decades living in a remote cave, dedicating his existence entirely to prayer and spiritual reflection.
   这位中世纪隐士在一个偏远的洞穴中生活了数十年，将毕生精力完全献给了祈祷和精神反思。

2. Although she was a brilliant scientist, she preferred the life of a **hermit**, rarely attending conferences or social gatherings.
   尽管她是一位杰出的科学家，但她更喜欢隐居的生活，很少参加会议或社交聚会。

3. The philosopher's reputation as a **hermit** only increased public curiosity about his unconventional theories and mysterious lifestyle.
   这位哲学家作为隐士的名声只增加了公众对他非传统理论和神秘生活方式的好奇心。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "hermit" as "her-mit" — she (her) knitted (mit/mitten) alone in solitude, like a hermit living apart from society.
把"hermit"想成"her-mit"——她（her）独自编织手套（mit/mitten），就像隐士远离社会独居一样。
