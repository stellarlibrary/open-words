# 🚫 balk

🔊 /bɔːk/<br>
🏷️ *v. n. | 动词 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** (v.) To hesitate or be unwilling to proceed; to refuse or recoil from something; to obstruct or prevent. (n.) An obstacle, hindrance, or impediment.

**中文：** （动词）犹豫不决；畏缩不前；阻止，阻碍。（名词）障碍，阻碍物。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- hesitate（犹豫）
- recoil（畏缩）
- refuse（拒绝）
- hinder（阻碍）
- obstacle（障碍）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Balk" is typically followed by "at" when used as a verb: "She balked at the idea." It conveys a strong reluctance or refusal, often triggered by fear, moral objection, or practical concern. In baseball, a "balk" is an illegal motion by the pitcher that allows base runners to advance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Several board members **balked** at the proposed merger, fearing it would compromise the company's long-standing independence.
   几位董事会成员对提议的合并**犹豫不决**，担心这会损害公司长期以来的独立性。

2. The experienced horse suddenly **balked** at the water crossing, refusing to advance despite its rider's persistent urging.
   那匹经验丰富的马在过水处突然**畏缩不前**，尽管骑手不断催促仍拒绝前进。

3. Bureaucratic regulations continue to **balk** innovation in the technology sector, frustrating entrepreneurs who seek rapid market entry.
   官僚法规继续**阻碍**科技行业的创新，令寻求快速进入市场的企业家感到沮丧。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Balk" sounds like "block" — when you balk, you mentally block yourself from moving forward. Imagine walking toward something scary and your feet just block (balk) in place.

"Balk"听起来像"block（阻挡）"——当你balk时，你在心理上阻止自己前进。想象走向可怕的东西时，你的脚就像被阻挡（balk）住了一样停在原地。
