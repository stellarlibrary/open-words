# 🌌 primordial

🔊 /praɪˈmɔːrdiəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Existing at or from the very beginning of time; fundamental, basic, or essential in origin
**中文：** adj. 原始的；根本的；原生的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- primeval（原始的）
- primal（最初的）
- elemental（基本的）
- aboriginal（原始的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **primordial soup / primordial instinct / primordial force / primordial chaos**

👉 Example: Scientists believe that life on Earth originated in the primordial soup of organic molecules billions of years ago.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **primordial** forces that shaped the universe continue to fascinate physicists studying the origins of matter and energy.
   塑造宇宙的原始力量继续吸引着研究物质和能量起源的物理学家。

2. Deep within the ocean trench, researchers discovered organisms thriving in conditions reminiscent of Earth's **primordial** environment.
   在深海海沟中，研究人员发现了在类似地球原始环境的条件下繁衍生息的生物体。

3. The philosopher argued that the desire for community is a **primordial** human impulse rooted in our earliest evolutionary history.
   这位哲学家认为，对群体的渴望是一种根植于我们最早进化历史中的原始人类冲动。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "primordial" as "prime" + "ordial" (order) — the prime order of things, the very first arrangement from which everything else emerged.
把"primordial"想成"prime（最初的）"+"ordial（秩序）"——事物的最初秩序，万物由此诞生的最原始排列。
