# 🩹 mutilate

🔊 /ˈmjuːtɪleɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To inflict violent damage on a body or limb; to destroy or severely damage something, rendering it imperfect or incomplete
**中文：** v. 切断，毁坏；使…残缺不全；使…支离破碎

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- maim（使残废）
- disfigure（毁容）
- mangle（严重损坏）
- deface（损毁）
- dismember（肢解）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mutilate a body / mutilate a text / mutilate beyond recognition / deliberately mutilate**

👉 Example: The editor complained that the publisher had mutilated her manuscript by removing several critical chapters.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Vandals broke into the gallery overnight and **mutilated** several priceless sculptures that dated back to the sixteenth century.
   破坏者在夜间闯入画廊，毁坏了几座可追溯到十六世纪的无价雕塑。

2. Scholars lamented that centuries of neglect and conflict had **mutilated** the ancient manuscript beyond any hope of full restoration.
   学者们哀叹，数百年的忽视和冲突已将这部古代手稿损毁得无法完全修复。

3. The new legislation imposed severe penalties on anyone who attempted to **mutilate** currency or counterfeit official government documents.
   新立法对任何试图毁损货币或伪造官方政府文件的人施加严厉处罚。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mutil-ate" as "mute-il-ate" — when you mutilate something, you "mute" or silence its original form, leaving it damaged and incomplete.
把"mutilate"想成"mute（沉默）+ il + ate"——当你mutilate某物时，你让它原来的形态"沉默"了，使其残缺不全。
