# 🏚️ seedy

🔊 /ˈsiːdi/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Full of seeds; gone to seed; shabby and run-down; disreputable or squalid
**中文：** adj. 多籽的，结籽的；低级的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- shabby（破旧的）
- squalid（肮脏的）
- disreputable（声名狼藉的）
- run-down（破败的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **seedy neighborhood / seedy bar / seedy hotel / seedy appearance**

👉 Example: The detective followed the suspect into a seedy district where few respectable citizens dared to venture.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist investigated the **seedy** underworld of illegal gambling operations concealed behind ordinary storefronts throughout the city.
   记者调查了隐藏在城市普通店面背后的低级非法赌博活动地下世界。

2. Despite its **seedy** exterior, the old hotel possessed a fascinating history dating back to the nineteenth century.
   尽管外表破旧，这家老旅馆却拥有可追溯到十九世纪的迷人历史。

3. The once-prosperous commercial avenue had become increasingly **seedy** after years of economic decline and municipal neglect.
   这条曾经繁荣的商业街在多年经济衰退和市政疏忽后变得越来越破败。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of a plant that has "gone to seed" — it looks worn out and past its best, just like a seedy neighborhood looks run-down.
想想一株"结了籽"的植物——它看起来衰败不堪，就像一个破旧的街区一样显得低级和颓废。
