# 🌿 primeval

🔊 /praɪˈmiːvəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Of or relating to the earliest ages of the world; ancient, primitive, or existing from the beginning of time
**中文：** adj. 原始的；初期的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ancient（远古的）
- primordial（原始的）
- prehistoric（史前的）
- primal（最初的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **primeval forest / primeval instinct / primeval fear / primeval landscape**

👉 Example: The explorers ventured deep into the primeval forest where no human had set foot for centuries.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **primeval** forest remained untouched by civilization, its towering trees forming a dense canopy that blocked nearly all sunlight.
   这片原始森林未被文明触及，参天大树形成了茂密的树冠，几乎遮挡了所有阳光。

2. A **primeval** fear gripped the villagers as the volcanic eruption illuminated the night sky with rivers of molten lava.
   当火山喷发用熔岩之河照亮夜空时，一种原始的恐惧攫住了村民们。

3. Scientists discovered fossils in the cave that provided valuable evidence about **primeval** ecosystems dating back millions of years.
   科学家在洞穴中发现的化石提供了关于数百万年前原始生态系统的宝贵证据。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "primeval" as "prime" + "eval" (age) — the prime age, the very first era of existence when the world was new and untouched.
把"primeval"想成"prime（最初的）"+"eval（时代）"——最初的时代，世界崭新且未被触及的最早纪元。
