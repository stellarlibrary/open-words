# 🤝 credence

🔊 /ˈkriːdəns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Belief in or acceptance of something as true; the mental attitude of accepting something as believable or trustworthy
**中文：** n. 信任；信用；凭证

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- belief（信念）
- trust（信任）
- faith（信赖）
- acceptance（接受）
- confidence（信心）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **give credence to / lend credence to / gain credence / lack credence**

👉 Example: The newly discovered documents lent credence to the historian's controversial theory.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The forensic evidence gave **credence** to the detective's hypothesis that the crime had been meticulously planned in advance.
   法医证据为侦探关于该犯罪经过精心策划的假设提供了可信度。

2. Scholars are reluctant to give **credence** to claims that lack rigorous empirical support and peer-reviewed verification.
   学者们不愿相信缺乏严格实证支持和同行评审验证的说法。

3. The whistleblower's testimony lent considerable **credence** to allegations of systematic fraud within the corporation's financial division.
   举报人的证词为有关该公司财务部门系统性欺诈的指控提供了相当大的可信度。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "credence" sharing the root "cred" with "credit" — both relate to trust and belief in something's value.
把"credence"和"credit（信用）"联系起来——它们共享词根"cred"，都与信任和相信某事物的价值有关。
