# ⚖️ legislate

🔊 /ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make or enact laws; to bring about a particular result by making laws or imposing legal regulations.
**中文：** v. 制定法律；通过立法；用立法规定

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- enact（制定）
- decree（颁布）
- ordain（规定）
- prescribe（规定）
- codify（编纂成法典）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **legislate against / legislate for / legislate on / legislate change / legislate morality**

👉 Example: Critics argued that the government cannot effectively legislate morality through punitive measures alone.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The parliament voted overwhelmingly to **legislate** stricter environmental protections in response to the escalating climate crisis.
   议会以压倒性多数投票通过了更严格的环境保护立法，以应对不断加剧的气候危机。

2. Many scholars argue that it is fundamentally impossible to **legislate** ethical behavior without broader cultural and educational reform.
   许多学者认为，如果没有更广泛的文化和教育改革，从根本上不可能通过立法来规范道德行为。

3. The committee was established to **legislate** new standards for data privacy across all federal government agencies.
   该委员会的成立旨在为所有联邦政府机构制定新的数据隐私标准。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "legislate" as "legal + create" — to legislate is to create something legal, that is, to make laws.
把"legislate"想成"legal + create"——legislate就是创造合法的东西，也就是制定法律。
