# 💥 destructive

🔊 /dɪˈstrʌktɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Causing great and irreparable damage; tending to destroy or undermine
**中文：** adj. 破坏性的；毁灭性的；毁害的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- devastating（毁灭性的）
- ruinous（毁灭的）
- harmful（有害的）
- damaging（破坏性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **destructive force / destructive behavior / destructive criticism / destructive impact**

👉 Example: The destructive power of the hurricane left thousands of homes in ruins along the coast.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee warned that **destructive** logging practices were accelerating deforestation and threatening indigenous habitats throughout the region.
   委员会警告说，破坏性的伐木行为正在加速森林砍伐并威胁整个地区的原住民栖息地。

2. Critics argued that the senator's **destructive** rhetoric was eroding public trust in democratic institutions and civil discourse.
   批评者认为，这位参议员的破坏性言论正在侵蚀公众对民主制度和公民话语的信任。

3. Without proper intervention, **destructive** patterns of behavior in childhood can persist well into adulthood and affect relationships.
   如果没有适当的干预，童年时期的破坏性行为模式可能会持续到成年并影响人际关系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "destructive" as "de-struct-ive" — it tears down the "structure" of things, leaving nothing but destruction behind.
把"destructive"想成"de-struct-ive"——它拆毁事物的"结构"（structure），只留下破坏。
