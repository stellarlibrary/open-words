# 👑 abdicate

🔊 /ˈæbdɪkeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To renounce or relinquish a throne, right, power, or responsibility formally
**中文：** v. 退位；放弃（权力、职责）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- renounce（放弃）
- relinquish（交出）
- resign（辞职）
- step down（退位）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **abdicate the throne / abdicate responsibility / abdicate power / abdicate in favor of**

👉 Example: King Edward VIII abdicated the British throne in 1936 to marry the woman he loved.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The aging monarch chose to **abdicate** the throne in favor of his eldest son during a formal ceremony.
   年迈的君主在正式仪式上选择退位，将王位让给了长子。

2. Critics accused the regulatory agency of **abdicating** its responsibility to protect consumers from fraudulent practices.
   批评者指责监管机构放弃了其保护消费者免受欺诈行为侵害的职责。

3. The board chairman was pressured to **abdicate** his position after the company's massive financial scandal became public.
   在公司大规模财务丑闻曝光后，董事长被迫放弃了他的职位。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ab-di-cate" as "ab (away) + dictate" — you stop dictating or ruling, you give away your power.
把"abdicate"想成"ab（离开）+ dictate（发号施令）"——你停止发号施令，放弃了你的权力。
