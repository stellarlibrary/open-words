# ⚠️ reck

🔊 /rɛk/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To have care, concern, or regard for something; to be of concern or importance (archaic/literary usage)
**中文：** v. 顾虑；介意；和…相干

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- heed（留意）
- regard（关心）
- mind（介意）
- care（在乎）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reck not / reck little / reck nothing of** (primarily found in archaic or poetic contexts)

👉 Example: The fearless knight recked nothing of the dangers that lay ahead on his perilous quest.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient warrior **recked** little of personal safety as he charged headlong into the midst of the enemy forces.
   这位古代战士毫不顾虑个人安危，一头冲入敌军之中。

2. Classical poets often portrayed heroes who **recked** nothing of mortal peril in their relentless pursuit of glory and honor.
   古典诗人常常描绘英雄们不顾凡人的危险，不懈追求荣耀与荣誉。

3. The philosopher argued that those who **reck** not the consequences of their actions inevitably bring ruin upon themselves.
   这位哲学家认为，那些不顾虑自身行为后果的人终将自取灭亡。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "reck" as the root of "reckless" — if you "reck" something, you care about it; if you are "reck-less," you are without care.
把"reck"想成"reckless（鲁莽的）"的词根——如果你"reck"某事，说明你在意它；如果你"reck-less"，就是不在意。
