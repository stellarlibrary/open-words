# 🤫 understate

🔊 /ˌʌndərˈsteɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To describe or represent something as being smaller, less important, or less serious than it actually is
**中文：** v. 少说，少报；保守地说

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- downplay（淡化）
- minimize（最小化）
- diminish（减少）
- play down（轻描淡写）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **understate the problem / understate the risks / understate the impact / it cannot be understated**

👉 Example: To say that the hurricane was merely inconvenient would be to grossly understate its devastating impact.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The official report appeared to **understate** the actual number of casualties, sparking widespread criticism from journalists.
   官方报告似乎少报了实际伤亡人数，引发了记者们的广泛批评。

2. One cannot **understate** the significance of this archaeological discovery for our understanding of early human civilization.
   这一考古发现对我们理解早期人类文明的重要性怎么说都不为过。

3. Corporate executives frequently **understate** potential environmental risks in order to reassure shareholders and maintain stock prices.
   企业高管经常淡化潜在的环境风险，以安抚股东并维持股价。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "under-state" as stating something under its true value — you say less than what is actually the case.
把"understate"想成在真实价值"之下"陈述——你说的比实际情况要少。
