# 😈 infamous

🔊 /ˈɪnfəməs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Well known for some bad quality or deed; notoriously evil; deserving of the worst condemnation
**中文：** adj. 声名狼藉的；无耻的；邪恶的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- notorious（臭名昭著的）
- disreputable（名声不好的）
- scandalous（可耻的）
- villainous（邪恶的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **infamous for / infamous criminal / infamous act / infamous reputation / the infamous case of**

👉 Example: The city's infamous crime rate deterred many potential investors from committing capital to the region.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **infamous** dictator was ultimately brought before an international tribunal to answer for crimes against humanity.
   这位**声名狼藉**的独裁者最终被带上国际法庭，为反人类罪行接受审判。

2. The experiment became **infamous** in the scientific community for its egregious violations of established ethical research standards.
   这项实验因严重违反既定的科学研究伦理标准而在科学界**臭名昭著**。

3. That **infamous** speech is widely regarded by scholars as the catalyst that ignited widespread civil unrest across the nation.
   那场**臭名昭著**的演讲被学者们普遍认为是引发全国大范围社会动荡的导火索。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-famous" as "famous for the wrong reasons" — not famous in a good way, but known widely for something terrible or shameful.
把"infamous"想成"in-famous"，即"因为坏事而出名"——不是好的出名，而是因为可怕或可耻的事情而广为人知。
