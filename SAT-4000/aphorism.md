# 💬 aphorism

🔊 /ˈæf.ə.rɪz.əm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A pithy, concise statement that expresses a general truth or principle; a memorable maxim or adage that encapsulates wisdom.

**中文：** 格言；警句

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- maxim（格言）
- adage（谚语）
- proverb（箴言）
- axiom（公理）
- epigram（隽语）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

Common collocations: **famous aphorism**, **witty aphorism**, **ancient aphorism**, **coin an aphorism**, **the aphorism that...**.

> The philosopher was celebrated for his memorable **aphorisms** about human nature.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Benjamin Franklin's **aphorisms** about thrift and industry remain widely quoted in contemporary discourse.
   本杰明·富兰克林关于节俭和勤勉的格言至今仍在当代话语中被广泛引用。

2. The professor began each lecture with a thought-provoking **aphorism** drawn from classical Greek philosophy.
   教授每堂课都以一句引自古希腊哲学的发人深省的格言开始。

3. Her writing is distinguished by elegant **aphorisms** that distill complex moral truths into memorable phrases.
   她的写作以优雅的格言见长，将复杂的道德真理凝练为令人难忘的短语。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Aphorism" sounds like "a-FOUR-ism." Imagine a wise saying so good it scores a perfect **four** out of four — that is an **aphorism**.

谐音记忆：aphorism 听起来像 "a-four-ism"（一个得满分四分的主义）。想象一句智慧短语好到得满分——那就是 aphorism（格言）。也可以记住：aph- 来自希腊语 "界定"，格言就是用简短的话界定真理。
