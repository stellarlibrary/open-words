# 🗑️ hackneyed

🔊 /ˈhæknid/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Lacking originality or freshness; made commonplace and trite by overuse
**中文：** adj. 陈腐的；平庸的；常见的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- trite（陈腐的）
- cliched（老套的）
- banal（平庸的）
- stale（过时的）
- overused（过度使用的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **hackneyed phrase / hackneyed expression / hackneyed idea / hackneyed plot**

👉 Example: The literary critic dismissed the novel for relying on hackneyed plot devices and predictable character arcs.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The editor urged the young writer to replace **hackneyed** metaphors with original imagery that would engage discerning readers.
   编辑敦促年轻作家用原创意象来替换陈腐的隐喻，以吸引有鉴赏力的读者。

2. Despite its **hackneyed** premise, the film succeeded commercially because of exceptional performances by the lead actors.
   尽管前提老套，这部电影由于主演们出色的表演而在商业上取得了成功。

3. The professor warned students against using **hackneyed** arguments in their dissertations, emphasizing the need for intellectual originality.
   教授警告学生不要在论文中使用陈腐的论点，强调知识原创性的必要。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "hackneyed" as derived from "hackney" — a horse hired out so often it becomes worn and tired, just like an overused expression becomes stale.
把"hackneyed"想成源自"hackney（出租马）"——一匹被频繁出租以至于疲惫不堪的马，就像一个被过度使用的表达变得陈腐一样。
