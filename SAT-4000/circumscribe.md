# ⭕ circumscribe

🔊 /ˈsɜːrkəmskraɪb/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To restrict or confine within limits; (in geometry) to draw a figure around another so that it touches at certain points without intersecting.
**中文：** 限制，约束；（几何）外切，外接。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- restrict（限制）
- confine（约束）
- limit（限定）
- constrain（制约）
- bound（限定）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **circumscribe authority / circumscribe freedom / circumscribed by regulations / circumscribe a circle**

👉 Example: The new legislation was designed to circumscribe the power of the executive branch.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The constitution was carefully drafted to **circumscribe** the authority of the monarch and protect individual liberties.
   宪法经过精心起草，旨在限制君主的权力并保护个人自由。

2. Budgetary constraints effectively **circumscribed** the scope of the research project, forcing the team to narrow its focus.
   预算限制有效地制约了研究项目的范围，迫使团队缩小了研究重点。

3. In advanced geometry, students learn to **circumscribe** a polygon around a circle so that each side is tangent.
   在高等几何中，学生学习如何将多边形外接于圆，使每条边都与圆相切。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "circum-" (around) + "scribe" (write/draw) — to draw a line around something, creating a boundary that limits it.
把"circumscribe"拆分为"circum-（周围）"+"scribe（画/写）"——在某物周围画一条线，形成一个限制它的边界。
