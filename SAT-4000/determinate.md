# 🎯 determinate

🔊 /dɪˈtɜːrmɪnət/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having exact and discernible limits or form; precisely defined and fixed
**中文：** adj. 确定的；决定的；限定的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- definite（明确的）
- fixed（固定的）
- precise（精确的）
- specific（具体的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **determinate form / determinate length / determinate outcome / determinate structure**

👉 Example: The contract stipulates a determinate period of employment lasting exactly three years.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The mathematician demonstrated that the equation had a **determinate** solution that could be verified through independent calculation.
   数学家证明该方程有一个确定的解，可以通过独立计算加以验证。

2. In botany, **determinate** growth refers to a pattern in which stems stop elongating once a terminal flower bud develops.
   在植物学中，确定性生长是指茎在顶端花芽长出后停止伸长的生长模式。

3. The court ruled that the prisoner should serve a **determinate** sentence of fifteen years without the possibility of early parole.
   法院裁定该囚犯应服十五年的确定刑期，不得提前假释。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "determinate" as "determine + ate" — once something is determined, it is set, fixed, and has a clear ending.
把"determinate"想成"determine + ate"——一旦某事被确定（determine），它就是固定的、有明确界限的。
