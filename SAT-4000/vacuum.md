# 🌀 vacuum

🔊 /ˈvækjuːm/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A space entirely devoid of matter; an absence of something expected or normal. (v.) To clean using a vacuum cleaner
**中文：** n. 真空；空白 v. 用真空吸尘器清扫

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- void（空白）
- emptiness（空虚）
- vacancy（空缺）
- nothingness（虚无）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **power vacuum / vacuum of leadership / in a vacuum / create a vacuum / vacuum the floor**

👉 Example: The sudden resignation of the CEO created an immediate power vacuum within the organization.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The departure of several senior officials created a dangerous **vacuum** of leadership at a critical moment in the nation's history.
   几位高级官员的离任在国家历史的关键时刻造成了危险的领导真空。

2. No scientific experiment can be conducted in a perfect **vacuum**, as some trace particles will always remain present.
   没有任何科学实验能在完美的真空中进行，因为总会有一些微量粒子存在。

3. Policy decisions should never be made in a **vacuum** without considering the broader social and economic implications involved.
   政策决定绝不应该在真空中做出，而不考虑所涉及的更广泛的社会和经济影响。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vacuum" as "vacu-um" — "vacu" sounds like "vacate" (to empty), so a vacuum is a completely vacated, empty space.
把"vacuum"想成"vacu-um"——"vacu"听起来像"vacate"（腾空），所以vacuum就是一个完全被腾空的空间。
