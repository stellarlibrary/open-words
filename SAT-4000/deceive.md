# 🃏 deceive

🔊 /dɪˈsiːv/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause someone to believe something that is not true; to mislead deliberately
**中文：** v. 欺骗；蒙蔽；行骗

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- mislead（误导）
- delude（欺骗）
- beguile（哄骗）
- dupe（愚弄）
- hoodwink（蒙骗）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **deceive someone into doing something / deceive the public / deceive oneself / appearances can deceive**

👉 Example: The con artist managed to deceive dozens of investors into handing over their life savings.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The spy managed to **deceive** foreign intelligence agencies for over a decade by maintaining an elaborate and convincing false identity.
   这名间谍通过维持一个精心设计且令人信服的虚假身份，成功欺骗了外国情报机构十多年。

2. Researchers warned that misleading statistical graphs can easily **deceive** the general public into drawing inaccurate conclusions about complex data.
   研究人员警告说，误导性的统计图表很容易欺骗公众，使其对复杂数据得出不准确的结论。

3. The defendant attempted to **deceive** the jury by presenting fabricated evidence and offering contradictory testimony during the lengthy trial.
   被告试图通过出示伪造证据和在漫长的审判中提供矛盾的证词来欺骗陪审团。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-ceive" as related to "receive" — when you are deceived, you receive false information that you mistakenly believe to be true.
把"deceive"和"receive"联系起来——当你被deceive时，你接收到了虚假信息却误以为是真的。
