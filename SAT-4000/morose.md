# 😞 morose

🔊 /məˈroʊs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Sullen and ill-tempered; gloomy, withdrawn, and unsociable
**中文：** adj. 郁闷的；孤僻的；乖僻的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sullen（闷闷不乐的）
- gloomy（忧郁的）
- melancholy（忧伤的）
- sulky（生闷气的）
- dour（阴沉的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **morose expression / morose silence / morose demeanor / become morose**

👉 Example: He grew increasingly morose after the rejection, refusing to speak with friends or leave his apartment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The usually cheerful professor became **morose** and withdrawn after learning that his decades-long research grant had been terminated.
   这位通常开朗的教授在得知他长达数十年的研究资助被终止后变得郁郁寡欢、沉默寡言。

2. A **morose** atmosphere settled over the office when employees received news of the company's impending bankruptcy and closure.
   当员工们收到公司即将破产和关闭的消息时，一种郁闷的气氛笼罩了整个办公室。

3. The therapist noted that persistent **morose** behavior in adolescents may indicate underlying depression that warrants professional evaluation.
   治疗师指出，青少年持续的郁闷行为可能表明潜在的抑郁症，需要进行专业评估。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "morose" as "more + ose (dose of sadness)" — a morose person carries more than their fair dose of gloom and sullenness.
把"morose"想成"more + ose（一剂悲伤）"——郁闷的人承受了超出常人的忧郁和阴沉。
