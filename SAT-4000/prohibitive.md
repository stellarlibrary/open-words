# 💰 prohibitive

🔊 /prəˈhɪbɪtɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Serving to forbid or restrict; (of a price or cost) excessively high, making something unaffordable
**中文：** adj. 禁止的；禁止性的；抑制的；（费用，价格等）过高的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- exorbitant（过高的）
- forbidding（令人望而却步的）
- restrictive（限制性的）
- excessive（过度的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prohibitive cost / prohibitive price / prohibitively expensive / prohibitive regulations**

👉 Example: The prohibitive cost of healthcare forces many families to forgo essential medical treatments.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **prohibitive** cost of tuition at elite universities prevents many talented students from pursuing higher education.
   精英大学过高的学费阻止了许多有才华的学生接受高等教育。

2. Import tariffs have made foreign goods **prohibitive** for the average consumer in the developing nation.
   进口关税使发展中国家的普通消费者对外国商品望而却步。

3. The **prohibitive** regulations imposed on small businesses created significant barriers to entry in the market.
   对小企业施加的限制性法规在市场准入方面造成了重大障碍。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "prohibit-ive" — something so costly it practically "prohibits" you from buying it, as if there were a law against your wallet.
把"prohibitive"想成"prohibit的形容词"——某物贵到实际上"禁止"你购买，仿佛有一条针对你钱包的法律。
