# 💒 inborn

🔊 /ˈɪnˌbɔːrn/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Existing from birth; natural and inherent rather than acquired through experience or learning
**中文：** adj. 天生的；先天的；生来的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- innate（天生的）
- inherent（内在的）
- natural（自然的）
- congenital（先天的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inborn talent / inborn ability / inborn instinct / inborn trait / inborn tendency**

👉 Example: Many scientists believe that the capacity for language acquisition is an inborn ability shared by all humans.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Researchers discovered that certain **inborn** genetic variations can significantly influence an individual's susceptibility to autoimmune disorders.
   研究人员发现，某些先天的基因变异可以显著影响个体对自身免疫疾病的易感性。

2. The prodigy's **inborn** musical talent was evident from the age of three, when she began composing simple melodies.
   这位神童天生的音乐才能在三岁时就显而易见，当时她开始创作简单的旋律。

3. Philosophers have long debated whether moral reasoning is an **inborn** capacity or a skill developed through socialization.
   哲学家们长期以来一直在争论道德推理是一种先天能力还是通过社会化发展的技能。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-born" literally — a quality that is "born in" you, something you were born with, not something you had to learn or acquire.
把"inborn"直接理解为"生来就有的"——一种"生在你体内"的品质，是与生俱来的，而非后天习得的。
