# 🛡️ intercept

🔊 /ˌɪntərˈsɛpt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To stop, seize, or catch something or someone in transit before reaching the intended destination. (n.) The act of intercepting; a point where a line crosses an axis
**中文：** vt. 中途拦截；截击；截取 n. 拦截；截距

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- seize（截获）
- block（阻截）
- obstruct（阻挡）
- catch（截住）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **intercept a message / intercept a pass / intercept communications / intercept a shipment**

👉 Example: Border security agents managed to intercept the illegal shipment before it crossed the frontier.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Intelligence agencies were able to **intercept** encrypted communications that revealed the details of the planned covert operation abroad.
   情报机构截获了加密通信，揭示了计划中的海外秘密行动的细节。

2. The defense system was specifically designed to **intercept** incoming missiles at high altitude before they could reach populated areas.
   该防御系统专门设计用于在高空拦截来袭导弹，以防其到达人口密集区域。

3. Customs officials managed to **intercept** a large quantity of contraband goods concealed within ordinary commercial shipping containers.
   海关官员成功截获了隐藏在普通商业集装箱中的大量违禁物品。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inter-cept" — "inter" means between, and "cept" comes from Latin "capere" meaning to take, so to intercept is to take something in between, catching it mid-journey.
把"intercept"想成"inter(中间) + cept(抓取)"——在中途抓住某物，即拦截。
