# 🏚️ dilapidate

🔊 /dɪˈlæpɪdeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To fall into a state of disrepair or ruin; to cause a building or structure to become decayed or deteriorated through neglect
**中文：** v. 毁坏；荒废；使倒塌

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- decay（衰败）
- deteriorate（恶化）
- crumble（崩塌）
- degrade（退化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dilapidated building / dilapidated house / dilapidated structure / dilapidated condition**

👉 Example: Years of neglect had caused the once-grand estate to dilapidate beyond recognition.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The abandoned factory began to **dilapidate** rapidly after the company ceased all maintenance operations and relocated overseas.
   在公司停止所有维护作业并迁往海外后，这座废弃工厂迅速荒废。

2. Municipal authorities warned that the historic courthouse would **dilapidate** further unless restoration funds were allocated immediately.
   市政当局警告说，除非立即拨付修复资金，否则这座历史悠久的法院将进一步毁坏。

3. Prolonged exposure to harsh weather conditions caused the wooden bridge to **dilapidate** and become structurally unsafe for pedestrians.
   长期暴露在恶劣天气条件下，导致这座木桥毁损，行人通行变得不安全。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "di-LAPID-ate" as containing "lapid" (like "lapse") — when maintenance lapses, buildings dilapidate and fall apart.
把"dilapidate"想成包含"lapid"（像"lapse"流逝）——当维护工作流逝时，建筑就会荒废倒塌。
