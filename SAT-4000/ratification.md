# ✅ ratification

🔊 /ˌrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The formal act of giving consent or approval to a treaty, contract, or agreement, making it officially valid
**中文：** n. 批准；承认；认可

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- approval（批准）
- endorsement（认可）
- confirmation（确认）
- validation（生效）
- sanction（准许）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ratification of a treaty / ratification process / seek ratification / require ratification**

👉 Example: The treaty required ratification by a two-thirds majority of the senate before it could take effect.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **ratification** of the international climate accord was celebrated as a landmark achievement in global environmental diplomacy.
   国际气候协议的批准被视为全球环境外交的里程碑式成就。

2. Several member states delayed **ratification** of the trade agreement due to concerns about its impact on domestic industries.
   几个成员国因担心贸易协议对国内产业的影响而推迟了批准。

3. Constitutional scholars debated whether the **ratification** process adequately reflected the will of the general populace at that time.
   宪法学者争论批准程序是否充分反映了当时普通民众的意愿。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ratification" as "ratify + action" — it is the action of making something officially ratified, like stamping a document with formal approval.
把"ratification"想成"ratify（批准）+ action（行动）"——它是正式批准某事的行为，就像在文件上盖上正式批准的印章。
