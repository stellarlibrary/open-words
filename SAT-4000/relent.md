# 💧 relent

🔊 /rɪˈlent/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To become less severe, harsh, or strict; to soften in attitude or determination; to slacken or abate
**中文：** v. 变温和，变宽厚；减弱，缓和

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- yield（让步）
- soften（软化）
- subside（减弱）
- abate（缓和）
- capitulate（屈服）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **relent in one's demands / finally relent / refuse to relent / relent under pressure**

👉 Example: After weeks of heated negotiations, the union leaders finally agreed to relent on their demands.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor refused to **relent** on the strict grading policy despite numerous complaints from students throughout the semester.
   尽管整个学期学生们多次投诉，这位教授仍拒绝在严格的评分政策上做出让步。

2. After days of relentless rainfall, the storm began to **relent**, allowing rescue crews to reach the flooded areas.
   经过数天的持续降雨，暴风雨开始减弱，使救援队得以到达被洪水淹没的地区。

3. Public pressure eventually forced the corporation to **relent** and withdraw its controversial environmental deregulation proposal.
   公众压力最终迫使该公司做出让步，撤回其有争议的环境放松管制提案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "relent" as "re-lent" — you lent your anger away again, becoming softer and more forgiving each time.
把"relent"想成"re-lent"——你再次把愤怒借出去了，每次都变得更温和、更宽容。
