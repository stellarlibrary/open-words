# 📕 illiterate

🔊 /ɪˈlɪtərət/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Unable to read or write; lacking education or knowledge in a particular area. (n.) A person who cannot read or write
**中文：** adj. 文盲的；未受教育的；不识字的 n. 文盲

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- uneducated（未受教育的）
- unlettered（不识字的）
- ignorant（无知的）
- unschooled（未受学校教育的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **functionally illiterate / financially illiterate / computer illiterate / illiterate population / politically illiterate**

👉 Example: The nonprofit organization launched a campaign to help functionally illiterate adults acquire basic reading skills.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. International development agencies estimate that hundreds of millions of adults worldwide remain functionally **illiterate** despite increased global investment in education.
   国际发展机构估计，尽管全球教育投资增加，全世界仍有数亿成年人实际上是文盲。

2. The survey revealed that a surprising percentage of young professionals were financially **illiterate** and unable to manage basic personal budgets.
   调查显示，令人惊讶的一定比例的年轻专业人士是金融文盲，无法管理基本的个人预算。

3. Historians note that in medieval Europe, even many members of the nobility were **illiterate** and relied entirely on clergy for written communication.
   历史学家指出，在中世纪欧洲，甚至许多贵族成员也是文盲，完全依赖神职人员进行书面交流。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "il-literate" — the prefix "il-" means "not," and "literate" means able to read, so illiterate means "not able to read."
把"illiterate"拆成"il（不）+ literate（识字的）"——就是不识字的，即文盲。
