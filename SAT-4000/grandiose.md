# 🏰 grandiose

🔊 /ˈɡrændioʊs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Impressive or magnificent in appearance or style; excessively grand or ambitious, often pretentiously so
**中文：** adj. 宏伟的；堂皇的；冠冕堂皇的，浮夸的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- magnificent（宏伟的）
- imposing（壮观的）
- ostentatious（炫耀的）
- extravagant（奢华的）
- pretentious（自命不凡的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **grandiose plans / grandiose ambitions / grandiose scheme / grandiose vision**

👉 Example: The architect's grandiose design for the new civic center exceeded the city's available budget by millions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The dictator's **grandiose** vision for the capital city required the demolition of entire historic neighborhoods to erect monumental structures.
   独裁者对首都的宏伟构想需要拆除整个历史街区来建造纪念性建筑。

2. Critics warned that the company's **grandiose** expansion strategy was financially unsustainable and would likely lead to inevitable bankruptcy.
   批评人士警告说，该公司浮夸的扩张战略在财务上不可持续，可能会导致不可避免的破产。

3. Although his **grandiose** promises impressed voters initially, the newly elected mayor struggled to deliver on any meaningful commitments.
   尽管他冠冕堂皇的承诺最初给选民留下了深刻印象，但这位新当选的市长却难以兑现任何有意义的承诺。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "grandiose" as "grand + i + ose" — the suffix "-ose" means "full of," so grandiose literally means full of grandness, often excessively so.
把"grandiose"想成"grand（宏大的）+ i + ose（充满）"——后缀"-ose"意为"充满"，所以grandiose字面意思是充满宏大，往往过度了。
