# 🔬 diminutive

🔊 /dɪˈmɪnjʊtɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Extremely or unusually small; tiny. (n.) A word or suffix indicating smallness, such as "booklet" or "-ling"
**中文：** adj. 小的；微小的；小型的 n. 指小词；小称

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- tiny（微小的）
- miniature（微型的）
- petite（娇小的）
- minuscule（极小的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **diminutive stature / diminutive figure / diminutive size / diminutive form / diminutive suffix**

👉 Example: Despite her diminutive stature, she commanded enormous respect and authority in the courtroom.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **diminutive** hummingbird, weighing barely three grams, demonstrated remarkable endurance during its annual migration across the Gulf of Mexico.
   这只微小的蜂鸟仅重三克，却在每年穿越墨西哥湾的迁徙中展现出惊人的耐力。

2. In linguistics, a **diminutive** suffix such as "-ette" or "-ling" is appended to a word to convey smallness or affection.
   在语言学中，像"-ette"或"-ling"这样的指小后缀被附加在单词上以表达微小或亲昵之意。

3. The **diminutive** entrepreneur built a global technology empire that belied her modest physical presence and quiet demeanor in meetings.
   这位身材娇小的企业家建立了一个全球科技帝国，与她在会议中谦逊的外表和安静的举止形成鲜明对比。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "diminutive" as "DIMINISH" + "ive" — something diminutive has been diminished in size, making it extremely small.
把"diminutive"想成"DIMINISH"（缩小）+ "ive"——diminutive的东西就是被缩小了的，因此极其微小。
