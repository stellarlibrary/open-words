# 🎵 dissonant

🔊 /ˈdɪsənənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Harsh and inharmonious in sound; lacking agreement or consistency
**中文：** adj. 刺耳的；不谐和的；不一致的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- discordant（不和谐的）
- cacophonous（刺耳的）
- jarring（不协调的）
- clashing（冲突的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dissonant chord / dissonant sound / dissonant voices / dissonant notes**

👉 Example: The dissonant tones of the experimental composition unsettled the traditionally minded audience.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The orchestra produced a deliberately **dissonant** passage to convey the emotional turmoil depicted in the final act of the opera.
   管弦乐队故意演奏了一段不谐和的乐段，以表达歌剧最后一幕中描绘的情感动荡。

2. Critics noted that the author's optimistic conclusion felt **dissonant** with the bleak and despairing tone of the preceding chapters.
   评论家指出，作者乐观的结局与前几章阴郁绝望的基调显得格格不入。

3. The **dissonant** blend of industrial noise and classical instrumentation defined the composer's unique and polarizing artistic style.
   工业噪音与古典乐器的不谐和融合定义了这位作曲家独特而两极分化的艺术风格。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-sonant" as "dis- (bad) + sonant (sounding)" — something that sounds bad or harsh to the ear.
把"dissonant"想成"dis-（不好）+ sonant（发声的）"——听起来刺耳不和谐的声音。
