# 🌅 aurora

🔊 /əˈrɔːrə/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A natural light display in the sky, especially in polar regions, caused by charged particles interacting with the atmosphere; the dawn or first light of morning.
**中文：** 极光；曙光

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dawn（黎明）
- daybreak（破晓）
- northern lights（北极光）
- sunrise（日出）
- first light（曙光）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **aurora borealis (northern lights)**, **aurora australis (southern lights)**, **aurora of a new era**

👉 Example: Travelers journeyed to the Arctic Circle to witness the magnificent aurora borealis illuminating the sky.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **aurora** borealis painted the northern sky with spectacular ribbons of green and violet light.
   北极光用壮观的绿色和紫色光带装点了北方的天空。

2. Poets have long celebrated the **aurora** as a symbol of hope and the promise of renewal.
   诗人们长久以来将曙光颂为希望与新生承诺的象征。

3. Scientists deployed sensitive instruments to monitor the **aurora** and study solar wind interactions with Earth.
   科学家们部署了灵敏的仪器来监测极光，并研究太阳风与地球的相互作用。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Aurora was the Roman goddess of the dawn. Picture her painting the sky with glowing colors each morning — that same glow appears as the aurora borealis at the poles.
Aurora是罗马的黎明女神。想象她每天早晨用绚丽的色彩绘制天空——同样的光辉在两极化为极光。
