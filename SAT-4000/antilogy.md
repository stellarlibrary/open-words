# 🔄 antilogy

🔊 /ænˈtɪlədʒi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A contradiction in terms or ideas; an inconsistency or self-contradictory statement within a text or argument.

**中文：** 前后矛盾；自相矛盾的说法或论述。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- contradiction（矛盾）
- inconsistency（不一致）
- paradox（悖论）
- discrepancy（差异）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Common collocations:** glaring antilogy, detect an antilogy, riddled with antilogy

**Example:** The critic identified a fundamental **antilogy** in the philosopher's argument.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The reviewer noted a striking **antilogy** between the author's opening thesis and concluding remarks.
   审稿人注意到作者的开篇论点与结论之间存在明显的前后矛盾。

2. Scholars have long debated whether the apparent **antilogy** in the manuscript was intentional or accidental.
   学者们长期争论该手稿中明显的前后矛盾究竟是有意为之还是无心之失。

3. The legislative proposal contained a troubling **antilogy** that rendered its key provisions virtually unenforceable.
   该立法提案包含令人担忧的自相矛盾之处，使其关键条款几乎无法执行。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "anti-" (against) + "-logy" (speech/logic). An antilogy is when speech or logic goes against itself — a self-contradiction.

联想："anti-"（反对）+ "-logy"（言论/逻辑）。antilogy 就是言论或逻辑自相矛盾——自己反对自己。
