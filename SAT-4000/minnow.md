# 🐟 minnow

🔊 /ˈmɪnoʊ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A small freshwater fish of the carp family; figuratively, a person or organization of little importance or influence.
**中文：** n. 鲦鱼（一种小淡水鱼）；鲤类小鱼

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- small fry（小人物）
- tiddler（小鱼）
- lightweight（无足轻重者）
- nobody（无名之辈）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a mere minnow / political minnow / minnow among giants / compared to the minnows**

👉 Example: In the world of global finance, the small startup was just a minnow among corporate giants.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The children spent the afternoon catching **minnows** in the shallow stream that ran behind their grandparents' country estate.
   孩子们花了整个下午在祖父母乡间庄园后面的浅溪中捕捉小鱼。

2. Compared to established multinational corporations, the fledgling company was merely a **minnow** in the vast technology marketplace.
   与老牌跨国公司相比，这家初创公司在广阔的科技市场中不过是条小鱼。

3. Ecologists discovered that the population of native **minnows** had declined sharply due to the introduction of invasive predatory species.
   生态学家发现，由于引入了入侵性捕食物种，本地鲦鱼的数量急剧下降。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "minnow" as "mini-now" — a mini fish that is so small it might as well be called "mini," swimming in rivers right now.
把"minnow"想成"mini-now"——一条小到可以叫"迷你"的鱼，此刻正在河里游泳。
