# 👑 dignify

🔊 /ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make something appear worthy and impressive; to confer dignity or honor upon; to give an undeservedly high status to something
**中文：** v. 使高贵；使尊荣；增威严

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ennoble（使高贵）
- elevate（提升）
- honor（尊敬）
- exalt（赞扬）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dignify with a response / dignify the occasion / not dignify something / dignify oneself**

👉 Example: The ambassador refused to dignify the baseless accusations with a formal public response.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The presence of distinguished international scholars helped to **dignify** the opening ceremony of the newly established research institute.
   杰出国际学者的出席为新成立的研究所的开幕式增添了庄严感。

2. Critics argued that responding to such absurd conspiracy theories would only **dignify** them and grant them undeserved public legitimacy.
   批评人士认为，回应如此荒谬的阴谋论只会抬高它们的地位，赋予它们不应有的公众合法性。

3. The restoration project sought to **dignify** the historic district by preserving its original architectural character and cultural heritage.
   修复项目旨在通过保护历史街区的原始建筑特色和文化遗产来提升其尊贵感。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dign-ify" as "to make (-ify) something have dignity" — just like "beautify" means to make beautiful, "dignify" means to make dignified.
把"dignify"想成"使（-ify）某物有尊严"——就像"beautify"意为使美丽，"dignify"意为使高贵。
