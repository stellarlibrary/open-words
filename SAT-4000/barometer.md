# 🌡️ barometer

🔊 /bəˈrɒmɪtər/<br>
🏷️ *n. | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** An instrument for measuring atmospheric pressure; figuratively, something that reflects changes in circumstances or opinions.

**中文：** 气压计；晴雨表；（比喻）反映变化的指标。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- gauge （标尺；衡量标准）
- indicator （指标）
- measure （度量）
- benchmark （基准）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Barometer" is frequently used in a figurative sense to mean an indicator or measure of change, especially in social, economic, or political contexts. The phrase "a barometer of" is a common collocation meaning "an indicator of."

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Consumer spending is often regarded as a reliable **barometer** of overall economic health.
   中文：消费者支出通常被视为整体经济健康状况的可靠晴雨表。

2. The scientist carefully adjusted the **barometer** to obtain an accurate atmospheric pressure reading.
   中文：科学家仔细调整气压计以获得准确的大气压力读数。

3. Public opinion polls serve as a useful **barometer** for predicting election outcomes nationwide.
   中文：民意调查是预测全国选举结果的有用指标。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Break it down: "baro" (pressure, from Greek "baros" meaning weight) + "meter" (measure). A barometer measures atmospheric pressure — and by extension, measures the "pressure" of any situation.

拆解记忆："baro"（压力，源自希腊语"baros"意为重量）+ "meter"（测量）。气压计测量大气压力——引申为衡量任何情况的"压力"或变化。
