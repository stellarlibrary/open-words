# 🪢 entangle

🔊 /ɪnˈtæŋɡl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause to become twisted together or caught in; to involve in complicated or compromising circumstances
**中文：** v. 使纠缠；卷入

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ensnare（诱入陷阱）
- enmesh（使陷入）
- complicate（使复杂化）
- embroil（使卷入）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **entangle in / entangle with / become entangled in a dispute / entangled affairs**

👉 Example: The diplomat became entangled in a web of political intrigue that threatened his career.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corporation became **entangled** in a lengthy legal dispute that consumed significant financial resources over several years.
   该公司卷入了一场旷日持久的法律纠纷，数年间消耗了大量财务资源。

2. Researchers discovered that subatomic particles can become **entangled** in ways that challenge conventional understanding of physics.
   研究人员发现亚原子粒子能以挑战传统物理学认知的方式发生纠缠。

3. The novelist skillfully **entangled** multiple plotlines to create a narrative of remarkable complexity and depth.
   这位小说家巧妙地将多条情节线交织在一起，创造出一部复杂而深刻的叙事作品。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "en-tangle" as getting caught in a "tangle" of threads — once tangled, it is very hard to separate.
把"entangle"想成被一团"tangle（缠结的线）"缠住——一旦纠缠在一起，就很难分开。
