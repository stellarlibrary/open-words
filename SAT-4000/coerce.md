# 💪 coerce

🔊 /koʊˈɜːrs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To compel or force someone to act or think in a certain way by using pressure, threats, or intimidation
**中文：** v. 强制；迫使；胁迫

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- compel（强迫）
- force（迫使）
- intimidate（恐吓）
- pressure（施压）
- constrain（约束）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **coerce someone into / coerce compliance / coerce a confession / coerced by threats**

👉 Example: The regime attempted to coerce dissidents into silence through surveillance and arbitrary detention.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The investigation revealed that the suspect had been **coerced** into signing a false confession through prolonged psychological intimidation.
   调查显示，嫌疑人是在长时间的心理恐吓下被迫签署了虚假供词。

2. International sanctions were imposed to **coerce** the authoritarian government into respecting fundamental human rights and civil liberties.
   国际制裁的实施旨在迫使该专制政府尊重基本人权和公民自由。

3. The court ruled that any agreement obtained by **coercing** one party through threats of violence was legally void and unenforceable.
   法院裁定，通过暴力威胁强迫一方获得的任何协议在法律上无效且不可执行。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "coerce" as "co-" (with) + "erce" (like "force") — to use force with someone, pushing them to do what you want against their will.
把"coerce"想成"co-"（与）+ "erce"（像"force"力量）——对某人使用力量，迫使他们违背意愿做你想做的事。
