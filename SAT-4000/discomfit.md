# 😣 discomfit

🔊 /dɪsˈkʌmfɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make someone feel uneasy, embarrassed, or confused; to thwart or defeat the plans of an opponent
**中文：** v. 挫败；扰乱；使为难

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- embarrass（使尴尬）
- disconcert（使不安）
- fluster（使慌乱）
- thwart（挫败）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **discomfit an opponent / visibly discomfited / discomfit the audience / discomfited by the question**

👉 Example: The unexpected question from the reporter clearly discomfited the spokesperson during the press conference.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The attorney's sharp cross-examination was designed to **discomfit** the witness and expose inconsistencies in her prior testimony.
   律师尖锐的盘问旨在使证人感到为难，并揭露她先前证词中的矛盾之处。

2. The rebel forces managed to **discomfit** the advancing army through a series of well-coordinated ambushes along the mountain passes.
   叛军通过在山口沿线一系列精心协调的伏击成功挫败了进攻的军队。

3. His blunt remarks about the project's failures clearly **discomfited** the board members who had championed the initiative from its inception.
   他对项目失败的直言不讳明显让那些从一开始就支持该倡议的董事会成员感到尴尬。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-comfort" — to discomfit someone is to take away their comfort, leaving them flustered and uneasy.
把"discomfit"联想为"dis-comfort"——让人失去舒适感，使其不安和慌乱。
