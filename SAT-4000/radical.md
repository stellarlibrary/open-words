# 💥 radical

🔊 /ˈrædɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or affecting the fundamental nature of something; far-reaching; advocating complete political or social reform. (n.) A person who advocates thorough reform; a root or base form in mathematics or chemistry.
**中文：** adj. 基本的；重要的；激进的；极端的；根本的；彻底的 n. 激进分子；根号；根式；原子团

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fundamental（根本的）
- extreme（极端的）
- revolutionary（革命性的）
- drastic（激烈的）
- thoroughgoing（彻底的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **radical change / radical reform / radical approach / radical departure / radical idea**

👉 Example: The committee proposed a radical overhaul of the education system to address long-standing inequalities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The administration's **radical** economic reforms provoked heated debate among legislators who feared their impact on vulnerable populations.
   政府激进的经济改革在立法者中引发了激烈辩论，他们担心这些改革对弱势群体的影响。

2. The discovery of penicillin represented a **radical** departure from previous medical practices and transformed the treatment of infections.
   青霉素的发现代表了与以往医疗实践的根本性偏离，彻底改变了感染的治疗方式。

3. In organic chemistry, a free **radical** is an atom or molecule with an unpaired electron that makes it highly reactive.
   在有机化学中，自由基是一种带有不成对电子的原子或分子，这使它具有高度反应活性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "radical" as coming from Latin "radix" meaning root — radical changes go to the very root of something, changing it completely from the ground up.
把"radical"想成来自拉丁语"radix（根）"——radical的变化深入事物的根本，从根基开始彻底改变。
