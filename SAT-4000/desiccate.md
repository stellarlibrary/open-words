# 🏜️ desiccate

🔊 /ˈdɛsɪkeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To dry out thoroughly; to remove moisture from something; to preserve by dehydrating
**中文：** v. 使干涸；使脱水；使变干

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dehydrate（使脱水）
- parch（烤干）
- dry（弄干）
- wither（枯萎）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **desiccate food / desiccated coconut / desiccated remains / desiccate the soil**

👉 Example: The relentless summer heat had completely desiccated the once-fertile farmland.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The archaeologists discovered **desiccated** plant specimens that had been preserved for thousands of years in the arid desert climate.
   考古学家发现了在干旱沙漠气候中保存了数千年的干燥植物标本。

2. Prolonged drought conditions will **desiccate** the reservoir, threatening the regional water supply for millions of residents.
   长期的干旱条件将使水库干涸，威胁到数百万居民的区域供水。

3. Scientists **desiccate** biological samples before analysis to prevent bacterial contamination and ensure accurate laboratory results.
   科学家在分析前将生物样本脱水，以防止细菌污染并确保实验室结果的准确性。

---

Think of "desiccate" as "desert + dry cake" — imagine a cake left in the desert, completely dried out and crumbling.
把"desiccate"想成"desert + dry cake"——想象一块蛋糕被遗忘在沙漠里，完全干透并碎裂。
