# 💒 precocious

🔊 /prɪˈkoʊʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having developed certain abilities or inclinations at an earlier age than usual; unusually mature or advanced for one's age.
**中文：** adj. 早熟的；过早发育的；超前发展的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- advanced（超前的）
- gifted（有天赋的）
- prodigious（非凡的）
- premature（过早的）
- forward（早熟的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **precocious child / precocious talent / precocious development / intellectually precocious**

👉 Example: The precocious young pianist performed a complex concerto that astonished the entire audience at the recital.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **precocious** child began reading classical literature at the age of five, far surpassing her peers in comprehension.
   这个早熟的孩子五岁就开始阅读古典文学，在理解力方面远远超过同龄人。

2. His **precocious** talent for mathematics earned him admission to a prestigious university program at the age of fourteen.
   他在数学方面早熟的天赋使他在十四岁时被一所著名大学的项目录取。

3. Teachers recognized her **precocious** intellect early on and recommended advanced coursework to keep her academically challenged.
   老师们很早就发现了她超前的智力，并推荐了高级课程以保持对她的学术挑战。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-COCIOUS" — "pre" (before) + "coc" (like "cook"). A precocious child is "cooked" before the usual time, ripening early.
把"precocious"想成"pre（提前）+ cocious（煮熟）"——一个早熟的孩子在正常时间之前就"煮熟"了，过早成熟。
