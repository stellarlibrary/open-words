# ⛪ pontiff

🔊 /ˈpɑːntɪf/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The Pope; the Bishop of Rome and head of the Roman Catholic Church; historically, a chief priest or bishop
**中文：** n. 教皇；罗马教宗；主教

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pope（教皇）
- bishop（主教）
- patriarch（宗主教）
- prelate（高级教士）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **the Supreme Pontiff / the Roman Pontiff / a reigning pontiff / papal pontiff**

👉 Example: The pontiff addressed the faithful gathered in Saint Peter's Square during the Easter ceremony.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **pontiff** issued an encyclical urging world leaders to address climate change with greater urgency and resolve.
   教皇发布了一份通谕，敦促世界领导人以更大的紧迫感和决心应对气候变化。

2. Thousands of pilgrims traveled to Rome to receive a blessing from the newly elected **pontiff** at the Vatican.
   成千上万的朝圣者前往罗马，在梵蒂冈接受新当选教皇的祝福。

3. Historians studied how the medieval **pontiff** wielded both spiritual authority and considerable political influence across Europe.
   历史学家研究了中世纪教皇如何在欧洲同时行使精神权威和巨大的政治影响力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pontiff" as "pont-iff" — like "pont" (bridge in Latin). The pontiff is the spiritual "bridge" between God and the people.
把"pontiff"想成"pont(桥)-iff"——拉丁语中"pont"意为桥。教皇是上帝与人民之间的精神"桥梁"。
