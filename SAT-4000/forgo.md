# 🚫 forgo

🔊 /fɔːrˈɡoʊ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To voluntarily give up or do without something; to abstain from or renounce
**中文：** v. 放弃；停止；抛弃

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- abstain（戒绝）
- renounce（放弃）
- relinquish（放弃）
- waive（放弃）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **forgo the opportunity / forgo pleasure / forgo one's salary / forgo a meal**

👉 Example: Many students forgo social activities to dedicate more time to their studies.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The executive chose to **forgo** her annual bonus so that additional funds could be allocated to employee training programs.
   这位高管选择放弃年度奖金，以便将额外资金分配给员工培训项目。

2. In the interest of public health, citizens were urged to **forgo** large gatherings during the peak of the epidemic.
   为了公共健康，市民们被敦促在疫情高峰期间放弃大型聚会。

3. The researcher decided to **forgo** publication of preliminary findings until the data could be independently verified and confirmed.
   研究人员决定放弃发表初步研究结果，直到数据能够被独立核实和确认。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "for-go" as "to let it go for good" — when you forgo something, you let it go willingly and move forward without it.
把"forgo"想成"let it go（放手）"——当你forgo某事时，你心甘情愿地放手，不再拥有它。
