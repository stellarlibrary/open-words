# 🚶 itinerate

🔊 /aɪˈtɪnəreɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To travel from place to place, especially to preach or perform official duties
**中文：** v. 巡回；巡回传教

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- travel（旅行）
- roam（漫游）
- wander（游荡）
- journey（巡游）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **itinerate across / itinerate through / itinerate among communities**

👉 Example: The minister would itinerate through rural villages each summer to preach and provide counsel.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. During the 18th century, many evangelists would **itinerate** across the countryside, delivering sermons to remote farming communities.
   在18世纪，许多布道者会在乡间巡回传教，向偏远的农村社区传道。

2. The traveling judge was required to **itinerate** through several districts to ensure that justice reached every province.
   巡回法官被要求在几个地区巡回，以确保每个省份都能得到司法服务。

3. Scholars who **itinerate** among universities often gain diverse perspectives that enrich their academic research significantly.
   在各大学之间巡回的学者往往能获得多元视角，极大地丰富他们的学术研究。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "itinerate" as related to "itinerary" — someone who itinerates follows an itinerary, traveling from place to place on a planned route.
把"itinerate"和"itinerary"联系起来——巡回的人按照行程表，从一个地方到另一个地方按计划路线旅行。
