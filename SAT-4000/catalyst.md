# ⚗️ catalyst

🔊 /ˈkætəlɪst/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself being consumed; a person or thing that precipitates change or action
**中文：** n. 催化剂；刺激因素

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- stimulus（刺激）
- impetus（推动力）
- trigger（触发因素）
- spark（导火索）
- facilitator（促进者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **catalyst for change / act as a catalyst / catalyst for growth / catalyst in a reaction**

👉 Example: The new policy served as a catalyst for widespread economic reform across the region.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The invention of the printing press served as a powerful **catalyst** for the intellectual and cultural transformation of Renaissance Europe.
   印刷术的发明是欧洲文艺复兴时期知识和文化变革的强大催化剂。

2. In organic chemistry, a platinum **catalyst** is frequently employed to accelerate hydrogenation reactions without being permanently altered.
   在有机化学中，铂催化剂经常被用来加速加氢反应而不会被永久改变。

3. The diplomatic crisis became a **catalyst** that compelled neighboring nations to negotiate a comprehensive regional peace agreement.
   这场外交危机成为促使邻国谈判达成全面区域和平协议的催化因素。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cat-a-list" — a cat with a list of changes to make. A catalyst makes things happen faster, like a cat knocking things off a shelf.
把"catalyst"想成"cat-a-list"——一只拿着变革清单的猫。催化剂让事情发生得更快，就像一只猫把东西从架子上打落一样。
