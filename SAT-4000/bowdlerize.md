# ✂️ bowdlerize

🔊 /ˈbaʊdləˌraɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To remove or alter passages considered vulgar or objectionable from a text; to expurgate or censor
**中文：** 删改；删除不妥的文句；修订（尤指删去被认为不雅的内容）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- expurgate（删除不当内容）
- censor（审查）
- sanitize（净化）
- redact（编辑删改）
- purge（清除）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bowdlerize a text / bowdlerize a novel / bowdlerize the original / bowdlerized version**

👉 Example: The publisher chose to bowdlerize the classic novel before releasing it for younger audiences.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics accused the studio of attempting to **bowdlerize** the playwright's original work by removing its most provocative scenes.
   评论家指责该制片厂试图删改剧作家的原作，删去了其中最具挑衅性的场景。

2. The school board's decision to **bowdlerize** several literary classics sparked a heated debate about censorship in education.
   校董会删改几部文学经典作品的决定引发了一场关于教育审查制度的激烈辩论。

3. Historians argue that to **bowdlerize** primary sources is to distort the authentic record of past societies and cultures.
   历史学家认为，删改原始资料就是在歪曲过去社会和文化的真实记录。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of Thomas Bowdler, who published a censored edition of Shakespeare — his name became the verb "bowdlerize," meaning to censor text.
想想托马斯·鲍德勒，他出版了莎士比亚的删节版——他的名字变成了动词"bowdlerize"，意为删改文本。
