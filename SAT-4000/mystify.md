# 🔮 mystify

🔊 /ˈmɪstɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To utterly bewilder or perplex someone; to make something obscure or mysterious; to confuse or puzzle
**中文：** v. 使神秘化；使迷惑，使困惑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bewilder（使困惑）
- perplex（使迷惑）
- baffle（使难住）
- confound（使混乱）
- puzzle（使费解）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mystify the audience / mystify experts / completely mystified / mystify with illusions**

👉 Example: The magician's ability to mystify even the most skeptical observers earned him worldwide acclaim.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden disappearance of the rare artifact continued to **mystify** investigators who had exhausted every conventional line of inquiry.
   这件珍贵文物的突然消失继续困扰着已用尽所有常规调查途径的探员们。

2. Quantum mechanics has the power to **mystify** even the most accomplished physicists with its counterintuitive principles and paradoxes.
   量子力学以其违反直觉的原理和悖论，甚至有能力让最杰出的物理学家感到困惑。

3. The artist deliberately sought to **mystify** her audience by incorporating cryptic symbols and ambiguous imagery into every painting.
   这位艺术家刻意在每幅画中融入神秘符号和模糊意象，以使观众感到迷惑。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mystify" as "mystery + fy (make)" — to mystify is to make something a mystery, leaving people confused and wondering.
把"mystify"想成"mystery（神秘）+ -fy（使成为）"——mystify就是使某事变成谜，让人困惑不解。
