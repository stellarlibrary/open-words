# 🌧️ precipitation

🔊 /prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Rain, snow, sleet, or hail that falls to the ground; the process of a substance being deposited from a solution; rash or hasty action.
**中文：** n. 降水；降水量；沉淀；沉淀物；鲁莽；急躁

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rainfall（降雨）
- downpour（倾盆大雨）
- condensation（冷凝）
- deposit（沉淀物）
- haste（仓促）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **annual precipitation / heavy precipitation / precipitation levels / chemical precipitation**

👉 Example: The region receives very little precipitation during the summer months, leading to frequent drought conditions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Average annual **precipitation** in the desert region rarely exceeds one hundred millimeters, making agriculture nearly impossible.
   该沙漠地区的年平均降水量很少超过一百毫米，使得农业几乎不可能进行。

2. Meteorologists predict increased **precipitation** across the eastern seaboard as the tropical storm moves northward along the coast.
   气象学家预测，随着热带风暴沿海岸向北移动，东部沿海地区的降水量将增加。

3. The chemist carefully controlled temperature and acidity to ensure complete **precipitation** of the dissolved minerals from the solution.
   化学家仔细控制温度和酸度，以确保溶液中溶解的矿物质完全沉淀。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "precipitation" as anything that "precipitates" downward — rain falling from clouds or solids settling from liquids.
把"precipitation"想成任何"向下沉降"的东西——雨从云中落下，或固体从液体中沉淀。
