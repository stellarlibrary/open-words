# 🤫 filch

🔊 /fɪltʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To steal something of little value in a sneaky or petty manner; to pilfer
**中文：** v. 窃取；偷窃

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pilfer（偷窃）
- swipe（顺手牵羊）
- purloin（偷取）
- snatch（抢夺）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **filch something from someone / filch small items / filch supplies**

👉 Example: The employee was caught trying to filch office supplies from the storage room after hours.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The investigation revealed that the clerk had been **filching** small amounts of cash from the register over several months.
   调查显示，这名店员在几个月内一直从收银机中偷取少量现金。

2. Students were warned that anyone caught **filching** books from the library would face immediate disciplinary action.
   学生们被警告，任何被发现从图书馆偷书的人将面临立即的纪律处分。

3. The resourceful street urchin would **filch** scraps of bread from market stalls to survive the harsh winter months.
   这个机灵的街头小孩会从集市摊位上偷面包屑来度过严酷的冬季。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "filch" as sounding like "snitch and snatch" — someone who filches quietly snatches small things when no one is looking.
把"filch"想成听起来像"悄悄地抓取"——偷窃的人趁无人注意时悄悄拿走小东西。
