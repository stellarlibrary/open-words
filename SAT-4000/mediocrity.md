# 📉 mediocrity

🔊 /ˌmiːdiˈɑːkrəti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The quality or state of being mediocre; a person of average or below-average ability or achievement
**中文：** n. 平庸；平凡；平庸之才

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ordinariness（平凡）
- averageness（平庸）
- commonplaceness（普通）
- banality（平淡无奇）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **settle for mediocrity / accept mediocrity / a life of mediocrity / rise above mediocrity**

👉 Example: The institution refused to settle for mediocrity and continually raised its academic standards.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The leader warned that complacency would inevitably lead the organization into a downward spiral of **mediocrity**.
   领导者警告说，自满会不可避免地将组织带入平庸的恶性循环。

2. She refused to accept **mediocrity** in any aspect of her academic career, constantly striving for excellence.
   她拒绝在学术生涯的任何方面接受平庸，始终追求卓越。

3. The report concluded that a tolerance for **mediocrity** among faculty had contributed to declining enrollment figures.
   报告得出结论，教师队伍中对平庸的容忍导致了入学人数的下降。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mediocrity" as "medium + city" — a city where everything is just medium, nothing stands out or excels.
把"mediocrity"想成"medium（中等）+ city（城市）"——一座一切都平平无奇的城市，没有什么出众的。
