# 📝 annotate

🔊 /ˈænəˌteɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To add notes, comments, or explanatory remarks to a text or diagram
**中文：** 注释；给…作注释；给…加注解

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- comment（注释）
- gloss（加注）
- elucidate（阐明）
- footnote（加脚注）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **annotate a text / annotate a manuscript / annotate a document / heavily annotated**

👉 Example: The professor asked students to carefully annotate the primary sources before the seminar discussion.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Scholars spent decades working to **annotate** the ancient manuscript with historical and linguistic commentary.
   学者们花了数十年时间为这部古代手稿添加历史和语言学注释。

2. The editor requested that all contributors **annotate** their references with brief explanations of each source's relevance.
   编辑要求所有撰稿人对其参考文献加注，简要说明每个来源的相关性。

3. Students who regularly **annotate** their readings tend to demonstrate stronger comprehension and retention of complex material.
   经常对阅读材料做注释的学生往往在理解和记忆复杂内容方面表现更好。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "annotate" as "a note + ate" — you add a note that gets absorbed into the text, enriching its meaning.
把"annotate"想成"a note + ate"——你添加的注释被文本"吃掉"（吸收），丰富了其含义。
