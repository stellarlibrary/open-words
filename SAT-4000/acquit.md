# ⚖️ acquit

🔊 /əˈkwɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To declare not guilty of a criminal charge; to conduct oneself in a specified way; to discharge a duty or obligation.
**中文：** 无罪释放；表现；脱卸义务和责任；清偿

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- exonerate（免罪）
- absolve（赦免）
- vindicate（证明无罪）
- discharge（释放）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **acquit someone of (a charge)**, **acquit oneself well/poorly**

👉 Example: The jury decided to acquit the defendant of all charges due to insufficient evidence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. After reviewing the evidence, the jury voted unanimously to **acquit** the accused of all charges.
   在审查证据后，陪审团一致投票宣判被告所有指控无罪。

2. Despite her inexperience, the young diplomat **acquitted** herself admirably during the international negotiations.
   尽管缺乏经验，这位年轻的外交官在国际谈判中表现出色。

3. The court chose to **acquit** the officer after new forensic evidence contradicted the prosecution's claims.
   在新的法医证据推翻了控方的主张后，法庭决定宣判该警官无罪。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"A-QUIT" — picture a person quitting the courtroom freely after being declared not guilty.
"A-QUIT"——想象一个人被宣判无罪后自由地"quit（离开）"法庭，轻松走出大门。
