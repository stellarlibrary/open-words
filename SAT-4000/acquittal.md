# 🏛️ acquittal

🔊 /əˈkwɪt.əl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A judgment or verdict that a person is not guilty of the crime charged; the act of fulfilling an obligation.
**中文：** 赦免；无罪开释；履行

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- exoneration（免罪）
- absolution（赦免）
- vindication（昭雪）
- clearance（清白证明）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **win/secure an acquittal**, **acquittal of (charges)**, **seek an acquittal**

👉 Example: The defense attorney celebrated the acquittal of her client after a lengthy trial.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **acquittal** of the defendant surprised many observers who had expected a guilty verdict.
   被告的无罪释放让许多预期有罪判决的旁观者感到意外。

2. His legal team worked tirelessly for months to secure a full **acquittal** on all criminal charges.
   他的法律团队数月来不懈努力，最终在所有刑事指控上获得了完全无罪释放。

3. The landmark **acquittal** set a significant legal precedent for similar cases across the nation.
   这一里程碑式的无罪判决为全国类似案件树立了重要的法律先例。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Acquittal" comes from "acquit" + "-al" — the noun form meaning the result of being acquitted, or set free.
"Acquittal"来自"acquit（宣判无罪）"加名词后缀"-al"，表示被宣判无罪的结果，想象法槌落下，被告重获自由。
