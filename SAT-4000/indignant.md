# 😤 indignant

🔊 /ɪnˈdɪɡnənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Feeling or showing anger or annoyance at what is perceived as unfair treatment; righteously angry
**中文：** adj. 愤愤不平的；义愤的；愤慨的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- outraged（愤怒的）
- incensed（激怒的）
- irate（发怒的）
- resentful（愤恨的）
- aggrieved（愤愤不平的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **indignant at / indignant about / indignant response / grow indignant**

👉 Example: The workers became indignant when management announced the elimination of their pension benefits.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor grew visibly **indignant** when the administration proposed cutting funding for the humanities research program entirely.
   当校方提出完全削减人文研究项目的资金时，这位教授明显变得愤愤不平。

2. Citizens wrote **indignant** letters to the editor protesting the government's decision to demolish the historic landmark downtown.
   市民们愤慨地给编辑写信，抗议政府拆除市中心历史地标的决定。

3. The diplomat issued an **indignant** statement rejecting the allegations of misconduct as entirely baseless and politically motivated.
   这位外交官发表了一份义愤填膺的声明，驳斥对其不当行为的指控完全毫无根据且出于政治目的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-dig-nant" as "not dignified" — when your dignity is violated, you feel indignant and righteously angry.
把"indignant"想成"in-dig-nant"（尊严受损）——当你的尊严被侵犯时，你会感到愤慨不平。
