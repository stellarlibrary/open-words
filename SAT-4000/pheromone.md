# 🐜 pheromone

🔊 /ˈferəmoʊn/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A chemical substance produced and released by an animal, especially an insect, that affects the behavior or physiology of others of its species
**中文：** n. 信息素；外激素（昆虫分泌以刺激同种昆虫之化合物质）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- chemical signal（化学信号）
- ectohormone（外激素）
- semiochemical（信号化学物质）
- scent marker（气味标记物）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **release pheromones / pheromone trail / pheromone receptor / sex pheromone**

👉 Example: Ants release pheromones to create invisible trails that guide other colony members to food sources.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The queen bee releases specific **pheromones** that regulate the social behavior and reproductive activity of the entire hive.
   蜂王释放特定的信息素来调节整个蜂巢的社会行为和繁殖活动。

2. Researchers discovered that certain **pheromones** can influence mate selection among various species of nocturnal insects.
   研究人员发现某些信息素可以影响各种夜行昆虫物种间的配偶选择。

3. The agricultural team employed synthetic **pheromones** as an environmentally safe method to disrupt pest mating patterns in crops.
   农业团队使用合成信息素作为一种环保方法来干扰农作物中害虫的交配模式。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pher-o-mone" as "fear-a-moan" — imagine insects moaning in fear when they detect a chemical signal from a predator.
把"pheromone"想成"fear-a-moan"——想象昆虫在检测到来自捕食者的化学信号时发出恐惧的呻吟。
