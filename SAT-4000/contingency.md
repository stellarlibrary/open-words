# 🎲 contingency

🔊 /kənˈtɪndʒənsi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A future event or circumstance that is possible but cannot be predicted with certainty; a provision for an unforeseen occurrence
**中文：** n. 偶然性；意外事故；可能性；偶然事件

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- eventuality（可能发生的事）
- possibility（可能性）
- emergency（紧急情况）
- uncertainty（不确定性）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **contingency plan / contingency fund / prepare for contingencies / in case of contingency**

👉 Example: The organization established a contingency fund to address potential financial emergencies.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The military leaders developed a detailed **contingency** plan in the event that diplomatic negotiations collapsed entirely.
   军事领导人制定了一份详细的应急计划，以防外交谈判完全破裂。

2. Prudent investors always allocate resources for **contingencies** that could disrupt the stability of the broader market.
   谨慎的投资者总是为可能扰乱更广泛市场稳定的意外事件分配资源。

3. The research team prepared for every foreseeable **contingency** before launching the expedition into the remote wilderness.
   研究团队在前往偏远荒野进行探险之前，为每一个可预见的意外情况做好了准备。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "contingency" as "it can touch you by chance" — something contingent might happen and affect you unexpectedly.
把"contingency"想成"可能偶然触及你的事"——偶然事件可能发生并意外地影响到你。
