# 🎵 ballad

🔊 /ˈbæləd/<br>
🏷️ *n. | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A narrative poem or song, often of folk origin, that tells a story in simple stanzas; a slow, romantic, or sentimental popular song.

**中文：** 歌谣，民谣；叙事诗；（流行音乐中的）抒情慢歌。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- folk song（民歌）
- anthem（颂歌）
- ditty（小曲）
- lyric（抒情诗）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Ballad" has two main modern uses: (1) a traditional narrative song or poem that tells a story, often passed down orally through generations; (2) a slow, emotional popular song, especially a love song. In literary contexts, it refers specifically to a poetic form with alternating four-stress and three-stress lines.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The wandering minstrel performed a haunting **ballad** about a tragic love affair that captivated the entire audience.
   那位流浪吟游诗人演唱了一首关于悲惨爱情的动人**歌谣**，吸引了全场观众。

2. Traditional Scottish **ballads** have been preserved for centuries through oral tradition and careful scholarly documentation alike.
   传统的苏格兰**民谣**通过口头传统和细致的学术记录被保存了数百年。

3. The singer concluded her concert with a poignant **ballad** that moved many audience members to visible tears.
   那位歌手以一首感人的**抒情歌曲**结束了她的演唱会，令许多观众感动落泪。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Ballad" comes from "ballare" (to dance) — originally, ballads were songs people danced to. Think: a "ball" (dance) + "ad" = a song for a ball or dance gathering.

"Ballad"来自"ballare（跳舞）"——最初，民谣是人们跳舞时唱的歌。联想："ball（舞会）"+"ad"= 一首舞会上的歌曲。
