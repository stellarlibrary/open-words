# ⚔️ usurp

🔊 /juːˈzɜːrp/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To seize and hold power, position, or rights by force or without legal authority
**中文：** 篡夺；夺取；侵占

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- seize（夺取）
- supplant（取代）
- appropriate（侵占）
- commandeer（征用）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **usurp power / usurp the throne / usurp authority / usurp someone's role**

👉 Example: The ambitious general attempted to usurp the throne by staging a military coup.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The military faction conspired to **usurp** control of the government through a carefully orchestrated series of political maneuvers.
   军事派系密谋通过一系列精心策划的政治手段篡夺政府的控制权。

2. Critics argued that the executive branch had attempted to **usurp** powers traditionally reserved for the legislative body.
   批评者认为行政部门试图篡夺传统上属于立法机构的权力。

3. The newly appointed manager gradually began to **usurp** the responsibilities of her colleagues without formal authorization.
   新任命的经理在没有正式授权的情况下逐渐侵占了同事们的职责。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "u-SURP" as "you serpent" — a sneaky serpent that slithers in to seize power that doesn't belong to it.
把"usurp"想成"you serpent（你这条蛇）"——一条狡猾的蛇悄悄溜进来夺取不属于它的权力。
