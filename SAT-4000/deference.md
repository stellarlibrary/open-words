# 🙇 deference

🔊 /ˈdɛfərəns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Respectful submission or yielding to the judgment, opinion, or will of another; courteous regard
**中文：** n. 顺从，尊重

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- respect（尊重）
- reverence（敬畏）
- compliance（顺从）
- submission（服从）
- regard（尊敬）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in deference to / show deference / out of deference / deference to authority**

👉 Example: In deference to the chairman's wishes, the committee postponed the controversial discussion.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The young scholar spoke with great **deference** when addressing the distinguished professor who had pioneered the field.
   这位年轻学者在向开创该领域的杰出教授发言时表现出极大的尊重。

2. In **deference** to local customs, the visiting diplomats removed their shoes before entering the sacred temple.
   出于对当地习俗的尊重，到访的外交官们在进入神圣的寺庙前脱掉了鞋子。

3. The new manager earned the team's **deference** through consistent fairness and a willingness to listen to every concern.
   新经理通过始终如一的公正和倾听每一个问题的意愿赢得了团队的尊重。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "deference" as "defer" + "ence" — the noun form of deferring to someone, showing you respect their authority enough to yield.
把"deference"想成"defer（顺从）+ ence（名词后缀）"——表示对他人的服从和尊重。
