# 🏕️ primitive

🔊 /ˈprɪmɪtɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or denoting the earliest times in history; very basic or unsophisticated. (n.) A person belonging to a preliterate or tribal society
**中文：** adj. 原始的，远古的；简单的，粗糙的 n. 原始人

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rudimentary（初步的）
- archaic（古老的）
- crude（粗糙的）
- primeval（原始的）
- unsophisticated（不复杂的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **primitive society / primitive tools / primitive conditions / primitive instincts**

👉 Example: The archaeologists uncovered primitive tools that revealed how early humans adapted to their harsh environment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **primitive** conditions of the remote outpost forced the research team to rely on basic survival skills and improvised equipment.
   偏远前哨站原始的条件迫使研究团队依靠基本的生存技能和临时设备。

2. Anthropologists studied the **primitive** art found on cave walls to understand the symbolic thinking of early human civilizations.
   人类学家研究洞穴壁上发现的原始艺术，以了解早期人类文明的象征性思维。

3. Although the prototype appeared **primitive** in design, it successfully demonstrated the core principles behind the revolutionary new technology.
   尽管这个原型在设计上显得简单粗糙，但它成功地展示了这项革命性新技术背后的核心原理。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "primitive" as "prime" + "itive" — relating to the prime (first) state of things, before they became complex or refined.
把"primitive"想成"prime（最初的）"+"itive"——与事物的最初状态有关，在它们变得复杂或精致之前。
