# 🐢 laggard

🔊 /ˈlæɡərd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Falling behind; slow to act or respond. (n.) A person who falls behind or is slow to keep up
**中文：** adj. 落后的；迟钝的 n. 落后者；迟钝者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- straggler（落伍者）
- sluggard（懒汉）
- dawdler（磨蹭的人）
- slowpoke（慢性子）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **economic laggard / laggard performance / laggard in reform / fall behind as a laggard**

👉 Example: The country remained a laggard in adopting renewable energy compared to its European neighbors.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee identified several **laggard** nations that had failed to meet the agreed-upon emission reduction targets.
   委员会指出了几个未能达到既定减排目标的落后国家。

2. Investors quickly divested from the **laggard** stocks that consistently underperformed relative to the broader market index.
   投资者迅速抛售了那些相对于大盘指数持续表现不佳的落后股票。

3. The principal urged the **laggard** students to seek tutoring before their academic standing deteriorated any further.
   校长敦促那些落后的学生在学业成绩进一步下滑之前寻求辅导。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "lag" + "-ard" — someone who lags behind gets the "-ard" label, like a person stuck in permanent lag.
把"laggard"想成"lag（落后）"加上"-ard（人）"——一个总是落后的人就是laggard。
