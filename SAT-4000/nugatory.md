# 🪶 nugatory

🔊 /ˈnjuːɡətɔːri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Having no purpose or value; useless; having no force in law; invalid
**中文：** adj. 无价值的；琐碎的；无效的；无法律效力的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- trivial（琐碎的）
- worthless（无价值的）
- futile（徒劳的）
- insignificant（无足轻重的）
- invalid（无效的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **nugatory efforts / nugatory provisions / nugatory effect / nugatory clause**

👉 Example: The court declared the unsigned amendment nugatory and therefore unenforceable.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee's recommendations proved entirely **nugatory** because the administration had already implemented an alternative plan.
   委员会的建议完全无效，因为行政部门已经实施了替代方案。

2. Critics dismissed the proposed reforms as **nugatory** measures that would fail to address the underlying structural problems.
   批评者认为拟议的改革是无价值的举措，无法解决根本性的结构问题。

3. Without proper authorization, the contractual provisions were rendered **nugatory** and could not be enforced in any jurisdiction.
   没有适当的授权，合同条款被视为无效，无法在任何司法管辖区执行。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "nugatory" as "no good story" — something so worthless and trivial that there is no good story to tell about it.
把"nugatory"想成"no good story"——某事物如此无价值和琐碎，以至于没有什么好故事可讲。
