# 🌪️ tribulation

🔊 /ˌtrɪbjʊˈleɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A cause of great trouble or suffering; a state of severe distress or affliction.
**中文：** n. 苦难；磨难；忧患

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- adversity（逆境）
- affliction（苦难）
- hardship（艰辛）
- ordeal（磨难）
- suffering（痛苦）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **trials and tribulations / great tribulation / endure tribulation / times of tribulation**

👉 Example: The refugees endured years of tribulation before finally reaching safety in a neighboring country.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The autobiography chronicles the author's many **tribulations** during the civil war, including displacement and personal loss.
   这本自传记录了作者在内战期间经历的许多磨难，包括流离失所和亲人离世。

2. Despite the **tribulations** of economic recession, the small business managed to survive through innovation and determination.
   尽管经历了经济衰退的苦难，这家小企业通过创新和决心得以生存。

3. The historical documentary examines the **tribulations** faced by early settlers who crossed vast deserts with limited supplies.
   这部历史纪录片考察了早期定居者在物资有限的情况下穿越广阔沙漠所面临的磨难。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tribulation" as related to "trouble" — both start with "tr" and involve hardship. A tribulation is an intense form of trouble.
把"tribulation"与"trouble"联系起来——两者都以"tr"开头，都涉及困难。tribulation是更强烈的trouble。
