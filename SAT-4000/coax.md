# 🪄 coax

🔊 /koʊks/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To persuade someone gently or by flattery; to obtain something by persistent gentle persuasion; to manipulate carefully into a desired condition
**中文：** v. 哄；哄诱；慢慢将……弄好；劝诱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cajole（哄骗）
- persuade（劝说）
- wheedle（巧言诱骗）
- entice（引诱）
- beguile（诱骗）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **coax someone into / coax someone out of / coax a response from / coax something to life**

👉 Example: She managed to coax the frightened kitten out from under the porch with a saucer of warm milk.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat skillfully **coaxed** both parties into resuming negotiations after months of hostile silence and mutual distrust.
   这位外交官巧妙地劝诱双方在数月的敌对沉默和相互不信任之后恢复谈判。

2. With patience and expertise, the mechanic **coaxed** the old engine back to life despite its severe corrosion damage.
   凭借耐心和专业技能，机械师慢慢地让这台严重腐蚀的旧发动机重新运转起来。

3. The professor **coaxed** a thoughtful response from the reticent student by asking carefully worded and encouraging questions.
   教授通过措辞精心且鼓励性的问题，引导这位沉默寡言的学生给出了深思熟虑的回答。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "coax" as a "coach" who gently guides and encourages — a coax is like coaching someone softly into doing something.
把"coax"想成"coach"（教练）轻柔地引导和鼓励——coax就像温和地指导某人去做某事。
