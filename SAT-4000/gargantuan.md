# 🏔️ gargantuan

🔊 /ɡɑːrˈɡæntʃuən/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Enormous; extraordinarily large in size, extent, or degree
**中文：** adj. 巨大的；庞大的；极大的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- enormous（巨大的）
- colossal（庞大的）
- immense（极大的）
- mammoth（巨型的）
- titanic（巨大的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **gargantuan task / gargantuan appetite / gargantuan proportions / gargantuan effort**

👉 Example: Building the transcontinental railway was a gargantuan undertaking that transformed the nation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The cleanup operation after the industrial disaster proved to be a **gargantuan** task that required years of sustained effort.
   工业灾难后的清理工作被证明是一项艰巨的任务，需要多年持续不断的努力。

2. The ancient cathedral, with its **gargantuan** stone pillars and soaring vaults, left visitors speechless with awe and admiration.
   这座古老的大教堂以其巨大的石柱和高耸的拱顶令游客们惊叹不已，肃然起敬。

3. Addressing climate change demands a **gargantuan** commitment of financial resources and international cooperation among all participating nations.
   应对气候变化需要各参与国投入庞大的财政资源并进行国际合作。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of Gargantua, the giant king in Rabelais' famous novel — anything "gargantuan" is as impossibly huge as that legendary giant.
想想拉伯雷著名小说中的巨人国王高康大（Gargantua）——任何"gargantuan"的东西都像那个传说中的巨人一样大得不可思议。
