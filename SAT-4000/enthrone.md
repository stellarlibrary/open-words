# 👑 enthrone

🔊 /ɪnˈθroʊn/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To install a monarch on a throne; to exalt or revere someone or something to a position of great authority or importance
**中文：** v. 使登基；崇拜

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- crown（加冕）
- install（任命就职）
- exalt（提升地位）
- inaugurate（正式就任）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **enthrone a king / enthrone a principle / enthroned in power / enthrone as leader**

👉 Example: The ceremony to enthrone the new monarch drew dignitaries from nations around the world.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient ritual to **enthrone** the emperor involved elaborate ceremonies that lasted for an entire week of celebration.
   为皇帝举行登基的古老仪式包含持续整整一周的隆重庆典。

2. Modern society has effectively **enthroned** technology as the dominant force shaping economic progress and daily human interaction.
   现代社会实际上已将技术奉为塑造经济进步和日常人类互动的主导力量。

3. The reformers sought to **enthrone** democratic principles at the heart of the newly established constitutional government.
   改革者们力图将民主原则确立为新建立的宪政政府的核心。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "en-throne" — literally placing someone "on a throne," elevating them to the highest seat of power.
把"enthrone"想成"en（使）+ throne（王座）"——将某人放上王座，即使其登基。
