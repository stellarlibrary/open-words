# 😶 acquiesce

🔊 /ˌækwiˈɛs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To accept something reluctantly but without protest; to consent or comply passively
**中文：** 默许；勉强同意

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- comply（遵从）
- consent（同意）
- concede（让步）
- yield（屈服）
- submit（顺从）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **acquiesce in / acquiesce to / reluctantly acquiesce / silently acquiesce**

👉 Example: The committee reluctantly acquiesced to the chairman's proposal despite their private reservations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Rather than risk further confrontation, the smaller nation chose to **acquiesce** to the demands of the dominant power.
   为了避免进一步对抗，这个小国选择默许了强国的要求。

2. The board members ultimately **acquiesced** in the merger proposal after months of prolonged and contentious negotiations.
   经过数月漫长而激烈的谈判后，董事会成员最终默许了合并提案。

3. Citizens should never passively **acquiesce** to policies that fundamentally undermine their constitutional rights and freedoms.
   公民绝不应消极地默认那些从根本上损害其宪法权利和自由的政策。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "acquiesce" as "a-quiet-yes" — you say yes quietly, without enthusiasm, giving silent consent.
把"acquiesce"想成"a-quiet-yes"——你安静地说"是"，没有热情，表示沉默的同意。
