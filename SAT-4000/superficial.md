# 🪞 superficial

🔊 /ˌsuːpərˈfɪʃəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Existing or occurring at or on the surface; appearing to be true or real only until examined more closely; lacking depth
**中文：** adj. 表面的；肤浅的，浅薄的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- shallow（肤浅的）
- cursory（粗略的）
- surface-level（表面的）
- cosmetic（表面的）
- perfunctory（敷衍的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **superficial knowledge / superficial wound / superficial analysis / superficial resemblance**

👉 Example: The inspector's superficial examination failed to detect the structural damage hidden beneath the surface.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist's **superficial** coverage of the crisis failed to address the underlying social and economic causes.
   记者对这场危机的肤浅报道未能触及其深层的社会和经济原因。

2. Despite their **superficial** similarities, the two philosophical traditions differ profoundly in their fundamental assumptions.
   尽管表面上有相似之处，这两种哲学传统在基本假设上有着深刻的不同。

3. The doctor confirmed that the patient's injuries were merely **superficial** and would heal completely within a few days.
   医生确认患者的伤势仅是表面的，几天内就会完全愈合。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "superficial" as "super-fish-al" — a fish that only swims at the surface never goes deep, just like superficial thinking.
把"superficial"想成"super-fish-al"——一条只在水面游的鱼从不深入，就像肤浅的思考一样。
