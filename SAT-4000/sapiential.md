# 📚 sapiential

🔊 /ˌseɪpiˈɛnʃəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Of, relating to, or conferring wisdom; characterized by wisdom or discernment
**中文：** adj. 明智的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- wise（智慧的）
- sagacious（睿智的）
- judicious（明智的）
- prudent（审慎的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sapiential literature / sapiential tradition / sapiential teachings / sapiential texts**

👉 Example: The Book of Proverbs is considered one of the most important sapiential texts in the biblical canon.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Scholars of theology devoted considerable attention to the **sapiential** books of the Old Testament for their enduring moral teachings.
   神学学者们对旧约中的智慧书给予了相当大的关注，因为其中蕴含着持久的道德教诲。

2. The professor's **sapiential** approach to mentorship emphasized critical thinking and ethical reasoning over mere memorization of facts.
   这位教授充满智慧的指导方法强调批判性思维和伦理推理，而非仅仅记忆事实。

3. Ancient **sapiential** traditions across many cultures share remarkable thematic parallels regarding justice, virtue, and the pursuit of knowledge.
   许多文化中的古代智慧传统在正义、美德和追求知识方面有着惊人的主题相似性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sapiential" from "Homo sapiens" — sapiens means "wise" in Latin, so sapiential means relating to wisdom, the defining trait of our species.
把"sapiential"和"Homo sapiens（智人）"联系起来——sapiens在拉丁语中意为"智慧的"，所以sapiential意味着与智慧有关，这是我们物种的标志性特征。
