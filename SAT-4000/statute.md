# ⚖️ statute

🔊 /ˈstætʃuːt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A written law passed by a legislative body; a rule or regulation enacted by an organization or institution
**中文：** n. 成文法；法令，法规；章程，规则；条例

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- law（法律）
- regulation（法规）
- ordinance（条例）
- enactment（法令）
- decree（法令）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **statute of limitations / federal statute / statutory requirement / by statute**

👉 Example: The new statute requires all publicly traded companies to disclose executive compensation annually.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The legislature passed a **statute** mandating that all employers provide a minimum of twelve weeks of paid parental leave.
   立法机关通过了一项法令，要求所有雇主提供至少十二周的带薪育儿假。

2. The defense attorney argued that the prosecution had exceeded the **statute** of limitations for filing criminal charges in this case.
   辩护律师辩称，检方在此案中提起刑事指控已超过诉讼时效。

3. Under the recently enacted **statute**, corporations must submit comprehensive environmental impact reports before initiating large-scale construction projects.
   根据最近颁布的法规，企业在启动大型建设项目前必须提交全面的环境影响报告。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "statute" as "state + ute" — a rule that the state puts into writing; it stands as a permanent written law.
把"statute"想成"state + ute"——国家以书面形式确立的规则，作为永久的成文法存在。
