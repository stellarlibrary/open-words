# 💀 macabre

🔊 /məˈkɑːbrə/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Disturbing and horrifying because of involvement with or depiction of death and injury; gruesome and ghastly
**中文：** 恐怖的；令人毛骨悚然的；以死亡为主题的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gruesome（可怕的）
- ghastly（骇人的）
- morbid（病态的）
- grim（阴森的）
- horrifying（令人恐惧的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **macabre tale / macabre humor / macabre scene / macabre fascination / danse macabre**

👉 Example: Edgar Allan Poe is renowned for his macabre stories that explore themes of death and psychological terror.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The museum's exhibit featured **macabre** artwork from the medieval period that depicted scenes of plague and widespread mortality.
   博物馆的展览展出了中世纪的恐怖艺术作品，描绘了瘟疫和大面积死亡的场景。

2. The detective developed a **macabre** fascination with the unsolved case, spending years studying its gruesome and disturbing details.
   这位侦探对这桩悬案产生了令人毛骨悚然的迷恋，花了数年研究其可怕而令人不安的细节。

3. Her **macabre** sense of humor often unsettled colleagues, though she insisted it was merely a coping mechanism for workplace stress.
   她令人毛骨悚然的幽默感常常让同事们不安，尽管她坚称这只是应对工作压力的一种方式。

把"macabre"想成"ma-cab（出租车）-re"——想象午夜乘坐出租车穿过墓地的恐怖经历，让你毛骨悚然。
