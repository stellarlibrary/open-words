# 💭 delusion

🔊 /dɪˈluːʒən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A false belief or impression; a mistaken or misleading perception of reality
**中文：** n. 迷惑，欺骗；错觉

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- illusion（幻觉）
- misconception（误解）
- fallacy（谬论）
- fantasy（幻想）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **delusion of grandeur / under the delusion that / suffer from delusions / harbor a delusion**

👉 Example: He was under the delusion that his unprepared speech would impress the entire academic committee.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician operated under the **delusion** that his constituents would continue to support him despite repeated ethical violations.
   这位政客在错觉中行事，以为尽管屡次违反道德准则，选民仍会继续支持他。

2. Psychologists warn that persistent **delusions** of grandeur may indicate underlying mental health conditions requiring professional intervention.
   心理学家警告说，持续的自大妄想可能表明潜在的心理健康问题，需要专业干预。

3. The researcher dismissed the popular theory as a collective **delusion** unsupported by any credible empirical evidence.
   研究人员将这一流行理论视为缺乏任何可信实证支持的集体错觉而予以否定。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-lusion" as removing ("de-") the real illusion — a delusion is when you lose touch with reality.
把"delusion"想成去掉（"de-"）真实的幻象——delusion就是你与现实脱节时的错觉。
