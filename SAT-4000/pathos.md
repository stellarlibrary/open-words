# 😢 pathos

🔊 /ˈpeɪθɒs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A quality that evokes pity, sadness, or compassion; an element in art or literature that arouses feelings of sympathy
**中文：** n. 痛苦，感伤；悲悯，同情；哀婉动人的词句

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- poignancy（辛酸）
- sadness（悲伤）
- compassion（同情）
- sentimentality（感伤）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sense of pathos / full of pathos / appeal to pathos / emotional pathos / dramatic pathos**

👉 Example: The documentary was filled with pathos as it depicted the struggles of displaced refugee families.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The novelist's portrayal of the elderly widow living in isolation was laden with profound **pathos** that moved readers deeply.
   小说家对独居老寡妇的描写充满了深刻的悲悯，深深打动了读者。

2. Aristotle identified **pathos** as one of the three essential modes of persuasion alongside logos and ethos in rhetorical theory.
   亚里士多德将悲情确定为修辞理论中与逻辑和品格并列的三种基本说服方式之一。

3. The speech derived its remarkable power from genuine **pathos** rather than from calculated sentimentality or theatrical exaggeration.
   这篇演讲的非凡力量源于真挚的感伤，而非刻意的煽情或夸张的戏剧表演。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pathos" as the root of "sympathy" and "empathy" — all share the Greek root meaning "feeling" or "suffering."
把"pathos"想成"sympathy（同情）"和"empathy（共情）"的词根——它们都共享希腊语中表示"感受"或"痛苦"的词根。
