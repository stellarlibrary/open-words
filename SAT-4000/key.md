# 🔑 key

🔊 /kiː/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb, adjective | 名词, 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A device for opening a lock; the crucial factor or means of achieving something; a button on a keyboard. (v.) To enter data using a keyboard. (adj.) Of paramount importance; crucial
**中文：** n. 钥匙；关键；键；电键 v. 键入 adj. 关键的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- crucial（关键的）
- essential（必要的）
- vital（至关重要的）
- pivotal（核心的）
- fundamental（基本的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **key factor / key role / key to success / key in data / key issue**

👉 Example: Effective communication is the key to building strong professional relationships.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee identified several **key** factors that contributed to the significant decline in regional economic growth.
   委员会确定了导致区域经济增长大幅下降的几个关键因素。

2. Researchers believe that early intervention is the **key** to preventing the spread of antibiotic-resistant infections.
   研究人员认为，早期干预是防止耐药性感染扩散的关键。

3. The assistant was asked to **key** in all the survey responses before the deadline for the quarterly report.
   助理被要求在季度报告截止日期前将所有调查回复键入系统。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of a "key" that unlocks a door — just as a physical key opens locks, a key idea "unlocks" understanding of complex problems.
想象一把打开门的"钥匙"——正如实体钥匙打开锁一样，关键想法能"打开"对复杂问题的理解。
