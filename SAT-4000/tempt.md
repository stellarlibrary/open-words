# 😈 tempt

🔊 /tɛmpt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To entice or attract someone to do something unwise or wrong; to arouse desire; to risk provoking
**中文：** v. 诱惑；引起；冒…的风险；使感兴趣

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- entice（引诱）
- lure（诱惑）
- seduce（诱惑）
- attract（吸引）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tempt fate / tempt someone into / be tempted to / tempt someone with / sorely tempted**

👉 Example: The generous scholarship offer tempted her to transfer to a more prestigious university.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The lucrative salary package was designed to **tempt** experienced professionals away from their current positions at rival firms.
   丰厚的薪酬方案旨在诱惑有经验的专业人士离开他们在竞争对手公司的现有职位。

2. Critics warned that relaxing financial regulations would **tempt** investors into taking excessive and irresponsible risks.
   批评人士警告说，放松金融监管将诱使投资者承担过度且不负责任的风险。

3. Despite the obvious dangers, the explorers were **tempted** to venture deeper into the uncharted cave system beneath the mountain.
   尽管存在明显的危险，探险者们仍被吸引着深入山下未经勘探的洞穴系统。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tempt" as "temp-t" — something temporarily attractive that pulls you toward it, like a tempting treat.
把"tempt"想成一种"临时的吸引力"——某种暂时诱人的东西把你拉向它，就像诱人的美食。
