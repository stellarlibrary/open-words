# ⛓️ chattel

🔊 /ˈtʃætəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A personal possession; movable property as distinguished from real estate; historically, a person treated as property
**中文：** n. 动产；奴隶；私人财产

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- possession（财产）
- property（所有物）
- belonging（私人物品）
- asset（资产）
- movable（动产）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **goods and chattels / chattel slavery / personal chattel / chattel mortgage**

👉 Example: Under the old legal system, enslaved people were classified as chattel rather than human beings.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The legal distinction between real property and **chattel** remains a fundamental concept in modern property law.
   不动产与动产之间的法律区分仍然是现代财产法中的一个基本概念。

2. Historians have documented how **chattel** slavery systematically denied the basic humanity of millions of people.
   历史学家记录了动产奴隶制如何系统性地剥夺了数百万人的基本人性。

3. The bankrupt merchant was forced to surrender all his goods and **chattels** to satisfy his outstanding debts.
   破产的商人被迫交出所有货物和动产来偿还未清债务。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "chattel" as related to "cattle" — in ancient times, cattle were the most common movable property people owned.
把"chattel"和"cattle（牛）"联系起来——在古代，牛是人们拥有的最常见的动产。
