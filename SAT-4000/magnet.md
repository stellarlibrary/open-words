# 🧲 magnet

🔊 /ˈmæɡnɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A piece of material that produces a magnetic field and attracts iron or steel; something or someone that powerfully attracts.
**中文：** n. 磁铁；磁体；磁石；有吸引力的人或物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- lodestone（天然磁石）
- attraction（吸引物）
- draw（吸引力）
- lure（诱惑物）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **magnet for (tourists/attention) / bar magnet / magnet school / fridge magnet**

👉 Example: The new museum has become a magnet for tourists from around the world.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The coastal city has long served as a **magnet** for artists seeking inspiration from its dramatic landscapes.
   这座沿海城市长期以来一直是寻求从壮丽景观中获得灵感的艺术家们的向往之地。

2. Scientists demonstrated how a powerful **magnet** can align iron filings into visible patterns along its field lines.
   科学家展示了强磁铁如何使铁屑沿磁力线排列成可见的图案。

3. The prestigious university acts as a **magnet** for talented scholars and researchers from every continent.
   这所著名大学如同磁石一般吸引着来自各大洲的优秀学者和研究人员。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "magnet" as coming from Magnesia, the ancient Greek region where magnetic stones were first discovered — a magnet pulls things toward it.
"magnet"源自古希腊地区Magnesia（马格尼西亚），那里最早发现了磁石——磁铁把东西吸向自己。
