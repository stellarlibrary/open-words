# 🚫 dissuade

🔊 /dɪˈsweɪd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To persuade someone not to take a particular course of action; to discourage or deter
**中文：** v. 劝阻；劝止

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- deter（阻止）
- discourage（劝阻）
- disincline（使不愿意）
- divert（转移）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dissuade someone from doing something / dissuade from a course of action / nothing could dissuade**

👉 Example: Her colleagues tried to dissuade her from resigning, but she had already made her decision.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor attempted to **dissuade** the student from dropping the course by highlighting its relevance to her future career.
   教授试图劝阻这名学生退课，强调该课程与她未来职业的相关性。

2. Despite numerous warnings, nothing could **dissuade** the explorer from venturing into the remote and uncharted mountain territory.
   尽管多次警告，没有什么能劝阻这位探险家进入偏远未知的山区。

3. The committee sought to **dissuade** investors from withdrawing their funds during the period of heightened market volatility.
   委员会试图劝阻投资者在市场剧烈波动期间撤回资金。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-suade" as "dis- (away) + suade (persuade)" — to persuade someone away from doing something.
把"dissuade"想成"dis-（远离）+ suade（劝说）"——劝说某人远离某个行动，即劝阻。
