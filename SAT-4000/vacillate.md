# 🔄 vacillate

🔊 /ˈvæsɪleɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To waver between different opinions or actions; to be indecisive; to sway unsteadily
**中文：** v. 犹豫；踌躇；摇摆

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- waver（动摇）
- hesitate（犹豫）
- fluctuate（波动）
- dither（犹豫不决）
- equivocate（含糊其辞）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vacillate between ... and ... / vacillate on a decision / vacillate over a matter**

👉 Example: The committee continued to vacillate between the two proposals for weeks.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator continued to **vacillate** on the proposed legislation, frustrating both supporters and opponents of the bill.
   这位参议员在提议的立法问题上持续摇摆不定，令法案的支持者和反对者都感到沮丧。

2. Unable to **vacillate** any longer, the board of directors finally committed to the ambitious restructuring plan.
   董事会再也无法继续犹豫，最终决定投身于这项雄心勃勃的重组计划。

3. Historians note that the king's tendency to **vacillate** between alliances ultimately led to the downfall of his dynasty.
   历史学家指出，国王在各联盟之间摇摆不定的倾向最终导致了他的王朝覆灭。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vacillate" as "vac-ill-ate" — like a vacuum that is ill, it sucks in one direction then another, never settling.
把"vacillate"想成"vac-ill-ate"——就像一个"生病的真空"，一会儿往这边吸，一会儿往那边吸，永远定不下来。
