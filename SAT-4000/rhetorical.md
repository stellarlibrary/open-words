# 🎙️ rhetorical

🔊 /rɪˈtɔːrɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Of, relating to, or concerned with the art of rhetoric; expressed in terms intended to persuade or impress; asked merely for effect with no answer expected
**中文：** adj. 修辞学的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- oratorical（演说的）
- eloquent（雄辩的）
- persuasive（有说服力的）
- grandiloquent（夸大的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rhetorical question / rhetorical device / rhetorical strategy / rhetorical flourish / rhetorical effect**

👉 Example: The speaker posed a rhetorical question to emphasize the urgency of the environmental crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor analyzed the **rhetorical** strategies employed by famous orators to persuade audiences and shape public opinion.
   教授分析了著名演说家用来说服听众和塑造公众舆论的修辞策略。

2. Her closing argument was a masterful display of **rhetorical** skill that left the jury visibly moved and deeply contemplative.
   她的结案陈词是修辞技巧的精湛展示，使陪审团明显感动并陷入深思。

3. Students must learn to distinguish between **rhetorical** appeals based on logic and those that rely primarily on emotional manipulation.
   学生必须学会区分基于逻辑的修辞诉求和主要依赖情感操控的修辞诉求。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rhetoric" + "-al" — relating to rhetoric, the ancient art of persuasive speaking. A "rhetorical question" needs no answer.
把"rhetorical"想成"rhetoric（修辞学）+ -al（的）"——与修辞相关的，古老的说服艺术。"修辞性问题"不需要回答。
