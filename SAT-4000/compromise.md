# 🤝 compromise

🔊 /ˈkɑːmprəmaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To settle a dispute by mutual concession; to expose to risk or danger. (n.) An agreement reached by each side making concessions
**中文：** v. 妥协；折衷；危及……安全 n. 妥协；折衷

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- concession（让步）
- settlement（和解）
- middle ground（折衷）
- jeopardize（危及）
- negotiate（协商）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reach a compromise / compromise on / compromise one's principles / compromise security**

👉 Example: The two parties were unable to reach a compromise on the proposed budget amendments.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. After weeks of negotiation, both nations agreed to **compromise** on trade tariffs to avoid an escalating economic conflict.
   经过数周谈判，两国同意在贸易关税上妥协，以避免经济冲突升级。

2. Sharing the leaked documents could seriously **compromise** the safety of intelligence operatives working in the region.
   泄露这些文件可能会严重危及在该地区工作的情报人员的安全。

3. The final **compromise** required each department to reduce its annual budget by an equal percentage across the board.
   最终的折衷方案要求每个部门按相同比例全面削减年度预算。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "com-promise" — both sides make a promise together (com) to meet in the middle.
把"compromise"拆成"com（共同）+ promise（承诺）"——双方共同承诺各退一步，在中间相遇。
