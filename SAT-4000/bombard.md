# 💥 bombard

🔊 /bɑːmˈbɑːrd/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To attack a place or person continuously with bombs, shells, or other missiles; to subject someone to a barrage of questions, criticism, or information. (n.) A type of early cannon.
**中文：** v. 轰炸；炮击；连续攻击（以问题、批评等） n. 射石炮（早期大炮）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- shell（炮轰）
- barrage（连续攻击）
- assail（攻击）
- besiege（围攻）
- pummel（连续猛击）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bombard with questions / bombard with information / bombard a city / bombard with requests / bombard with criticism**

👉 Example: Reporters bombarded the spokesperson with questions about the sudden policy reversal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The opposition party **bombarded** the minister with pointed questions regarding the unexplained discrepancies in the annual budget report.
   反对党就年度预算报告中无法解释的差异向部长连番发问。

2. Allied forces **bombarded** the enemy fortifications continuously for three days before the infantry launched its ground assault.
   盟军连续三天轰炸敌方防御工事，然后步兵才发起地面进攻。

3. Modern consumers are constantly **bombarded** with advertising messages across every digital platform and social media channel available.
   现代消费者在每个数字平台和社交媒体渠道上不断受到广告信息的轰炸。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bombard" as "bomb + hard" — to hit someone hard with bombs, or figuratively, to hit them hard with an overwhelming flood of questions or information.
把"bombard"想成"bomb（炸弹）+ hard（猛烈地）"——用炸弹猛烈攻击某人，或比喻性地用大量问题或信息猛烈冲击他们。
