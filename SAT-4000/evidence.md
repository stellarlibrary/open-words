# 🔍 evidence

🔊 /ˈɛvɪdəns/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Facts or information indicating whether a belief or proposition is true; signs or indications. (v.) To indicate clearly; to be evidence of
**中文：** n. 证据；根据；论据；迹象；痕迹；征兆 v. 证明

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- proof（证据）
- testimony（证词）
- indication（迹象）
- documentation（文件证据）
- confirmation（确认）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **evidence suggests / present evidence / sufficient evidence / in evidence / as evidenced by**

👉 Example: The growing body of evidence suggests that regular exercise significantly reduces the risk of chronic disease.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prosecution presented compelling **evidence** that linked the defendant directly to the scene of the crime through forensic analysis.
   控方出示了令人信服的证据，通过法医分析将被告与犯罪现场直接联系起来。

2. Archaeological **evidence** unearthed at the excavation site indicates that an advanced civilization thrived in this region over three millennia ago.
   在发掘现场出土的考古证据表明，三千多年前这个地区曾有一个先进的文明繁荣发展。

3. The sharp decline in enrollment **evidenced** a broader trend of public dissatisfaction with the institution's outdated academic curriculum.
   入学人数的急剧下降证明了公众对该机构过时学术课程更广泛的不满趋势。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "e-vid-ence" as containing "vid" (see, from Latin videre) — evidence is what allows you to see the truth clearly.
把"evidence"想成含有"vid"（看，源自拉丁语videre）——证据就是让你清楚看到真相的东西。
