# 🔒 immutable

🔊 /ɪˈmjuːtəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Not able to be changed; fixed and permanent over time
**中文：** adj. 不变的；不可变的；永远不变的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unchangeable（不可改变的）
- permanent（永久的）
- fixed（固定的）
- unalterable（不可更改的）
- constant（恒定的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **immutable law / immutable truth / immutable principle / immutable fact**

👉 Example: The laws of thermodynamics are considered immutable truths in the field of physics.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosopher argued that certain moral principles are **immutable** and transcend the boundaries of culture and time.
   这位哲学家主张某些道德原则是不变的，超越了文化和时间的界限。

2. In computer science, an **immutable** object cannot be modified after it has been created and initialized.
   在计算机科学中，不可变对象在创建和初始化之后不能被修改。

3. The constitution was designed to enshrine **immutable** rights that no government could lawfully revoke or diminish.
   宪法旨在确立任何政府都不能合法撤销或削弱的不可变权利。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-mutable" as "not mutable" — "im" means not, and "mutable" means changeable, so immutable means not changeable.
把"immutable"拆成"im-mutable"——"im"表示"不"，"mutable"表示"可变的"，所以immutable就是"不可变的"。
