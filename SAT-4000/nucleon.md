# ⚛️ nucleon

🔊 /ˈnjuːkliɒn/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A proton or neutron; a subatomic particle found in the nucleus of an atom
**中文：** n. 核子（质子或中子的统称）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- proton（质子）
- neutron（中子）
- baryon（重子）
- subatomic particle（亚原子粒子）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **nucleon number / nucleon mass / individual nucleon / nucleon interactions**

👉 Example: Each nucleon within the atomic nucleus is held together by the strong nuclear force.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The mass of an atom is primarily determined by the total number of **nucleons** contained within its nucleus.
   原子的质量主要由其原子核内所含核子的总数决定。

2. Physicists use particle accelerators to study the fundamental interactions between **nucleons** at extremely high energy levels.
   物理学家使用粒子加速器在极高能量水平下研究核子之间的基本相互作用。

3. The binding energy per **nucleon** varies across different elements and determines the stability of each atomic nucleus.
   每个核子的结合能因不同元素而异，并决定了每个原子核的稳定性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "nucle-on" — a particle that is "on" the nucleus; nucleons are the building blocks sitting inside the nucleus.
把"nucleon"想成"nucle(核) + on(上面的)"——核子就是构成原子核的基本粒子。
