# 😤 perverse

🔊 /pərˈvɜːrs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Showing a deliberate desire to behave in an unreasonable or unacceptable way; contrary to the accepted standard
**中文：** adj. 执拗的；任性的；不通情理的；堕落的；违反常情的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- obstinate（固执的）
- contrary（对立的）
- wayward（任性的）
- stubborn（倔强的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **perverse pleasure / perverse logic / perverse incentive / perverse outcome**

👉 Example: He took a perverse delight in contradicting every suggestion offered during the meeting.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee reached a **perverse** conclusion that contradicted all available evidence presented by the expert witnesses.
   委员会得出了一个违反常理的结论，与专家证人提供的所有现有证据相矛盾。

2. She felt a **perverse** satisfaction in refusing the generous offer, even though acceptance would have been advantageous.
   她在拒绝那个慷慨的提议时感到一种执拗的满足，尽管接受本会对她有利。

3. The policy created **perverse** incentives that actually encouraged the very behavior it was originally designed to prevent.
   该政策产生了适得其反的激励机制，实际上鼓励了它最初旨在防止的行为。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "per-verse" as "per" (thoroughly) + "verse" (turn) — someone who is thoroughly turned away from what is normal or reasonable.
把"perverse"想成"per"（完全地）+"verse"（转向）——一个完全偏离正常或合理方向的人。
