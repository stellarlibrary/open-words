# 🗡️ blunt

🔊 /blʌnt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, verb | 形容词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Not sharp; lacking a keen edge or point. Direct and straightforward in speech, often to the point of rudeness. (v.) To make or become less sharp or effective.
**中文：** adj. 钝的，不锋利的；生硬的；直率的 v. 使迟钝；使减弱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dull（钝的）
- frank（坦率的）
- brusque（唐突的）
- candid（直言不讳的）
- forthright（直截了当的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **blunt instrument / blunt criticism / blunt the impact / blunt force / be blunt about**

👉 Example: The critic was refreshingly blunt in her assessment of the novel's weaknesses.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator's **blunt** remarks about the failing economy startled both journalists and fellow legislators during the press conference.
   参议员在新闻发布会上对经济衰退的直率评论震惊了记者和其他立法者。

2. Years of repeated use had **blunted** the blade so severely that it could no longer cut through even the thinnest material.
   多年的反复使用使刀刃严重变钝，甚至连最薄的材料都切不动了。

3. The professor's **blunt** assessment of the research proposal forced the graduate students to reconsider their entire methodology.
   教授对研究提案的直率评价迫使研究生们重新审视他们的整个研究方法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "blunt" as a knife that is "bl-unt" (barely usable, not terrific) — a dull knife that cannot cut, just like blunt words cut straight to the point without finesse.
把"blunt"想成一把"勉强能用"的刀——钝刀切不了东西，正如直率的话语直奔主题，毫不修饰。
