# 🐴 hackney

🔊 /ˈhækni/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A horse suitable for ordinary riding or driving; a carriage for hire. (v.) To use or employ excessively; to make common or trite through overuse
**中文：** n. 乘用马；出租马车 v. 役使；出租

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- overuse（过度使用）
- exploit（利用）
- hire out（出租）
- wear out（用旧）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **hackney coach / hackney carriage / hackney a phrase / hackneyed expression**

👉 Example: In nineteenth-century London, one could easily hackney a carriage to travel across the sprawling city.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **hackney** carriages lined up along the cobblestone streets, awaiting passengers traveling to the theater district each evening.
   出租马车排列在鹅卵石街道上，每晚等待前往剧院区的乘客。

2. Politicians often **hackney** the same patriotic slogans until they lose all meaning and persuasive power among the electorate.
   政客们常常反复使用同样的爱国口号，直到这些口号在选民中失去所有意义和说服力。

3. The sturdy **hackney** breed was historically prized for its reliable temperament and exceptional endurance over long distances.
   哈克尼马种历来因其可靠的性情和长途卓越的耐力而备受推崇。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "hackney" as "hack + knee" — a horse that has been hacked (ridden hard) until its knees are worn, suggesting overuse and hiring out.
把"hackney"想成"hack（骑马）+ knee（膝盖）"——一匹被骑到膝盖磨损的马，暗示过度使用和出租。
