# 🃏 sophistical

🔊 /səˈfɪstɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Of, relating to, or characterized by sophistry; misleadingly subtle or deceptive in reasoning
**中文：** adj. 诡辩的；强词夺理的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fallacious（谬误的）
- specious（似是而非的）
- casuistic（诡辩的）
- misleading（误导的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sophistical argument / sophistical reasoning / sophistical trick / sophistical logic**

👉 Example: The debater employed sophistical reasoning that sounded convincing but ultimately lacked logical validity.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor exposed the **sophistical** nature of the thesis, demonstrating that its conclusions rested on deliberately flawed premises.
   教授揭露了该论文诡辩的本质，证明其结论建立在刻意错误的前提之上。

2. Voters grew increasingly skeptical of the candidate's **sophistical** promises, which sounded appealing but defied all economic logic.
   选民们对候选人诡辩式的承诺越来越持怀疑态度，这些承诺听起来诱人却违反所有经济逻辑。

3. The committee rejected the **sophistical** defense presented by the corporation, noting its reliance on distorted statistical interpretations.
   委员会驳回了该公司提出的强词夺理的辩护，指出其依赖于被歪曲的统计解读。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sophistical" as "sophist + ical" — like a Sophist's argument, it sounds sophisticated on the surface but is ultimately deceptive.
把"sophistical"想成"sophist + ical"——就像智者派的论证，表面听起来很精妙，实际上却是骗人的。
