# 💚 reassure

🔊 /ˌriːəˈʃʊr/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To say or do something to remove someone's doubts or fears; to restore confidence
**中文：** vt. 使放心，使安心，安慰；使消除疑虑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- comfort（安慰）
- encourage（鼓励）
- soothe（抚慰）
- console（安慰）
- hearten（鼓舞）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reassure someone that… / reassure the public / reassure investors / feel reassured**

👉 Example: The doctor took time to reassure the patient that the procedure carried minimal risk.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Government officials sought to **reassure** the public that all necessary measures had been taken to ensure national security.
   政府官员试图让公众放心，所有必要的措施均已采取以确保国家安全。

2. The manager personally called each client to **reassure** them that the project would be delivered on the original schedule.
   经理亲自致电每位客户，让他们放心项目将按原定计划交付。

3. Parents often need to **reassure** their children that starting at a new school will eventually become a positive experience.
   家长往往需要安慰孩子，让他们相信进入新学校最终会成为一段积极的经历。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-assure" as "to assure again" — when someone is worried, you assure them once more to make them feel safe.
把"reassure"想成"re + assure"——当某人担心时，你再次给予保证，让他们感到安心。
