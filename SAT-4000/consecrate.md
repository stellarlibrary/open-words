# ⛪ consecrate

🔊 /ˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make or declare something sacred; to dedicate formally to a religious or divine purpose; to devote entirely to a specific purpose.
**中文：** v. 奉献；使神圣；供神用

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sanctify（使神圣）
- dedicate（奉献）
- hallow（使神圣化）
- bless（祝圣）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **consecrate a church / consecrate ground / consecrate one's life to / consecrated land**

👉 Example: The bishop arrived to consecrate the new cathedral in a solemn ceremony attended by thousands of worshippers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient ritual was performed to **consecrate** the temple grounds, ensuring they would be regarded as sacred by all visitors.
   这一古老的仪式旨在使寺庙场地神圣化，确保所有来访者都将其视为圣地。

2. Lincoln declared that the living could not **consecrate** the battlefield more than the brave soldiers who had fought there.
   林肯宣称，活着的人无法比在那里战斗过的勇敢士兵更能使这片战场变得神圣。

3. She chose to **consecrate** her life to the advancement of medical research for the benefit of future generations.
   她选择将自己的一生奉献给医学研究的进步，以造福后代。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con-secr-ate" as "with (con) sacred (secr)" — to consecrate something is to make it sacred together with a formal ceremony.
把"consecrate"想成"使之与(con)神圣(secr)结合"——使某物通过正式仪式变得神圣。
