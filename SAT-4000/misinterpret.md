# 🔄 misinterpret

🔊 /ˌmɪsɪnˈtɜːrprɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To interpret or understand something incorrectly; to distort the meaning of words, actions, or data
**中文：** v. 曲解；误解

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- misconstrue（误解）
- misunderstand（误会）
- distort（歪曲）
- misread（误读）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **misinterpret the data / misinterpret a statement / easily misinterpreted / misinterpret intentions**

👉 Example: The journalist was accused of misinterpreting the senator's remarks to create a more sensational headline.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Students often **misinterpret** statistical correlations as evidence of direct causation, leading to fundamentally flawed analytical conclusions.
   学生们经常将统计相关性误解为直接因果关系的证据，从而得出根本性错误的分析结论。

2. The ambassador warned that foreign governments might **misinterpret** the military exercise as a deliberate act of provocation.
   大使警告说，外国政府可能会将这次军事演习曲解为蓄意的挑衅行为。

3. Researchers cautioned that the ambiguous wording of the survey could cause respondents to **misinterpret** several critical questions.
   研究人员提醒说，问卷中模糊的措辞可能导致受访者误解几个关键问题。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mis-interpret" as "miss the interpretation" — you missed the correct meaning and arrived at the wrong one.
把"misinterpret"想成"miss the interpretation（错过正确的解释）"——你错过了正确的含义，得出了错误的理解。
