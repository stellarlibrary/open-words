# 🔄 recant

🔊 /rɪˈkænt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To withdraw or renounce a formerly held belief or statement, especially publicly and formally
**中文：** v. 宣布放弃；公开认错；撤回

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- retract（撤回）
- renounce（放弃）
- disavow（否认）
- abjure（发誓放弃）
- revoke（撤销）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **recant a statement / recant one's beliefs / recant testimony / forced to recant**

👉 Example: Under intense pressure from the church authorities, Galileo was forced to recant his support for the heliocentric model.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The witness was compelled to **recant** her earlier testimony after new forensic evidence contradicted her original account of events.
   在新的法医证据与她最初的事件陈述相矛盾后，证人被迫撤回了先前的证词。

2. The politician publicly **recanted** his controversial remarks, acknowledging that they were insensitive and factually inaccurate.
   这位政治家公开撤回了其有争议的言论，承认这些言论不当且与事实不符。

3. Throughout history, scholars who challenged prevailing doctrines were often pressured to **recant** their findings under threat of persecution.
   纵观历史，挑战主流学说的学者常常在迫害威胁下被迫放弃自己的研究发现。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-cant" as "can't say it again" — when you recant, you take back what you said because you can't stand by it anymore.
把"recant"想成"re + cant (can't)"——当你recant时，你收回说过的话，因为你无法再坚持下去了。
