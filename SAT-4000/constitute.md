# 🏗️ constitute

🔊 /ˈkɑːnstɪtuːt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To compose or make up a whole; to establish or enact formally; to appoint to an office or function
**中文：** v. 构成，组成；建立，设立；任命

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- compose（构成）
- comprise（包含）
- establish（建立）
- form（形成）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **constitute a threat / constitute a majority / constitute a violation / constitute evidence**

👉 Example: These irregularities may constitute a serious violation of international trade agreements.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The thirteen original colonies collectively **constituted** the foundation upon which the United States of America was established.
   最初的十三个殖民地共同构成了美利坚合众国建立的基础。

2. Legal scholars debated whether the executive order could **constitute** an overreach of presidential authority under the constitution.
   法律学者们争论总统行政令是否构成宪法下总统权力的越权行为。

3. Women now **constitute** nearly half of the enrollment in graduate programs at leading research universities worldwide.
   女性现在几乎占全球顶级研究型大学研究生入学人数的一半。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con-STITUTE" — like an "institute" that is put together ("con") from many parts to form a complete organization.
把"constitute"想成"con（共同）+ stitute（建立）"——像一个由许多部分组合在一起形成完整组织的"机构"。
