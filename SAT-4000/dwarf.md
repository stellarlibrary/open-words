# 🧝 dwarf

🔊 /dwɔːrf/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun, adjective | 动词, 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make something appear small by comparison. (n.) A person, animal, or plant much below the ordinary size. (adj.) Unusually small in size
**中文：** v. 使相形见绌；变矮小 n. 侏儒；矮子 adj. 矮小的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- overshadow（使黯然失色）
- diminish（缩小）
- miniature（微型的）
- tower over（高耸于）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dwarf a competitor / dwarfed by comparison / dwarf planet / dwarf species**

👉 Example: The massive skyscraper dwarfed every other structure in the surrounding neighborhood.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corporation's annual revenue **dwarfed** the combined earnings of its three closest competitors in the industry.
   该公司的年收入使其行业内三个最接近的竞争对手的总收入相形见绌。

2. Scientists discovered a new **dwarf** species of frog in the tropical rainforest, measuring barely one centimeter in length.
   科学家在热带雨林中发现了一种新的矮小蛙类物种，体长仅约一厘米。

3. The significance of the initial findings was **dwarfed** by the groundbreaking results of the subsequent experimental trials.
   初步发现的重要性被后续实验的突破性结果所掩盖。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dwarf" as in Snow White's seven dwarfs — they are tiny, and anything next to a giant is "dwarfed" by comparison.
想想白雪公主的七个小矮人——他们很矮小，任何东西在巨人旁边都会显得"矮小"。
