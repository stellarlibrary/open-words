# 😤 provoke

🔊 /prəˈvoʊk/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To stimulate or incite someone to action or feeling; to deliberately anger or annoy; to give rise to a reaction or emotion
**中文：** v. 驱使；激怒；煽动；惹起；挑动；激发；招惹

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- incite（煽动）
- irritate（激怒）
- elicit（引出）
- goad（刺激）
- instigate（唆使）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **provoke a response / provoke anger / provoke debate / provoke controversy**

👉 Example: His inflammatory remarks were clearly intended to provoke outrage among the audience.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator's controversial proposal **provoked** an intense debate among legislators regarding the future of environmental policy.
   参议员那项有争议的提案在立法者之间引发了关于环境政策未来的激烈辩论。

2. Researchers discovered that certain chemical compounds can **provoke** an immune response even in previously unexposed subjects.
   研究人员发现某些化合物即使在先前未接触过的受试者中也能激发免疫反应。

3. The editorial was deliberately written to **provoke** public discourse on the ethical implications of artificial intelligence.
   这篇社论有意撰写以挑动公众对人工智能伦理影响的讨论。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pro-VOKE" as "pro-VOICE" — when you provoke someone, you push them to raise their voice in anger or reaction.
把"provoke"想成"pro-voice"——当你激怒某人时，你迫使他们提高嗓门来表达愤怒或反应。
