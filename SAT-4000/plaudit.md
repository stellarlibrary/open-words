# 👏 plaudit

🔊 /ˈplɔːdɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An expression of praise or approval; enthusiastic applause or acclaim
**中文：** n. 喝彩；赞美

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- acclaim（赞扬）
- applause（掌声）
- commendation（表彰）
- accolade（荣誉）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **win plaudits / receive plaudits / earn plaudits from**

👉 Example: The director earned plaudits from critics for her bold and innovative approach to storytelling.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The young scientist received widespread **plaudits** for her groundbreaking research on renewable energy storage solutions.
   这位年轻科学家因其在可再生能源储存方面的开创性研究而获得广泛赞誉。

2. Despite initial skepticism, the new policy won **plaudits** from economists who praised its balanced fiscal approach.
   尽管最初受到质疑，这项新政策赢得了经济学家的赞美，他们称赞其平衡的财政方针。

3. The orchestra's flawless performance drew enthusiastic **plaudits** from the distinguished audience at the concert hall.
   管弦乐队完美的演出赢得了音乐厅内杰出观众的热烈喝彩。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "plaudit" as related to "applaud" — when you applaud someone, you give them plaudits of praise and approval.
把"plaudit"和"applaud（鼓掌）"联系起来——当你为某人鼓掌时，你就是在给予他们喝彩和赞美。
