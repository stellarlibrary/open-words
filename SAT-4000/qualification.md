# 🎓 qualification

🔊 /ˌkwɒlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A credential or accomplishment that makes someone suitable for a particular role; a condition or requirement; a modification or limitation of a statement
**中文：** n. 资格，合格；技能；限定，条件；合格证

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- credential（资质）
- certification（证书）
- prerequisite（先决条件）
- reservation（保留意见）
- stipulation（规定）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **academic qualification / without qualification / qualification for a position / necessary qualifications**

👉 Example: The job listing specified advanced degrees as one of the key qualifications for the research position.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee accepted her statement with the **qualification** that further empirical evidence would be required before any policy changes.
   委员会接受了她的声明，但附加了一个限定条件：在进行任何政策变更之前需要更多的实证证据。

2. His extensive clinical experience served as the primary **qualification** that distinguished him from the other candidates for the position.
   他丰富的临床经验是使他在该职位的其他候选人中脱颖而出的主要资质。

3. The researcher offered her endorsement without **qualification**, stating that the methodology was both rigorous and thoroughly documented.
   研究人员毫无保留地给予了认可，称该方法论既严谨又有充分的文献记录。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "qualifi-cation" — "qualify" means to meet a standard, and "-cation" makes it a noun, so it is the thing that proves you meet the standard.
把"qualification"想成"qualify（合格）+ -cation（名词后缀）"——证明你达到标准的东西，即资格。
