# ✋ interrupt

🔊 /ˌɪntəˈrʌpt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To stop the continuous progress of something; to break in on someone's speech or action. (n.) A break or halt in continuity
**中文：** v. 中断；打断；插嘴；妨碍 n. 中断

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disrupt（扰乱）
- suspend（暂停）
- interject（插话）
- disturb（打扰）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **interrupt a conversation / interrupt the process / interrupt someone's speech / without interruption**

👉 Example: Please do not interrupt the speaker during the formal presentation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden power outage **interrupted** the entire broadcast, leaving millions of viewers without a signal for several minutes.
   突然的停电中断了整个广播，使数百万观众在几分钟内失去了信号。

2. It is considered impolite to **interrupt** a colleague during a professional meeting, especially when they are presenting critical findings.
   在专业会议上打断同事的发言被认为是不礼貌的，尤其是当他们在展示重要发现时。

3. Construction noise from the adjacent building constantly **interrupted** the students' ability to concentrate on their examinations.
   来自隔壁建筑的施工噪音不断干扰学生在考试中集中注意力的能力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inter-rupt" as "inter" (between) + "rupt" (break) — something breaking in between, cutting off the flow of activity.
把"interrupt"想成"inter"（之间）+ "rupt"（断裂）——某事在中间断开，切断了活动的进程。
