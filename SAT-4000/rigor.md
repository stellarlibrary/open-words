# 🔬 rigor

🔊 /ˈrɪɡər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Strictness, severity, or exactness; the quality of being thorough, exhaustive, and accurate
**中文：** n. 严格，严厉

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- strictness（严格）
- severity（严厉）
- precision（精确）
- thoroughness（彻底）
- exactitude（精确）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **academic rigor / intellectual rigor / scientific rigor / rigor of the law / with rigor**

👉 Example: The doctoral program is renowned for the intellectual rigor it demands of all candidates.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journal's editorial board is committed to maintaining the highest standards of scientific **rigor** in every published research article.
   该期刊的编辑委员会致力于在每篇发表的研究论文中保持最高标准的科学严谨性。

2. Critics argued that the study lacked methodological **rigor**, rendering its conclusions unreliable and unsuitable for informing public policy decisions.
   批评者认为该研究缺乏方法论上的严谨性，导致其结论不可靠，不适合为公共政策决策提供参考。

3. The **rigor** of military training prepared the recruits both physically and mentally for the demanding conditions they would face abroad.
   严格的军事训练使新兵在身体和心理上为他们将在海外面临的严酷条件做好了准备。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rigor" as in "rigor mortis" — the stiffness of death is absolute and unyielding, just like strict standards that refuse to bend.
把"rigor"想成"rigor mortis"（尸僵）——死亡的僵硬是绝对不屈的，就像拒绝妥协的严格标准一样。
