# ⚡ polarization

🔊 /ˌpoʊlərɪˈzeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The division of something into two sharply contrasting groups or opinions; (physics) the restriction of light waves to vibrate in one direction
**中文：** n. 两极分化；极化；偏振

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- division（分裂）
- divergence（分歧）
- dichotomy（二分法）
- schism（分裂）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **political polarization / polarization of society / polarization of light / increasing polarization**

👉 Example: The polarization of public opinion has made bipartisan compromise increasingly difficult to achieve.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The growing **polarization** between liberal and conservative factions has undermined constructive dialogue in the legislature.
   自由派和保守派之间日益加剧的两极分化削弱了立法机构中的建设性对话。

2. Scientists measured the **polarization** of light passing through the crystal to determine its optical properties accurately.
   科学家测量了穿过晶体的光的偏振，以准确测定其光学性质。

3. Social media algorithms have contributed significantly to the **polarization** of political discourse in democratic societies worldwide.
   社交媒体算法极大地加剧了全球民主社会中政治话语的两极分化。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "polar" as in the North and South Poles — polarization pushes things to opposite extremes, just like the two poles of the Earth.
把"polarization"想成"polar（极地的）"——两极分化就像把事物推向南北极那样的两个极端。
