# ⚖️ malfeasance

🔊 /mælˈfiːzəns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Wrongdoing or misconduct, especially by a public official; illegal or unethical professional conduct
**中文：** n. 渎职；违法行为；不正当行为

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- misconduct（不端行为）
- wrongdoing（违法行为）
- corruption（腐败）
- malpractice（渎职）
- transgression（违反）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **corporate malfeasance / official malfeasance / accused of malfeasance / evidence of malfeasance**

👉 Example: The mayor was removed from office after investigators uncovered evidence of financial malfeasance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The investigation revealed widespread **malfeasance** among senior officials who had embezzled millions from public infrastructure funds.
   调查揭露了高级官员中普遍存在的渎职行为，他们从公共基础设施基金中挪用了数百万。

2. Corporate **malfeasance** at the energy company led to billions of dollars in losses for investors and pensioners alike.
   这家能源公司的违法行为导致投资者和养老金领取者共损失了数十亿美元。

3. The ethics committee launched a formal inquiry into allegations of **malfeasance** brought against the district attorney's office.
   伦理委员会对针对地区检察官办公室的渎职指控展开了正式调查。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mal-feasance" as "bad doing" — "mal" means bad and "feasance" comes from "faire" (to do in French), so it literally means doing something bad, especially in office.
把"malfeasance"想成"坏的行为"——"mal"意为坏的，"feasance"来自法语"faire"（做），所以字面意思就是做坏事，尤指在职务中。
