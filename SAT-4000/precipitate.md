# ⚡ precipitate

🔊 /prɪˈsɪpɪteɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb, adjective | 名词, 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A substance separated from a solution during a chemical reaction. (v.) To cause to happen suddenly; to throw down; to condense and fall as rain or snow. (adj.) Done with excessive haste; rash.
**中文：** n. 沉淀物 v. 促成；使沉淀；猛地落下 adj. 突如其来的；急促的；鲁莽的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- hasten（加速）
- trigger（触发）
- provoke（引发）
- rash（鲁莽的）
- abrupt（突然的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **precipitate a crisis / precipitate action / precipitate a reaction / precipitate change**

👉 Example: The assassination of the archduke precipitated a chain of events that led to the outbreak of war.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden withdrawal of foreign investment **precipitated** a severe economic downturn that lasted for several years.
   外国投资的突然撤出促成了一场持续数年的严重经济衰退。

2. Critics argued that the government's **precipitate** decision to deregulate the industry ignored the potential environmental consequences.
   批评人士认为，政府仓促决定放松对该行业的管制，忽视了潜在的环境后果。

3. When the chemical solution cooled, a white **precipitate** formed at the bottom of the glass beaker.
   当化学溶液冷却时，一种白色沉淀物在玻璃烧杯底部形成。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "precipitate" as rain that "precipitates" from the sky — things falling down suddenly, whether substances, events, or decisions.
把"precipitate"想成从天而降的雨——无论是物质、事件还是决定，都是突然落下的。
