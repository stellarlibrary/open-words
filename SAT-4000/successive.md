# 🔄 successive

🔊 /səkˈsɛsɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Following in uninterrupted order; consecutive; occurring one after another without a break
**中文：** adj. 连续的，相继的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- consecutive（连续的）
- sequential（依次的）
- subsequent（随后的）
- continuous（不断的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **successive generations / successive years / successive victories / successive waves / successive attempts**

👉 Example: The team celebrated three successive championship titles, an unprecedented achievement in the league's history.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. After five **successive** quarters of declining revenue, the board of directors voted to replace the chief executive officer.
   在连续五个季度收入下降后，董事会投票决定更换首席执行官。

2. **Successive** governments have failed to address the chronic housing shortage that plagues the nation's major urban centers.
   历届政府都未能解决困扰该国主要城市中心的长期住房短缺问题。

3. The archaeological site revealed **successive** layers of human habitation spanning more than three thousand years of civilization.
   该考古遗址揭示了跨越三千多年文明的连续人类居住层。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "successive" as "success-ive" — one success following another in a row, like dominoes falling one after the next.
把"successive"想成"success-ive"——一个成功接着一个成功，就像多米诺骨牌一个接一个地倒下。
