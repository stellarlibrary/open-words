# 🎩 priggish

🔊 /ˈprɪɡɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Self-righteously moralistic and superior; excessively proper or correct in behavior and attitude
**中文：** adj. 一本正经的；自负的；死板的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- self-righteous（自以为是的）
- smug（沾沾自喜的）
- prudish（过分拘谨的）
- sanctimonious（假装虔诚的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **priggish attitude / priggish behavior / priggish tone / seem priggish**

👉 Example: His priggish insistence on correcting everyone's grammar made him unpopular at social gatherings.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's **priggish** demeanor alienated many students who found his constant moral lectures insufferable and condescending.
   这位教授一本正经的态度疏远了许多学生，他们觉得他不断的道德说教令人难以忍受且居高临下。

2. Her **priggish** refusal to participate in any lighthearted activities made her colleagues regard her as unapproachable.
   她死板地拒绝参加任何轻松的活动，使得同事们认为她难以接近。

3. The reviewer adopted a **priggish** tone throughout the article, dismissing contemporary art as morally inferior to classical works.
   评论者在整篇文章中采用了自负的语气，将当代艺术贬为在道德上不如古典作品。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "priggish" as "prig" + "ish" — a prig is someone who acts overly proper, so priggish means behaving like a stuck-up know-it-all.
把"priggish"想成"prig（道学先生）"+"ish（像……的）"——prig是指过分正经的人，所以priggish就是表现得像自以为是的学究。
