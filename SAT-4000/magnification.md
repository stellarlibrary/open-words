# 🔬 magnification

🔊 /ˌmæɡnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The action or process of making something appear larger than it is; the degree to which something is or can be magnified.
**中文：** n. 放大；放大率；放大倍数

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- enlargement（放大）
- amplification（扩大）
- enhancement（增强）
- expansion（扩展）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **high / low magnification / magnification power / under magnification / level of magnification**

👉 Example: Under high magnification, the cell structure revealed intricate details invisible to the naked eye.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. At higher **magnification**, the microscope revealed a complex network of previously undetected cellular structures.
   在更高的放大倍数下，显微镜揭示了一个此前未被发现的复杂细胞结构网络。

2. The telescope's impressive **magnification** allowed astronomers to observe distant galaxies with remarkable clarity and precision.
   望远镜令人印象深刻的放大倍数使天文学家能够以惊人的清晰度和精度观测遥远的星系。

3. Adjusting the **magnification** of the lens is essential for obtaining accurate measurements in laboratory experiments.
   调整镜头的放大倍数对于在实验室实验中获得准确的测量结果至关重要。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "magni" (great, large) + "fication" (making) — magnification is the process of "making something great" or larger so you can see it clearly.
把"magnification"想成"magni"（大的）+"fication"（使之成为）——放大就是"使之变大"的过程，让你能看得更清楚。
