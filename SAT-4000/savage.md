# 🐺 savage

🔊 /ˈsævɪdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun, verb | 形容词, 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Fierce, violent, and uncontrolled; cruel and vicious. (n.) A brutal or vicious person. (v.) To attack ferociously; to maul
**中文：** adj. 野蛮的，未开化的 n. 原始的人，粗鲁的人

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ferocious（凶猛的）
- brutal（残忍的）
- barbarous（野蛮的）
- fierce（凶猛的）
- vicious（恶毒的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **savage attack / savage criticism / savage beast / savage cuts / savage blow**

👉 Example: The documentary exposed the savage conditions endured by workers in unregulated overseas factories.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The editorial delivered a **savage** critique of the administration's failure to address the worsening humanitarian crisis along the border.
   这篇社论对政府未能解决边境日益恶化的人道主义危机进行了猛烈的批评。

2. The **savage** winds of the hurricane tore through the coastal town, reducing entire neighborhoods to piles of unrecognizable debris.
   飓风的狂暴大风席卷了这个沿海小镇，将整个社区夷为一片无法辨认的废墟。

3. Critics **savaged** the new legislation, arguing that it would disproportionately harm vulnerable communities already struggling with economic hardship.
   批评者猛烈抨击了这项新立法，认为它会不成比例地伤害已经在经济困难中挣扎的弱势群体。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "savage" as "save-age" reversed — in an age before civilization could save people, life was savage, wild, and brutal.
把"savage"想成"save（拯救）+ age（时代）"的反转——在文明尚未拯救人类的时代，生活是野蛮、原始和残酷的。
