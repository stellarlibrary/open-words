# 🚫 preclude

🔊 /prɪˈkluːd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To prevent from happening; to make impossible; to exclude or rule out.
**中文：** v. 阻止；排除；妨碍；使不可能

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- prevent（阻止）
- prohibit（禁止）
- forestall（预防）
- hinder（妨碍）
- exclude（排除）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **preclude the possibility / preclude someone from doing / preclude any chance / preclude further action**

👉 Example: The severe weather conditions precluded any possibility of launching the rescue mission that afternoon.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The restrictive terms of the contract effectively **precluded** the company from pursuing any competing business ventures.
   合同中的限制性条款实际上阻止了该公司从事任何竞争性商业活动。

2. A lack of sufficient funding **precluded** the researchers from conducting the large-scale clinical trials they had originally planned.
   资金不足使研究人员无法进行他们原先计划的大规模临床试验。

3. The judge ruled that the new evidence did not **preclude** the defendant from receiving a fair and impartial trial.
   法官裁定，新证据并不妨碍被告接受公正和公平的审判。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-CLUDE" — "clude" comes from Latin "claudere" (to close). To preclude is to close the door before someone can enter.
把"preclude"想成"pre（预先）+ clude（关闭）"——在某人进入之前就把门关上，即预先阻止。
