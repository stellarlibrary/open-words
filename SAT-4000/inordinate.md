# ⚠️ inordinate

🔊 /ɪnˈɔːrdɪnət/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Unusually or disproportionately large; exceeding reasonable limits; excessive
**中文：** 过度的；无节制的；紊乱的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- excessive（过度的）
- disproportionate（不相称的）
- exorbitant（过分的）
- immoderate（无节制的）
- undue（不当的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inordinate amount of / inordinate delay / inordinate influence / inordinate pride**

👉 Example: The project consumed an inordinate amount of time due to repeated bureaucratic delays.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics argued that the government spent an **inordinate** amount of taxpayer funds on a program with minimal measurable outcomes.
   批评者认为政府将过多的纳税人资金花在了一个成果甚微的项目上。

2. The professor devoted an **inordinate** portion of the lecture to a single historical event, leaving little time for discussion.
   教授将过多的讲座时间花在了一个历史事件上，几乎没有留下讨论的时间。

3. An **inordinate** number of appeals were filed against the new regulation, overwhelming the administrative review board entirely.
   针对新法规提交了过多的上诉，完全压垮了行政审查委员会。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-ordin-ate" — "in" (not) + "ordin" (order). Something inordinate is not in order; it has gone beyond normal limits.
把"inordinate"想成"in（不）+ ordin（秩序）"——不符合秩序的，超出了正常的限度。
