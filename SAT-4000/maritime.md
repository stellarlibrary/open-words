# 🚢 maritime

🔊 /ˈmærɪtaɪm/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Connected with the sea, especially in relation to navigation, shipping, or naval matters; situated near or bordering the coast
**中文：** adj. 海的；海事的；海运的；沿海的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- nautical（航海的）
- naval（海军的）
- oceanic（海洋的）
- seafaring（航海的）
- coastal（沿海的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **maritime law / maritime industry / maritime trade / maritime climate / maritime province**

👉 Example: International maritime law governs the rights and responsibilities of nations with respect to their use of the world's oceans.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The museum's **maritime** collection includes navigational instruments, ship models, and logbooks from the eighteenth-century colonial era.
   该博物馆的海事藏品包括十八世纪殖民时代的航海仪器、船舶模型和航海日志。

2. Countries with significant coastlines often develop robust **maritime** industries encompassing fishing, shipping, and offshore energy production.
   拥有漫长海岸线的国家通常会发展出涵盖渔业、航运和近海能源生产的强大海事产业。

3. The regional economy depends heavily on **maritime** trade, with the port handling millions of tons of cargo each year.
   该地区的经济严重依赖海上贸易，港口每年处理数百万吨的货物。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Maritime" starts with "mari-" from Latin "mare" meaning sea (like "marine") — anything maritime is connected to the sea.
"Maritime"以"mari-"开头，源自拉丁语"mare"意为海洋（如"marine"）——任何maritime的事物都与海洋有关。
