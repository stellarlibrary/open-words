# 🔥 excoriate

🔊 /ɪkˈskɔːrieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To censure or criticize severely; to strip or wear off the skin; to abrade
**中文：** 痛斥；责骂；擦伤；磨掉皮肤

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- denounce（谴责）
- lambaste（严厉批评）
- castigate（严惩）
- rebuke（斥责）
- abrade（磨损）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **excoriate someone for / publicly excoriate / excoriate a policy / excoriate the administration**

👉 Example: The editorial excoriated the mayor for his failure to address the housing crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator **excoriated** the proposed budget cuts, arguing they would devastate essential public services relied upon by millions.
   这位参议员严厉批评了拟议的预算削减，认为这将摧毁数百万人依赖的基本公共服务。

2. Leading medical journals **excoriated** the pharmaceutical company for concealing critical data about the drug's dangerous side effects.
   权威医学期刊痛斥这家制药公司隐瞒了关于该药物危险副作用的关键数据。

3. The literary critic **excoriated** the novel as intellectually shallow and derivative, lacking any semblance of original thought.
   这位文学评论家严厉抨击这部小说在思想上肤浅且缺乏创意，毫无原创性可言。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-CORE-iate" — criticism so harsh it cuts to the core, stripping away all pretense just as skin is stripped away.
把"excoriate"想成"ex-CORE-iate"——批评如此严厉以至于直击核心，剥去所有伪装，就像剥去皮肤一样。
