# 🌊 meander

🔊 /miˈændər/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To follow a winding course; to wander aimlessly. (n.) A winding curve or bend; an aimless wandering
**中文：** v. 蜿蜒缓慢流动；漫步 n. 曲流；漫步

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- wander（漫游）
- ramble（漫步）
- wind（蜿蜒）
- stroll（闲逛）
- roam（游荡）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **meander through / meander along / meander across / the river meanders**

👉 Example: The river meanders through the valley, creating fertile plains on either side of its winding path.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient river **meanders** through the countryside, carving intricate patterns into the landscape over thousands of years.
   这条古老的河流蜿蜒穿过乡间，历经数千年在地貌上刻下了复杂的纹理。

2. The professor's lecture tended to **meander** from one unrelated topic to another, leaving students confused about the main thesis.
   教授的讲座往往从一个无关话题漫无目的地转到另一个，令学生对主旨感到困惑。

3. Visitors often **meander** through the botanical gardens, pausing to admire the rare specimens displayed along the winding paths.
   游客们常常漫步穿过植物园，停下来欣赏蜿蜒小径旁展示的珍稀品种。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "me-and-her" wandering together — "meander" sounds like a leisurely walk with someone, following no particular direction.
把"meander"想成"me and her"一起漫步——听起来像和某人悠闲地走路，没有特定方向。
