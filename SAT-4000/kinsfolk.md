# 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 kinsfolk

🔊 /ˈkɪnzfoʊk/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) One's relatives or family members collectively; people related by blood or marriage
**中文：** n. 亲属；家属

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- relatives（亲戚）
- kin（亲属）
- kinsmen（同族）
- family（家人）
- kindred（亲族）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **gather with kinsfolk / distant kinsfolk / one's kinsfolk / among kinsfolk**

👉 Example: During the holiday season, people travel great distances to reunite with their kinsfolk.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The historian traced the migration patterns of entire **kinsfolk** groups across the European continent during the medieval period.
   这位历史学家追溯了中世纪时期整个亲属群体在欧洲大陆的迁徙模式。

2. In many traditional societies, disputes were resolved not by courts but by councils of respected **kinsfolk** and elders.
   在许多传统社会中，纠纷不是由法庭而是由受尊敬的亲属和长老组成的议事会来解决。

3. The anthropologist observed that obligations to one's **kinsfolk** often take precedence over individual ambitions in communal cultures.
   这位人类学家观察到，在集体文化中，对亲属的义务往往优先于个人抱负。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "kins-folk" as "kin's folk" — the folk (people) who are your kin (family). It literally means your family people.
把"kinsfolk"想成"kin's folk"——属于你的kin（亲族）的folk（人们），字面意思就是你的家族成员。
