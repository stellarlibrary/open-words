# 🏺 artifact

🔊 /ˈɑːrtɪfækt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** An object made by a human being, typically one of cultural or historical interest.

**中文：** 人工制品；手工艺品

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- relic 遗物
- antiquity 古物
- handiwork 手工制品
- remnant 残留物

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Artifact" is commonly used in archaeology and museum contexts to refer to objects that reveal information about past civilizations. In computing, it can also mean a byproduct of a process.

"artifact" 常用于考古和博物馆语境中，指揭示过去文明信息的物品。在计算机领域，也可指某个过程的副产品。

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The museum displayed a rare **artifact** from the ancient Egyptian dynasty last weekend.
   博物馆上周末展出了一件来自古埃及王朝的稀有文物。

2. Researchers carefully excavated each **artifact** to preserve its historical significance for future study.
   研究人员仔细挖掘每件文物，以保存其历史意义供未来研究。

3. The clay **artifact** provided compelling evidence of advanced craftsmanship in the Bronze Age civilization.
   这件陶制文物提供了青铜时代文明拥有高超工艺的有力证据。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "arti-" (art) + "-fact" (made) — something made by art or skill, a man-made object.

将 "artifact" 拆分为 "arti-"（艺术）+ "-fact"（制造）——由艺术或技巧制造的东西，即人工制品。
