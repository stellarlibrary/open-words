# 🏷️ stigma

🔊 /ˈstɪɡmə/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The pistil tip of a flower; disgrace; a stain on reputation; a brand; a distinguishing mark
**中文：** n. 污名，耻辱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disgrace（耻辱）
- shame（羞耻）
- dishonor（不名誉）
- blemish（污点）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **social stigma / carry a stigma / stigma attached to / stigma surrounding**

👉 Example: There is still a significant stigma attached to seeking professional help for mental health issues.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Public health campaigns have worked to reduce the **stigma** associated with mental illness and encourage people to seek treatment.
   公共健康运动致力于减少与精神疾病相关的污名，鼓励人们寻求治疗。

2. The **stigma** of bankruptcy followed the entrepreneur for years, making it difficult to secure new financing from investors.
   破产的污名跟随这位企业家多年，使其难以从投资者那里获得新的融资。

3. Sociologists have documented how the **stigma** surrounding certain occupations can limit social mobility across generations.
   社会学家已经记录了围绕某些职业的污名如何限制跨代社会流动性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "stigma" as "stick + mark" — a stigma is like a mark that sticks to your reputation and will not come off.
把"stigma"想成"stick（粘住）+ mark（标记）"——污名就像粘在你名誉上的标记，怎么也去不掉。
