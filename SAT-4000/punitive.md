# ⚖️ punitive

🔊 /ˈpjuːnɪtɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Inflicting or intended as punishment; serving to penalize or discipline
**中文：** adj. 惩罚性的；刑罚的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disciplinary（纪律性的）
- penal（刑罚的）
- retributive（报应的）
- corrective（矫正的）
- castigatory（惩戒的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **punitive measures / punitive damages / punitive action / punitive sanctions / punitive tariffs**

👉 Example: The court awarded punitive damages to deter the corporation from engaging in similar misconduct.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The regulatory agency imposed **punitive** fines on the corporation for repeatedly violating environmental protection standards over several years.
   监管机构对该公司多年来反复违反环境保护标准的行为处以惩罚性罚款。

2. Critics argued that the **punitive** policies disproportionately affected low-income communities while failing to address the root causes of crime.
   批评者认为惩罚性政策对低收入社区产生了不成比例的影响，却未能解决犯罪的根本原因。

3. The jury awarded **punitive** damages exceeding ten million dollars to send a clear message against corporate negligence and fraud.
   陪审团判处超过一千万美元的惩罚性赔偿，以明确警告企业不得疏忽和欺诈。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "punit-ive" — it contains "punish"; punitive actions are designed to punish wrongdoers and deter future violations.
"punitive"包含"punish（惩罚）"——punitive行为旨在惩罚违规者并遏制未来的违法行为。
