# 🧐 staid

🔊 /steɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Sedate, respectable, and unadventurous; fixed in manner; calm, steady, and conservative in character
**中文：** adj. 认真的，沉着的；固定的；古板的，保守的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sedate（沉着的）
- solemn（庄重的）
- composed（镇定的）
- conservative（保守的）
- dignified（庄严的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **staid demeanor / staid institution / staid appearance / staid atmosphere / staid tradition**

👉 Example: The staid atmosphere of the old library was a welcome contrast to the noise of the city.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's **staid** demeanor in the lecture hall earned him a reputation for being serious and thoroughly reliable.
   这位教授在讲堂上沉着的举止为他赢得了认真可靠的声誉。

2. The once **staid** institution gradually embraced modern technology and more progressive approaches to higher education.
   这所一度保守的学府逐渐接受了现代技术和更为进步的高等教育方法。

3. Despite her **staid** appearance, she possessed a sharp wit and a remarkably creative mind that surprised everyone.
   尽管她外表沉稳，但她拥有敏锐的机智和非凡的创造力，令所有人感到惊讶。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "staid" as "stayed" — someone who has stayed the same for so long that they are set in their ways, calm, and unchanging.
把"staid"想成"stayed"——一个长期保持不变的人，性格固定、沉着且一成不变。
