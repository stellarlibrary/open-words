# 🤢 unsavoury

🔊 /ʌnˈseɪvəri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (British spelling of unsavory) Disagreeable to taste or smell; morally offensive or objectionable; having a bad reputation
**中文：** 难吃的；令人讨厌的；道德上令人反感的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- distasteful（令人反感的）
- disreputable（名声不好的）
- objectionable（令人讨厌的）
- unpalatable（难以下咽的）
- repugnant（令人厌恶的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **unsavoury character / unsavoury reputation / unsavoury business / unsavoury affair**

👉 Example: The politician was widely criticized for his involvement in several unsavoury financial transactions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee launched a formal inquiry into the **unsavoury** allegations surrounding the procurement of government contracts.
   委员会对围绕政府合同采购的令人不齿的指控展开了正式调查。

2. Residents expressed concern about the **unsavoury** individuals who had been frequenting the abandoned warehouse near the school.
   居民对经常出没于学校附近废弃仓库的声名狼藉的人表示担忧。

3. The memoir revealed several **unsavoury** episodes from the author's early career that had previously been kept from public view.
   这本回忆录揭示了作者早期职业生涯中几段此前未公开的令人不快的经历。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "un-savour-y" as "not savoury" — the British spelling of unsavory; something lacking flavor or moral decency that you cannot savour.
把"unsavoury"想成"un（不）+ savoury（美味的）"——unsavory的英式拼写，指缺乏风味或道德体面的事物。
