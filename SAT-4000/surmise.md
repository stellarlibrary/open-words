# 🔍 surmise

🔊 /sərˈmaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To guess or infer something without sufficient evidence. (n.) A guess or conjecture based on incomplete information
**中文：** v. 推测，猜测 n. 推测，猜测

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conjecture（推测）
- speculate（猜测）
- guess（猜想）
- infer（推断）
- suppose（假设）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **surmise that / can only surmise / reasonable to surmise / mere surmise**

👉 Example: From his nervous behavior, she could only surmise that he was hiding something important.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Based on the limited archaeological evidence available, researchers could only **surmise** that the ancient settlement had been abandoned quite abruptly.
   根据有限的考古证据，研究人员只能推测这个古代定居点曾被相当突然地遗弃。

2. Her **surmise** about the company's impending merger proved remarkably accurate when the official announcement was made the following week.
   她关于公司即将合并的推测在下周官方宣布时被证明非常准确。

3. Without access to the classified documents, the journalist was forced to **surmise** the details of the agreement from publicly available sources.
   在无法获取机密文件的情况下，记者不得不从公开来源推测协议的细节。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "surmise" as "sir + mice" — imagine a sir (gentleman) guessing how many mice are hiding in the walls, with no way to know for sure.
把"surmise"想成"sir + mice"——想象一位绅士在猜测墙壁里藏了多少只老鼠，却无从确知。
