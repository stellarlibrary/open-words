# 🔄 displace

🔊 /dɪsˈpleɪs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To move something from its usual or proper place; to take the place of; to force someone to leave their home
**中文：** v. 移置；转移；取代

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- supplant（取代）
- replace（替代）
- relocate（转移）
- dislodge（驱逐）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **displace workers / displace populations / displace a rival / displaced persons / displaced by conflict**

👉 Example: Automation continues to displace workers in manufacturing sectors across the industrialized world.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The devastating flood **displaced** thousands of families, forcing them to seek temporary shelter in neighboring provinces.
   这场毁灭性的洪水使数千个家庭流离失所，迫使他们到邻省寻求临时庇护。

2. Digital streaming platforms have largely **displaced** traditional media formats such as compact discs and physical rentals.
   数字流媒体平台已在很大程度上取代了光盘和实体租赁等传统媒体格式。

3. The construction of the reservoir will **displace** several indigenous communities that have inhabited the region for centuries.
   水库的建设将迫使在该地区居住了数百年的多个原住民社区迁移。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-place" as "dis" (away from) + "place" — to move something away from its place, either physically or by replacement.
把"displace"想成"dis"（远离）+"place"（位置）——将某物从其位置移走，无论是物理移动还是替代。
