# 🐺 devour

🔊 /dɪˈvaʊər/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To eat food or prey hungrily or quickly; to destroy completely; to consume eagerly with the senses or mind
**中文：** v. 狼吞虎咽地吃；破坏，吞没；毁灭

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- consume（消耗）
- gobble（狼吞虎咽）
- engulf（吞没）
- ravage（吞噬）
- demolish（吞食殆尽）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **devour food / devour a book / devour with eyes / be devoured by flames**

👉 Example: The wildfires devoured thousands of acres of forest within a matter of hours.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The hungry wolves **devoured** their prey within minutes, leaving nothing but scattered bones on the frozen ground.
   饥饿的狼群在几分钟内就将猎物吞食殆尽，冰冻的地面上只留下散落的骨头。

2. She **devoured** every book in the library on the subject, driven by an insatiable curiosity about ancient civilizations.
   她如饥似渴地阅读了图书馆里关于该主题的每一本书，对古代文明有着无法满足的好奇心。

3. The massive fire **devoured** the historic cathedral before emergency services could arrive to contain the blaze effectively.
   大火在紧急救援人员赶到有效控制火势之前就已经吞噬了这座历史悠久的大教堂。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "devour" as "de-vour" sounding like "the power" to eat — devour means to eat with such power and speed that nothing remains.
把"devour"想成发音像"the power(力量)"——devour就是以巨大的力量和速度进食，什么都不剩。
