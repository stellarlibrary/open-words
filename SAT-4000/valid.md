# ✅ valid

🔊 /ˈvælɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Well-grounded; sound; legally acceptable; having force or effectiveness
**中文：** adj. 有效的；有根据的；合法的；站得住脚的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- legitimate（合法的）
- sound（合理的）
- well-founded（有根据的）
- cogent（有说服力的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **valid argument / valid point / valid reason / valid passport / valid concern**

👉 Example: The court ruled that the contract was no longer valid due to a procedural error.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher presented a **valid** argument supported by extensive empirical data collected over a five-year longitudinal study.
   研究人员提出了一个有力的论点，该论点得到了为期五年的纵向研究所收集的大量实证数据的支持。

2. Your passport must remain **valid** for at least six months beyond your intended date of departure from the country.
   您的护照有效期必须在您计划离开该国的日期之后至少还有六个月。

3. Critics raised several **valid** concerns about the proposed legislation's potential impact on civil liberties and individual privacy rights.
   批评者对拟议立法可能对公民自由和个人隐私权产生的影响提出了几项合理的担忧。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "valid" as sharing a root with "value" — something valid has real value because it is well-grounded and legally sound.
把"valid"想成和"value"（价值）同根——有效的东西有真正的价值，因为它有根据且合法。
