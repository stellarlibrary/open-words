# ✂️ rescind

🔊 /rɪˈsɪnd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To revoke, cancel, or repeal a law, order, or agreement; to make void
**中文：** v. 解除；废除；撤回；取消

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- revoke（撤销）
- repeal（废除）
- annul（废止）
- abrogate（取消）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rescind a contract / rescind an order / rescind a decision / rescind a policy**

👉 Example: The board voted unanimously to rescind the outdated regulation that had hindered innovation for years.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The university administration decided to **rescind** the admission offer after discovering falsified credentials in the application.
   大学行政部门在发现申请中伪造的资历后决定撤回录取通知。

2. International pressure compelled the government to **rescind** the controversial decree restricting freedom of the press nationwide.
   国际压力迫使政府废除了限制全国新闻自由的争议性法令。

3. The court ruled that the company had no legal authority to **rescind** the contract without providing adequate prior notice.
   法院裁定该公司无权在未提供充分事先通知的情况下解除合同。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rescind" as "re-scissor" — you take scissors and cut the agreement again, rescinding it completely.
把"rescind"想成"re-scissor"——你拿起剪刀再次剪断协议，彻底废除它。
