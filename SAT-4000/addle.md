# 🌀 addle

🔊 /ˈæd.əl/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make confused or muddled; to become rotten (of an egg) (adj.) Confused, muddled; rotten or putrid
**中文：** v. 使混乱，使糊涂；使腐坏 adj. 糊涂的；腐坏的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- confuse（使混乱）
- muddle（使糊涂）
- befuddle（使迷惑）
- bewilder（使困惑）
- scramble（搅乱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **addle one's brain / addle-brained / addle-headed / addled thinking / addled mind**

👉 Example: The sheer volume of contradictory evidence seemed to addle even the most seasoned investigators.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Years of misinformation had **addled** the public's understanding of the fundamental scientific principles involved.
   多年的错误信息使公众对所涉及的基本科学原理的理解变得混乱不清。

2. The philosopher warned that excessive reliance on technology would **addle** the minds of future generations.
   这位哲学家警告说，过度依赖技术会使后代的头脑变得糊涂。

3. His **addled** reasoning during the debate revealed a fundamental misunderstanding of the economic policy.
   他在辩论中混乱的推理暴露了对经济政策的根本性误解。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "addle" as "add-le" — when you ADD too much to your brain, it gets muddLEd. Also, an addled egg has gone bad, just like addled thinking has gone wrong.
把"addle"想成"add-le"——当你往大脑里ADD（加入）太多东西时，它就变得muddLEd（糊涂了）。同样，addled的鸡蛋变质了，就像addled的思维出了问题。
