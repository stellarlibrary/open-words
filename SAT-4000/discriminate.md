# 🔍 discriminate

🔊 /dɪˈskrɪmɪˌneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To recognize a distinction or difference between things; to treat a person or group unfairly based on prejudice
**中文：** v. 区别，辨别；歧视

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- distinguish（区分）
- differentiate（辨别）
- segregate（隔离）
- prejudice（偏见对待）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **discriminate against / discriminate between / discriminate in favor of**

👉 Example: Federal law prohibits employers from discriminating against candidates on the basis of age or gender.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. A trained sommelier can **discriminate** between subtle differences in flavor and aroma that most casual wine drinkers would never notice.
   受过训练的侍酒师能够辨别出大多数普通饮酒者永远不会注意到的风味和香气的细微差别。

2. The new legislation was designed to prevent landlords from **discriminating** against prospective tenants based on their ethnic background or religion.
   这项新立法旨在防止房东基于潜在租户的种族背景或宗教信仰而对其进行歧视。

3. Scientists must **discriminate** carefully between correlation and causation when interpreting data from observational studies in public health research.
   科学家在解读公共卫生研究中观察性研究的数据时，必须仔细区分相关性和因果关系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-crimin-ate" — "crimin" looks like "crime," and unfair discrimination is almost like committing a crime against equality.
把"discriminate"中的"crimin"联想到"crime"（犯罪）——不公正的歧视几乎就像是对平等的犯罪。
