# 🖐️ tangible

🔊 /ˈtændʒɪbəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Perceptible by touch; clear and definite; real and concrete. (n.) A tangible thing; tangible assets
**中文：** adj. 确实的，触摸得到的，具体的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- palpable（可触知的）
- concrete（具体的）
- substantial（实质的）
- perceptible（可感知的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tangible evidence / tangible results / tangible benefits / tangible assets**

👉 Example: The new policy has produced tangible improvements in student performance across the district.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The research team demanded **tangible** evidence before accepting any conclusions drawn from the preliminary experimental data.
   研究团队要求提供确实的证据，然后才接受从初步实验数据中得出的任何结论。

2. After years of theoretical debate, the initiative finally yielded **tangible** results that stakeholders could measure objectively.
   经过多年的理论争论，该计划终于产生了利益相关者可以客观衡量的具体成果。

3. The economic sanctions had no **tangible** effect on the regime's policies despite being enforced for over a decade.
   尽管经济制裁已执行了十多年，但对该政权的政策没有产生切实的影响。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tang-ible" — something you can "tang" (touch) is tangible. If you can hold it in your hands, it is real and concrete.
把"tangible"想成"tang-ible"——能"触碰(tang)"到的东西就是tangible的。能握在手中的东西就是真实而具体的。
