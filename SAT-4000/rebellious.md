# 🔥 rebellious

🔊 /rɪˈbɛljəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Showing a desire to resist authority, control, or convention; defiant and difficult to manage
**中文：** adj. 谋反的，反叛的；反抗的；难控制的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- defiant（挑衅的）
- disobedient（不服从的）
- insubordinate（不顺从的）
- unruly（难驾驭的）
- mutinous（反叛的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rebellious teenager / rebellious spirit / rebellious behavior / rebellious streak**

👉 Example: The rebellious students organized a protest against the administration's restrictive new policies.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The author's **rebellious** attitude toward literary conventions made her one of the most innovative writers of her generation.
   这位作家对文学传统的反叛态度使她成为同代人中最具创新精神的作家之一。

2. Throughout history, **rebellious** movements have often served as catalysts for significant social and political transformation.
   纵观历史，反抗运动往往是重大社会和政治变革的催化剂。

3. The manager struggled to supervise the **rebellious** employee who consistently refused to follow established workplace protocols.
   经理很难管理这位叛逆的员工，因为他一贯拒绝遵守既定的工作场所规范。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rebel-lious" as "full of rebel" — a rebellious person has the spirit of a rebel, always pushing back against authority.
把"rebellious"想成"充满叛逆的"——一个rebellious的人拥有反叛者的精神，总是抵抗权威。
