# ⭐ significant

🔊 /sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Important or large enough to have an effect; having a particular meaning; noteworthy. (n.) A symbol; something meaningful
**中文：** adj. 有意义的，意味深长的；重要的，重大的；非偶然的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- important（重要的）
- meaningful（有意义的）
- notable（显著的）
- substantial（实质性的）
- consequential（重大的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **significant impact / significant difference / statistically significant / significant progress / significant other**

👉 Example: The study revealed a statistically significant correlation between sleep deprivation and cognitive decline.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researchers discovered a **significant** increase in biodiversity after the conservation program had been implemented for five years.
   研究人员发现，保护计划实施五年后，生物多样性出现了显著增长。

2. The diplomat's **significant** gesture of reconciliation paved the way for renewed negotiations between the two nations.
   这位外交官意味深长的和解姿态为两国之间重新谈判铺平了道路。

3. A **significant** portion of the budget was allocated to infrastructure development in underserved rural communities.
   预算的很大一部分被分配给了服务不足的农村社区的基础设施建设。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "significant" as "sign-ificant" — something that serves as a sign or signal must be important and meaningful enough to notice.
把"significant"想成"sign（标志）+ ificant"——能作为标志或信号的事物一定足够重要和有意义，值得注意。
