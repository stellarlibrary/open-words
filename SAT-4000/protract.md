# ⏳ protract

🔊 /proʊˈtrækt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To prolong or extend in time or duration; to draw out or lengthen beyond the usual scope
**中文：** v. 延长；拖延；伸展

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- prolong（延长）
- extend（延伸）
- lengthen（加长）
- drag out（拖延）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **protract a negotiation / protracted conflict / protracted debate / protracted illness**

👉 Example: The lawyers attempted to protract the proceedings in order to exhaust their opponent's resources.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The border dispute **protracted** negotiations between the two countries for over a decade with no resolution in sight.
   边界争端使两国之间的谈判拖延了十多年，看不到解决的前景。

2. Critics argued that the administration deliberately sought to **protract** the inquiry to avoid releasing damaging information before elections.
   批评者认为政府故意拖延调查，以避免在选举前公布不利信息。

3. The **protracted** drought severely diminished agricultural output, forcing thousands of farming families to seek alternative livelihoods elsewhere.
   长期的干旱严重减少了农业产出，迫使数千个农业家庭到其他地方寻找替代生计。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pro-tract" as "pulling the track forward" — stretching the track ahead makes the journey longer, just as protracting extends time.
把"protract"想成"pro（向前）+ tract（拉）"——向前拉长轨道使旅程更长，正如protract表示延长时间。
