# 🔍 cavil

🔊 /ˈkævɪl/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To raise trivial or unnecessary objections; to find fault without good reason. (n.) A petty or trivial objection
**中文：** v. 吹毛求疵，挑剔，无端指责 n. 吹毛求疵，小题大做的反对意见

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- quibble（吹毛求疵）
- nitpick（挑剔）
- carp（挑刺）
- gripe（抱怨）
- censure（指责）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cavil at / cavil about / raise a cavil / petty cavil**

👉 Example: The critic would cavil at every minor detail rather than appreciate the work as a whole.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The opposition party continued to **cavil** at the proposed legislation, focusing on trivial wording rather than substantive policy issues.
   反对党继续对拟议的立法吹毛求疵，专注于琐碎的措辞而非实质性的政策问题。

2. Rather than offering constructive feedback, the reviewer chose to **cavil** about inconsequential formatting errors throughout the manuscript.
   评审者没有提供建设性反馈，而是选择对手稿中无关紧要的格式错误挑剔不休。

3. His persistent **cavils** about the seating arrangement delayed the entire conference by nearly an hour without justification.
   他对座位安排持续不断的吹毛求疵毫无道理地将整个会议推迟了近一个小时。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cavil" as a "cave" + "ill" — someone who is ill-tempered, hiding in a cave, and complaining about every little thing.
把"cavil"想成"cave（洞穴）"+"ill（不舒服）"——一个脾气暴躁的人躲在洞穴里，对每件小事都抱怨不停。
