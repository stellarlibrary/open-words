# 🗿 colossus

🔊 /kəˈlɒs.əs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A statue of enormous size; a person or thing of immense power, importance, or stature; something extraordinarily large.

**中文：** 巨像；巨人；巨大的东西。指巨大的雕像，也指具有巨大权力、重要性或地位的人或事物。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- giant（巨人）
- titan（泰坦）
- behemoth（庞然大物）
- leviathan（巨型事物）
- monument（纪念碑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Collocation:** "a colossus of / intellectual colossus / industrial colossus" — used figuratively to describe someone or something of extraordinary magnitude.

**Example:** Shakespeare remains a colossus of English literature whose influence extends across centuries.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The Colossus of Rhodes was an ancient **colossus** that stood at the harbor entrance and symbolized the city's remarkable prosperity.
   罗德岛巨像是一座古代**巨像**，矗立在港口入口处，象征着这座城市非凡的繁荣。

2. The multinational corporation had grown into a **colossus** that dominated global markets and influenced economic policy worldwide.
   这家跨国公司已发展成为一个**巨头**，主导着全球市场并影响着世界各地的经济政策。

3. Many historians regard Napoleon as a **colossus** who reshaped the political and legal landscape of an entire continent.
   许多历史学家将拿破仑视为一位**巨人**，他重塑了整个欧洲大陆的政治和法律格局。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

**English:** Think of the "Colossus of Rhodes" — one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, a gigantic bronze statue. Anything called a "colossus" is similarly enormous.

**中文：** 想想"罗德岛巨像"——古代世界七大奇迹之一，一座巨大的青铜雕像。任何被称为"colossus"的事物都同样巨大无比。
