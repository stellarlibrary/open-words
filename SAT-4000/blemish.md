# 🔍 blemish

🔊 /ˈblɛmɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A flaw, defect, or imperfection that mars appearance or quality. (v.) To spoil, stain, or impair; to damage the reputation or quality of something.
**中文：** n. 瑕疵；污点；缺点 v. 弄脏，玷污，损害

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flaw（瑕疵）
- defect（缺陷）
- stain（污点）
- tarnish（玷污）
- imperfection（不完美）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **blemish on one's record / blemish one's reputation / without blemish / a minor blemish**

👉 Example: A single act of dishonesty can permanently blemish an otherwise exemplary career.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The minor scandal left an indelible **blemish** on the senator's previously unblemished record of public service.
   这一小丑闻给这位参议员此前无可挑剔的公共服务记录留下了不可磨灭的**污点**。

2. The antique dealer carefully examined the porcelain vase for any crack or **blemish** that might diminish its value.
   古董商仔细检查了瓷花瓶上是否有可能降低其价值的裂纹或**瑕疵**。

3. Fraudulent data in the research paper served to **blemish** the entire department's scholarly credibility and standing.
   研究论文中的伪造数据**损害**了整个部门的学术信誉和地位。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "blemish" as "blame + ish" — something blame-ish, a mark or flaw that attracts blame or criticism.
把"blemish"想成"blame + ish"——像受责备一样的东西，一个招致指责或批评的瑕疵或污点。
