# 🌊 onrush

🔊 /ˈɒnrʌʃ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A surging rush forward; a strong onward movement or flow
**中文：** n. 猛冲；突进；袭击；急流

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- surge（汹涌）
- torrent（洪流）
- onslaught（猛攻）
- flood（涌流）
- stampede（蜂拥）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **onrush of water / onrush of emotion / onrush of events / onrush of progress**

👉 Example: The onrush of technological innovation has fundamentally transformed modern communication and daily life.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden **onrush** of floodwater overwhelmed the aging dam and devastated several communities located downstream.
   突如其来的洪水猛冲摧毁了老化的大坝，并重创了下游几个社区。

2. Historians often attribute the decline of traditional industries to the relentless **onrush** of mechanization during the nineteenth century.
   历史学家通常将传统工业的衰落归因于十九世纪机械化的猛烈推进。

3. She felt an overwhelming **onrush** of grief when she received the unexpected news about her beloved mentor's passing.
   当她收到关于敬爱的导师去世的意外消息时，一股悲痛的浪潮向她袭来。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "on + rush" — a rush that keeps coming ON, like a wave that charges forward without stopping.
把"onrush"想成"on + rush"——一股不断涌来（on）的冲击（rush），就像一波接一波永不停歇的浪潮。
