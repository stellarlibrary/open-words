# 🏛️ collective

🔊 /kəˈlɛktɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Done by or belonging to all members of a group; shared or joint. (n.) A cooperative enterprise or organization owned and operated by its members
**中文：** adj. 集体的；共同的 n. 集体；集团；集合体

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- communal（共有的）
- shared（共同的）
- joint（联合的）
- cooperative（合作社）
- aggregate（集合的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **collective effort / collective bargaining / collective memory / collective responsibility**

👉 Example: The success of the project was the result of a collective effort by every member of the team.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The union representatives entered into **collective** bargaining negotiations to secure better wages and working conditions for employees.
   工会代表参加了集体谈判，以为员工争取更好的工资和工作条件。

2. The community formed an agricultural **collective** to share resources and distribute profits equitably among all participating farmers.
   社区组建了一个农业集体，以共享资源并在所有参与农民之间公平分配利润。

3. Through **collective** action, the citizens successfully pressured the local government to abandon the controversial development plan.
   通过集体行动，市民们成功迫使当地政府放弃了这项有争议的开发计划。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "collect-ive" — when you "collect" people together, you form a collective group working as one unit.
把"collective"想成"collect-ive"——当你把人们"收集"到一起时，就形成了一个作为整体运作的集体。
