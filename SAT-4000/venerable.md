# 🏛️ venerable

🔊 /ˈvenərəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Accorded great respect because of age, wisdom, or character; impressive by reason of age or historic associations
**中文：** adj. 庄严的，值得尊敬的；珍贵的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- revered（受尊崇的）
- esteemed（受尊敬的）
- respected（受尊重的）
- august（威严的）
- distinguished（杰出的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **venerable institution / venerable tradition / venerable elder / venerable scholar / venerable leader**

👉 Example: The venerable institution has been educating students for over three centuries and remains a pillar of academic excellence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **venerable** professor, who had taught at the university for five decades, received a lifetime achievement award at the annual ceremony.
   这位德高望重的教授在大学执教五十年，在年度典礼上获得了终身成就奖。

2. The city council voted unanimously to preserve the **venerable** cathedral, recognizing its architectural significance and cultural heritage value.
   市议会一致投票决定保护这座庄严的大教堂，承认其建筑意义和文化遗产价值。

3. Scholars continue to consult the **venerable** texts of classical philosophy as foundational references in contemporary ethical discourse.
   学者们在当代伦理学讨论中继续将古典哲学的经典文本作为基础参考。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "venerable" as "vener-" (from Latin "venerari," to worship) + "-able" — someone or something worthy of being worshipped or deeply respected.
把"venerable"想成"vener-"（来自拉丁语"venerari"，意为崇拜）加上"-able"——值得崇拜或深深尊敬的人或事物。
