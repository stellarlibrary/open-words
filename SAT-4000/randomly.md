# 🎲 randomly

🔊 /ˈrændəmli/<br>
🏷️ *adverb | 副词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adv.) In a manner lacking any definite plan, order, or purpose; occurring by chance without a predictable pattern
**中文：** adv. 随便地，任意地；无目的地；随机地

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- arbitrarily（任意地）
- haphazardly（随意地）
- indiscriminately（不加选择地）
- unsystematically（无系统地）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **randomly selected / randomly assigned / randomly distributed / randomly generated**

👉 Example: Participants in the clinical trial were randomly assigned to either the treatment group or the control group.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The subjects were **randomly** selected from a pool of five thousand volunteers to ensure the study's results remained statistically unbiased.
   受试者从五千名志愿者中随机挑选，以确保研究结果在统计上保持无偏差。

2. Documents had been **randomly** scattered across the office floor, suggesting that someone had searched the premises in considerable haste.
   文件被随意散落在办公室地板上，表明有人曾匆忙搜查过该场所。

3. The algorithm **randomly** generates unique encryption keys for each user session, significantly enhancing the overall security of the system.
   该算法为每个用户会话随机生成唯一的加密密钥，显著增强了系统的整体安全性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "randomly" as "random + ly" — like rolling dice, outcomes happen by chance with no discernible order or pattern.
把"randomly"想成"random + ly"——就像掷骰子一样，结果随机发生，没有可辨别的顺序或规律。
