# 📐 side

🔊 /saɪd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb, adjective | 名词, 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An aspect; a lateral surface; an edge or border. (v.) To support or align with a particular party. (adj.) Lateral, on the side
**中文：** n. 边，立体的面

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- aspect（方面）
- flank（侧面）
- edge（边缘）
- surface（表面）
- faction（派别）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **side with someone / on the side of / side effect / take sides / side by side**

👉 Example: The committee members were reluctant to side with either faction during the heated debate.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The architect carefully examined every **side** of the geometric structure to ensure dimensional accuracy before construction.
   建筑师在施工前仔细检查了几何结构的每一个面，以确保尺寸精确。

2. Several prominent senators chose to **side** with the opposition on the controversial environmental regulation proposal.
   几位著名参议员在有争议的环境法规提案上选择支持反对派。

3. The report presented both **sides** of the argument so that readers could form their own informed opinions.
   该报告呈现了争论的双方观点，以便读者形成自己明智的判断。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "side" as the edge you stand on — every object has sides, and every argument has sides; you must choose which side to stand on.
把"side"想成你所站的那一边——每个物体都有边，每个争论都有立场；你必须选择站在哪一边。
