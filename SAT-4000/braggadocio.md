# 🎭 braggadocio

🔊 /ˌbræɡəˈdoʊʃiˌoʊ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Boastful or arrogant behavior; empty bragging; also, a person given to excessive boasting
**中文：** 自夸；吹牛；吹牛大王

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bluster（虚张声势）
- boastfulness（吹嘘）
- bombast（夸大之词）
- swagger（大话）
- bravado（虚张声势）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **empty braggadocio / mere braggadocio / full of braggadocio / braggadocio and bluster**

👉 Example: His speech was filled with braggadocio, yet offered no concrete evidence to support his extravagant claims.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The candidate's **braggadocio** during the debate alienated moderate voters who preferred thoughtful discourse over empty boasting.
   这位候选人在辩论中的自吹自擂疏远了那些更喜欢深思熟虑讨论而非空洞吹嘘的温和派选民。

2. Beneath his outward **braggadocio**, colleagues detected a deep insecurity that he desperately sought to conceal from others.
   在他外表的虚张声势之下，同事们察觉到他拼命试图掩饰的深层不安全感。

3. Literary critics dismissed the memoir as little more than **braggadocio**, lacking the introspection essential to genuine autobiography.
   文学评论家认为这本回忆录不过是自吹自擂，缺乏真正自传所必需的自省精神。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bragg-adocio" as "brag" with an Italian-sounding ending — like a theatrical, exaggerated version of bragging.
把"braggadocio"想成"brag（吹牛）"加上一个意大利语式的词尾——就像吹牛的夸张戏剧化版本。
