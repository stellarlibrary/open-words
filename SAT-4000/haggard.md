# 😩 haggard

🔊 /ˈhæɡərd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Looking exhausted, gaunt, and worn out, especially from fatigue, worry, or suffering; wild and untamed in appearance
**中文：** adj. 憔悴的；形容枯槁的；野性的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gaunt（憔悴的）
- drawn（面容消瘦的）
- emaciated（消瘦的）
- wan（苍白的）
- careworn（忧心忡忡的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **haggard face / haggard appearance / haggard expression / look haggard / haggard eyes**

👉 Example: After weeks of sleepless nights caring for her ailing mother, she appeared visibly haggard and exhausted.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The refugees arrived at the border crossing looking **haggard** and malnourished after enduring weeks of arduous travel on foot.
   难民们在经历了数周艰苦的徒步旅行后，抵达边境口岸时看起来憔悴不堪、营养不良。

2. The defense attorney's **haggard** appearance after the grueling three-month trial revealed the immense toll the case had taken.
   经过长达三个月的艰苦审判后，辩护律师憔悴的外表表明这个案件给他造成了巨大的消耗。

3. Historical accounts describe the soldiers as **haggard** and demoralized following the prolonged siege that lasted through the brutal winter.
   历史记载描述了这些士兵在经历了整个严冬的漫长围困后，变得面容枯槁、士气低落。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "haggard" as "hag + guard" — imagine a guard who has been on duty so long he looks like a haggard old hag, worn and exhausted.
把"haggard"想成"hag（老巫婆）+ guard（守卫）"——想象一个站岗太久的守卫，看起来像个憔悴的老巫婆，疲惫不堪。
