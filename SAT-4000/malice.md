# 🖤 malice

🔊 /ˈmælɪs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The desire to cause harm or suffering to another person; deliberate ill will or spite
**中文：** n. 恶意；怨恨；预谋

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- spite（恶意）
- animosity（敌意）
- ill will（恶意）
- hostility（敌意）
- vindictiveness（报复心）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bear malice / with malice / malice aforethought / out of malice / malice toward someone**

👉 Example: The prosecution argued that the defendant had acted with malice aforethought in planning the crime.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court determined that the defamatory article had been published with actual **malice** and a deliberate intent to ruin reputations.
   法院认定这篇诽谤性文章是带着实际恶意和蓄意毁坏名誉的目的发表的。

2. She bore no **malice** toward her former colleagues, despite the unfair treatment she had endured during her tenure.
   尽管在任期间遭受了不公正的待遇，她对前同事们并不怀有恶意。

3. The historian argued that the policy was driven not by **malice** but by profound ignorance of its devastating consequences.
   这位历史学家认为，这项政策并非出于恶意，而是出于对其毁灭性后果的严重无知。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "malice" as "mal-ice" — a heart as cold as ice with bad ("mal") intentions, wanting to harm others.
把"malice"想成"mal-ice"——一颗像冰一样冷的心怀着坏的（"mal"）意图，想要伤害别人。
