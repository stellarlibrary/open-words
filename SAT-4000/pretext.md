# 🎭 pretext

🔊 /ˈpriːtɛkst/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A reason given to justify an action that is not the real reason; a false excuse or pretense
**中文：** n. 借口；托辞；幌子

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- excuse（借口）
- guise（幌子）
- pretense（假装）
- subterfuge（托辞）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **under the pretext of / on the pretext that / use as a pretext / a mere pretext**

👉 Example: He visited her office under the pretext of delivering documents, but his real intention was to gather information.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government used the threat of national security as a **pretext** for implementing sweeping surveillance measures on its own citizens.
   政府以国家安全威胁为借口，对本国公民实施了大范围的监控措施。

2. She called the meeting under the **pretext** of discussing quarterly budgets, but her true aim was to address ongoing personnel conflicts.
   她以讨论季度预算为幌子召开会议，但真正目的是解决持续存在的人事冲突。

3. Historians argue that the border dispute served merely as a **pretext** for a military intervention that had been planned months in advance.
   历史学家认为，边境争端不过是数月前就已策划好的军事干预行动的一个借口。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-text" as the "text that comes before" — the cover story you write before revealing the real story underneath.
把"pretext"想成"pre-text"——在揭示真正故事之前写的"前文"，即掩盖真实意图的说辞。
