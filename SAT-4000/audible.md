# 👂 audible

🔊 /ˈɔːdɪbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** Able to be heard; loud enough or clear enough to be perceived by the ear.

**中文：** 听得见的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- perceptible（可感知的）
- discernible（可辨别的）
- detectable（可察觉的）
- hearable（可听到的）
- distinct（清晰的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Audible" is the opposite of "inaudible." It commonly modifies nouns like "sound," "sigh," "whisper," or "gasp." The phrase "barely audible" is frequently used to describe something at the edge of hearing.

"audible" 是 "inaudible" 的反义词。常修饰 sound、sigh、whisper、gasp 等名词。短语 "barely audible"（勉强听得见）使用频率很高。

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's voice was barely **audible** above the noise of construction outside.
   教授的声音在外面施工噪音中几乎**听不到**。

2. She let out an **audible** sigh of relief when the final exam results were announced.
   当期末考试成绩公布时，她发出了一声**听得见的**如释重负的叹息。

3. The defendant's response was so quiet that the judge asked for an **audible** reply.
   被告的回答太小声了，法官要求他给出一个**听得见的**答复。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Audible" contains the Latin root "aud-" meaning "to hear" (as in "audio" and "audience"). If something is audible, your "audio system" — your ears — can pick it up.

"audible" 包含拉丁词根 "aud-"，意为"听"（如 audio、audience）。如果某物是 audible 的，你的"音频系统"——耳朵——就能接收到它。
