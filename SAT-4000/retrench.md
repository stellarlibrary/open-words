# ✂️ retrench

🔊 /rɪˈtrɛntʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To cut down or reduce expenses; to delete or remove; to economize strictly
**中文：** v. 删除；减少；紧缩开支；节省；缩减

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- curtail（削减）
- economize（节省）
- reduce（减少）
- downsize（缩减）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **retrench spending / retrench costs / retrench operations / retrench staff**

👉 Example: The corporation was forced to retrench its workforce after a sharp decline in quarterly revenue.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Faced with mounting debt, the university administration decided to **retrench** several academic programs and support services.
   面对不断增加的债务，大学管理层决定削减多个学术项目和支持服务。

2. The government announced plans to **retrench** public spending in order to address the growing national budget deficit.
   政府宣布计划紧缩公共支出，以应对日益增长的国家预算赤字。

3. Many corporations chose to **retrench** their overseas operations during the prolonged period of global economic uncertainty.
   在全球经济长期不确定的时期，许多企业选择缩减其海外业务。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-trench" as digging back into the trenches — retreating and cutting back to a more defensive, economical position.
把"retrench"想成"重新挖战壕"——退回战壕，削减开支，回到更保守、更节省的状态。
