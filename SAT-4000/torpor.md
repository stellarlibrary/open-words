# 😴 torpor

🔊 /ˈtɔːrpər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A state of physical or mental inactivity; lethargy; a condition of dormancy or reduced metabolic activity in animals
**中文：** 不活泼；麻木；懒散；迟钝；蛰伏状态

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- lethargy（昏睡）
- sluggishness（迟缓）
- dormancy（休眠）
- stupor（恍惚）
- apathy（冷漠）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fall into torpor / state of torpor / shake off torpor / mental torpor / winter torpor**

👉 Example: The prolonged heatwave induced a kind of torpor among the city's residents.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The nation seemed to sink into a collective **torpor** following the economic downturn, with public engagement declining sharply.
   经济衰退后，整个国家似乎陷入了集体性的麻木状态，公众参与度急剧下降。

2. Hummingbirds enter a state of **torpor** at night to conserve energy when ambient temperatures drop below a critical threshold.
   当环境温度降至临界值以下时，蜂鸟会在夜间进入蛰伏状态以节省能量。

3. The new director was determined to shake the organization out of its bureaucratic **torpor** and restore a culture of innovation.
   新任主管决心将组织从官僚式的懒散中唤醒，恢复创新文化。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "torpor" as "tor-POOR" — when you feel poor in energy, you are in a state of torpor, unable to move or think actively.
想想"torpor"读起来像"tor-POOR"——当你精力"贫乏"时，你就处于torpor状态，无法积极地行动或思考。
