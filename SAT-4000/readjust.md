# 🔄 readjust

🔊 /ˌriːəˈdʒʌst/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To adjust or adapt again to changed circumstances; to alter or correct something that was previously set or arranged
**中文：** v. 再调整；重新适应；重新校准

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- recalibrate（重新校准）
- realign（重新调整）
- modify（修改）
- adapt（适应）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **readjust to / readjust expectations / readjust priorities / readjust the settings**

👉 Example: After returning from abroad, she needed time to readjust to the pace of life in her hometown.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Employees were given several weeks to **readjust** to the new corporate structure following the unexpected merger with a rival firm.
   在与竞争公司意外合并后，员工们有数周时间来重新适应新的公司架构。

2. The central bank decided to **readjust** interest rates in response to the rapidly shifting conditions in the global financial market.
   中央银行决定重新调整利率，以应对全球金融市场迅速变化的形势。

3. Veterans often face considerable challenges when they attempt to **readjust** to civilian life after prolonged periods of military service.
   退伍军人在长期服役后往往面临重新适应平民生活的巨大挑战。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-adjust" as "adjust again" — the prefix "re-" means to do it once more, so readjust means to fine-tune something a second time.
把"readjust"拆成"re（再次）+ adjust（调整）"——前缀"re-"表示再做一次，所以readjust就是再次调整。
