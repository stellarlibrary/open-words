# 🍷 bacchanalia

🔊 /ˌbækəˈneɪliə/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A wild and drunken celebration or revelry; originally referring to the ancient Roman festival honoring Bacchus, the god of wine.

**中文：** 酒神节；大酒宴；狂欢作乐。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- revelry（狂欢）
- orgy（狂欢聚会）
- carousal（痛饮狂欢）
- debauchery（放荡）
- merrymaking（欢宴）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Bacchanalia" often carries a negative connotation, implying excessive and unrestrained indulgence. When capitalized, it specifically refers to the ancient Roman festivals. In modern usage, it describes any wild, uncontrolled party or celebration.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. What began as a dignified reception gradually descended into a full-blown **bacchanalia** lasting until dawn.
   （原本庄重的招待会逐渐演变成一场持续到黎明的彻底**狂欢**。）

2. The historian described the ancient Roman **Bacchanalia** as ceremonies that often alarmed the conservative members of the Senate.
   （历史学家将古罗马的**酒神节**描述为经常令参议院保守成员感到震惊的仪式。）

3. Critics condemned the lavish festival as nothing more than a **bacchanalia** of wasteful spending and excess.
   （批评人士谴责这场奢华的节日不过是一场浪费和过度消费的**狂欢**。）

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Bacchanalia" comes from "Bacchus," the Roman god of wine. Picture Bacchus hosting an enormous, wild wine party — that is a bacchanalia.

"Bacchanalia"源自"Bacchus（巴克斯）"，罗马酒神。想象巴克斯举办一场盛大而疯狂的葡萄酒派对——那就是"酒神狂欢节"。
