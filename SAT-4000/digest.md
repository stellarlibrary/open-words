# 📚 digest

🔊 /daɪˈdʒɛst (v.), ˈdaɪdʒɛst (n.)/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To break down food in the body; to absorb and understand information. (n.) A compilation or summary of written material
**中文：** v. 消化；吸收 n. 文摘，摘要

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- absorb（吸收）
- assimilate（消化理解）
- summary（摘要）
- compendium（概要）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **digest information / digest food / a digest of / easy to digest / hard to digest**

👉 Example: Students need sufficient time to digest complex theoretical material before applying it in practice.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. It took the committee several weeks to fully **digest** the implications of the comprehensive regulatory reform proposal.
   委员会花了几周时间才完全消化这项全面监管改革提案的影响。

2. The editorial team published a monthly **digest** of the most significant scientific breakthroughs reported in leading academic journals.
   编辑团队出版了一份月度文摘，汇集了顶级学术期刊中报道的最重要科学突破。

3. Some readers find it difficult to **digest** dense philosophical texts without supplementary commentary and guided discussion sessions.
   一些读者发现，如果没有补充评论和引导讨论，很难消化晦涩的哲学文本。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "digest" as your stomach and brain both breaking things down — food is digested physically, ideas are digested mentally.
把"digest"想成你的胃和大脑都在分解东西——食物在身体里被消化，思想在头脑中被消化。
