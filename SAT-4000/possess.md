# 🔑 possess

🔊 /pəˈzɛs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To have or own something; to have a particular quality or feature; to dominate or control someone's mind
**中文：** v. 占有，拥有；具备；控制；使掌握；迷住

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- own（拥有）
- hold（持有）
- retain（保有）
- command（掌控）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **possess knowledge / possess the ability / possess qualities / legally possess / what possessed you**

👉 Example: To succeed in this field, one must possess both technical expertise and strong interpersonal skills.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The museum is fortunate to **possess** one of the finest collections of Renaissance paintings in the entire Western Hemisphere.
   这家博物馆有幸拥有整个西半球最优秀的文艺复兴时期绘画收藏之一。

2. Successful leaders typically **possess** a rare combination of empathy, decisiveness, and the capacity to inspire collective action.
   成功的领导者通常具备同理心、果断力以及激发集体行动能力的稀有组合。

3. Investigators determined that the suspect did not legally **possess** the classified documents found during the search of his residence.
   调查人员确定嫌疑人并不合法持有在搜查其住所时发现的机密文件。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "possess" as "po-SESS" — to sit (sess, from Latin "sedere") upon something is to possess it, like a king sitting on his throne.
把"possess"想成"po-SESS"——坐在（sess，源自拉丁语"sedere"）某物之上就是占有它，就像国王坐在他的王座上。
