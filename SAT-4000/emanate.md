# ✨ emanate

🔊 /ˈɛməneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To issue or spread out from a source; to emit or radiate something such as light, heat, or a quality
**中文：** v. 放射；发散；流出，发出

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- radiate（辐射）
- emit（发出）
- exude（散发）
- originate（源自）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **emanate from a source / emanate light / emanate warmth / emanate confidence**

👉 Example: A strange odor seemed to emanate from the chemistry laboratory down the corridor.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. A warm golden light **emanated** from the ancient lantern, casting intricate shadows across the walls of the medieval chamber.
   一道温暖的金色光芒从古老的灯笼中散发出来，在中世纪房间的墙壁上投射出复杂的影子。

2. The new regulations **emanated** from the federal agency's growing concern over environmental degradation in coastal ecosystems.
   新法规源于联邦机构对沿海生态系统环境退化日益加深的担忧。

3. An unmistakable aura of authority **emanated** from the seasoned diplomat as she addressed the assembly of world leaders.
   当这位经验丰富的外交官向各国领导人发表讲话时，她身上散发出一种不容置疑的权威气质。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "emanate" as "E-MAN-ate" — energy from a man radiates outward. Something that emanates flows out from its source.
把"emanate"想成"E-MAN-ate"——能量从一个人身上向外辐射。散发就是从源头向外流出。
