# 🌊 mainstream

🔊 /ˈmeɪnˌstriːm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The prevailing current of thought, influence, or activity; the ideas, attitudes, or activities regarded as conventional or accepted by the majority.
**中文：** n. 主流；主流思想；主流趋势

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- convention（惯例）
- norm（规范）
- majority（多数派）
- establishment（主流体制）
- orthodoxy（正统观念）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mainstream media / mainstream culture / enter the mainstream / mainstream opinion / outside the mainstream**

👉 Example: The band's unique sound eventually entered the mainstream after years of performing in small venues.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. What was once considered an alternative lifestyle has gradually become part of the cultural **mainstream** in many societies.
   曾经被视为另类的生活方式已逐渐成为许多社会文化主流的一部分。

2. The documentary challenged **mainstream** assumptions about nutrition and prompted widespread public debate among health experts.
   这部纪录片挑战了关于营养的主流观念，并在健康专家中引发了广泛的公众讨论。

3. Many innovative technologies begin on the margins before eventually being adopted by the **mainstream** consumer market.
   许多创新技术始于边缘领域，最终才被主流消费市场所采用。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "main" (principal) + "stream" (flow) — the mainstream is the "main flow" of a river where most of the water goes, just as mainstream ideas are what most people follow.
把"mainstream"想成"main"（主要的）+"stream"（溪流）——主流就是河流中水量最大的"主要水流"，正如主流思想是大多数人所追随的。
