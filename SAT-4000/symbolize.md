# 🔣 symbolize

🔊 /ˈsɪmbəlaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To represent by a symbol; to serve as a symbol of
**中文：** v. 象征，作为…的象征

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- represent（代表）
- signify（表示）
- denote（象征）
- embody（体现）
- typify（象征）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **symbolize freedom / symbolize hope / symbolize unity / symbolize the struggle / commonly symbolized by**

👉 Example: The dove has long been used to symbolize peace and reconciliation across many cultures.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The newly erected monument was designed to **symbolize** the nation's enduring commitment to democratic principles and individual liberty.
   新建的纪念碑旨在象征国家对民主原则和个人自由的持久承诺。

2. In many literary traditions, the changing of seasons is employed to **symbolize** the inevitable passage of time and human mortality.
   在许多文学传统中，季节的更替被用来象征时间的不可避免的流逝和人类的有限生命。

3. The artist used contrasting colors and bold geometric forms to **symbolize** the tension between tradition and modernity in contemporary society.
   艺术家使用对比色和大胆的几何形状来象征当代社会中传统与现代之间的紧张关系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "symbol + ize" — to "make into a symbol." When something symbolizes an idea, it becomes the visual shorthand for that concept.
想想"symbol（符号）+ ize（使…化）"——"使成为符号"。当某物象征一个概念时，它就成为该概念的视觉速记。
