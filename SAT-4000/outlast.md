# ⏳ outlast

🔊 /ˌaʊtˈlæst/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To last longer than; to survive or endure beyond the duration of something or someone
**中文：** v. 比…长久；比…活得长；从…中逃生

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- outlive（比…活得长）
- survive（幸存）
- endure（持久）
- withstand（经受住）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **outlast the competition / outlast a rival / outlast expectations / outlast a storm**

👉 Example: The ancient castle has outlasted centuries of warfare, natural disasters, and political upheaval.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The company's commitment to quality allowed it to **outlast** numerous competitors who had entered the market earlier.
   该公司对品质的坚持使其比许多更早进入市场的竞争对手存续更久。

2. These durable materials are designed to **outlast** conventional building products by at least three decades.
   这些耐用材料的设计使其比传统建筑产品至少多使用三十年。

3. Her determination and resilience enabled her to **outlast** every other contestant in the grueling endurance challenge.
   她的决心和韧性使她在这场艰苦的耐力挑战中比所有其他参赛者坚持得更久。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "out + last" — to last out beyond others, to keep going when everything else has stopped.
把"outlast"拆成"out（超过）+ last（持续）"——比其他事物持续得更久，在一切都停止后仍然继续。
