# 🔄 agitation

🔊 /ˌædʒ.ɪˈteɪ.ʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A state of anxiety or nervous excitement; the act of stirring or disturbing; public campaigning for a cause.
**中文：** 激动；搅动；煽动；烦乱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- turmoil（动荡）
- unrest（不安）
- disturbance（骚乱）
- anxiety（焦虑）
- commotion（骚动）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **political agitation, state of agitation, public agitation, cause agitation**
👉 Example: The political agitation in the region led to widespread calls for democratic reform.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden announcement caused considerable **agitation** among the shareholders attending the annual meeting.
   这一突然的公告在参加年度会议的股东中引起了相当大的骚动。

2. Prolonged **agitation** of the chemical compound may alter its molecular structure in unpredictable ways.
   长时间搅动该化合物可能会以不可预测的方式改变其分子结构。

3. The growing **agitation** for workers' rights eventually compelled the government to enact sweeping labor reforms.
   日益高涨的工人权利运动最终迫使政府颁布了全面的劳动改革法案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Agitation" = "agitate" + "-tion" — the noun form of shaking things up, whether physically or emotionally.
"Agitation"="agitate"+"-tion"——无论是物理上的搅动还是情绪上的激动，都是"摇动"的名词形式。
