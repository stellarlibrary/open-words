# 🧊 glacial

🔊 /ˈɡleɪʃəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Relating to or produced by glaciers; extremely cold; slow-moving; unfriendly in manner
**中文：** adj. 冰的；冰冷的；冰河时代的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- frigid（寒冷的）
- icy（冰冷的）
- frozen（冻结的）
- arctic（极寒的）
- frosty（冷淡的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **glacial pace / glacial period / glacial stare / glacial erosion**

👉 Example: Reform proceeded at a glacial pace, frustrating advocates who demanded immediate action.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee's **glacial** pace of deliberation caused widespread frustration among citizens awaiting critical legislative reform.
   委员会极其缓慢的审议速度引起了等待关键立法改革的公民的广泛不满。

2. Geologists analyzed the **glacial** deposits to reconstruct climate patterns from the last major ice age period.
   地质学家分析了冰川沉积物，以重建上一个主要冰河时期的气候模式。

3. The diplomat received a **glacial** reception from her counterparts, signaling deep tensions between the two nations.
   这位外交官受到对方冰冷的接待，表明两国之间存在严重紧张关系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "glacial" as related to "glacier" — anything glacial is as cold, slow, and unyielding as a massive glacier.
把"glacial"想成与"glacier（冰川）"相关——任何glacial的东西都像巨大的冰川一样寒冷、缓慢和坚硬。
