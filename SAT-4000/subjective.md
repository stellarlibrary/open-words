# 🧠 subjective

🔊 /səbˈdʒɛktɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Based on or influenced by personal feelings, tastes, or opinions rather than facts; relating to the individual's perception
**中文：** adj. 主观的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- personal（个人的）
- biased（有偏见的）
- individual（个体的）
- partial（片面的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **subjective opinion / subjective experience / subjective judgment / subjective interpretation**

👉 Example: The evaluation of abstract art is largely subjective, as viewers often reach vastly different conclusions about the same work.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor cautioned students that their analysis should rely on empirical evidence rather than **subjective** personal impressions.
   教授提醒学生，他们的分析应依赖实证证据，而非主观的个人印象。

2. Critics noted that the journalist's report was heavily **subjective**, reflecting her own political leanings rather than balanced factual coverage.
   评论人士指出，这位记者的报道高度主观，反映的是她个人的政治倾向而非平衡的事实报道。

3. The court dismissed the testimony as **subjective** speculation, arguing that it lacked sufficient corroborating evidence to be admissible.
   法院以主观臆测为由驳回了该证词，认为它缺乏足够的佐证证据而不可采纳。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "subjective" as "subject + -ive" — it comes from the subject (the person), meaning it is colored by the individual's own perspective.
把"subjective"想成"subject（主体）+ -ive"——来自主体（人），意味着受到个人自身视角的影响。
