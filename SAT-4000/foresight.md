# 🔭 foresight

🔊 /ˈfɔːrsaɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The ability to predict or the action of predicting what will happen or be needed in the future; prudent planning
**中文：** n. 先见；远见；预见；深谋远虑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- prudence（审慎）
- forethought（深思熟虑）
- prescience（先见之明）
- vision（远见）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **have the foresight to / lack foresight / remarkable foresight / with great foresight**

👉 Example: She had the foresight to invest in renewable energy long before it became mainstream.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The city planner demonstrated remarkable **foresight** by designing infrastructure capable of accommodating future population growth.
   城市规划者展现了非凡的远见，设计了能够适应未来人口增长的基础设施。

2. Without the **foresight** to diversify their investments, many firms suffered devastating losses during the financial downturn.
   由于缺乏分散投资的远见，许多公司在金融衰退期间遭受了毁灭性损失。

3. His **foresight** in stockpiling essential medical supplies proved invaluable when the unexpected health crisis struck the region.
   他储备基本医疗物资的远见在意外的健康危机袭击该地区时被证明是无价的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fore-sight" as "seeing before" — like having eyes that can look into the future, foresight means you can see problems and opportunities before they arrive.
把"foresight"想成"提前看见"——就像拥有能看到未来的眼睛，foresight意味着你能在问题和机遇到来之前就看到它们。
