# 📜 prescript

🔊 /ˈpriːskrɪpt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Prescribed or laid down as a rule or direction. (n.) An ordinance, law, or command; a prescribed rule or regulation
**中文：** adj. 规定的；指定的；有时效的 n. 规定；命令；法令

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ordinance（法令）
- decree（命令）
- mandate（指令）
- regulation（规定）
- directive（指示）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a prescript rule / legal prescript / follow the prescript / prescript of the authority**

👉 Example: The military officer followed every prescript of the command without deviation or question.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient legal **prescript** required all citizens to contribute a portion of their harvest to the communal granary each autumn.
   这项古老的法令要求所有公民每年秋天将一部分收成贡献给公共粮仓。

2. Scholars debated whether the royal **prescript** had been intended as a permanent statute or merely a temporary wartime measure.
   学者们争论这项皇家命令是作为永久性法规还是仅仅作为临时战时措施。

3. The committee issued a **prescript** guideline outlining the ethical standards that all researchers must adhere to during clinical trials.
   委员会发布了一项规定性指南，概述了所有研究人员在临床试验中必须遵守的伦理标准。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-script" as "a script written before" — rules that are written down in advance for everyone to follow.
把"prescript"想成"pre-script"——提前写好的"剧本"，即事先制定好让所有人遵守的规定。
