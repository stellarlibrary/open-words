# 😐 tolerable

🔊 /ˈtɒlərəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Able to be endured or accepted; moderately good but not outstanding; passable
**中文：** 可以忍受的；可容忍的；还可以的；尚好的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bearable（可忍受的）
- acceptable（可接受的）
- passable（尚可的）
- adequate（足够的）
- endurable（能忍耐的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tolerable conditions / tolerable level / tolerable pain / barely tolerable**

👉 Example: The heat was barely tolerable during the afternoon hours of the expedition.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The noise from the construction site was **tolerable** during the morning but became unbearable by midafternoon.
   建筑工地的噪音在上午还可以忍受，但到了下午就变得无法忍受了。

2. The committee deemed the environmental impact of the new factory to be within **tolerable** limits for the region.
   委员会认为新工厂对环境的影响在该地区可容忍的范围之内。

3. Although the accommodations were modest, the visiting scholars found the living conditions perfectly **tolerable** for their stay.
   虽然住宿条件简朴，来访学者们觉得居住条件对于他们的逗留来说完全可以接受。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "toler-able" as "able to tolerate" — if something is tolerable, you are able to put up with it even if it is not ideal.
把"tolerable"想成"able to tolerate"——如果某事是tolerable的，说明你能够忍受它，即使它并不理想。
