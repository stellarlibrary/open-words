# ⚖️ extenuate

🔊 /ɪkˈstɛnjuˌeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To lessen the seriousness of an offense by providing partial excuses; to mitigate; to underestimate or make light of
**中文：** v. 减轻；低估；为…找借口

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- mitigate（减轻）
- palliate（缓和）
- diminish（减少）
- excuse（辩解）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **extenuating circumstances / extenuate the offense / extenuate the guilt / nothing can extenuate**

👉 Example: The defense attorney argued that extenuating circumstances should be considered when determining the sentence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The lawyer attempted to **extenuate** his client's actions by presenting evidence of prolonged psychological distress and hardship.
   律师试图通过提出长期心理困扰和困苦的证据来减轻其委托人行为的严重性。

2. No amount of rationalization can **extenuate** the ethical violations committed by the corporation against vulnerable communities.
   再多的辩解也无法减轻该公司对弱势群体所犯的道德违规行为的严重性。

3. The judge acknowledged the **extenuating** factors but maintained that the severity of the crime warranted a substantial penalty.
   法官承认了减轻处罚的因素，但坚持认为犯罪的严重性需要处以重罚。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-tenu-ate" — "tenu" relates to "thin" (like tenuous), so to extenuate is to make an offense appear thinner or less serious.
把"extenuate"想成"ex+tenu(薄的)+ate"——把罪行变"薄"，使其看起来不那么严重，即减轻。
