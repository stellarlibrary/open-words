# ☀️ drought

🔊 /draʊt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall leading to a shortage of water; a prolonged absence or lack
**中文：** n. 干旱；旱灾；缺乏

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- aridity（干旱）
- dryness（干燥）
- scarcity（匮乏）
- shortage（短缺）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **severe drought / prolonged drought / drought conditions / drought-resistant**

👉 Example: The prolonged drought devastated crops and threatened the region's food supply.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The severe **drought** forced farmers to abandon their fields and seek alternative livelihoods in neighboring urban centers.
   严重的干旱迫使农民放弃了他们的田地，转而到邻近的城市中心寻求替代生计。

2. Government agencies implemented strict water rationing policies to help communities endure the unprecedented **drought** conditions.
   政府机构实施了严格的水资源配给政策，以帮助社区度过前所未有的干旱状况。

3. The **drought** of creative talent in the industry prompted universities to expand their arts and design programs.
   行业内创意人才的匮乏促使大学扩大了艺术和设计项目。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "drought" as "dry + ought" — the land ought to have water, but it is completely dry.
把"drought"想成"dry + ought"——土地本应有水，但却完全干涸了。
