# 🔴 cardinal

🔊 /ˈkɑːrdɪnəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A leading dignitary of the Roman Catholic Church; a North American songbird with bright red plumage. (adj.) Of the greatest importance; fundamental; deep red in color
**中文：** n. 红衣主教；枢机主教；北美红雀 adj. 主要的，基本的；最重要的；深红色的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fundamental（基本的）
- principal（主要的）
- paramount（至高的）
- essential（必要的）
- primary（首要的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cardinal rule / cardinal sin / cardinal virtue / cardinal number / cardinal principle**

👉 Example: Honesty is regarded as a cardinal virtue in nearly every ethical framework.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor emphasized that intellectual integrity is a **cardinal** principle of rigorous academic scholarship and research.
   教授强调，学术诚信是严格学术研究的基本原则。

2. The newly appointed **cardinal** delivered an eloquent address before the assembled clergy at the Vatican ceremony.
   新任命的红衣主教在梵蒂冈仪式上向聚集的神职人员发表了雄辩的讲话。

3. Neglecting to verify one's sources before publication is considered a **cardinal** sin in professional journalism.
   在发表前忽略核实消息来源在专业新闻界被视为不可饶恕的大错。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cardinal" as sharing the root "card-" with "core" — a cardinal rule is a core rule, the most important one. Cardinals in the church wear deep red robes, making them stand out as principal figures.
把"cardinal"想成与"core"（核心）同源——一条cardinal rule就是核心规则，最重要的规则。教会中的枢机主教穿着深红色的长袍，使他们作为主要人物格外醒目。
