# 🎬 playwright

🔊 /ˈpleɪraɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who writes plays for the theater, television, or radio
**中文：** n. 剧作家

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dramatist（戏剧家）
- screenwriter（编剧）
- scriptwriter（剧本作者）
- author（作家）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **renowned playwright / aspiring playwright / playwright and director / works of a playwright**

👉 Example: The celebrated playwright spent three years crafting the script before it premiered on Broadway.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **playwright** skillfully wove themes of social injustice into a compelling narrative that resonated with modern audiences.
   这位剧作家巧妙地将社会不公的主题融入引人入胜的叙事中，引起了现代观众的共鸣。

2. As a young **playwright**, she faced numerous rejections before her debut work was finally produced at a major theater.
   作为一名年轻的剧作家，她在处女作最终被一家大剧院搬上舞台之前曾遭受无数次拒绝。

3. The university invited a distinguished **playwright** to deliver the keynote address at the annual arts and humanities symposium.
   大学邀请了一位杰出的剧作家在年度人文艺术研讨会上发表主题演讲。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "playwright" as "play" + "wright" (an old word for craftsman) — a craftsman who builds plays, just as a shipwright builds ships.
把"playwright"想成"play（戏剧）"+"wright（工匠）"——一个打造戏剧的工匠，就像shipwright（造船工）造船一样。
