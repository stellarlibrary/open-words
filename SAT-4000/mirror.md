# 🪞 mirror

🔊 /ˈmɪrər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A reflective surface; a faithful representation or model. (v.) To reflect; to closely resemble or represent
**中文：** n. 镜子；真实的写照；榜样 v. 反射；反映

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reflect（反映）
- replicate（复制）
- imitate（模仿）
- echo（回应）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mirror image / mirror the reality / hold up a mirror to / mirror the trends**

👉 Example: Literature often mirrors the social and political conditions of its time.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The novelist's work **mirrors** the profound social inequalities that persist in contemporary urban communities across the nation.
   这位小说家的作品反映了当代全国城市社区中持续存在的深刻社会不平等。

2. The student's academic performance **mirrored** the dedication and discipline she consistently applied to her daily study routine.
   这位学生的学业表现反映了她在日常学习中始终保持的专注和自律。

3. Public opinion polls serve as a **mirror** of the electorate's shifting attitudes toward government economic policy reform.
   民意调查是选民对政府经济政策改革态度不断变化的真实写照。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mirror" as "mi-rror" — "mi" sounds like "me," and a mirror shows "me" to myself, reflecting reality back.
把"mirror"想成"mi-rror"——"mi"听起来像"me（我）"，镜子把"我"展示给自己，反映现实。
