# :shield: repel

:speaker: /rɪˈpɛl/<br>
:label: *verb | 动词*

---

## :books: Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To drive back or ward off an attacker; to refuse to accept; to cause feelings of distaste or disgust
**中文：** v. 击退；抵制；使厌恶；拒绝；排斥

---

## :link: Synonyms | 近义词
- repulse（击退）
- deter（阻止）
- rebuff（拒绝）
- resist（抵抗）
- disgust（使厌恶）

---

## :pushpin: Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **repel an attack / repel invaders / repel insects / repel moisture**

:point_right: Example: The fortress was designed to repel even the most determined siege by hostile forces.

---

## :memo: Examples | 例句

1. The outnumbered garrison managed to **repel** three successive waves of enemy soldiers attempting to breach the fortifications.
   寡不敌众的守军成功击退了三次试图突破防御工事的敌军进攻。

2. The special coating on the fabric is engineered to **repel** water and prevent moisture from penetrating the material.
   这种织物上的特殊涂层经过设计，能够防水并防止湿气渗透材料。

3. His arrogant demeanor and dismissive attitude tended to **repel** potential colleagues rather than attract meaningful collaboration.
   他傲慢的态度和轻视的举止往往使潜在的同事感到厌恶，而非吸引有意义的合作。

---

## :jigsaw: Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "repel" as "re-PEL" — imagine pelting something back repeatedly, pushing it away each time it approaches.
把"repel"想成"re-pel（反复投掷）"——想象你不断把东西打回去，每次它靠近时都把它推开。
