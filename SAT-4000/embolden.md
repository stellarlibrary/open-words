# 💪 embolden

🔊 /ɪmˈboʊldən/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To give someone the courage or confidence to do something; to encourage or inspire boldness
**中文：** v. 使有胆量；使大胆；鼓励

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- encourage（鼓励）
- empower（赋权）
- hearten（振奋）
- fortify（增强信心）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **embolden someone to act / emboldened by success / embolden the opposition / feel emboldened**

👉 Example: The early victory emboldened the troops to advance deeper into enemy territory.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The favorable court ruling **emboldened** civil rights activists to pursue even more ambitious legislative reforms across the country.
   有利的法院裁决鼓励了民权活动人士在全国范围内推动更具雄心的立法改革。

2. His unexpected success in the preliminary rounds **emboldened** the young debater to challenge far more experienced competitors.
   预赛中出乎意料的成功使这位年轻辩手有胆量去挑战经验丰富得多的竞争者。

3. Critics warned that the lenient sentence would **embolden** other corporate executives to engage in similar fraudulent practices.
   批评者警告说，从轻判决会助长其他企业高管从事类似欺诈行为的胆量。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "em-BOLD-en" — to make someone BOLD, filling them with the courage to take action they previously feared.
把"embolden"想成"em-BOLD-en"——让某人变得大胆（BOLD），赋予他们勇气去做之前害怕的事情。
