# 🗣️ colloquial

🔊 /kəˈloʊ.kwi.əl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** Relating to or characteristic of ordinary, informal conversation rather than formal speech or writing; vernacular.

**中文：** 白话的；通俗的；口语体的。指与日常非正式对话相关的语言风格，而非正式演讲或书面语。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- informal（非正式的）
- vernacular（方言的）
- conversational（会话的）
- casual（随意的）
- everyday（日常的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Collocation:** "colloquial language / colloquial expression / colloquial tone" — describes language that is informal but not necessarily incorrect.

**Example:** Academic papers should avoid colloquial expressions and maintain a formal register throughout.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor advised students to eliminate **colloquial** language from their dissertations to maintain an appropriate academic tone.
   教授建议学生在论文中去除**口语化的**语言，以保持恰当的学术语气。

2. Many **colloquial** expressions that originate in spoken discourse eventually find their way into standard written English.
   许多源于口头话语的**口语**表达最终进入了标准书面英语。

3. The novelist deliberately employed **colloquial** dialogue to create authentic characters that resonated with contemporary readers.
   这位小说家刻意使用**通俗的**对话来塑造能引起当代读者共鸣的真实人物。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

**English:** "Colloquial" shares its root with "colloquy" (conversation) — from Latin *colloquium* (con- together + loqui to speak). Language used when people speak together casually.

**中文：** "Colloquial"与"colloquy"（谈话）同根——来自拉丁语 *colloquium*（con- 一起 + loqui 说话）。人们随意交谈时使用的语言，即"口语的"。
