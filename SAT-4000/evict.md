# 🚪 evict

🔊 /ɪˈvɪkt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To expel someone from a property, especially with the support of the law; to force out
**中文：** v. 驱逐；逐出

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- expel（驱逐）
- oust（赶走）
- remove（移除）
- dispossess（剥夺）
- eject（逐出）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **evict tenants / evict from a property / evict illegally / evict by force**

👉 Example: The landlord filed a court order to evict the tenants who had not paid rent for three consecutive months.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The city council voted to **evict** all unauthorized occupants from the condemned apartment building before the scheduled demolition date.
   市议会投票决定在预定拆除日期之前将所有未经授权的住户从这栋危楼中驱逐出去。

2. Historians documented how colonial authorities would routinely **evict** indigenous communities from their ancestral lands without any legal justification.
   历史学家记录了殖民当局如何经常在没有任何法律依据的情况下将原住民社区从其祖传土地上驱逐。

3. The court ruled that the property management company could not legally **evict** the elderly residents without providing adequate alternative housing.
   法院裁定，物业管理公司在没有提供足够替代住房的情况下不能合法地驱逐老年居民。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "e-vict" as "e" (out) + "vict" (conquer, from Latin vincere) — to conquer someone out of their home, to force them to leave.
把"evict"想成"e"（出去）+ "vict"（征服，源自拉丁语vincere）——把某人从家中"征服"出去，迫使他们离开。
