# 💡 emit

🔊 /ɪˈmɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To produce and discharge something, especially gas, radiation, light, or sound; to issue formally
**中文：** v. 发出；放射；发行

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- radiate（辐射）
- discharge（排放）
- release（释放）
- exude（散发）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **emit light / emit radiation / emit a signal / emit greenhouse gases**

👉 Example: The factory was found to emit toxic pollutants that exceeded the legal limits established by environmental regulations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The newly discovered star was observed to **emit** powerful bursts of X-ray radiation at remarkably regular intervals.
   这颗新发现的恒星被观测到以极为规律的间隔发出强烈的X射线辐射脉冲。

2. Certain species of deep-sea organisms can **emit** bioluminescent light to attract prey in the absolute darkness of the ocean.
   某些深海生物可以发出生物荧光来在海洋的绝对黑暗中吸引猎物。

3. The government's new policy requires all industrial facilities to drastically reduce the amount of carbon dioxide they **emit** annually.
   政府的新政策要求所有工业设施大幅减少其每年排放的二氧化碳量。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "e-mit" as "e(x)-mit(send)" — to send something out, like light or sound being sent into the environment.
把"emit"想成"e（出）+ mit（送）"——把某物送出去，就像光或声音被释放到环境中。
