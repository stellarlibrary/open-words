# 🔮 foretell

🔊 /fɔːrˈtɛl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To predict or prophesy a future event; to indicate in advance what will happen
**中文：** v. 预言；预示；预告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- predict（预测）
- prophesy（预言）
- prognosticate（预示）
- foresee（预见）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **foretell the future / foretell events / foretell doom / foretell what lies ahead**

👉 Example: Ancient oracles claimed the power to foretell the fate of entire kingdoms.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. No economic model can reliably **foretell** the precise timing of a market collapse with absolute certainty.
   没有任何经济模型能够绝对可靠地预言市场崩溃的确切时间。

2. The scientist's groundbreaking research **foretold** the environmental consequences that industrial nations would face decades later.
   这位科学家的开创性研究预示了工业化国家几十年后将面临的环境后果。

3. Early warning signs in the patient's bloodwork **foretold** the onset of a serious autoimmune condition.
   患者血液检查中的早期预警信号预示了一种严重自身免疫疾病的发作。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fore-tell" as "telling before" — someone who foretells is literally telling you about events before they happen, like a fortune teller.
把"foretell"想成"提前告诉"——foretell的人就是在事情发生之前告诉你，就像算命先生一样。
