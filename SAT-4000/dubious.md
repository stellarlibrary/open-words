# 🤨 dubious

🔊 /ˈdjuːbiəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Doubtful or uncertain; of questionable quality, character, or validity; hesitating to believe
**中文：** adj. 可疑的；半信半疑的；有问题的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- questionable（可疑的）
- skeptical（怀疑的）
- suspect（令人怀疑的）
- uncertain（不确定的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dubious about / dubious claim / dubious distinction / dubious origins**

👉 Example: The committee remained dubious about the feasibility of the proposed budget reforms.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scientific community regarded the researcher's **dubious** claims with considerable skepticism until independent laboratories could verify the results.
   科学界对该研究人员可疑的声明持相当怀疑的态度，直到独立实验室能够验证结果。

2. The antique dealer's **dubious** reputation made experienced collectors hesitant to purchase items from his establishment.
   这位古董商可疑的名声使经验丰富的收藏家对从他的店铺购买物品犹豫不决。

3. Investors grew increasingly **dubious** about the projected returns after the company failed to meet its quarterly financial targets.
   在公司未能达到季度财务目标后，投资者对预期回报越来越持怀疑态度。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dubious" as coming from "doubt" — the Latin root "dubium" means doubt. If something is dubious, you have good reason to doubt it.
把"dubious"想成来自"doubt"——拉丁语词根"dubium"意为怀疑。如果某事是dubious的，你有充分理由怀疑它。
