# 🪤 decoy

🔊 /ˈdiːkɔɪ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person, animal, or object used to lure others into a trap; a bait. (v.) To lure or entice by means of a decoy
**中文：** n. 诱饵；诱骗 v. 诱骗

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- lure（诱饵）
- bait（饵）
- trap（陷阱）
- enticement（引诱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **act as a decoy / use a decoy / decoy someone into / set up a decoy**

👉 Example: The detectives used an undercover officer as a decoy to catch the suspected thief in the act.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The military strategists employed inflatable tanks as **decoys** to mislead enemy reconnaissance aircraft about the true position of forces.
   军事战略家使用充气坦克作为诱饵，误导敌方侦察机对真实兵力位置的判断。

2. Hunters traditionally carve wooden duck **decoys** and place them on the water surface to attract migratory waterfowl.
   猎人传统上雕刻木制的鸭子诱饵，将其放在水面上吸引迁徙的水禽。

3. The cybersecurity team set up a **decoy** server to divert hackers away from the organization's actual sensitive databases.
   网络安全团队设置了一台诱饵服务器，将黑客从组织实际的敏感数据库中引开。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "decoy" as "de- + coy" — something that acts coy and innocent to trick you into coming closer, like bait in a trap.
把"decoy"想成"de- + coy（害羞的）"——某个东西装作害羞天真来诱骗你靠近，就像陷阱中的诱饵。
