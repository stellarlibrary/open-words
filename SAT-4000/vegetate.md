# 🛋️ vegetate

🔊 /ˈvɛdʒɪteɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To live a dull, inactive, or unchallenging life; to exist with minimal mental or physical stimulation
**中文：** v. 过单调呆板的生活；无所事事地活着

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- stagnate（停滞）
- languish（萎靡不振）
- idle（闲散度日）
- deteriorate（衰退）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vegetate in front of / vegetate at home / vegetate all day / refuse to vegetate**

👉 Example: After retirement, he feared he would simply vegetate at home without meaningful activities to pursue.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The psychologist warned that prolonged isolation could cause individuals to **vegetate** rather than engage in productive intellectual pursuits.
   心理学家警告说，长期的孤立可能导致个人过着单调呆板的生活，而非从事有成效的智力活动。

2. Critics accused the administration of allowing the rural economy to **vegetate** while concentrating all development resources in urban centers.
   批评者指责政府让农村经济停滞不前，同时将所有发展资源集中在城市中心。

3. She refused to **vegetate** after her injury and instead enrolled in online courses to keep her mind actively stimulated.
   她拒绝在受伤后无所事事，而是报名参加了在线课程以保持头脑活跃。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vegetate" as "living like a vegetable" — just sitting still, growing roots, doing nothing active or productive.
把"vegetate"想成"像蔬菜一样活着"——只是静静地坐着，扎根不动，不做任何积极或有成效的事。
