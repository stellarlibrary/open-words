# 🕰️ era

🔊 /ˈɪrə/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A long and distinct period of history marked by particular characteristics, events, or developments
**中文：** n. 时代；年代；纪元

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- epoch（纪元）
- age（时代）
- period（时期）
- time（时期）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a new era / the modern era / mark an era / the end of an era**

👉 Example: The invention of the printing press ushered in a new era of mass communication and literacy.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The discovery of penicillin marked the beginning of a transformative **era** in medicine that saved countless millions of lives worldwide.
   青霉素的发现标志着医学变革时代的开始，拯救了全球无数人的生命。

2. Scholars often regard the Renaissance as an **era** of unprecedented intellectual achievement and artistic innovation across European civilization.
   学者们通常将文艺复兴视为欧洲文明中空前的智力成就和艺术创新的时代。

3. The digital **era** has fundamentally altered how societies communicate, conduct commerce, and access information on a global scale.
   数字时代从根本上改变了社会在全球范围内沟通、进行商业活动和获取信息的方式。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "era" as "E-R-A" — "Events Remembered Always" — a stretch of time so significant that it is always remembered.
把"era"想成"E-R-A"——"Events Remembered Always（永远被记住的事件）"——一段意义重大、永远被铭记的时间。
