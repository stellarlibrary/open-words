# 😈 notorious

🔊 /noʊˈtɔːriəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Famous or well known, typically for some bad quality or deed; infamous
**中文：** adj. 声名狼藉的；臭名昭著的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- infamous（臭名昭著的）
- disreputable（名声不好的）
- scandalous（可耻的）
- ignominious（不光彩的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **notorious for something / notorious criminal / a notorious case / notoriously difficult**

👉 Example: The region is notorious for its unpredictable weather patterns and sudden storms.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The city became **notorious** for its high crime rates and widespread corruption within the local government administration.
   这座城市因其高犯罪率和地方政府内部的普遍腐败而变得声名狼藉。

2. The **notorious** case of corporate fraud prompted legislators to introduce stricter financial regulations across the entire industry.
   这起臭名昭著的企业欺诈案促使立法者在整个行业引入更严格的金融监管。

3. That stretch of highway is **notorious** for causing accidents due to its sharp curves and inadequate lighting conditions.
   那段公路因急弯和照明不足而频繁引发事故，臭名远扬。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "not-or-ious" — a person who is "noted" but not for anything good; they are noted for being terrible.
把"notorious"想成"noted(被注意的)"的负面版——出名但不是因为好事，而是因为坏事而被人记住。
