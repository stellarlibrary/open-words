# 🎨 avant-garde

🔊 /ˌævɒ̃ˈɡɑːrd/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** The avant-garde refers to people or works that are experimental, innovative, or pushing the boundaries of what is accepted as the norm in art, culture, or ideas.

**中文：** 先锋派，前卫派；指在艺术、文化或思想领域中具有实验性、创新性或突破常规的人或作品。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- vanguard （先锋）
- innovators （革新者）
- pioneers （开拓者）
- trailblazers （先驱者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Avant-garde" is borrowed from French, meaning "advance guard." It is often used as both a noun and an adjective to describe cutting-edge art, music, or literature that challenges traditional conventions.

"avant-garde"源自法语，意为"前卫部队"。它既可用作名词也可用作形容词，用来描述挑战传统惯例的前沿艺术、音乐或文学。

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **avant-garde** movement of the early twentieth century fundamentally transformed the landscape of modern art.
   *二十世纪初的**先锋派**运动从根本上改变了现代艺术的面貌。*

2. Many critics dismissed her **avant-garde** compositions as incomprehensible, yet they later gained widespread acclaim.
   *许多评论家认为她的**前卫**作品难以理解，但后来这些作品获得了广泛赞誉。*

3. The gallery exclusively showcases works by the **avant-garde**, attracting collectors from around the world.
   *该画廊专门展示**先锋派**的作品，吸引了来自世界各地的收藏家。*

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "avant" (before/ahead) + "garde" (guard) — the avant-garde are the "advance guards" who march ahead of everyone else into new artistic territory.

联想"avant"（前方）+"garde"（卫兵）——先锋派就是那些走在所有人前面、进入新艺术领域的"前卫士兵"。
