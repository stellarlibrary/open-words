# 📤 dismissal

🔊 /dɪsˈmɪsəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act of removing someone from a position of employment; the act of rejecting or refusing to consider something
**中文：** n. 解雇；免职；驳回

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- termination（终止雇用）
- discharge（解雇）
- removal（免职）
- rejection（拒绝）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **wrongful dismissal / grounds for dismissal / dismissal of charges / summary dismissal**

👉 Example: The employee filed a formal complaint alleging wrongful dismissal based on discriminatory practices.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden **dismissal** of the research director raised serious concerns about the institution's commitment to academic freedom.
   研究主任的突然免职引发了人们对该机构维护学术自由承诺的严重担忧。

2. Labor unions protested the mass **dismissal** of factory workers, demanding that management negotiate fair severance agreements.
   工会抗议大规模解雇工厂工人，要求管理层协商公平的遣散协议。

3. The court's **dismissal** of the environmental lawsuit was widely criticized by conservation groups and legal scholars alike.
   法院驳回这起环境诉讼的裁决遭到了环保组织和法学学者的广泛批评。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dismiss-al" as the noun form of "dismiss" — adding "-al" turns the action of sending away into the official event of being let go.
把"dismissal"想成"dismiss（解雇）+ -al（名词后缀）"——把解雇的动作变成正式的免职事件。
