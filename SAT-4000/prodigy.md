# 💒 prodigy

🔊 /ˈprɒdɪdʒi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person, especially a young one, endowed with exceptional abilities or qualities; a remarkable thing; a wonder or marvel
**中文：** n. 奇才；天才；奇迹，奇事；奇观；预兆

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- genius（天才）
- wonder（奇迹）
- marvel（奇观）
- phenomenon（非凡的人或事）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **child prodigy / musical prodigy / math prodigy / prodigy of nature**

👉 Example: The child prodigy composed her first symphony at the remarkable age of seven.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Mozart is widely regarded as one of the greatest musical **prodigies** in the entire history of Western classical music.
   莫扎特被广泛认为是整个西方古典音乐史上最伟大的音乐天才之一。

2. The young chess **prodigy** defeated several grandmasters before reaching the age of twelve, astonishing the international community.
   这位年轻的国际象棋天才在十二岁之前击败了多位特级大师，令国际社会震惊。

3. Scientists hailed the volcanic formation as a **prodigy** of nature that offered unprecedented insight into geological processes.
   科学家们将这一火山构造誉为自然奇观，为地质过程提供了前所未有的认识。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "prodigy" as "prod-i-gy" — someone who can "produce" amazing things at an incredibly young age, a true genius.
把"prodigy"想成"produce（产生）"的变体——能在极小年纪就创造出惊人成就的天才。
