# 🔨 coercive

🔊 /koʊˈɜːrsɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Relating to or using force, threats, or intimidation to compel compliance; having the quality of compelling obedience through pressure
**中文：** adj. 强制的；胁迫的；高压的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- forceful（强制的）
- oppressive（压迫的）
- compulsory（强制性的）
- intimidating（恐吓的）
- authoritarian（专制的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **coercive measures / coercive power / coercive tactics / coercive control / coercive force**

👉 Example: The human rights report condemned the government's coercive tactics used against peaceful demonstrators.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The international tribunal declared that **coercive** interrogation techniques violated established conventions governing the treatment of prisoners.
   国际法庭宣布胁迫性审讯技术违反了关于囚犯待遇的既定公约。

2. Scholars argued that **coercive** diplomacy, when employed without restraint, often generates more resistance than genuine cooperation from nations.
   学者们认为，强制外交如果不加节制地使用，往往会产生更多的抵抗而非国家间的真正合作。

3. The new legislation aimed to criminalize **coercive** control within domestic relationships, protecting victims from sustained psychological abuse.
   新立法旨在将家庭关系中的强制控制定为犯罪，保护受害者免受持续的心理虐待。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "coercive" as "coerce" + "-ive" (having the nature of) — something coercive has the nature of forcing or pressuring others.
把"coercive"想成"coerce"（强制）+ "-ive"（具有……性质的）——coercive的事物具有强迫或施压他人的性质。
