# 😨 trepidation

🔊 /ˌtrepɪˈdeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A feeling of fear or agitation about something that may happen; nervous anxiety; apprehension
**中文：** n. 恐惧；惊恐；忧虑；不安

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- apprehension（忧虑）
- anxiety（焦虑）
- dread（恐惧）
- unease（不安）
- foreboding（预感）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **with trepidation / a sense of trepidation / feel trepidation / filled with trepidation**

👉 Example: She approached the podium with considerable trepidation before delivering her keynote address.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The students awaited their examination results with considerable **trepidation**, uncertain whether their preparation had been sufficient.
   学生们怀着相当大的忧虑等待考试结果，不确定他们的准备是否充分。

2. It was with great **trepidation** that the committee approved the experimental treatment, fully aware of its unpredictable risks.
   委员会怀着极大的不安批准了这项实验性治疗，充分意识到其不可预测的风险。

3. Despite her **trepidation** about public speaking, she delivered a compelling address that captivated the entire international audience.
   尽管她对公开演讲感到恐惧，她还是发表了一场引人入胜的演讲，吸引了全场国际听众。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "trepidation" as "trip + id + ation" — the fear you feel when you might trip and fall in front of everyone.
把"trepidation"想成"trip + id + ation"——当你可能在众人面前绊倒时所感受到的恐惧。
