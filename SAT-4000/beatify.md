# 🙏 beatify

🔊 /biˈætɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To declare a deceased person to be among the blessed in heaven (a step toward canonization in the Catholic Church); to make supremely happy
**中文：** v. 行宣福礼；使享福；赐福

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bless（祝福）
- sanctify（使神圣化）
- glorify（赞美）
- exalt（提升）
- consecrate（奉献）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **beatify a saint / beatify the deceased / beatified by the Pope / process to beatify**

👉 Example: The Vatican announced its decision to beatify the missionary who devoted her life to serving the poor.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The Pope formally **beatified** the beloved nun in a grand ceremony attended by hundreds of thousands of faithful pilgrims.
   教皇在一场盛大的仪式上正式为这位深受爱戴的修女行宣福礼，数十万信徒朝圣者出席。

2. The Church required documented miracles before it could officially **beatify** any candidate for eventual sainthood recognition.
   教会要求在正式为任何候选人行宣福礼以最终获得圣徒认定之前，必须有有据可查的奇迹。

3. The community celebrated when the diocese began the lengthy canonical process to **beatify** their revered local priest.
   当教区开始为其受尊敬的本地神父进行冗长的宣福礼教规程序时，社区进行了庆祝。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "beatify" as "beat-ify" — to make someone "beat" (blessed) by officially declaring them holy, like adding "-ify" means to make.
把"beatify"想成"beat-ify"——让某人变得"beat"（蒙福的），通过正式宣布他们为神圣的，就像加上"-ify"表示"使成为"。
