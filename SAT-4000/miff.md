# 😤 miff

🔊 /mɪf/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A slight feeling of annoyance; a petty quarrel. (v.) To offend or annoy someone, usually in a minor way.
**中文：** n. 微怒；小争执 v. 使……恼怒；发脾气

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- irritate（激怒）
- annoy（使烦恼）
- offend（冒犯）
- displease（使不快）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **be miffed at / be miffed about / a bit miffed / slightly miffed**

👉 Example: She was clearly miffed about being excluded from the planning committee without explanation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor was visibly **miffed** when several students arrived late to the important guest lecture on Monday.
   教授在几名学生周一迟到参加重要客座讲座时明显感到恼火。

2. A minor **miff** between the two diplomats threatened to derail the carefully negotiated trade agreement between both nations.
   两位外交官之间的小争执险些破坏两国之间精心谈判的贸易协定。

3. It would **miff** anyone to discover that their original research had been published without proper attribution or credit.
   发现自己的原创研究未经适当署名就被发表，任何人都会感到恼怒。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "miff" as a small "piff" of anger — just a tiny puff of irritation, not a full explosion of rage.
把"miff"想成一小"噗"的怒气——只是一点点恼火，而不是一场大爆发。
