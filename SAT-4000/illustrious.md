# 🌟 illustrious

🔊 /ɪˈlʌstrɪəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Well known and highly distinguished; famous and admired for great achievements
**中文：** adj. 杰出的，著名的，光荣的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- renowned（著名的）
- distinguished（杰出的）
- eminent（卓越的）
- celebrated（享有盛名的）
- prestigious（有声望的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **illustrious career / illustrious history / illustrious figure / illustrious predecessor**

👉 Example: The university boasts an illustrious history of producing Nobel laureates and world leaders.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The museum dedicated an entire wing to the **illustrious** achievements of scientists who transformed modern medicine forever.
   博物馆专门辟出一个展厅，展示那些永远改变现代医学的杰出科学家的成就。

2. She followed in the footsteps of her **illustrious** grandmother, who had been the first female senator in the country.
   她追随杰出祖母的脚步，她的祖母曾是该国第一位女性参议员。

3. The **illustrious** conductor received a standing ovation after leading the orchestra through a flawless evening performance.
   这位著名的指挥家在带领乐团完成一场完美的晚间演出后获得了全场起立鼓掌。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "illustrious" as "illustr(ate) + ious" — someone so great that their life story could illustrate a book of achievements.
把"illustrious"想成"illustr(ate)（展示）+ ious"——一个人如此杰出，他的人生故事可以写成一本成就画册。
