# 🏰 anglo-saxon

🔊 /ˌæŋɡloʊ ˈsæksən/<br>
🏷️ *n. Anglo-Saxon | 名词：盎格鲁—撒克逊人*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A member of the Germanic peoples who settled in England from the 5th century; also refers to the Old English language or culture of that period.
**中文：** 五世纪起定居于英格兰的日耳曼民族成员；也指那个时期的古英语或文化。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- Teuton（条顿人）
- Saxon（撒克逊人）
- Germanic settler（日耳曼定居者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **Anglo-Saxon heritage, Anglo-Saxon culture, Anglo-Saxon period, Anglo-Saxon traditions**
👉 Example: The Anglo-Saxon period profoundly shaped the legal and linguistic foundations of modern England.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The museum's new exhibition explores how **Anglo-Saxon** artisans crafted intricate jewelry from gold and precious gems.
   博物馆的新展览探索了盎格鲁—撒克逊工匠如何用黄金和宝石制作精美的珠宝。

2. Scholars believe that **Anglo-Saxon** legal customs laid the groundwork for the English common law tradition.
   学者们认为盎格鲁—撒克逊的法律习俗为英国普通法传统奠定了基础。

3. The discovery of the burial ship revealed remarkable insights into **Anglo-Saxon** society and its hierarchical structure.
   这艘葬船的发现揭示了关于盎格鲁—撒克逊社会及其等级结构的重要见解。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Anglo" comes from the Angles tribe and "Saxon" from the Saxons — two Germanic tribes that together colonized Britain and gave England its very name.
"Anglo"来自盎格尔部落，"Saxon"来自撒克逊部落——这两个日耳曼部落共同殖民了不列颠，并赋予了英格兰它的名字。
