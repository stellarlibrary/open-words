# 🌀 revolution

🔊 /ˌrevəˈluːʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A forcible overthrow of a government or social order; a dramatic and wide-reaching change; the action of revolving around an axis or center
**中文：** n. 旋转

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- upheaval（剧变）
- transformation（变革）
- rotation（旋转）
- rebellion（叛乱）
- overhaul（彻底改革）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **industrial revolution / scientific revolution / revolution in thinking / one full revolution / revolution per minute**

👉 Example: The Earth completes one full revolution around the Sun approximately every 365 days.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The digital **revolution** has fundamentally altered how individuals communicate, access information, and conduct business across the globe.
   数字革命从根本上改变了全球个人交流、获取信息和开展业务的方式。

2. The planet completes a single **revolution** around its star in approximately three hundred and seventy Earth days.
   该行星绕其恒星完成一次旋转大约需要三百七十个地球日。

3. Historians often debate whether gradual reform or sudden **revolution** is more effective at producing lasting institutional change.
   历史学家经常争论渐进式改革还是突然的革命在产生持久的制度变革方面更为有效。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "revolve" + "-tion" — something that revolves or turns completely around, whether a planet in orbit or a society in upheaval.
把"revolution"想成"revolve（旋转）+ -tion（名词后缀）"——完全转一圈的事物，无论是轨道上的行星还是剧变中的社会。
