# ⚖️ barrister

🔊 /ˈbærɪstər/<br>
🏷️ *n. | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A lawyer who is qualified to argue cases in higher courts; especially used in British legal systems to distinguish from a solicitor.

**中文：** 律师；出庭律师（尤指英国法律体系中有资格在高等法院出庭辩护的律师）。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- attorney （律师）
- advocate （辩护律师）
- counsel （法律顾问）
- lawyer （律师）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

In the British legal system, a "barrister" is distinct from a "solicitor." A barrister specializes in courtroom advocacy and providing legal opinions, while a solicitor handles legal matters outside of court. In American English, the general term "lawyer" or "attorney" is more commonly used. "Barrister" appears frequently in formal and legal contexts.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The experienced **barrister** delivered a compelling argument that persuaded the jury to acquit.
   中文：这位经验丰富的出庭律师发表了令人信服的辩词，说服陪审团作出无罪裁决。

2. She trained for many years before being admitted to the bar as a qualified **barrister**.
   中文：她经过多年训练才获准成为一名合格的出庭律师。

3. The **barrister** carefully reviewed all the evidence before presenting the case in court.
   中文：出庭律师在法庭陈述案件之前仔细审查了所有证据。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Barrister" contains "bar" — in legal terms, "the bar" refers to the legal profession. A barrister is someone who has "passed the bar" and can argue cases in court.

拆解记忆："barrister"包含"bar"——在法律术语中，"the bar"指法律界。barrister就是"通过了bar（律师资格考试）"并能在法庭上辩护的人。
