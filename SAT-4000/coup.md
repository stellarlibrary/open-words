# ⚡ coup

🔊 /kuː/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A sudden and decisive seizure of power, especially by a small group; a brilliant and notably successful stroke or action
**中文：** n. 政变；妙计；出乎意料的行动

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- overthrow（推翻）
- takeover（接管）
- feat（壮举）
- stroke（妙招）
- triumph（胜利）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **military coup / coup d'etat / stage a coup / pull off a coup**

👉 Example: The rebels attempted to stage a coup against the incumbent government under the cover of darkness.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The military **coup** toppled the democratically elected government and installed an authoritarian regime that suppressed civil liberties for decades.
   这场军事政变推翻了民主选举产生的政府，建立了一个压制公民自由长达数十年的专制政权。

2. Securing the endorsement of the renowned scientist was considered a major **coup** for the fledgling research institute seeking credibility.
   获得这位著名科学家的支持被认为是这家寻求公信力的新兴研究机构的一大妙招。

3. Historians continue to debate whether the **coup** was driven primarily by ideological conviction or by personal ambition among its leaders.
   历史学家们继续争论这场政变究竟是主要由意识形态信念还是由领导人的个人野心所驱动。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "coup" as a quick "swoop" — a sudden, swift action that seizes control before anyone can react.
把"coup"联想成一次快速的"突袭"——一个突然、迅速的行动，在任何人反应过来之前就夺取了控制权。
