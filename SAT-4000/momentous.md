# 🏛️ momentous

🔊 /moʊˈmɛntəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Of great importance or significance, especially in having a bearing on future events; historically decisive
**中文：** adj. 重要的；重大的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- significant（重大的）
- pivotal（关键的）
- consequential（重要的）
- historic（历史性的）
- epoch-making（划时代的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a momentous decision / a momentous occasion / a momentous event / a momentous day**

👉 Example: The court's ruling proved to be a momentous decision that reshaped the legal landscape for decades to come.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The discovery of penicillin was a **momentous** breakthrough that revolutionized modern medicine and saved countless lives worldwide.
   青霉素的发现是一项重大突破，彻底改变了现代医学并在全球范围内挽救了无数生命。

2. Historians consider the fall of the Berlin Wall to be one of the most **momentous** events of the twentieth century.
   历史学家认为柏林墙的倒塌是二十世纪最重大的事件之一。

3. The board of directors faced a **momentous** choice that would determine the corporation's strategic direction for the coming decade.
   董事会面临一个重大抉择，这将决定公司未来十年的战略方向。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "moment-ous" as "full of moment" — in older English, "moment" meant importance, so momentous means full of great importance.
把"momentous"想成"充满moment（重要性）的"——在古英语中，"moment"有"重要性"之意，所以momentous就是非常重要的。
