# 💰 plunder

🔊 /ˈplʌndər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To rob or steal goods by force, especially during war or civil disorder. (n.) Stolen goods or the act of plundering.
**中文：** v. 掠夺；抢劫；侵吞 n. 抢夺；战利品；掠夺品

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- loot（掠夺）
- pillage（劫掠）
- ransack（洗劫）
- despoil（掠夺）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **plunder a city / plunder resources / plunder wealth / war plunder**

👉 Example: The invading forces proceeded to plunder the coastal villages, seizing valuables and provisions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Colonial powers systematically **plundered** the natural resources of occupied territories for centuries without any accountability.
   殖民列强数百年来系统性地掠夺被占领土的自然资源，而无需承担任何责任。

2. The museum returned ancient artifacts that had been **plundered** from archaeological sites during periods of political instability.
   博物馆归还了在政治动荡时期从考古遗址掠夺的古代文物。

3. Corporate executives were accused of **plundering** the pension fund to finance their own extravagant personal expenditures.
   公司高管被指控侵吞养老基金以资助其个人的奢侈开销。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "plunder" as "plun-der" sounding like "pull under" — thieves pull everything under their control by force.
把"plunder"想成"pull under"（拽到手下）——盗贼用武力把一切抢到自己手中。
