# 🔄 derivative

🔊 /dɪˈrɪvətɪv/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Something derived from another source; a by-product or offshoot. (adj.) Imitative of the work of another; not original
**中文：** n. 衍生物；派生物 adj. 派生的；衍生的；缺乏独创性的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- offshoot（分支）
- by-product（副产品）
- imitative（模仿的）
- unoriginal（非原创的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **derivative work / derivative of / financial derivative / derivative style**

👉 Example: Critics dismissed the novel as derivative, noting its heavy reliance on earlier literary conventions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The financial **derivative** market has grown enormously in complexity, requiring sophisticated models to assess underlying risk accurately.
   金融衍生品市场的复杂性大幅增长，需要精密的模型来准确评估潜在风险。

2. Many scholars argue that his philosophical framework is largely **derivative**, drawing too heavily from earlier existentialist thinkers without innovation.
   许多学者认为他的哲学框架在很大程度上是派生的，过度借鉴了早期存在主义思想家而缺乏创新。

3. This chemical compound is a **derivative** of a naturally occurring substance found exclusively in deep-sea marine organisms.
   这种化合物是一种衍生物，源自仅在深海海洋生物中发现的天然物质。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "derivative" as "derived from another" — like a river that derives its water from a larger source, a derivative comes from something original.
把"derivative"想成"从另一个东西衍生出来的"——就像一条河从更大的水源获得水一样，衍生物来自原始事物。
