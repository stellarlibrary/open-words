# 📝 waive

🔊 /weɪv/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To refrain from insisting on or claiming; to voluntarily give up a right, privilege, or requirement
**中文：** v. 放弃（权利）；搁置；免除

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- relinquish（放弃）
- forgo（放弃）
- renounce（宣布放弃）
- surrender（交出）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **waive a right / waive a fee / waive a requirement / waive a claim**

👉 Example: The university agreed to waive the application fee for students demonstrating financial hardship.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The defendant chose to **waive** his right to a jury trial and instead requested a hearing before the judge.
   被告选择放弃陪审团审判的权利，转而要求由法官进行听证。

2. Several prestigious universities have decided to **waive** standardized testing requirements for the upcoming admissions cycle.
   几所知名大学已决定免除下一个招生周期的标准化考试要求。

3. The landlord agreed to **waive** the late penalty after the tenant explained the circumstances surrounding the delayed payment.
   房东在租户解释了延迟付款的情况后同意免除滞纳金。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "waive" as "wave goodbye" — you wave your hand to say goodbye to a right or fee, letting it go.
把"waive"想成"wave goodbye"——你挥手告别一项权利或费用，将其放弃。
