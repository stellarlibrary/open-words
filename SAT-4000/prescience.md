# 🔭 prescience

🔊 /ˈprɛʃəns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The ability to know or foresee what will happen in the future; foreknowledge of events
**中文：** n. 先见；预知；先见之明

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- foresight（远见）
- foreknowledge（预知）
- clairvoyance（洞察力）
- prognostication（预测）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **remarkable prescience / with great prescience / prescience about / a degree of prescience**

👉 Example: The analyst's prescience about the market crash earned her widespread recognition in the financial world.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The author's **prescience** about technological advances was remarkable, as many predictions in the novel have since become reality.
   这位作者对技术进步的预见力非凡，小说中的许多预测后来都成为了现实。

2. With extraordinary **prescience**, the urban planner designed infrastructure that would accommodate population growth for the next fifty years.
   凭借非凡的先见之明，这位城市规划师设计了可容纳未来五十年人口增长的基础设施。

3. Critics praised the economist's **prescience** in warning about the housing bubble years before the devastating financial crisis unfolded.
   批评家们赞扬这位经济学家的远见，他在毁灭性金融危机爆发前几年就警告了房地产泡沫。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-science" as "science before it happens" — knowing the outcome before the experiment is even conducted.
把"prescience"想成"pre-science"——在实验开始之前就知道结果，即"提前的科学"。
