# 🚫 ostracize

🔊 /ˈɑːstrəsaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To exclude someone from a group or society by common consent; to banish or shun; historically, to banish by popular vote using potsherds.
**中文：** v. 放逐；排斥；按贝壳流放法放逐

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- banish（放逐）
- exclude（排斥）
- shun（回避）
- blackball（投票反对）
- exile（流放）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ostracize from / socially ostracized / ostracize someone for / ostracized by peers**

👉 Example: The community ostracized the whistleblower for exposing corruption within the local government.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. In ancient Athens, citizens could **ostracize** a political leader by inscribing his name on a potsherd and casting it as a vote.
   在古代雅典，公民可以通过在陶片上刻写政治领袖的名字并将其作为投票来放逐他。

2. The researcher was **ostracized** by her academic peers after publishing findings that contradicted the prevailing consensus.
   这位研究人员在发表了与主流共识相矛盾的研究结果后，遭到了学术同行的排斥。

3. Adolescents who are **ostracized** by their classmates often experience lasting psychological effects that persist into adulthood.
   被同学排斥的青少年往往会经历持续到成年的长期心理影响。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "OSTRA-cize" — "ostra" comes from Greek "ostrakon" (potsherd), the clay piece ancient Greeks wrote names on to vote someone out of the city.
把"ostracize"中的"ostra"联想成希腊语"ostrakon"（陶片）——古希腊人在陶片上写名字来投票将某人逐出城邦。
