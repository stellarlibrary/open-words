# 🍃 foliate

🔊 /ˈfoʊliˌeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To split into thin layers; to hammer metal into a thin sheet or leaf. (adj.) Having leaves or leaf-like structures; decorated with leaf-shaped ornaments
**中文：** v. 把…打成箔；分成薄片 adj. 有叶的；叶状的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- leafy（多叶的）
- laminate（分层的）
- verdant（翠绿的）
- leaf-shaped（叶形的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **foliate decoration / foliate pattern / foliate design / foliate capital (in architecture)**

👉 Example: The medieval cathedral featured elaborately foliate carvings along the stone columns of the nave.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The architect incorporated **foliate** motifs into the building's facade, drawing inspiration from classical Gothic ornamentation and natural forms.
   建筑师将**叶状**图案融入建筑的立面，从古典哥特式装饰和自然形态中汲取灵感。

2. Artisans in the workshop learned to **foliate** gold into extremely thin sheets used for gilding sacred manuscripts and religious icons.
   工坊里的工匠学会将黄金**打成**极薄的箔片，用于为神圣手稿和宗教圣像镀金。

3. The **foliate** patterns carved into the ancient temple walls revealed the civilization's deep reverence for nature and botanical symbolism.
   古庙墙壁上雕刻的**叶状**图案揭示了该文明对自然和植物象征的深切敬仰。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "foliate" as containing "foli-" from Latin "folium" meaning "leaf" — foliate things are leaf-like, just as foliage is a mass of leaves.
把"foliate"想成包含"foli-"，来自拉丁语"folium"意为"叶子"——foliate的东西是叶状的，就像foliage是一大片树叶一样。
