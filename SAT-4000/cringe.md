# 😬 cringe

🔊 /krɪndʒ/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To shrink back in fear or disgust; to behave in a servile or excessively flattering way. (n.) An act of cringing
**中文：** v. 畏缩；奉承；阿谀 n. 畏缩

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flinch（退缩）
- cower（畏缩）
- recoil（后退）
- grovel（卑躬屈膝）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cringe at / cringe with embarrassment / cringe in fear / make someone cringe**

👉 Example: The audience collectively cringed at the speaker's deeply offensive and poorly timed remark.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat **cringed** visibly when his colleague made an inappropriate comment during the formal international summit.
   当他的同事在正式的国际峰会上发表不当言论时，这位外交官明显地畏缩了。

2. Many scholars **cringe** at the oversimplification of complex historical events in popular mainstream documentaries.
   许多学者对大众主流纪录片中对复杂历史事件的过度简化感到不适。

3. The subordinate would **cringe** and flatter excessively whenever the authoritarian director entered the conference room.
   每当那位专制的主管走进会议室时，那个下属就会卑躬屈膝、过度奉承。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cringe" sounding like "cr-inge" — you crinkle up and shrink inward, like crumpling paper when something makes you uncomfortable.
把"cringe"想成"皱缩"——你像揉皱纸张一样蜷缩起来，因为某件事让你感到不舒服。
