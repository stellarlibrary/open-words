# 🎭 affectation

🔊 /ˌæfɛkˈteɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Behavior, speech, or writing that is artificial and designed to impress; pretense or false display.
**中文：** 做作；矫揉造作；假装。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pretense（假装）
- pretension（矫饰）
- artificiality（做作）
- mannerism（习性癖好）
- pose（姿态）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mere, obvious, annoying, without, display of**
👉 Example: His use of obscure vocabulary was a transparent affectation rather than genuine erudition.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The critic dismissed the author's elaborate prose style as mere **affectation** lacking any authentic emotional depth.
   评论家认为这位作者华丽的散文风格不过是矫揉造作，缺乏真正的情感深度。

2. She spoke with an **affectation** of a British accent despite having never traveled outside her home country.
   她说话时假装一口英国口音，尽管她从未离开过自己的祖国。

3. His apparent indifference to wealth was widely regarded as an **affectation** by those who knew him personally.
   他对财富表面上的漠不关心被那些私下了解他的人普遍视为一种假装。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Affect" + "-ation" — an affectation is the act of "affecting" (putting on) a false appearance.
"affect"（假装）+"-ation"（行为后缀）——affectation就是"假装"出虚假外表的行为。
