# 🆘 recourse

🔊 /ˈriːkɔːrs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A source of help in a difficult situation; the act of turning to someone or something for assistance; a legal right to demand payment
**中文：** n. 求援，求助；追索权；依赖

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- resort（求助）
- refuge（避难）
- remedy（补救办法）
- appeal（诉求）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **have recourse to / without recourse / legal recourse / last recourse**

👉 Example: Citizens who feel wronged by the decision have legal recourse through the appellate court system.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Without any legal **recourse**, the displaced tenants were forced to seek assistance from nonprofit advocacy organizations.
   由于没有任何法律追索权，这些被迫搬迁的租户不得不向非营利倡导组织寻求帮助。

2. The committee determined that diplomatic negotiation was the only viable **recourse** to resolve the international trade dispute.
   委员会认定外交谈判是解决这一国际贸易争端的唯一可行求助途径。

3. Students who believe their grades were calculated unfairly have **recourse** to a formal academic appeals process.
   认为成绩计算不公的学生可以通过正式的学术申诉程序寻求救济。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-course" as "running the course again" — when you hit a dead end, you go back and find a new course of action to seek help.
把"recourse"想成"re-course"——当你走到死胡同时，你重新找一条路，也就是寻求帮助。
