# ⚖️ quorum

🔊 /ˈkwɔːrəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The minimum number of members required to be present for an assembly to conduct official business or make valid decisions
**中文：** n. 法定人数；选出的团体

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- majority（多数）
- minimum number（最低人数）
- threshold（门槛）
- required attendance（法定出席数）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reach a quorum / constitute a quorum / lack a quorum / quorum of members**

👉 Example: The board meeting was postponed because they failed to reach a quorum of voting members.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The legislative session could not proceed because the chamber lacked a **quorum**, with fewer than half the representatives present.
   立法会议无法进行，因为议院缺乏法定人数，到场的代表不到一半。

2. After considerable delay, enough delegates finally arrived to establish a **quorum** and begin the formal voting procedures.
   经过相当长时间的延迟，终于有足够的代表到达以构成法定人数并开始正式投票程序。

3. The organization's bylaws stipulate that a **quorum** of two-thirds of all registered members is necessary for constitutional amendments.
   该组织的章程规定，修改宪章需要全体注册成员三分之二的法定人数。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "quorum" as "core-um" — you need a core group of people present before any official decisions can be made.
把"quorum"想成"core-um"——你需要一个核心人数到场，才能做出任何正式决定。
