# 🏛️ aqueduct

🔊 /ˈækwɪdʌkt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A large structure, often resembling a bridge, designed to carry water across a valley or other gap in the landscape
**中文：** n. 渡槽；导水管；沟渠

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conduit（管道）
- channel（水渠）
- watercourse（水道）
- canal（运河）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **Roman aqueduct / build an aqueduct / ancient aqueduct / aqueduct system**

👉 Example: The Romans built extensive aqueducts to supply fresh water to their cities.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient **aqueduct** spanning the valley has endured for over two thousand years as a marvel of engineering.
   横跨山谷的古老渡槽已经屹立了两千多年，堪称工程奇迹。

2. Engineers designed a modern **aqueduct** to transport water from the mountain reservoir to the drought-stricken coastal region.
   工程师们设计了一座现代渡槽，将水从山区水库输送到受干旱侵袭的沿海地区。

3. The crumbling **aqueduct** was carefully restored by archaeologists who sought to preserve its historical and cultural significance.
   考古学家精心修复了这座日渐破损的渡槽，以保护其历史和文化意义。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Break it into "aqua" (water) + "duct" (to lead) — an aqueduct literally leads water from one place to another.
把"aqueduct"拆成"aqua"（水）+"duct"（引导）——渡槽的本义就是把水从一个地方引导到另一个地方。
