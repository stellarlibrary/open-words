# 🌀 precession

🔊 /prɪˈseʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The slow, conical movement of the axis of a spinning body; the act of preceding or going forward.
**中文：** n. 岁差；进动；先行；领先

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rotation（旋转）
- wobble（摆动）
- oscillation（振荡）
- gyration（回旋）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **axial precession / precession of the equinoxes / gyroscopic precession / precession cycle**

👉 Example: The precession of the equinoxes is responsible for the gradual shift in the position of constellations over millennia.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **precession** of Earth's rotational axis completes a full cycle approximately every twenty-six thousand years.
   地球自转轴的岁差大约每两万六千年完成一个完整的周期。

2. Astronomers must account for **precession** when calculating the precise coordinates of distant celestial objects over time.
   天文学家在计算遥远天体随时间变化的精确坐标时，必须考虑岁差。

3. The physicist demonstrated gyroscopic **precession** by showing how a spinning top tilts and traces a circular path.
   物理学家通过展示陀螺如何倾斜并划出圆形轨迹来演示回旋进动。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-cession" as a "procession" that goes before — the Earth's axis slowly parades in a circle ahead of its original position.
把"precession"想成一个"走在前面的队列"——地球的轴在其原始位置前方缓慢地画圈行进。
