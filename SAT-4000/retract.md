# ↩️ retract

🔊 /rɪˈtrækt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To draw back or withdraw; to take back a statement, accusation, or promise; to pull inward
**中文：** v. 缩回；缩进；取消；收回；撤销

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- withdraw（撤回）
- recant（撤回）
- revoke（撤销）
- rescind（废除）
- disavow（否认）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **retract a statement / retract an offer / retract a claim / retract landing gear**

👉 Example: The newspaper was compelled to retract its earlier claims after new evidence disproved the allegations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Under mounting legal pressure, the journalist was forced to **retract** the controversial allegations published in the previous edition.
   在越来越大的法律压力下，这位记者被迫撤回上一期刊登的争议性指控。

2. The university decided to **retract** the researcher's published paper after an investigation revealed significant data fabrication.
   在调查揭示了严重的数据造假后，大学决定撤回该研究人员已发表的论文。

3. The cat instinctively **retracted** its claws after the veterinarian gently examined and treated the injured paw.
   在兽医轻柔地检查和治疗受伤的爪子后，猫本能地缩回了它的爪子。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-TRACT" — "tract" means "pull" (from Latin "trahere") — to pull back something you said or did, like reeling in a fishing line.
把"retract"想成"re-TRACT"——"tract"意为"拉"（源自拉丁语"trahere"）——把你说过或做过的事拉回来，就像收回钓鱼线一样。
