# 😟 untoward

🔊 /ˌʌntəˈwɔːrd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Unexpected and inappropriate or inconvenient; unfavorable; difficult to manage or guide
**中文：** 不幸的；麻烦的；倔强的；出乎意料的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unfavorable（不利的）
- adverse（不利的）
- inauspicious（不吉利的）
- troublesome（麻烦的）
- unfortunate（不幸的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **untoward incident / untoward event / untoward consequences / nothing untoward**

👉 Example: The inspection revealed nothing untoward, and the facility was granted full operational clearance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The security team conducted a thorough sweep of the premises but found nothing **untoward** prior to the dignitary's arrival.
   安全团队在要人到来之前对场所进行了彻底的搜查，但未发现任何异常情况。

2. Several **untoward** developments in the supply chain forced the manufacturer to delay the product launch by three months.
   供应链中几个不利的发展迫使制造商将产品发布推迟了三个月。

3. The ambassador assured the press that the negotiations had proceeded smoothly and that no **untoward** incidents had occurred.
   大使向媒体保证谈判进展顺利，没有发生任何不愉快的事件。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "un-toward" as "not going toward the right direction" — something untoward moves away from what is expected or desired, bringing trouble.
把"untoward"想成"un（不）+ toward（朝向）"——不朝正确方向发展的事情，偏离了预期或期望，带来了麻烦。
