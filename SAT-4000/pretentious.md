# 🦚 pretentious

🔊 /prɪˈtɛnʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Attempting to impress by affecting greater importance, talent, or culture than is actually possessed; showy or ostentatious
**中文：** adj. 自命不凡的；炫耀的；做作的；自负的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ostentatious（炫耀的）
- pompous（自大的）
- affected（做作的）
- grandiose（浮夸的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **pretentious behavior / pretentious language / sound pretentious / overly pretentious**

👉 Example: The film critic dismissed the movie as pretentious for using obscure symbolism that added nothing to the narrative.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The reviewer described the novel as **pretentious**, arguing that its deliberately obscure prose served only to confuse rather than enlighten.
   评论家认为这部小说矫揉造作，指出其故意晦涩的文风只会让人困惑而非启发。

2. Many patrons found the new restaurant unbearably **pretentious**, with unnecessarily elaborate descriptions for remarkably simple dishes on the menu.
   许多顾客觉得这家新餐厅令人难以忍受地做作，菜单上对极其简单的菜肴用了不必要的华丽描述。

3. The architect avoided **pretentious** design elements, favoring clean lines and functional spaces that served the community's actual needs.
   这位建筑师避免了浮夸的设计元素，偏好简洁的线条和功能性空间来满足社区的实际需求。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pretent-ious" as "pretend + ious" — someone who is full of pretending, always acting like they are more important or cultured than they really are.
把"pretentious"想成"pretend + ious"——一个充满"假装"的人，总是表现得比实际上更重要或更有文化。
