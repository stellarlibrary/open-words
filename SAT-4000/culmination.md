# ⛰️ culmination

🔊 /ˌkʌlmɪˈneɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The highest point or climax of something; the final result of a process or development
**中文：** n. 顶点；高潮

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- climax（高潮）
- apex（顶点）
- pinnacle（巅峰）
- zenith（最高点）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **the culmination of / reach a culmination / culmination of years of work / culmination of efforts**

👉 Example: The graduation ceremony was the culmination of four years of dedicated study and hard work.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The peace treaty represented the **culmination** of decades of diplomatic negotiations between the two rival nations.
   该和平条约代表了两个敌对国家数十年外交谈判的最终成果。

2. The successful satellite launch was the **culmination** of years of collaborative research among international scientists.
   卫星的成功发射是国际科学家多年合作研究的顶点。

3. Her appointment as chief justice marked the **culmination** of a distinguished and exemplary legal career spanning thirty years.
   她被任命为首席大法官标志着她三十年杰出法律生涯的巅峰。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "culmin-ation" as related to "column" — imagine climbing to the top of a tall column to reach the highest point.
把"culmination"联想到"column（柱子）"——想象攀登到高柱的顶端，到达最高点。
