# 🎭 grandiloquent

🔊 /ɡrænˈdɪləkwənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Pompous or extravagant in language, style, or manner; using lofty or inflated speech to impress
**中文：** adj. 夸张的，浮夸的，华而不实的；大言不惭的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pompous（浮夸的）
- bombastic（夸大的）
- pretentious（自命不凡的）
- rhetorical（辞藻华丽的）
- grandiose（堂皇的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **grandiloquent speech / grandiloquent language / grandiloquent rhetoric / grandiloquent style**

👉 Example: The senator's grandiloquent rhetoric failed to mask the superficiality of his proposed policy reforms.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The critic dismissed the novel's **grandiloquent** prose, arguing that its ornate language obscured rather than enhanced the narrative's meaning.
   评论家对这部小说浮夸的散文不屑一顾，认为其华丽的语言模糊而非增强了叙事的意义。

2. His **grandiloquent** acceptance speech at the awards ceremony drew polite applause but quietly amused the more discerning audience members.
   他在颁奖典礼上夸张的获奖感言赢得了礼貌的掌声，但却暗暗逗乐了更有鉴赏力的观众。

3. Effective academic writing avoids **grandiloquent** expressions and instead favors clarity, precision, and logical coherence in argumentation.
   有效的学术写作避免华而不实的表达，转而注重论证中的清晰、精确和逻辑连贯。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "grandiloquent" as "grand + eloquent" — someone who tries too hard to be eloquent, making their speech sound overly grand and pompous.
把"grandiloquent"想成"grand（宏大的）+ eloquent（雄辩的）"——一个过于努力想显得雄辩的人，使得他的讲话听起来过分浮夸和自大。
