# 🎯 occasion

🔊 /əˈkeɪʒən/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A particular time or event; a suitable opportunity; a reason or cause. (v.) To cause or bring about
**中文：** n. 时机，机会；场合；时节；理由 v. 引起，惹起

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- event（事件）
- opportunity（机会）
- cause（引起）
- circumstance（情况）
- prompt（促使）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **on occasion / occasion for celebration / rise to the occasion / occasion surprise**

👉 Example: The diplomatic summit provided an occasion for the two leaders to discuss bilateral trade agreements.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The centennial anniversary of the university provided a fitting **occasion** to reflect upon its distinguished academic legacy.
   大学百年校庆为回顾其杰出的学术传统提供了一个恰当的场合。

2. The sudden resignation of the director **occasioned** widespread speculation about internal conflicts within the organization's leadership.
   主任的突然辞职引起了人们对该组织领导层内部冲突的广泛猜测。

3. On rare **occasion**, the reclusive author would grant interviews to discuss the philosophical themes underlying her acclaimed novels.
   在少数场合下，这位深居简出的作家会接受采访，讨论她备受赞誉的小说中的哲学主题。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "occasion" as "oc-CASE-ion" — a special case or situation that arises, giving you an opportunity or causing something to happen.
把"occasion"想成"oc-CASE-ion"——一个出现的特殊情况（case），给你一个机会或导致某事发生。
