# 🔪 whet

🔊 /wɛt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To sharpen a blade or tool; to stimulate or excite interest, appetite, or desire. (n.) The act of sharpening; something that stimulates
**中文：** v. 磨快；刺激；促进；兴奋 n. 磨；开胃物；刺激物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sharpen（磨快）
- stimulate（刺激）
- kindle（激发）
- arouse（唤起）
- excite（激发）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **whet one's appetite / whet curiosity / whet interest / whet a blade**

👉 Example: The opening chapter of the novel was designed to whet the reader's appetite for the mysteries that followed.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The compelling documentary served to **whet** public curiosity about the unexplored regions of the deep ocean floor.
   这部引人入胜的纪录片激发了公众对深海海底未开发区域的好奇心。

2. The professor's introductory lecture was carefully crafted to **whet** the students' appetite for the advanced material ahead.
   教授的导论讲座经过精心设计，以激发学生们对后续高级内容的兴趣。

3. Early archaeological discoveries at the site **whetted** the enthusiasm of researchers who then secured additional funding for excavation.
   该遗址早期的考古发现激发了研究人员的热情，他们随后获得了额外的发掘资金。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "whet" as sharpening a knife before a meal — it sharpens both the blade and your appetite, making you eager for more.
把"whet"想成饭前磨刀——它既磨利了刀刃，也磨利了你的食欲，让你渴望更多。
