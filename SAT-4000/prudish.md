# 😳 prudish

🔊 /ˈpruːdɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Having or showing an excessively proper or modest attitude, especially regarding matters of sexuality or propriety
**中文：** adj. 装正经的；过分规矩的；过分拘谨的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- priggish（假正经的）
- puritanical（清教徒式的）
- strait-laced（古板的）
- prim（拘谨的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prudish attitude / prudish about something / overly prudish / prudish sensibilities**

👉 Example: Critics dismissed the censorship board as overly prudish in its objections to the award-winning film.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Some readers considered the literary critic's objections to the novel's language unnecessarily **prudish** and out of touch with modern standards.
   一些读者认为文学评论家对小说语言的反对过于拘谨，与现代标准脱节。

2. The Victorian era is often characterized as a **prudish** period when even the mention of certain topics was deemed socially unacceptable.
   维多利亚时代通常被描述为一个过分拘谨的时期，甚至提及某些话题都被认为是社会上不可接受的。

3. Her **prudish** reaction to the comedian's mildly irreverent humor surprised everyone who knew her relaxed personality.
   她对喜剧演员略显不敬的幽默的过分拘谨反应让所有了解她轻松个性的人都感到惊讶。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "prudish" as "prude + ish" — someone who is "prude-ish" acts excessively proper, as if always blushing at anything remotely improper.
把"prudish"想成"prude + ish"——一个"假正经式的"人行为过分得体，仿佛对任何稍有不当的事情都会脸红。
