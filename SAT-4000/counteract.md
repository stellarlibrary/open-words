# 🛡️ counteract

🔊 /ˌkaʊntərˈækt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To act against something in order to reduce its force or neutralize it; to hinder or oppose effectively
**中文：** v. 抵消；中和；阻碍

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- neutralize（中和）
- offset（抵消）
- negate（使无效）
- mitigate（缓解）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **counteract the effects / counteract a threat / counteract the influence / counteract the impact**

👉 Example: The medication was designed to counteract the harmful effects of the toxin in the bloodstream.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government introduced new regulations to **counteract** the negative environmental impact of rapid industrial expansion in the region.
   政府出台了新法规以抵消该地区快速工业扩张对环境的负面影响。

2. Scientists developed an innovative compound that could effectively **counteract** the venom of several previously untreatable snake species.
   科学家研发了一种创新化合物，能够有效中和几种先前无法治疗的蛇毒。

3. The central bank raised interest rates to **counteract** the inflationary pressures that had been steadily eroding consumer purchasing power.
   央行提高利率以抵消一直在稳步侵蚀消费者购买力的通胀压力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "counter-act" as "acting counter" — performing an action that goes against and cancels out another force.
把"counteract"想成"counter（对抗）+ act（行动）"——采取对抗性行动来抵消另一种力量。
