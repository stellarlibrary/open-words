# ➕ add

🔊 /æd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To join or combine something with another so as to increase the size, number, or amount; to put together numbers to calculate their total
**中文：** v. 加；增加；加起来；做加法；补充说

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- append（附加）
- supplement（补充）
- augment（增加）
- incorporate（纳入）
- attach（附上）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **add to / add up / add value / add detail / add weight to**

👉 Example: The researcher decided to add a new chapter to address the reviewers' concerns.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee voted to **add** several provisions to the bill before sending it to the full senate.
   委员会投票决定在将法案提交参议院全体会议之前增加若干条款。

2. These findings **add** considerable weight to the argument that early intervention yields better outcomes.
   这些发现为早期干预能产生更好结果的论点增添了相当大的分量。

3. The director plans to **add** three new departments to the institute over the next fiscal year.
   主任计划在下一个财政年度为该研究所增设三个新部门。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "add" as placing one thing on top of another — from Latin "addere" meaning "to put to." When you add, you are literally putting more onto what already exists.
把"add"想成把一个东西放到另一个东西上面——源自拉丁语"addere"，意为"放上去"。当你add时，你就是在已有的基础上再放更多。
