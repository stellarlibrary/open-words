# 🎭 ostensible

🔊 /ɑːˈstensəbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Stated or appearing to be true, but not necessarily so; seeming; put forward to conceal the real purpose or reason.
**中文：** adj. 表面的；假装的；虚假的；可公开的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- apparent（表面上的）
- seeming（看似的）
- professed（声称的）
- purported（据称的）
- superficial（表面的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ostensible reason / ostensible purpose / ostensible goal / ostensible motive**

👉 Example: The ostensible purpose of the meeting was to discuss budgets, but the real agenda was quite different.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **ostensible** reason for the military intervention was humanitarian aid, though critics suspected geopolitical motives were at play.
   军事干预的表面原因是人道主义援助，但批评人士怀疑背后有地缘政治动机。

2. Her **ostensible** enthusiasm for the project masked a deep reluctance to commit her department's limited resources.
   她对该项目表面上的热情掩盖了她不愿投入部门有限资源的深层抵触。

3. The **ostensible** leader of the organization held no real authority, as all major decisions were made behind closed doors.
   该组织的名义领导人并无实权，因为所有重大决定都是在幕后做出的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "os-TEN-sible" — something you "TEN-d to show" on the surface, but the truth lies hidden underneath.
把"ostensible"中的"TEN"联想成"tend to show"（倾向于展示）——表面上展示出来的，但真相隐藏在下面。
