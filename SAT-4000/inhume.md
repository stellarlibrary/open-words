# ⚰️ inhume

🔊 /ɪnˈhjuːm/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To place a dead body in a grave or tomb; to bury
**中文：** v. 埋葬；土葬

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bury（埋葬）
- inter（安葬）
- entomb（入墓）
- sepulcher（埋入坟墓）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inhume the remains / inhume the body / inhume in a cemetery / inhumed with honors**

👉 Example: The fallen soldiers were inhumed with full military honors at the national cemetery.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Ancient civilizations often **inhumed** their dead with valuable artifacts believed to be necessary for the afterlife journey.
   古代文明通常将死者与被认为是来世旅程所需的贵重物品一起埋葬。

2. The archaeologists carefully excavated the site where tribal leaders had been **inhumed** over three thousand years ago.
   考古学家仔细发掘了三千多年前部落首领被埋葬的遗址。

3. Local regulations required that all remains be **inhumed** within designated cemetery grounds rather than on private property.
   当地法规要求所有遗体必须埋葬在指定的墓地范围内，而非私人土地上。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-hume" as putting someone "in the humus" — humus is the organic layer of soil, so to inhume means to place into the earth.
把"inhume"想成把人放"in the humus（腐殖质）"——腐殖质是土壤的有机层，所以inhume就是放入大地中，即埋葬。
