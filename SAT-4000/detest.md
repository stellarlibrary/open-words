# 😤 detest

🔊 /dɪˈtɛst/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To dislike intensely; to feel strong aversion or hatred toward someone or something
**中文：** v. 厌恶；憎恨

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- abhor（憎恶）
- loathe（厌恶）
- despise（鄙视）
- abominate（痛恨）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **detest someone / detest something / utterly detest / detest the idea of**

👉 Example: She utterly detested dishonesty and refused to associate with those who practiced deception.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Many citizens openly **detest** corruption in government and have organized large-scale protests demanding institutional reform and accountability.
   许多公民公开憎恨政府腐败，并组织了大规模抗议活动，要求制度改革和问责。

2. The renowned author was known to **detest** superficial literary criticism that ignored the deeper thematic complexities of her work.
   这位著名作家以厌恶肤浅的文学批评而闻名，那些批评忽视了她作品中更深层的主题复杂性。

3. Although he professed to **detest** public speaking, the professor delivered his farewell lecture with remarkable eloquence and composure.
   尽管他声称厌恶公开演讲，这位教授还是以非凡的口才和沉着发表了告别演说。

---

把"detest"想成"de-test"——你太讨厌考试了，想要"de-test"（去掉所有考试）。
