# 💫 prepossess

🔊 /ˌpriːpəˈzɛs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To inspire a favorable impression in advance; to preoccupy the mind with a bias or prejudice; to possess beforehand
**中文：** vt. 使先有好感；使偏爱；预先灌输情感；使先怀偏见；预先拥有

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- charm（迷住）
- captivate（吸引）
- bias（使偏向）
- prejudice（使产生偏见）
- impress（给…留下深刻印象）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prepossessing appearance / prepossessed in favor of / prepossess an audience / not prepossessing**

👉 Example: Her calm, articulate manner immediately prepossessed the interview panel in her favor.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The candidate's confident demeanor and eloquent speech **prepossessed** the selection committee well before the formal evaluation had begun.
   这位候选人自信的举止和雄辩的演讲在正式评估开始前就给了遴选委员会良好的印象。

2. Critics warned that media coverage had **prepossessed** the public against the defendant, making an impartial trial exceedingly difficult.
   批评人士警告说，媒体报道已使公众对被告产生了偏见，使公正审判变得极其困难。

3. The old manor, though lacking a **prepossessing** exterior, contained an extraordinary collection of Renaissance art and rare manuscripts.
   这座古老的庄园虽然外观不怎么吸引人，却收藏了非凡的文艺复兴时期艺术品和珍贵手稿。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-possess" as "possessing someone's mind before" they form their own opinion — creating a favorable bias in advance.
把"prepossess"想成"pre（预先）+ possess（占有）"——预先占有某人的心思，即让人先有好感或偏见。
