# 💨 dissipate

🔊 /ˈdɪsɪpeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To scatter or vanish gradually; to squander or waste resources, energy, or opportunities
**中文：** vi. 消散 vt. 使消散；浪费；挥霍

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- scatter（消散）
- disperse（分散）
- squander（浪费）
- vanish（消失）
- waste（挥霍）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dissipate energy / dissipate heat / dissipate a fortune / dissipate fears**

👉 Example: The morning fog began to dissipate as the sun climbed higher above the eastern horizon.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The initial public enthusiasm for the reform proposal began to **dissipate** as implementation difficulties became increasingly apparent.
   随着实施困难变得越来越明显，公众对改革提案最初的热情开始消散。

2. The young heir managed to **dissipate** his entire family fortune through years of reckless spending and poor investments.
   这位年轻的继承人通过多年的挥霍无度和糟糕的投资，将全部家产挥霍殆尽。

3. Engineers designed the cooling system specifically to **dissipate** excess thermal energy generated by the high-performance computing processors.
   工程师们专门设计了冷却系统，以消散高性能计算处理器产生的多余热能。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-sip-ate" — imagine taking small "sips" until everything is gone, gradually dissipating all resources.
把"dissipate"想成"dis-sip-ate"——想象一小口一小口地喝，直到一切都消失，逐渐消散所有资源。
