# 🦴 fossil

🔊 /ˈfɑːsəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The preserved remains or traces of organisms from a past geological age. (adj.) Of or relating to fossils; outdated or antiquated
**中文：** n. 化石 adj. 化石的；陈腐的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- remnant（残余物）
- relic（遗物）
- petrification（石化物）
- vestige（遗迹）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fossil fuels / fossil record / fossil remains / living fossil / fossil evidence**

👉 Example: Scientists analyzed the fossil record to trace the evolutionary history of marine species.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The paleontologists unearthed a remarkably well-preserved **fossil** that provided new insights into dinosaur behavior during the Cretaceous period.
   古生物学家出土了一块保存极为完好的化石，为了解白垩纪恐龙行为提供了新的见解。

2. Global efforts to reduce dependence on **fossil** fuels have accelerated as nations commit to ambitious carbon neutrality targets.
   随着各国致力于实现雄心勃勃的碳中和目标，全球减少对化石燃料依赖的努力已经加速。

3. The discovery of a rare **fossil** in the remote sedimentary formation challenged existing theories about early vertebrate migration patterns.
   在偏远沉积地层中发现的一块罕见化石挑战了关于早期脊椎动物迁徙模式的现有理论。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fossil" as coming from Latin "fossilis" meaning "dug up" — fossils are ancient remains dug up from the earth.
把"fossil"想成来自拉丁语"fossilis"，意思是"挖掘出来的"——化石就是从地下挖掘出来的远古遗骸。
