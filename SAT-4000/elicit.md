# 🎣 elicit

🔊 /ɪˈlɪsɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To draw out or bring forth a response, reaction, or information from someone; to evoke or extract
**中文：** v. 抽出；引出；引起；得出

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- evoke（引起）
- extract（提取）
- provoke（激发）
- prompt（促使）
- draw out（引出）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **elicit a response / elicit information / elicit sympathy / elicit support / elicit from**

👉 Example: The lawyer's careful questioning was designed to elicit crucial information from the reluctant witness.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher designed the survey questions specifically to **elicit** honest and thoughtful responses from all participants.
   研究人员专门设计了调查问卷，以引出所有参与者诚实而深思熟虑的回答。

2. The professor's provocative lecture on ethics managed to **elicit** a spirited and intellectually rigorous debate among students.
   教授关于伦理学的挑衅性讲座成功地引发了学生之间一场激烈而严谨的辩论。

3. Humanitarian organizations often use compelling narratives to **elicit** public sympathy and secure generous donations for relief efforts.
   人道主义组织经常使用引人注目的叙事来引起公众同情并为救援工作获得慷慨的捐款。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "elicit" as "e-licit" — you lure (licit sounds like "lure-it") a response out, drawing hidden information into the open.
把"elicit"想成"e-licit"——你引诱出一个回应，将隐藏的信息引出到明面上。
