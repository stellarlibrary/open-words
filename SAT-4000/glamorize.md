# ✨ glamorize

🔊 /ˈɡlæməˌraɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make something appear glamorous or more attractive than it really is; to romanticize or idealize
**中文：** v. 美化；使有魅力

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- romanticize（浪漫化）
- idealize（理想化）
- embellish（美化）
- glorify（美化）
- beautify（美化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **glamorize violence / glamorize war / glamorize a lifestyle / glamorize the past**

👉 Example: Critics argue that the film tends to glamorize criminal behavior rather than portray its consequences.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The documentary was criticized for attempting to **glamorize** a dangerous lifestyle without addressing its devastating consequences.
   这部纪录片因试图美化一种危险的生活方式而未提及其毁灭性后果而受到批评。

2. Advertising campaigns that **glamorize** excessive consumption have raised serious ethical concerns among public health advocates.
   美化过度消费的广告活动在公共卫生倡导者中引发了严重的伦理关切。

3. Historians warn against the tendency to **glamorize** colonial expansion while ignoring the suffering it inflicted on indigenous populations.
   历史学家警告不要美化殖民扩张的倾向，同时忽视其对原住民造成的苦难。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "glamorize" as "glamour + ize" — to add glamour to something, making it sparkle more than it deserves.
把"glamorize"想成"glamour（魅力）+ ize（使……化）"——给某物添加魅力，使它比实际更光鲜。
