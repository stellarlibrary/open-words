# 🎸 chord

🔊 /kɔːrd/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (Music) A group of notes sounded together in harmony. (Mathematics/Geometry) A straight line connecting two points on a curve or circle
**中文：** n. 和弦；弦

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- harmony（和声）
- triad（三和弦）
- accord（和谐）
- line segment（线段）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **strike a chord / chord progression / major chord / minor chord / vocal chords (note: anatomically "cords")**

👉 Example: The opening chord of the symphony struck a powerful emotional chord with every listener in the hall.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The pianist played a haunting minor **chord** that resonated through the concert hall and captivated the entire audience.
   钢琴家弹奏了一个萦绕人心的小调**和弦**，回荡在音乐厅中，吸引了全场观众。

2. In geometry, a **chord** is defined as a line segment whose endpoints both lie on the circumference of a circle.
   在几何学中，**弦**被定义为两个端点都在圆周上的线段。

3. The speaker's remarks about perseverance struck a deep **chord** with graduates facing an uncertain economic landscape.
   演讲者关于坚持不懈的言论深深引起了面对不确定经济前景的毕业生们的**共鸣**。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "chord" as "c + horde" — a horde (group) of notes played together creates a chord in music.
把"chord"想成"c + horde"——一群（horde）音符一起演奏就构成了音乐中的和弦。
