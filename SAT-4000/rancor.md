# 😠 rancor

🔊 /ˈræŋkər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Deep-seated, bitter resentment or ill will; long-lasting hatred or animosity
**中文：** n. 深仇；怨恨；敌意

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- resentment（怨恨）
- animosity（敌意）
- bitterness（苦涩）
- hostility（敌对）
- malice（恶意）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **without rancor / deep rancor / harbor rancor / rancor toward**

👉 Example: Despite the painful divorce, she spoke of her former partner without any visible rancor or bitterness.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The political debate was conducted with such intense **rancor** that productive dialogue between the two parties became virtually impossible.
   政治辩论充满了如此强烈的怨恨，以至于两党之间的建设性对话几乎变得不可能。

2. Years of unresolved grievances had bred deep **rancor** between the neighboring communities, threatening the region's long-term stability.
   多年未解决的不满在相邻社区之间滋生了深深的怨恨，威胁着该地区的长期稳定。

3. The historian noted that the treaty was signed without **rancor**, marking a rare moment of genuine reconciliation between former enemies.
   历史学家指出，该条约的签署没有怨恨，标志着前敌人之间真正和解的罕见时刻。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rancor" as "rank + core" — at the core of your feelings is something rank and foul, a deep festering resentment.
把"rancor"想成"rank + core"——在你情感的核心是某种腐臭的东西，一种深深的、溃烂的怨恨。
