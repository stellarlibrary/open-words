# 🍯 cajole

🔊 /kəˈdʒoʊl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To persuade someone by sustained coaxing, flattery, or sweet talk; to wheedle or entice through ingratiation
**中文：** 以甜言蜜语哄骗；诱骗；用花言巧语劝诱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- coax（哄劝）
- wheedle（花言巧语哄骗）
- flatter（奉承）
- beguile（诱骗）
- inveigle（诱骗）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cajole someone into doing something / cajole someone out of something / cajole with flattery**

👉 Example: The lobbyist attempted to cajole the senator into supporting the controversial amendment through persistent flattery.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The salesman tried to **cajole** reluctant customers into purchasing the premium package by offering exaggerated promises of value.
   推销员试图用夸大的价值承诺以甜言蜜语哄骗犹豫不决的客户购买高级套餐。

2. Despite her attempts to **cajole** the committee, the proposal was ultimately rejected on the grounds of insufficient evidence.
   尽管她试图用花言巧语说服委员会，但该提案最终因证据不足而被否决。

3. Parents sometimes **cajole** their children into eating vegetables by disguising them in more palatable and appealing dishes.
   父母有时通过将蔬菜伪装成更可口诱人的菜肴来哄骗孩子吃蔬菜。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cajole" as "ca-JEWEL" — offering someone a jewel (flattery) to get what you want, sweet-talking them into agreement.
把"cajole"想成"ca-JEWEL（珠宝）"——用珠宝般的甜言蜜语来获取你想要的东西，哄骗对方同意。
