# 🌧 misfortune

🔊 /ˌmɪsˈfɔːrtʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Bad luck; an unfortunate event, condition, or circumstance; a disaster or calamity
**中文：** n. 不幸；灾祸，灾难

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- adversity（逆境）
- calamity（灾难）
- hardship（困苦）
- tribulation（苦难）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **suffer misfortune / a stroke of misfortune / have the misfortune to / bring misfortune**

👉 Example: She had the misfortune of losing her entire investment during the financial crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The family endured great **misfortune** when a devastating flood destroyed their home and all of their possessions.
   当一场毁灭性的洪水摧毁了他们的家园和所有财产时，这个家庭遭受了巨大的不幸。

2. Through sheer determination, the entrepreneur overcame every **misfortune** and ultimately built a thriving international business enterprise.
   凭借坚定的决心，这位企业家克服了每一次不幸，最终建立了一个蓬勃发展的国际商业企业。

3. The historian argued that the nation's **misfortune** stemmed largely from decades of ineffective governance and systemic corruption.
   这位历史学家认为，该国的不幸主要源于数十年的无效治理和系统性腐败。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mis-fortune" as "missed fortune" — your good fortune missed you, so you ended up with bad luck instead.
把"misfortune"想成"missed fortune（错过的好运）"——好运与你擦肩而过，所以你遭遇了厄运。
