# 😖 contort

🔊 /kənˈtɔːrt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To twist or bend out of normal shape; to distort or misrepresent the meaning of something
**中文：** v. 扭曲；歪曲；曲解

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- distort（扭曲）
- twist（扭转）
- warp（弯曲）
- deform（使变形）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **contort one's face / contort the body / contort the truth / contorted with pain**

👉 Example: The gymnast was able to contort her body into seemingly impossible positions with remarkable ease.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The intense pain caused his face to **contort** into an expression that alarmed everyone present in the examination room.
   剧烈的疼痛使他的脸扭曲成一种让检查室里所有人都感到担忧的表情。

2. Politicians sometimes **contort** the facts to suit their narrative, making it difficult for voters to discern the truth.
   政客有时会歪曲事实以迎合他们的叙事，使选民难以辨别真相。

3. The metal beams had been **contorted** beyond recognition by the tremendous force of the earthquake that struck the region.
   该地区遭受的强烈地震将金属梁扭曲得面目全非。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con-tort" as "totally tortured twist" — when something is contorted, it looks as though it has been tortured into an unnatural shape.
把"contort"想成"con（完全）+ tort（扭转）"——被扭曲的东西看起来像是被折磨成了不自然的形状。
