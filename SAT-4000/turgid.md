# 🎈 turgid

🔊 /ˈtɜːrdʒɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Swollen and distended; (of language or style) tediously pompous or bombastic; excessively ornate or complex
**中文：** 肿胀的；浮夸的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- swollen（肿胀的）
- bombastic（浮夸的）
- pompous（自大的）
- inflated（膨胀的）
- grandiloquent（夸大的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **turgid prose / turgid style / turgid language / turgid writing**

👉 Example: The reviewer criticized the novel for its turgid prose that obscured an otherwise compelling narrative.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's **turgid** lectures were so overloaded with jargon that even advanced students struggled to extract meaningful content.
   教授浮夸的讲座充斥着大量术语，以至于连高年级学生都难以从中提取有意义的内容。

2. After the heavy rainfall, the **turgid** river swelled beyond its banks and threatened the surrounding agricultural communities.
   大雨过后，肿胀的河流涨出了河岸，威胁着周围的农业社区。

3. Critics dismissed the politician's memoir as a **turgid** exercise in self-promotion, lacking both clarity and genuine insight.
   评论家们认为这位政客的回忆录是一篇浮夸的自我推销之作，既缺乏清晰性也缺乏真正的洞察力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "turgid" as "t-ur-gid" — sounds like "too rigid" and swollen, like an overinflated balloon about to burst with pompous hot air.
把"turgid"想成发音像"too rigid"——又僵硬又膨胀，就像一个充满浮夸热气的即将爆裂的气球。
