# 😏 taunt

🔊 /tɔːnt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb, adjective | 名词, 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A remark made to anger, wound, or provoke someone. (v.) To provoke or challenge with insulting remarks; to mock or ridicule. (adj.) Very tall
**中文：** v. 嘲弄，奚落

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- mock（嘲笑）
- ridicule（讥讽）
- jeer（嘲弄）
- deride（嘲笑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **taunt someone about / taunt someone with / hurl taunts / cruel taunts**

👉 Example: The opposing fans taunted the visiting team with derisive chants throughout the entire match.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The defendant's attorney argued that persistent **taunts** from coworkers had contributed significantly to his client's emotional distress.
   被告律师辩称，同事们持续的嘲弄对其当事人的情绪困扰有重大影响。

2. Critics **taunted** the administration for promising sweeping reforms while delivering only incremental and superficial policy changes.
   批评者奚落政府承诺进行全面改革，却只做出了渐进和表面的政策调整。

3. Despite being **taunted** relentlessly by her opponents, the candidate maintained her composure and delivered a compelling final address.
   尽管不断遭到对手的嘲弄，这位候选人依然保持镇定，发表了一场令人信服的最终演讲。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "taunt" as "haunt with words" — a taunt is like a verbal haunting that follows and torments the target.
把"taunt"想成"用语言来纠缠(haunt)"——taunt就像言语上的纠缠，追着目标进行折磨和嘲弄。
