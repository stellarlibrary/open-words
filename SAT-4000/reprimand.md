# 📢 reprimand

🔊 /ˈrɛprɪmænd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A severe or formal expression of disapproval; an official rebuke. (v.) To address with a formal expression of disapproval; to censure sharply
**中文：** n. 谴责；训斥；严责；惩戒 v. 谴责；训斥

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rebuke（斥责）
- admonition（告诫）
- censure（谴责）
- reproach（责备）
- chastisement（惩罚）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **issue a reprimand / formal reprimand / reprimand someone for / receive a reprimand / severe reprimand**

👉 Example: The officer received a formal reprimand for failing to follow established protocol during the investigation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board issued a stern **reprimand** to the executive who had authorized expenditures without obtaining proper approval.
   董事会对未经适当批准便授权支出的高管发出了严厉的训斥。

2. Teachers should **reprimand** disruptive students firmly yet constructively to maintain a productive classroom learning environment.
   教师应坚定而有建设性地训斥扰乱课堂秩序的学生，以维持良好的课堂学习环境。

3. The senator faced a public **reprimand** from the ethics committee after revelations of undisclosed financial conflicts of interest.
   在未披露的财务利益冲突被曝光后，该参议员面临道德委员会的公开谴责。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "repri-mand" as a "command to reprimand" — an authority figure commands you to stand and hear your formal scolding.
把"reprimand"想成"repri"+"mand"（命令）——一个权威人物命令你站好，听取正式的训斥。
