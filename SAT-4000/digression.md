# 🔀 digression

🔊 /daɪˈɡrɛʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing; a deviation from the central topic or course
**中文：** n. 离题；脱轨；题外话

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- deviation（偏离）
- tangent（离题）
- detour（绕道）
- aside（题外话）
- excursion（偏离主题）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a brief digression / digression from the topic / make a digression / pardon the digression**

👉 Example: The lecturer made a brief digression to share an amusing anecdote before returning to the main argument.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The author's frequent **digression** from the central narrative made the novel intellectually stimulating but structurally challenging to follow.
   作者频繁偏离中心叙事使这部小说在智识上具有启发性，但在结构上难以跟随。

2. Pardon the **digression**, but this historical context is essential for understanding the significance of the current policy debate.
   请原谅这段离题，但这个历史背景对于理解当前政策辩论的重要性至关重要。

3. Effective academic writing requires discipline to avoid unnecessary **digression** that might weaken the coherence of the central argument.
   有效的学术写作需要自律，避免不必要的离题，以免削弱核心论点的连贯性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "di-gress-ion" as "going (gress) away (di-)" — like "progress" means stepping forward, "digress" means stepping away from the topic.
把"digression"想成"走开（di- + gress）"——就像"progress"意为向前走，"digress"意为偏离主题。
