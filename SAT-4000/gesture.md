# 👋 gesture

🔊 /ˈdʒɛstʃər/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A movement of the body, especially the hands or head, to express an idea or feeling; a symbolic action. (v.) To make a gesture or signal
**中文：** n. 姿态；手势；姿势 v. 作手势；用动作示意

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- signal（信号）
- motion（动作）
- indication（示意）
- sign（手势）
- wave（挥手）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a gesture of goodwill / make a gesture / gesture toward / an empty gesture / a symbolic gesture**

👉 Example: The president's visit was seen as a gesture of solidarity with the affected community.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The chairman made a conciliatory **gesture** by inviting all opposing parties to participate in the forthcoming negotiations.
   主席做出了和解姿态，邀请所有反对方参与即将举行的谈判。

2. She **gestured** toward the exit to indicate that the confidential meeting had concluded and attendees should leave.
   她向出口做了个手势，示意机密会议已经结束，与会者应该离开。

3. Critics dismissed the donation as a mere public relations **gesture** rather than a genuine commitment to charitable reform.
   批评者认为这项捐赠不过是公关姿态，而非对慈善改革的真正承诺。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gest-ure" — "gest" sounds like "jest" (a playful act); a gesture is a physical act that communicates meaning, like a jest communicates humor.
把"gesture"中的"gest"想成"jest"（玩笑）——手势是一种传达意义的肢体动作，就像玩笑传达幽默一样。
