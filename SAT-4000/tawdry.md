# 💍 tawdry

🔊 /ˈtɔːdri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Showy but cheap and of poor quality; sordid or unpleasant. (n.) Cheap and gaudy finery
**中文：** adj. 俗丽的；花哨而庸俗的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gaudy（俗丽的）
- garish（花哨的）
- cheap（廉价的）
- tasteless（庸俗的）
- vulgar（粗俗的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tawdry decorations / tawdry affair / tawdry imitation / tawdry spectacle**

👉 Example: The once-grand ballroom had been reduced to a tawdry venue with plastic chandeliers and peeling wallpaper.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The critic dismissed the production as a **tawdry** spectacle that substituted flashy costumes for genuine artistic substance.
   评论家将这部作品斥为一场俗丽的表演，用花哨的服装代替了真正的艺术内涵。

2. Investigators uncovered a **tawdry** scheme in which executives had embezzled charitable donations for personal luxury purchases.
   调查人员揭露了一个卑劣的计划，高管们挪用慈善捐款用于个人奢侈消费。

3. The antique shop was filled with **tawdry** reproductions that attempted to imitate the elegance of genuine Victorian craftsmanship.
   古董店里摆满了俗丽的仿制品，试图模仿真正维多利亚时代工艺的优雅。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tawdry" as "taud-ry" — sounds like "taught poorly," something made or taught poorly ends up looking cheap and tasteless.
把"tawdry"想成发音类似"taught poorly"——粗制滥造的东西最终看起来廉价而俗气。
