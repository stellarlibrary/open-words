# 🥀 poignant

🔊 /ˈpɔɪnjənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Evoking a keen sense of sadness or regret; deeply moving; sharp or incisive in expression.
**中文：** adj. 尖锐的；辛酸的；深刻的；切中要害的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- touching（感人的）
- moving（令人动容的）
- heartfelt（衷心的）
- bittersweet（苦乐参半的）
- piercing（尖锐的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **poignant reminder / poignant moment / poignant memoir / poignant story**

👉 Example: The documentary offered a poignant portrayal of families separated by decades of conflict.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The veteran's **poignant** account of wartime sacrifice left the entire audience in reflective silence for several minutes.
   那位老兵对战时牺牲的**辛酸**叙述让全场观众陷入沉思的寂静中好几分钟。

2. Her farewell letter contained a **poignant** reminder that time spent with loved ones should never be taken for granted.
   她的告别信包含一个**深刻的**提醒：与亲人相处的时光永远不应被视为理所当然。

3. The novelist's **poignant** depiction of childhood poverty resonated deeply with readers from diverse socioeconomic backgrounds worldwide.
   小说家对童年贫困的**感人**描写深深引起了全世界不同社会经济背景读者的共鸣。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "poignant" as "point" + "ant" — a sharp point like an ant's sting that pierces your emotions deeply.
把"poignant"想成"point"（尖端）+"ant"（蚂蚁）——像蚂蚁的刺一样尖锐地刺痛你的情感。
