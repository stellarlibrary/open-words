# 😖 aversion

🔊 /əˈvɜːrʒən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** A strong feeling of dislike, repugnance, or antipathy toward something or someone; also, the object of such dislike.

**中文：** 厌恶；讨厌的人或物。指对某事或某人的强烈反感、憎恶或排斥，也可指令人厌恶的对象。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- antipathy （反感）
- repugnance （厌恶）
- distaste （嫌恶）
- loathing （憎恶）
- revulsion （强烈反感）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Aversion" is commonly used with the prepositions "to" or "for." The phrase "have an aversion to" is the most standard construction. It can refer to both the feeling itself and the thing that causes the feeling.

"aversion"常与介词"to"或"for"搭配使用。"have an aversion to"是最常见的搭配。它既可以指厌恶的感觉本身，也可以指引起厌恶的事物。

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Her deep **aversion** to dishonesty made her a trusted and highly respected leader in the organization.
   *她对不诚实的深深**厌恶**使她成为组织中备受信赖和尊重的领导者。*

2. The professor developed an **aversion** to bureaucratic procedures after years of navigating institutional red tape.
   *在多年应对机构繁文缛节之后，这位教授对官僚程序产生了**厌恶**。*

3. Cold weather has always been his greatest **aversion**, which is why he relocated to a tropical climate.
   *寒冷的天气一直是他最**讨厌的东西**，这就是他搬到热带气候地区的原因。*

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Aversion" contains "a-version" — imagine you see a terrible "version" of something and you feel strong dislike. Your aversion makes you want to look away.

"aversion"包含"a-version"——想象你看到某物的一个糟糕"版本"，你感到强烈的反感。你的厌恶让你想要把目光移开。
