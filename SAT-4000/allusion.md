# 📜 allusion

🔊 /əˈluːʒən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** An indirect or passing reference to something, especially a literary, historical, or cultural reference.
**中文：** 暗示；间接提及；典故

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reference（提及）
- intimation（暗示）
- hint（暗示）
- insinuation（影射）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **make an allusion to, literary allusion, biblical allusion, classical allusion**
👉 Example: The poet made a subtle allusion to Greek mythology in the opening stanza.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's lecture contained a clever **allusion** to Shakespeare's treatment of jealousy in Othello.
   教授的讲座中巧妙地引用了莎士比亚在《奥赛罗》中对嫉妒的描写。

2. Her speech was filled with **allusions** to historical events that shaped the nation's founding principles.
   她的演讲中充满了对塑造国家建国原则的历史事件的典故。

3. The novel's title is an **allusion** to a famous passage from the Bible's Book of Ecclesiastes.
   这部小说的标题是对《圣经·传道书》中一段著名篇章的暗示。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "allusion" as "a illusion of meaning" — it hints at something without stating it directly, like an illusion that you must see through.
把 allusion 想象成 "a + illusion（幻觉）"——它像幻觉一样不直接说明，而是暗示，需要你自己去领悟。
