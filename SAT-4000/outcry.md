# 📢 outcry

🔊 /ˈaʊtkraɪ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A strong expression of public disapproval or protest; a loud shout or exclamation
**中文：** n. 强烈抗议；大声疾呼；尖叫；倒彩

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- protest（抗议）
- uproar（喧嚣）
- clamor（呼喊）
- objection（反对）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **public outcry / outcry against / outcry over / raise an outcry**

👉 Example: The proposed policy change provoked a public outcry from citizens across the entire country.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government's decision to raise taxes sparked an immediate **outcry** from both business leaders and ordinary citizens.
   政府加税的决定立即引发了商界领袖和普通公民的强烈抗议。

2. There was a widespread **outcry** against the destruction of the historic landmark to build a commercial complex.
   拆毁历史地标建造商业综合体的举措引起了广泛的强烈抗议。

3. The environmental **outcry** over industrial pollution led to the enactment of stricter regulatory laws nationwide.
   对工业污染的环保呼声促成了全国范围内更严格的监管法律的出台。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "out + cry" — when many people cry out loudly together, it becomes a public outcry of protest.
把"outcry"拆成"out（大声地）+ cry（呼喊）"——当很多人一起大声呼喊时，就形成了公众的强烈抗议。
