# 💥 shatter

🔊 /ˈʃætər/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To break suddenly into many small pieces; to destroy or ruin completely. (n.) A fragment or broken piece; a state of disorder
**中文：** v. 打碎，使散开；粉碎，破坏

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- smash（打碎）
- demolish（摧毁）
- fragment（使破碎）
- pulverize（粉碎）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **shatter a window / shatter expectations / shatter dreams / shatter confidence**

👉 Example: The unexpected scandal shattered public confidence in the administration's ability to govern transparently.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The explosion **shattered** every window in the building, sending fragments of glass cascading onto the pavement below.
   爆炸震碎了大楼里的每一扇窗户，玻璃碎片纷纷落到下面的人行道上。

2. New archaeological findings have **shattered** long-held assumptions about the origins of early human civilization.
   新的考古发现粉碎了人们长期以来对早期人类文明起源的假设。

3. The athlete's career was **shattered** by a devastating knee injury sustained during the championship tournament.
   这位运动员的职业生涯因锦标赛中遭受的严重膝伤而毁于一旦。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "shatter" as the sound glass makes when it breaks — a sharp, scattering crash into countless tiny pieces.
把"shatter"想象成玻璃破碎时的声音——尖锐的碎裂声，碎成无数小片。
