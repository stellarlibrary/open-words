# 🌦️ capricious

🔊 /kəˈprɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Given to sudden, unpredictable changes of mood or behavior; fickle and unreliable
**中文：** adj. 反复无常的；任性的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fickle（善变的）
- mercurial（变化无常的）
- erratic（不稳定的）
- whimsical（异想天开的）
- volatile（易变的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **capricious weather / capricious behavior / capricious ruler / capricious nature**

👉 Example: The capricious nature of the stock market makes long-term predictions extremely difficult.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **capricious** climate of the region made agricultural planning a formidable challenge for local farmers.
   该地区反复无常的气候使农业规划成为当地农民面临的一项艰巨挑战。

2. Citizens grew weary of the **capricious** regulations that seemed to change without warning or logical justification.
   公民们对那些似乎毫无预警或合理依据就改变的反复无常的法规感到厌倦。

3. Her **capricious** management style undermined team morale and eroded trust among her most dedicated subordinates.
   她反复无常的管理风格削弱了团队士气，侵蚀了下属中最敬业者的信任。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "capricious" as related to "Capricorn" the goat — goats jump unpredictably from rock to rock, just like capricious behavior.
把"capricious"联想到山羊座"Capricorn"——山羊在岩石间不可预测地跳来跳去，就像反复无常的行为一样。
