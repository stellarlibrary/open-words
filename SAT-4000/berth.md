# 🚢 berth

🔊 /bɜːrθ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A fixed bed or bunk on a ship, train, or other means of transport; a place in a dock for a ship to moor. (v.) To moor a ship in a designated place; to provide sleeping accommodations
**中文：** n. 卧铺；停泊处，锚位；差事 v. 使停泊；为……提供铺位

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dock（码头）
- mooring（系泊处）
- bunk（铺位）
- anchorage（锚地）
- slip（泊位）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **berth a ship / secure a berth / sleeping berth / give a wide berth to**

👉 Example: The captain carefully guided the vessel into its assigned berth along the eastern pier.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The harbor master directed the cargo vessel to **berth** at the newly constructed terminal on the north side of the port.
   港务长指示货船在港口北侧新建的码头停泊。

2. Passengers traveling overnight were advised to reserve a sleeping **berth** well in advance of the departure date.
   过夜旅行的乘客被建议在出发日期之前提前预订卧铺。

3. Experienced sailors always give a wide **berth** to rocky outcroppings that could damage the hull of their vessel.
   经验丰富的水手总是对可能损坏船体的岩石露头保持宽阔的距离。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "berth" as rhyming with "earth" — a berth is your spot on earth to rest, whether a bunk on a train or a ship's parking place.
把"berth"想成和"earth（大地）"押韵——berth就是你在船上或火车上休息的"一方天地"，也是船只停靠的"一块地盘"。
