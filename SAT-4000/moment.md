# ⏳ moment

🔊 /ˈmoʊmənt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A very brief period of time; a particular point in time; importance or significance in influence or effect
**中文：** n. 片刻，瞬间，时刻；重要，契机

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- instant（瞬间）
- juncture（时刻）
- second（片刻）
- significance（重要性）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **at this moment / a moment of truth / of great moment / seize the moment**

👉 Example: The signing of the treaty was a moment of great historical significance for both nations involved.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scientist paused for a **moment** to reflect on the profound implications that the experimental results would have on future research.
   这位科学家停顿了片刻，思考实验结果对未来研究将产生的深远影响。

2. Scholars regard the invention of the printing press as a **moment** of extraordinary consequence in the history of Western civilization.
   学者们将印刷术的发明视为西方文明史上一个极其重要的时刻。

3. She recognized that this was a decisive **moment** in her career and resolved to present her findings with absolute clarity.
   她意识到这是她职业生涯中的一个决定性时刻，决心以绝对清晰的方式展示她的研究成果。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "moment" as "mo-ment" — "mo" sounds like "more" but it is actually less, just a fleeting instant that demands your attention.
把"moment"想成"mo-ment"——"mo"听起来像"more（更多）"，但实际上是更少，只是一个转瞬即逝的瞬间。
