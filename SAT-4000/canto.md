# 📖 canto

🔊 /ˈkæntoʊ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A major division of a long poem, comparable to a chapter in a book.
**中文：** n. 长诗中的篇；篇章

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- section（部分）
- division（篇章）
- chapter（章节）
- stanza（节）
- passage（段落）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **canto of a poem / opening canto / final canto / Canto I / divided into cantos**

👉 Example: The professor assigned the first three cantos of Dante's Inferno for the next seminar discussion.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Dante's *Divine Comedy* is divided into one hundred **cantos**, each written in the terza rima rhyme scheme.
   但丁的《神曲》分为一百篇，每篇都采用三韵体的韵律方案。

2. The opening **canto** of Byron's *Don Juan* establishes the satirical tone that pervades the entire epic poem.
   拜伦《唐璜》的开篇第一篇确立了贯穿整部史诗的讽刺基调。

3. Literary critics have devoted extensive analysis to the thematic structure of each **canto** in Ezra Pound's work.
   文学评论家对埃兹拉·庞德作品中每一篇的主题结构进行了广泛分析。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "canto" as related to "chant" — each canto is a section of a long poem that could be chanted or recited aloud.
"canto"与"chant（吟唱）"相关——每个"canto"是长诗中可以被吟诵的一个篇章。
