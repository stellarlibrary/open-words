# ✂️ abbreviate

🔊 /əˈbriːvieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To shorten a word, phrase, or text; to make briefer or more concise
**中文：** v. 缩写；缩短；使简短；使省略

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- shorten（缩短）
- condense（压缩）
- abridge（删节）
- truncate（截短）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **abbreviate a word / abbreviated form / commonly abbreviated as / abbreviate to**

👉 Example: The term "United Nations" is commonly abbreviated to "UN" in international documents.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The editor asked the author to **abbreviate** the lengthy introduction so it would fit within the publication guidelines.
   编辑要求作者缩短过长的引言，以符合出版指南的要求。

2. In academic writing, researchers often **abbreviate** frequently used technical terms after their first full mention.
   在学术写作中，研究人员通常在首次完整提及后缩写常用的技术术语。

3. The committee voted to **abbreviate** the standard review process from six months to just eight weeks.
   委员会投票决定将标准审查流程从六个月缩短为仅八周。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Abbreviate" itself is a long word that means to make things shorter — ironic! Remember: "abbreviate" has "brevi" from Latin "brevis" meaning short.
"Abbreviate"本身是一个表示缩短事物的长词——多讽刺！记住："abbreviate"中的"brevi"来自拉丁语"brevis"，意为短。
