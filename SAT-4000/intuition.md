# 🔮 intuition

🔊 /ˌɪntjuˈɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The ability to understand or know something immediately, without conscious reasoning; a gut feeling or instinct
**中文：** n. 直觉；直觉力；直觉的知识

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- instinct（本能）
- insight（洞察力）
- hunch（预感）
- perception（感知）
- sixth sense（第六感）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **trust one's intuition / rely on intuition / intuition tells / sharp intuition / female intuition**

👉 Example: Her intuition told her that the deal was too good to be true, and she was ultimately proven right.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Experienced physicians often rely on **intuition** as much as clinical data when diagnosing rare and complex conditions.
   经验丰富的医生在诊断罕见复杂病症时，往往和依赖临床数据一样依赖直觉。

2. His strong **intuition** about market trends allowed him to make profitable investments well before economic shifts occurred.
   他对市场趋势敏锐的直觉使他能在经济变化发生之前就进行有利可图的投资。

3. The philosopher argued that moral **intuition** plays a fundamental role in shaping our ethical judgments and decisions.
   这位哲学家认为，道德直觉在塑造我们的伦理判断和决策中起着根本性作用。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "intuition" as "in-tuition" — knowledge you have "in" you without needing any "tuition" or formal teaching.
把"intuition"想成"in-tuition"——不需要"tuition（学费/教导）"就已经存"in（在）"你内心的知识，即直觉。
