# 🎭 parody

🔊 /ˈpærədi/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A humorous or satirical imitation of a serious work or style; a feeble imitation. (v.) To produce a humorously exaggerated imitation of something
**中文：** n. 诙谐的模仿作品；拙劣的模仿 v. 拙劣模仿

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- satire（讽刺作品）
- spoof（戏仿）
- caricature（漫画式模仿）
- burlesque（滑稽模仿）
- mockery（嘲弄）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a parody of / parody a style / political parody / self-parody / parody account**

👉 Example: The comedian's parody of the famous speech had the entire audience laughing uncontrollably.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The satirical magazine published a brilliant **parody** of the government's official report, exposing its contradictions through sharp humor.
   这本讽刺杂志发表了一篇对政府官方报告的精彩戏仿，通过尖锐的幽默揭露了其中的矛盾。

2. Some critics argued that the film was not a genuine homage but rather a shallow **parody** of the original cinematic masterpiece.
   一些评论家认为这部电影并非真正的致敬，而是对原创电影杰作的肤浅模仿。

3. The student skillfully **parodied** the professor's distinctive lecture style, capturing every mannerism with affectionate and remarkable precision.
   这位学生巧妙地模仿了教授独特的讲课风格，以亲切而惊人的精确度捕捉了每一个习惯动作。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "par-ody" as "pair-odd" — an odd pair of the original, a funny and exaggerated copy that mirrors it.
把"parody"想成"pair（配对）+ odd（奇怪的）"——原作的一个奇怪翻版，一个滑稽夸张的模仿。
