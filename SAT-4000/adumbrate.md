# 🌑 adumbrate

🔊 /ˈædʌmbreɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To foreshadow or give a vague indication of; to outline or sketch broadly; to overshadow
**中文：** v. 预示；画…的轮廓；遮蔽

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- foreshadow（预示）
- outline（勾勒轮廓）
- presage（预兆）
- portend（预示）
- overshadow（遮蔽）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **adumbrate a plan / adumbrate the future / adumbrate an idea / dimly adumbrated**

👉 Example: The opening chapter adumbrates the themes of loss and redemption that pervade the entire novel.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The economist's early writings **adumbrated** the financial crisis that would devastate global markets a decade later.
   这位经济学家早期的著作预示了十年后重创全球市场的金融危机。

2. The architect only **adumbrated** the design during the initial presentation, promising detailed blueprints at the next meeting.
   在初次展示中，建筑师只是勾勒了设计轮廓，承诺下次会议提供详细蓝图。

3. Dark clouds **adumbrated** the valley below, casting an ominous shadow over the small farming communities nestled there.
   乌云遮蔽了下方的山谷，给坐落在那里的小型农业社区投下了不祥的阴影。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "adumbrate" as "a-DUMB-rate" — containing "umbra" (Latin for shadow), it means to shadow forth or foreshadow something vaguely.
"adumbrate"中包含"umbra"（拉丁语意为"影子"），意思是用影子般模糊的方式预示或勾勒某事物的轮廓。
