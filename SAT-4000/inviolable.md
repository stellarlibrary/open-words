# 🔒 inviolable

🔊 /ɪnˈvaɪələbl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Never to be broken, infringed, or dishonored; sacred and protected from violation
**中文：** adj. 不可侵犯的；神圣的；不可亵渎的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sacrosanct（神圣不可侵犯的）
- sacred（神圣的）
- untouchable（不可触碰的）
- inviolate（未受侵犯的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inviolable right / inviolable principle / inviolable oath / inviolable territory**

👉 Example: Freedom of speech is considered an inviolable right in many democratic constitutions around the world.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The constitution enshrines **inviolable** rights that no government authority may lawfully restrict or revoke under any circumstances.
   宪法规定了不可侵犯的权利，任何政府机关在任何情况下都不得合法地限制或撤销。

2. The two nations signed a treaty declaring their shared border an **inviolable** boundary that neither side would contest.
   两国签署了一项条约，宣布其共同边界为不可侵犯的界线，双方均不得争议。

3. The scholar maintained that the pursuit of truth should be treated as an **inviolable** principle within academic institutions.
   这位学者坚持认为，追求真理应被视为学术机构内不可亵渎的原则。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-VIOLATE-able" — "violate" means to break or dishonor, and "in-" means "not," so inviolable means something that cannot be violated or broken.
把"inviolable"想成"in-VIOLATE-able"——"violate"意为违反或亵渎，"in-"表示"不"，所以inviolable就是不能被违反或亵渎的。
