# 🔔 admonition

🔊 /ˌædməˈnɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A firm warning or expression of disapproval; a piece of earnest advice or counsel against fault or oversight.
**中文：** 警告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- warning（警告）
- caution（告诫）
- reprimand（训斥）
- rebuke（斥责）
- counsel（忠告）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **stern/gentle admonition**, **admonition against something**, **heed an admonition**

👉 Example: The teacher's admonition against plagiarism was taken seriously by every student.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Despite repeated **admonition** from the board, the company continued its questionable accounting practices.
   尽管董事会多次警告，该公司仍继续其不当的会计做法。

2. The physician's **admonition** to reduce stress ultimately motivated the patient to change his lifestyle.
   医生关于减轻压力的告诫最终促使患者改变了生活方式。

3. Her mother's gentle **admonition** about the importance of honesty stayed with her throughout life.
   母亲关于诚实重要性的温和告诫伴随了她一生。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "admonish" + "-tion" — the noun form of admonishing; a warning that monitors your behavior.
联想"admonish"（告诫）+"-tion"（名词后缀）——告诫的名词形式；"moni"像"monitor"（监督），监督你行为的警告。
