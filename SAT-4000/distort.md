# 🪞 distort

🔊 /dɪˈstɔːrt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To pull or twist out of shape; to give a misleading or false account or impression of something
**中文：** v. 扭曲；使失真；歪曲；曲解

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- warp（扭曲）
- twist（歪曲）
- misrepresent（歪曲）
- deform（使变形）
- falsify（篡改）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **distort the truth / distort the facts / distort reality / distort an image / distort the meaning**

👉 Example: The media was accused of distorting the politician's statements to fit a particular narrative.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The intense heat caused the metal framework to **distort** beyond repair, compromising the structural integrity of the entire building.
   高温导致金属框架严重扭曲无法修复，损害了整栋建筑的结构完整性。

2. Critics argued that the documentary sought to **distort** historical events by omitting crucial context and selectively presenting evidence.
   批评者认为这部纪录片试图通过省略关键背景和选择性地呈现证据来歪曲历史事件。

3. The flawed lens continued to **distort** the captured images, rendering them unsuitable for the precision required in scientific analysis.
   有缺陷的镜头持续使拍摄的图像失真，使其无法满足科学分析所需的精度要求。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-tort" as "dis- (badly) + tort (twist)" — to twist something badly, either physically or in meaning.
把"distort"想成"dis-（不好地）+ tort（扭转）"——将某物严重扭曲，无论是物理上还是意义上。
