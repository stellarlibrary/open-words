# 🤨 mistrust

🔊 /ˌmɪsˈtrʌst/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Lack of trust or confidence; suspicion. (v.) To regard with suspicion or doubt; to have no confidence in
**中文：** n. 不信任；猜疑 v. 不信任；怀疑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- distrust（不信任）
- suspicion（怀疑）
- skepticism（怀疑态度）
- doubt（疑虑）
- wariness（警惕）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mistrust of authority / deep mistrust / mutual mistrust / mistrust someone's motives**

👉 Example: Years of broken promises had fostered a deep mistrust between the community and local officials.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The public's growing **mistrust** of corporate institutions has led to increased demands for transparency and accountability.
   公众对企业机构日益增长的不信任导致了对透明度和问责制更高的要求。

2. She could not help but **mistrust** his sudden generosity, suspecting there were ulterior motives behind his offer.
   她不禁怀疑他突然的慷慨，猜测他的提议背后有不可告人的动机。

3. Decades of political corruption have cultivated a pervasive **mistrust** that undermines democratic participation among citizens.
   数十年的政治腐败培养了一种普遍的不信任感，削弱了公民的民主参与。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mis-trust" as "misplaced trust" — when trust is placed wrongly, you end up with mistrust and suspicion.
把"mistrust"想成"misplaced trust"——当信任被错误地放置时，你最终会产生不信任和怀疑。
