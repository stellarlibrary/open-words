# ⏪ retroactive

🔊 /ˌrɛtroʊˈæktɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Taking effect from a date in the past; applying to a period before enactment
**中文：** adj. 追溯的；有追溯效力的；涉及以往的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- retrospective（追溯的）
- backward-looking（回溯的）
- ex post facto（事后的）
- reactive（反应性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **retroactive effect / retroactive pay / retroactive law / retroactive to (a date)**

👉 Example: The salary increase was made retroactive to the beginning of the fiscal year.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The new tax legislation was applied on a **retroactive** basis, affecting all transactions completed within the previous calendar year.
   新税法以追溯方式适用，影响上一日历年度内完成的所有交易。

2. Workers celebrated when the union secured a **retroactive** pay raise covering the six months of prolonged contract negotiations.
   当工会争取到涵盖六个月漫长合同谈判期间的追溯加薪时，工人们欢呼庆祝。

3. Constitutional scholars debated whether the **retroactive** enforcement of the amended statute violated fundamental principles of legal fairness.
   宪法学者们争论修正后法规的追溯执行是否违反了法律公正的基本原则。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "retro-active" as "actively going retro" — the law actively reaches back into the past to take effect.
把"retroactive"想成"积极地回到过去"——法律积极地回溯到过去生效。
