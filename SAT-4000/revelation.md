# 💡 revelation

🔊 /ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A surprising and previously unknown fact that has been disclosed; the act of revealing something; a divine or supernatural disclosure
**中文：** n. 启示；揭露；出乎意料的事

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disclosure（揭露）
- discovery（发现）
- epiphany（顿悟）
- unveiling（揭示）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **startling revelation / shocking revelation / revelation about / come as a revelation**

👉 Example: The memoir contained several startling revelations about the inner workings of the royal household.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scientist's groundbreaking **revelation** about the structure of dark matter fundamentally altered the direction of contemporary astrophysics research.
   这位科学家关于暗物质结构的突破性发现从根本上改变了当代天体物理学研究的方向。

2. The leaked financial records proved to be a **revelation**, exposing years of systematic fraud within the multinational corporation's accounting department.
   泄露的财务记录是一个重大揭露，暴露了这家跨国公司会计部门多年的系统性欺诈行为。

3. For many audience members, the documentary was a genuine **revelation** that challenged their long-held assumptions about the historical event.
   对许多观众来说，这部纪录片是一个真正的启示，挑战了他们对这一历史事件的长期看法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "revelation" as a "revel-ation" — a celebration of truth, because when something hidden is finally revealed, it feels like a momentous occasion.
把"revelation"想成"真相的庆典"——当隐藏的事物最终被揭示时，就像一个重大的时刻值得欢庆。
