# 😴 tedious

🔊 /ˈtiːdiəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Too long, slow, or dull; tiresome or monotonous
**中文：** adj. 沉闷的；冗长乏味的；单调的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- monotonous（单调的）
- tiresome（令人厌烦的）
- wearisome（使人疲倦的）
- dreary（沉闷的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tedious task / tedious process / tedious work / tedious lecture**

👉 Example: The tedious process of cataloging thousands of specimens required extraordinary patience and attention to detail.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **tedious** nature of the data entry work caused several interns to resign before completing their assignments.
   数据录入工作的乏味性质导致几名实习生在完成任务之前辞职。

2. Although the research was groundbreaking, the **tedious** methodology section of the paper discouraged many readers from finishing it.
   尽管这项研究具有开创性，但论文中冗长的方法论部分使许多读者望而却步。

3. Proofreading legal documents is a **tedious** but essential responsibility that demands unwavering concentration and precision.
   校对法律文件是一项乏味但必不可少的职责，需要持续的专注和精确。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tedious" as containing "tedium" — imagine sitting through a dull meeting that feels like it will never end.
把"tedious"想成包含"tedium"（无聊）——想象坐在一个沉闷的会议中，感觉永远不会结束。
