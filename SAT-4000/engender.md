# 🌱 engender

🔊 /ɪnˈdʒendər/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To cause or give rise to a feeling, situation, or condition; to produce or bring about
**中文：** v. 使产生；造成；引起

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- generate（产生）
- provoke（引发）
- foster（促进）
- incite（激起）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **engender trust / engender fear / engender controversy / engender support**

👉 Example: The leader's transparent communication style helped engender trust among all stakeholders.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The controversial policy decision **engendered** widespread public debate and prompted citizens to organize formal protests across the region.
   这项有争议的政策决定引发了广泛的公众辩论，并促使公民在全地区组织正式抗议。

2. Effective mentorship programs can **engender** a lasting sense of confidence and professional ambition in young scholars and researchers.
   有效的导师计划可以在年轻学者和研究人员中产生持久的信心和职业抱负。

3. The economic crisis **engendered** profound social changes that reshaped the political landscape of the entire nation for decades.
   经济危机引发了深刻的社会变革，在数十年间重塑了整个国家的政治格局。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "engender" as "en-GENDER" — to give birth to or generate something new, just as gender relates to creation and origin.
把"engender"想成"en-GENDER"——产生或创造新事物，就像gender（性别）与创造和起源有关一样。
