# ⛪ christen

🔊 /ˈkrɪsən/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To give a name to someone or something, especially at a baptism ceremony; to name and dedicate formally
**中文：** v. 为…命名；为…施洗礼

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- baptize（施洗礼）
- name（命名）
- designate（指定名称）
- dub（授予称号）
- inaugurate（为…举行开幕式）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **christen a ship / christen a child / christen something after someone / newly christened**

👉 Example: The mayor was invited to christen the new library that had been built in honor of the city's founder.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The community gathered at the harbor to **christen** the newly constructed vessel before its inaugural voyage across the Atlantic.
   社区居民聚集在港口为新建的船只**命名**，然后它将进行横跨大西洋的首航。

2. The parents decided to **christen** their daughter in the same historic chapel where three generations had been baptized.
   父母决定在三代人都曾受洗的同一座历史悠久的教堂里为女儿**施洗礼**。

3. The university chose to **christen** the new research facility after the Nobel laureate who had once studied there.
   大学选择以曾在此学习的诺贝尔奖获得者的名字**命名**这座新研究设施。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "christen" as "Christ + en" — to christen originally meant to make someone a Christian through baptism, which involved giving a name.
把"christen"想成"Christ + en"——christen最初意为通过洗礼使人成为基督徒，洗礼过程中会赐予名字。
