# 🎯 cue

🔊 /kjuː/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A signal or prompt that initiates an action; a hint or clue. (v.) To give a signal or prompt to someone
**中文：** n. 提示，暗示；线索 v. 给…暗示

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- signal（信号）
- prompt（提示）
- hint（暗示）
- indication（指示）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **take a cue from / on cue / cue someone to do something / visual cue**

👉 Example: The director gave the actor a cue to begin the dramatic monologue on stage.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor used a subtle verbal **cue** to signal the teaching assistant to distribute the examination papers.
   教授用一个微妙的口头暗示通知助教分发考试试卷。

2. Experienced public speakers learn to read audience **cues** and adjust their delivery style accordingly.
   经验丰富的演讲者会学习解读观众的暗示并相应地调整表达方式。

3. The conductor raised his baton to **cue** the orchestra for the opening movement of the symphony.
   指挥举起指挥棒，提示乐团开始演奏交响曲的第一乐章。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cue" as the letter "Q" — when it is your turn in a queue, someone gives you a cue to step forward.
把"cue"想成字母"Q"——当你在队列（queue）中轮到时，有人给你一个提示（cue）让你向前走。
