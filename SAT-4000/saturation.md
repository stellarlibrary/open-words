# 💧 saturation

🔊 /ˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The state or process of being thoroughly soaked or filled to capacity; the degree of vividness of a color; the point at which no more can be absorbed or accepted
**中文：** n. 饱和

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- permeation（渗透）
- impregnation（浸渍）
- soaking（浸泡）
- fullness（充满）
- surfeit（过量）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **market saturation / color saturation / saturation point / saturation level / saturation bombing**

👉 Example: The market reached saturation point, leaving little room for new competitors to enter the industry.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **saturation** of the housing market caused property values to decline sharply in several major metropolitan areas across the country.
   住房市场的饱和导致全国几个主要大都市地区的房产价值急剧下降。

2. Increasing the color **saturation** in the digital photograph made the landscape appear more vibrant and visually striking to viewers.
   提高数码照片的色彩饱和度使风景在观众看来更加鲜艳和视觉震撼。

3. The soil had reached complete **saturation** after weeks of continuous rainfall, significantly raising the risk of landslides in the region.
   经过数周的持续降雨，土壤已经达到完全饱和，显著增加了该地区发生山体滑坡的风险。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "satur-ation" — like a sponge that is "saturated" and cannot absorb one more drop. Saturation is the state of being completely full.
把"saturation"想成一块"饱和"的海绵，再也吸不进一滴水。饱和就是完全充满的状态。
