# 🔥 fervor

🔊 /ˈfɜːrvər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Intense and passionate feeling; great warmth of emotion or enthusiasm
**中文：** n. 热情；热烈；热心；炽热

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- passion（热情）
- zeal（热忱）
- ardor（热烈）
- enthusiasm（热情）
- intensity（强烈）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **religious fervor / patriotic fervor / revolutionary fervor / with great fervor / fervor for**

👉 Example: The crowd cheered with patriotic fervor as the national team scored the winning goal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The missionary's religious **fervor** drove her to dedicate three decades of service to communities in remote regions.
   这位传教士的宗教热情驱使她将三十年奉献给偏远地区的社区服务。

2. Revolutionary **fervor** swept through the nation as citizens demanded fundamental changes to the existing political structure.
   革命热情席卷全国，公民们要求对现有政治体制进行根本性变革。

3. The professor lectured with such **fervor** that even the most disengaged students found themselves captivated by the material.
   这位教授讲课充满热情，连最不感兴趣的学生都被课程内容深深吸引。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fervor" as "fever" with an "or" twist — like having a fever of excitement, burning with intense emotional heat.
把"fervor"想成带有"or"变化的"fever"（发烧）——就像兴奋的发烧，燃烧着强烈的情感热度。
