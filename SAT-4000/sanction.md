# ⚖️ sanction

🔊 /ˈsæŋkʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A penalty or punishment imposed for violating a law or rule; official approval or permission. (v.) To impose a penalty on; to give official approval or permission
**中文：** v. 同意，许可，准许

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- authorize（批准）
- approve（认可）
- penalize（制裁）
- endorse（支持）
- ratify（批准）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **economic sanctions / sanction against / officially sanctioned / impose sanctions**

👉 Example: The United Nations voted to impose economic sanctions against the country for violating international law.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The regulatory board refused to **sanction** the proposed merger until both companies met all compliance requirements.
   监管委员会拒绝批准拟议的合并，直到两家公司满足所有合规要求。

2. International **sanctions** were imposed on the regime in response to its persistent violations of human rights treaties.
   由于该政权持续违反人权条约，国际社会对其实施了制裁。

3. The committee voted unanimously to **sanction** the new research protocol after a thorough review of its ethical implications.
   委员会在全面审查其伦理影响后一致投票批准了新的研究方案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sanction" as having two faces: it can mean "to approve" or "to punish." Remember "sanct" from "sanctuary" (a safe, approved place) for approval, and "sanctions" as penalties.
把"sanction"想成有两面含义：既可以表示"批准"也可以表示"惩罚"。记住"sanct"来自"sanctuary（庇护所，安全的、被认可的地方）"代表批准，而"sanctions"作为复数名词则代表制裁。
