# 🌋 boiling

🔊 /ˈbɔɪlɪŋ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun, adverb | 形容词, 名词, 副词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) At or near the temperature at which a liquid bubbles and turns to vapor; extremely angry or agitated. (n.) The process of a liquid reaching its boiling point. (adv.) Extremely hot.
**中文：** adj. 沸腾的；激昂的 n. 沸腾；煮沸；起泡 adv. 极热地

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- scalding（滚烫的）
- simmering（即将沸腾的）
- seething（翻涌的）
- bubbling（冒泡的）
- sweltering（酷热的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **boiling point / boiling water / boiling hot / boiling with rage / bring to a boil**

👉 Example: Tensions between the two nations had reached boiling point after months of unresolved disputes.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The political debate reached a **boiling** point when both candidates exchanged sharp personal accusations before the live audience.
   当两位候选人在现场观众面前互相进行尖锐的人身攻击时，政治辩论达到了白热化。

2. The chemist carefully monitored the **boiling** solution to ensure that the volatile compound did not exceed critical temperature thresholds.
   化学家仔细监测沸腾的溶液，以确保挥发性化合物不超过临界温度阈值。

3. Public frustration was **boiling** over as citizens demanded immediate government accountability for the contaminated water supply.
   随着市民要求政府立即对受污染的供水负责，公众的不满情绪已达到沸点。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "boiling" as water that is so agitated it cannot stay still — just like a person who is "boiling with anger" cannot contain their intense emotions any longer.
把"boiling"想成水剧烈翻滚无法平静——就像一个"怒火中烧"的人再也无法压抑强烈的情绪。
