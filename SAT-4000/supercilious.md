# 😤 supercilious

🔊 /ˌsuːpərˈsɪliəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Behaving or looking as though one thinks one is superior to others; disdainfully proud; haughty
**中文：** adj. 自大的，傲慢的，目空一切的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- haughty（高傲的）
- arrogant（傲慢的）
- condescending（居高临下的）
- disdainful（轻蔑的）
- imperious（专横的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **supercilious attitude / supercilious tone / supercilious manner / supercilious glance**

👉 Example: His supercilious manner alienated colleagues who otherwise would have supported his proposal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's **supercilious** demeanor discouraged students from asking questions during lectures and office hours.
   教授目空一切的态度使学生们在课堂和办公时间不敢提问。

2. She responded with a **supercilious** smile that made it perfectly clear she considered the suggestion beneath her.
   她报以一个傲慢的微笑，清楚地表明她认为这个建议不值一提。

3. The reviewer's **supercilious** critique of the young artist's work revealed more about his own arrogance than any flaw.
   评论家对这位年轻艺术家作品的自大批评，更多暴露了他自己的傲慢而非任何缺陷。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "supercilious" as "super-silly-us" — someone who acts superior is being super silly to us with their arrogance.
把"supercilious"想成"super-silly-us"——一个自以为高人一等的人在我们看来是超级愚蠢的傲慢。
