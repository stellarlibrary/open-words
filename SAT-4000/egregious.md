# 🚨 egregious

🔊 /ɪˈɡriːdʒəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Outstandingly bad; shocking; remarkably flagrant or conspicuously terrible
**中文：** adj. 惊人的；过分的；极坏的；恶名昭彰的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flagrant（公然的）
- outrageous（令人震惊的）
- glaring（明显的）
- appalling（骇人的）
- heinous（极恶的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **egregious error / egregious violation / egregious misconduct / egregious example**

👉 Example: The audit uncovered egregious accounting irregularities that had persisted for nearly a decade.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court described the defendant's conduct as an **egregious** violation of professional ethics that warranted severe disciplinary action.
   法院将被告的行为描述为严重违反职业道德，应受到严厉的纪律处分。

2. The report documented **egregious** failures in the safety inspection process that directly contributed to the industrial accident.
   该报告记录了安全检查流程中的严重失误，这些失误直接导致了工业事故的发生。

3. Historians regard the policy as one of the most **egregious** injustices perpetrated by the colonial administration during that era.
   历史学家认为这项政策是殖民政府在那个时代实施的最恶劣的不公正行为之一。

把"egregious"想成"e-GREG-ious"——想象一个叫Greg的人犯了如此可怕的错误，以至于每个人都称之为"Greg式的"，意为坏得令人震惊。
