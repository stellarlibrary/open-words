# ✨ polish

🔊 /ˈpɑːlɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make smooth and shiny by rubbing; to refine or improve something. (n.) A substance used for polishing; elegance or refinement of style
**中文：** v. 磨光，擦亮；使优美，润饰 n. 擦光剂，上光蜡；优雅，精良

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- refine（润饰）
- burnish（磨光）
- buff（擦亮）
- enhance（提升）
- elegance（优雅）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **polish a surface / polish an essay / nail polish / polish up / add polish to**

👉 Example: The editor helped the author polish the manuscript before submitting it for publication.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The apprentice spent hours learning to **polish** the antique silverware until it gleamed with a brilliant, mirror-like finish.
   学徒花了数小时学习擦亮古董银器，直到它闪耀出明亮的镜面般光泽。

2. The professor advised her students to **polish** their thesis arguments carefully before presenting them at the academic conference.
   教授建议学生在学术会议上展示论文论点之前仔细润色。

3. Her prose possessed a remarkable **polish** that distinguished it from the crude, hastily written drafts of her contemporaries.
   她的散文具有非凡的精致，使其有别于同时代人那些粗糙、仓促写成的草稿。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "polish" as what you do to make something shine — whether it is a shoe or an essay, polishing makes it gleam with quality.
把"polish"想成让东西发光的动作——无论是鞋子还是文章，打磨都能使其焕发品质的光彩。
