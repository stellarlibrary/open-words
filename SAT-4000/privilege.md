# 👑 privilege

🔊 /ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A special right or advantage granted to a particular person or group. (v.) To grant a special right or advantage to someone
**中文：** n. 特权，优惠，特许 v. 给予优惠，给予特权

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- prerogative（特权）
- entitlement（权利）
- advantage（优势）
- benefit（利益）
- immunity（豁免权）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **privilege of / privileged access / attorney-client privilege / abuse a privilege**

👉 Example: Attending a world-class university is a privilege that comes with great responsibility.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator argued that access to quality healthcare should be a right rather than a **privilege** reserved for the wealthy.
   这位参议员主张，获得优质医疗保健应当是一项权利，而非富人独享的特权。

2. The court upheld the attorney-client **privilege**, preventing the prosecution from accessing confidential communications between the lawyer and defendant.
   法院维护了律师与客户之间的保密特权，阻止控方获取律师与被告之间的机密通信。

3. The institution has historically **privileged** empirical research over theoretical speculation in its approach to academic scholarship.
   该机构在学术研究方法上历来优先重视实证研究而非理论推测。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "privilege" as "private-ledge" — a private ledge that only special people can stand on, above everyone else.
把"privilege"想成"private-ledge"——一个只有特殊人物才能站上的私人高台，高于其他所有人。
