# 🐘 ponderous

🔊 /ˈpɑːndərəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Slow and clumsy because of great weight; dull, tedious, or excessively labored in style
**中文：** adj. 笨重的；沉闷的；呆板的；冗长的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cumbersome（笨重的）
- unwieldy（不灵活的）
- laborious（费力的）
- tedious（冗长的）
- lumbering（笨拙的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ponderous prose / ponderous pace / ponderous movement / ponderous style**

👉 Example: The elephant moved with ponderous grace through the dense forest, shaking the ground with each step.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The author's **ponderous** writing style, filled with lengthy digressions, made the novel difficult to finish.
   这位作者笨重冗长的写作风格充满了大段题外话，使得小说难以读完。

2. The **ponderous** machinery required several workers to operate and could not be easily relocated between sites.
   这台笨重的机器需要几名工人操作，且无法在场地之间轻松转移。

3. Critics described the bureaucratic process as **ponderous** and inefficient, discouraging citizens from filing applications.
   批评者形容这一官僚程序沉闷且低效，令市民不愿提交申请。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ponder-ous" as "so heavy it makes you ponder" — something so weighty and slow that it forces you to pause and think about how to move it.
把"ponderous"想成"沉重到让你沉思(ponder)"——某物如此沉重缓慢，迫使你停下来想该怎么搬动它。
