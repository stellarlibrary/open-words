# 😳 embarrass

🔊 /ɪmˈbærəs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause someone to feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed; to make someone feel ill at ease in a social situation
**中文：** v. 使局促不安；使困窘；使窘迫

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- humiliate（羞辱）
- mortify（使难堪）
- fluster（使慌乱）
- discomfit（使尴尬）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **embarrass someone publicly / embarrass oneself / deeply embarrassed / embarrass the government**

👉 Example: His careless remarks during the meeting managed to embarrass the entire delegation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The leaked documents threatened to **embarrass** several high-ranking officials who had previously denied any involvement in the scandal.
   泄露的文件可能会使几位此前否认参与丑闻的高级官员陷入尴尬。

2. She did not wish to **embarrass** her colleague by pointing out the factual errors in his presentation before the audience.
   她不想在观众面前指出同事演讲中的事实错误而使他难堪。

3. The unexpected policy reversal was likely to **embarrass** the administration and undermine public confidence in its leadership.
   这一出乎意料的政策逆转很可能使政府陷入困窘，并削弱公众对其领导力的信心。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "em-BARE-ass" — imagine being bare (exposed) in public, which would certainly cause great embarrassment.
把"embarrass"想成"em-BARE-ass"——想象在公共场合赤身裸体（暴露），这肯定会让人非常尴尬。
