# 🏪 kiosk

🔊 /ˈkiːɒsk/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A small open-fronted structure used as a booth for selling newspapers, refreshments, or tickets; a public telephone booth; a pavilion or gazebo
**中文：** n. 凉亭；公用电话亭；报摊；售货亭

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- booth（摊位）
- stand（售货台）
- stall（货摊）
- pavilion（亭子）
- newsstand（报摊）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **information kiosk / self-service kiosk / newspaper kiosk / ticket kiosk / kiosk display**

👉 Example: Travelers can purchase tickets from the self-service kiosk located near the main entrance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The airport installed dozens of automated **kiosk** terminals to reduce passenger wait times during peak travel seasons.
   机场安装了数十台自动售货亭终端，以减少旅客在高峰旅行季节的等待时间。

2. A small **kiosk** on the corner of the square has sold newspapers and magazines to local residents for over thirty years.
   广场角落的一个小报摊已经向当地居民出售报纸和杂志超过三十年了。

3. The university placed an interactive **kiosk** in the lobby so that visitors could easily navigate the campus and facilities.
   大学在大厅放置了一台互动信息亭，方便访客轻松浏览校园和设施。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "kiosk" as "key-ask" — you walk up to the little booth and "ask" for the "key" information or item you need.
把"kiosk"想成"key-ask"——你走到小亭子前，"ask"（询问）你需要的"key"（关键）信息或物品。
