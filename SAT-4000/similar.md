# 🔄 similar

🔊 /ˈsɪmɪlər/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Resembling without being identical; having a likeness. (n.) A thing that is similar to another
**中文：** adj. 相似的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- alike（相似的）
- comparable（可比较的）
- analogous（类似的）
- resembling（类似的）
- akin（相似的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **similar to / similar in nature / bear a similar resemblance / similar pattern / strikingly similar**

👉 Example: The two proposals were remarkably similar in scope, though they differed considerably in methodology.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee noted that the two candidates possessed **similar** qualifications, making the final selection exceptionally difficult.
   委员会注意到两位候选人资质相似，这使得最终遴选异常困难。

2. Researchers found that **similar** environmental conditions in both regions produced nearly identical patterns of vegetation growth.
   研究人员发现，两个地区相似的环境条件产生了几乎相同的植被生长模式。

3. The architectural style of the new library is **similar** to that of classical European designs from the Renaissance period.
   新图书馆的建筑风格与文艺复兴时期欧洲古典设计风格相似。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "similar" as "simil-ar" from Latin "similis" meaning "like" — things that are similar share a likeness, like a simile in literature.
把"similar"想成来自拉丁语"similis"意为"像"——相似的事物之间有共同之处，就像文学中的明喻（simile）。
