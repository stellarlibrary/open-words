# 😒 begrudge

🔊 /bɪˈɡrʌdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To envy someone the possession or enjoyment of something; to give reluctantly or resentfully
**中文：** v. 羡慕，嫉妒；吝惜，舍不得给

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- envy（嫉妒）
- resent（怨恨）
- grudge（吝惜）
- covet（觊觎）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **begrudge someone something / begrudge every penny / begrudge the time spent**

👉 Example: She did not begrudge her colleague the promotion, though she had worked equally hard.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board members did not **begrudge** the substantial investment required to modernize the aging infrastructure.
   董事会成员并不吝惜对老化基础设施进行现代化改造所需的大量投资。

2. Citizens should not **begrudge** funding for public education, as it yields profound long-term societal benefits.
   公民不应吝惜对公共教育的资助，因为它会产生深远的长期社会效益。

3. He openly **begrudged** his rival the prestigious award, believing his own research was far more original.
   他公开嫉妒对手获得的那项著名奖项，认为自己的研究远更具独创性。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "be-grudge" — to hold a grudge about giving something, so you resent or envy others for having it.
把"begrudge"想成"be-grudge"——对给予某物心怀怨恨，因此嫉妒或舍不得给别人。
