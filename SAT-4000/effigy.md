# 🗿 effigy

🔊 /ˈɛfɪdʒi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A sculpture or model of a person; a crude representation of someone, often used in protest or ritual
**中文：** n. 雕像；肖像；模拟像

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- statue（雕像）
- likeness（肖像）
- figure（人像）
- image（形象）
- dummy（人偶）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **burn in effigy / hang in effigy / an effigy of / stone effigy**

👉 Example: Angry demonstrators burned the dictator in effigy outside the embassy to express their outrage.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Protesters gathered in the central square to burn an **effigy** of the corrupt governor as a symbol of their collective outrage.
   抗议者聚集在中央广场焚烧腐败州长的模拟像，以此作为他们集体愤怒的象征。

2. The medieval tomb featured a carved stone **effigy** of the knight lying in full armor with his hands clasped in prayer.
   这座中世纪墓葬中有一尊骑士的石雕像，他身穿全套盔甲，双手合十祈祷。

3. The museum's collection includes a remarkably well-preserved wax **effigy** of the queen dating back to the sixteenth century.
   博物馆的藏品中包括一尊保存极为完好的女王蜡像，可追溯至十六世纪。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "effigy" as "a figure of somebody" — an effigy is a figure or image created to represent a real person, often burned in protest.
把"effigy"想成"某人的塑像"——effigy是为代表真人而创建的形象或图像，常在抗议中被焚烧。
