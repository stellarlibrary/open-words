# 📜 myth

🔊 /mɪθ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A traditional story explaining natural or social phenomena, often involving supernatural beings; a widely held but false belief or idea
**中文：** n. 神话；虚构的人，虚构的事；虚构的理论，荒诞的说法

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- legend（传说）
- fable（寓言）
- folklore（民间传说）
- fallacy（谬论）
- misconception（误解）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **debunk a myth / popular myth / urban myth / myth and reality**

👉 Example: Scientists have long sought to debunk the myth that humans only use ten percent of their brains.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **myth** that classical music enhances infant intelligence has been thoroughly discredited by contemporary neuroscience research.
   古典音乐能提高婴儿智力的**神话**已被当代神经科学研究彻底推翻。

2. Ancient Greek **myths** served as powerful allegorical narratives that explained the origins of natural phenomena to early civilizations.
   古希腊**神话**作为强有力的寓言叙事，向早期文明解释了自然现象的起源。

3. The persistent **myth** of the self-made individual ignores the extensive social networks that enable professional success.
   白手起家的**神话**忽略了促成职业成功的广泛社会网络。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "myth" as "my-th(ought)" — my thought that isn't real, just an imaginary story or false belief.
把"myth"想成"my thought"——我的想法，但它不是真实的，只是一个虚构的故事或错误的信念。
