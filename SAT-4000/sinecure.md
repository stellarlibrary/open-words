# 💤 sinecure

🔊 /ˈsaɪnɪˌkjʊr/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A position or office that requires little or no work but provides a salary or income
**中文：** n. 工作清闲而报酬丰厚的职位，挂名职务，闲职

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cushy job（轻松的工作）
- honorary position（荣誉职位）
- nominal post（挂名职位）
- figurehead role（名义上的角色）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a political sinecure / hold a sinecure / given a sinecure / serve as a sinecure**

👉 Example: The ambassadorial post was widely regarded as a sinecure for loyal party supporters.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics argued that the newly created advisory role was merely a **sinecure** designed to reward the minister's political allies.
   批评者认为，新设立的顾问职位不过是一个为奖励部长政治盟友而设的闲职。

2. After decades of dedicated public service, he was quietly offered a **sinecure** with generous compensation and minimal duties.
   在数十年的忠诚公共服务之后，他被悄悄地提供了一个报酬丰厚而职责极少的闲职。

3. The university professorship had become a **sinecure**, as the holder was never required to teach or conduct research.
   这个大学教授职位已经变成了一个挂名职务，因为担任者从不需要教学或进行研究。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sine-cure" as Latin "sine cura" meaning "without care" — a job without any cares or responsibilities.
把"sinecure"想成拉丁语"sine cura"，意为"没有操心"——一份不需要操心或负责的工作。
