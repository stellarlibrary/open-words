# ⛔ repulse

🔊 /rɪˈpʌls/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To drive back by force; to reject with rudeness or coldness; to cause feelings of revulsion. (n.) The act of repelling or rejecting; a rebuff
**中文：** v. 拒绝；驱逐；憎恶 n. 拒绝；击退；排斥

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- repel（击退）
- rebuff（断然拒绝）
- reject（拒绝）
- disgust（使厌恶）
- drive back（驱退）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **repulse an attack / repulse an enemy / repulse advances / meet with a repulse**

👉 Example: The garrison managed to repulse the enemy assault despite being heavily outnumbered.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The defenders were able to **repulse** three successive waves of attack before reinforcements finally arrived at the fortress.
   守军在援军最终抵达堡垒之前成功击退了三波连续进攻。

2. His arrogant demeanor continued to **repulse** potential allies, leaving him increasingly isolated within the political coalition.
   他傲慢的态度不断排斥潜在的盟友，使他在政治联盟中日益孤立。

3. The diplomat's proposal met with an immediate **repulse** from the delegation, who found the terms entirely unacceptable.
   外交官的提议立即遭到代表团的拒绝，他们认为这些条件完全不可接受。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "repulse" as "re-pulse" — like a pulse pushing back, sending a force outward to drive something away or reject it.
把"repulse"想成"re-pulse"——像脉冲一样向外推，发出一股力量把某物驱开或拒绝掉。
