# 🔍 discern

🔊 /dɪˈsɜːrn/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To perceive or recognize something with the senses or intellect; to distinguish or differentiate between things
**中文：** v. 识别；领悟；认出；辨别

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- perceive（察觉）
- distinguish（辨别）
- detect（发现）
- recognize（认出）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **discern a pattern / discern the difference / discern the truth / difficult to discern**

👉 Example: It was difficult to discern the real motive behind his seemingly generous offer.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The analyst was able to **discern** a recurring pattern in the data that had eluded previous researchers for years.
   分析师能够识别出数据中一个多年来令其他研究人员困惑的反复出现的模式。

2. Without proper training, it is nearly impossible to **discern** authentic artifacts from expertly crafted modern reproductions.
   没有适当的训练，几乎不可能辨别出真正的文物和精心制作的现代仿品。

3. The court must **discern** whether the defendant acted with deliberate intent or merely through negligence and poor judgment.
   法庭必须辨别被告是蓄意行事还是仅仅出于疏忽和判断失误。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-cern" — "cern" sounds like "concern"; when you are concerned about something, you look closely to discern the truth.
把"discern"想成"分开(dis)+筛选(cern)"——把事物分开仔细筛选，就是辨别和识别的意思。
