# 🌑 overshadow

🔊 /ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cast a shadow over; to make seem less important or impressive by comparison; to dominate or diminish
**中文：** v. 遮蔽；使…失色；使阴暗；夺去…的光彩

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- eclipse（使黯然失色）
- outshine（胜过）
- diminish（削弱）
- dwarf（使相形见绌）
- obscure（遮蔽）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **overshadow an achievement / overshadowed by events / overshadow a rival / completely overshadowed**

👉 Example: Her remarkable academic achievements were overshadowed by the scandal involving her department.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The younger sibling's extraordinary talent in music consistently **overshadowed** the elder brother's considerable accomplishments in academic research.
   弟弟在音乐方面的非凡才华一直使哥哥在学术研究方面的卓越成就黯然失色。

2. The tragic events of that autumn **overshadowed** the nation's centennial celebration, casting a somber mood across every planned festivity.
   那个秋天的悲剧事件使国家的百年庆典黯然失色，为每一项计划中的庆祝活动蒙上了阴影。

3. The towering skyscraper **overshadowed** the historic cathedral, both literally blocking its sunlight and figuratively diminishing its cultural prominence.
   这座高耸的摩天大楼使历史悠久的大教堂黯然失色，既遮挡了它的阳光，也削弱了它的文化地位。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "over-shadow" — something so large it casts its "shadow over" everything else, making other things seem dim and unimportant.
把"overshadow"想成某物大到把"影子投在"其他一切事物之上，使其他事物显得暗淡而不重要。
