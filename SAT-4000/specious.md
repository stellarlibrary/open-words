# 🎭 specious

🔊 /ˈspiːʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Seemingly plausible but actually wrong; superficially attractive but lacking real merit or substance.
**中文：** adj. 华而不实的，徒有其表的；似是而非的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- misleading（误导的）
- fallacious（谬误的）
- deceptive（欺骗性的）
- spurious（虚假的）
- plausible（貌似合理的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **specious argument / specious reasoning / specious claim / specious logic**

👉 Example: The defense attorney's specious argument initially sounded convincing but quickly fell apart under scrutiny.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician's **specious** promises of immediate economic recovery misled many voters during the campaign season.
   这位政客关于经济立即复苏的似是而非的承诺在竞选期间误导了许多选民。

2. Scholars dismissed the hypothesis as **specious** because it relied on outdated data and flawed statistical methods.
   学者们认为该假设华而不实，因为它依赖于过时的数据和有缺陷的统计方法。

3. The advertisement made **specious** claims about the product's ability to reverse aging within just two weeks.
   该广告对产品在两周内逆转衰老的能力做出了似是而非的声明。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "specious" as "species of lies" — it looks like truth on the surface but is actually a species of deception underneath.
把"specious"想成"species of lies"（谎言的品种）——表面看起来像真相，实际上是一种伪装的欺骗。
