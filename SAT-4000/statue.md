# 🗿 statue

🔊 /ˈstætʃuː/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A carved or cast figure of a person or animal, especially one that is life-size or larger. (v.) To adorn with statues
**中文：** n. 塑像，雕像

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sculpture（雕塑）
- figurine（小雕像）
- effigy（雕像）
- monument（纪念碑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **erect a statue / bronze statue / marble statue / statue of liberty**

👉 Example: The city council voted to erect a statue in honor of the renowned civil rights leader.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient marble **statue** discovered during the excavation was remarkably well preserved despite centuries of burial underground.
   在发掘过程中发现的古代大理石雕像尽管在地下埋藏了数个世纪，仍保存得非常完好。

2. The committee commissioned a bronze **statue** of the university's founder to be placed prominently in the main courtyard.
   委员会委托制作了一尊大学创始人的铜像，安放在主庭院的显眼位置。

3. Visitors often pause to admire the towering **statue** at the entrance of the national museum for its extraordinary craftsmanship.
   参观者经常驻足欣赏国家博物馆入口处那座高耸的雕像，赞叹其非凡的工艺。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "statue" as "stay true" — a statue stays true to the form of the person it represents, frozen in time forever.
把"statue"想成"stay true"——雕像忠实地保留了所代表之人的形态，永远定格在时间中。
