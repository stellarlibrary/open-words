# 💭 connotation

🔊 /ˌkɒnəˈteɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An idea or feeling that a word invokes in addition to its literal or primary meaning; an implied or associated meaning.
**中文：** n. 内涵；含蓄；暗示，隐含意义

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- implication（暗示）
- undertone（言外之意）
- suggestion（暗示）
- overtone（弦外之音）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **negative connotation / positive connotation / cultural connotation / carry a connotation**

👉 Example: The word "home" carries warm connotations of comfort and safety beyond its literal meaning of a dwelling.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The word "thrifty" has a positive **connotation**, whereas "stingy" conveys a distinctly negative impression of the same behavior.
   "thrifty"一词具有积极的内涵，而"stingy"则对同一行为传达出明显的负面印象。

2. Skilled writers carefully consider the **connotation** of each word to ensure their language evokes the intended emotional response.
   技巧娴熟的作家仔细考虑每个词的内涵，以确保其语言唤起预期的情感反应。

3. The political term acquired a deeply unfavorable **connotation** after it became associated with corruption and governmental misconduct.
   该政治术语在与腐败和政府不当行为联系起来后，获得了极为不利的隐含意义。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con-notation" as "with (con) extra notes" — a connotation is the extra meaning that comes with a word beyond its dictionary definition.
把"connotation"想成"带有(con)额外注释(notation)"——内涵就是一个词在字典定义之外所附带的额外含义。
