# 🌊 cataclysm

🔊 /ˈkætəklɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A large-scale and violent disaster or upheaval; a sudden, devastating event such as a flood or earthquake; a momentous transformation
**中文：** n. 灾难；大洪水；地震；大变动

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- catastrophe（灾难）
- calamity（灾祸）
- upheaval（剧变）
- deluge（大洪水）
- disaster（灾害）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **natural cataclysm / political cataclysm / global cataclysm / cataclysm of war**

👉 Example: The volcanic eruption was a cataclysm that reshaped the entire geography of the region.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The asteroid impact sixty-six million years ago was a **cataclysm** that led to the mass extinction of the dinosaurs.
   六千六百万年前的小行星撞击是一场导致恐龙大规模灭绝的大灾难。

2. Historians regard the revolution as a **cataclysm** that fundamentally altered the nation's political and social institutions forever.
   历史学家将这场革命视为一场从根本上永远改变了国家政治和社会制度的大变动。

3. The financial **cataclysm** of the early twentieth century devastated global markets and plunged millions of people into poverty.
   二十世纪初的金融灾难摧毁了全球市场，使数百万人陷入贫困。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cata-clysm" as "cat-a-clash" — imagine a catastrophic clash so powerful it destroys everything, like a cataclysm.
把"cataclysm"想成"cat-a-clash"——想象一场灾难性的冲撞，如此强大以至于摧毁一切，就像一场大灾变。
