# 🗣️ recriminate

🔊 /rɪˈkrɪmɪneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make counteraccusations; to respond to an accusation by accusing the accuser in return
**中文：** v. 反责；反唇相讥

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- countercharge（反控）
- retaliate（报复）
- retort（反驳）
- accuse in return（反过来指责）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **recriminate against / mutual recrimination / recriminate bitterly / begin to recriminate**

👉 Example: Rather than resolving the conflict, both parties continued to recriminate against each other publicly.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Instead of seeking a productive compromise, the two political factions continued to **recriminate** during the heated parliamentary session.
   两个政治派别在激烈的议会会议中没有寻求建设性的妥协，而是继续互相指责。

2. The divorcing couple spent most of their court appearances choosing to **recriminate** rather than negotiate a fair settlement.
   这对离婚夫妇在大部分出庭时间里选择互相指责，而非协商一个公平的解决方案。

3. Historians note that the allied nations began to **recriminate** openly once the shared threat of the common enemy had dissipated.
   历史学家指出，一旦共同敌人的威胁消失，盟国就开始公开互相指责。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-criminate" as "crime back" — when someone accuses you of a crime, you throw the accusation right back at them.
把"recriminate"想成"re-crime"——当别人指控你犯罪时，你把指控原封不动地丢回去，即反唇相讥。
