# ✊ dissent

🔊 /dɪˈsɛnt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To disagree with a widely held opinion or an official decision. (n.) The expression of disagreement with prevailing views
**中文：** v. 持异议；不同意 n. 异议；不同意见

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disagree（不同意）
- protest（抗议）
- object（反对）
- opposition（反对）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **voice dissent / political dissent / dissent from a ruling / right to dissent**

👉 Example: Several justices chose to dissent from the majority opinion in the landmark ruling.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator openly expressed her **dissent** against the proposed legislation, citing its potential harm to vulnerable communities.
   这位参议员公开表达了对拟议立法的异议，指出其对弱势群体的潜在危害。

2. In a democratic society, citizens must retain the fundamental right to **dissent** without fear of governmental retaliation.
   在民主社会中，公民必须保留在不惧怕政府报复的情况下持异议的基本权利。

3. Two justices chose to **dissent** from the ruling, arguing that it violated established constitutional principles and legal precedents.
   两位大法官选择对该裁决提出异议，认为它违反了既定的宪法原则和法律先例。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-sent" as sending a different message — your "sent" opinion goes in a "dis" (different) direction.
把"dissent"想成发送不同的信息——你"sent"（发送）的意见朝着"dis"（不同的）方向。
