# 💥 trigger

🔊 /ˈtrɪɡər/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause an event or situation to happen or begin; to set off. (n.) A lever that releases a mechanism; something that initiates a process or reaction.
**中文：** v. 引发；引起；触发 n. 扳机；触发器；诱因

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- provoke（引发）
- spark（引起）
- activate（激活）
- catalyst（催化剂）
- stimulus（刺激物）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **trigger a response / trigger a crisis / pull the trigger / trigger point / trigger warning**

👉 Example: The sudden rise in oil prices triggered a chain reaction that destabilized financial markets worldwide.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The environmental report warned that continued deforestation could **trigger** irreversible changes in global weather patterns and ecosystems.
   环境报告警告说，持续的森林砍伐可能引发全球天气模式和生态系统不可逆转的变化。

2. Scientists identified a specific genetic mutation that acts as a **trigger** for the onset of the rare autoimmune disorder.
   科学家们确定了一种特定的基因突变，它是这种罕见自身免疫疾病发作的触发因素。

3. The controversial policy announcement **triggered** widespread protests across the country, drawing millions of citizens into the streets.
   这项有争议的政策公告引发了全国范围内的大规模抗议，数百万公民走上街头。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of pulling a "trigger" on a gun — one small action triggers a powerful chain reaction. The word always implies starting something bigger.
想象扣动枪的"扳机"——一个小动作触发了强大的连锁反应。这个词总是暗示着启动更大的事情。
