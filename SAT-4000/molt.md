# 🪶 molt

🔊 /moʊlt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To shed feathers, skin, hair, or an outer covering periodically as part of a natural growth process. (n.) The process or period of shedding
**中文：** v. 脱毛；换毛 n. 换毛；脱皮；换毛期

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- shed（脱落）
- slough（蜕皮）
- exuviate（蜕皮）
- cast off（脱去）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **molt feathers / molt its skin / during the molt / molting season**

👉 Example: Many birds molt their feathers in late summer to prepare for the demands of autumn migration.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The emperor penguin will **molt** all of its feathers simultaneously, remaining on land until the new plumage has fully grown.
   帝企鹅会同时脱掉所有羽毛，一直留在陆地上直到新羽完全长出。

2. Arthropods must **molt** their rigid exoskeletons several times throughout their life cycle in order to continue growing.
   节肢动物在其生命周期中必须多次蜕去坚硬的外骨骼才能继续生长。

3. During the annual **molt**, the deer shed their antler velvet, revealing the hard bone structure beneath the soft outer layer.
   在每年的换角期，鹿脱去鹿茸上的绒皮，露出柔软外层下的坚硬骨骼结构。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "molt" as sounding like "melt" — imagine an animal's old skin or feathers melting away to reveal a fresh new layer underneath.
把"molt"想成听起来像"melt（融化）"——想象动物旧的皮肤或羽毛融化脱落，露出下面崭新的一层。
