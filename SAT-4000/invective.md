# 🗯️ invective

🔊 /ɪnˈvɛktɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Abusive or denouncing in language. (n.) Insulting, abusive, or highly critical language; a violent verbal attack
**中文：** adj. 谩骂的；恶言的；非难的 n. 恶言漫骂；猛烈抨击

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- vituperation（辱骂）
- tirade（长篇激烈的演说）
- diatribe（抨击）
- abuse（辱骂）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **hurl invective / stream of invective / bitter invective / political invective / personal invective**

👉 Example: The debate quickly deteriorated into a bitter exchange of personal invective between the two candidates.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The opposition leader hurled **invective** at the ruling party, accusing them of systematic corruption and deliberate negligence.
   反对派领导人对执政党进行了猛烈抨击，指责他们系统性腐败和蓄意渎职。

2. Literary critics noted that the author's prose was laced with sharp **invective** directed at the hypocrisy of contemporary society.
   文学评论家指出，该作者的散文中充满了对当代社会虚伪行为的尖锐讽刺。

3. Rather than offering constructive criticism, the reviewer resorted to personal **invective** that undermined her professional credibility entirely.
   这位评论者没有提出建设性的批评，而是诉诸人身攻击，完全损害了她的专业信誉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "invective" as "in + vect (carry)" — words carried in to attack someone, like verbal missiles hurled at a target.
把"invective"想成"in（向内）+ vect（携带）"——把攻击性的话语投向某人，就像语言导弹射向目标。
