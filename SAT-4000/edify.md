# 📚 edify

🔊 /ˈɛdɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To instruct or enlighten someone morally or intellectually; to uplift through knowledge or education
**中文：** v. 启发；教化；熏陶；教诲

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- enlighten（启发）
- instruct（教导）
- illuminate（启迪）
- uplift（提升）
- educate（教育）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **edify the audience / edify the mind / edify and inspire / edifying experience**

👉 Example: The documentary sought to edify viewers about the complex history of civil rights movements worldwide.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor's lectures never failed to **edify** her students, challenging them to reconsider their assumptions about history.
   教授的讲座总能启发她的学生，促使他们重新审视自己对历史的假设。

2. Great literature has the power to **edify** readers by exposing them to diverse perspectives and profound moral questions.
   伟大的文学作品能够通过让读者接触多元视角和深刻的道德问题来启迪他们。

3. The museum curated an exhibition specifically designed to **edify** young visitors about the principles of scientific inquiry.
   博物馆策划了一个专门旨在教育年轻参观者了解科学探究原则的展览。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "edify" as building an "edifice" of knowledge — just as an edifice is a grand building, to edify is to build up someone's mind and character.
把"edify"想成建造知识的"edifice"（大厦）——正如edifice是一座宏伟的建筑，edify就是构建一个人的思想和品格。
