# 💰 reimburse

🔊 /ˌriːɪmˈbɜːrs/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To pay back or compensate someone for money they have spent or losses they have incurred; to make repayment.
**中文：** v. 偿还；赔偿；补偿

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- repay（偿还）
- compensate（补偿）
- refund（退款）
- indemnify（赔偿）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reimburse someone for / reimburse expenses / reimburse the cost / fully reimbursed**

👉 Example: The company will reimburse employees for all reasonable travel expenses incurred during business trips.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The insurance company agreed to **reimburse** the policyholder for all medical expenses resulting from the documented workplace injury.
   保险公司同意偿还投保人因记录在案的工伤而产生的所有医疗费用。

2. The university policy requires students to submit original receipts before the institution will **reimburse** them for conference-related costs.
   大学政策要求学生提交原始收据，之后学校才会报销与会议相关的费用。

3. After the product recall, the manufacturer pledged to **reimburse** affected consumers for the full purchase price without delay.
   产品召回后，制造商承诺将立即向受影响的消费者全额退还购买价格。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-im-burse" as "re + in + purse" — putting money back into someone's purse, paying them back what they spent.
把"reimburse"想成"re（再次）+ im（进入）+ burse（钱包）"——把钱放回某人的钱包里，即偿还他们花出去的钱。
