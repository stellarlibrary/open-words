# 🔮 expect

🔊 /ɪkˈspekt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To regard something as likely to happen; to anticipate or look forward to; to consider something as due or required
**中文：** v. 期待；预期；盼望；指望；认为

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- anticipate（预料）
- await（等待）
- foresee（预见）
- presume（推测）
- count on（指望）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **expect results / expect someone to do something / expect the unexpected / be expected to**

👉 Example: Analysts expect the central bank to raise interest rates at the upcoming quarterly policy meeting.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Researchers **expect** the clinical trial results to demonstrate significant improvements in patient outcomes within the next two years.
   研究人员预计临床试验结果将在未来两年内显示出患者预后的显著改善。

2. The board of directors **expects** all senior executives to adhere strictly to the organization's newly revised code of ethics.
   董事会要求所有高级管理人员严格遵守该组织新修订的道德准则。

3. Citizens **expect** their elected representatives to address pressing social issues with transparency, competence, and unwavering accountability.
   公民期望他们选出的代表以透明、能力和坚定的问责态度来解决紧迫的社会问题。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-spect" — "spect" means to look (like spectator); to expect is to look ahead at what you believe will happen.
把"expect"想成"ex-spect"——"spect"意为看（如spectator观众）；expect就是向前看你认为将会发生的事情。
