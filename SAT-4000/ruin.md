# 🏚️ ruin

🔊 /ˈruːɪn/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The remains of a destroyed building or city; destruction or collapse; financial downfall. (v.) To destroy completely; to cause financial bankruptcy
**中文：** v. 破产；堕落，毁灭 n. 毁灭，崩溃；废墟，遗迹

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- destruction（毁灭）
- devastation（破坏）
- downfall（衰败）
- wreckage（残骸）
- demolish（摧毁）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in ruins / fall into ruin / ruin one's reputation / financial ruin / the ruins of**

👉 Example: The prolonged economic recession threatened to ruin countless small businesses across the region.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ancient castle has fallen into **ruin** over the centuries, yet its crumbling walls still attract thousands of tourists annually.
   这座古堡几个世纪以来已沦为废墟，但其残垣断壁每年仍吸引着成千上万的游客。

2. A single act of dishonesty can **ruin** a professional reputation that took an entire career to build and cultivate.
   一次不诚实的行为可以毁掉一个用整个职业生涯建立和培养起来的专业声誉。

3. Archaeologists painstakingly excavated the **ruins** of the Roman settlement, uncovering artifacts that illuminated daily life in antiquity.
   考古学家们辛苦地发掘了罗马定居点的遗迹，出土的文物揭示了古代的日常生活。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ruin" as "rain + uin" — imagine heavy rain pouring down on a building until it becomes a ruin, destroyed and collapsed.
把"ruin"想成"rain（雨）+ uin"——想象大雨倾盆而下，直到建筑变成废墟，被摧毁和倒塌。
