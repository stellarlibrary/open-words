# 💥 disastrous

🔊 /dɪˈzæstrəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Causing great damage, destruction, or suffering; having extremely unfortunate consequences
**中文：** adj. 灾难性的；损失惨重的；悲惨的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- catastrophic（灾难性的）
- devastating（毁灭性的）
- calamitous（灾难的）
- ruinous（毁灭性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **disastrous consequences / disastrous effects / disastrous results / prove disastrous**

👉 Example: The disastrous flood displaced thousands of families and destroyed critical infrastructure.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The company's **disastrous** decision to expand without adequate research led to bankruptcy within eighteen months.
   公司在没有充分调研的情况下扩张的灾难性决定导致其在十八个月内破产。

2. Environmentalists warned that continued deforestation would have **disastrous** consequences for biodiversity and global climate patterns.
   环保人士警告说，持续的森林砍伐将对生物多样性和全球气候模式产生灾难性后果。

3. The general's **disastrous** military campaign resulted in the loss of nearly half the regiment and a humiliating retreat.
   将军灾难性的军事行动导致近一半军团阵亡，并被迫屈辱撤退。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-astr-ous" — "astr" resembles "astro" (star); in ancient times, a bad star alignment meant disaster was coming.
把"disastrous"想成"坏的(dis)+星星(astr)"——在古代，星象不吉意味着灾难即将来临。
