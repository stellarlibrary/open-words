# ⚡ polarize

🔊 /ˈpoʊləraɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To divide into two sharply opposing groups or sets of opinions; (physics) to cause light waves to vibrate in a single plane
**中文：** v. （使）两极分化；（使）极化；（使）偏振

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- divide（分裂）
- split（使分裂）
- separate（使分离）
- alienate（使疏远）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **polarize opinion / polarize the electorate / polarize debate / deeply polarize**

👉 Example: The controversial ruling is likely to polarize voters ahead of the upcoming election.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The proposed legislation on immigration reform has **polarized** the nation into fiercely opposing ideological camps.
   关于移民改革的拟议立法使全国分裂为激烈对立的意识形态阵营。

2. Inflammatory rhetoric from political leaders tends to **polarize** communities rather than foster meaningful reconciliation and unity.
   政治领导人的煽动性言论往往使社区两极分化，而非促进有意义的和解与团结。

3. The documentary aimed to inform the public, but critics argued it would only **polarize** an already divided audience.
   这部纪录片旨在告知公众，但批评人士认为它只会使本已分裂的观众更加两极分化。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "polarize" as making people go to opposite "poles" — like magnets pushing apart, opinions are forced to extremes.
把"polarize"想成让人们各奔"两极"——就像磁铁互相排斥一样，观点被推向极端。
