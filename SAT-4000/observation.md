# 🔭 observation

🔊 /ˌɑːbzərˈveɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The act of carefully watching or monitoring; a remark or comment based on something one has seen or noticed; the gathering of information through direct examination
**中文：** n. 观察；监视；观察报告

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- examination（检查）
- surveillance（监视）
- remark（评论）
- scrutiny（审视）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **make an observation / under observation / keen observation / observation of phenomena**

👉 Example: The scientist's careful observation of the experiment revealed unexpected results.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Through meticulous **observation** of celestial movements, early astronomers were able to predict eclipses with remarkable accuracy.
   通过对天体运动的仔细观察，早期天文学家能够以惊人的精确度预测日食。

2. The patient was placed under continuous **observation** in the intensive care unit following the complex surgical procedure.
   在复杂的外科手术后，患者被安置在重症监护室进行持续观察。

3. Her astute **observation** about the correlation between economic inequality and social unrest sparked a vigorous academic debate.
   她关于经济不平等与社会动荡之间相关性的敏锐观察引发了一场激烈的学术辩论。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "observation" as "observe + station" — a station where you stand to observe and watch things carefully, like an observation deck.
把"observation"想成"observe + station"——一个你站在那里仔细观察事物的站点，就像观景台一样。
