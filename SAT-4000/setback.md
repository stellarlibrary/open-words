# 🚧 setback

🔊 /ˈsetbæk/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A reversal or check in progress; an obstacle or difficulty that delays or prevents advancement
**中文：** n. 挫折，阻碍

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- obstacle（障碍）
- hindrance（阻碍）
- reversal（逆转）
- impediment（妨碍）
- complication（困难）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **suffer a setback / major setback / temporary setback / overcome a setback / experience a setback**

👉 Example: The project suffered a significant setback when the principal investigator resigned unexpectedly.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The peace negotiations experienced a major **setback** when both parties refused to compromise on territorial boundaries.
   和平谈判遭遇了重大挫折，因为双方都拒绝在领土边界问题上妥协。

2. Despite the financial **setback** caused by the economic recession, the company managed to recover within two fiscal years.
   尽管经济衰退造成了财务上的挫折，该公司还是在两个财政年度内成功恢复。

3. The researchers viewed the failed experiment not as a permanent **setback** but as a valuable learning opportunity.
   研究人员将这次失败的实验视为宝贵的学习机会，而非永久性的挫折。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "setback" as being "set back" — your progress is literally set backward, pushed away from the goal you were approaching.
把"setback"想成被"set back"——你的进展被字面意义上地往后推了，远离了你正在接近的目标。
