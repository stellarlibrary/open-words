# 🗽 emancipate

🔊 /ɪˈmænsɪpeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To set free from legal, social, or political restrictions; to liberate from bondage or oppression
**中文：** v. 解放；释放；解除

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- liberate（解放）
- free（释放）
- release（解除）
- unshackle（解开束缚）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **emancipate slaves / emancipate from oppression / emancipate oneself / emancipate women**

👉 Example: The new legislation sought to emancipate workers from exploitative labor conditions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The proclamation was designed to **emancipate** millions of enslaved people and fundamentally reshape the nation's social structure.
   该宣言旨在解放数百万被奴役的人，并从根本上重塑国家的社会结构。

2. Education has long been regarded as a powerful tool to **emancipate** individuals from the constraints of poverty and ignorance.
   教育长期以来被视为将个人从贫困和无知的束缚中解放出来的有力工具。

3. The reform movement sought to **emancipate** citizens from outdated legal codes that had restricted their fundamental civil liberties.
   这场改革运动旨在将公民从限制其基本公民自由的过时法律中解放出来。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "e-MAN-cipate" — to take the "man" out of "captive." When you emancipate someone, you free them from captivity.
把"emancipate"想成"e-MAN-cipate"——把"人"从"囚禁"中带出来。解放就是让人重获自由。
