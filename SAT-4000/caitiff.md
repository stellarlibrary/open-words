# 😈 caitiff

🔊 /ˈkeɪtɪf/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Despicable and cowardly; base and contemptible. (n.) A despicable or cowardly person
**中文：** adj. 卑劣的；胆小的 n. 卑鄙的人；胆小鬼

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cowardly（胆小的）
- despicable（卑鄙的）
- craven（怯懦的）
- base（卑劣的）
- contemptible（可鄙的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **caitiff wretch / caitiff behavior / a miserable caitiff / caitiff coward**

👉 Example: The knight denounced the traitor as a caitiff who had betrayed his sworn oath of loyalty.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The medieval chronicle described the deserter as a **caitiff** knight unworthy of the honor bestowed upon his noble lineage.
   中世纪编年史将那名逃兵描述为一个卑鄙的骑士，不配其高贵血统所赋予的荣誉。

2. Only a **caitiff** would abandon defenseless civilians to pursue personal safety during such a devastating siege.
   只有胆小鬼才会在如此毁灭性的围困中抛弃手无寸铁的平民去追求个人安全。

3. Shakespeare often employed the word **caitiff** to emphasize the moral degradation and cowardice of villainous characters.
   莎士比亚经常使用"caitiff"一词来强调反面角色的道德堕落和怯懦。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "caitiff" as "KAY-tiff" — a "tiff" (quarrel) that a "Kay" (coward) runs away from, too base and scared to stand firm.
把"caitiff"想成"KAY-tiff"——一个"Kay（胆小鬼）"遇到"tiff（争吵）"就逃跑，太卑劣、太害怕而不敢坚持立场。
