# 🔄 paradoxical

🔊 /ˌpærəˈdɒksɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Seemingly contradictory or absurd but possibly true; having the nature of a paradox
**中文：** adj. 矛盾的；诡论的；似非而是的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- contradictory（矛盾的）
- incongruous（不协调的）
- ironic（讽刺的）
- absurd（荒谬的）
- puzzling（令人困惑的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **paradoxical situation / paradoxical effect / paradoxical nature / paradoxical result**

👉 Example: The medication had the paradoxical effect of making the patient more anxious rather than calm.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. It may seem **paradoxical**, but increased government regulation sometimes leads to greater innovation within the private sector.
   这看似矛盾，但增加政府监管有时反而会促进私营部门更大的创新。

2. The researcher noted the **paradoxical** finding that students who studied fewer hours often performed better on standardized examinations.
   研究人员注意到一个矛盾的发现：学习时间较少的学生在标准化考试中往往表现更好。

3. The **paradoxical** nature of light, behaving as both a particle and a wave, remains one of the central mysteries of physics.
   光既表现为粒子又表现为波的矛盾特性，仍然是物理学的核心奥秘之一。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "paradoxical" as "paradox" + "-ical" — it describes anything that has the quality of a paradox, something that contradicts itself yet holds truth.
把"paradoxical"想成"paradox"（悖论）+ "-ical"（形容词后缀）——描述任何具有悖论性质的事物，自相矛盾却又蕴含真理。
