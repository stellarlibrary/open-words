# 💔 bereft

🔊 /bɪˈrɛft/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Deprived of or lacking something, especially a nonmaterial quality; suffering the loss of a loved one
**中文：** adj. 被剥夺的；丧失的；失去亲人的；缺乏的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- deprived（被剥夺的）
- devoid（缺乏的）
- stripped（被剥除的）
- forlorn（孤苦的）
- destitute（匮乏的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bereft of hope / bereft of ideas / bereft of comfort / left bereft / utterly bereft**

👉 Example: After the company collapsed, the investors were left utterly bereft of their life savings.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The once-thriving village was now **bereft** of its young population, who had migrated to the cities seeking employment.
   这个曾经繁荣的村庄现在失去了年轻人口，他们已迁往城市寻找就业机会。

2. **Bereft** of any viable strategy, the general was forced to order a full retreat from the contested territory.
   由于缺乏任何可行的策略，将军被迫下令从争议领土全面撤退。

3. The widow stood **bereft** at the graveside, her silent grief conveying more than any words could possibly express.
   寡妇孤独地站在墓旁，她无声的悲伤比任何言语所能表达的都更深沉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bereft" as the past tense of "bereave" — once you have been bereaved (robbed of something), you are left bereft.
把"bereft"想成"bereave（丧失）"的过去分词——一旦你被夺走了什么，你就处于bereft（丧失的）状态。
