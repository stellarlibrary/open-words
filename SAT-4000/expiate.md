# 🙏 expiate

🔊 /ˈɛkspiˌeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make amends for wrongdoing; to atone for guilt or sin through suffering or good deeds
**中文：** v. 赎罪；补偿

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- atone（赎罪）
- redeem（弥补）
- amend（补偿）
- rectify（纠正）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **expiate one's sins / expiate guilt / expiate a crime / expiate wrongdoing**

👉 Example: He devoted the rest of his life to charitable work in an effort to expiate his past crimes.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The convicted official sought to **expiate** his crimes by dedicating years of community service to underprivileged neighborhoods.
   这位被定罪的官员试图通过多年为贫困社区提供社区服务来赎罪。

2. Many ancient cultures practiced elaborate rituals designed to **expiate** collective guilt and restore spiritual harmony within society.
   许多古代文化通过精心设计的仪式来赎清集体罪过并恢复社会的精神和谐。

3. She felt that no amount of apology could truly **expiate** the damage her careless words had inflicted on others.
   她觉得再多的道歉也无法真正弥补她不经意的话语对他人造成的伤害。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "expi-ate" as "exit + pay" — you pay for your wrongs so they can exit your conscience, leaving you free from guilt.
把"expiate"想成"exit + pay"——你为过错付出代价，让罪恶感从良心中消退，使你摆脱负罪感。
