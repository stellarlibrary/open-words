# 🔍 criticize

🔊 /ˈkrɪtɪˌsaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To express disapproval of someone or something; to evaluate or analyze the merits and faults of a work or idea
**中文：** v. 批评；评论；非难

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- censure（谴责）
- denounce（公开指责）
- evaluate（评价）
- reproach（责备）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **criticize someone for / criticize harshly / criticize publicly / openly criticize**

👉 Example: Several prominent scholars publicly criticized the government's proposed education reform plan.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The editorial board **criticized** the administration's environmental policy for lacking measurable goals and clear accountability.
   编辑委员会批评了政府环境政策缺乏可衡量的目标和明确的问责机制。

2. Literary analysts often **criticize** the novel for its inconsistent narrative structure and underdeveloped supporting characters.
   文学分析人士常常批评这部小说叙事结构不一致且配角形象不够丰满。

3. It is important to **criticize** constructively rather than merely finding fault without offering viable alternative solutions.
   重要的是进行建设性的批评，而不是仅仅挑毛病却不提供可行的替代方案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "critic-ize" — a critic is someone who judges, so to criticize is to act like a critic and point out flaws.
把"criticize"想成"critic（评论家）+ ize（使）"——评论家是评判的人，所以criticize就是像评论家一样指出缺陷。
