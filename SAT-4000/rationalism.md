# 📚 rationalism

🔊 /ˈræʃənəlɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The philosophical doctrine that reason, rather than experience or authority, is the foundation of knowledge and truth
**中文：** n. 理性主义；唯理主义

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- intellectualism（理智主义）
- reasoning（推理）
- logic（逻辑）
- freethinking（自由思想）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **embrace rationalism / age of rationalism / philosophical rationalism / scientific rationalism**

👉 Example: The Enlightenment was deeply rooted in rationalism and the pursuit of knowledge through reason.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosopher Descartes is widely regarded as the father of modern **rationalism** in Western intellectual tradition.
   哲学家笛卡尔被广泛认为是西方思想传统中现代理性主义之父。

2. **Rationalism** emphasizes that certain truths can be discovered through pure reason without reliance on sensory experience.
   理性主义强调某些真理可以通过纯粹的理性发现，而无需依赖感官经验。

3. The debate between **rationalism** and empiricism has shaped the development of epistemology for several centuries.
   理性主义与经验主义之间的争论已经影响了认识论数个世纪的发展。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rational-ism" — the "-ism" turns "rational" into a belief system: the belief that reason is supreme.
把"rationalism"想成"rational（理性的）+ ism（主义）"——用理性作为最高信仰的主义。
