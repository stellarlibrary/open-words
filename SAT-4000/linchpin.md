# 🔑 linchpin

🔊 /ˈlɪntʃˌpɪn/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person or thing that is essential to an enterprise or organization; originally, a pin passed through an axle to keep a wheel in position.
**中文：** n. 关键；关键人物；制轮楔；轮辖

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cornerstone（基石）
- keystone（关键）
- mainstay（支柱）
- anchor（核心）
- crux（关键点）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **the linchpin of / serve as a linchpin / act as the linchpin / a linchpin in**

👉 Example: Effective communication serves as the linchpin of any successful organizational strategy.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senior diplomat served as the **linchpin** of the fragile peace negotiations between the two historically adversarial nations.
   这位资深外交官是两个历史上敌对国家之间脆弱和平谈判的关键人物。

2. Reliable infrastructure investment remains the **linchpin** of sustained economic development in rapidly industrializing regions across the globe.
   可靠的基础设施投资仍然是全球快速工业化地区持续经济发展的关键。

3. The newly appointed director quickly became the **linchpin** of the research initiative, coordinating efforts across multiple departments.
   新任命的主任迅速成为该研究项目的核心，协调多个部门的工作。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "linch + pin" — a small pin that holds a big wheel in place; without it, everything falls apart.
把"linchpin"想成"linch（轮轴）+ pin（销钉）"——一个小销钉固定住大轮子，没有它一切都会散架。
