# 🏳️ capitulate

🔊 /kəˈpɪtʃʊˌleɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cease to resist; to surrender, often under agreed-upon conditions; to yield or give in.
**中文：** vi. 有条件投降；屈服；停止抵抗

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- surrender（投降）
- yield（屈服）
- submit（顺从）
- concede（让步）
- acquiesce（默许）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **capitulate to demands / capitulate to pressure / refuse to capitulate / forced to capitulate**

👉 Example: After weeks of mounting pressure, the besieged garrison finally capitulated to the opposing forces.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government refused to **capitulate** to the terrorists' demands, maintaining a firm stance throughout the prolonged crisis.
   政府拒绝向恐怖分子的要求屈服，在整个漫长的危机中保持了坚定立场。

2. After exhausting all legal avenues, the corporation was forced to **capitulate** and accept the regulatory commission's ruling.
   在穷尽所有法律途径后，该公司被迫屈服并接受监管委员会的裁决。

3. The negotiator warned that to **capitulate** too readily would undermine their credibility in all future diplomatic engagements.
   谈判代表警告说，过于轻易地屈服将损害他们在未来所有外交活动中的信誉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "capital" — when you capitulate, you surrender your capital (resources and power) to the victor under negotiated terms.
把"capitulate"联想为"capital（资本）"——投降时，你在谈判条件下把资本（资源和权力）交给胜利者。
