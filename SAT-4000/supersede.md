# 🔄 supersede

🔊 /ˌsuːpərˈsiːd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To take the place of; to replace; to come immediately after
**中文：** v. 代替，取代

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- replace（替换）
- supplant（取代）
- displace（取代）
- succeed（继任）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **supersede a law / supersede an older version / supersede a previous decision / be superseded by**

👉 Example: The new regulation will supersede all previous guidelines on environmental compliance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The recently enacted legislation will **supersede** the outdated statutes that have governed trade policy for over two decades.
   新颁布的立法将取代已经管辖贸易政策二十多年的过时法规。

2. Digital communication tools have largely **superseded** traditional methods of correspondence in modern corporate environments.
   数字通信工具在现代企业环境中已在很大程度上取代了传统的通信方式。

3. The professor's revised theory **superseded** his earlier model, offering a more comprehensive explanation of the phenomenon.
   这位教授修订后的理论取代了他早期的模型，对该现象提供了更全面的解释。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "super-sede" as "super sit" (from Latin sedere, to sit) — someone superior comes to sit in your seat, replacing you.
把"supersede"想成"super sit"（来自拉丁语sedere，坐）——一个更优秀的人来坐你的位置，取代了你。
