# 🛐 idolatry

🔊 /aɪˈdɒlətri/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The worship of idols or images; extreme admiration or devotion to someone or something
**中文：** n. 偶像崇拜；盲目崇拜

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- worship（崇拜）
- veneration（敬仰）
- adulation（谄媚）
- reverence（尊崇）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **idolatry of wealth / idolatry of celebrities / commit idolatry / border on idolatry**

👉 Example: The philosopher argued that modern consumer culture encourages a form of idolatry directed toward material possessions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Several religious traditions explicitly prohibit **idolatry**, instructing followers to direct their devotion solely toward the divine rather than physical objects.
   几种宗教传统明确禁止偶像崇拜，要求信徒只将虔诚奉献给神灵而非实物。

2. The critic described the public's fascination with the billionaire as a dangerous form of **idolatry** that discouraged legitimate scrutiny.
   评论家将公众对那位亿万富翁的迷恋描述为一种危险的盲目崇拜，阻碍了正当的审视。

3. Historians have noted that **idolatry** in ancient civilizations often served complex social and political functions beyond mere religious practice.
   历史学家指出，古代文明中的偶像崇拜通常具有超越单纯宗教实践的复杂社会和政治功能。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "idol + atry" — the practice (-atry) of worshipping an idol, like treating someone as a god.
把"idolatry"拆成"idol（偶像）+ atry（行为）"——就是崇拜偶像的行为，像把某人当作神一样。
