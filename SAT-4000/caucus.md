# 🏛️ caucus

🔊 /ˈkɔːkəs/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A closed meeting of members of a political party or faction to select candidates or decide policy; a group within a legislative body. (v.) To hold or attend such a meeting
**中文：** n. 干部会议；核心会议；领导人秘密会议 v. 召开干部会议

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- assembly（集会）
- convention（大会）
- faction（派系）
- conclave（秘密会议）
- council（会议）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **party caucus / congressional caucus / caucus meeting / caucus with members**

👉 Example: The party leaders held a caucus to determine their strategy before the legislative vote.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The congressional **caucus** convened behind closed doors to formulate a unified position on the proposed immigration reform legislation.
   国会核心小组会议在闭门状态下召开，以就拟议的移民改革立法制定统一立场。

2. In the Iowa presidential **caucus**, registered voters gather in precincts to publicly declare their support for a candidate.
   在爱荷华州总统初选会议中，注册选民在选区集合公开表明对某位候选人的支持。

3. The minority party decided to **caucus** with independent legislators in an effort to build a viable coalition against the bill.
   少数党决定与独立立法者召开核心会议，以努力建立一个可行的联盟来反对该法案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "caucus" as "cause-us" — a group gets together because a common cause unites us to make political decisions together.
把"caucus"想成"cause-us"——一群人聚在一起，因为共同的事业（cause）将我们（us）联合起来共同做出政治决策。
