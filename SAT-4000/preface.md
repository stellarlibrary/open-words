# 📖 preface

🔊 /ˈprɛfɪs/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An introduction to a book or speech. (v.) To introduce or begin a speech or event with preliminary remarks
**中文：** n. 前言；引语；序言 v. 为…加序言；以…开始

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- introduction（引言）
- foreword（前言）
- preamble（序言）
- prologue（序幕）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **preface a book / preface remarks with / write a preface / in the preface**

👉 Example: The author chose to preface her novel with a personal anecdote about her childhood in rural France.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. In the **preface** to his memoir, the diplomat acknowledged the many colleagues who had shaped his understanding of foreign policy.
   在回忆录的前言中，这位外交官感谢了许多塑造了他外交政策理解的同事们。

2. She decided to **preface** her presentation with a brief summary of the research methodology used throughout the study.
   她决定在演讲开头先简要概述整个研究中使用的研究方法。

3. The **preface** of the academic journal outlined the editorial standards and peer-review procedures adopted for that special issue.
   该学术期刊的前言概述了该特刊所采用的编辑标准和同行评审程序。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-face" as the "face before" — the first face you see before entering the main content, like a greeting at the door.
把"preface"想成"pre（之前）+ face（面孔）"——在进入正文之前看到的第一张面孔，就像门口的迎接。
