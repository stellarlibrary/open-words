# ⏱️ interval

🔊 /ˈɪntərvəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A space of time between two events or actions; a pause or gap; a break between parts of a performance
**中文：** n. 间隔；间距；幕间休息

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gap（间隙）
- pause（暂停）
- intermission（幕间休息）
- span（跨度）
- break（间歇）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **at regular intervals / at intervals of / a brief interval / during the interval**

👉 Example: The doctor recommended checking blood pressure at regular intervals throughout the day.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The meteorological station recorded temperature readings at thirty-minute **intervals** to ensure accurate climate data for the research project.
   气象站每隔三十分钟记录一次温度读数，以确保研究项目的气候数据准确。

2. There was a brief **interval** between the two acts of the opera, during which the audience discussed the stunning performance.
   歌剧的两幕之间有一个短暂的中场休息，观众在此期间讨论了这场精彩的演出。

3. The scientist observed that volcanic eruptions in the region occurred at irregular **intervals**, making precise prediction extremely difficult.
   科学家观察到该地区的火山喷发以不规则的间隔发生，使得精确预测极为困难。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inter-val" as "inter" (between) + "val" (wall) — a wall of time standing between two events, creating a gap or pause.
把"interval"想成"inter"（之间）+ "val"（墙）——一堵时间之墙立在两个事件之间，形成一个间隔或停顿。
