# ✂️ separate

🔊 /ˈsepəreɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective, noun | 动词, 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause to move or be apart; to divide or disconnect. (adj.) Forming or viewed as a unit apart; individual and distinct. (n.) A thing that is separate; an offprint
**中文：** adj. 分开的 v. 分开

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- divide（分开）
- isolate（隔离）
- distinct（不同的）
- detach（分离）
- independent（独立的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **separate from / separate into / separate entity / on separate occasions / keep separate**

👉 Example: The teacher asked the students to separate into smaller groups for the collaborative research project.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The Supreme Court ruling established that church and state must remain **separate** to preserve fundamental democratic freedoms.
   最高法院的裁决确立了教会与国家必须保持分离以维护基本民主自由的原则。

2. Researchers must **separate** correlation from causation before drawing any definitive conclusions from observational study data.
   研究人员在从观察性研究数据得出任何确定性结论之前必须将相关性与因果关系分开。

3. The two departments operate as **separate** entities with distinct budgets, leadership structures, and strategic objectives.
   这两个部门作为独立实体运营，拥有各自不同的预算、领导结构和战略目标。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "separate" as "se-PAR-ate" — there is "a PAR" (a pair) in the middle being split apart, reminding you of division.
把"separate"想成"se-PAR-ate"——中间有"a PAR"（一对）被拆分开来，提醒你分离的含义。
