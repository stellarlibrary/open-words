# 🛡️ mitigate

🔊 /ˈmɪtɪɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make less severe, serious, or painful; to lessen the gravity or impact of something
**中文：** v. 使缓和；使减轻

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- alleviate（减轻）
- diminish（减少）
- lessen（减轻）
- ease（缓解）
- moderate（缓和）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mitigate the effects / mitigate risk / mitigate damage / mitigating circumstances / mitigate against**

👉 Example: Engineers designed the flood barriers specifically to mitigate the devastating effects of seasonal storms.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government introduced new regulations to **mitigate** the environmental damage caused by decades of industrial pollution.
   政府出台了新规定，以减轻数十年工业污染造成的环境损害。

2. Defense attorneys presented several **mitigating** factors to persuade the judge to impose a more lenient sentence.
   辩护律师提出了几个减轻处罚的因素，以说服法官判处更宽大的刑罚。

3. Early intervention programs can effectively **mitigate** the long-term consequences of childhood learning disabilities in students.
   早期干预项目能够有效减轻儿童学习障碍对学生造成的长期影响。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mitigate" as "make it a gate" — you build a gate to block and reduce the flow of problems coming your way.
把"mitigate"想成"make it a gate"——你建造一扇门来阻挡和减少涌向你的问题。
