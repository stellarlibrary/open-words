# 😠 opprobrium

🔊 /əˈproʊbriəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Harsh criticism or censure; public disgrace or infamy resulting from shameful conduct
**中文：** n. 耻辱，不名誉；责骂，咒骂

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disgrace（耻辱）
- infamy（恶名）
- reproach（责备）
- condemnation（谴责）
- stigma（污名）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **heap opprobrium on / face opprobrium / public opprobrium / bring opprobrium upon**

👉 Example: The executive's fraudulent dealings brought widespread opprobrium upon the entire corporation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician faced intense public **opprobrium** after revelations of corruption and misuse of government funds became widely known.
   在腐败和滥用政府资金的丑闻被广泛曝光后，这位政客面临了强烈的公众谴责。

2. Throughout history, whistleblowers have often endured considerable **opprobrium** before their courageous contributions were finally recognized and appreciated.
   纵观历史，举报人在其勇敢的贡献最终被认可和赞赏之前，往往要忍受相当大的骂名。

3. The scientist's discredited research earned her lasting **opprobrium** within the academic community and effectively ended her professional career.
   这位科学家被推翻的研究使她在学术界蒙受了持久的耻辱，并实际上终结了她的职业生涯。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "opprobrium" as "oh, PROBE him" — when someone is probed and exposed publicly, they face opprobrium and disgrace.
把"opprobrium"想成"oh, PROBE him（去调查他）"——当某人被公开调查和曝光时，他就会面临耻辱和谴责。
