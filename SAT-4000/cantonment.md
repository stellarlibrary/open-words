# 🏕️ cantonment

🔊 /kænˈtɒnmənt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A military camp or quarters; a permanent military station, especially in British India.
**中文：** n. 宿营地；军营；兵营

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- barracks（兵营）
- garrison（驻军地）
- encampment（营地）
- military base（军事基地）
- quarters（营房）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **military cantonment / cantonment area / establish a cantonment / cantonment board**

👉 Example: The British colonial administration established a cantonment on the outskirts of the city for stationing troops.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The regiment was ordered to withdraw to the **cantonment** and await further instructions from the commanding officer.
   该团奉命撤回兵营，等待指挥官的进一步指示。

2. During the colonial era, many Indian cities developed distinct civilian and **cantonment** areas with separate administrative structures.
   在殖民时代，许多印度城市形成了具有独立行政结构的平民区和军营区。

3. Archaeological excavations at the former **cantonment** site revealed artifacts that shed light on nineteenth-century military life.
   对前军营遗址的考古发掘揭示了反映十九世纪军事生活的文物。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "canton-ment" — a canton is a district, and a cantonment is a district set aside for military housing and operations.
把"cantonment"拆成"canton（区域）+ ment"——军营就是专门划出来供军队驻扎和行动的区域。
