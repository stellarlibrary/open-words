# 🕳️ gap

🔊 /ɡæp/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A break or opening in something; a space between two things; a difference or disparity between two situations, amounts, or groups
**中文：** n. 间隙；缺口；差距；隔阂；不足；空白

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- opening（开口）
- breach（缺口）
- disparity（差距）
- void（空白）
- chasm（鸿沟）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bridge the gap / close the gap / widen the gap / gap between / knowledge gap**

👉 Example: Education is often seen as the best way to bridge the gap between social classes.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The widening income **gap** between the wealthiest citizens and the working poor has become a central issue in national politics.
   最富有的公民与劳动贫困人口之间日益扩大的收入差距已成为国家政治的核心议题。

2. Researchers identified a significant **gap** in the existing literature, which their comprehensive longitudinal study aimed to address.
   研究人员发现了现有文献中的一个重大空白，他们的综合纵向研究旨在填补这一不足。

3. The ceasefire created a brief **gap** in hostilities that allowed humanitarian organizations to deliver essential supplies to civilians.
   停火在敌对行动中创造了短暂的间歇，使人道主义组织得以向平民运送必需物资。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gap" as the space you see when something is missing — like a gap in a fence or a gap in your knowledge that needs to be filled.
把"gap"想象成缺少某物时看到的空间——就像栅栏上的缺口或知识中需要填补的空白。
