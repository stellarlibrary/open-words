# 🔀 juxtapose

🔊 /ˌdʒʌkstəˈpoʊz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To place two or more things side by side, especially for comparison or contrast
**中文：** v. 并置；并列；把…并列放在一起以进行比较

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- compare（比较）
- contrast（对比）
- place side by side（并排放置）
- collocate（并置）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **juxtapose A with B / juxtapose two ideas / juxtapose images / juxtapose contrasting elements**

👉 Example: The documentary juxtaposes scenes of extreme wealth with those of devastating poverty.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The artist chose to **juxtapose** vibrant modern paintings with austere classical sculptures to highlight the evolution of aesthetic values.
   这位艺术家选择将充满活力的现代绘画与朴素的古典雕塑并置，以突出审美价值观的演变。

2. The essay effectively **juxtaposes** the philosophical traditions of Eastern and Western civilizations to reveal their surprising commonalities.
   这篇文章有效地将东西方文明的哲学传统并列，以揭示它们令人惊讶的共同点。

3. By **juxtaposing** statistical data with personal testimonies, the report conveyed the full human impact of the environmental crisis.
   通过将统计数据与个人证词并列，该报告传达了环境危机对人类的全面影响。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Break it down: "juxta" (next to) + "pose" (to place) — to juxtapose is literally to place things next to each other for comparison.
拆分记忆："juxta"（紧邻）+ "pose"（放置）——juxtapose字面意思就是将事物放在一起进行比较。
