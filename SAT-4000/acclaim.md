# 🎉 acclaim

🔊 /əˈkleɪm/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To praise enthusiastically and publicly. (n.) Enthusiastic approval or public praise.
**中文：** (v.) 称赞，为…喝彩。(n.) 欢呼，喝彩；称赞。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- praise（赞扬）
- applause（掌声）
- commendation（表彰）
- ovation（喝彩）
- tribute（赞颂）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **win/receive/earn acclaim**; **critical/widespread/international acclaim**

👉 Example: The novel received critical acclaim for its compelling narrative.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The scientist was widely **acclaimed** for her groundbreaking research in quantum computing.
   这位科学家因其在量子计算领域的开创性研究而广受赞誉。

2. His debut symphony earned international **acclaim** and established him as a leading composer.
   他的首部交响曲赢得了国际赞誉，确立了他作为顶尖作曲家的地位。

3. The humanitarian organization received universal **acclaim** for its tireless efforts during the crisis.
   该人道主义组织因在危机中不懈的努力而获得了普遍赞誉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Acclaim" sounds like "a claim" of greatness — when people acclaim you, they claim you are great.
acclaim 听起来像"a claim（一个声明）"——当人们 acclaim 你时，就是在宣称你很出色。
