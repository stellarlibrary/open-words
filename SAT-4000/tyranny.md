# ⚔️ tyranny

🔊 /ˈtɪrəni/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Cruel, unreasonable, or arbitrary use of power or control; a government in which a single ruler holds absolute power oppressively
**中文：** n. 暴政；专横；暴虐

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- despotism（专制）
- oppression（压迫）
- autocracy（独裁）
- authoritarianism（威权主义）
- absolutism（专制主义）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tyranny of the majority / resist tyranny / under tyranny / overthrow tyranny**

👉 Example: The citizens organized a resistance movement to oppose the tyranny of the ruling regime.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The political philosopher argued that the **tyranny** of an unchecked majority can be as dangerous as rule by a single despot.
   这位政治哲学家认为，不受制约的多数人的暴政可能与单一暴君的统治一样危险。

2. After decades of **tyranny**, the people finally overthrew the authoritarian government and established a democratic constitution.
   经过数十年的暴政，人民终于推翻了威权政府，建立了民主宪法。

3. Critics have described the new surveillance legislation as a form of digital **tyranny** that threatens fundamental civil liberties.
   批评者将新的监控立法描述为一种威胁基本公民自由的数字暴政。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tyranny" as "tyrant + ny" — the system run by a tyrant, where one ruler cruelly dominates everyone.
把"tyranny"想成"tyrant（暴君）+ ny"——由暴君运行的制度，一个统治者残酷地支配所有人。
