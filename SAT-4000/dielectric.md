# ⚡ dielectric

🔊 /ˌdaɪɪˈlɛktrɪk/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having the property of transmitting electric force without conduction; insulating. (n.) A nonconducting substance used as an insulator in electrical devices
**中文：** adj. 非传导性的 n. 电介质

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- insulator（绝缘体）
- nonconductor（非导体）
- insulating material（绝缘材料）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dielectric constant / dielectric material / dielectric strength / dielectric layer**

👉 Example: The engineer selected a dielectric material with a high breakdown voltage for the capacitor design.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The capacitor stores energy in the electric field created between its plates, separated by a thin **dielectric** layer.
   电容器将能量储存在其极板之间产生的电场中，极板之间由一层薄的电介质隔开。

2. Researchers developed a novel **dielectric** material that significantly improved the performance of high-frequency electronic circuits.
   研究人员开发了一种新型电介质材料，显著提升了高频电子电路的性能。

3. The **dielectric** properties of ceramic materials make them indispensable in the manufacturing of modern semiconductor devices.
   陶瓷材料的介电特性使其在现代半导体器件的制造中不可或缺。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dielectric" as "die-electric" — electricity "dies" when it hits this material because it cannot conduct through it.
把"dielectric"想成"die-electric"——电流遇到这种材料就"死了"，因为它无法传导电流。
