# ⚡ rebuke

🔊 /rɪˈbjuːk/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To express sharp disapproval or criticism. (n.) A stern reprimand or expression of strong disapproval
**中文：** v. 指责，非难 n. 指责，谴责；非难

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reprimand（训斥）
- admonish（告诫）
- censure（谴责）
- reproach（责备）
- chastise（批评）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rebuke someone for… / sharp rebuke / public rebuke / stern rebuke**

👉 Example: The editorial served as a stern rebuke to lawmakers who failed to act on the pressing environmental crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The chairman publicly **rebuked** the committee member for making unauthorized statements to the press about confidential deliberations.
   主席公开指责该委员会成员就机密审议内容向新闻界发表未经授权的声明。

2. The court's decision was widely interpreted as a sharp **rebuke** of the government's failure to uphold constitutional protections.
   法院的裁决被广泛解读为对政府未能维护宪法保护的严厉谴责。

3. Teachers should **rebuke** disruptive behavior firmly yet constructively, ensuring that students understand the reason for the correction.
   教师应坚定而有建设性地批评破坏性行为，确保学生理解纠正的原因。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-buke" as sounding like "re-book" — imagine someone being so harshly criticized that they have to go back and "re-book" their approach entirely.
把"rebuke"想象成"re-book"——想象某人被严厉批评后不得不回去重新"预订"（重新规划）自己的做法。
