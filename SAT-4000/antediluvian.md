# 🦕 antediluvian

🔊 /ˌæn.tɪ.dɪˈluː.vi.ən/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** Of or belonging to the time before the Biblical Flood; extremely old, outdated, or antiquated; resembling something from a bygone era.

**中文：** 大洪水前的；远古的；极其陈旧的；过时的。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- archaic（古老的）
- antiquated（陈旧的）
- obsolete（过时的）
- primeval（原始的）
- prehistoric（史前的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Common collocations:** antediluvian ideas, antediluvian attitudes, antediluvian technology, seem antediluvian

**Example:** His **antediluvian** views on gender roles were met with strong disapproval from the audience.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor dismissed the theory as **antediluvian**, arguing that modern research had rendered it entirely obsolete.
   教授认为这一理论极其陈旧，认为现代研究已使其完全过时。

2. The factory still relied on **antediluvian** machinery that had not been upgraded since the early twentieth century.
   这家工厂仍然依赖自二十世纪初以来从未升级过的陈旧机器。

3. Critics described the institution's policies as **antediluvian** and called for immediate and comprehensive reform.
   批评者称该机构的政策极其过时，并呼吁立即进行全面改革。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Ante-" (before) + "diluvian" (from Latin *diluvium*, meaning flood) = before the great flood, hence extremely ancient. 前缀 ante-（之前）+ diluvian（来自拉丁语 diluvium，洪水）= 大洪水之前的，因此表示"极其古老的、陈旧的"。
