# 🪬 superstitious

🔊 /ˌsuːpərˈstɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having or showing a belief in superstitions; arising from or characterized by irrational fear of the unknown or mysterious
**中文：** adj. 迷信的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- credulous（轻信的）
- irrational（非理性的）
- gullible（容易受骗的）
- unscientific（不科学的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **superstitious belief / superstitious person / superstitious about / superstitious practice**

👉 Example: Many sailors were superstitious about beginning a voyage on a Friday.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Despite her advanced education, she remained **superstitious** about walking under ladders and breaking mirrors.
   尽管受过高等教育，她仍然对从梯子下走过和打碎镜子持迷信态度。

2. The anthropologist studied how **superstitious** rituals in ancient cultures served important social and psychological functions.
   这位人类学家研究了古代文化中迷信仪式如何发挥重要的社会和心理功能。

3. Critics argued that the policy was based on **superstitious** reasoning rather than empirical evidence and sound analysis.
   批评者认为该政策基于迷信的推理，而非实证依据和合理的分析。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "super-stitious" as standing "super still" — frozen with irrational fear, like a superstitious person afraid to move.
把"superstitious"想成"super still"（超级静止）——因为非理性的恐惧而僵住，就像一个迷信的人害怕移动一样。
