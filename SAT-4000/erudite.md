# 📚 erudite

🔊 /ˈɛrjʊdaɪt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having or showing great knowledge or learning. (n.) A person of great knowledge or learning
**中文：** adj. 博学的 n. 饱学之士

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- scholarly（学术的）
- learned（博学的）
- knowledgeable（知识渊博的）
- intellectual（有学问的）
- eruditious（博学的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **erudite scholar / erudite discussion / erudite commentary / erudite professor**

👉 Example: The erudite professor captivated the audience with her profound analysis of ancient philosophy.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **erudite** historian delivered a lecture that seamlessly wove together political theory, economics, and cultural anthropology.
   这位博学的历史学家发表了一场将政治理论、经济学和文化人类学无缝结合的演讲。

2. Her **erudite** commentary on Renaissance literature demonstrated an exceptional depth of understanding rarely found among contemporary critics.
   她对文艺复兴文学的博学评论展现了当代评论家中罕见的非凡理解深度。

3. The panel discussion featured several **erudite** experts whose insights illuminated the complex relationship between technology and society.
   小组讨论邀请了几位博学的专家，他们的见解阐明了技术与社会之间的复杂关系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "erudite" as "e-rude-ite" — an erudite person would never be rude because their vast education has taught them wisdom and civility.
把"erudite"想成"e-rude（粗鲁）-ite"——博学的人绝不会粗鲁，因为广博的教育赋予了他们智慧和礼貌。
