# 🐐 scapegoat

🔊 /ˈskeɪpɡoʊt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who is blamed for the wrongdoings or mistakes of others; a substitute. (v.) To make someone bear the blame for others
**中文：** n. 替罪羊

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- whipping boy（替罪羊）
- fall guy（替罪者）
- victim（受害者）
- patsy（替身）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **make a scapegoat of / use as a scapegoat / become a scapegoat / scapegoat for failure**

👉 Example: The junior employee was unfairly made a scapegoat for the entire department's poor performance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Throughout history, minority groups have frequently been used as **scapegoats** during periods of economic decline and social unrest.
   纵观历史，少数群体在经济衰退和社会动荡时期经常被当作替罪羊。

2. The manager attempted to **scapegoat** his assistant for the failed project rather than accepting responsibility for his own decisions.
   经理试图让助手为失败的项目背锅，而不是为自己的决定承担责任。

3. Political analysts warned that the administration was seeking a convenient **scapegoat** to divert public attention from its policy failures.
   政治分析人士警告说，政府正在寻找一个方便的替罪羊，以转移公众对其政策失败的注意力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "scapegoat" as "escape + goat" — in ancient rituals, a goat was sent into the wilderness carrying the sins of the people, allowing them to escape blame.
把"scapegoat"想成"escape + goat"——在古代仪式中，一只山羊被送入荒野，承载着人们的罪孽，使人们得以逃脱责罚。
