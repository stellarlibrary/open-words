# 🛑 inertia

🔊 /ɪˈnɜːrʃə/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A tendency to remain unchanged or at rest; resistance to change or movement; (physics) the property of matter by which it retains its velocity unless acted upon by an external force
**中文：** n. 惯性；惰性；迟钝；不活动

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sluggishness（迟钝）
- lethargy（懒散）
- passivity（被动）
- stagnation（停滞）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **overcome inertia / bureaucratic inertia / moment of inertia / political inertia / institutional inertia**

👉 Example: The new CEO sought to overcome the organizational inertia that had stifled innovation for years.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Bureaucratic **inertia** prevented the government from implementing reforms despite overwhelming public support for change.
   官僚**惰性**使政府无法实施改革，尽管公众对变革的支持压倒性地强烈。

2. According to Newton's first law, an object in motion will maintain its velocity due to **inertia** unless acted upon.
   根据牛顿第一定律，运动中的物体由于**惯性**将保持其速度，除非受到外力作用。

3. The scholar attributed the nation's economic decline to cultural **inertia** and a deep reluctance to embrace modernization.
   这位学者将国家的经济衰退归因于文化**惰性**以及对现代化的深深抗拒。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inertia" as related to "inert" — just as something inert does not move, inertia is the force that keeps things staying exactly as they are.
把"inertia"和"inert"联系起来——就像惰性的东西不会动一样，inertia就是让事物保持原样的力量。
