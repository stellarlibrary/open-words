# 🕵️ implicate

🔊 /ˈɪmplɪkeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To show or suggest that someone is involved in something, especially a crime; to convey indirectly; to have as a consequence or implication
**中文：** v. 使卷入；涉及；暗指；影响

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- incriminate（牵连）
- involve（使卷入）
- entangle（使纠缠）
- suggest（暗示）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **implicate someone in something** (a crime, a scandal, a conspiracy)

👉 Example: The newly discovered documents implicate several senior officials in the corruption scandal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The forensic evidence collected at the scene was sufficient to **implicate** the suspect in the series of financial crimes.
   在现场收集的法医证据足以证明嫌疑人与这一系列金融犯罪有关。

2. Recent studies **implicate** environmental pollution as a significant contributing factor in the rising rates of respiratory disease.
   最近的研究表明环境污染是呼吸系统疾病发病率上升的重要因素。

3. The whistleblower's testimony served to **implicate** multiple executives who had previously denied any knowledge of the illegal practices.
   举报人的证词将多名此前否认知情的高管牵涉其中。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "IMPLICATE" as "I'M-PLI-CATE" — imagine pulling someone into a complicated situation, folding (plicare) them into trouble.
把"implicate"想成"im（进入）+ plic（折叠）"——把某人折叠进复杂的麻烦中，即牵连某人。
