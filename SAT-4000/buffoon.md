# 🤡 buffoon

🔊 /bəˈfuːn/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A person who amuses others by ridiculous behavior; a clown or fool, often one whose antics are considered undignified
**中文：** n. 丑角；滑稽剧演员；小丑；愚蠢的人

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- clown（小丑）
- jester（弄臣）
- fool（傻瓜）
- joker（爱开玩笑的人）
- harlequin（丑角）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **act like a buffoon / court buffoon / play the buffoon / complete buffoon**

👉 Example: His colleagues regarded him as a buffoon because of his constant attempts to provoke laughter during serious meetings.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The political commentator dismissed the candidate as a **buffoon** whose erratic public statements undermined any claim to serious leadership.
   政治评论员将该候选人斥为小丑，认为其反复无常的公开言论削弱了任何严肃领导力的主张。

2. In Shakespeare's plays, the **buffoon** often delivers profound truths disguised beneath layers of comedy and apparent foolishness.
   在莎士比亚的戏剧中，丑角常常在喜剧和表面愚蠢的层层伪装下传递深刻的真理。

3. The diplomat warned that behaving like a **buffoon** on the international stage would irreparably damage the nation's credibility abroad.
   这位外交官警告说，在国际舞台上表现得像个小丑会不可挽回地损害国家在海外的信誉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "buff-OON" — the "OON" sounds like "cartoon," and a buffoon acts like a silly cartoon character making everyone laugh.
把"buffoon"想成"buff-OON"——"OON"听起来像"cartoon（卡通）"，而小丑就像一个滑稽的卡通人物逗大家笑。
