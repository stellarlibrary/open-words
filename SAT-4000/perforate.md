# 🕳️ perforate

🔊 /ˈpɜːrfəreɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make a hole or holes through something; to pierce or penetrate a surface, especially in a series of small holes
**中文：** v. 穿孔于；打孔穿透；在…上打齿孔

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pierce（刺穿）
- puncture（穿刺）
- penetrate（穿透）
- bore（钻孔）
- punch（打孔）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **perforate a membrane / perforate the surface / perforated edge / perforated ulcer**

👉 Example: The machine is designed to perforate sheets of stamps along precise dotted lines.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The surgeon discovered that the sharp bone fragment had **perforated** the intestinal wall, requiring immediate emergency intervention.
   外科医生发现尖锐的骨碎片已经穿透了肠壁，需要立即进行紧急处理。

2. Industrial machines **perforate** thousands of metal sheets per hour with remarkable precision and consistent uniform spacing.
   工业机器每小时以惊人的精度和一致的均匀间距打孔数千块金属板。

3. The archaeologists carefully examined the **perforated** clay vessel, noting that each hole served a specific ceremonial or functional purpose.
   考古学家仔细检查了这个穿孔的陶器，注意到每个孔都有特定的仪式或功能用途。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "per-FOR-ate" as "thoroughly FOR making holes ATE through" — to perforate is to make holes that go all the way through.
把"perforate"想成"per（穿过）+ for + ate"——彻底地打穿孔洞，完全穿透。
