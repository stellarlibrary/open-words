# 🏚️ recluse

🔊 /ˈrɛkluːs/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who lives in seclusion or isolation from society. (adj.) Living in seclusion; withdrawn from the world
**中文：** n. 隐居者；隐士 adj. 隐居的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- hermit（隐士）
- loner（独居者）
- solitary（独居的人）
- ascetic（苦行者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **become a recluse / live as a recluse / recluse lifestyle / famous recluse**

👉 Example: After years of public scrutiny, the celebrity became a recluse in the countryside.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The brilliant mathematician became a **recluse** after winning the prestigious award, refusing all interviews and public appearances.
   这位杰出的数学家在获得这项著名奖项后成为了隐士，拒绝所有采访和公开露面。

2. Scholars discovered that the **recluse** had secretly authored dozens of influential manuscripts during decades of voluntary isolation.
   学者们发现这位隐居者在数十年的自愿隔离期间秘密撰写了数十部有影响力的手稿。

3. The aging novelist chose to live as a **recluse** in a remote mountain cabin far from the distractions of modern life.
   这位年迈的小说家选择在偏远的山间小屋中过隐居生活，远离现代生活的纷扰。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "recluse" as "re-close" — a recluse closes the door on society again and again, shutting out the world.
把"recluse"想成"re-close"——隐士一次又一次地对社会关上大门，将世界拒之门外。
