# 💥 obtrude

🔊 /əbˈtruːd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To thrust something forward or upon others without invitation; to impose oneself or one's ideas on others
**中文：** v. 逼使；强迫；冲出；强行闯入

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- impose（强加）
- intrude（侵入）
- thrust（猛推）
- interject（插入）
- force（强迫）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **obtrude upon / obtrude oneself / obtrude opinions / obtrude into**

👉 Example: She did not wish to obtrude her personal views upon the committee during the formal proceedings.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The chairman politely asked the guest not to **obtrude** his political opinions upon the audience during the academic symposium.
   主席礼貌地要求这位嘉宾不要在学术研讨会上将他的政治观点强加给听众。

2. Harsh industrial noise began to **obtrude** upon the peaceful silence of the countryside as factories expanded nearby.
   随着附近工厂的扩张，刺耳的工业噪音开始闯入乡村的宁静之中。

3. The author cautioned against allowing personal biases to **obtrude** into what should remain an objective scientific analysis.
   作者告诫不要让个人偏见强行介入本应保持客观的科学分析之中。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ob-TRUDE" as "ob (toward) + trude (thrust)" — to thrust yourself toward others, forcing your presence or ideas on them.
把"obtrude"想成"ob（朝向）+ trude（推）"——把自己推向别人，强加你的存在或想法。
