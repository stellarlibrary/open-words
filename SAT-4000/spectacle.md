# 👓 spectacle

🔊 /ˈspektəkəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A visually striking performance or display; an impressive sight or scene; (plural) eyeglasses.
**中文：** n. 奇观，景象

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- display（展示）
- exhibition（展览）
- sight（景象）
- pageant（盛典）
- marvel（奇观）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **make a spectacle of / grand spectacle / public spectacle / a pair of spectacles**

👉 Example: The opening ceremony was a magnificent spectacle that captivated audiences around the world.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The aurora borealis presented a breathtaking **spectacle** of luminous colors dancing across the Arctic night sky.
   北极光呈现出一幅令人叹为观止的奇观，绚烂的色彩在北极夜空中舞动。

2. The senator made a **spectacle** of himself by shouting accusations during the otherwise dignified congressional hearing.
   这位参议员在原本庄严的国会听证会上大声指责，使自己出了洋相。

3. Thousands gathered to witness the **spectacle** of the rare solar eclipse visible from the southern hemisphere.
   数千人聚集在一起观赏从南半球可见的罕见日食奇观。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "spect-acle" as related to "spectator" — a spectacle is something so impressive that it draws spectators to watch.
把"spectacle"联想到"spectator"（观众）——奇观就是令人印象深刻到吸引观众前来观看的事物。
