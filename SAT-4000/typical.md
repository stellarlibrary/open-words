# 🔖 typical

🔊 /ˈtɪpɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having the distinctive qualities or characteristics of a particular type, group, or category; representative or characteristic
**中文：** adj. 典型的；特有的；有代表性的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- characteristic（特有的）
- representative（有代表性的）
- standard（标准的）
- quintessential（典型的）
- archetypal（原型的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **typical of / typical example / typical behavior / typical response**

👉 Example: This rainfall pattern is typical of tropical climates during the monsoon season.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher noted that the specimen displayed characteristics **typical** of organisms found in deep-sea hydrothermal vent ecosystems.
   研究人员指出，该标本展现了深海热液喷口生态系统中生物体的典型特征。

2. It is **typical** for new employees to require several months of training before they can work independently.
   新员工通常需要几个月的培训才能独立工作，这是很典型的情况。

3. The architect designed a building **typical** of the modernist movement, featuring clean lines and functional minimalism.
   建筑师设计了一座现代主义运动典型风格的建筑，具有简洁的线条和功能性极简主义。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "typical" as "type + ical" — something that perfectly fits its type, a textbook example of a category.
把"typical"想成"type（类型）+ ical"——完美符合其类型的事物，即某一类别的教科书式范例。
