# 🎭 sanctimonious

🔊 /ˌsæŋktɪˈmoʊniəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Making a hypocritical show of religious devotion, piety, righteousness, or moral superiority
**中文：** adj. 假装神圣的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- hypocritical（虚伪的）
- self-righteous（自以为是的）
- pious（假虔诚的）
- holier-than-thou（自命清高的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sanctimonious attitude / sanctimonious tone / sanctimonious lecture / sanctimonious preaching**

👉 Example: His sanctimonious lectures about honesty rang hollow when his own dishonest dealings were exposed.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician's **sanctimonious** speeches about family values were undermined by revelations of his own personal misconduct.
   这位政治家关于家庭价值观的伪善演讲被揭露出的个人不端行为所破坏。

2. Critics described the editorial as **sanctimonious** and condescending, noting that the author failed to practice what he preached.
   批评者称这篇社论虚伪且居高临下，指出作者言行不一。

3. She grew weary of her colleague's **sanctimonious** moralizing, especially given his well-documented ethical lapses at the firm.
   她对同事假装圣洁的说教感到厌倦，尤其是考虑到他在公司有据可查的道德失误。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sancti-monious" as "sancti (sacred) + monious (like ceremonious)" — someone who makes a big ceremony of being sacred, but it is all just an act.
把"sanctimonious"想成"sancti（神圣的）+ monious（像ceremonious仪式化的）"——一个大张旗鼓表现神圣的人，但一切不过是做戏。
