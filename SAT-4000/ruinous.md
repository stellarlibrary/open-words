# 💥 ruinous

🔊 /ˈruːɪnəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Causing or tending to cause ruin; destructive; disastrous; dilapidated or in a state of decay
**中文：** adj. 破坏性的，灾难性的，毁灭性的；已成废墟的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- destructive（破坏性的）
- devastating（毁灭性的）
- catastrophic（灾难性的）
- disastrous（灾祸的）
- calamitous（惨重的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ruinous consequences / ruinous cost / ruinous policy / ruinous state / ruinous effects**

👉 Example: The corporation's ruinous spending practices eventually led to its inevitable bankruptcy and dissolution.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prolonged civil war had a **ruinous** impact on the nation's infrastructure, leaving roads and bridges utterly destroyed.
   旷日持久的内战对国家基础设施造成了毁灭性的影响，道路和桥梁被彻底摧毁。

2. Financial advisors warned that the **ruinous** interest rates on high-risk loans would ultimately bankrupt many vulnerable borrowers.
   财务顾问警告说，高风险贷款的灾难性利率最终将使许多脆弱的借款人破产。

3. The once-magnificent cathedral now stands in a **ruinous** condition, its crumbling facade a somber reminder of past grandeur.
   这座曾经宏伟的大教堂如今已是一片废墟，残破的外墙令人感伤地提醒着往日的辉煌。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ruinous" as "ruin + ous" — full of ruin. If something is ruinous, it is full of destruction and leads to ruin.
把"ruinous"想成"ruin（毁灭）+ ous（充满的）"——充满毁灭的。如果某事是ruinous的，它就充满破坏并导致毁灭。
