# ⚖️ impartial

🔊 /ɪmˈpɑːrʃəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and not favoring one side over another
**中文：** adj. 公平的；公正的；不偏不倚的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unbiased（无偏见的）
- neutral（中立的）
- objective（客观的）
- fair（公平的）
- disinterested（无私的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **impartial judge / impartial jury / impartial observer / impartial analysis**

👉 Example: A truly impartial judge must set aside all personal opinions before examining the evidence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The mediator was praised for maintaining an **impartial** stance throughout the lengthy and contentious labor negotiations.
   这位调解人因在漫长而有争议的劳资谈判中保持公正立场而受到赞扬。

2. Journalists have a professional obligation to provide **impartial** reporting that presents all perspectives on a given issue.
   记者有职业义务提供公正的报道，呈现某一问题的所有观点。

3. The committee selected an **impartial** auditor to investigate the financial irregularities discovered in the annual corporate report.
   委员会选择了一位公正的审计员来调查年度公司报告中发现的财务违规行为。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-partial" as "not partial" — if you are not partial to any side, you are impartial, meaning fair and unbiased.
把"impartial"拆成"im-partial"——"im"表示"不"，"partial"表示"偏袒的"，所以impartial就是"不偏不倚的、公正的"。
