# 🏔️ mount

🔊 /maʊnt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To climb or ascend; to increase in amount or degree; to fix in position for display or use; to organize and initiate. (n.) A mountain or hill; a support or setting; a horse for riding
**中文：** v. 登上，爬上；增加；安装，装配，固定，镶嵌；准备上演 n. 山峰；支架，底座，底板；乘骑用马

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ascend（攀登）
- climb（爬上）
- install（安装）
- increase（增加）
- peak（山峰）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mount an attack / mount a campaign / mount pressure / mount a horse / mount a display**

👉 Example: Public pressure continued to mount as the government delayed its response to the environmental crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The research team managed to **mount** the delicate instrument onto the spacecraft with extraordinary precision and care.
   研究团队以非凡的精确度和细心将精密仪器安装到了航天器上。

2. Evidence continued to **mount** against the defendant, compelling the prosecution to seek a more severe penalty.
   对被告不利的证据持续增加，迫使检方寻求更严厉的处罚。

3. The explorers prepared to **mount** the treacherous summit despite the deteriorating weather conditions and dwindling supplies.
   尽管天气状况恶化且物资日益减少，探险者们仍准备攀登这座险峻的山峰。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mount" as "MOUN-tain" — you mount (climb) a mountain, and the mountain itself is a mount (peak).
把"mount"想成"mountain（山）"——你攀登(mount)一座山，而山本身就是一座mount（山峰）。
