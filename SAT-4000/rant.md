# 🗯️ rant

🔊 /rænt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To speak or shout at length in an angry, impassioned, or extravagant manner. (n.) A long, angry, or wildly enthusiastic speech or tirade
**中文：** v. 咆哮；痛骂；怒气冲冲地叫嚷；夸夸其谈 n. 咆哮；激昂的演说

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- tirade（长篇激烈的演说）
- harangue（长篇大论）
- fulminate（痛斥）
- diatribe（抨击）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **rant about / rant against / go on a rant / rant and rave**

👉 Example: The senator went on a lengthy rant against the proposed legislation during the committee hearing.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The editorial was little more than an extended **rant** against modern educational policies, offering no constructive alternatives or evidence-based solutions.
   这篇社论不过是对现代教育政策的长篇抨击，没有提供任何建设性的替代方案或基于证据的解决办法。

2. She began to **rant** about bureaucratic inefficiency, citing numerous examples of delayed permits and misallocated public funds in her district.
   她开始痛骂官僚低效，列举了她所在地区许可证延迟和公共资金分配不当的大量例子。

3. The critic's **rant** against the new architectural design sparked a vigorous public debate about urban aesthetics and cultural preservation.
   评论家对新建筑设计的激烈抨击引发了一场关于城市美学和文化保护的热烈公众辩论。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "rant" as sounding like "ran + t" — someone who ran to the stage and talked loudly, unable to stop their angry outburst.
把"rant"想成"ran + t"——某人跑上台大声说话，无法停止自己愤怒的爆发。
