# 🎖️ regiment

🔊 /ˈredʒɪmənt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A permanent unit of an army; a large number of people or things. (v.) To organize rigidly; to subject to strict control or discipline.
**中文：** v. 把…编成团；严格地管制 n. 团；大量

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- battalion（营）
- brigade（旅）
- organize（组织）
- discipline（管制）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **regiment of soldiers / regiment one's life / regimented schedule / a whole regiment of**

👉 Example: The strict boarding school was known to regiment every aspect of the students' daily lives.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The commanding officer led an entire **regiment** of infantry troops through the treacherous mountain terrain during the campaign.
   指挥官率领整个步兵团在战役期间穿越了险峻的山地地形。

2. Critics argued that the new policy would **regiment** workers into rigid schedules, stifling creativity and individual initiative.
   批评者认为新政策会将工人严格管制在僵化的时间表中，扼杀创造力和个人主动性。

3. A whole **regiment** of volunteers assembled at dawn to assist with disaster relief efforts in the flood-ravaged community.
   一大批志愿者在黎明时分集合，协助在遭受洪灾的社区开展救灾工作。

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "regiment" as "regimen + t" — a regimen taken to the extreme, with strict military-style organization and control.
把"regiment"想成"regimen + t"——一种极端的养生/管理方式，带有严格的军事化组织和控制。
