# 🛡️ preempt

🔊 /priˈɛmpt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To take action in order to prevent something from happening; to acquire or appropriate something before others can
**中文：** v. 先占；先取；以先买权获得；预先制止；抢在…之前做或说

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- forestall（预先阻止）
- anticipate（预先考虑）
- supplant（取代）
- preclude（排除）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **preempt a threat / preempt an attack / preempt criticism / preempt a decision**

👉 Example: The company decided to preempt potential lawsuits by issuing a voluntary product recall.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government chose to **preempt** the crisis by implementing emergency regulations before the situation could deteriorate further.
   政府选择在局势进一步恶化之前实施紧急法规，以预先阻止危机的发生。

2. Several nations argued that the military intervention was necessary to **preempt** an imminent threat to regional security.
   几个国家辩称军事干预对于预先制止对地区安全的紧迫威胁是必要的。

3. The professor decided to **preempt** potential confusion by addressing the most common misconceptions at the start of the lecture.
   教授决定在讲座开始时就澄清最常见的误解，以抢先消除可能的困惑。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-empt" as "before + empty" — you empty the field before others arrive, seizing the opportunity first.
把"preempt"想成"pre（之前）+ empt（清空）"——在别人到达之前清空场地，抢先占据机会。
