# 🍼 wean

🔊 /wiːn/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To accustom a child or young animal to food other than its mother's milk; to cause someone to give up a habit or dependence. (n.) (Scottish) A young child
**中文：** v. 使断奶；使断念；使放弃；使丢弃 n.（苏格兰）幼儿

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- detach（使脱离）
- disengage（使摆脱）
- withdraw（使戒除）
- separate（使分离）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **wean someone off / wean someone from / wean a baby / wean oneself off**

👉 Example: The therapist helped the patient wean herself off the medication gradually over several months.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Health experts recommend that mothers **wean** their infants gradually to ensure a smooth nutritional transition during early development.
   健康专家建议母亲逐步给婴儿断奶，以确保早期发育期间营养过渡顺利。

2. The government introduced new policies designed to **wean** the national economy off its heavy dependence on fossil fuels.
   政府出台了新政策，旨在使国家经济摆脱对化石燃料的严重依赖。

3. It took considerable effort to **wean** the organization from outdated practices that had persisted for more than two decades.
   花了相当大的努力才使该组织放弃了持续了二十多年的过时做法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "wean" as "we end" — we end the dependence, whether it is milk for a baby or a bad habit for an adult.
把"wean"想成"we end（我们结束）"——我们结束依赖，无论是婴儿的母乳还是成人的坏习惯。
