# 🏛️ bureaucracy

🔊 /bjʊˈrɑːkrəsi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A system of government characterized by complex procedures, hierarchical authority, and numerous officials; excessive administrative red tape
**中文：** n. 官僚主义；官僚机构；官僚政治；行政系统

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- administration（行政机构）
- red tape（繁文缛节）
- officialdom（官僚体系）
- civil service（公务员制度）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **government bureaucracy / bloated bureaucracy / cut through bureaucracy / bureaucracy and red tape**

👉 Example: Entrepreneurs often complain that excessive bureaucracy stifles innovation and delays progress.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sprawling **bureaucracy** of the federal government made it exceedingly difficult for small businesses to obtain necessary permits.
   联邦政府庞大的官僚机构使小企业极难获得必要的许可证。

2. Reformers argued that reducing **bureaucracy** would improve public services and restore citizens' confidence in government institutions.
   改革者认为，减少官僚主义将改善公共服务并恢复公民对政府机构的信心。

3. The university's **bureaucracy** required students to submit multiple forms across several departments before finalizing their enrollment.
   大学的行政系统要求学生在多个部门提交多份表格才能完成注册。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bureau" (office) + "cracy" (rule) — bureaucracy is rule by offices and officials, often implying slow, complicated processes.
"bureau"（办公室）+ "cracy"（统治）——官僚主义就是由办公室和官员统治，通常暗示缓慢而复杂的流程。
