# ⏱️ concurrent

🔊 /kənˈkɜːrənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Existing, happening, or done at the same time; operating or acting in conjunction; agreeing or consistent
**中文：** adj. 并发的；同时发生的；一致的；协作的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- simultaneous（同时的）
- parallel（并行的）
- coexisting（共存的）
- coincident（同时发生的）
- synchronized（同步的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **concurrent with / concurrent events / concurrent sessions / run concurrently / concurrent jurisdiction**

👉 Example: The two criminal sentences were ordered to run concurrently rather than consecutively.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The judge ruled that the two prison sentences would be served **concurrent** with each other, reducing the total time of incarceration.
   法官裁定两项刑期同时执行，从而减少了总监禁时间。

2. Several **concurrent** developments in technology and policy created an unprecedented opportunity for renewable energy adoption worldwide.
   技术和政策方面的几项同步发展为全球可再生能源的采用创造了前所未有的机遇。

3. The university offers **concurrent** enrollment programs that allow high school students to earn college credits before graduation.
   该大学提供同步入学项目，允许高中生在毕业前获得大学学分。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con (together) + current (running)" — things running together at the same time are concurrent.
把"concurrent"想成"con（一起）+ current（跑）"——同时一起跑的事物就是concurrent（并发的）。
