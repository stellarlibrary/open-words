# 😒 fulsome

🔊 /ˈfʊlsəm/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Excessively flattering or insincerely lavish to the point of being offensive or disgusting; overdone
**中文：** adj. 令人生厌的；过度的；因过分而显得可厌的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- excessive（过度的）
- unctuous（油腔滑调的）
- sycophantic（谄媚的）
- obsequious（奉承的）
- cloying（甜腻的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fulsome praise / fulsome flattery / fulsome compliments / fulsome tribute**

👉 Example: His fulsome praise of the director seemed more like calculated flattery than genuine admiration.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat's **fulsome** compliments toward the host nation struck many observers as insincere and politically motivated.
   这位外交官对东道国过度的赞美让许多观察人士觉得不真诚且带有政治动机。

2. Critics warned that **fulsome** endorsements from industry lobbyists could undermine the credibility of the scientific review panel.
   批评者警告说，行业游说者的过度吹捧可能会损害科学评审小组的公信力。

3. The biography was marred by **fulsome** adulation that obscured any objective assessment of the leader's actual accomplishments.
   这部传记因过度的奉承而失色，掩盖了对这位领导人实际成就的客观评价。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fulsome" as "full + some more" — so full of flattery that it becomes too much and turns unpleasant.
把"fulsome"想成"full（满的）+ some more（再多一些）"——奉承太满以至于过头，变得令人反感。
