# 🩺 remission

🔊 /rɪˈmɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A temporary or permanent decrease in the severity of disease symptoms; forgiveness or pardon; the cancellation of a debt or penalty
**中文：** n. 缓解；宽恕；豁免

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- abatement（减轻）
- reprieve（缓刑）
- pardon（宽恕）
- alleviation（缓和）
- amnesty（赦免）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in remission / go into remission / complete remission / remission of sins**

👉 Example: After months of intensive treatment, the patient's cancer went into complete remission.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The oncologist confirmed that the patient's lymphoma had entered full **remission** following six months of rigorous chemotherapy treatment.
   肿瘤科医生确认，经过六个月的严格化疗后，患者的淋巴瘤已完全缓解。

2. The medieval doctrine of **remission** of sins played a central role in the theological disputes of the Reformation era.
   中世纪的赎罪教义在宗教改革时期的神学争论中发挥了核心作用。

3. The court granted a partial **remission** of the financial penalties imposed on the corporation due to its cooperative compliance efforts.
   由于该公司积极配合合规工作，法院对其所受的经济处罚予以部分豁免。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "remission" as "re-mission" — your body is on a new mission to recover, sending the disease into retreat.
把"remission"想成"re-mission"——你的身体开始了恢复的新使命，疾病开始消退。
