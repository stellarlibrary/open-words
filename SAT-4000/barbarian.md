# ⚔️ barbarian

🔊 /bɑːrˈbɛəriən/<br>
🏷️ *adj. & n. | 形容词 & 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** (adj.) Uncivilized, savage, or primitive in manner or culture; (n.) a person from a wild or uncivilized people.

**中文：** (adj.) 野蛮的；未开化的；(n.) 野蛮人；未开化的人。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- savage （野蛮的）
- uncivilized （未开化的）
- brutish （粗野的）
- primitive （原始的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Barbarian" can function as both an adjective and a noun. As an adjective, it describes behavior or customs that are crude and uncivilized. As a noun, it refers to a person regarded as culturally inferior or savage. The word carries strong negative connotations and should be used carefully in modern contexts.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **barbarian** tribes eventually overwhelmed the weakened defenses of the ancient empire.
   中文：野蛮部落最终击溃了古老帝国薄弱的防线。

2. His **barbarian** conduct at the formal banquet shocked every distinguished guest present.
   中文：他在正式宴会上野蛮的举止震惊了在场的每一位贵宾。

3. Historians now challenge the notion that all **barbarian** societies lacked sophisticated governance.
   中文：历史学家如今质疑所有野蛮社会都缺乏精密治理体系的观念。

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

Think of "bar-bar-ian" — the ancient Greeks called foreigners "barbaroi" because their speech sounded like "bar bar" (nonsense babbling), hence "uncivilized."

联想记忆：古希腊人称外族人为"barbaroi"，因为他们的语言听起来像"bar bar"（胡言乱语），因此意为"野蛮的、未开化的"。
