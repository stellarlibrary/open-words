# ⚖️ corrupt

🔊 /kəˈrʌpt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, verb | 形容词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Dishonest or immoral, especially through bribery; debased or impure. (v.) To cause to become dishonest or morally degraded; to spoil or destroy
**中文：** adj. 腐败的；贪污的 v. 使堕落；使腐烂；贿赂；收买

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dishonest（不诚实的）
- depraved（堕落的）
- tainted（被玷污的）
- venal（贪赃枉法的）
- unscrupulous（不道德的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **corrupt official / corrupt practices / corrupt data / corrupt the youth / morally corrupt**

👉 Example: The investigation exposed several corrupt officials who had accepted bribes from corporate lobbyists.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist's investigation revealed how **corrupt** practices within the regulatory agency had allowed hazardous pollutants to go unreported.
   记者的调查揭示了监管机构内部的腐败行为如何使危险污染物未被报告。

2. Philosophers have long debated whether absolute power will inevitably **corrupt** even the most principled and virtuous leaders.
   哲学家们长期以来一直在争论绝对权力是否会不可避免地使即使是最有原则和美德的领导者也变得堕落。

3. The malware was designed to **corrupt** essential system files, rendering the entire network inoperable within a matter of hours.
   该恶意软件旨在破坏关键系统文件，在数小时内使整个网络无法运行。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cor-rupt" as "core" + "rupt" (break) — corruption breaks the core integrity of a person, system, or institution.
把"corrupt"想成"core"（核心）加上"rupt"（破裂）——腐败打破了一个人、系统或机构的核心诚信。
