# 📜 chronicle

🔊 /ˈkrɒnɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A factual written account of important historical events in the order of their occurrence. (v.) To record events in a factual and detailed way
**中文：** n. 编年史 v. 记录；把…载入编年史

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- record（记录）
- annals（年鉴）
- account（记述）
- narrative（叙述）
- document（记载）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **chronicle the events / chronicle the history / chronicle of / historical chronicle**

👉 Example: The journalist spent a decade chronicling the social transformations that reshaped the nation after independence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The medieval **chronicle** provides an invaluable account of daily life and political upheaval during the twelfth century.
   这部中世纪**编年史**对十二世纪的日常生活和政治动荡提供了极为宝贵的记述。

2. The documentary seeks to **chronicle** the remarkable achievements of women in science over the past two hundred years.
   这部纪录片旨在**记录**过去两百年间女性在科学领域的杰出成就。

3. Historians rely on ancient **chronicles** to reconstruct the sequence of military campaigns conducted by the Roman Empire.
   历史学家依靠古代**编年史**来重建罗马帝国所进行的军事战役的顺序。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "chronicle" as "chrono + icle" — "chrono" means time, so a chronicle is a record organized by time, like a timeline of events.
把"chronicle"想成"chrono + icle"——"chrono"意为时间，因此chronicle是按时间组织的记录，就像事件的时间线。
