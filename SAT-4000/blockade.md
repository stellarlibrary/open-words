# 🚧 blockade

🔊 /blɒˈkeɪd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An obstruction or barrier that prevents movement or access, especially a military or economic measure to isolate an area. (v.) To seal off or obstruct a place to prevent entry or exit.
**中文：** n. 封锁；阻塞；障碍 v. 封锁；阻挡

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- siege（围困）
- embargo（禁运）
- obstruction（阻碍）
- barricade（路障）
- cordon（封锁线）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **impose a blockade / lift a blockade / naval blockade / blockade a port / run a blockade**

👉 Example: The allied fleet imposed a strict naval blockade to cut off the enemy's supply routes.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The naval **blockade** effectively severed all maritime supply lines, forcing the besieged nation to seek diplomatic negotiations.
   海上**封锁**有效切断了所有海上补给线，迫使被围困的国家寻求外交谈判。

2. Protesters attempted to **blockade** the entrance to the legislative building, demanding immediate reform of environmental policies.
   抗议者试图**封锁**立法大楼的入口，要求立即改革环境政策。

3. The international community debated whether the prolonged economic **blockade** constituted a violation of humanitarian legal principles.
   国际社会讨论了长期的经济**封锁**是否构成对人道主义法律原则的违反。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "blockade" as "block + aid" — to block aid and supplies from reaching a place, which is exactly what a blockade does.
把"blockade"想成"block + aid"——阻止援助和物资到达某个地方，这正是封锁的作用。
