# ❌ countermand

🔊 /ˌkaʊntərˈmænd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A cancellation of a previously issued order. (v.) To revoke or cancel an order or command that has already been given
**中文：** n. 取消 v. 取消，撤消

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- revoke（撤销）
- rescind（废除）
- annul（宣告无效）
- override（推翻）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **countermand an order / countermand a directive / countermand instructions / countermand a decision**

👉 Example: The general was forced to countermand his earlier order when new intelligence revealed a hidden ambush.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The CEO decided to **countermand** the previous directive after the board expressed serious concerns about the proposed merger strategy.
   首席执行官在董事会对拟议的合并策略表示严重关切后决定撤销先前的指令。

2. Military headquarters issued an urgent communication to **countermand** the advance order before the troops reached the compromised position.
   军事总部发出紧急通讯，在部队到达暴露阵地之前撤销了推进命令。

3. The judge chose to **countermand** the earlier ruling after newly discovered evidence cast significant doubt on the original verdict.
   法官在新发现的证据对原判决产生重大质疑后选择撤销了先前的裁定。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "counter-mand" as "counter + mandate" — to go against a mandate, meaning to cancel an official command.
把"countermand"想成"counter（对抗）+ mand（命令）"——对抗命令，即取消一项官方指令。
