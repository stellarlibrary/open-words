# 🙏 supplicant

🔊 /ˈsʌplɪkənt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who makes a humble or earnest plea; one who begs or entreats
**中文：** n. 恳求者，恳请者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- petitioner（请愿者）
- beggar（乞求者）
- pleader（恳求者）
- appellant（上诉人）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **humble supplicant / supplicant before the court / act as a supplicant / supplicant at the altar**

👉 Example: The supplicant knelt before the king, begging for mercy and clemency.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **supplicant** approached the throne with lowered eyes, humbly requesting the monarch's pardon for his past transgressions.
   恳求者低垂着双眼走向王座，谦卑地请求君主赦免他过去的罪行。

2. In medieval society, a **supplicant** at a cathedral might spend hours in prayer before presenting a formal petition.
   在中世纪社会，大教堂里的恳求者可能会花数小时祈祷，然后才提交正式的请愿书。

3. The diplomat refused to appear as a **supplicant**, insisting instead on negotiating from a position of equal standing.
   这位外交官拒绝以恳求者的姿态出现，而是坚持在平等的立场上进行谈判。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "suppli-cant" as "supply can't" — a supplicant is someone who can't supply for themselves and must beg others for help.
把"supplicant"想成"supply can't"——恳求者是一个无法自己供给、必须向他人乞求帮助的人。
