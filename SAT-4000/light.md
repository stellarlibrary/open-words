# 💡 light

🔊 /laɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective, verb | 名词, 形容词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The natural agent that makes things visible; a source of illumination. (adj.) Having little weight; not heavy. (v.) To ignite or illuminate.
**中文：** n. 光；光线；灯 adj. 轻的；明亮的 v. 点着；变亮

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- illumination（照明）
- radiance（光辉）
- brightness（明亮）
- glow（光芒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **shed light on / come to light / in light of / light a fire / light reading**

👉 Example: The investigation shed new light on the causes of the environmental disaster.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Recent archaeological discoveries have shed considerable **light** on the daily customs and rituals of ancient Mediterranean civilizations.
   最近的考古发现为了解古代地中海文明的日常习俗和仪式提供了重要线索。

2. In **light** of the new empirical evidence, the research committee decided to revise its original theoretical framework.
   鉴于新的实证证据，研究委员会决定修订其原有的理论框架。

3. The professor used a powerful laser **light** to demonstrate the fundamental principles of optical refraction to the students.
   教授使用强激光来向学生演示光折射的基本原理。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "light" as the opposite of both "dark" and "heavy" — it brightens and lifts everything it touches.
把"light"想成"dark（黑暗）"和"heavy（沉重）"的反义词——它既照亮又减轻一切。
