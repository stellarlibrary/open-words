# 💥 catastrophe

🔊 /kəˈtæstrəfi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An event causing great and often sudden damage, suffering, or destruction; a complete failure or disaster
**中文：** n. 大灾难；大祸；惨败

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disaster（灾难）
- calamity（灾祸）
- cataclysm（大灾变）
- tragedy（悲剧）
- debacle（惨败）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **avert a catastrophe / natural catastrophe / environmental catastrophe / on the brink of catastrophe**

👉 Example: The international community worked tirelessly to avert a humanitarian catastrophe in the region.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The nuclear meltdown was the worst industrial **catastrophe** in the nation's history, displacing thousands of residents permanently.
   这次核泄漏是该国历史上最严重的工业灾难，导致数千名居民永久性搬迁。

2. Economists warned that failure to address the mounting debt could precipitate a financial **catastrophe** of unprecedented magnitude.
   经济学家警告说，不解决不断增加的债务可能会引发前所未有的金融灾难。

3. The expedition ended in total **catastrophe** when severe weather conditions forced the team to abandon all equipment.
   当恶劣的天气条件迫使团队放弃所有设备时，这次远征以彻底的惨败告终。
