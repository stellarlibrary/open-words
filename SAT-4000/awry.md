# 😵‍💫 awry

🔊 /əˈraɪ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, adverb | 形容词，副词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** Away from the expected course; wrong or amiss; turned or twisted to one side.

**中文：** 错误的；扭曲的；歪斜地。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- amiss（出差错的）
- askew（歪斜的）
- crooked（弯曲的）
- wrong（错误的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Awry" is commonly used in the phrase "go awry," meaning that something has gone wrong or not according to plan. It can also describe something physically twisted or out of alignment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The carefully orchestrated plan went **awry** when unexpected storms disrupted all transportation routes across the region.
   （当意外的暴风雨扰乱了整个地区的所有交通路线时，精心策划的计划出了**差错**。）

2. His tie was slightly **awry**, giving him a disheveled appearance that contrasted with his formal attire.
   （他的领带有些**歪斜**，使他看起来凌乱不堪，与他的正式着装形成对比。）

3. When negotiations went **awry**, the diplomats were forced to reconvene and draft an entirely new proposal.
   （当谈判出现**偏差**时，外交官们被迫重新召开会议，起草一份全新的提案。）

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Awry" sounds like "a rye" — imagine a rye bread that came out of the oven twisted and crooked, not the way it was supposed to look.

"Awry"听起来像"a rye（一个黑麦面包）"——想象一个从烤箱里出来的黑麦面包扭曲变形了，完全不是预期的样子，一切都"出了错"。
