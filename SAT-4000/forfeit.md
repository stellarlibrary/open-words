# ⚖️ forfeit

🔊 /ˈfɔːrfɪt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A penalty; something lost or surrendered as punishment. (v.) To lose or give up a right, privilege, or possession as a consequence of an error or offense
**中文：** n. 罚金；没收物 v. 丧失（权利等）；没收

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- surrender（交出）
- relinquish（放弃）
- sacrifice（牺牲）
- lose（丧失）
- penalty（罚金）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **forfeit the right / forfeit a game / forfeit one's claim / forfeit property**

👉 Example: Athletes who violate anti-doping regulations may forfeit their medals and titles.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corporation was compelled to **forfeit** millions of dollars in assets after the court found evidence of widespread fraud.
   在法院发现大规模欺诈证据后，该公司被迫没收了数百万美元的资产。

2. By refusing to appear before the tribunal, the defendant effectively **forfeited** his right to present a legal defense.
   被告拒绝出庭，实际上丧失了进行法律辩护的权利。

3. Nations that violate international treaties risk **forfeiting** the diplomatic protections those agreements were designed to guarantee.
   违反国际条约的国家有可能丧失这些协议旨在保障的外交保护。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "for-feit" as "for a feat gone wrong" — when you commit a wrongful feat, you must pay a forfeit as punishment.
把"forfeit"想成"因为做错事"——当你犯了错误，你必须以forfeit（罚金或丧失权利）作为惩罚。
