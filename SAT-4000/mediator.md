# 🤝 mediator

🔊 /ˈmiːdiˌeɪtər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A person who attempts to resolve a dispute between two or more parties; an intermediary or go-between
**中文：** n. 调停者；仲裁人；中介物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- arbitrator（仲裁者）
- intermediary（中间人）
- negotiator（谈判者）
- go-between（调解人）
- peacemaker（和事佬）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **act as a mediator / serve as mediator / appoint a mediator / neutral mediator**

👉 Example: The United Nations appointed a mediator to facilitate peace negotiations between the two conflicting nations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The court appointed a professional **mediator** to help the disputing parties reach a mutually acceptable settlement agreement.
   法院指定了一名专业调停者来帮助争议双方达成双方都能接受的和解协议。

2. Throughout history, Switzerland has frequently served as a **mediator** in international conflicts due to its longstanding policy of neutrality.
   纵观历史，瑞士因其长期的中立政策而经常在国际冲突中充当调停者。

3. An effective **mediator** must remain impartial while guiding both sides toward a resolution that addresses their fundamental concerns.
   一位有效的调停者必须保持公正，同时引导双方达成解决其根本关切的方案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "medi-ator" — "medi" means middle, so a mediator is the person who stands in the middle between two sides to help them find common ground.
把"mediator"想成"medi-ator"——"medi"意为中间，所以调停者就是站在双方中间帮助他们找到共同点的人。
