# 🌋 outbreak

🔊 /ˈaʊtbreɪk/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A sudden occurrence or eruption of something unwelcome, such as war or disease. (v.) To burst forth or erupt suddenly.
**中文：** n. （战争的）爆发；（疾病的）发作 v. 爆发

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- eruption（爆发）
- onset（开始）
- surge（激增）
- epidemic（流行病）
- flare-up（突然爆发）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **outbreak of war / outbreak of disease / outbreak of violence / contain an outbreak**

👉 Example: International organizations mobilized rapidly to contain the outbreak of the infectious disease.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **outbreak** of hostilities between the neighboring nations disrupted international trade routes and displaced thousands of civilians.
   邻国之间敌对行动的爆发扰乱了国际贸易路线，并导致数千名平民流离失所。

2. Health authorities traced the **outbreak** of foodborne illness to contaminated produce distributed by a single regional supplier.
   卫生当局将食源性疾病的爆发追溯到一家区域供应商分销的受污染农产品。

3. The **outbreak** of protests across several major cities signaled deep public dissatisfaction with the government's economic policies.
   几个主要城市抗议活动的爆发表明公众对政府经济政策的深刻不满。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "out-break" — something that "breaks out" suddenly and forcefully, like a disease escaping containment or violence erupting without warning.
把"outbreak"拆成"out"（出来）+"break"（爆裂）——某事物突然猛烈地"爆发出来"，就像疾病冲破控制或暴力毫无预警地爆发。
