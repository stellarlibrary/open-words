# 🌑 foreshadow

🔊 /fɔːrˈʃædoʊ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To indicate or suggest beforehand; to serve as a warning or sign of a future event
**中文：** v. 预示；成为…的前兆

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- portend（预示）
- presage（预兆）
- herald（预报）
- augur（预示）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **foreshadow events / foreshadow a crisis / foreshadow the outcome / foreshadow what is to come**

👉 Example: The author's use of dark imagery in the opening chapter foreshadows the tragic ending.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The early economic indicators clearly **foreshadowed** the severe recession that would engulf the nation within months.
   早期的经济指标清楚地预示了将在数月内席卷全国的严重衰退。

2. The protagonist's recurring nightmares **foreshadow** the catastrophic events that unfold in the final act of the novel.
   主角反复出现的噩梦预示了小说最后一幕中展开的灾难性事件。

3. Rising tensions along the border **foreshadowed** the diplomatic breakdown that would eventually lead to open conflict.
   边境日益紧张的局势预示了最终导致公开冲突的外交破裂。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fore-shadow" as a "shadow cast before" — just as your shadow arrives before you when walking toward light, a foreshadow is a sign that appears before the event itself.
把"foreshadow"想成"前方的影子"——就像你走向光源时影子先于你到达一样，foreshadow就是事件发生之前出现的征兆。
