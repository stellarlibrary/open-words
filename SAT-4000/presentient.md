# 🌀 presentient

🔊 /prɪˈsɛnʃənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having a presentiment or premonition; feeling or sensing something before it occurs
**中文：** adj. 有预感的；有预知能力的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- intuitive（直觉的）
- premonitory（预兆的）
- foreboding（有不祥预感的）
- anticipatory（预期的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **presentient awareness / presentient feeling / presentient of danger / a presentient sense**

👉 Example: She had a presentient awareness that the seemingly calm situation would soon take a dangerous turn.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The experienced captain had a **presentient** feeling about the voyage and ordered additional safety equipment before the ship departed.
   这位经验丰富的船长对此次航行有一种预感，在船出发前下令准备了额外的安全设备。

2. Many survivors of the disaster reported a **presentient** unease in the hours before the catastrophic event struck their community.
   许多灾难幸存者报告说，在灾难性事件袭击他们的社区之前几个小时就有一种预感般的不安。

3. The novelist's **presentient** grasp of social tensions allowed her to craft narratives that anticipated real-world conflicts with eerie accuracy.
   这位小说家对社会紧张局势的预知性把握使她能够以令人惊叹的准确性创作出预见现实冲突的叙事。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-sentient" as "sensing before" — feeling or perceiving something before it actually happens.
把"presentient"想成"pre-sentient"——在事情实际发生之前就"感知"到了，即"提前感应"。
