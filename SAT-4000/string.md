# 🧵 string

🔊 /strɪŋ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A thin cord, a series of items arranged in a line; the strings of a musical instrument. (v.) To thread or hang; to tie or fasten
**中文：** v. 缚，捆；悬挂 n. 弦，线，细绳；一串，一行

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cord（绳索）
- thread（线）
- sequence（序列）
- strand（股）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a string of events / string together / pull strings / no strings attached / string of pearls**

👉 Example: The diplomat used his influence to pull strings behind the scenes and secure the agreement.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prosecutor presented a **string** of compelling evidence that left the jury with little doubt about the defendant's guilt.
   检察官出示了一连串令人信服的证据，使陪审团对被告的罪行几乎毫无疑问。

2. The committee attempted to **string** together several unrelated proposals into a single cohesive legislative package.
   委员会试图将几项不相关的提案串联成一个统一的立法方案。

3. A **string** of diplomatic failures throughout the decade ultimately weakened the nation's standing in international affairs.
   十年间一连串的外交失败最终削弱了该国在国际事务中的地位。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "string" as something that connects — a string ties beads together, just as a string of events links one occurrence to the next.
把"string"想成连接的东西——一根线把珠子串在一起，就像一连串事件将一件事与下一件事联系起来。
