# 📚 anecdote

🔊 /ˈænɪkˌdoʊt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A short, amusing or interesting story about a real incident or person, often used to illustrate a point
**中文：** 轶事；奇闻；秘史

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- tale（故事）
- story（叙述）
- narrative（叙事）
- account（描述）
- vignette（小品文）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tell an anecdote / personal anecdote / amusing anecdote / anecdotal evidence**

👉 Example: The professor opened her lecture with a humorous anecdote about her own research blunders.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The keynote speaker captivated the audience with a personal **anecdote** about overcoming adversity during her early career.
   主讲人用一段关于她早期职业生涯中克服逆境的个人轶事吸引了观众。

2. While entertaining, a single **anecdote** should never be treated as sufficient evidence to support a broad scientific claim.
   虽然有趣，但单一轶事永远不应被视为支持广泛科学主张的充分证据。

3. His memoir is filled with charming **anecdotes** that vividly illuminate the cultural landscape of postwar Europe.
   他的回忆录充满了迷人的轶事，生动地展现了战后欧洲的文化面貌。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "anec-dote" as "a neck note" — a little note you pass around by whispering at the neck, like sharing a short story.
把"anecdote"想成"a neck note"——一个你凑到脖子旁边悄悄传递的小纸条，就像分享一段小故事。
