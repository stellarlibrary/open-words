# ⚔️ subjugate

🔊 /ˈsʌbdʒəˌɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To bring under domination or control; to conquer and force into submission
**中文：** v. 征服，镇压

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conquer（征服）
- overpower（制服）
- suppress（镇压）
- dominate（统治）
- vanquish（击败）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **subjugate a nation / subjugate the population / subjugate one's desires / subjugate opposition**

👉 Example: The invading army sought to subjugate the entire region through a combination of military force and political coercion.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The imperial forces attempted to **subjugate** the rebellious provinces, deploying thousands of soldiers to enforce compliance with central authority.
   帝国军队试图征服叛乱的省份，部署了数千名士兵以强制服从中央权威。

2. Critics argued that the new legislation was designed to **subjugate** civil liberties under the guise of protecting national security interests.
   批评人士认为，新立法旨在以保护国家安全利益为幌子来压制公民自由。

3. The philosopher urged individuals to **subjugate** their baser impulses through disciplined reason and unwavering commitment to moral principles.
   这位哲学家敦促人们通过严格的理性和对道德原则的坚定承诺来克制自己低级的冲动。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sub-jug-ate" as putting someone "under the yoke" (jugum in Latin) — forcing them to carry a heavy burden of obedience.
把"subjugate"想成把某人"置于轭下"（拉丁语jugum意为轭）——强迫他们承担服从的沉重负担。
