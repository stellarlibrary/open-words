# ⚔️ wield

🔊 /wiːld/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To hold and use a weapon or tool; to have and be able to exercise power, authority, or influence
**中文：** v. 使用；行使；挥舞；支配，掌权

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- brandish（挥舞）
- exercise（行使）
- employ（运用）
- exert（施加）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **wield power / wield influence / wield authority / wield a weapon / wield a sword**

👉 Example: The prime minister wielded considerable influence over the country's foreign policy decisions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Throughout history, monarchs who **wield** absolute power without accountability have frequently provoked rebellion among their oppressed subjects.
   纵观历史，不受约束地行使绝对权力的君主经常激起被压迫臣民的反抗。

2. The lobbyist group **wielded** significant influence over legislative proceedings, shaping policies that favored corporate interests over public welfare.
   这个游说团体对立法程序施加了重大影响，制定了有利于企业利益而非公共福利的政策。

3. Skilled rhetoricians **wield** language as a powerful instrument capable of persuading even the most skeptical audiences.
   熟练的修辞学家将语言作为强有力的工具来使用，能够说服最持怀疑态度的听众。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "wield" as "we + held" — we held the power, we wielded it to make things happen.
把"wield"想成"we + held（我们握住）"——我们握住了权力，我们行使权力来推动事情发生。
