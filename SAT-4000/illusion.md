# 🪞 illusion

🔊 /ɪˈluːʒən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A false perception or belief; something that deceives the senses or the mind
**中文：** n. 幻觉，错觉；假象

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- delusion（错觉）
- mirage（幻象）
- fantasy（幻想）
- hallucination（幻觉）
- misconception（误解）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **optical illusion / under the illusion / create an illusion / shatter the illusion**

👉 Example: Many voters were under the illusion that the new policy would immediately reduce taxes for everyone.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The magician created a convincing **illusion** that made the entire audience believe the assistant had genuinely vanished from the stage.
   魔术师制造了一个令人信服的幻象，让全场观众相信助手真的从舞台上消失了。

2. The economic prosperity of the previous decade proved to be an **illusion** built upon unsustainable levels of consumer debt.
   前十年的经济繁荣被证明是建立在不可持续的消费者债务水平上的假象。

3. The desert heat often produces the **illusion** of shimmering water on the distant horizon, deceiving weary travelers.
   沙漠的高温常在远处地平线上制造出闪闪发光的水面幻觉，欺骗疲惫的旅行者。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "illusion" as "ill + vision" — a sick or distorted vision that tricks you into seeing something that is not real.
把"illusion"想成"ill（不好的）+ vision（视觉）"——一种不正常的视觉，让你看到并非真实存在的东西。
