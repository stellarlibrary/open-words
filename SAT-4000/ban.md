# 🚷 ban

🔊 /bæn/<br>
🏷️ *v. n. | 动词 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** (v.) To officially prohibit or forbid something by law, rule, or decree. (n.) An official order that prohibits something; a formal prohibition or embargo.

**中文：** （动词）禁止，取缔，明令禁止。（名词）禁令，禁止令。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- prohibit（禁止）
- forbid（禁止）
- embargo（禁运）
- outlaw（宣布非法）
- proscribe（禁止）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Ban" is a strong, direct word implying an official or authoritative prohibition. It is commonly used with "on" as a noun: "a ban on smoking." As a verb, it takes a direct object: "The government banned the substance." It differs from "forbid" in that "ban" usually implies a formal, public, or legal action.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The city council voted unanimously to **ban** single-use plastic bags in all retail establishments starting next year.
   市议会一致投票决定从明年起在所有零售场所**禁止**使用一次性塑料袋。

2. The international community imposed a comprehensive **ban** on nuclear weapons testing to prevent further environmental contamination.
   国际社会实施了全面的核武器试验**禁令**，以防止进一步的环境污染。

3. Several nations have **banned** the export of critical raw materials deemed essential to their own national security interests.
   几个国家已经**禁止**出口被视为对本国国家安全利益至关重要的关键原材料。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Ban" is short, sharp, and final — just like a ban itself. Think of a big red "BANNED" stamp slamming down on a document. The word is as blunt as the action it describes.

"Ban"这个词简短、尖锐而决绝——就像禁令本身一样。想象一个大红色的"BANNED（已禁止）"印章猛地盖在文件上。这个词和它所描述的行为一样直截了当。
