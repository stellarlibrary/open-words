# 😟 reluctant

🔊 /rɪˈlʌktənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Unwilling and hesitant; feeling or showing aversion, hesitation, or disinclination toward doing something
**中文：** adj. 不情愿的；勉强的；顽抗的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unwilling（不愿意的）
- hesitant（犹豫的）
- disinclined（不乐意的）
- loath（不愿的）
- averse（反对的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reluctant to do something / reluctant participant / reluctant hero / reluctant agreement**

👉 Example: The witness was reluctant to testify, fearing retaliation from the accused party.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Many investors remain **reluctant** to commit substantial capital to emerging markets due to concerns about political instability.
   由于对政治不稳定的担忧，许多投资者仍不愿向新兴市场投入大量资金。

2. The **reluctant** leader eventually accepted the nomination after colleagues convinced her of the urgent need for reform.
   这位不情愿的领导者在同事们说服她改革的迫切需要后，最终接受了提名。

3. Students were **reluctant** to voice their opinions in class until the professor created a more supportive and inclusive atmosphere.
   学生们不愿在课堂上表达自己的观点，直到教授营造了一个更具支持性和包容性的氛围。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "reluctant" as "re-luc-tant" — imagine someone pulling back (re-) from a task, dragging their feet and resisting every step.
把"reluctant"想成有人在一项任务前不断后退——拖着脚步，每一步都在抵抗。
