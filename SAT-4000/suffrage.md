# 🗳️ suffrage

🔊 /ˈsʌfrɪdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The right to vote in political elections; the act of voting; the right to participate in government
**中文：** n. 投票；选举权，参政权

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- franchise（选举权）
- ballot（投票）
- enfranchisement（赋予选举权）
- voting right（投票权）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **universal suffrage / women's suffrage / suffrage movement / extend suffrage to**

👉 Example: The suffrage movement of the early twentieth century transformed democratic participation across the Western world.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The amendment granted universal **suffrage** to all citizens regardless of race, gender, or socioeconomic background in the nation.
   该修正案授予所有公民普选权，不论其种族、性别或社会经济背景。

2. Historians credit the **suffrage** movement with fundamentally reshaping democratic institutions and expanding political representation for marginalized groups.
   历史学家认为选举权运动从根本上重塑了民主制度，并扩大了边缘群体的政治代表权。

3. Several developing nations have only recently achieved full **suffrage** for women after decades of sustained advocacy and legislative reform.
   几个发展中国家在经过数十年的持续倡导和立法改革后，才最近实现了妇女的完全选举权。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "suffrage" as "suffer + age" — people suffered for ages to win the right to vote.
把"suffrage"想成"suffer + age"——人们经历了漫长的苦难才赢得了投票权。
