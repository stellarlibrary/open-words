# 👋 valediction

🔊 /ˌvælɪˈdɪkʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act of saying farewell; a farewell address or statement, especially one given at a graduation ceremony
**中文：** n. 告别演说；告别词

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- farewell（告别）
- goodbye（道别）
- parting words（临别赠言）
- send-off（送别）
- leave-taking（告辞）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **deliver a valediction / valediction speech / final valediction / a moving valediction**

👉 Example: The graduating student delivered a heartfelt valediction that moved the entire audience to tears.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The retiring professor's **valediction** to the university community was a stirring reflection on decades of scholarly achievement.
   这位即将退休的教授对大学社区的告别演说是对数十年学术成就的感人回顾。

2. In her eloquent **valediction**, the ambassador expressed gratitude for the diplomatic progress achieved during her tenure abroad.
   在她雄辩的告别辞中，大使对她在海外任期内取得的外交进展表示了感激。

3. The poet's final collection served as a literary **valediction**, bidding farewell to themes that had defined his entire career.
   这位诗人的最后一部诗集是一篇文学告别词，向定义了他整个职业生涯的主题道别。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "valediction" as "vale + diction" — "vale" is Latin for farewell, and "diction" means speech, so it is literally a farewell speech.
把"valediction"想成"vale + diction"——"vale"是拉丁语中的"再见"，"diction"意思是言辞，所以字面意思就是告别演说。
