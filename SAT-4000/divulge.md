# 🤫 divulge

🔊 /dɪˈvʌldʒ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make known something private or secret; to reveal or disclose confidential information
**中文：** v. 泄露；暴露；透露

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reveal（揭露）
- disclose（公开）
- expose（暴露）
- leak（泄露）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **divulge a secret / divulge information / divulge details / refuse to divulge**

👉 Example: The whistleblower decided to divulge classified documents revealing widespread government surveillance of citizens.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist refused to **divulge** the identity of her confidential source despite intense pressure from law enforcement authorities.
   尽管受到执法部门的强烈压力，这位记者仍拒绝透露其机密消息来源的身份。

2. Former employees were contractually prohibited from **divulging** proprietary trade secrets to competitors for a period of five years.
   前雇员在合同上被禁止在五年内向竞争对手泄露专有商业机密。

3. The diplomat carefully avoided **divulging** sensitive details about the ongoing negotiations between the two rival nations.
   这位外交官小心翼翼地避免透露两个对立国家之间正在进行的谈判的敏感细节。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "divulge" as "di-vulge" where "vulge" sounds like "vulgar" — making something common or public that was once private.
把"divulge"想成"di(散开)+vulge(传播)"——把原本私密的事情传播出去，公之于众。
