# 🛑 stagnate

🔊 /ˈstæɡneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cease to flow or move; to become inactive, sluggish, or stale; to fail to develop or progress
**中文：** vt. 停滞，变萧条；淤塞

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- languish（衰弱）
- stall（停顿）
- deteriorate（恶化）
- idle（闲置）
- atrophy（萎缩）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **stagnate economically / wages stagnate / growth stagnates / stagnate in a position**

👉 Example: Without continued investment in innovation, the industry will inevitably stagnate.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The regional economy began to **stagnate** after the closure of several major manufacturing plants in the area.
   几家大型制造工厂关闭后，该地区的经济开始停滞不前。

2. Critics warned that student achievement would **stagnate** if the outdated curriculum was not thoroughly revised.
   批评人士警告说，如果不彻底修订过时的课程，学生的成绩将会停滞。

3. His career seemed to **stagnate** after he declined the promotion and remained in the same role for years.
   在他拒绝晋升并在同一职位上待了多年后，他的职业生涯似乎陷入了停滞。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "stagnate" as "stag-wait" — like a stag waiting endlessly without moving forward, stuck in the same place with no progress.
把"stagnate"想成"stag-wait"——就像一只雄鹿无尽地等待，不向前移动，停留在原地没有进展。
