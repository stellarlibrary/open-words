# 🤚 interpose

🔊 /ˌɪntərˈpoʊz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To place or insert between one thing and another; to put forth as an interruption or intervention; to intervene or come between
**中文：** v. 提出（异议等）；使插入；使干涉

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- intervene（介入）
- insert（插入）
- interject（插话）
- mediate（调解）
- intrude（侵入）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **interpose an objection / interpose oneself / interpose between / interpose a barrier**

👉 Example: The judge allowed the attorney to interpose an objection before the witness continued testifying.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat sought to **interpose** herself between the two conflicting nations before their dispute escalated into armed conflict.
   这位外交官试图在两个冲突国家之间进行干预，以防争端升级为武装冲突。

2. The defense attorney moved to **interpose** a formal objection when the prosecutor introduced evidence that had not been disclosed.
   当检察官出示未公开的证据时，辩护律师提出了正式异议。

3. A thick curtain of fog **interposed** itself between the approaching vessel and the harbor, obscuring the navigational markers completely.
   一层厚厚的浓雾横亘在驶来的船只与港口之间，完全遮挡了导航标志。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inter-POSE" — imagine someone striking a pose right in between two people talking, physically inserting themselves into the conversation.
把"interpose"想成"inter-POSE"——想象某人在两个正在交谈的人之间摆姿势，把自己插入对话之中。
