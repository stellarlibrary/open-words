# 📜 purport

🔊 /pɜːrˈpɔːrt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To claim or profess, often falsely; to appear to be or intend. (n.) The meaning, substance, or main point of something
**中文：** v. 声称；意图；意指 n. 意义，主旨；意图

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- claim（声称）
- profess（自称）
- allege（宣称）
- gist（要旨）
- substance（实质）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **purport to be / purport to show / the purport of a document / purported benefits**

👉 Example: The document purports to be an authentic medieval manuscript, but scholars have raised serious doubts.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The email **purported** to be from the company's chief executive, but cybersecurity analysts identified it as phishing.
   这封电子邮件声称来自公司首席执行官，但网络安全分析师将其识别为网络钓鱼邮件。

2. The general **purport** of the senator's lengthy speech was that military spending should be significantly increased.
   这位参议员冗长演讲的大意是军事开支应该大幅增加。

3. Several studies **purport** to demonstrate a causal link, yet the methodology employed remains highly questionable.
   几项研究声称证明了因果关系，但所采用的方法论仍然非常值得质疑。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pur-port" as "purpose + report" — a purport is the purpose behind a report, or what someone reports their purpose to be (often falsely).
把"purport"想成"purpose（目的）+ report（报告）"——purport是报告背后的目的，或某人报告其目的所在（通常是虚假的）。
