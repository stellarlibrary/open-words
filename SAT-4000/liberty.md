# 🏛️ liberty

🔊 /ˈlɪbərti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The state of being free from oppressive restrictions; the power to act, speak, or think freely; an instance of presumptuous or improper behavior
**中文：** n. 自由；冒失，冒昧，失礼

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- freedom（自由）
- independence（独立）
- autonomy（自治）
- emancipation（解放）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **at liberty to / take the liberty of / civil liberties / liberty and justice**

👉 Example: Citizens must remain vigilant to protect their civil liberties from government overreach.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The constitution guarantees every citizen the **liberty** to express their opinions freely without fear of government persecution.
   宪法保障每位公民自由表达意见而不必担心政府迫害的权利。

2. He took the **liberty** of rearranging the entire office layout without consulting his colleagues or obtaining managerial approval first.
   他冒昧地重新安排了整个办公室布局，事先没有征求同事意见或获得管理层批准。

3. The museum exhibition explored how generations of activists fought tirelessly for **liberty** and equal rights throughout American history.
   博物馆展览探索了几代活动家如何在美国历史上为自由和平等权利而不懈奋斗。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "liberty" and the Statue of Liberty — she stands tall with a torch, symbolizing freedom and the right to live without oppression.
想到"liberty"就想到自由女神像——她高举火炬，象征着自由和不受压迫地生活的权利。
