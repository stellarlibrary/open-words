# 🎺 fanfare

🔊 /ˈfænˌfɛr/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A showy outward display or ostentatious publicity; a short, loud piece of music played on brass instruments, typically to announce something
**中文：** n. 喇叭或号角嘹亮的吹奏声；吹牛，炫耀

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flourish（夸示）
- spectacle（盛况）
- ostentation（炫耀）
- pomp（排场）
- ballyhoo（大肆宣传）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **with great fanfare / without fanfare / media fanfare / fanfare of trumpets**

👉 Example: The new policy was introduced with great fanfare but ultimately failed to deliver results.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corporation launched its latest product with enormous **fanfare**, including a globally televised ceremony attended by prominent celebrities.
   该公司以巨大的排场推出了最新产品，包括一场有知名人士出席的全球电视转播仪式。

2. Despite the initial **fanfare** surrounding the diplomatic agreement, critics soon questioned whether it would produce any tangible results.
   尽管外交协议最初备受瞩目，批评者很快就质疑它是否会产生任何切实的成果。

3. The researcher published her groundbreaking findings without **fanfare**, preferring to let the scientific evidence speak entirely for itself.
   这位研究员低调地发表了她的突破性发现，更愿意让科学证据完全自己说话。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fan + fare" — like the fare (show) put on for fans, a big flashy display to impress an audience.
把"fanfare"想成"fan（粉丝）+ fare（表演）"——为粉丝上演的大型华丽表演，就是炫耀和排场。
