# ❌ cancellation

🔊 /ˌkænsəˈleɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act of deciding that something previously planned or arranged will not take place; the act of annulling or revoking something.
**中文：** n. 取消；删除；废除；注销

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- annulment（废止）
- revocation（撤销）
- abolition（废除）
- termination（终止）
- withdrawal（撤回）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cancellation of / cancellation fee / flight cancellation / order cancellation / cancellation policy**

👉 Example: The cancellation of the conference disappointed hundreds of researchers who had planned to attend.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The abrupt **cancellation** of the trade agreement left both nations scrambling to renegotiate their economic partnerships.
   贸易协定的突然取消使两国不得不仓促重新谈判其经济伙伴关系。

2. Due to severe weather conditions, the airline announced the **cancellation** of all departing flights until further notice.
   由于恶劣天气条件，航空公司宣布取消所有出发航班，直到另行通知。

3. The **cancellation** of the research grant forced the laboratory to suspend its ongoing experiments indefinitely.
   研究资助的取消迫使实验室无限期暂停其正在进行的实验。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cancel-lation" as "cancel + action" — the action of canceling something, crossing it out and making it void.
把"cancellation"想成"cancel（取消）+ action（行为）"——取消某事的行为，把它划掉使之无效。
