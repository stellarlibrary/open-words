# 🚫 misbehavior

🔊 /ˌmɪsbɪˈheɪvjər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Improper or bad conduct; behavior that violates accepted norms or standards of decency
**中文：** n. 品行不端；不礼貌

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- misconduct（不当行为）
- wrongdoing（不法行为）
- transgression（违规）
- impropriety（不正当行为）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **student misbehavior / patterns of misbehavior / punish misbehavior / classroom misbehavior**

👉 Example: Repeated misbehavior in the workplace can result in formal disciplinary proceedings.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The school implemented a new disciplinary policy to address the rising incidents of student **misbehavior** during unsupervised lunch periods.
   学校实施了一项新的纪律政策，以解决无人监督的午餐时间学生不当行为增多的问题。

2. The committee investigated allegations of **misbehavior** among senior officials who had reportedly abused their positions of authority.
   委员会调查了据报滥用职权的高级官员品行不端的指控。

3. Psychologists suggest that childhood **misbehavior** often signals underlying emotional distress rather than a deliberate intention to cause trouble.
   心理学家认为，童年时期的不良行为往往是潜在情绪困扰的信号，而非故意捣乱。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mis-behavior" — "mis" means bad or wrong, added to "behavior." It is simply the noun form of behaving badly.
把"misbehavior"拆成"mis（不良的）+ behavior（行为）"——就是"不良行为"的名词形式，简单直接。
