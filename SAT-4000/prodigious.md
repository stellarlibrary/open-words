# 🌟 prodigious

🔊 /prəˈdɪdʒəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Remarkably great in extent, size, or degree; extraordinary; enormous; marvelous
**中文：** adj. 惊人的，异常的；巨大的；奇妙的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- enormous（巨大的）
- immense（庞大的）
- extraordinary（非凡的）
- remarkable（惊人的）
- stupendous（惊人的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prodigious talent / prodigious amount / prodigious effort / prodigious achievement**

👉 Example: The young pianist demonstrated prodigious talent that left the entire audience in stunned silence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher produced a **prodigious** volume of work, publishing over fifty peer-reviewed articles in a single decade.
   这位研究人员产出了惊人的工作量，在十年内发表了五十多篇同行评审论文。

2. Her **prodigious** memory allowed her to recall detailed information from lectures attended several months earlier.
   她惊人的记忆力使她能够回忆起几个月前所听讲座的详细信息。

3. The construction project required a **prodigious** amount of resources, including thousands of tons of imported steel.
   该建设项目需要大量资源，包括数千吨进口钢材。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "prodigious" as "prodigy + ous" — something so great it belongs to a prodigy; amazingly large or impressive.
把"prodigious"想成"prodigy（天才）+ ous（形容词后缀）"——天才般的，惊人的，巨大的。
