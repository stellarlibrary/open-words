# 🎶 troubadour

🔊 /ˈtruːbədɔːr/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A medieval lyric poet who composed and sang songs, especially about courtly love; broadly, a singer or poet
**中文：** n. 行吟诗人；（中世纪的）游吟歌手

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- minstrel（吟游诗人）
- bard（诗人）
- balladeer（民谣歌手）
- poet（诗人）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **medieval troubadour / wandering troubadour / troubadour tradition / troubadour poetry**

👉 Example: The troubadour traveled from castle to castle, entertaining noble audiences with songs of love and chivalry.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **troubadour** tradition of southern France profoundly influenced the development of European vernacular literature during the medieval period.
   法国南部的行吟诗人传统深刻影响了中世纪欧洲方言文学的发展。

2. Scholars regard the **troubadour** as a pivotal figure in the history of Western music and the evolution of lyric poetry.
   学者们认为行吟诗人是西方音乐史和抒情诗歌发展中的关键人物。

3. The modern singer-songwriter has often been compared to a **troubadour**, carrying personal stories and social commentary through melody.
   现代创作型歌手经常被比作行吟诗人，通过旋律传递个人故事和社会评论。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "trouba-dour" — "trouba" sounds like "trouble" and "dour" like "door," imagine a poet knocking on doors, singing about the troubles of love.
把"troubadour"想成"trouba（像trouble，烦恼）+ dour（像door，门）"——想象一个诗人敲开一扇扇门，吟唱关于爱情烦恼的歌谣。
