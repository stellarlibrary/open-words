# 🔄 reconsider

🔊 /ˌriːkənˈsɪdər/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To think about something again, especially with the intention of changing a decision or opinion
**中文：** v. 重新考虑；重新审议

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reassess（重新评估）
- reevaluate（重新评价）
- rethink（重新思考）
- review（审查）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reconsider a decision / reconsider one's position / reconsider the matter / ask someone to reconsider**

👉 Example: The board was asked to reconsider its decision to close the community library.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. After reviewing the compelling evidence, the committee agreed to **reconsider** its original ruling on the case.
   在审查了令人信服的证据后，委员会同意重新审议对该案件的原始裁决。

2. The professor urged the administration to **reconsider** the proposed budget cuts that would affect vital research programs.
   教授敦促行政部门重新考虑拟议的预算削减，因为这将影响重要的研究项目。

3. Many voters called upon the legislature to **reconsider** the controversial amendment before the final ratification vote.
   许多选民呼吁立法机构在最终批准投票前重新审议这项有争议的修正案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-consider" as "consider again" — the prefix "re-" means to do something once more, so you are thinking it over a second time.
把"reconsider"拆成"re-consider"——前缀"re-"表示再次，所以就是再考虑一次。
