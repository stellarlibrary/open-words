# 🔮 portent

🔊 /ˈpɔːrtent/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A sign or warning that something momentous or calamitous is likely to happen; an omen; a prodigy or marvel
**中文：** n. 前兆；预示；迹象；异常之物

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- omen（预兆）
- harbinger（先驱）
- presage（前兆）
- forewarning（预警）
- augury（占卜）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a portent of doom / an ominous portent / a portent of change / dark portent**

👉 Example: The sudden drop in stock prices was seen as a portent of the economic downturn that followed.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Ancient Romans interpreted unusual celestial events as **portents** of great political upheaval or divine displeasure.
   古罗马人将异常的天象解读为重大政治动荡或神灵不悦的前兆。

2. The scientist warned that the accelerating loss of biodiversity could be a **portent** of ecological collapse worldwide.
   这位科学家警告说，生物多样性的加速丧失可能是全球生态崩溃的预兆。

3. Many historians view the assassination as a **portent** of the widespread conflict that would engulf the continent.
   许多历史学家将这次暗杀视为即将席卷整个大陆的大规模冲突的前兆。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "por-tent" as "pour tent" — imagine pouring out the contents of a fortune-teller's tent, revealing signs and omens of the future.
把"portent"想成"pour(倒出) tent(帐篷)"——想象倒出占卜师帐篷里的东西，揭示未来的迹象和预兆。
