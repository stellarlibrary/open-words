# 📜 ratify

🔊 /ˈrætɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To give formal consent or approval to something, making it officially valid or binding
**中文：** v. 批准；认可

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- approve（批准）
- endorse（认可）
- confirm（确认）
- validate（使生效）
- sanction（准许）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **ratify a treaty / ratify an agreement / ratify a constitution / ratify a decision**

👉 Example: The legislature voted overwhelmingly to ratify the proposed amendment to the constitution.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. All participating nations must **ratify** the arms reduction treaty before its provisions can be formally implemented in law.
   所有参与国必须批准军备削减条约，其条款才能在法律上正式实施。

2. The board of directors convened an emergency session to **ratify** the merger agreement with the competing pharmaceutical firm.
   董事会召开紧急会议，批准与竞争对手制药公司的合并协议。

3. It took nearly three years for enough states to **ratify** the constitutional amendment guaranteeing equal protection under the law.
   足够多的州批准保障法律面前人人平等的宪法修正案花了将近三年时间。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ratify" as "rat-ify" — imagine a king's rat stamp that makes documents official; to ratify is to stamp formal approval.
把"ratify"想成"rat（老鼠）+ ify"——想象国王的老鼠印章使文件正式生效；批准就是盖上正式的印章。
