# 🌱 inchoate

🔊 /ɪnˈkoʊeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, verb | 形容词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Just begun and not fully formed or developed; rudimentary. (v.) To begin or commence
**中文：** adj. 早期的；刚开始的；未充分发展的；未形成的 v. 开始

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- nascent（新生的）
- embryonic（萌芽的）
- rudimentary（初步的）
- undeveloped（未发展的）
- budding（崭露头角的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inchoate idea / inchoate plan / inchoate feelings / inchoate stage / inchoate form**

👉 Example: The proposal remained inchoate, lacking the detailed analysis necessary for the committee's approval.

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher's **inchoate** theory required extensive empirical testing before it could be presented to the broader scientific community.
   这位研究人员尚未成熟的理论需要大量的实证测试，才能向更广泛的科学界展示。

2. In its **inchoate** stages, the democratic movement consisted of loosely organized groups with no unified political platform.
   在早期阶段，这场民主运动由松散组织的团体组成，没有统一的政治纲领。

3. The author's **inchoate** manuscript revealed brilliant ideas that nonetheless demanded substantial revision and structural refinement.
   这位作者尚未完善的手稿展现了精彩的想法，但仍然需要大量的修改和结构上的完善。

Think of "in-CHOATE" as the opposite of "complete" — something inchoate is like a baby chick still in its egg, barely formed and just beginning to take shape.
把"inchoate"想成"complete（完整的）"的反义——inchoate就像一只还在蛋壳里的小鸡，刚刚开始成形，远未完全发展。
