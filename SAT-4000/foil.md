# ⚔️ foil

🔊 /fɔɪl/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To prevent the success of; to frustrate or thwart a plan or attempt. (n.) A thin sheet of metal; a person or thing that contrasts with and enhances the qualities of another
**中文：** v. 挫败；阻止；衬托 n. 箔；金属薄片；陪衬

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- thwart（挫败）
- frustrate（阻挠）
- prevent（阻止）
- counterpart（对照物）
- contrast（对比）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **foil a plot / foil an attempt / serve as a foil / aluminum foil / perfect foil**

👉 Example: The detective managed to foil the elaborate scheme just hours before the criminals could execute their plan.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Intelligence agents worked around the clock to **foil** the conspiracy before it could threaten national security or public safety.
   情报人员夜以继日地工作，以**挫败**这一阴谋，防止其威胁国家安全或公共安全。

2. In the novel, the quiet protagonist serves as a **foil** to the ambitious antagonist, highlighting their fundamentally different moral values.
   在小说中，安静的主人公作为野心勃勃的反派的**陪衬**，凸显了他们根本不同的道德价值观。

3. Security measures at the border successfully **foiled** multiple smuggling attempts during the coordinated international enforcement operation last autumn.
   边境安全措施在去年秋天的国际联合执法行动中成功**阻止**了多起走私企图。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of wrapping something in aluminum "foil" to block it — just as you foil a plan by blocking it from succeeding.
想象用铝"箔"把东西包起来挡住——就像你挫败一个计划，阻止它成功一样。
