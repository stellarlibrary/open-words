# 👎 contemptible

🔊 /kənˈtɛmptɪbəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Deserving contempt; despicable; worthy of scorn due to moral or ethical failings
**中文：** adj. 可鄙的；卑劣的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- despicable（卑鄙的）
- vile（卑劣的）
- loathsome（令人厌恶的）
- abhorrent（令人憎恶的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **contemptible behavior / contemptible act / morally contemptible / a contemptible lie**

👉 Example: The committee condemned the official's contemptible abuse of power as a violation of public trust.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist exposed the **contemptible** scheme in which executives had embezzled millions from the employee pension fund.
   记者揭露了高管从员工养老基金中挪用数百万的卑劣计划。

2. History judges those who remained silent in the face of injustice as equally **contemptible** as the perpetrators themselves.
   历史将那些面对不公却保持沉默的人视为与施害者同样可鄙。

3. The professor described the deliberate falsification of research data as a **contemptible** betrayal of scientific integrity.
   教授将故意伪造研究数据描述为对科学诚信的卑劣背叛。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "contempt-ABLE" — something so bad it is "able" to earn your contempt, fully deserving of your scorn.
把"contemptible"想成"能够（able）引起蔑视（contempt）的"——坏到让人不由得鄙视。
