# ✂️ curtail

🔊 /kɜːrˈteɪl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To reduce in extent or quantity; to cut short or impose a restriction on
**中文：** v. 缩减；减少；剪短

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reduce（减少）
- diminish（缩减）
- restrict（限制）
- abridge（缩短）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **curtail spending / curtail rights / curtail freedom / curtail activities**

👉 Example: The government decided to curtail public spending in order to address the growing budget deficit.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The administration announced measures to **curtail** civil liberties temporarily in response to the escalating national security threat.
   政府宣布采取措施暂时缩减公民自由，以应对不断升级的国家安全威胁。

2. Budget constraints forced the university to **curtail** several research programs that had been running for over a decade.
   预算限制迫使大学缩减了几个已经运行了十多年的研究项目。

3. The new regulations were designed to **curtail** industrial emissions and promote sustainable environmental practices across the sector.
   新法规旨在减少工业排放，并促进整个行业的可持续环保实践。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cur-tail" as "cut the tail" — when you cut the tail of something, you shorten it or reduce it.
把"curtail"想成"cut the tail（剪掉尾巴）"——当你剪掉某物的尾巴时，就是在缩短或减少它。
