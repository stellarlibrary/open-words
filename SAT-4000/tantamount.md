# ⚖️ tantamount

🔊 /ˈtæntəmaʊnt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Equivalent in seriousness, effect, or value; virtually the same as
**中文：** adj. 等价的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- equivalent（等同的）
- commensurate（相称的）
- identical（相同的）
- synonymous（同义的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **tantamount to (always followed by "to")** — e.g., tantamount to treason / tantamount to a confession / tantamount to failure

👉 Example: Refusing to vote on the measure was tantamount to rejecting it entirely.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee argued that reducing the research budget was **tantamount** to abandoning the university's commitment to scientific progress.
   委员会认为削减研究预算等同于放弃大学对科学进步的承诺。

2. In many legal systems, silence during an interrogation can be considered **tantamount** to an admission of guilt.
   在许多法律体系中，审讯期间的沉默可被视为等同于认罪。

3. The diplomat warned that imposing unilateral sanctions without consultation would be **tantamount** to a declaration of economic warfare.
   外交官警告说，未经协商就实施单边制裁将等同于宣布经济战。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tant-amount" — "that amount" is the same amount, meaning equivalent or equal in value.
把"tantamount"想成"tant-amount"——"那个数量(that amount)"是一样的数量，意味着等价或等同。
