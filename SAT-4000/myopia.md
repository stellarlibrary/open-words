# 👓 myopia

🔊 /maɪˈoʊpiə/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A condition of the eye in which distant objects appear blurred (nearsightedness); figuratively, a lack of foresight or long-term thinking
**中文：** n. 近视；目光短浅，缺乏远见

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- nearsightedness（近视）
- shortsightedness（目光短浅）
- narrow-mindedness（狭隘）
- tunnel vision（管见）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **intellectual myopia / political myopia / suffer from myopia / cultural myopia**

👉 Example: The committee's myopia prevented them from anticipating the long-term consequences of their decision.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics accused the administration of dangerous **myopia** in prioritizing short-term economic gains over sustainable environmental policies.
   批评者指责政府目光短浅，将短期经济利益置于可持续环境政策之上，十分危险。

2. The increasing prevalence of **myopia** among children has prompted researchers to investigate the effects of prolonged screen exposure.
   儿童近视的发病率不断上升，促使研究人员调查长时间接触屏幕的影响。

3. Corporate **myopia** led the company to ignore emerging market trends, ultimately resulting in a catastrophic decline in revenue.
   企业的目光短浅导致公司忽视了新兴市场趋势，最终造成收入的灾难性下降。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "my-opia" as "my optics are poor" — someone with myopia has poor vision, either literally or figuratively, seeing only what is close.
把"myopia"想成"my optics（我的视力）are poor（很差）"——有myopia的人视力不好，无论是字面上还是比喻上，只能看到近处的东西。
