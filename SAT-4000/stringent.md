# ⚖️ stringent

🔊 /ˈstrɪndʒənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Strict, precise, and exacting; imposing rigorous standards or severe conditions
**中文：** adj. 严厉的；迫切的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- strict（严格的）
- rigorous（严谨的）
- exacting（苛刻的）
- demanding（要求高的）
- severe（严厉的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **stringent regulations / stringent requirements / stringent measures / stringent controls**

👉 Example: The government imposed stringent regulations on industrial emissions to address growing environmental concerns.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The pharmaceutical company must comply with **stringent** safety standards before any new medication can be approved for public use.
   制药公司在任何新药获批上市之前必须遵守严格的安全标准。

2. Under increasingly **stringent** budgetary constraints, the university was forced to reduce faculty positions across multiple departments.
   在日益严格的预算限制下，大学被迫削减多个院系的教职岗位。

3. The new trade agreement includes **stringent** provisions designed to protect intellectual property rights in the digital economy.
   新的贸易协议包含严格的条款，旨在保护数字经济中的知识产权。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "string-ent" as being pulled tight like a string — stringent rules are tightly enforced with no slack or flexibility.
把"stringent"想成像绳子一样拉紧——严格的规则被紧紧执行，没有松懈或灵活性。
