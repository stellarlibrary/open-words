# 🌦️ fickle

🔊 /ˈfɪkəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Changing frequently, especially in regard to loyalties, interests, or affection; unpredictable and inconstant
**中文：** adj. 变化无常的；浮躁的；薄情的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- capricious（反复无常的）
- volatile（易变的）
- inconstant（不稳定的）
- mercurial（善变的）
- erratic（不稳定的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fickle weather / fickle audience / fickle nature / fickle friend / fickle fortune**

👉 Example: The fickle nature of public opinion makes long-term political planning extremely difficult.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **fickle** consumer market forced the company to constantly innovate and adapt its product offerings each quarter.
   变化无常的消费市场迫使公司每个季度不断创新和调整其产品供应。

2. Investors learned that **fickle** stock prices could swing dramatically in response to even the smallest geopolitical events.
   投资者了解到，变化无常的股价可能因最小的地缘政治事件而出现剧烈波动。

3. The **fickle** spring weather alternated between warm sunshine and freezing rain, frustrating farmers throughout the growing season.
   变幻无常的春季天气在温暖阳光和冰冷雨水之间交替，让农民在整个生长季节备感沮丧。

---

Think of "fickle" as "flick-le" — like flicking a switch back and forth, constantly changing direction without commitment.
把"fickle"想成"flick-le"——就像来回拨动开关，不断改变方向而没有坚定性。
