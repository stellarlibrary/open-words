# 👑 pretension

🔊 /prɪˈtɛnʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A claim or assertion of a right or merit; an unwarranted display of importance or superiority; affectation
**中文：** n. 自负；骄傲；要求；主张；借口

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- affectation（做作）
- pretentiousness（自命不凡）
- claim（主张）
- conceit（自负）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **pretension to / without pretension / literary pretensions / have pretensions of**

👉 Example: The small restaurant served excellent food without any pretension or unnecessary formality.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The author's **pretension** to literary greatness was undermined by the consistently mediocre quality of her published works.
   这位作者对文学伟大成就的自负因其出版作品一贯平庸的质量而站不住脚。

2. Despite his **pretension** to aristocratic lineage, historical records revealed that his family had quite ordinary and humble origins.
   尽管他声称自己有贵族血统，但历史记录显示他的家族出身相当普通和卑微。

3. The gallery was praised for displaying contemporary art with honesty and accessibility, entirely free of intellectual **pretension**.
   这家画廊因以诚实和通俗的方式展示当代艺术、完全没有知识分子的故作高深而受到赞扬。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-tension" as "tension before the truth" — the tension you create by putting on airs before people discover who you really are.
把"pretension"想成"pre-tension"——在人们发现你真实面目之前，你因装腔作势而制造出的"紧张感"。
