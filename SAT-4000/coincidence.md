# ✨ coincidence

🔊 /kəʊˈɪnsɪdəns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A remarkable occurrence of events or circumstances at the same time by accident but seemingly connected; the state of corresponding or being in agreement
**中文：** n. 巧合；一致；同时发生；符合

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- chance（偶然）
- fluke（侥幸）
- concurrence（同时发生）
- correspondence（一致）
- synchronicity（共时性）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **by coincidence / mere coincidence / sheer coincidence / strange coincidence / what a coincidence**

👉 Example: It was no mere coincidence that both nations announced their trade agreements on the same day.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. By remarkable **coincidence**, both researchers independently arrived at identical conclusions despite working on separate continents entirely.
   非常巧合的是，两位研究人员尽管在完全不同的大陆工作，却独立地得出了相同的结论。

2. The detective refused to dismiss the timing of the events as mere **coincidence** and continued to investigate potential connections.
   侦探拒绝将事件发生的时间仅仅视为巧合，继续调查可能存在的联系。

3. Statisticians caution that the **coincidence** of two variables does not necessarily imply a causal relationship between them.
   统计学家警告说，两个变量的一致性并不一定意味着它们之间存在因果关系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "coincidence" as "co-incidence" — two "incidents" happening "co" (together) at the same time, as if by fate or pure chance.
把"coincidence"想成"co（共同）-incidence（事件）"——两个"事件"同时"一起"发生，好像是命运安排或纯属偶然。
