# 🔥 exacerbate

🔊 /ɪɡˈzæsərˌbeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make a problem, situation, or negative feeling worse; to aggravate or intensify
**中文：** v. 使恶化，使加剧；激怒，使发怒

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- aggravate（加剧）
- worsen（恶化）
- intensify（加强）
- inflame（激化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **exacerbate a problem / exacerbate the situation / exacerbate tensions / exacerbate symptoms**

👉 Example: The prolonged drought exacerbated the already severe food shortage in the region.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The government's austerity measures only served to **exacerbate** the economic inequality that had been growing for decades.
   政府的紧缩措施只是加剧了数十年来不断加深的经济不平等。

2. Climate scientists warn that rising global temperatures will **exacerbate** the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events.
   气候科学家警告，全球气温升高将加剧极端天气事件的频率和强度。

3. The inflammatory remarks by the political leader **exacerbated** tensions between the two communities already on the verge of conflict.
   这位政治领导人的煽动性言论加剧了两个已处于冲突边缘的社区之间的紧张关系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-ACERB-ate" — "acerb" means bitter or harsh, so to exacerbate is to make something extra bitter or worse.
把"exacerbate"想成"ex-ACERB-ate"——"acerb"意为苦涩或严酷，所以exacerbate就是让事情变得更加苦涩或更糟。
