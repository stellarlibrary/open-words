# ⏸️ intermit

🔊 /ˌɪntərˈmɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause to stop or pause temporarily; to cease for a time before resuming
**中文：** v. 使中断；间歇，中断

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pause（暂停）
- suspend（中止）
- cease（停止）
- discontinue（中断）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **intermit briefly / intermit periodically / intermit and resume / intermit at intervals**

👉 Example: The fever would intermit for several hours before returning with renewed intensity.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The heavy rainfall would **intermit** for brief periods, only to resume with even greater intensity throughout the afternoon.
   大雨会短暂中断，但整个下午又以更大的强度恢复。

2. The committee decided to **intermit** the proceedings until additional evidence could be gathered and properly reviewed by counsel.
   委员会决定中断诉讼程序，直到收集到更多证据并由律师适当审查。

3. The patient's symptoms would **intermit** unpredictably, making it extremely difficult for physicians to establish a definitive diagnosis.
   患者的症状会不可预测地间歇出现，使得医生极难做出明确的诊断。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inter-MIT" — imagine MIT university taking a break "inter" (between) semesters, causing studies to intermit or pause temporarily.
把"intermit"想成"inter-MIT"——想象麻省理工学院在学期"之间"（inter）休息，导致学习暂时中断。
