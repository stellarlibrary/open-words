# 🔀 garble

🔊 /ˈɡɑːrbl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To distort or scramble a message so that it is misleading or incomprehensible; to take selectively from a text in a way that distorts the original meaning
**中文：** v. 断章取义；歪曲；混淆；篡改

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- distort（歪曲）
- scramble（搅乱）
- muddle（混淆）
- misrepresent（曲解）
- jumble（混杂）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **garble a message / garbled transmission / garbled account / garbled version**

👉 Example: The poor phone connection garbled his words, making the instructions impossible to follow.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The translator inadvertently **garbled** the diplomat's remarks, creating a misunderstanding that nearly derailed the sensitive negotiations.
   翻译无意中歪曲了外交官的讲话，造成了一场几乎破坏敏感谈判的误解。

2. Critics accused the media outlet of deliberately **garbling** the senator's statements to manufacture a controversy before the election.
   批评者指责该媒体机构故意断章取义地引用参议员的声明，以在选举前制造争议。

3. Atmospheric interference **garbled** the radio transmission, leaving the rescue team unable to determine the precise coordinates of survivors.
   大气干扰使无线电传输信号混乱，救援队无法确定幸存者的确切坐标。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "garble" as "garbage + babble" — when words become garbled, they turn into garbage-like babble that nobody can understand.
把"garble"想成"garbage + babble"——当话语被garble后，它们变成了像垃圾一样的胡言乱语，没人能理解。
