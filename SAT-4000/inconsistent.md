# 🔀 inconsistent

🔊 /ˌɪnkənˈsɪstənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Not staying the same throughout; containing contradictory elements; not compatible or in agreement
**中文：** adj. 不一致的；前后矛盾的；不协调的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- contradictory（矛盾的）
- erratic（不稳定的）
- incompatible（不相容的）
- variable（多变的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **inconsistent with / inconsistent results / inconsistent behavior / inconsistent performance**

👉 Example: The witness's testimony was inconsistent with the physical evidence presented by the prosecution.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researcher's findings were **inconsistent** with the prevailing theory, prompting a thorough review of the methodology.
   该研究者的发现与主流理论不一致，促使人们对研究方法进行彻底审查。

2. The student's **inconsistent** academic performance suggested that external factors were affecting her ability to concentrate.
   该学生不稳定的学业表现表明，外部因素正在影响她的注意力集中能力。

3. Several critics argued that the government's economic policies were fundamentally **inconsistent** with its stated reform objectives.
   多位评论家认为，政府的经济政策与其宣称的改革目标从根本上是矛盾的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-consistent" as "not consistent" — something that is not consistent keeps changing and contradicts itself.
把"inconsistent"拆分为"in（不）+ consistent（一致的）"——不一致的，就是前后矛盾、自相矛盾。
