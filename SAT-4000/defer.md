# ⏳ defer

🔊 /dɪˈfɜːr/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To postpone or delay; to yield respectfully to the judgment or authority of another
**中文：** v. 推迟，延期；听从，顺从

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- postpone（推迟）
- delay（延迟）
- yield（让步）
- submit（服从）
- comply（遵从）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **defer a decision / defer to someone's judgment / defer payment / defer to authority**

👉 Example: The committee voted to defer the final decision until additional research could be completed.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The board elected to **defer** the vote on the merger proposal until all financial documents had been thoroughly reviewed.
   董事会决定推迟对合并提案的投票，直到所有财务文件都经过彻底审查。

2. Junior physicians often **defer** to the expertise of senior consultants when making complex diagnostic decisions.
   初级医生在做出复杂诊断决定时，通常会听从资深顾问的专业意见。

3. The university agreed to **defer** her enrollment for one academic year so she could complete her internship abroad.
   大学同意将她的入学推迟一个学年，以便她在国外完成实习。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-fer" as "de-" (away) + "fer" (carry, from Latin ferre) — to carry something away to a later time, or to carry yourself away in submission to another.
把"defer"想成"de-"（离开）+ "fer"（携带）——把事情带到以后再做，即推迟；或在他人面前退让，即顺从。
