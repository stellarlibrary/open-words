# 👑 prerogative

🔊 /prɪˈrɒɡətɪv/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An exclusive right or privilege belonging to a particular person or group. (adj.) Having a special privilege
**中文：** n. 特权；专有权利 adj. 有特权的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- privilege（特权）
- entitlement（权利）
- right（权利）
- preeminence（优先权）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **royal prerogative / presidential prerogative / exercise one's prerogative / a prerogative of**

👉 Example: It is the manager's prerogative to assign tasks based on individual team members' strengths.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee chair exercised her **prerogative** to table the motion until additional evidence could be gathered and reviewed.
   委员会主席行使了她的特权，将动议搁置，直到收集和审查更多证据。

2. Throughout history, the power to declare war has been considered the **prerogative** of sovereign rulers and national legislatures.
   纵观历史，宣战权一直被视为主权统治者和国家立法机构的特权。

3. Selecting the methodology for a dissertation is typically the **prerogative** of the doctoral candidate in consultation with the advisor.
   选择论文的研究方法通常是博士生在与导师协商后的专有权利。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-rogative" as "pre-ask" (from Latin rogare, to ask) — someone who gets to ask first has the special privilege.
把"prerogative"想成"pre-rogare"（拉丁语rogare意为"询问"）——先被询问的人拥有特权。
