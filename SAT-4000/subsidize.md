# 💰 subsidize

🔊 /ˈsʌbsɪdaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To support financially; to grant money to assist or encourage an enterprise
**中文：** v. 给补助金／津贴／奖金

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fund（资助）
- finance（提供资金）
- sponsor（赞助）
- underwrite（承保资助）
- endow（捐赠）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **subsidize an industry / heavily subsidized / government-subsidized / subsidize housing**

👉 Example: The federal government has long subsidized agricultural production to ensure food security nationwide.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Many governments **subsidize** renewable energy projects to accelerate the transition away from fossil fuel dependence.
   许多政府资助可再生能源项目，以加速摆脱对化石燃料的依赖。

2. Critics argue that continuing to **subsidize** failing industries only delays necessary economic restructuring and reform.
   批评人士认为，继续补贴失败的产业只会推迟必要的经济结构调整和改革。

3. The university **subsidizes** tuition for low-income students through a combination of grants and institutional scholarships.
   该大学通过助学金和院校奖学金相结合的方式为低收入学生提供学费补助。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "subsid-ize" as related to "subsidy" — to subsidize is to provide a subsidy, giving financial aid from below to prop something up.
把"subsidize"想成和"subsidy"（补贴）相关——subsidize就是提供补贴，从下面给予经济支持以支撑某事物。
