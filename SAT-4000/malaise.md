# 😶 malaise

🔊 /mæˈleɪz/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A general feeling of discomfort, unease, or illness without a clearly identifiable cause; a vague sense of mental or moral unwell-being affecting a group or society.
**中文：** n. 不舒服；不适；心神不安；萎靡不振

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unease（不安）
- discomfort（不适）
- lethargy（倦怠）
- languor（无精打采）
- anxiety（焦虑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **economic malaise / general malaise / a sense of malaise / national malaise / cultural malaise**

👉 Example: A deep malaise settled over the company as rumors of layoffs began to circulate among employees.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prolonged economic **malaise** eroded public confidence in the government's ability to manage fiscal policy effectively.
   长期的经济低迷削弱了公众对政府有效管理财政政策能力的信心。

2. The patient reported a persistent **malaise** that could not be attributed to any specific diagnosis after thorough examination.
   患者报告了一种持续性的不适感，经过全面检查后无法归因于任何具体的诊断。

3. Scholars argue that a cultural **malaise** has gripped modern society, manifesting as widespread apathy and declining civic engagement.
   学者们认为一种文化萎靡已笼罩现代社会，表现为广泛的冷漠和公民参与的减少。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mal" (bad, from French) + "aise" (ease) — malaise is "bad ease," a state where you feel neither well nor at ease, like a lingering discomfort you cannot pinpoint.
把"malaise"想成"mal"（坏的，源自法语）+"aise"（舒适）——malaise就是"不舒适"，一种既不健康也不安心的状态，像是一种挥之不去却又说不清的不适。
