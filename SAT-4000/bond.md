# 🔗 bond

🔊 /bɑːnd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A binding agreement; a certificate of debt; a connection or tie between people; an adhesive force. (v.) To join or unite firmly; to form a close relationship
**中文：** n. 债券；结合；约定；粘合剂；纽带 v. 结合，团结在一起

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- tie（纽带）
- connection（联系）
- attachment（依附）
- link（连接）
- union（结合）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bond between / bond with / form a bond / chemical bond / government bond**

👉 Example: The shared experience of hardship can form an unbreakable bond between individuals who endure it together.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **bond** between the two nations was strengthened through decades of diplomatic cooperation and cultural exchange programs.
   两国之间的纽带通过数十年的外交合作和文化交流项目得到了加强。

2. Investors chose to purchase government **bonds** as a stable asset during the period of considerable market volatility.
   在市场大幅波动期间，投资者选择购买政府债券作为稳定资产。

3. The newly adopted child began to **bond** with her foster parents after several weeks of patient and compassionate interaction.
   经过数周耐心而富有同情心的互动，这个被收养的孩子开始与养父母建立亲密关系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bond" as something that "binds" — whether it binds people emotionally or binds atoms chemically, it holds things together.
把"bond"想成一种"绑定"——无论是在情感上连接人们还是在化学上结合原子，它都将事物维系在一起。
