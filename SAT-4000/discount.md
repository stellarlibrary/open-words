# 💰 discount

🔊 /ˈdɪskaʊnt/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A reduction in the usual price of something. (v.) To reduce the price of something; to regard something as unworthy of consideration or dismiss
**中文：** n. 折扣 v. 打折；忽视

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- reduction（减少）
- deduction（扣除）
- disregard（忽视）
- dismiss（不予理会）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **at a discount / discount rate / discount the possibility / discount a theory / heavily discounted**

👉 Example: Analysts cautioned investors not to discount the potential impact of rising interest rates on the housing market.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics argue that we should not **discount** the long-term environmental consequences of rapid industrial expansion in developing nations.
   批评人士认为，我们不应忽视发展中国家快速工业扩张的长期环境后果。

2. The retailer offered a substantial **discount** on all merchandise during the annual clearance event to reduce excess seasonal inventory.
   零售商在年度清仓活动中对所有商品提供大幅折扣，以减少多余的季节性库存。

3. Historians warn against **discounting** the role of ordinary citizens in shaping major political movements throughout recorded human history.
   历史学家警告不要忽视普通公民在有记载的人类历史中塑造重大政治运动的作用。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-count" — to "not count" something. A price discount means part of the price does not count; to discount an idea means it does not count.
把"discount"拆成"dis-count"——"不计算"某物。价格折扣意味着部分价格不算数；忽视一个观点意味着它不值得考虑。
