# 🍽️ satiate

🔊 /ˈseɪʃieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To satisfy fully or to excess; to supply with more than enough so as to weary or disgust. (adj.) Fully satisfied; glutted
**中文：** v. 使充分满足；享用过度；使厌腻

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sate（使满足）
- glut（使饱食）
- surfeit（使过量）
- gorge（使吃撑）
- cloy（使厌腻）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **satiate one's appetite / satiate one's curiosity / satiate a desire / satiate a craving**

👉 Example: No amount of praise could satiate his insatiable desire for public recognition.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The lavish banquet was designed to **satiate** even the most demanding guests with an abundance of exotic delicacies.
   这场奢华的宴会旨在用丰富的珍馐美味充分满足即便是最挑剔的宾客。

2. After years of relentless travel, she finally felt **satiated** and longed for the quiet stability of a permanent home.
   经过多年不停的旅行，她终于感到厌倦，渴望一个安定之所带来的宁静稳定。

3. The documentary failed to **satiate** the public's curiosity, leaving many critical questions about the scandal entirely unanswered.
   这部纪录片未能满足公众的好奇心，留下了许多关于丑闻的关键问题完全未解答。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sati-ate" — "sat" sounds like "satisfied." When you satiate, you eat until you are so satisfied that you cannot take another bite.
把"satiate"想成"sat（满足）+ iate"——当你satiate时，你吃到完全满足，再也吃不下一口。
