# 🔀 juxtaposition

🔊 /ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act or an instance of placing two or more things side by side, often to compare or contrast; the state of being placed close together
**中文：** n. 并置，并列；毗邻；对比

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- contrast（对比）
- comparison（比较）
- adjacency（邻近）
- collocation（并置）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in juxtaposition with / striking juxtaposition / juxtaposition of ideas / juxtaposition between**

👉 Example: The juxtaposition of ancient ruins and modern skyscrapers made the cityscape remarkably dramatic.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **juxtaposition** of wealth and poverty within the same neighborhood revealed the profound economic inequality of the region.
   同一社区内贫富的并列对比揭示了该地区深刻的经济不平等。

2. In her novel, the author employs a deliberate **juxtaposition** of humor and tragedy to create a deeply compelling narrative.
   在她的小说中，作者刻意将幽默与悲剧并置，创造了一个引人入胜的叙事。

3. The **juxtaposition** of the candidate's public statements with his private correspondence exposed significant contradictions in his views.
   这位候选人公开声明与私人信件的对比暴露了他观点中的重大矛盾。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "juxtaposition" as "just-a-position" next to something else — it is the position of two things placed just beside each other.
把"juxtaposition"想成"just-a-position"（就在旁边的位置）——它是两个事物恰好被放在彼此旁边的状态。
