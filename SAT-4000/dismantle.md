# 🔧 dismantle

🔊 /dɪsˈmæntl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To take apart or pull down a structure, system, or institution; to disassemble piece by piece
**中文：** v. 拆除；拆卸；取消；摧毁

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disassemble（拆卸）
- demolish（拆毁）
- deconstruct（解构）
- strip down（拆解）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **dismantle a system / dismantle a structure / dismantle barriers / dismantle an organization**

👉 Example: The new administration vowed to dismantle the bureaucratic barriers that hindered economic growth.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Engineers were hired to carefully **dismantle** the aging bridge before constructing a modern replacement on the same site.
   工程师们被聘请来仔细拆除这座老化的桥梁，然后在同一地点建造一座现代化的替代桥梁。

2. The reform movement sought to **dismantle** systemic inequities that had persisted within the institution for several decades.
   这场改革运动旨在消除在该机构中持续了数十年的系统性不公平现象。

3. International negotiators agreed to **dismantle** the weapons program as a prerequisite for lifting the longstanding economic sanctions.
   国际谈判代表同意拆除武器计划，作为解除长期经济制裁的先决条件。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-mantle" as "removing the mantle (covering)" — you strip away each layer until nothing remains of the structure.
把"dismantle"想成"dis（去除）+ mantle（覆盖物）"——一层一层剥去覆盖物，直到结构被完全拆除。
