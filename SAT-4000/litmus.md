# 🧪 litmus

🔊 /ˈlɪtməs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A dye obtained from lichens used as an acid-base indicator; figuratively, a decisive test of quality or character
**中文：** n. 石蕊；（比喻）试金石，关键性测试

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- indicator（指示剂）
- benchmark（基准）
- touchstone（试金石）
- criterion（标准）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **litmus test / litmus paper / a political litmus test / serve as a litmus test**

👉 Example: The candidate's stance on healthcare became a litmus test for voters in the election.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator's vote on the bill served as a **litmus** test for her commitment to environmental reform.
   该参议员对该法案的投票成为检验她对环境改革承诺的试金石。

2. Scientists use **litmus** paper to determine quickly whether a solution is acidic or alkaline in laboratory settings.
   科学家在实验室中使用石蕊试纸来快速确定溶液是酸性还是碱性。

3. Public reaction to the policy proposal became a **litmus** test for the administration's overall popularity and credibility.
   公众对该政策提案的反应成为检验政府整体声望和公信力的试金石。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "litmus" as "lit + must" — it must light up (change color) to reveal the truth, just like a crucial test.
把"litmus"想成"lit（点亮）+ must（必须）"——它必须变色来揭示真相，就像一个关键的测试。
