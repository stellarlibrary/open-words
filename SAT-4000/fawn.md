# 🦌 fawn

🔊 /fɔːn/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To seek favor or approval by flattery and servile behavior. (n.) A young deer, typically less than one year old
**中文：** v. 奉承；讨好 n. 小鹿

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flatter（奉承）
- grovel（卑躬屈膝）
- toady（谄媚）
- kowtow（叩头讨好）
- cringe（卑躬屈膝）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fawn over / fawn on someone / fawn upon / fawning admiration / fawning behavior**

👉 Example: The courtiers would fawn over the king in hopes of receiving royal patronage and favor.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ambitious assistant would constantly **fawn** over the director, complimenting every decision in hopes of earning a promotion.
   这位野心勃勃的助理不断奉承主管，对每个决定都加以赞美，以期获得晋升。

2. Political commentators criticized the journalist for **fawn**ing over the candidate rather than asking tough and substantive questions.
   政治评论员批评这位记者对候选人一味奉承，而不是提出尖锐而有实质性的问题。

3. A spotted **fawn** emerged cautiously from the forest edge, drawing the silent attention of every wildlife photographer present.
   一只有斑点的小鹿小心翼翼地从森林边缘走出，吸引了在场每位野生动物摄影师的默默关注。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of a baby deer (fawn) that follows its mother everywhere — just like a person who fawns follows someone around with excessive flattery.
想象一只小鹿（fawn）到处跟着妈妈——就像一个阿谀奉承的人到处跟着别人，过度讨好。
