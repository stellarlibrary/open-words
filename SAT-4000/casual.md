# 👕 casual

🔊 /ˈkæʒuəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relaxed and unconcerned; not formal or ceremonious; happening by chance; irregular or temporary
**中文：** adj. 随便的；非正式的；临时的；偶然的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- informal（非正式的）
- nonchalant（漫不经心的）
- incidental（偶然的）
- offhand（随意的）
- temporary（临时的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **casual attire / casual conversation / casual remark / casual worker / casual acquaintance**

👉 Example: The company adopted a casual dress code to create a more relaxed workplace atmosphere.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Her **casual** remark about the merger inadvertently revealed confidential information to the assembled journalists.
   她关于合并的随意评论无意中向在场的记者透露了机密信息。

2. The professor maintained a **casual** demeanor during lectures, which helped students feel comfortable asking questions.
   教授在讲课时保持随和的态度，这让学生们在提问时感到轻松自在。

3. Many industries rely on **casual** laborers who are hired on a temporary basis during peak production seasons.
   许多行业依赖临时工，他们在生产高峰期被临时雇用。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "casual" as "cause-you-all" to relax — a casual setting causes everyone to feel at ease and informal.
把"casual"想成"cause-you-all"放松——一个casual的环境让每个人都感到轻松和非正式。
