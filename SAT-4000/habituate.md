# 🧠 habituate

🔊 /həˈbɪtʃueɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make or become accustomed to something through prolonged or repeated exposure
**中文：** v. 使习惯于

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- accustom（使习惯）
- acclimate（使适应）
- familiarize（使熟悉）
- condition（使适应）
- inure（使习惯）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **habituate to / habituate oneself to / become habituated / habituate an animal**

👉 Example: Over time, the researchers managed to habituate the wild primates to human presence for closer observation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Prolonged exposure to urban noise can **habituate** residents to decibel levels that would otherwise cause significant discomfort.
   长期暴露在城市噪音中可以使居民习惯于本来会造成明显不适的分贝水平。

2. The training program was designed to **habituate** new employees to the demanding pace of the corporate environment.
   该培训项目旨在使新员工适应公司环境中高要求的工作节奏。

3. Scientists attempted to **habituate** the endangered birds to captivity before introducing them into the breeding program.
   科学家们试图让这些濒危鸟类适应圈养环境，然后再将它们纳入繁殖计划。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "habituate" as "habit + uate" — to turn something into a habit through repeated practice until it feels natural.
把"habituate"想成"habit（习惯）+ uate（动词后缀）"——通过反复练习将某事变成习惯，直到感觉自然。
