# ⚠️ breach

🔊 /briːtʃ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A gap or break, especially in a wall or barrier; a violation or infraction of a law, obligation, or standard. (v.) To make a gap in; to break through; to violate or fail to observe
**中文：** n. 违背；破坏；破裂，裂口；缺口 vt. 打破；突破；违反

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- violation（违反）
- infringement（侵犯）
- rupture（破裂）
- gap（缺口）
- transgression（违规）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **breach of contract / breach of trust / security breach / breach the wall / breach protocol**

👉 Example: The data breach exposed millions of customer records to unauthorized third parties.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The company faced severe legal consequences after a significant **breach** of contract was discovered during the regulatory audit process.
   该公司在监管审计过程中被发现严重违反合同后，面临了严厉的法律后果。

2. Enemy forces managed to **breach** the fortress walls after a prolonged siege that lasted several months without interruption.
   敌军在持续数月不间断的围攻之后，终于突破了堡垒的城墙。

3. The security **breach** compromised sensitive government databases, prompting an immediate and comprehensive investigation by federal authorities.
   这次安全漏洞危及了敏感的政府数据库，促使联邦当局立即展开全面调查。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "breach" as "break + each" — when you breach something, you break each barrier or rule that stands in your way.
把"breach"想成"break + each"——当你breach某物时，你打破了每一个（each）挡在你面前的障碍或规则。
