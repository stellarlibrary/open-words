# 🏖️ furlough

🔊 /ˈfɜːrloʊ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A leave of absence, especially one granted to a soldier or an employee; a temporary layoff. (v.) To grant a leave of absence to; to lay off temporarily
**中文：** n. 休假；暂时解雇 v. 准假

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- leave（休假）
- layoff（解雇）
- respite（暂缓）
- recess（休息）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **on furlough / grant a furlough / furlough employees / military furlough**

👉 Example: During the budget crisis, the agency decided to furlough hundreds of non-essential workers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The soldier was granted a two-week **furlough** to visit his family before being deployed to the overseas base.
   这名士兵在被派往海外基地之前获准休假两周以探望家人。

2. The company was forced to **furlough** nearly a third of its workforce due to the severe economic downturn.
   由于严重的经济衰退，该公司被迫暂时解雇了近三分之一的员工。

3. After months of stressful negotiations, the diplomat welcomed her brief **furlough** as a much-needed period of rest.
   经过数月紧张的谈判，这位外交官欢迎她短暂的休假，视其为急需的休息时间。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fur-lough" as "fur-low" — when work is low, you get sent home on furlough, like an animal retreating to its fur den.
把"furlough"想成"fur-low"——当工作量低的时候，你被送回家休假，就像动物回到毛皮窝里一样。
