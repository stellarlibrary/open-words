# 📉 depreciate

🔊 /dɪˈpriːʃieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To diminish in value over time; to belittle or disparage; to reduce the recorded value of an asset in accounting
**中文：** v. 使贬值；贬低；轻视；折旧

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- devalue（贬值）
- diminish（减少）
- belittle（贬低）
- disparage（轻视）
- cheapen（降低价值）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **depreciate in value / depreciate an asset / depreciate over time / currency depreciates**

👉 Example: New automobiles begin to depreciate in value the moment they leave the dealership lot.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The national currency continued to **depreciate** against the dollar, causing widespread concern among economists and investors.
   本国货币对美元持续贬值，引起了经济学家和投资者的广泛关注。

2. Companies are required to **depreciate** their capital equipment over a predetermined period according to established accounting standards.
   公司须按照既定的会计准则在规定期限内对资本设备进行折旧处理。

3. Critics argue that such dismissive remarks serve only to **depreciate** the contributions of dedicated frontline workers.
   批评人士认为，这种轻蔑的言论只会贬低敬业的一线工作者的贡献。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "depreciate" as "de (down) + preci (price)" — the price goes down, meaning the value decreases over time.
把"depreciate"想成"de（向下）+ preci（价格）"——价格下降，即价值随时间递减。
