# 🙅 abjure

🔊 /æbˈdʒʊr/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To formally renounce or reject a belief, cause, or claim; to solemnly swear to give up; to avoid or abstain from
**中文：** vt. 发誓放弃；公开放弃（信仰、主张）；避免

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- renounce（放弃）
- forsake（抛弃）
- relinquish（放弃）
- recant（撤回）
- repudiate（否认）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **abjure violence / abjure one's faith / abjure allegiance / abjure a claim**

👉 Example: The dissident was forced to abjure his political beliefs under intense pressure from the regime.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The new citizens were required to **abjure** all allegiance to foreign sovereigns during the naturalization ceremony.
   新公民在入籍仪式上被要求宣誓放弃对外国君主的一切效忠。

2. The reformed leader publicly **abjured** the use of violence as a means of achieving political change.
   这位改过自新的领导人公开宣布放弃以暴力作为实现政治变革的手段。

3. After years of internal conflict, she decided to **abjure** her family's extremist ideology and start anew.
   经过多年的内心挣扎，她决定放弃家族的极端主义意识形态，重新开始。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Abjure" = "ab-" (away) + "jure" (swear, as in "jury"). To abjure is to swear away from something — a solemn rejection.
"Abjure" = "ab-"（远离）+ "jure"（发誓，如"jury"陪审团）。Abjure就是发誓远离某事——庄严的放弃。
