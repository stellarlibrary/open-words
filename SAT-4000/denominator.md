# 🔢 denominator

🔊 /dɪˈnɒmɪneɪtər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The number below the line in a fraction; a shared characteristic or standard used for comparison
**中文：** n. 分母；标准；命名者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- divisor（除数）
- standard（标准）
- common factor（公因数）
- benchmark（基准）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **common denominator / lowest common denominator / denominator of a fraction**

👉 Example: Finding the common denominator is essential when adding fractions with different bases.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The teacher explained that students must find a common **denominator** before they can accurately add or subtract unlike fractions.
   老师解释说，学生在准确加减不同分数之前必须找到公分母。

2. Critics argue that mass media often appeals to the lowest common **denominator**, sacrificing intellectual depth for broad popularity.
   批评者认为大众媒体经常迎合最低标准，为了广泛的流行度而牺牲思想深度。

3. Trust and mutual respect serve as the common **denominator** among all successful long-term professional partnerships and collaborations.
   信任和相互尊重是所有成功的长期专业伙伴关系与合作的共同标准。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "denominator" as "the namer down below" — in a fraction, the denominator sits below the line and names the type of parts.
把"denominator"想成"下面的命名者"——在分数中，分母位于横线下方，命名了部分的类型。
