# 🚧 impasse

🔊 /ˈɪmpæs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A situation in which no progress is possible; a deadlock or dead end
**中文：** n. 僵局；死路

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- deadlock（僵局）
- stalemate（僵持）
- standstill（停滞）
- gridlock（僵局）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reach an impasse / break the impasse / political impasse / at an impasse**

👉 Example: The peace talks reached an impasse when neither side was willing to compromise on territorial claims.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The trade negotiations reached an **impasse** after both delegations refused to make further concessions on key economic issues.
   双方代表团拒绝在关键经济问题上做出进一步让步后，贸易谈判陷入了僵局。

2. The city council found itself at an **impasse** over the proposed zoning changes, with members evenly divided on the matter.
   市议会在拟议的分区变更问题上陷入了僵局，成员们在此问题上意见各半。

3. A skilled diplomat was brought in to break the **impasse** between the two warring factions and restore productive dialogue.
   一位经验丰富的外交官被请来打破两个交战派系之间的僵局，恢复建设性对话。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "im-passe" — "im" means not, "passe" sounds like "pass," so an impasse is a situation you cannot pass through, a dead end.
把"impasse"想成"im-passe"——"im"表示"不"，"passe"听起来像"pass（通过）"，所以impasse就是"无法通过的局面"，即僵局。
