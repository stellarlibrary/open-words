# 📝 interpolate

🔊 /ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To insert or introduce something new or foreign into a text or conversation; to alter a text by inserting new material; to estimate values between known data points
**中文：** v. 篡改；插入新语句

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- insert（插入）
- interject（插话）
- introduce（引入）
- falsify（篡改）
- add（添加）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **interpolate data / interpolate a remark / interpolate values / interpolate into a text**

👉 Example: The editor was accused of interpolating misleading passages into the original manuscript.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Scholars discovered that a medieval scribe had **interpolated** several passages into the ancient manuscript that were not part of the original text.
   学者们发现一位中世纪抄写员在古代手稿中篡改插入了几段不属于原文的内容。

2. The mathematician used a proven algorithm to **interpolate** missing values between the known data points in the experimental dataset.
   数学家使用一种经过验证的算法在实验数据集中已知数据点之间插值出缺失的数值。

3. The senator paused to **interpolate** a brief but pointed remark about fiscal responsibility during her speech on healthcare reform.
   参议员在医疗改革演讲中停下来插入了一句简短但尖锐的关于财政责任的评论。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "inter-POLE-ate" — imagine placing new poles (posts) between existing ones in a fence, just as you interpolate new content between existing parts.
把"interpolate"想成"inter-POLE-ate"——想象在栅栏的现有柱子之间放置新柱子，就像在现有内容之间插入新内容一样。
