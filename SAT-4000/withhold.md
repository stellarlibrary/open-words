# 🤐 withhold

🔊 /wɪðˈhoʊld/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To hold back; to refrain from giving, granting, or allowing; to suppress or conceal information
**中文：** v. 保留，不给；隐瞒；抑制；拒绝

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- retain（保留）
- suppress（压制）
- conceal（隐瞒）
- restrain（抑制）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **withhold information / withhold payment / withhold consent / withhold evidence / withhold judgment**

👉 Example: The witness was charged with obstruction for deliberately withholding critical evidence from the investigation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The regulatory agency decided to **withhold** approval until the pharmaceutical company provided additional safety data.
   监管机构决定暂不批准，直到制药公司提供额外的安全数据。

2. Critics argued that the administration had deliberately chosen to **withhold** vital information from the general public.
   批评人士认为政府故意选择向公众隐瞒重要信息。

3. The landlord cannot legally **withhold** the security deposit without providing documented evidence of property damage.
   房东不能在没有提供财产损坏书面证据的情况下合法地扣留保证金。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "with-hold" as "holding something with you" — keeping it back and refusing to release it to others.
把"withhold"想成"with（和自己在一起）+ hold（持有）"——把东西握在手中不给别人。
