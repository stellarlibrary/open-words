# 🌫️ obscure

🔊 /əbˈskjʊr/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, verb | 形容词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Not clearly understood or expressed; not well known; dim or dark. (v.) To make unclear, conceal, or overshadow
**中文：** adj. 昏暗的，朦胧的；晦涩的，不清楚的；不著名的 v. 使模糊不清，掩盖；隐藏

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- unclear（不清楚的）
- vague（模糊的）
- conceal（隐藏）
- enigmatic（神秘的）
- overshadow（使黯然失色）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **obscure meaning / obscure reference / obscure the truth / obscure origins**

👉 Example: The poet's obscure symbolism has puzzled scholars for generations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The author deliberately employed **obscure** literary allusions that only the most erudite readers could identify and appreciate.
   作者刻意使用了晦涩的文学典故，只有最博学的读者才能识别和欣赏。

2. Heavy clouds of smoke from the wildfire began to **obscure** the mountain landscape, reducing visibility to mere meters.
   野火产生的浓烟开始遮蔽山区景观，将能见度降低到仅几米。

3. The politician attempted to **obscure** the true nature of the scandal by diverting public attention to unrelated issues.
   该政客试图通过将公众注意力转移到无关问题上来掩盖丑闻的真实性质。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ob-SCURE" as "obstacle to being sure" — when something is obscure, it creates an obstacle to understanding clearly.
把"obscure"想成"obstacle to being sure"——当某事物是obscure的，它构成了清楚理解的障碍。
