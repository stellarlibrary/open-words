# ⚛️ electron

🔊 /ɪˈlektrɒn/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A stable subatomic particle with a negative electric charge, found in all atoms and acting as the primary carrier of electricity in solids
**中文：** n. 电子

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- negative charge carrier（负电荷载体）
- subatomic particle（亚原子粒子）
- beta particle（贝塔粒子）
- lepton（轻子）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **electron cloud / electron orbit / free electron / valence electron / electron microscope**

👉 Example: The valence electron determines the chemical bonding behavior of an atom in molecular interactions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Each **electron** orbiting the nucleus occupies a specific energy level and cannot exist between these discrete quantum states.
   围绕原子核运行的每个电子占据一个特定的能级，不能存在于这些离散的量子态之间。

2. The flow of free **electron** particles through a copper conductor constitutes the electrical current measured in laboratory circuits.
   自由电子粒子通过铜导体的流动构成了实验室电路中测量的电流。

3. Scientists used an **electron** microscope to observe the nanoscale structures that are invisible to conventional optical instruments.
   科学家使用电子显微镜来观察传统光学仪器无法看到的纳米级结构。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "electron" as "electric on" — the electron is the tiny particle that switches electricity on by carrying charge through materials.
把"electron"想成"electric on（电力开启）"——电子是通过在材料中携带电荷来开启电力的微小粒子。
