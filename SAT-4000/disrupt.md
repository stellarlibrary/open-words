# 💥 disrupt

🔊 /dɪsˈrʌpt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To interrupt the normal course of something; to cause disorder or turmoil; to break apart
**中文：** v. 破坏；使瓦解；使中断；使分裂

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- interrupt（打断）
- disturb（扰乱）
- upset（打乱）
- destabilize（使不稳定）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **disrupt a process / disrupt the market / disrupt communication / disrupt the flow**

👉 Example: The sudden power outage disrupted all communication systems across the entire campus.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The new technology has the potential to **disrupt** traditional industries and fundamentally reshape global commerce.
   这项新技术有可能颠覆传统行业，并从根本上重塑全球商业格局。

2. Protesters attempted to **disrupt** the legislative session by chanting slogans and blocking the entrances to the chamber.
   抗议者试图通过高喊口号和封堵议事厅入口来扰乱立法会议。

3. Severe weather conditions continued to **disrupt** international flight schedules, stranding thousands of travelers at major airports.
   恶劣的天气状况持续中断国际航班时刻表，导致数千名旅客滞留在各大机场。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-rupt" as "dis" (apart) + "rupt" (break) — to break things apart and cause chaos.
把"disrupt"想成"dis"（分开）+"rupt"（打破）——把事物打散，造成混乱。
