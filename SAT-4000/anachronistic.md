# ⏳ anachronistic

🔊 /əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪk/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Belonging to a period other than the one being portrayed; outdated or old-fashioned in a way that seems misplaced in the current time
**中文：** 时代错误的；过时的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- outdated（过时的）
- antiquated（陈旧的）
- archaic（古老的）
- obsolete（废弃的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **anachronistic views / anachronistic practices / seem anachronistic / deeply anachronistic / anachronistic elements**

👉 Example: His anachronistic views on gender roles made him increasingly isolated among his younger colleagues.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The inclusion of a digital clock in the Renaissance painting was clearly **anachronistic** and undermined the artwork's historical credibility.
   文艺复兴画作中出现的数字时钟明显是时代错误的，削弱了作品的历史可信度。

2. Critics argued that the institution's rigid hierarchical structure was **anachronistic** in a society that increasingly valued collaboration and equality.
   批评者认为，该机构僵化的等级结构在一个日益重视合作与平等的社会中已经过时。

3. The diplomat's **anachronistic** reliance on formal written correspondence delayed negotiations that required swift and direct communication.
   这位外交官过时地依赖正式书面信函，延误了需要快速直接沟通的谈判。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Remember "anachronistic" as "ana (against) + chronic (time) + -istic" — having characteristics that are against the current time period.
记住"anachronistic"为"ana（相反）+ chronic（时间）+ -istic（具有...特征的）"——具有与当前时代相悖特征的。
