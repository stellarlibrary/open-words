# 🎨 realism

🔊 /ˈriːəlɪzəm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The attitude or practice of accepting a situation as it is and dealing with it accordingly; an artistic or literary movement representing things as they actually are
**中文：** n. 现实主义；实在论；务实态度

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pragmatism（务实主义）
- naturalism（自然主义）
- practicality（实际性）
- verisimilitude（逼真）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **political realism / literary realism / sense of realism / social realism / a dose of realism**

👉 Example: The novel was praised for its unflinching realism in depicting the harsh conditions of urban poverty.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The director's commitment to cinematic **realism** required filming on location rather than relying on artificial studio sets and digital effects.
   这位导演对电影写实主义的执着要求实地拍摄，而非依赖人造摄影棚布景和数字特效。

2. Her foreign policy approach was grounded in **realism**, prioritizing national interests and strategic calculations over ideological considerations.
   她的外交政策立足于现实主义，将国家利益和战略考量置于意识形态之上。

3. The art critic argued that nineteenth-century **realism** represented a deliberate rejection of the romanticized ideals that dominated earlier movements.
   这位艺术评论家认为，十九世纪的现实主义代表了对早期运动中盛行的浪漫化理想的刻意拒绝。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "realism" as "real + ism" — it is the philosophy or movement dedicated to portraying things as they really are, without embellishment.
把"realism"拆成"real（真实的）+ ism（主义）"——这是一种致力于如实描绘事物、不加修饰的哲学或运动。
