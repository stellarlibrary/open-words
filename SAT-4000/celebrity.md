# 🌟 celebrity

🔊 /sɪˈlebrɪti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A famous or well-known person, especially in entertainment or public life; the state of being famous or celebrated
**中文：** n. 名人，名流；名声，声望

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fame（名声）
- renown（声望）
- luminary（杰出人物）
- notable（名人）
- star（明星）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **celebrity status / celebrity culture / minor celebrity / achieve celebrity**

👉 Example: She achieved celebrity almost overnight after her debut novel became a bestseller.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The rise of social media has fundamentally transformed how individuals attain **celebrity** and maintain public visibility in modern society.
   社交媒体的兴起从根本上改变了个人在现代社会中获得名声和维持公众关注度的方式。

2. Several prominent **celebrities** lent their voices to the humanitarian campaign, significantly amplifying awareness of the global crisis.
   几位知名名人为人道主义运动发声，极大地提高了公众对全球危机的关注度。

3. The scholar argued that contemporary **celebrity** culture often prioritizes superficial appeal over genuine intellectual or artistic achievement.
   这位学者认为，当代名人文化往往把肤浅的吸引力置于真正的学术或艺术成就之上。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "celebrity" as "celeb-rity" — a "celeb" is someone with such high social "rity" (priority) that everyone knows their name.
把"celebrity"想成"celeb-rity"——"celeb"是一个社会"rity（优先级）"如此之高以至于人人都知道其名字的人。
