# 🕳️ nadir

🔊 /ˈneɪdɪr/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The lowest point; the point of greatest adversity or despair; (astronomy) the point on the celestial sphere directly below an observer
**中文：** n. 最低点，最底点；天底；深渊

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bottom（底部）
- lowest point（最低点）
- rock bottom（最低谷）
- depths（深渊）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **reach the nadir / at the nadir of / the nadir of one's career**

👉 Example: The economy reached its nadir during the prolonged recession before finally showing signs of recovery.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomatic relationship between the two nations reached its **nadir** following the controversial territorial dispute in the region.
   两国外交关系在该地区有争议的领土争端后跌至**最低点**。

2. Historians consider the widespread famine of that decade to represent the **nadir** of the country's modern economic development.
   历史学家认为那十年间的大范围饥荒代表了该国现代经济发展的**最低谷**。

3. After reaching the **nadir** of her academic career, the researcher secured a groundbreaking grant that revitalized her work.
   在学术生涯跌至**谷底**后，这位研究员获得了一项突破性资助，重振了她的工作。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "nadir" as "nay + dear" — you say "nay, dear" when things are at their absolute worst, the lowest possible point.
把"nadir"想成"nay + dear"——当事情处于最糟糕的时候，你说"不，亲爱的"，表示到了最低点。
