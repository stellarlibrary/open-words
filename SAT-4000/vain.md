# 🪞 vain

🔊 /veɪn/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having an excessively high opinion of one's appearance or abilities; producing no result; useless
**中文：** adj. 徒劳的；自负的；虚荣的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- conceited（自负的）
- futile（徒劳的）
- narcissistic（自恋的）
- fruitless（无成果的）
- egotistical（自大的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in vain / vain attempt / vain effort / vain hope / vain person**

👉 Example: All efforts to negotiate a peaceful resolution proved to be in vain.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The rescue team made a **vain** attempt to reach the trapped miners before the second tunnel collapse occurred.
   救援队在第二次隧道坍塌发生之前做了一次徒劳的尝试去营救被困矿工。

2. Critics described the celebrity as profoundly **vain**, noting her obsession with public image and constant need for admiration.
   评论家形容这位名人极其虚荣，指出她对公众形象的执念和对赞美的持续渴望。

3. The diplomat's **vain** efforts to broker a ceasefire highlighted the deep mistrust between the two warring factions.
   这位外交官徒劳的斡旋停火努力凸显了两个交战派系之间深深的不信任。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vain" as someone always checking their "vein" in the mirror — so focused on their own appearance that all else is futile.
把"vain"想成一个人总是对着镜子看自己的"vein"（血管/外表）——如此专注于自己的外貌，其他一切都变得徒劳。
