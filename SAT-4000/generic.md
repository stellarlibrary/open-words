# 🏷️ generic

🔊 /dʒəˈnɛrɪk/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Relating to or characteristic of a whole group or class; not specific; lacking individuality or distinctiveness
**中文：** adj. 类的；一般的；属的；普通的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- general（一般的）
- common（普通的）
- universal（通用的）
- nonspecific（非特定的）
- unbranded（无品牌的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **generic term / generic brand / generic drug / generic name**

👉 Example: Many patients choose generic drugs because they are significantly cheaper than brand-name alternatives.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor warned students against using **generic** statements that lacked specific evidence to support their arguments.
   教授警告学生不要使用缺乏具体证据支持论点的笼统表述。

2. Hospitals increasingly prescribe **generic** medications to reduce healthcare costs without compromising patient treatment outcomes.
   医院越来越多地开具仿制药，以在不影响患者治疗效果的情况下降低医疗成本。

3. The report was criticized for its **generic** recommendations that failed to address the unique challenges of each community.
   该报告因其未能解决各社区独特挑战的笼统建议而受到批评。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "generic" as sharing the root "gen-" (meaning "kind" or "class") — something generic belongs to the general class, not standing out as special.
把"generic"和词根"gen-"（意为"种类"或"类别"）联系起来——generic的事物属于一般类别，没有什么特别之处。
