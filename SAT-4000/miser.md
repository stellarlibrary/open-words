# 💰 miser

🔊 /ˈmaɪzər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A person who hoards wealth and spends as little money as possible; a stingy or greedy individual
**中文：** n. 守财奴；吝啬鬼

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- skinflint（吝啬鬼）
- tightwad（小气鬼）
- penny-pincher（一毛不拔的人）
- cheapskate（小气鬼）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **old miser / notorious miser / live like a miser / a miser with money**

👉 Example: Despite his enormous fortune, the old miser refused to spend a single penny on charity.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The wealthy **miser** lived in a dilapidated house, refusing to pay for even the most basic repairs.
   那个富有的守财奴住在一栋破旧的房子里，连最基本的维修费都不愿支付。

2. Critics described the corporation's founder as a **miser** who exploited workers while amassing unprecedented personal wealth.
   批评人士将该公司创始人描述为一个剥削工人同时积累空前个人财富的吝啬鬼。

3. In the classic novel, the **miser** ultimately discovers that hoarding gold cannot substitute for genuine human connection.
   在这部经典小说中，守财奴最终发现囤积黄金无法替代真正的人际关系。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "miser" as related to "misery" — a miser makes themselves and others miserable by refusing to share or spend.
把"miser"和"misery（痛苦）"联系起来——守财奴因拒绝分享或花钱而让自己和他人都感到痛苦。
