# ✨ extraordinary

🔊 /ɪkˈstrɔːrdəneri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Very unusual or remarkable; beyond what is ordinary or expected; exceptionally great
**中文：** adj. 非凡的；特别的；离奇的；格外的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- remarkable（非凡的）
- exceptional（特殊的）
- phenomenal（惊人的）
- outstanding（杰出的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **extraordinary ability / extraordinary circumstances / extraordinary measures / extraordinary achievement**

👉 Example: The committee convened an extraordinary session to address the unprecedented crisis.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The young pianist demonstrated **extraordinary** talent during her debut performance, captivating the entire audience with her virtuosity.
   这位年轻的钢琴家在首演中展现了非凡的才华，以其精湛技艺征服了全场观众。

2. Under **extraordinary** circumstances, the board of directors may authorize emergency funding without following standard approval procedures.
   在特殊情况下，董事会可以授权紧急拨款而无需遵循标准审批程序。

3. The archaeological discovery was deemed **extraordinary** because it fundamentally challenged established theories about ancient human migration patterns.
   这一考古发现被认为是非凡的，因为它从根本上挑战了关于古代人类迁徙模式的既定理论。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "extraordinary" as "extra + ordinary" — something so far beyond the ordinary that it demands special attention.
把"extraordinary"想成"extra + ordinary"——远超寻常的事物，值得特别关注。
