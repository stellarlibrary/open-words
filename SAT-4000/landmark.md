# 🗽 landmark

🔊 /ˈlændmɑːrk/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A recognizable natural or man-made feature used for navigation; an important event or achievement marking a turning point. (adj.) Of great significance or importance
**中文：** n. 明显的陆标，地标；划时代的事件 adj. 有重大意义的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- milestone（里程碑）
- monument（纪念碑）
- turning point（转折点）
- beacon（标志物）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **landmark decision / landmark case / historical landmark / landmark achievement**

👉 Example: The Supreme Court issued a landmark ruling that fundamentally reshaped civil rights legislation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **landmark** agreement between the two nations established a framework for peaceful cooperation and mutual economic development.
   两国之间的这项里程碑式协议建立了和平合作和共同经济发展的框架。

2. Tourists gathered around the famous **landmark** to photograph the centuries-old cathedral that dominated the city skyline.
   游客们聚集在这座著名的地标周围，拍摄主导城市天际线的百年大教堂。

3. The discovery of penicillin is widely regarded as a **landmark** event in the history of modern medicine.
   青霉素的发现被广泛认为是现代医学史上一个划时代的事件。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "land" + "mark" — a mark on the land that stands out, whether a physical monument or a significant historical moment.
把"landmark"想成"land（土地）"和"mark（标记）"——土地上醒目的标记，无论是实体建筑还是重大历史时刻。
