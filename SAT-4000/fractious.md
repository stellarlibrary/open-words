# 😤 fractious

🔊 /ˈfrækʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Easily irritated or annoyed; stubbornly unruly and difficult to control or manage
**中文：** adj. 易怒的；乖张的；倔强的；难以对待的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- irritable（易怒的）
- quarrelsome（好争吵的）
- peevish（暴躁的）
- unruly（难管教的）
- contentious（好争论的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fractious debate / fractious child / fractious relationship / fractious coalition / fractious crowd**

👉 Example: The fractious debate in parliament delayed the passage of the reform bill for several months.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **fractious** committee members could not reach consensus on the budget proposal despite weeks of intensive deliberation and negotiation.
   尽管经过数周的深入讨论和谈判，这些易怒的委员会成员仍无法就预算提案达成共识。

2. Managing a **fractious** coalition of political parties with divergent ideologies proved to be the prime minister's greatest governing challenge.
   管理一个由意识形态各异的政党组成的难以驾驭的联盟被证明是总理面临的最大执政挑战。

3. The teacher developed innovative strategies to engage the **fractious** students who had long been resistant to traditional classroom instruction.
   这位教师开发了创新策略来吸引那些长期抗拒传统课堂教学的桀骜不驯的学生。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fractious" as "fracture + ious" — a fractious person is like a fracture in a group, always breaking harmony and causing conflict.
把"fractious"想成"fracture"（断裂）+"ious"——一个fractious的人就像团体中的裂缝，总是打破和谐、制造冲突。
