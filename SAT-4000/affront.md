# 😤 affront

🔊 /əˈfrʌnt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词，名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To insult openly or deliberately; to offend someone's dignity. (n.) A deliberate insult or act of disrespect.
**中文：** (v.) 冒犯，公开侮辱 (n.) 轻蔑；公开侮辱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- insult（侮辱）
- offense（冒犯）
- slight（轻视）
- indignity（侮辱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **personal affront, an affront to dignity, feel affronted, deliberate affront**
👉 Example: His dismissive remarks were a direct affront to the entire delegation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ambassador considered the unauthorized publication of private correspondence a grave **affront** to diplomatic protocol.
   大使认为未经授权发布私人信函是对外交礼仪的严重冒犯。

2. She was deeply **affronted** by the chairman's condescending tone during the formal board meeting yesterday.
   她对主席在昨天正式董事会上居高临下的语气深感受辱。

3. The controversial policy was widely regarded as an **affront** to the fundamental principles of academic freedom.
   这项有争议的政策被广泛视为对学术自由基本原则的公然侮辱。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Affront" contains "front" — imagine someone insulting you right to your front (face), a bold and open act of disrespect.
"Affront"里有"front"（前面）——想象某人当着你的面（正面）侮辱你，这是一种大胆公开的无礼行为。
