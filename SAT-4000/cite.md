# 📜 cite

🔊 /saɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To quote or refer to a source as evidence or authority; to summon to appear in court; to commend for outstanding service.
**中文：** 引用，引证；传讯；表彰；想起。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- quote（引用）
- reference（引述）
- mention（提及）
- invoke（援引）
- summon（传唤）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cite a source / cite evidence / cite an example / cite a case / cited for bravery**

👉 Example: Researchers must properly cite all sources to maintain academic integrity in their publications.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The attorney chose to **cite** several landmark Supreme Court decisions to strengthen her argument before the appellate panel.
   律师选择引用几项具有里程碑意义的最高法院判决，以加强她在上诉小组面前的论点。

2. The professor encouraged students to **cite** primary sources rather than relying on secondary or tertiary references in dissertations.
   教授鼓励学生在论文中引用第一手资料，而不是依赖二手或三手参考文献。

3. The commanding officer decided to **cite** the young soldier for exceptional valor demonstrated during the perilous rescue operation.
   指挥官决定表彰这位年轻士兵在危险的救援行动中表现出的非凡英勇。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cite" sounding like "sight" — when you cite something, you bring it into sight for others to see as evidence.
"cite"听起来像"sight（视线）"——当你引用某物时，你把它带入他人的视线中作为证据。
