# 🧪 distill

🔊 /dɪˈstɪl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To purify a liquid by heating and cooling; to extract the essential meaning or most important aspects of something
**中文：** v. 蒸馏；提炼；提取精华

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- extract（提取）
- purify（净化）
- refine（精炼）
- concentrate（浓缩）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **distill water / distill the essence / distill knowledge / distill from experience**

👉 Example: The author managed to distill decades of research into a single, highly accessible volume.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The laboratory technician was able to **distill** the contaminated water sample until it achieved the required standard of purity.
   实验室技术人员能够对受污染的水样进行蒸馏，直到达到所要求的纯度标准。

2. The editor's task was to **distill** the lengthy manuscript into a concise summary without sacrificing any of the essential arguments.
   编辑的任务是将冗长的手稿提炼成简洁的摘要，同时不牺牲任何核心论点。

3. Over generations, the community managed to **distill** its ancestral wisdom into a collection of proverbs and oral traditions.
   经过几代人，这个社区成功地将祖先的智慧提炼成一系列谚语和口头传统。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-till" as "drip till pure" — liquid drips through the process until only the pure essence remains.
把"distill"想成"drip till pure（滴到纯净为止）"——液体经过蒸馏过程不断滴落，直到只剩下纯净精华。
