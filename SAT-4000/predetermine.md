# 🎯 predetermine

🔊 /ˌpriːdɪˈtɜːrmɪn/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To establish or decide in advance; to determine beforehand the outcome or conditions of something
**中文：** v. 预先确定；预先决定；预先查明

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- preestablish（预先建立）
- preset（预设）
- foreordain（预先注定）
- prearrange（预先安排）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **predetermined outcome / predetermined criteria / predetermined schedule / predetermined factors**

👉 Example: The committee used predetermined criteria to evaluate each candidate's qualifications fairly.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researchers argued that genetic factors alone do not **predetermine** an individual's intelligence or academic achievement.
   研究人员认为，仅凭遗传因素并不能预先决定一个人的智力或学业成就。

2. The experiment followed a **predetermined** protocol to ensure consistency and reliability across all trial conditions.
   该实验遵循预先确定的方案，以确保所有试验条件的一致性和可靠性。

3. Critics accused the committee of having **predetermined** the winner before the formal selection process even began.
   批评者指责委员会在正式遴选程序开始之前就预先确定了获胜者。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-determine" as "determine before" — like setting the answers to a test before the students even sit down.
把"predetermine"想成"pre（提前）+ determine（决定）"——就像在学生坐下之前就设定好了考试答案。
