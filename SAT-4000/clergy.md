# ⛪ clergy

🔊 /ˈklɜːrdʒi/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The body of people ordained for religious duties, especially in the Christian Church; ministers, priests, and rabbis collectively
**中文：** n. 神职人员；牧师；教士（总称）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- priesthood（神职）
- ministry（牧师团）
- clerics（教士）
- ecclesiastics（神职人员）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **members of the clergy / the clergy and laity / ordained clergy / local clergy**

👉 Example: The clergy gathered at the cathedral for the annual interfaith conference on social justice.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **clergy** played a pivotal role in mediating the conflict between the feuding communities in the region.
   神职人员在调解该地区敌对社区之间的冲突中发挥了关键作用。

2. Many members of the **clergy** openly advocated for educational reform throughout the nineteenth century.
   许多神职人员在整个十九世纪公开倡导教育改革。

3. The local **clergy** organized a charitable initiative to provide food and shelter for displaced families.
   当地牧师组织了一项慈善活动，为流离失所的家庭提供食物和住所。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "clergy" as related to "clerk" — in medieval times, clerks were often churchmen who could read and write, serving as the educated clergy.
把"clergy"和"clerk（书记员）"联系起来——在中世纪，书记员通常是能读会写的教会人士，即神职人员。
