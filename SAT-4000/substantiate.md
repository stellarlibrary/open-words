# 🔍 substantiate

🔊 /səbˈstænʃieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To provide evidence to support or prove the truth of something; to give substance or form to
**中文：** v. 使实体化；证实

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- verify（验证）
- confirm（确认）
- corroborate（佐证）
- validate（证实）
- authenticate（鉴定）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **substantiate a claim / substantiate allegations / substantiate with evidence / substantiate the theory**

👉 Example: The journalist was unable to substantiate the allegations despite months of intensive investigation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prosecutor asked the witness to **substantiate** her testimony with documented records and physical evidence from the scene.
   检察官要求证人用书面记录和现场物证来证实她的证词。

2. Further experiments are needed to **substantiate** the preliminary findings before the results can be published in a peer-reviewed journal.
   在结果发表于同行评审期刊之前，需要进一步的实验来证实初步发现。

3. The historian uncovered archival documents that helped **substantiate** the long-disputed claim about the origin of the settlement.
   历史学家发现了档案文件，帮助证实了关于该定居点起源的长期争议说法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "substantiate" as "making something substantial" — you give substance to a claim by backing it up with solid proof.
把"substantiate"想成"使某事变得有实质"——你通过提供确凿的证据来为一项主张赋予实质内容。
