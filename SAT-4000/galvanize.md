# ⚡ galvanize

🔊 /ˈɡælvənaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To shock or excite someone into taking action; to coat iron or steel with a protective layer of zinc; to stimulate by electric current
**中文：** v. 刺激；激励；镀锌；电镀；电击

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- stimulate（刺激）
- energize（激发）
- electrify（使兴奋）
- spur（激励）
- motivate（激发动力）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **galvanize into action / galvanize public opinion / galvanize support / galvanize a movement**

👉 Example: The leader's passionate speech galvanized the crowd into action against the injustice.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The documentary **galvanized** public opinion and prompted legislators to introduce stricter environmental protection regulations nationwide.
   这部纪录片激发了公众舆论，促使立法者在全国范围内推出更严格的环境保护法规。

2. Community organizers worked tirelessly to **galvanize** local residents into demanding better infrastructure for their aging neighborhoods.
   社区组织者不懈努力，激励当地居民要求为老旧社区改善基础设施。

3. The economic crisis **galvanized** the government to implement sweeping fiscal reforms that fundamentally reshaped the national budget.
   经济危机促使政府实施全面的财政改革，从根本上重塑了国家预算。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of Luigi Galvani, the scientist who discovered that electricity could make frog legs twitch — to "galvanize" is to jolt something into sudden action.
想想路易吉·伽伐尼，这位发现电流能让青蛙腿抽动的科学家——"galvanize"就是用震动促使某事突然行动起来。
