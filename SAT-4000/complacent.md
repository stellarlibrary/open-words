# 😏 complacent

🔊 /kəmˈpleɪsənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Showing smug or uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements, often unaware of potential dangers
**中文：** adj. 自满的；得意的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- self-satisfied（自满的）
- smug（沾沾自喜的）
- unconcerned（不关心的）
- contented（满足的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **become complacent / too complacent / complacent about / complacent attitude**

👉 Example: We must not become complacent about the progress we have made so far.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The once-dominant corporation grew **complacent** and failed to adapt to rapidly changing consumer preferences in the market.
   这家曾经占主导地位的公司变得自满，未能适应市场中迅速变化的消费者偏好。

2. Voters should never become **complacent** about democratic institutions, as freedoms can erode gradually without vigilant oversight.
   选民不应对民主制度感到自满，因为自由可能在缺乏警惕监督的情况下逐渐被侵蚀。

3. The surgeon warned against being **complacent** during routine procedures, as unexpected complications could arise at any moment.
   外科医生告诫不要在常规手术中自满，因为意外情况随时可能出现。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "complacent" as "com-PLEASANT" — everything feels so pleasant that you stop worrying and become dangerously satisfied.
把"complacent"想成"com-PLEASANT"——一切都太愉快了，以至于你不再担忧，变得危险地自满。
