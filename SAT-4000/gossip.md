# 🗣️ gossip

🔊 /ˈɡɑːsɪp/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Casual or idle talk about other people's private affairs; rumor. (v.) To engage in spreading rumors or talking about others' personal matters
**中文：** n. 闲话，流言，小道传闻 v. 传播流言蜚语；说长道短

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rumor（谣言）
- hearsay（传闻）
- tittle-tattle（闲聊）
- chatter（闲扯）
- scandal（丑闻）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **gossip about / spread gossip / idle gossip / gossip column**

👉 Example: The tabloid columnist was known for publishing unverified gossip about prominent public figures.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor warned students that spreading **gossip** about classmates could violate the university's code of academic conduct.
   教授警告学生，传播关于同学的流言蜚语可能违反大学的学术行为准则。

2. Historians caution that much of what we know about ancient rulers is based on **gossip** recorded by biased court chroniclers.
   历史学家提醒我们，关于古代统治者的许多认知都基于有偏见的宫廷编年史家记录的传闻。

3. Colleagues who **gossip** excessively in the workplace often undermine team cohesion and diminish overall organizational productivity.
   在工作场所过度说长道短的同事往往会破坏团队凝聚力并降低整体组织生产力。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gossip" as "go sip" — people who gossip are like those who go sipping tea while chatting about everyone else's business.
把"gossip"想成"go sip"（去喝一口）——爱说闲话的人就像一边喝茶一边聊别人家事的人。
