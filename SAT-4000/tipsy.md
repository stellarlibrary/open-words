# 🍷 tipsy

🔊 /ˈtɪpsi/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Slightly intoxicated from alcohol; unsteady or tilted, as if about to fall over
**中文：** adj. 微醉的；喝醉的；不稳的；歪斜的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- inebriated（醉酒的）
- intoxicated（陶醉的）
- unsteady（不稳的）
- woozy（头晕的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **feel tipsy / get tipsy / a little tipsy / slightly tipsy / tipsy after a drink**

👉 Example: After just two glasses of champagne, she felt pleasantly tipsy at the reception.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Several guests became noticeably **tipsy** after the reception, laughing loudly and swaying on the dance floor.
   几位客人在招待会后明显有些醉意，在舞池中大声笑着，身体摇摇晃晃。

2. The professor warned students that even a **tipsy** state can significantly impair judgment and decision-making abilities.
   教授警告学生，即使是微醉状态也会严重损害判断力和决策能力。

3. Historical accounts describe the diplomat as becoming **tipsy** during negotiations, which led to several costly concessions.
   历史记载描述这位外交官在谈判期间变得微醉，导致了几项代价高昂的让步。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tipsy" as "tip + sy" — when you are tipsy, you tip over to the side because you cannot keep your balance.
把"tipsy"想成"tip（倾倒）+ sy"——当你微醉时，你会因无法保持平衡而向一边倾倒。
