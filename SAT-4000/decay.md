# 🍂 decay

🔊 /dɪˈkeɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To decline in quality, power, or vitality; to rot or decompose. (n.) The process of gradual deterioration or decomposition
**中文：** v. 衰退；衰减；（使）腐烂 n. 衰退；腐烂

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- decompose（分解）
- deteriorate（恶化）
- decline（衰退）
- rot（腐烂）
- degenerate（退化）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **decay over time / tooth decay / radioactive decay / urban decay / fall into decay**

👉 Example: Without proper maintenance, the historic building began to decay and posed a serious safety hazard.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The abandoned factory had been left to **decay** for decades, its crumbling walls now overtaken by vegetation and wildlife.
   这座废弃的工厂已经被遗弃了几十年，摇摇欲坠的墙壁如今被植被和野生动物占据。

2. Physicists measure the rate of radioactive **decay** to determine the approximate age of ancient fossils and geological specimens.
   物理学家通过测量放射性衰变的速率来确定古代化石和地质标本的大致年代。

3. The once-prosperous neighborhood fell into visible **decay** after major industries closed and residents began moving to suburban areas.
   在主要工业关闭、居民开始搬往郊区后，这个曾经繁荣的街区明显衰落了。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "de-cay" as things going "de-" (down) like a "cave-in" — everything collapses and falls apart over time.
把"decay"想成事物往下"de-"走——一切随着时间崩塌瓦解，就像东西慢慢腐烂一样。
