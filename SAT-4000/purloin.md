# 🕵️ purloin

🔊 /pɜːrˈlɔɪn/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To steal something, especially in a sneaky or underhanded manner; to take dishonestly
**中文：** v. 偷窃；盗取

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- steal（偷窃）
- pilfer（小偷小摸）
- filch（顺手牵羊）
- swipe（偷走）
- appropriate（挪用）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **purloin documents / purloin secrets / purloin funds / purloin from**

👉 Example: The spy managed to purloin classified documents from the embassy without raising any suspicion.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The employee was terminated after investigators discovered she had **purloined** confidential files from the corporate database.
   调查人员发现她从公司数据库中窃取了机密文件后，该员工被解雇。

2. Edgar Allan Poe's famous story revolves around a minister who **purloins** a compromising letter from the queen.
   埃德加·爱伦·坡的著名故事围绕一位大臣从女王那里窃取一封有损名誉的信件展开。

3. The auditor uncovered evidence that the treasurer had been **purloining** funds from the charity for personal use.
   审计员发现了财务主管一直在挪用慈善机构资金供个人使用的证据。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pur-loin" as "purr + loin" — imagine a cat purring quietly while sneaking away with a loin of meat, stealing it silently.
把"purloin"想成"purr（猫叫）+ loin（腰肉）"——想象一只猫安静地发出呼噜声，悄悄叼走一块腰肉，无声地偷走它。
