# 🌊 tumult

🔊 /ˈtjuːmʌlt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A loud, confused noise, especially one caused by a large mass of people; a state of confusion, disorder, or agitation
**中文：** 骚动；骚乱；激动

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- uproar（骚动）
- commotion（混乱）
- turmoil（动荡）
- pandemonium（大混乱）
- agitation（激动）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in tumult / tumult of emotions / political tumult / tumult and confusion**

👉 Example: The announcement of the verdict caused a tumult in the crowded courtroom.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden resignation of the prime minister plunged the entire nation into political **tumult** and widespread uncertainty.
   首相的突然辞职使整个国家陷入了政治骚乱和普遍的不确定性之中。

2. A great **tumult** arose in the assembly hall when the controversial amendment was introduced without prior consultation.
   当有争议的修正案未经事先协商就被提出时，会议大厅里爆发了巨大的骚动。

3. Despite the emotional **tumult** she experienced after the tragedy, the leader maintained her composure and addressed the public.
   尽管她在悲剧发生后经历了情感上的激动，这位领导人仍保持镇定并向公众发表了讲话。
