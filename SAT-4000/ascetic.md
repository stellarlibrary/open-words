# 🧘 ascetic

🔊 /əˈsɛtɪk/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** (adj.) Characterized by severe self-discipline and abstention from all forms of indulgence, typically for religious reasons. (n.) A person who practices severe self-discipline and abstinence.

**中文：** (adj.) 苦行的 (n.) 苦行者；禁欲者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- austere 苦行的
- abstinent 禁欲的
- spartan 简朴的
- self-denying 自我克制的
- monk 修道者

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Ascetic" describes both the lifestyle of extreme simplicity and the person who follows it. It is often associated with religious or spiritual practices but can describe any severely disciplined way of living.

"ascetic" 既可描述极度简朴的生活方式，也可指遵循这种生活方式的人。它常与宗教或精神修行相关，但也可描述任何极其自律的生活方式。

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **ascetic** monk renounced all material possessions and devoted his life entirely to spiritual contemplation.
   这位苦行僧放弃了一切物质财富，将一生完全献给了精神冥想。

2. She adopted an **ascetic** lifestyle, eating only simple meals and sleeping on a bare wooden floor.
   她过着苦行般的生活，只吃简单的饭菜，睡在光秃秃的木地板上。

3. The philosopher argued that **ascetic** discipline strengthens the mind and liberates one from worldly desires.
   这位哲学家认为苦行的自律能强化心灵，使人从世俗欲望中解脱出来。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Ascetic" sounds like "a-skeptic" of pleasure — someone who is skeptical of worldly pleasures and chooses to live without them.

"Ascetic" 听起来像 "a-skeptic"（对快乐持怀疑态度的人）——一个对世俗享乐持怀疑态度并选择放弃它们的人。
