# 🕊️ auspice

🔊 /ˈɔːspɪs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Patronage, sponsorship, or support (usually plural: auspices); a sign or omen, especially a favorable one derived from observing birds in ancient Rome.
**中文：** 赞助，主办；吉兆

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- patronage（赞助）
- sponsorship（主办）
- omen（预兆）
- aegis（庇护）
- portent（征兆）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **under the auspices of**, **favorable auspices**, **auspices of the government/organization**

👉 Example: The cultural exchange program was organized under the auspices of the United Nations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The international conference was held under the **auspices** of the World Health Organization in Geneva.
   这次国际会议在世界卫生组织的主办下于日内瓦举行。

2. Ancient Roman priests interpreted the flight patterns of birds as **auspices** before major military campaigns.
   古罗马祭司在重大军事行动前将鸟的飞行模式解读为吉凶之兆。

3. The research initiative proceeded under the **auspices** of a prestigious university with generous philanthropic funding.
   该研究项目在一所著名大学的赞助下进行，获得了慷慨的慈善资助。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
"Auspice" comes from Latin "auspex" — "avis" (bird) + "specere" (to look at). Romans looked at birds for omens. Today, "under the auspices of" means under someone's protective guidance.
"Auspice"来自拉丁语"auspex"——"avis"（鸟）+"specere"（观察）。罗马人观鸟以占卜吉凶。如今，"under the auspices of"意为在某人的保护和指导下。
