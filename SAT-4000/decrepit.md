# 🏚️ decrepit

🔊 /dɪˈkrepɪt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Worn out or weakened by old age, illness, or long use; in a state of decay or disrepair
**中文：** adj. 衰老的；破旧的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dilapidated（破旧的）
- feeble（衰弱的）
- worn-out（损坏的）
- ramshackle（摇摇欲坠的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **decrepit building / decrepit condition / decrepit old man / decrepit infrastructure**

👉 Example: The decrepit farmhouse had been abandoned for decades and was barely standing amid the overgrown fields.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The city council voted to demolish the **decrepit** warehouse district and replace it with modern affordable housing units.
   市议会投票决定拆除破旧的仓库区，代之以现代经济适用房。

2. The **decrepit** bridge posed a serious safety hazard and was immediately closed to all vehicular and pedestrian traffic.
   这座破旧的桥梁构成了严重的安全隐患，立即禁止所有车辆和行人通行。

3. Despite his **decrepit** appearance, the elderly scholar continued to deliver compelling lectures at the university with remarkable clarity.
   尽管外表衰老，这位年迈的学者仍继续在大学发表引人入胜的演讲，思路极为清晰。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "decrepit" as "de- + crept" — something that has crept into decay, slowly falling apart over time like an old abandoned building.
把"decrepit"想成"de- + crept（爬行）"——某物慢慢地爬向衰败，随着时间推移逐渐崩塌，就像一栋废弃的旧建筑。
