# 🌿 morass

🔊 /məˈræs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An area of muddy or boggy ground; a complicated or confused situation that is difficult to escape from
**中文：** n. 沼泽；困境；乱糟糟的一堆

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- quagmire（泥潭）
- swamp（沼泽）
- bog（沼泽地）
- muddle（混乱）
- tangle（纠缠）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a morass of paperwork / sink into a morass / a morass of confusion / political morass**

👉 Example: The new regulations created a morass of bureaucratic procedures that frustrated both citizens and officials.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The expedition was forced to halt when the soldiers found themselves trapped in an impenetrable **morass** of mud and reeds.
   当士兵们发现自己被困在泥泞和芦苇丛组成的难以穿越的沼泽中时，远征队被迫停下。

2. Investigators struggled to navigate the **morass** of conflicting testimonies and falsified documents surrounding the financial scandal.
   调查人员在围绕金融丑闻的相互矛盾的证词和伪造文件的泥潭中艰难前行。

3. Without clear leadership, the organization sank deeper into a **morass** of indecision and internal conflict over resource allocation.
   由于缺乏明确的领导，该组织在资源分配问题上陷入了优柔寡断和内部冲突的困境。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "morass" as "more + ass (stuck)" — when you are stuck in a morass, the more you struggle, the more stuck you become.
把"morass"想成"more + ass（被困住）"——当你陷入泥潭时，越挣扎就越陷越深。
