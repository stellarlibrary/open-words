# ✅ legitimate

🔊 /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, verb | 形容词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Conforming to the law or to rules; valid, justifiable, or reasonable. (v.) To make lawful or justify; to give legitimate status to something.
**中文：** adj. 合法的；正当的；合理的 v. 使合法；使正当化

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- lawful（合法的）
- valid（有效的）
- justifiable（合理的）
- authorized（经授权的）
- genuine（真正的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **legitimate concern / legitimate claim / legitimate authority / legitimate business / legitimate reason**

👉 Example: The court ruled that the citizens had a legitimate grievance against the municipality's zoning decision.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The investigation confirmed that the organization was a **legitimate** charity and not a fraudulent scheme designed to exploit donors.
   调查证实该组织是一个合法的慈善机构，而非旨在欺骗捐赠者的欺诈计划。

2. Workers raised **legitimate** concerns about safety protocols after a series of preventable industrial accidents occurred at the plant.
   在工厂发生一系列可预防的工业事故后，工人们提出了关于安全规程的合理关切。

3. The new government sought to **legitimate** its authority by holding free elections and establishing an independent judiciary.
   新政府试图通过举行自由选举和建立独立司法体系来使其权力合法化。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "legitimate" as "legal + intimate" — something so closely tied to the law that it is fully accepted and recognized as proper.
把"legitimate"想成"legal + intimate"——与法律如此紧密相关，以至于被完全接受和认可为正当的。
