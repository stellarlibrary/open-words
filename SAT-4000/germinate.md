# 🌱 germinate

🔊 /ˈdʒɜːrmɪneɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To begin to grow or sprout; to cause a seed to develop; to come into existence and develop
**中文：** v. 使发芽；使生长；使发展

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sprout（发芽）
- bud（萌芽）
- develop（发展）
- originate（起源）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **germinate seeds / germinate an idea / begin to germinate / germinate in the mind**

👉 Example: The seeds will germinate within two weeks if given sufficient moisture and warmth.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The innovative concept began to **germinate** in her mind during the international conference on sustainable development.
   这个创新概念在可持续发展国际会议期间开始在她脑海中萌芽。

2. Scientists discovered that certain ancient seeds could still **germinate** after remaining dormant for thousands of years.
   科学家发现某些古代种子在休眠数千年后仍然能够发芽。

3. Political dissent had begun to **germinate** among the populace long before the revolutionary movement gained visible momentum.
   在革命运动获得明显势头之前，政治异见早已在民众中开始萌生。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "germ-inate" — a "germ" is the seed of something; to germinate is to let that seed grow and come alive.
把"germinate"想成"germ"（胚芽）+"inate"——胚芽就是事物的种子；germinate就是让种子生长并焕发生机。
