# 🌟 radiate

🔊 /ˈreɪdieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb, adjective | 动词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To emit energy, especially light or heat, in all directions; to spread outward from a central point; to display clearly. (adj.) Having rays or arranged in a radiating pattern.
**中文：** v. 放射；辐射；散布；传播；流露 adj. 辐射状的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- emit（散发）
- emanate（发出）
- diffuse（扩散）
- spread（传播）
- beam（照射）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **radiate heat / radiate light / radiate confidence / radiate warmth / radiate outward from**

👉 Example: The newly appointed director seemed to radiate confidence as she addressed the entire assembly.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The volcanic rock continued to **radiate** intense heat for several hours after the eruption had finally subsided.
   火山岩在喷发最终平息后仍持续辐射强烈的热量达数小时。

2. Several major highways **radiate** outward from the capital city, connecting it to distant provinces and commercial centers.
   几条主要高速公路从首都向外辐射，将其与偏远省份和商业中心连接起来。

3. The ambassador's ability to **radiate** composure during the crisis earned her widespread admiration from the international community.
   这位大使在危机期间流露从容镇定的能力为她赢得了国际社会的广泛赞誉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "radiate" as "radi-ate" — from Latin "radius" meaning ray, so to radiate is to send out rays like the sun sending light in every direction.
把"radiate"想成"radi-ate"——来自拉丁语"radius（射线）"，所以radiate就是像太阳向四面八方发出光线一样散发能量。
