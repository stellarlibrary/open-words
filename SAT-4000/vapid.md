# 😶 vapid

🔊 /ˈvæpɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Lacking liveliness, interest, or flavor; dull and uninspiring
**中文：** adj. 无趣味的；索然乏味的；枯燥的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- insipid（乏味的）
- bland（平淡的）
- dull（无聊的）
- tedious（冗长乏味的）
- lackluster（无光彩的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vapid conversation / vapid remarks / vapid entertainment / vapid expression / vapid prose**

👉 Example: The critic dismissed the film as a vapid spectacle devoid of any meaningful narrative depth.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The literary review described the bestselling novel as **vapid** and superficial, lacking the thematic complexity expected of serious modern fiction.
   文学评论将这本畅销小说描述为索然乏味且肤浅，缺乏严肃现代小说应有的主题复杂性。

2. Audiences grew weary of the politician's **vapid** speeches, which offered optimistic slogans but no substantive policy proposals or concrete solutions.
   观众对这位政客乏味的演讲感到厌倦，这些演讲只有乐观的口号，却没有实质性的政策建议或具体方案。

3. The professor warned students against producing **vapid** essays that merely restated common knowledge without offering original analysis or critical insight.
   教授告诫学生不要写出乏味的文章，仅仅复述常识而不提供原创分析或批判性见解。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vapid" as related to "vapor" — like steam that evaporates and leaves nothing behind, vapid things lack substance.
把"vapid"想成与"vapor"（蒸气）相关——就像蒸发后什么都不留下的蒸气，乏味的东西缺乏实质。
