# 😌 venial

🔊 /ˈviːniəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (Of a fault or offense) slight and pardonable; not seriously wrong
**中文：** adj. 可原谅的；（罪过）轻微的；可宽恕的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pardonable（可原谅的）
- forgivable（可宽恕的）
- excusable（可辩解的）
- minor（轻微的）
- trivial（微不足道的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **venial sin / venial offense / venial fault / venial transgression**

👉 Example: The teacher treated the student's tardiness as a venial offense rather than a serious disciplinary matter.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee considered the minor procedural error a **venial** lapse that did not warrant formal disciplinary action.
   委员会认为这一轻微的程序错误是可宽恕的失误，不需要正式的纪律处分。

2. In Catholic theology, a **venial** sin weakens one's relationship with God but does not sever it entirely.
   在天主教神学中，小罪削弱人与上帝的关系，但不会完全断绝。

3. His occasional grammatical mistakes were **venial** flaws in an otherwise brilliant and meticulously researched dissertation.
   他偶尔的语法错误是一篇本来出色且研究细致的论文中可原谅的小瑕疵。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "venial" as "ve-NIAL" sounding like "denial" — it is so minor that you can be in denial about its seriousness.
把"venial"想成发音接近"denial"（否认）——错误如此轻微，你可以否认它的严重性。
