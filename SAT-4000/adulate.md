# 🙇 adulate

🔊 /ˈædʒəleɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To praise or admire excessively and slavishly; to flatter in a servile manner
**中文：** v. 过分称赞；谄媚；奉承

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flatter（奉承）
- fawn（讨好）
- grovel（谄媚）
- toady（拍马屁）
- sycophant（阿谀奉承）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **adulate someone / adulate a leader / adulating fans / publicly adulated**

👉 Example: The young followers adulated the celebrity without questioning any of his controversial remarks.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The courtiers constantly **adulated** the king, hoping to secure favorable positions within the royal administration.
   朝臣们不断地奉承国王，希望在皇家行政机构中谋得有利的职位。

2. Critics warned that societies which **adulate** political leaders without scrutiny risk descending into authoritarian governance.
   评论家警告说，不加审视地谄媚政治领袖的社会有滑向专制统治的危险。

3. The media's tendency to **adulate** wealthy entrepreneurs often obscures the ethical questions surrounding their business practices.
   媒体过分吹捧富有企业家的倾向往往掩盖了其商业行为背后的伦理问题。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "adulate" as "adult-ATE" — an adult who ate too much praise and flattery, becoming bloated with false compliments.
把"adulate"想成"adult-ATE"——一个吃了(ate)太多奉承话的成年人(adult)，被虚假赞美撑得膨胀。
