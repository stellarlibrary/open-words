# 💰 surcharge

🔊 /ˈsɜːrtʃɑːrdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An extra charge or fee added to the usual cost. (v.) To impose an additional charge; to overload or overburden
**中文：** v. 额外收费 n. 额外费用

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- additional fee（附加费）
- extra charge（额外收费）
- levy（征收）
- premium（附加费）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **surcharge on / impose a surcharge / fuel surcharge / credit card surcharge**

👉 Example: The airline added a fuel surcharge to every ticket purchased during the peak travel season.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The hotel imposed a significant **surcharge** on guests who requested late checkout, adding thirty dollars to the final bill.
   酒店对要求延迟退房的客人征收了一笔可观的额外费用，在最终账单上增加了三十美元。

2. Many restaurants have begun to **surcharge** customers who pay by credit card in order to offset rising transaction processing costs.
   许多餐厅开始对使用信用卡付款的顾客收取额外费用，以抵消不断上涨的交易处理成本。

3. The regulatory agency ruled that the **surcharge** applied to imported goods was excessive and constituted an unfair barrier to trade.
   监管机构裁定对进口商品征收的附加费过高，构成了不公平的贸易壁垒。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "surcharge" as "sur (over) + charge" — it is a charge that goes over and above the normal amount you owe.
把"surcharge"想成"sur（超过）+ charge（收费）"——这是超出你正常应付金额之上的额外收费。
