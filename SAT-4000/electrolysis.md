# 🧪 electrolysis

🔊 /ɪˌlekˈtrɒlɪsɪs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A chemical process in which an electric current is passed through a liquid or solution to cause a chemical reaction, typically decomposition of a substance
**中文：** n. 电解；电解作用

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- electrochemical decomposition（电化学分解）
- electrolytic process（电解过程）
- electrochemical reaction（电化学反应）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **electrolysis of water / undergo electrolysis / electrolysis process / perform electrolysis**

👉 Example: The electrolysis of water produces hydrogen and oxygen gases at the two electrodes.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Through **electrolysis**, scientists can decompose water molecules into hydrogen and oxygen gases for use in clean energy applications.
   通过电解，科学家可以将水分子分解为氢气和氧气，用于清洁能源应用。

2. The industrial **electrolysis** of brine solution remains the primary method for producing chlorine and sodium hydroxide worldwide.
   盐水溶液的工业电解仍然是全球生产氯气和氢氧化钠的主要方法。

3. Researchers improved the efficiency of **electrolysis** by using platinum catalysts that significantly reduced the required energy input.
   研究人员通过使用铂催化剂提高了电解效率，显著降低了所需的能量输入。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "electro-lysis" as "electric" + "lysis (breaking)" — electrolysis is the process of breaking apart substances using electricity.
把"electrolysis"想成"electro（电）"加"lysis（分解）"——电解就是用电来分解物质的过程。
