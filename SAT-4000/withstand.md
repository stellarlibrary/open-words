# 🛡️ withstand

🔊 /wɪðˈstænd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To resist or endure successfully; to remain undamaged or unaffected by force, pressure, or hardship
**中文：** v. 抵挡；反抗；经得住；耐

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- endure（忍受）
- resist（抵抗）
- tolerate（忍耐）
- sustain（承受）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **withstand pressure / withstand scrutiny / withstand the test of time / withstand attack / withstand temperatures**

👉 Example: The ancient fortress was designed to withstand prolonged sieges and harsh winter conditions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The newly engineered alloy can **withstand** temperatures exceeding two thousand degrees without any structural deformation.
   这种新研发的合金能够承受超过两千度的温度而不发生任何结构变形。

2. Her carefully constructed argument was strong enough to **withstand** the rigorous scrutiny of the peer review panel.
   她精心构建的论点足够有力，经得住同行评审小组的严格审查。

3. Communities along the coastline built reinforced seawalls designed to **withstand** the devastating force of hurricane-driven storm surges.
   沿海社区建造了加固的海堤，旨在抵御飓风引发的毁灭性风暴潮。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "with-stand" as "standing with strength" — standing firm against whatever force comes your way.
把"withstand"想成"with（面对）+ stand（站立）"——面对压力依然屹立不倒。
