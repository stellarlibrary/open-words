# 🎨 dilettante

🔊 /ˌdɪlɪˈtænti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who cultivates an area of interest without real commitment or knowledge; an amateur who dabbles superficially in a field
**中文：** n. 业余爱好者；一知半解者；浅尝辄止的人

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- amateur（业余爱好者）
- dabbler（涉猎者）
- nonprofessional（非专业人士）
- trifler（浅尝者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mere dilettante / intellectual dilettante / dilettante in art / dilettante approach**

👉 Example: Critics dismissed him as a dilettante who lacked the discipline to master any single artistic medium.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The seasoned musicians regarded the new member as a **dilettante** whose superficial understanding of music theory undermined the ensemble's credibility.
   经验丰富的音乐家们认为这位新成员是个一知半解的业余爱好者，其肤浅的乐理知识损害了乐团的声誉。

2. In academia, a **dilettante** who drifts between disciplines without depth is unlikely to produce meaningful or original scholarly contributions.
   在学术界，一个在各学科之间浅尝辄止的业余爱好者不太可能做出有意义的原创学术贡献。

3. She refused to remain a **dilettante** and committed herself to years of rigorous training to become a professional portrait painter.
   她拒绝继续做一个业余爱好者，投身于多年的严格训练以成为一名专业肖像画家。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dilettante" as related to "delight" — a dilettante takes delight in many things but never commits deeply to any one pursuit.
把"dilettante"和"delight"（喜悦）联系起来——业余爱好者对许多事物感到愉悦，但从不深入投入任何一项。
