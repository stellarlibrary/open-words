# 🚀 trajectory

🔊 /trəˈdʒɛktəri/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The path followed by a projectile or moving object; the course or progression of something over time
**中文：** 轨道；弹道；（事物发展的）轨迹，路线

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- path（路径）
- course（路线）
- arc（弧线）
- route（路线）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **career trajectory / upward trajectory / flight trajectory / alter the trajectory / trajectory of growth**

👉 Example: The economist predicted that current policies would alter the trajectory of the nation's economic recovery.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The physicist calculated the precise **trajectory** of the satellite to ensure it would enter the correct orbital path.
   物理学家精确计算了卫星的**轨道**，以确保它进入正确的轨道路径。

2. Her remarkable career **trajectory** took her from a junior analyst position to chief executive officer within a single decade.
   她非凡的职业**轨迹**使她在十年内从初级分析师升至首席执行官。

3. The committee analyzed the **trajectory** of population growth to develop more effective long-term urban planning strategies.
   委员会分析了人口增长的**轨迹**，以制定更有效的长期城市规划策略。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "trajectory" as "tra-ject-ory" — from "traject" meaning to throw across, like the path a thrown object follows through the air.
把"trajectory"想成"tra（穿越）+ ject（投掷）+ ory"——从"投掷穿越"而来，就像被抛出的物体在空中划过的路径。
