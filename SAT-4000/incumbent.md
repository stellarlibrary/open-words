# 🏛️ incumbent

🔊 /ɪnˈkʌmbənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Currently holding a particular office or position; morally obligatory. (n.) The current holder of an office or position
**中文：** adj. 现任的；职责所在的，负有义务的 n. 在职者；现任者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- officeholder（在职者）
- obligatory（义务的）
- current（现任的）
- binding（有约束力的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **the incumbent president / it is incumbent upon / incumbent duty / defeat the incumbent**

👉 Example: It is incumbent upon every citizen to participate responsibly in the democratic process.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **incumbent** senator faced an unexpectedly strong challenge from a relatively unknown candidate in the primary election.
   这位现任参议员在初选中面临着一位相对不知名的候选人出乎意料的强劲挑战。

2. The board declared that it was **incumbent** upon all executives to disclose potential conflicts of interest before major decisions.
   董事会宣布所有高管有义务在做出重大决定前披露潜在的利益冲突。

3. Despite the advantages of name recognition, the **incumbent** governor lost the election due to widespread voter dissatisfaction.
   尽管享有知名度优势，这位现任州长仍因选民的广泛不满而输掉了选举。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "incumbent" as "in-come-bent" — the person who is bent on staying in their current position, already sitting in the seat of power.
把"incumbent"想成"in-come-bent"——一个执着于留在现任职位上的人，已经坐在权力的位子上。
