# 🌊 washout

🔊 /ˈwɒʃaʊt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The erosion of soil or a road surface by a sudden flow of water; an event or person that is a complete failure
**中文：** n. 冲刷；冲蚀；失败者；失败的事

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- erosion（侵蚀）
- failure（失败）
- fiasco（惨败）
- flop（失败）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a total washout / the event was a washout / road washout / washout from flooding**

👉 Example: The outdoor concert turned out to be a complete washout after torrential rain began in the early afternoon.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Heavy rainfall overnight caused a severe **washout** along the mountain highway, forcing authorities to close several critical routes.
   夜间的暴雨导致山区公路严重冲毁，迫使当局关闭了几条关键路线。

2. The fundraising gala was considered a **washout** after fewer than a quarter of the invited guests actually attended the event.
   在受邀嘉宾中不到四分之一实际出席后，这场筹款晚会被认为是一次失败。

3. Engineers conducted a thorough assessment of the **washout** damage to determine whether the bridge foundations remained structurally sound.
   工程师们对冲刷损害进行了全面评估，以确定桥梁地基是否仍然结构完好。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "wash-out" literally — water washes everything out, leaving destruction; or plans get "washed out" and ruined.
把"washout"直接理解为"冲刷出去"——水把一切冲走，留下破坏；或者计划被"冲走"而失败。
