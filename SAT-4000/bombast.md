# 🎭 bombast

🔊 /ˈbɑːmbæst/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) High-sounding language with little meaning; inflated or pretentious speech or writing. (adj.) Pompous or extravagant in language.
**中文：** n. 夸大的言辞；华而不实的话语 adj. 夸大的；浮夸的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- grandiloquence（夸张的言辞）
- pomposity（浮夸）
- rhetoric（华丽辞藻）
- bluster（大话）
- fustian（空洞的言辞）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **empty bombast / mere bombast / political bombast / full of bombast / bombast and bluster**

👉 Example: The candidate's speech was dismissed as mere bombast, lacking any substantive policy proposals.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics dismissed the politician's address as pure **bombast**, noting that it contained grand promises but absolutely no actionable plans.
   批评家们认为这位政治家的讲话纯粹是夸夸其谈，指出其中包含宏大承诺但完全没有可操作的计划。

2. The literary reviewer observed that the author's latest novel was marred by excessive **bombast** rather than genuine emotional depth.
   文学评论家指出，这位作者的最新小说被过度的浮夸辞藻所损害，而非具有真正的情感深度。

3. Beneath all the **bombast** of the keynote address, attentive listeners could detect very little of real intellectual substance.
   在主旨演讲的所有夸夸其谈之下，细心的听众几乎察觉不到真正有知识内涵的内容。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bombast" as a "bomb blast" of words — an explosion of loud, impressive-sounding language that creates a lot of noise but ultimately causes no real impact or meaning.
把"bombast"想成文字的"bomb blast（炸弹爆炸）"——一连串响亮、听起来令人印象深刻的言辞，制造了很多噪音但最终没有真正的影响或意义。
