# ⛏️ excavate

🔊 /ˈɛkskəˌveɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To dig out material from the ground; to uncover something buried by careful removal of earth or rock
**中文：** v. 挖掘，开凿；挖洞

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dig（挖掘）
- unearth（出土）
- exhume（掘出）
- hollow out（挖空）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **excavate a site / excavate ruins / excavate a tunnel / excavate artifacts**

👉 Example: The archaeological team spent three years excavating the ancient burial site outside the city.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Archaeologists began to **excavate** the ruins of an ancient Roman settlement discovered beneath a modern parking lot in central London.
   考古学家开始挖掘在伦敦市中心一个现代停车场下方发现的古罗马定居点遗址。

2. Construction workers had to carefully **excavate** the hillside before the foundation for the new hospital could be properly laid.
   建筑工人必须仔细开凿山坡，然后才能为新医院正确地打好地基。

3. The team **excavated** a collection of remarkably well-preserved pottery fragments dating back more than three thousand years.
   该团队挖掘出一批保存极为完好的陶器碎片，其年代可追溯到三千多年前。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ex-CAVE-ate" — to excavate is to create a cave by digging out, removing earth to uncover what lies beneath.
把"excavate"想成"ex-CAVE-ate"——挖掘就像挖出一个洞穴，移走泥土来揭示地下隐藏的东西。
