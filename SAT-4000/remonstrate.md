# 🗣️ remonstrate

🔊 /ˈrɛmənˌstreɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make a forcefully reproachful protest; to argue earnestly against an action or decision
**中文：** v. 责备，告诫；抗议；表示异议

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- protest（抗议）
- object（反对）
- expostulate（规劝）
- reproach（责备）
- challenge（质疑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **remonstrate with someone / remonstrate against / remonstrate about / remonstrate that**

👉 Example: The senator remonstrated with the committee about the potential consequences of the proposed legislation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Several delegates **remonstrated** vigorously against the proposed amendments, arguing that they would undermine fundamental constitutional protections.
   几位代表强烈抗议拟议的修正案，认为这些修正案会破坏基本的宪法保护。

2. The physician **remonstrated** with the patient about the serious health risks associated with continuing to disregard medical advice.
   医生就继续无视医嘱所带来的严重健康风险对患者进行了告诫。

3. Community leaders **remonstrated** before the city council, urging officials to reconsider the demolition of the historic landmark building.
   社区领袖在市议会面前提出抗议，敦促官员重新考虑拆除这座历史地标建筑。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "remonstrate" as "re-demonstrate" — you demonstrate your objection again, making your protest heard loud and clear.
把"remonstrate"想成"re-demonstrate"——你再次表明你的反对意见，让你的抗议被清楚地听到。
