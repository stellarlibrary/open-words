# 📚 treatise

🔊 /ˈtriːtɪs/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A formal and systematic written discourse on some subject, generally longer and more detailed than an essay
**中文：** n. 论述；论文；专著

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dissertation（学术论文）
- monograph（专著）
- exposition（阐述）
- thesis（论文）
- tract（小册子）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a treatise on philosophy / write a treatise / publish a treatise / scholarly treatise**

👉 Example: The professor spent a decade composing a comprehensive treatise on constitutional law.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. John Locke's celebrated **treatise** on government profoundly influenced the architects of modern democratic constitutions worldwide.
   约翰·洛克关于政府的著名论著深刻影响了全世界现代民主宪法的设计者。

2. The researcher published an exhaustive **treatise** examining the long-term ecological consequences of industrial pollution on marine ecosystems.
   该研究人员发表了一篇详尽的论著，探讨工业污染对海洋生态系统的长期影响。

3. Her landmark **treatise** on behavioral economics challenged prevailing assumptions and reshaped the entire field of study.
   她关于行为经济学的里程碑式专著挑战了主流假设，并重塑了整个研究领域。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "treatise" as "treat + ise" — a treatise treats a subject thoroughly, giving it the full intellectual treatment.
把"treatise"想成"treat + ise"——一篇论著彻底地"处理"一个主题，给予其全面的学术探讨。
