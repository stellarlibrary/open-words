# 🧙 witchcraft

🔊 /ˈwɪtʃkræft/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The practice of magic, especially the use of spells and the invocation of supernatural powers; sorcery
**中文：** n. 巫术；魔法；魔力

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sorcery（巫术）
- wizardry（魔法）
- enchantment（魔力）
- necromancy（通灵术）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **practice witchcraft / accused of witchcraft / witchcraft and sorcery / the witchcraft of**

👉 Example: In colonial Massachusetts, dozens of individuals were accused of practicing witchcraft during the Salem trials.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The historical tribunal condemned several women to death on unsubstantiated charges of **witchcraft** and heresy.
   历史上的法庭以毫无根据的巫术和异端罪名判处数名妇女死刑。

2. Anthropologists have studied how accusations of **witchcraft** served as mechanisms of social control in traditional communities.
   人类学家研究了巫术指控如何在传统社区中充当社会控制机制。

3. The novel explores the boundary between folk medicine and **witchcraft** in seventeenth-century rural England.
   这部小说探讨了十七世纪英国乡村民间医学与巫术之间的界限。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "witch-craft" as the "craft of a witch" — the skills and practices associated with a witch's magical abilities.
把"witchcraft"想成"witch（女巫）+ craft（技艺）"——女巫的技艺，即巫术和魔法。
