# ⏮️ precede

🔊 /prɪˈsiːd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To come before something in time, order, or position; to be earlier or higher in rank than
**中文：** v. 领先；在…之前；优先；先于

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- antecede（先于）
- predate（早于）
- forerun（先行）
- come before（在…之前）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **precede something / preceded by / immediately precede / precede in importance**

👉 Example: A brief moment of silence preceded the announcement that would change the course of the entire debate.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. A comprehensive literature review must **precede** any original research to ensure the study addresses genuine gaps in existing knowledge.
   全面的文献综述必须先于任何原创研究，以确保该研究解决现有知识中的真正空白。

2. The economic recession that **preceded** the political upheaval created conditions of widespread discontent among the working population.
   政治动荡之前的经济衰退在劳动人口中造成了广泛的不满情绪。

3. In formal diplomatic protocol, the ambassador's address must **precede** all other speeches delivered at the official state reception.
   在正式外交礼仪中，大使的致辞必须先于在官方国宴上发表的所有其他演讲。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-cede" — "pre" means before, "cede" means to go — to precede is literally to "go before" something else.
把"precede"想成"pre（前）+cede（走）"——precede字面意思就是"走在前面"。
