# 🧽 absorb

🔊 /əbˈzɔːrb/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To take in or soak up a substance; to fully engage one's attention; to assimilate information or bear the cost of something
**中文：** v. 吸收；使全神贯注；承受；理解，消化

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- assimilate（吸收）
- soak up（吸取）
- engross（使全神贯注）
- consume（耗费）
- incorporate（纳入）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **absorb information / absorb the cost / absorb attention / absorbed in / absorb nutrients**

👉 Example: She was so absorbed in the novel that she missed her train stop entirely.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The new policy requires companies to **absorb** the increased tariff costs rather than passing them to consumers.
   新政策要求企业自行承受增加的关税成本，而不是转嫁给消费者。

2. Students who actively engage with the material tend to **absorb** complex concepts far more effectively.
   积极参与学习材料的学生往往能更有效地吸收复杂概念。

3. The researcher was completely **absorbed** in analyzing the data when the fire alarm suddenly interrupted her work.
   研究员完全沉浸在分析数据中，火警警报突然打断了她的工作。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Picture a sponge: "ab-SORB" — it sorbs (soaks) things in. Whether it is water, knowledge, or costs, to absorb means to take it all in completely.
想象一块海绵："ab-SORB"——它会sorb（吸入）东西。无论是水、知识还是成本，absorb意味着完全吸纳。
