# 📰 libel

🔊 /ˈlaɪbəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A published false statement that damages a person's reputation. (v.) To defame someone by publishing false and damaging statements
**中文：** n. 诽谤罪，中伤 v. 诽谤，中伤，诬蔑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- defamation（诽谤）
- slander（口头诽谤）
- calumny（诬蔑）
- vilification（中伤）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **libel suit / libel laws / commit libel / sue for libel**

👉 Example: The celebrity filed a libel suit against the tabloid for publishing fabricated stories.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The politician filed a **libel** lawsuit against the newspaper after it published unfounded allegations about his financial dealings.
   该政客在报纸刊登有关其财务交易的毫无根据的指控后，对该报提起了诽谤诉讼。

2. Journalists must verify their sources carefully to avoid **libeling** public figures and facing costly legal consequences in court.
   记者必须仔细核实消息来源，以避免诽谤公众人物并在法庭上面临高昂的法律后果。

3. The court ruled that the article constituted **libel** because it contained demonstrably false claims intended to damage her reputation.
   法院裁定该文章构成诽谤，因为它包含了旨在损害她声誉的明显虚假声明。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "libel" as "li-bel" — sounds like "lie bell," ringing a bell of lies that damages someone's reputation in public.
把"libel"想成"lie bell"——敲响谎言之钟，在公众面前损害某人的声誉。
