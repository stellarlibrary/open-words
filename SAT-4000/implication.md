# 💡 implication

🔊 /ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A conclusion that can be drawn from something although it is not explicitly stated; a likely consequence or effect; the act of being involved or implicated
**中文：** n. 含义；暗示；牵连，卷入；可能的结果，影响

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- connotation（隐含意义）
- suggestion（暗示）
- ramification（影响）
- consequence（后果）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **by implication / have implications for / the implication is that / far-reaching implications**

👉 Example: The new trade agreement has profound implications for the economic stability of developing nations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator's remarks carried the **implication** that further budget cuts would inevitably lead to significant reductions in public services.
   参议员的言论暗示进一步削减预算将不可避免地导致公共服务大幅缩减。

2. Researchers are still studying the long-term **implications** of artificial intelligence on global employment patterns and workforce development.
   研究人员仍在研究人工智能对全球就业模式和劳动力发展的长期影响。

3. The legal **implications** of the court's landmark ruling extend far beyond this particular case to affect constitutional interpretation broadly.
   法院这一里程碑式裁决的法律影响远远超出本案，广泛影响宪法解释。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "IMPLICATION" as something "implied" plus "action" — the hidden meaning that leads to real-world consequences and actions.
把"implication"想成"imply（暗示）+ action（行动）"——隐含的意思最终带来真实的后果和行动。
