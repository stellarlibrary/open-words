# 🔄 commonplace

🔊 /ˈkɑːmənˌpleɪs/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A trite or obvious remark; a platitude. (adj.) Not unusual; ordinary; lacking originality or distinction
**中文：** n. 老生常谈；平凡的事 adj. 平凡的；陈腐的；司空见惯的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ordinary（普通的）
- mundane（平凡的）
- banal（陈腐的）
- trite（老套的）
- platitude（陈词滥调）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a commonplace occurrence / commonplace remarks / become commonplace / nothing but commonplaces**

👉 Example: What was once considered revolutionary technology has now become entirely commonplace in daily life.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Smartphones have become so **commonplace** in modern society that it is difficult to imagine conducting daily affairs without them.
   智能手机在现代社会已变得如此普遍，以至于很难想象没有它们如何处理日常事务。

2. The politician's speech was filled with **commonplaces** about unity and progress, offering no substantive proposals for meaningful reform.
   这位政治家的演讲充满了关于团结和进步的老生常谈，没有提出任何有实质意义的改革建议。

3. Although electric vehicles were once a novelty, they have rapidly become **commonplace** on roads throughout the industrialized world.
   虽然电动汽车曾经是新奇事物，但它们已在整个工业化世界的道路上迅速变得司空见惯。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "commonplace" literally as a "common place" — something found in every common place, so ordinary that nobody notices it anymore.
把"commonplace"直接理解为"common place"——在每个常见的地方都能找到的东西，普通到没人再注意它了。
