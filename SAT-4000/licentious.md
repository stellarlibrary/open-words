# 🎭 licentious

🔊 /laɪˈsɛnʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Sexually immoral or unrestrained; disregarding accepted rules or conventions, especially in a reckless manner
**中文：** adj. 放肆的，放纵的；不道德的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- dissolute（放荡的）
- debauched（堕落的）
- immoral（不道德的）
- profligate（放纵的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **licentious behavior / licentious lifestyle / licentious conduct / morally licentious**

👉 Example: The novel was banned for its licentious content that shocked conservative audiences of the era.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The historian documented the **licentious** behavior of the aristocracy during the declining years of the ancient empire.
   历史学家记录了古代帝国衰落时期贵族阶层放纵的行为。

2. Critics condemned the play as **licentious** and morally corrupt, arguing it would exert a harmful influence on young audiences.
   评论家谴责这部戏剧放肆且道德败坏，认为它会对年轻观众产生有害影响。

3. The reformers launched a campaign against the **licentious** practices that had become widespread among the ruling political elite.
   改革者们发起了一场运动，反对在统治政治精英中已经普遍存在的放纵行为。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "licentious" as "license" + "ious" — someone who takes too much license with moral rules, acting without any restraint.
把"licentious"想成"license"（许可）+"ious"——一个在道德规则上给自己太多许可的人，行为毫无节制。
