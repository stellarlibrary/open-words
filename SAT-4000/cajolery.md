# 🎭 cajolery

🔊 /kəˈdʒoʊləri/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The act of persuading through flattery and sweet talk; coaxing or wheedling language intended to deceive or manipulate
**中文：** 甜言蜜语；诱骗；谄媚；花言巧语的哄骗行为

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- flattery（奉承）
- coaxing（哄劝）
- blandishment（甜言蜜语）
- wheedling（花言巧语）
- sweet talk（甜言蜜语）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **resort to cajolery / use cajolery / a mixture of cajolery and threats / resist cajolery**

👉 Example: Neither cajolery nor intimidation could persuade the witness to alter her sworn testimony.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat employed a combination of **cajolery** and subtle pressure to secure the reluctant nation's cooperation in negotiations.
   外交官运用甜言蜜语和微妙的施压相结合的方式，确保了这个不情愿的国家在谈判中的合作。

2. No amount of **cajolery** could convince the seasoned judge to reduce the sentence imposed upon the convicted fraudster.
   再多的花言巧语也无法说服这位经验丰富的法官减轻对已定罪欺诈犯的量刑。

3. The memoir revealed how corporate executives relied on **cajolery** to manipulate regulators and circumvent established compliance standards.
   回忆录揭示了企业高管如何依靠甜言蜜语来操纵监管机构并规避既定的合规标准。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cajolery" as "cajole" + "-ry" (the practice of) — cajolery is the noun form, the art of sweet-talking and flattering.
把"cajolery"想成"cajole（哄骗）+ -ry（……的行为）"——cajolery是名词形式，指甜言蜜语和奉承的技巧。
