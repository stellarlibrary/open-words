# 🎭 genre

🔊 /ˈʒɑːnrə/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A category or type of artistic composition, especially in literature, music, or film, characterized by a particular style, form, or content
**中文：** n. 类型；种类；体裁；流派

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- category（类别）
- type（类型）
- style（风格）
- classification（分类）
- form（形式）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **literary genre / film genre / music genre / genre fiction / genre conventions**

👉 Example: The novel defied traditional genre boundaries by blending elements of science fiction, romance, and historical narrative.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The emergence of streaming platforms has significantly blurred the boundaries between traditional film **genre** categories and audience expectations.
   流媒体平台的出现显著模糊了传统电影类型类别与观众期望之间的界限。

2. Scholars argue that the detective fiction **genre** reflects deep societal anxieties about justice, morality, and the limits of reason.
   学者认为侦探小说这一体裁反映了社会对正义、道德和理性局限的深层焦虑。

3. The composer masterfully combined elements from multiple musical **genre** traditions to create a profoundly original and innovative composition.
   这位作曲家巧妙地融合了多种音乐流派的元素，创作了一部极具原创性和创新性的作品。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "genre" as coming from French, meaning "kind" or "type" — when you walk into a bookstore, books are sorted by genre into different kinds.
"genre"来自法语，意为"种类"或"类型"——当你走进书店时，书籍按体裁分成不同种类。
