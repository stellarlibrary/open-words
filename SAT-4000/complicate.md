# 🔀 complicate

🔊 /ˈkɒmplɪkeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make something more difficult or complex; to cause to become entangled or intricate; to worsen or aggravate a condition
**中文：** v. 使复杂化；使恶化

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- confuse（使混乱）
- entangle（使纠缠）
- exacerbate（使恶化）
- muddle（使混乱）
- perplex（使困惑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **complicate matters / complicate the situation / further complicate / complicate the process**

👉 Example: The sudden resignation of the lead negotiator served only to complicate an already difficult situation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The new trade restrictions threatened to **complicate** diplomatic relations between the two nations at an already volatile time.
   新的贸易限制可能会在本已动荡的时期使两国之间的外交关系更加复杂。

2. Introducing additional variables at this stage would only **complicate** the experiment and make the results far more difficult to interpret.
   在现阶段引入额外变量只会使实验更加复杂，使结果更难以解释。

3. The patient's pre-existing heart condition could significantly **complicate** the surgical procedure and extend the anticipated recovery period considerably.
   患者既有的心脏病可能会显著使手术过程复杂化，并大大延长预期的恢复期。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "com-PLI-CATE" as "many folds or plies" — when you complicate something, you fold it over many times until it becomes tangled and hard to undo.
把"complicate"想成"许多褶皱或层"——当你使某事复杂化时，你把它折叠了很多次，直到它变得纠缠不清、难以解开。
