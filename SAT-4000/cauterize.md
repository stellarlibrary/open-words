# 🔥 cauterize

🔊 /ˈkɔːtəraɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To burn or sear tissue with a heated instrument or caustic substance, typically to stop bleeding or prevent infection; figuratively, to make emotionally numb or insensitive
**中文：** v. 烧灼，烙（伤口以止血或防感染）；腐蚀；使麻木不仁

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sear（烧灼）
- scorch（烤焦）
- brand（烙印）
- desensitize（使麻木）
- singe（烧焦）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cauterize a wound / cauterize the bleeding / cauterize one's emotions**

👉 Example: The field medic had to cauterize the wound to prevent the soldier from bleeding out.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The surgeon used a specialized instrument to **cauterize** the tissue and effectively stop the excessive bleeding during the operation.
   外科医生使用专用器械烧灼组织，在手术中有效地止住了过度出血。

2. Years of witnessing human suffering had **cauterized** her emotional responses, leaving her seemingly indifferent to tragedy.
   多年目睹人类苦难使她的情感反应变得麻木，令她对悲剧似乎无动于衷。

3. Ancient physicians would **cauterize** open wounds with heated metal to prevent infection in the absence of modern antibiotics.
   古代医生会用加热的金属烧灼开放性伤口，以在没有现代抗生素的情况下防止感染。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cauter-ize" as related to "caution" — you need great caution when you cauterize, because you are literally burning flesh to heal.
把"cauterize"联想到"caution（谨慎）"——烧灼伤口时需要极度谨慎，因为你实际上是在用火烧肉来治疗。
