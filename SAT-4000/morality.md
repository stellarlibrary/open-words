# ⚖️ morality

🔊 /məˈrælɪti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior; virtuous conduct
**中文：** n. 道德；品行；美德

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ethics（伦理）
- virtue（美德）
- integrity（正直）
- righteousness（正义）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **public morality / question the morality of / sense of morality / morality of an action**

👉 Example: Philosophers have long debated the morality of using deception even when it serves a greater good.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor challenged students to examine the **morality** of economic policies that benefit the wealthy at the expense of others.
   教授要求学生审视那些以牺牲他人利益来造福富人的经济政策的道德性。

2. Questions of **morality** become increasingly complex when cultural traditions conflict with contemporary human rights standards.
   当文化传统与当代人权标准发生冲突时，道德问题变得愈加复杂。

3. The novel explores how personal **morality** can erode under the relentless pressures of ambition and political power.
   这部小说探讨了个人道德如何在野心和政治权力的无情压力下逐渐瓦解。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "morality" as "moral + ity" — the quality of being moral, a noun form that captures the essence of right and wrong.
把"morality"想成"moral + ity"——即具有道德的品质，是一个名词形式，概括了是非对错的本质。
