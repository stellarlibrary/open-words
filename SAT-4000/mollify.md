# 🕊️ mollify

🔊 /ˈmɒlɪfaɪ/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To calm or soothe someone who is angry or upset; to reduce the severity or intensity of something; to soften
**中文：** v. 平息，缓和；使…平静；使…变软

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- appease（安抚）
- placate（抚慰）
- soothe（使平静）
- pacify（平息）
- conciliate（调解）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mollify the critics / mollify public anger / mollify an opponent / mollify concerns**

👉 Example: The company issued a public apology in an attempt to mollify the outraged consumers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The diplomat's carefully chosen words helped to **mollify** the tensions that had been escalating between the two neighboring nations.
   外交官措辞审慎，帮助缓和了两个邻国之间不断升级的紧张局势。

2. The university administration offered several concessions to **mollify** the students who had been protesting tuition increases for weeks.
   大学管理层做出了几项让步，以平息连续数周抗议学费上涨的学生。

3. No amount of flattery could **mollify** the senator after she discovered that her colleagues had deliberately undermined her proposal.
   在发现同僚蓄意破坏她的提案后，再多的奉承也无法平息这位参议员的怒火。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "molli-fy" as coming from Latin "mollis" meaning "soft" — to mollify is to make someone's feelings softer and calmer.
把"mollify"想成来自拉丁语"mollis（柔软的）"——mollify就是让某人的情绪变得柔和、平静。
