# 🧱 subjacent

🔊 /sʌbˈdʒeɪsənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Situated below or underneath; of a lower rank or position
**中文：** adj. 在底下的，在下方的，在下级的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- underlying（底层的）
- inferior（下方的）
- subordinate（下级的）
- beneath（在下面的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **subjacent layer / subjacent rock / subjacent stratum / subjacent territory**

👉 Example: The geologist examined the subjacent rock formations to determine the age of the upper sedimentary deposits.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The engineers discovered that the **subjacent** layers of bedrock were insufficiently stable to support the proposed skyscraper foundation.
   工程师们发现底下的基岩层不够稳固，无法支撑拟建摩天大楼的地基。

2. Careful analysis of the **subjacent** geological strata revealed evidence of ancient volcanic activity beneath the coastal plain.
   对下方地质层的仔细分析揭示了海岸平原下方古代火山活动的证据。

3. The **subjacent** administrative regions were required to comply with all directives issued by the central governing authority.
   下级行政区域被要求遵守中央管理机构发布的所有指令。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sub-jacent" as "sub" (under) + "jacent" (lying) — something lying underneath, like a layer below the surface.
把"subjacent"想成"sub"（下面）+"jacent"（躺着的）——躺在下面的东西，就像表面以下的一层。
