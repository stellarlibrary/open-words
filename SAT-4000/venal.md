# 💰 venal

🔊 /ˈviːnəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Willing to act dishonestly in return for money or personal gain; open to bribery; corrupt
**中文：** adj. 贪污的；贿赂的；贪赃枉法的；用金钱买得的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- corrupt（腐败的）
- bribable（可贿赂的）
- mercenary（唯利是图的）
- dishonest（不诚实的）
- unscrupulous（无道德的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **venal officials / venal politician / venal practices / venal behavior**

👉 Example: The investigation revealed that venal officials had accepted bribes in exchange for favorable regulatory decisions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The historian argued that **venal** administrators in the colonial government systematically exploited local populations for personal enrichment.
   历史学家认为，殖民政府中贪污的官员系统性地剥削当地民众以中饱私囊。

2. Public trust in the judiciary eroded rapidly after several **venal** judges were found to have accepted payments from criminal organizations.
   在发现多名贪赃枉法的法官收受犯罪组织贿赂后，公众对司法机构的信任迅速瓦解。

3. The novelist portrayed a **venal** society in which every relationship was governed by financial self-interest rather than genuine loyalty.
   这位小说家描绘了一个贪婪的社会，其中每段关系都受经济私利而非真正忠诚的支配。

Think of "venal" as sounding like "vending" — a venal person is like a vending machine, doing whatever you want as long as you insert money.
把"venal"想成听起来像"vending（自动售货）"——一个venal的人就像自动售货机，只要你塞钱进去就会照做。
