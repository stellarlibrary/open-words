# 🛡️ quixotic

🔊 /kwɪkˈsɒtɪk/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Extremely idealistic; unrealistic and impractical; resembling or befitting Don Quixote; pursuing lofty but unattainable goals
**中文：** adj. 唐吉诃德式的；狂想家的；愚侠的；不切实际的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- idealistic（理想主义的）
- impractical（不切实际的）
- visionary（空想的）
- romantic（浪漫的）
- fanciful（异想天开的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **quixotic quest / quixotic pursuit / quixotic dream / quixotic endeavor**

👉 Example: His quixotic pursuit of a perfect society earned him both admiration and ridicule from peers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The activist's **quixotic** campaign to eliminate all forms of inequality was noble in spirit but profoundly impractical in execution.
   这位活动家消除一切不平等形式的唐吉诃德式运动精神上是崇高的，但在执行上极不现实。

2. Many dismissed the entrepreneur's **quixotic** vision of colonizing distant planets within a single decade as pure fantasy.
   许多人将这位企业家在十年内殖民遥远星球的狂想愿景斥为纯粹的幻想。

3. Despite criticism, she remained committed to her **quixotic** endeavor of reforming the entire educational system through voluntary effort alone.
   尽管受到批评，她仍然致力于仅通过自愿努力来改革整个教育系统的不切实际的事业。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of Don Quixote, the fictional knight who tilted at windmills — "quixotic" means chasing impossible dreams just like he did.
想想唐吉诃德，那个向风车冲锋的虚构骑士——"quixotic"意味着像他一样追逐不可能的梦想。
