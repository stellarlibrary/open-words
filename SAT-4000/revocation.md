# 🚫 revocation

🔊 /ˌrevəˈkeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The official cancellation or withdrawal of a decree, decision, license, or promise
**中文：** n. 撤回

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- cancellation（取消）
- annulment（废除）
- withdrawal（撤回）
- repeal（撤销）
- rescission（废止）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **revocation of a license / revocation of privileges / revocation of parole / revocation of an order**

👉 Example: The revocation of his medical license followed a lengthy investigation into professional misconduct.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **revocation** of the trade agreement sent shockwaves through international markets and disrupted established supply chains.
   贸易协定的撤回在国际市场引发了冲击波，并扰乱了既有的供应链。

2. Citizens protested the sudden **revocation** of their voting rights, arguing that the government had acted without constitutional authority.
   公民们抗议他们投票权的突然撤回，认为政府的行为缺乏宪法授权。

3. The court ordered the immediate **revocation** of the defendant's bail after evidence of witness tampering was presented.
   在提交了妨碍证人的证据后，法院下令立即撤销被告的保释。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "revoke" + "-ation" (the act of) — revocation is the act of revoking, of officially taking something back.
把"revocation"想成"revoke（撤回）+ -ation（行为）"——revocation就是撤回的行为，正式收回某物。
