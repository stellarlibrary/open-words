# ⚔️ skirmish

🔊 /ˈskɜːrmɪʃ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A brief, minor fight or conflict; a small-scale battle. (v.) To engage in a skirmish or minor conflict
**中文：** n. 小冲突

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- clash（冲突）
- scuffle（混战）
- encounter（遭遇战）
- altercation（争吵）
- fracas（喧闹）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **border skirmish / skirmish with / verbal skirmish / skirmish between**

👉 Example: A brief skirmish between the two patrols broke out near the disputed border at dawn.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **skirmish** along the northern border lasted only minutes but resulted in heightened diplomatic tensions between the two nations.
   北部边境的小冲突仅持续了几分钟，但导致两国之间的外交紧张局势加剧。

2. Opposition legislators **skirmished** with government representatives over the proposed budget cuts during the heated parliamentary session.
   反对派议员在激烈的议会会议期间就拟议的预算削减与政府代表发生了小争论。

3. Historians noted that the initial **skirmish** at the village bridge foreshadowed the devastating full-scale war that would follow.
   历史学家指出，村庄桥头的最初小冲突预示了随后将爆发的毁灭性全面战争。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "skirmish" as a "skirt-mish" — a fight that just skirts around the edges, never becoming a full battle.
把"skirmish"想成"skirt-mish"——一场只在边缘擦过的战斗，从未变成全面战役。
