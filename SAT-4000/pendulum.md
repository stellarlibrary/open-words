# 🕰️ pendulum

🔊 /ˈpendʒələm/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A weight hung from a fixed point that swings freely; a situation that oscillates between opposite extremes
**中文：** n. 钟摆；摆锤；摇摆不定的事态

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- oscillator（振荡器）
- swing（摇摆）
- fluctuation（波动）
- vacillation（犹豫不决）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **the pendulum swings / pendulum of opinion / pendulum effect / political pendulum**

👉 Example: The political pendulum has swung back toward more conservative economic policies.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **pendulum** of public opinion has shifted dramatically toward supporting renewable energy sources over the past decade.
   过去十年间，公众舆论的钟摆已大幅转向支持可再生能源。

2. Scientists observed how the **pendulum** maintained a remarkably consistent rhythm despite minor fluctuations in ambient temperature.
   科学家观察到，尽管环境温度有微小波动，钟摆仍保持着极其稳定的节奏。

3. The economic **pendulum** between governmental regulation and free market policies continues to define contemporary political discourse.
   政府监管与自由市场政策之间的经济摇摆继续主导着当代政治话语。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pend-ulum" — "pend" comes from Latin "pendere" meaning "to hang." A pendulum is something that hangs and swings back and forth.
把"pendulum"想成"pend（悬挂）+ ulum"——源自拉丁语"悬挂"，就是悬挂着来回摆动的东西。
