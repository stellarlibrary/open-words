# 🌪️ calamity

🔊 /kəˈlæmɪti/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A great disaster or misfortune; an event causing widespread damage, suffering, or distress
**中文：** 大灾祸；灾难；不幸事件

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disaster（灾难）
- catastrophe（大灾难）
- tragedy（悲剧）
- cataclysm（剧变）
- misfortune（不幸）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **natural calamity / great calamity / avert a calamity / calamity befalls / in times of calamity**

👉 Example: The earthquake was the worst calamity to strike the region in over a century of recorded history.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prolonged drought proved to be an unprecedented **calamity** for the agricultural communities that depended entirely on seasonal rainfall.
   长期干旱对完全依赖季节性降雨的农业社区来说是一场史无前例的灾难。

2. Historians regard the Great Fire of London as a devastating **calamity** that nonetheless catalyzed significant urban reconstruction reforms.
   历史学家将伦敦大火视为一场毁灭性的灾难，但它也催生了重大的城市重建改革。

3. The financial **calamity** of the global recession left millions unemployed and fundamentally reshaped economic policy for subsequent decades.
   全球经济衰退的金融灾难使数百万人失业，并从根本上重塑了此后数十年的经济政策。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "calamity" as "CALL-A-MEETING" — when a calamity strikes, everyone must call a meeting to deal with the disaster.
把"calamity"想成"CALL-A-MEETING（召开会议）"——当灾难降临时，每个人都必须召开会议来应对灾难。
