# 🔙 withdraw

🔊 /wɪðˈdrɔː/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To remove or take back; to retreat from a position; to retract a statement or revoke an offer
**中文：** v. 撤退；收回；撤销；取回

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- retract（收回）
- retreat（撤退）
- revoke（撤销）
- recede（后退）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **withdraw from / withdraw support / withdraw funds / withdraw a statement / withdraw troops**

👉 Example: The diplomat decided to withdraw the proposal after negotiations reached an impasse.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The commander ordered all remaining forces to **withdraw** from the contested region before the ceasefire deadline expired.
   指挥官命令所有剩余部队在停火期限届满前撤出争议地区。

2. Several major investors chose to **withdraw** their financial support after the corruption scandal became public knowledge.
   几位主要投资者在腐败丑闻公开后选择撤回他们的资金支持。

3. The student was permitted to **withdraw** from the advanced seminar without academic penalty before the official deadline.
   该学生被允许在官方截止日期前退出高级研讨课而不受学业处罚。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "with-draw" as "drawing back with you" — pulling something back toward yourself, whether it is troops, money, or words.
把"withdraw"想成"with（带着）+ draw（拉）"——把东西拉回来，无论是军队、金钱还是言论。
