# 💒 congenital

🔊 /kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Present from birth; innate or inherent, especially referring to a medical condition or trait existing since birth
**中文：** adj. 先天的，天生的；生来就有的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- innate（天生的）
- inborn（与生俱来的）
- inherent（固有的）
- native（天然的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **congenital defect / congenital condition / congenital disease / congenital abnormality**

👉 Example: Early screening can help detect congenital abnormalities before they become serious health risks.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Advances in prenatal medicine have significantly improved the early detection and treatment of **congenital** heart defects in newborns.
   产前医学的进步显著改善了新生儿先天性心脏缺陷的早期发现和治疗。

2. The study revealed that certain **congenital** conditions are influenced by both genetic predisposition and environmental factors during pregnancy.
   研究显示，某些先天性疾病受到遗传倾向和妊娠期环境因素的共同影响。

3. Despite being born with a **congenital** visual impairment, the musician developed an extraordinary sensitivity to pitch and rhythm.
   尽管天生患有先天性视力障碍，这位音乐家却培养出了对音高和节奏的非凡敏感度。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con-GENIT-al" — "genit" relates to "genesis" (birth/origin), so congenital means something present from one's genesis or birth.
把"congenital"想成"con-GENIT-al"——"genit"与"genesis"（起源/出生）有关，所以congenital意为从出生起就存在的。
