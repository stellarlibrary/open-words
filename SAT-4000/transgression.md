# 🚫 transgression

🔊 /trænzˈɡrɛʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** An act that violates a law, rule, or code of conduct; a sin or offense. In geology, the spread of the sea over land areas.
**中文：** n. 犯罪；违反；过错。（地质学）海侵。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- violation（违反）
- offense（过错）
- infringement（侵犯）
- trespass（侵犯）
- misdeed（不端行为）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **commit a transgression / forgive a transgression / serious transgression / minor transgression**

👉 Example: The committee deemed his unauthorized disclosure of classified information a serious transgression.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The student's **transgression** of academic integrity policies resulted in immediate suspension and a permanent mark on her record.
   该学生违反学术诚信政策导致立即停学，并在其档案中留下永久记录。

2. Historical records reveal that many perceived **transgressions** against colonial law were actually acts of legitimate political resistance.
   历史记录表明，许多被视为违反殖民法律的行为实际上是合法的政治抵抗行为。

3. Marine geologists study ancient **transgressions** of the sea to understand how coastlines have shifted over millions of years.
   海洋地质学家研究古代海侵现象，以了解海岸线在数百万年间是如何变迁的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "transgression" as "trans (across) + gression (stepping)" — stepping across the line into wrongdoing, like the sea stepping across onto land.
把"transgression"想成"跨越（trans）+ 步行（gression）"——踏过界限进入过错，就像大海跨越到陆地上。
