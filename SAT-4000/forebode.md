# 🌩️ forebode

🔊 /fɔːrˈboʊd/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To act as a warning or indication of something bad; to have a presentiment of something unfortunate
**中文：** v. 预示；预感；预兆

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- portend（预示）
- presage（预兆）
- foreshadow（预示）
- augur（预言）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **forebode disaster / forebode evil / forebode ill / forebode danger**

👉 Example: The dark clouds gathering on the horizon seemed to forebode an approaching storm.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The sudden silence of the forest animals seemed to **forebode** the arrival of a severe and devastating natural disaster.
   森林动物突然的沉寂似乎预示着一场严重而毁灭性的自然灾害的到来。

2. Many economists believe that the sharp decline in consumer spending **forebodes** a prolonged period of economic recession.
   许多经济学家认为，消费支出的急剧下降预示着一段漫长的经济衰退期。

3. The ominous tone of the chairman's opening remarks **foreboded** significant organizational changes that would affect every department.
   董事长开场白中不祥的语气预兆着将影响每个部门的重大组织变革。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fore + bode" — "fore" means before, and "bode" means to predict, so forebode means to predict something beforehand.
把"forebode"想成"fore + bode"——"fore"意为提前，"bode"意为预示，所以forebode就是提前预示某事。
