# 🗡️ mordacious

🔊 /mɔːrˈdeɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Biting or sharp in manner or style; caustic or sarcastic in speech or expression
**中文：** adj. 锐利的；辛辣的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- caustic（尖刻的）
- biting（辛辣的）
- acerbic（刻薄的）
- trenchant（锐利的）
- sardonic（讽刺的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mordacious wit / mordacious criticism / mordacious humor / mordacious remarks**

👉 Example: His mordacious commentary on social media trends earned him both admiration and resentment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The columnist was known for her **mordacious** prose, which spared neither politicians nor corporate executives from her sharp scrutiny.
   这位专栏作家以其辛辣的文笔著称，无论是政客还是企业高管都逃不过她犀利的审视。

2. Despite his **mordacious** tone during the debate, the senator's arguments lacked substantive evidence to support his sweeping claims.
   尽管这位参议员在辩论中语气尖刻，但他的论点缺乏实质性证据来支持其笼统的主张。

3. The literary critic's **mordacious** review of the novel discouraged many readers from picking up what was actually a thoughtful work.
   这位文学评论家对小说尖刻的评论使许多读者对这部实际上颇具深意的作品望而却步。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "mordacious" as coming from "mord" (Latin mordere = to bite) — mordacious words bite and sting like sharp teeth.
"mordacious"来自拉丁语"mordere"（咬）——辛辣的话语像锋利的牙齿一样尖锐刺痛。
