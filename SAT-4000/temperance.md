# ⚖️ temperance

🔊 /ˈtɛmpərəns/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) Moderation or self-restraint, especially in eating and drinking; abstinence from alcoholic beverages
**中文：** n. 戒酒；节欲；节制；（气候等的）温和

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- moderation（适度）
- abstinence（节制）
- sobriety（清醒）
- restraint（克制）
- self-control（自制）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **temperance movement / practice temperance / temperance society / advocate temperance**

👉 Example: The temperance movement of the nineteenth century sought to reduce alcohol consumption nationwide.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **temperance** movement in America ultimately led to the passage of the Eighteenth Amendment, which prohibited the sale of alcohol.
   美国的禁酒运动最终促成了第十八修正案的通过，该修正案禁止了酒类销售。

2. Ancient philosophers regarded **temperance** as one of the four cardinal virtues essential for leading a balanced and ethical life.
   古代哲学家将节制视为过上平衡和道德生活所必需的四大基本美德之一。

3. Her physician recommended **temperance** in dietary habits, advising moderation rather than the complete elimination of any food group.
   她的医生建议在饮食习惯上保持节制，提倡适度而非完全消除任何食物类别。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "temperance" as "temper" + "ance" — having the temper (disposition) to hold back, to practice restraint and moderation in all things.
把"temperance"想成"temper"（性情）+ "ance"——拥有克制的性情，在所有事情上实践约束和节制。
