# 🌉 bridge

🔊 /brɪdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A structure spanning a river, road, or gap; a card game; a connecting link. (v.) To build a bridge over; to overcome a gap or difficulty
**中文：** n. 桥；桥牌；桥接器；船桥 v. 架桥；渡过

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- span（跨越）
- overpass（天桥）
- link（连接）
- connect（连接）
- traverse（横跨）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **bridge the gap / bridge the divide / bridge differences / build bridges**

👉 Example: The new program was designed to bridge the gap between theoretical research and practical application.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The university sought to **bridge** the divide between the sciences and the humanities through interdisciplinary coursework.
   该大学试图通过跨学科课程来弥合科学与人文学科之间的鸿沟。

2. A newly constructed **bridge** over the river facilitated commerce and cultural exchange between the two neighboring towns.
   河上新建的桥促进了两个相邻城镇之间的商业和文化交流。

3. Effective diplomacy can **bridge** longstanding differences between nations that have historically been in conflict.
   有效的外交可以弥合历史上一直存在冲突的国家之间的长期分歧。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bridge" as something that brings two sides together — "br-idge" sounds like "bring edge to edge."
把"bridge"想成把两边连在一起的东西——"br-idge"听起来像"bring edge to edge"（把边缘连在一起）。
