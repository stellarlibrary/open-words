# 🎵 aural

🔊 /ˈɔːrəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** Relating to the ear or the sense of hearing.

**中文：** 听觉的；耳的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- auditory（听觉的）
- acoustic（声学的）
- auricular（耳的）
- hearing-related（与听力相关的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

"Aural" is a homophone of "oral" (relating to the mouth) in many dialects, which can cause confusion. In context, "aural" always pertains to hearing while "oral" pertains to speaking or the mouth. "Aural" is slightly less technical than "auditory" and appears in both everyday and academic usage.

在许多方言中，"aural" 与 "oral"（与口腔有关的）是同音词，容易造成混淆。在语境中，"aural" 始终与听觉有关，而 "oral" 与口语或口腔有关。"aural" 比 "auditory" 略显日常化，在日常和学术用法中均可见。

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The musician's exceptional **aural** skills allowed her to identify any note played on the piano.
   这位音乐家卓越的**听觉**能力使她能辨认出钢琴上弹奏的任何音符。

2. Language learners should develop both **aural** comprehension and written fluency simultaneously.
   语言学习者应同时发展**听觉**理解能力和书面表达流利度。

3. The doctor recommended an **aural** examination after the patient reported persistent ringing sounds.
   在患者报告持续出现耳鸣后，医生建议进行**耳部**检查。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

"Aural" starts with "aur-," which comes from the Latin "auris" meaning "ear." Think of an "aura" around your ear — the zone where sounds reach you. Everything "aural" happens in that listening zone.

"aural" 以 "aur-" 开头，源自拉丁语 "auris"，意为"耳朵"。想象你耳朵周围有一圈"光环"（aura）——声音到达你的区域。所有 "aural" 的事物都发生在这个聆听区域中。
