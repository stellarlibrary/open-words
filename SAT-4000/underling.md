# 👤 underling

🔊 /ˈʌndərlɪŋ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A person of lower rank or status; a subordinate, especially one regarded as unimportant or servile
**中文：** 下属；部下；走卒；手下人（常含贬义）

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- subordinate（下属）
- minion（奴才）
- lackey（跟班）
- flunky（马屁精）
- henchman（亲信走卒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **mere underling / loyal underling / send an underling / treat someone as an underling**

👉 Example: The CEO never attended minor meetings personally; he always sent an underling in his place.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The corrupt official delegated all illegal tasks to his **underlings** so that he could maintain plausible deniability if investigated.
   这位腐败官员把所有非法任务委派给手下，以便在被调查时保持合理的否认空间。

2. Despite being treated as a mere **underling** for years, she eventually rose through the ranks to become the company's president.
   尽管多年来一直被当作一个微不足道的下属对待，她最终逐步晋升为公司总裁。

3. The general's **underlings** carried out the orders without question, fearing severe punishment for any sign of disobedience or hesitation.
   将军的部下毫不质疑地执行命令，害怕因任何不服从或犹豫的迹象而受到严厉惩罚。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "under-ling" — a person who is UNDER someone else; the suffix "-ling" suggests someone small or lesser, like "duckling."
把"underling"想成"under（在下面的）+ ling（小的人）"——处于下位的小人物，就像"duckling"（小鸭子）中的"-ling"表示小。
