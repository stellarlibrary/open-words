# 😢 melancholy

🔊 /ˈmɛlənˌkɑːli/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Causing or expressing sadness; gloomy and thoughtful. (n.) A deep, persistent sadness or sorrow; a pensive mood
**中文：** adj. 忧郁的；使人悲伤的 n. 忧郁；悲哀；愁思

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sorrowful（悲伤的）
- somber（阴郁的）
- pensive（忧思的）
- wistful（惆怅的）
- mournful（哀伤的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **a sense of melancholy / melancholy mood / melancholy tone / tinged with melancholy**

👉 Example: A profound melancholy settled over the poet as she reflected on the passage of time.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The composer's final symphony carries a **melancholy** beauty that has moved audiences to tears for over a century.
   这位作曲家最后一部交响曲带有一种忧郁的美感，一个多世纪以来令听众感动落泪。

2. After the departure of her closest colleague, a quiet **melancholy** pervaded her daily routine at the university.
   在最亲密的同事离开后，一种淡淡的忧郁弥漫在她在大学的日常生活中。

3. The novel's **melancholy** tone reflects the author's own experience of loss and displacement during the civil conflict.
   小说忧郁的基调反映了作者在内战期间亲身经历的失去与流离。

把"melancholy"想成"melon（甜瓜）+ collie（牧羊犬）"——想象一只忧伤的牧羊犬坐在甜瓜旁边，看起来郁郁寡欢。
