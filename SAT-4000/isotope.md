# ⚛️ isotope

🔊 /ˈaɪsətoʊp/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Each of two or more forms of the same chemical element that have the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons in their nuclei
**中文：** 同位素

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- nuclide（核素）
- variant（变体）
- radioisotope（放射性同位素）
- isomer（异构体）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **radioactive isotope / stable isotope / isotope analysis / carbon isotope**

👉 Example: Scientists used carbon isotope dating to determine the approximate age of the ancient artifact.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Carbon-14 is a radioactive **isotope** widely employed by archaeologists to determine the age of organic materials up to fifty thousand years old.
   碳-14是一种放射性同位素，被考古学家广泛用于测定距今五万年以内有机材料的年代。

2. Researchers analyzed the oxygen **isotope** ratios in polar ice cores to reconstruct historical climate patterns over millennia.
   研究人员分析了极地冰芯中的氧同位素比率，以重建数千年来的历史气候模式。

3. Medical imaging techniques frequently utilize radioactive **isotopes** as tracers to detect abnormalities in organ function and blood flow.
   医学影像技术经常利用放射性同位素作为示踪剂来检测器官功能和血流的异常。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "iso-tope" as "iso (same) + topos (place)" — same place on the periodic table, because isotopes of an element occupy the same position.
把"isotope"拆分为"iso-（相同的）+ topos（位置）"——在周期表上同一位置，因为同一元素的同位素占据相同的位置。
