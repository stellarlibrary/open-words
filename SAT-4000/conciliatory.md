# 🕊️ conciliatory

🔊 /kənˈsɪliətɔːri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Intended to placate or pacify; aimed at making peace or resolving conflict through goodwill
**中文：** adj. 安抚的；调和的；抚慰的；和解的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- pacifying（安抚的）
- appeasing（抚慰的）
- placatory（和解的）
- mollifying（缓和的）
- peacemaking（调停的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **conciliatory tone / conciliatory gesture / conciliatory approach / conciliatory remarks**

👉 Example: The diplomat adopted a conciliatory tone in hopes of easing tensions between the two nations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The president issued a **conciliatory** statement following the summit, expressing willingness to engage in further diplomatic negotiations.
   总统在峰会后发表了一份安抚性声明，表示愿意参与进一步的外交谈判。

2. Her **conciliatory** gesture of offering a public apology helped restore trust among the disaffected members of the organization.
   她公开道歉这一和解姿态帮助恢复了组织中心怀不满的成员之间的信任。

3. Rather than escalating the dispute, the manager took a **conciliatory** approach and proposed a compromise acceptable to both parties.
   经理没有使争端升级，而是采取了调和的方式，提出了双方都能接受的妥协方案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "conciliatory" as related to "council" — in a council, people come together to settle disputes and make peace.
把"conciliatory"和"council（议会）"联系起来——在议会中，人们聚集在一起解决争端、达成和解。
