# 📋 probation

🔊 /proʊˈbeɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A period of testing or trial during which a person's fitness for a role is evaluated; in law, the supervised release of an offender as an alternative to imprisonment
**中文：** n. 试用；见习；鉴定，检验；缓刑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- trial period（试用期）
- testing（考验）
- parole（假释）
- apprenticeship（学徒期）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **on probation / probation period / probation officer / violation of probation**

👉 Example: New employees are typically placed on probation for the first six months of employment.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The judge sentenced the first-time offender to two years of **probation** instead of imprisonment, requiring regular check-ins with authorities.
   法官判处这名初犯两年缓刑而非监禁，要求其定期向当局报到。

2. During her six-month **probation** period, the new analyst demonstrated exceptional competence and was offered a permanent position.
   在六个月的试用期内，这位新分析师展现了卓越的能力，并获得了正式职位。

3. The university placed the student on academic **probation** after his grade point average fell below the minimum required standard.
   该大学在这名学生的平均成绩低于最低要求标准后对其实施了学业察看。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "probation" as "probe-ation" — a period of probing and testing someone to see if they meet the required standards.
把"probation"想成"probe-ation"——一段探查和考验某人是否达到所要求标准的时期。
