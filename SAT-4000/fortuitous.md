# 🍀 fortuitous

🔊 /fɔːrˈtuːɪtəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Happening by accident or chance rather than design; unexpectedly fortunate or lucky
**中文：** adj. 偶然的；意外的；幸运的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- accidental（偶然的）
- chance（偶然的）
- serendipitous（意外发现的）
- unplanned（计划外的）
- providential（天意的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fortuitous encounter / fortuitous discovery / fortuitous circumstance / fortuitous event**

👉 Example: Their fortuitous meeting at the conference led to a highly productive research collaboration.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **fortuitous** discovery of penicillin by Alexander Fleming revolutionized modern medicine and saved countless millions of lives worldwide.
   亚历山大·弗莱明对青霉素的偶然发现彻底改变了现代医学，拯救了全世界无数人的生命。

2. A **fortuitous** change in weather patterns allowed the rescue team to reach the stranded climbers before conditions deteriorated further.
   天气模式的意外变化使救援队得以在情况进一步恶化之前到达被困的登山者身边。

3. The investor's **fortuitous** decision to diversify her portfolio just before the market crash preserved the majority of her assets.
   这位投资者在市场崩盘前偶然做出的分散投资组合的决定保全了她的大部分资产。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fortuitous" as containing "fortune" — something fortuitous happens by fortune or luck, not by planning.
把"fortuitous"想成包含"fortune"（运气）——fortuitous的事情是靠运气或偶然发生的，而非计划好的。
