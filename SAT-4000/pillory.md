# 🔒 pillory

🔊 /ˈpɪləri/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A wooden framework with holes for the head and hands, used historically to publicly punish and humiliate offenders. (v.) To expose someone to public ridicule or scorn; to put in a pillory
**中文：** n. 颈手枷；示众 v. 给……上颈手枷；使……惹人嘲笑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- ridicule（嘲笑）
- denounce（谴责）
- stigmatize（污名化）
- humiliate（羞辱）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **pillory someone for / public pillory / pilloried in the press / pilloried by critics**

👉 Example: The politician was pilloried in the media for his contradictory statements on the proposed legislation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator was publicly **pilloried** by journalists after evidence of financial misconduct surfaced during the congressional investigation.
   在国会调查中出现财务不当行为的证据后，这位参议员遭到记者们的公开批判。

2. In medieval England, the **pillory** served as both a legal punishment and a method of public shaming for minor offenses.
   在中世纪英格兰，颈手枷既是一种法律惩罚，也是对轻微犯罪进行公开羞辱的手段。

3. The author was **pilloried** by literary critics who dismissed her unconventional narrative style as pretentious and undisciplined.
   这位作家遭到文学评论家的嘲讽，他们认为她非传统的叙事风格矫揉造作且缺乏章法。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pill-ory" as a "pillar of shame" — the pillory was a pillar-like device where people stood to be publicly shamed.
把"pillory"联想成"shame pillar"（耻辱柱）——颈手枷就是一种柱状刑具，让人站在那里当众受辱。
