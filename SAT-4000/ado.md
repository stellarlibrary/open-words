# 🌀 ado

🔊 /əˈduː/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Fuss, commotion, or trouble; unnecessary activity or excitement over something.
**中文：** 忙乱，纷扰，麻烦

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fuss（忙乱）
- commotion（骚动）
- turmoil（混乱）
- hubbub（喧闹）
- bustle（忙碌）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **without further ado**, **much ado about nothing**, **without more ado**

👉 Example: Without further ado, the chairman proceeded to announce the award recipients.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Without further **ado**, the keynote speaker began her presentation on sustainable development strategies.
   闲话不多说，主讲人开始了她关于可持续发展战略的演讲。

2. The media created much **ado** about the minor policy change, exaggerating its potential consequences.
   媒体对这一微小的政策变化大肆渲染，夸大了其潜在后果。

3. After considerable **ado** over the seating arrangements, the delegates finally settled into their assigned places.
   在座位安排引起一番纷扰之后，代表们终于在指定位置就座。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of Shakespeare's "Much Ado About Nothing" — a lot of fuss and commotion over trivial matters.
联想莎士比亚的《无事生非》（Much Ado About Nothing）——为琐事大费周章、忙乱不已。
