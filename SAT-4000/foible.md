# 😅 foible

🔊 /ˈfɔɪbəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A minor weakness or eccentricity in someone's character; a small flaw or peculiar habit
**中文：** n. 小缺点；弱点；癖好

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- weakness（弱点）
- shortcoming（缺点）
- quirk（怪癖）
- eccentricity（古怪）
- flaw（瑕疵）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **human foible / personal foible / minor foible / charming foible**

👉 Example: Despite his many foibles, the professor was widely respected for his groundbreaking contributions to the field.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The biography revealed the leader's private **foibles**, making the historical figure appear far more human and relatable to modern readers.
   这部传记揭示了这位领导人私下的**小缺点**，使这位历史人物在现代读者眼中显得更加真实和亲近。

2. Every great author has certain **foibles** in their writing style, yet these minor imperfections often add distinctive charm to their work.
   每位伟大的作家在写作风格上都有一些**癖好**，但这些小瑕疵往往为其作品增添独特的魅力。

3. The comedy succeeded by gently mocking the **foibles** of upper-class society without resorting to cruelty or mean-spirited satire.
   这部喜剧通过温和地嘲讽上层社会的**弱点**而大获成功，没有诉诸刻薄或恶意的讽刺。

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "foible" as "feeble" with the letters rearranged — a foible is a feeble, minor weakness that everyone has.
把"foible"想成"feeble（虚弱的）"字母重新排列——foible就是每个人都有的微不足道的小弱点。
