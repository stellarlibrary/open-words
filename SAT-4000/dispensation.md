# 📜 dispensation

🔊 /ˌdɪspɛnˈseɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act of distributing or dispensing; an exemption from a rule or obligation; a special permission
**中文：** n. 分配；免除；豁免

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- exemption（豁免）
- immunity（免除）
- distribution（分配）
- reprieve（缓刑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **grant a dispensation / special dispensation / dispensation from a rule / papal dispensation**

👉 Example: The committee granted a special dispensation allowing the student to submit the thesis after the deadline.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The religious authority granted a **dispensation** permitting the congregation to observe the ceremony under modified conditions.
   宗教权威机构给予了豁免，允许会众在修改后的条件下举行仪式。

2. Under the new political **dispensation**, citizens were afforded greater freedoms of expression and assembly than before.
   在新的政治体制下，公民获得了比以往更大的言论和集会自由。

3. The court issued a rare **dispensation** that exempted the organization from certain regulatory requirements for one year.
   法院发布了一项罕见的豁免令，使该组织在一年内免受某些监管要求的约束。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dispens-ation" from "dispense" — when you dispense something, you distribute it; a dispensation is the act of distributing, or being dispensed from a rule.
把"dispensation"想成来自"dispense"（分配）——当你分配某物时，就是dispensation；也可以指被免除某项规则的约束。
