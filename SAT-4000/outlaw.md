# 🏴‍☠️ outlaw

🔊 /ˈaʊtlɔː/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A person who has broken the law; a fugitive or criminal. (v.) To make something illegal; to ban or prohibit
**中文：** n. 歹徒；罪犯；亡命之徒 v. 宣布…为不合法；将…放逐；剥夺…的法律保护

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- criminal（罪犯）
- fugitive（逃犯）
- bandit（土匪）
- prohibit（禁止）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **outlaw a practice / outlaw weapons / notorious outlaw / outlaw organization**

👉 Example: The government moved swiftly to outlaw the sale of hazardous chemicals to unauthorized buyers.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The legislature voted to **outlaw** the use of certain pesticides that posed serious threats to public health.
   立法机构投票禁止使用对公众健康构成严重威胁的某些杀虫剂。

2. The legendary **outlaw** evaded capture for years by hiding in the remote mountain regions of the territory.
   这位传奇的亡命之徒在偏远山区藏匿多年，一直逃避追捕。

3. Several nations have agreed to **outlaw** the production and stockpiling of chemical weapons under the new treaty.
   多个国家已同意依据新条约禁止生产和储存化学武器。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "out + law" — someone who is outside the law, or to put something outside the boundary of what is legal.
把"outlaw"拆成"out（外面）+ law（法律）"——一个在法律之外的人，或将某事物置于合法范围之外。
