# 🚢 scuttle

🔊 /ˈskʌtl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A hatch or opening in a ship's deck or hull; a coal bucket; a basket. (v.) To sink a ship deliberately by cutting holes in the hull; to scurry or run hastily
**中文：** v. 凿洞沉船

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sabotage（破坏）
- wreck（毁坏）
- demolish（摧毁）
- scurry（急跑）
- undermine（暗中破坏）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **scuttle a ship / scuttle a plan / scuttle across / scuttle away**

👉 Example: The captain ordered the crew to scuttle the vessel rather than allow it to fall into enemy hands.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The naval commander made the difficult decision to **scuttle** the damaged warship to prevent its advanced technology from being captured.
   海军指挥官做出了艰难的决定，凿沉受损的军舰以防止其先进技术被缴获。

2. Opposition lawmakers worked strategically to **scuttle** the proposed trade agreement before it could reach a full congressional vote.
   反对派议员有策略地工作，以在提议的贸易协定进入国会全体投票前将其破坏。

3. The frightened mice began to **scuttle** across the laboratory floor when the overhead lights were suddenly switched on.
   当头顶的灯突然打开时，受惊的老鼠开始匆忙地跑过实验室地板。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "scuttle" as "cut-tle" — you CUT holes in the bottom of a ship to scuttle it and send it to the ocean floor.
把"scuttle"想成"cut-tle"（切割）——你在船底切洞来凿沉它，让它沉入海底。
