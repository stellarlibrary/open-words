# 🏋️ corporal

🔊 /ˈkɔːrpərəl/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to the human body; physical rather than spiritual. (n.) A low-ranking noncommissioned military officer
**中文：** adj. 肉体的；身体的 n. 下士

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bodily（身体的）
- physical（肉体的）
- somatic（躯体的）
- corporeal（有形的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **corporal punishment / corporal needs / corporal form / corporal in the army**

👉 Example: Many nations have enacted legislation that explicitly prohibits corporal punishment in schools.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The international convention called for the universal abolition of **corporal** punishment in all educational institutions across member states.
   该国际公约呼吁在所有成员国的教育机构中全面废除体罚。

2. Medieval philosophers devoted extensive debate to the distinction between **corporal** existence and the immortality of the human soul.
   中世纪哲学家对肉体存在与人类灵魂不朽之间的区别进行了大量辩论。

3. The young **corporal** demonstrated exceptional leadership during the military exercise, earning recognition from his commanding officers.
   这位年轻的下士在军事演习中展现了卓越的领导力，赢得了指挥官们的认可。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "corporal" as sharing the root "corp" (Latin corpus = body) — it relates to the physical body, just like a "corporation" is a body of people.
把"corporal"想成与"corp"（拉丁语corpus=身体）同根——它与肉体有关，就像"corporation"是一群人的集合体。
