# 🏭 fabricate

🔊 /ˈfæbrɪkeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To invent or concoct something, typically with deceptive intent; to construct or manufacture, especially from prepared components
**中文：** v. 捏造，编造；建造，制造；装配

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- forge（伪造）
- concoct（编造）
- manufacture（制造）
- falsify（篡改）
- construct（建造）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fabricate evidence / fabricate a story / fabricate components / fabricate an excuse**

👉 Example: The defendant was found guilty of attempting to fabricate evidence to mislead the investigation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journalist was terminated after editors discovered that she had **fabricated** key sources and quotations in multiple articles.
   编辑们发现这位记者在多篇文章中捏造了关键消息来源和引语后将其解雇。

2. Engineers at the facility **fabricate** precision components from titanium alloys for use in advanced aerospace applications.
   该工厂的工程师用钛合金制造精密零部件，用于先进的航空航天应用。

3. The committee determined that the witness had **fabricated** his entire testimony under pressure from external political interests.
   委员会认定该证人在外部政治利益的压力下捏造了全部证词。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fabric-ate" — just as fabric is woven together from threads, to fabricate is to weave together a story or build something from parts.
把"fabricate"想成"fabric（织物）+ ate"——就像织物由线编织而成，fabricate就是将故事编织在一起或从零部件组装东西。
