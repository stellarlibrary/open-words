# 🧠 volition

🔊 /voʊˈlɪʃən/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** The faculty or power of using one's will; a deliberate choice or decision made by one's own will
**中文：** n. 意志；意志力；决断力

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- will（意志）
- determination（决心）
- choice（选择）
- resolve（决意）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **of one's own volition / by volition / free volition / act of volition**

👉 Example: She left the company of her own volition, not because she was asked to resign.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The philosopher argued that true moral responsibility requires **volition**, the conscious ability to choose freely.
   这位哲学家认为，真正的道德责任需要意志，即自由选择的意识能力。

2. He emigrated to another country of his own **volition**, driven by a sincere desire for academic advancement.
   他出于自己的意愿移居到另一个国家，驱动力是对学术进步的真诚渴望。

3. Neuroscientists continue to debate whether human **volition** is genuinely free or merely an illusion of consciousness.
   神经科学家们继续争论人类的意志是否真正自由，还是仅仅是意识的幻觉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "volition" as "vol-" (will, from Latin "voluntas") + "-ition" — the act of willing, making a choice by your own free will.
把"volition"想成"vol-"（意愿，来自拉丁语"voluntas"）+"-ition"——出于自己自由意志做出选择的行为。
