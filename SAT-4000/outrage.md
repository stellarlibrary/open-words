# 😡 outrage

🔊 /ˈaʊtreɪdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An extremely strong reaction of anger or indignation; a cruel or violent act. (v.) To arouse fierce anger or indignation in someone
**中文：** n. 愤怒，愤慨；暴行；侮辱 v. 激怒；对…施暴行

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- indignation（愤慨）
- fury（狂怒）
- atrocity（暴行）
- infuriate（激怒）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **public outrage / express outrage / moral outrage / outrage at/over something**

👉 Example: The politician's remarks sparked widespread outrage among citizens across the country.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The discovery of widespread corruption in the government provoked **outrage** among taxpayers demanding accountability.
   政府大面积腐败的曝光在纳税人中引发了愤慨，他们要求问责。

2. Environmental activists were **outraged** by the corporation's decision to dump industrial waste into the river.
   环保活动人士对该公司将工业废物倾倒入河流的决定感到愤怒。

3. The senator described the policy as an **outrage** against fundamental human rights and constitutional principles.
   该参议员将这项政策描述为对基本人权和宪法原则的暴行。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "out-rage" as "rage that bursts out" — when anger goes beyond limits, it becomes outrage.
把"outrage"想成"rage out"——当愤怒超出了限度爆发出来，就成了极度愤慨。
