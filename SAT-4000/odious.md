# 😤 odious

🔊 /ˈoʊdiəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Extremely unpleasant, repulsive, or deserving hatred; arousing strong feelings of disgust
**中文：** adj. 可憎的；讨厌的；令人作呕的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- detestable（令人厌恶的）
- abhorrent（可恶的）
- repugnant（令人反感的）
- loathsome（可憎的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **odious behavior / odious crime / odious regime / odious comparison / odious task**

👉 Example: The committee condemned the odious practices that had persisted within the organization for decades.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The senator described the proposed legislation as **odious** and fundamentally incompatible with democratic principles and civil liberties.
   这位参议员将拟议的立法描述为令人可憎的，与民主原则和公民自由根本不相容。

2. Historians regard the regime's systematic persecution of minority groups as one of the most **odious** chapters in modern history.
   历史学家认为该政权对少数群体的系统性迫害是现代历史上最可憎的篇章之一。

3. The journalist exposed the **odious** working conditions that factory employees had been forced to endure without legal recourse.
   这位记者揭露了工厂员工被迫忍受的恶劣工作条件，且没有法律救济途径。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "odious" as "Oh, Deus!" — an exclamation of disgust, as if calling on God because something is so hateful and revolting.
把"odious"想成"Oh, Deus!"——一种厌恶的惊叹，仿佛因为某事太可憎而呼唤上帝。
