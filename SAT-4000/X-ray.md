# ☢️ X-ray

🔊 /ˈɛksreɪ/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A form of high-energy electromagnetic radiation capable of penetrating solid objects, used in medical imaging and scientific analysis
**中文：** n. X射线；X光

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- radiograph（射线照片）
- roentgen ray（伦琴射线）
- radiogram（X光片）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **X-ray examination / X-ray image / take an X-ray / X-ray machine / chest X-ray**

👉 Example: The doctor ordered an X-ray to determine whether the patient's wrist was fractured or merely sprained.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The physician examined the **X-ray** carefully and confirmed that the hairline fracture would require six weeks of immobilization.
   医生仔细检查了X光片，确认这条细微骨折需要固定六周。

2. Airport security personnel use advanced **X-ray** technology to screen luggage for prohibited items and potential threats.
   机场安保人员使用先进的X射线技术对行李进行违禁物品和潜在威胁的检查。

3. Wilhelm Roentgen's accidental discovery of the **X-ray** in 1895 revolutionized both medical diagnostics and scientific research.
   威廉·伦琴在1895年意外发现X射线，彻底改变了医学诊断和科学研究。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "X" as the unknown — when X-rays were discovered, they were mysterious rays that could see through solid objects, hence named "X" for unknown.
把"X"想成未知数——X射线被发现时，它们是能穿透固体的神秘射线，因此用代表未知的"X"来命名。
