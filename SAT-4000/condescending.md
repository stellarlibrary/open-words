# 😏 condescending

🔊 /ˌkɒndɪˈsɛndɪŋ/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义

**EN:** Displaying a patronizingly superior attitude toward others; behaving as though one is more important or knowledgeable than those around.

**中文：** 表现出优越感的；居高临下的；故意屈尊的。

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词

- patronizing（以恩人自居的）
- supercilious（高傲的）
- disdainful（轻蔑的）
- haughty（傲慢的）
- imperious（专横的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示

**Collocation:** condescending tone / condescending attitude / condescending manner

**Example:** His **condescending** remarks alienated many of his colleagues during the meeting.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The manager's **condescending** tone during the presentation made several experienced employees feel undervalued and resentful.
   经理在演示过程中居高临下的语气让几位资深员工感到被低估和不满。

2. Critics described the politician's speech as **condescending**, noting that it oversimplified issues facing educated voters.
   批评者形容这位政治家的演讲居高临下，指出它过度简化了受过教育的选民所面临的问题。

3. A truly effective leader avoids adopting a **condescending** manner when addressing subordinates about operational challenges.
   一位真正有效的领导者在向下属讲述运营挑战时会避免采用居高临下的方式。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧

**English:** "Condescending" comes from "condescend" + "-ing" — someone who is always going down to others' level with an air of superiority.

**中文：** "condescending" 由 "condescend"（屈尊）+ "-ing" 构成——一个人总是带着优越感"降低身段"对待别人，即"居高临下的"。
