# 🔥 turmoil

🔊 /ˈtɜːrmɔɪl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** A state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty; violent agitation or disorder
**中文：** 混乱，骚动

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- upheaval（剧变）
- chaos（混乱）
- disorder（无序）
- commotion（骚动）
- turbulence（动荡）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **in turmoil / political turmoil / emotional turmoil / economic turmoil / inner turmoil**

👉 Example: The country was thrown into turmoil after the sudden collapse of its financial system.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The region has been in constant **turmoil** since the disputed election results sparked violent protests across multiple provinces.
   自有争议的选举结果在多个省份引发暴力抗议以来，该地区一直处于持续的动荡之中。

2. Financial **turmoil** in the emerging markets caused investors worldwide to reassess their portfolios and adopt more cautious strategies.
   新兴市场的金融动荡导致全球投资者重新评估其投资组合并采取更为谨慎的策略。

3. The young scientist experienced great inner **turmoil** when asked to choose between her prestigious career and her family obligations.
   当被要求在她有声望的事业和家庭义务之间做出选择时，这位年轻的科学家经历了巨大的内心煎熬。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "turmoil" as "turn + oil" — imagine oil turning and churning violently in a pot, creating a bubbling mess of confusion.
把"turmoil"想成"turn + oil"——想象油在锅中剧烈翻转搅动，形成一片沸腾混乱的场面。
