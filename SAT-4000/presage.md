# 🔮 presage

🔊 /ˈprɛsɪdʒ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A feeling that something is about to happen; an omen or forewarning. (v.) To foretell or predict; to serve as an omen of
**中文：** n. 预感；前兆 v. 预示；预知；预言

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- portend（预示）
- foreshadow（预兆）
- forebode（预感）
- augur（预言）
- omen（征兆）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **presage disaster / presage change / a presage of doom / presage the future**

👉 Example: The sudden drop in stock prices seemed to presage an imminent economic recession.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The unusual seismic activity along the fault line appeared to **presage** a major earthquake in the coming months.
   沿断层线出现的异常地震活动似乎预示着未来几个月将发生一次大地震。

2. Many historians argue that the political unrest of the 1780s clearly **presaged** the revolutionary upheaval that would follow.
   许多历史学家认为，1780年代的政治动荡明显预示了随后的革命剧变。

3. The darkening skies and rising winds served as an ominous **presage** of the devastating storm that struck the coastal region.
   天色变暗和风力增强成为袭击沿海地区的毁灭性风暴的不祥前兆。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-sage" as "a sage who sees before" — a wise person who knows what will happen in advance.
把"presage"想成"pre-sage"——一个能"提前看见"的智者，预知未来的先知。
