# ⏱️ timer

🔊 /ˈtaɪmər/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A device that measures, records, or controls time intervals; a mechanism that activates at a preset time
**中文：** n. 定时器；计时器；计时员

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- chronometer（精密计时器）
- stopwatch（秒表）
- timekeeper（计时员）
- clock（时钟）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **set a timer / egg timer / countdown timer / timer goes off / built-in timer**

👉 Example: She set a timer for twenty minutes to ensure the experiment was conducted precisely.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The laboratory technician relied on a digital **timer** to ensure each chemical reaction was monitored with absolute precision.
   实验室技术员依靠数字计时器来确保每个化学反应都得到精确监控。

2. Productivity experts recommend using a **timer** to divide work into focused intervals separated by brief rest periods.
   生产力专家建议使用计时器将工作分成专注的时间段，中间穿插短暂的休息。

3. The building's sprinkler system was connected to an automatic **timer** that activated irrigation at predetermined intervals.
   大楼的喷水系统连接着一个自动定时器，按预设间隔启动灌溉。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "timer" as "time + er" — the "-er" suffix means "one who does," so a timer is something that does time, measuring it precisely.
把"timer"想成"time（时间）+ er（做……的东西）"——后缀"-er"表示"执行者"，所以timer就是计量时间的东西。
