# 📚 fable

🔊 /ˈfeɪbl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A short story, typically with animals as characters, conveying a moral; an untrue or fabricated account
**中文：** n. 寓言，神话；谎言，无稽之谈

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- parable（寓言）
- allegory（寓言故事）
- myth（神话）
- legend（传说）
- tale（故事）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **Aesop's fables / a moral fable / fable about / tell a fable / old fable**

👉 Example: The teacher read a classic fable about a tortoise and a hare to illustrate the value of perseverance.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Aesop's **fables** have endured for centuries, offering timeless moral lessons through deceptively simple animal narratives.
   伊索寓言历经数百年而不衰，通过看似简单的动物故事提供了永恒的道德教训。

2. The politician's account of events was dismissed by critics as nothing more than an elaborate **fable** lacking evidence.
   批评者认为该政客对事件的叙述不过是一个没有证据支持的精心编造的谎言。

3. In many cultures, **fables** serve as essential pedagogical tools for transmitting ethical values to younger generations.
   在许多文化中，寓言是向年轻一代传递伦理价值观的重要教育工具。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fable" as related to "fabulous" and "fabricate" — all come from Latin "fabula" meaning story, so a fable is a crafted story with a lesson.
把"fable"和"fabulous""fabricate"联系起来——它们都源自拉丁语"fabula"，意为故事，所以fable是一个精心编排的有教训的故事。
