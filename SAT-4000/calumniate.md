# 🗣️ calumniate

🔊 /kəˈlʌmnieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To make false and damaging statements about someone; to slander or defame
**中文：** v. 诽谤；中伤；诬蔑

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- slander（诽谤）
- defame（中伤）
- malign（诋毁）
- vilify（诬蔑）
- libel（诽谤）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **calumniate someone's character / calumniate a rival / calumniate publicly / calumniate without evidence**

👉 Example: Political opponents often calumniate each other's reputations during heated election campaigns.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The disgraced journalist sought to **calumniate** the whistleblower by fabricating evidence of financial impropriety and corruption.
   这位名誉扫地的记者试图通过捏造财务不当和腐败证据来诽谤举报人。

2. Throughout history, authoritarian regimes have routinely **calumniated** dissidents to undermine their credibility and silence opposition.
   纵观历史，专制政权经常诽谤异见人士以削弱其可信度并压制反对声音。

3. The defendant filed a countersuit, arguing that the plaintiff had deliberately **calumniated** him with baseless and malicious accusations.
   被告提起反诉，称原告蓄意以毫无根据的恶意指控诽谤他。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "calumniate" as "call-umn-iate" — to "call" someone names in a "column" (newspaper), spreading false accusations publicly.
把"calumniate"想成"call-umn-iate"——在报纸"专栏"（column）中"叫骂"（call）别人，公开散布不实指控。
