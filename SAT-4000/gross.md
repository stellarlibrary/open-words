# 📊 gross

🔊 /ɡroʊs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Total or aggregate, before any deductions; extremely obvious or flagrant; coarse or vulgar. (n.) The total amount or sum
**中文：** adj. 总共的；毛的；粗野的；恶劣的 n. 总额

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- total（总计的）
- aggregate（合计的）
- flagrant（公然的）
- blatant（明目张胆的）
- vulgar（粗俗的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **gross income / gross domestic product / gross negligence / gross misconduct / gross profit**

👉 Example: The company's gross revenue exceeded expectations, but net profit declined due to rising operational costs.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The auditor calculated the firm's **gross** earnings before deducting taxes, expenses, and other mandatory financial obligations.
   审计员在扣除税款、费用和其他强制性财务义务之前计算了公司的总收入。

2. The investigation revealed **gross** negligence on the part of the safety inspectors who failed to report critical structural defects.
   调查揭示了安全检查员的严重疏忽，他们未能报告关键的结构缺陷。

3. The nation's **gross** domestic product grew by three percent, signaling a modest but encouraging economic recovery this quarter.
   该国国内生产总值增长了百分之三，标志着本季度出现了温和但令人鼓舞的经济复苏。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "gross" as "the big total before anything is taken away" — gross weight includes packaging, gross income includes taxes.
把"gross"想成"在任何东西被扣除之前的总量"——毛重包括包装，总收入包括税款。
