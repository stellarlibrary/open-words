# 🏛️ lobby

🔊 /ˈlɑːbi/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A large entrance hall or waiting area; a group seeking to influence legislators. (v.) To try to influence a politician or official on an issue
**中文：** n. 大厅；休息室；游说团体 v. 对……进行游说

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- foyer（门厅）
- vestibule（前厅）
- advocate（倡导）
- campaign（游说活动）
- petition（请愿）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **lobby for / lobby against / lobby group / hotel lobby / lobby Congress**

👉 Example: Environmental organizations lobbied Congress aggressively for stricter emissions regulations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The pharmaceutical industry spent millions to **lobby** lawmakers against implementing price controls on prescription medications.
   制药行业花费数百万美元游说立法者反对对处方药实施价格管控。

2. Delegates gathered in the hotel **lobby** before proceeding to the conference hall for the opening ceremony of the summit.
   代表们在前往会议厅参加峰会开幕式之前聚集在酒店大厅。

3. Citizens organized a grassroots effort to **lobby** their representatives for increased funding to public education programs.
   公民们组织了一场草根运动，游说他们的议员增加对公共教育项目的拨款。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "lobby" as the place where people wait and talk — historically, lobbyists waited in the lobby of Parliament to persuade officials.
把"lobby"想成人们等待和交谈的地方——历史上，游说者在议会大厅等候，说服官员。
