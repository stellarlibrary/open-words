# 🧱 solid

🔊 /ˈsɒlɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having a firm, stable form; reliable and dependable; three-dimensional; strongly built; unanimous. (n.) A substance or object that is solid; a three-dimensional body
**中文：** adj. 固体的；坚硬的 n. 固体

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- firm（坚固的）
- sturdy（结实的）
- reliable（可靠的）
- substantial（坚实的）
- dense（致密的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **solid foundation / solid evidence / solid ground / solid state / solid structure**

👉 Example: The researchers needed solid evidence before publishing their findings in a peer-reviewed journal.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The engineering team constructed a **solid** framework capable of withstanding extreme seismic activity in the coastal region.
   工程团队建造了一个坚固的框架，能够承受沿海地区的极端地震活动。

2. Water transitions from a liquid to a **solid** state when the ambient temperature falls below zero degrees Celsius.
   当环境温度降至零摄氏度以下时，水从液态转变为固态。

3. The committee reached a **solid** consensus on the proposed reforms after extensive deliberation spanning several weeks.
   委员会经过长达数周的广泛审议后，就拟议的改革达成了一致共识。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "solid" as "sol-id" — like the solid ground (sol means ground in Latin) that gives you a firm identity to stand on.
把"solid"想成"sol-id"——就像坚实的地面（sol在拉丁语中意为地面），给你一个坚定的立足之地。
