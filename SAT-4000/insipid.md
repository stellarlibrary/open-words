# 😶 insipid

🔊 /ɪnˈsɪpɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Lacking flavor, vigor, or interest; dull and uninspiring
**中文：** adj. 清淡的；无趣的；乏味的；无情趣的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- bland（平淡的）
- vapid（无趣的）
- dull（沉闷的）
- tasteless（无味的）
- uninspiring（无启发性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **insipid taste / insipid conversation / insipid performance / insipid prose**

👉 Example: The novel's insipid dialogue failed to capture the emotional depth of the characters.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics dismissed the film as an **insipid** adaptation that stripped away all the complexity and nuance of the original novel.
   评论家将这部电影斥为一部乏味的改编作品，剥去了原著的所有复杂性和细微之处。

2. The once-celebrated chef received harsh reviews after serving an **insipid** menu that lacked both creativity and seasoning.
   这位曾经备受赞誉的厨师在端上一份缺乏创意和调味的清淡菜单后收到了严厉的评价。

3. The candidate's **insipid** campaign speeches failed to energize voters or distinguish her platform from her opponent's proposals.
   这位候选人乏味的竞选演讲未能激励选民，也未能使她的政纲区别于对手的提案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in + sipid" (from Latin "sapidus," tasty) — not tasty, without flavor, and by extension, without interest or excitement.
把"insipid"想成"in + sipid"（源自拉丁语"sapidus"，有味道的）——没有味道的，进而引申为没有趣味或缺乏吸引力的。
