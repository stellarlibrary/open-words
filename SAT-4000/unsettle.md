# 😰 unsettle

🔊 /ʌnˈsɛtl/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To cause to feel anxious or uneasy; to disturb the composure or stability of; to make uncertain or unstable
**中文：** 使动摇；使不安定；使心神不宁

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disturb（使不安）
- disconcert（使惊慌）
- unnerve（使紧张）
- destabilize（使不稳定）
- agitate（使焦虑）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **unsettle the market / unsettle someone's nerves / unsettle expectations / unsettling news**

👉 Example: The sudden policy reversal unsettled investors and caused a sharp decline in market confidence.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The unexpected resignation of the chief executive served to **unsettle** the entire organization during a critical transition period.
   首席执行官的意外辞职在关键过渡期使整个组织感到不安。

2. Contradictory findings from the latest clinical trials began to **unsettle** the scientific community's confidence in the treatment.
   最新临床试验中相互矛盾的结果开始动摇科学界对该疗法的信心。

3. The eerie silence that pervaded the abandoned village was enough to **unsettle** even the most experienced members of the expedition.
   弥漫在废弃村庄中的令人毛骨悚然的寂静足以让探险队中最有经验的成员也感到不安。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "un-settle" as undoing something that was settled — when you unsettle someone, you take away their sense of being settled, calm, and secure.
把"unsettle"想成"un（取消）+ settle（安定）"——当你unsettle某人时，你夺走了他们安定、平静和安全的感觉。
