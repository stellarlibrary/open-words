# 🧠 judicious

🔊 /dʒuːˈdɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Having, showing, or done with good judgment or sense; prudent and wise in practical matters
**中文：** adj. 明智的；审慎的；头脑精明的；判断正确的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- prudent（审慎的）
- wise（明智的）
- discerning（有辨别力的）
- sagacious（睿智的）
- sensible（明理的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **judicious use of / judicious decision / judicious choice / judicious approach**

👉 Example: A judicious use of limited resources can make the difference between project success and failure.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee's **judicious** allocation of the emergency funds ensured that the most vulnerable communities received assistance first.
   委员会对紧急资金的明智分配确保了最脆弱的社区首先获得援助。

2. Through **judicious** planning and careful negotiation, the diplomat was able to avert a potentially catastrophic international conflict.
   通过审慎的规划和谨慎的谈判，这位外交官成功避免了一场可能灾难性的国际冲突。

3. A **judicious** selection of primary sources is essential for any scholar seeking to produce credible and rigorous historical analysis.
   对原始资料的明智选择对于任何希望进行可信而严谨的历史分析的学者来说都至关重要。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "judicious" as "judge + icious" — like a wise judge who makes careful, well-reasoned decisions based on evidence.
把"judicious"想成"judge（法官）+ icious"——就像一位明智的法官，根据证据做出谨慎、合理的判断。
