# 🚫 refusal

🔊 /rɪˈfjuːzəl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An act of refusing or declining to do, accept, or allow something; the right to accept or reject before others.
**中文：** n. 拒绝；推却；优先取舍权

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- rejection（拒绝）
- denial（否认）
- decline（谢绝）
- rebuff（回绝）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **flat refusal / outright refusal / refusal to comply / first refusal**

👉 Example: Her firm refusal to compromise on ethical standards earned her the respect of colleagues.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee's **refusal** to approve the proposed budget led to a prolonged deadlock between the legislative branches.
   委员会拒绝批准拟议预算，导致立法机构之间陷入了长期僵局。

2. His persistent **refusal** to acknowledge the scientific evidence undermined his credibility among academic peers and researchers.
   他一再拒绝承认科学证据，损害了他在学术同行和研究人员中的信誉。

3. The tenant was granted first **refusal** on the property, giving her priority to purchase before any outside buyers.
   租户获得了该物业的优先购买权，使她有权在任何外部买家之前优先购买。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "refusal" as "refuse + al" — the noun form of "refuse," meaning the act of saying no or turning something down.
把"refusal"想成"refuse + al"——"refuse"的名词形式，表示说不或拒绝某事的行为。
