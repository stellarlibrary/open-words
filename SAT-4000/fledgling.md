# 🐣 fledgling

🔊 /ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ/<br>
🏷️ *noun, adjective | 名词, 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A young bird that has just grown its feathers and learned to fly; a person who is new and inexperienced. (adj.) New and inexperienced
**中文：** n. 刚会飞的幼鸟；无经验的人 adj. 新生的；无经验的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- novice（新手）
- beginner（初学者）
- neophyte（新入门者）
- newcomer（新来者）
- rookie（新人）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fledgling company / fledgling democracy / fledgling artist / fledgling industry / fledgling career**

👉 Example: The fledgling startup struggled to secure funding during its first year of operation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **fledgling** democracy faced enormous challenges as it attempted to establish stable institutions and a fair judicial system.
   这个新生的民主国家在试图建立稳定的制度和公正的司法体系时面临着巨大挑战。

2. As a **fledgling** researcher, she sought guidance from senior professors who could mentor her through the dissertation process.
   作为一名初出茅庐的研究人员，她寻求资深教授的指导以帮助她完成论文过程。

3. The nature documentary captured the remarkable moment when a **fledgling** eagle took its first flight from the mountainside nest.
   这部自然纪录片捕捉到了一只雏鹰从山腰巢穴中进行首次飞行的非凡时刻。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fledgling" as "fled-gling" — a little bird that just fled the nest for the first time, still young and inexperienced.
把"fledgling"想成"fled（逃离）+ ling（小东西）"——一只刚逃离巢穴的小鸟，还很年幼且没有经验。
