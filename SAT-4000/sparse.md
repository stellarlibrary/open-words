# 🌵 sparse

🔊 /spɑːrs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Thinly scattered or distributed; not dense; occurring at widely spaced intervals; meager in quantity
**中文：** adj. 稀少的，稀疏的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- scanty（稀少的）
- meager（贫乏的）
- scant（不足的）
- scattered（分散的）
- thin（稀薄的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **sparse population / sparse vegetation / sparse data / sparse evidence**

👉 Example: The sparse vegetation in the desert region made it difficult for any large animal population to survive.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **sparse** population of the remote highland region posed significant challenges for the government's infrastructure development plan.
   偏远高原地区稀少的人口给政府的基础设施发展计划带来了重大挑战。

2. Researchers noted that the **sparse** evidence available was insufficient to draw any definitive conclusions about the phenomenon.
   研究人员指出，现有的稀少证据不足以对这一现象得出任何确定性结论。

3. The landscape was covered with **sparse** shrubs and dry grasses, offering little shelter from the relentless midday sun.
   地面上覆盖着稀疏的灌木和干草，几乎无法遮挡正午无情的烈日。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sparse" as "space" with an "r" — when things are sparse, there is lots of space between them.
把"sparse"想成带"r"的"space"——当事物稀疏时，它们之间有很多空间。
