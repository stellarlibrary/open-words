# 📋 substantive

🔊 /ˈsʌbstəntɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having a firm basis in reality; meaningful and important; existing independently. (n.) A word functioning as a noun
**中文：** adj. 独立存在的；真实的，有实质的；大量的，巨额的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- meaningful（有意义的）
- significant（重大的）
- essential（本质的）
- tangible（实质的）
- material（重要的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **substantive changes / substantive issues / substantive progress / substantive discussion**

👉 Example: The negotiations failed to produce any substantive agreement on the key issues separating the two parties.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee demanded **substantive** reforms rather than superficial adjustments that would leave the core problems unresolved.
   委员会要求进行实质性改革，而不是让核心问题悬而未决的表面调整。

2. Critics dismissed the speech as lacking **substantive** content, noting that it offered no concrete proposals or actionable plans.
   批评人士认为该演讲缺乏实质性内容，指出它没有提出具体的提案或可行的计划。

3. The two leaders engaged in **substantive** dialogue that addressed longstanding territorial disputes and trade disagreements directly.
   两位领导人进行了实质性对话，直接处理了长期存在的领土争端和贸易分歧。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "substantive" as "full of substance" — something substantive has real weight, depth, and independent significance.
把"substantive"想成"充满实质"——substantive的事物具有真正的分量、深度和独立的重要性。
