# ⚖️ discrepant

🔊 /dɪˈskrepənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Not in agreement or harmony; showing a difference or inconsistency between two or more facts or claims
**中文：** adj. 有差异的，矛盾的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- inconsistent（不一致的）
- contradictory（矛盾的）
- conflicting（冲突的）
- divergent（分歧的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **discrepant results / discrepant accounts / discrepant findings / discrepant data**

👉 Example: The auditor flagged the discrepant figures in the quarterly financial report for further review.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The researchers obtained **discrepant** results when they attempted to replicate the original experiment under slightly different laboratory conditions.
   研究人员在稍有不同的实验室条件下试图重复原始实验时，得到了不一致的结果。

2. The two witnesses provided **discrepant** accounts of the incident, making it difficult for the jury to determine what actually occurred.
   两名证人对事件的描述存在矛盾，使陪审团难以确定实际发生了什么。

3. The committee noted several **discrepant** entries in the budget proposal that required immediate clarification from the finance department.
   委员会注意到预算提案中有几处差异条目，需要财务部门立即作出说明。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "dis-crep-ant" — "dis" means apart, and "crepant" sounds like "creep apart," things that creep apart become different and inconsistent.
把"discrepant"想成"dis-crep-ant"——"dis"表示分离，"crep"联想到"creep apart"（悄悄分开），悄悄分开的事物就变得不一致了。
