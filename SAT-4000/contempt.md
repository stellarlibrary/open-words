# 😤 contempt

🔊 /kənˈtɛmpt/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The feeling that a person or thing is beneath consideration, worthless, or deserving scorn; disregard for something regarded as unworthy
**中文：** n. 轻视；蔑视；耻辱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- disdain（鄙视）
- scorn（蔑视）
- derision（嘲笑）
- disrespect（不敬）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **contempt for / hold in contempt / beneath contempt / contempt of court / treat with contempt**

👉 Example: The judge held the witness in contempt for refusing to answer critical questions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The dictator showed open **contempt** for democratic institutions and systematically undermined the independence of the judiciary.
   这位独裁者公然蔑视民主制度，并系统性地破坏司法独立。

2. Her **contempt** for dishonesty was well known, and she never hesitated to confront those who bent the truth.
   她对不诚实的轻蔑众所周知，她从不犹豫地质问那些歪曲事实的人。

3. The attorney was fined for **contempt** of court after repeatedly interrupting the proceedings with unsubstantiated objections.
   这位律师因反复以毫无根据的异议打断诉讼程序而被判藐视法庭罪并处以罚款。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "con-TEMPT" — someone who cons and tempts others deserves your contempt, your complete scorn and disdain.
把"contempt"想成"con（欺骗）+ tempt（引诱）"——一个既欺骗又引诱他人的人值得你的蔑视。
