# 🎵 prelude

🔊 /ˈprɛljuːd/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An introductory event or action preceding something more important; a short piece of music serving as an introduction. (v.) To serve as a prelude or introduction to
**中文：** n. 先驱；前奏，序幕；前奏曲 v. 成为…的序幕

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- overture（序曲）
- prologue（序幕）
- preamble（前言）
- precursor（先驱）
- introduction（引言）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **prelude to / serve as a prelude / a brief prelude / prelude to war**

👉 Example: The diplomatic tensions of the 1930s served as a grim prelude to the outbreak of the Second World War.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The minor border skirmishes proved to be merely a **prelude** to the full-scale military conflict that erupted months later.
   小规模的边境冲突只不过是几个月后爆发的全面军事冲突的序幕。

2. The pianist performed a hauntingly beautiful **prelude** by Chopin before the orchestra commenced the main symphonic movement.
   钢琴家在管弦乐队开始主要交响乐章之前，演奏了一首肖邦令人难忘的优美前奏曲。

3. The chairman's opening remarks served as a **prelude** to the announcement of the company's ambitious new five-year strategic plan.
   董事长的开场致辞是公司宣布雄心勃勃的新五年战略计划的序幕。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-lude" as "pre-play" (from Latin "ludere" meaning to play) — the music or event that plays before the main show begins.
把"prelude"想成"pre（之前）+ lude（演奏，源自拉丁语ludere）"——在正式演出开始前演奏的音乐或发生的事件。
