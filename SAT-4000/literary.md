# 📖 literary

🔊 /ˈlɪtərɛri/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Relating to or concerned with literature; characteristic of formal written works rather than everyday speech
**中文：** adj. 文学的；文学上的；书面的；精通文学的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- scholarly（学术的）
- bookish（书卷气的）
- erudite（博学的）
- lettered（有学问的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **literary criticism / literary works / literary genre / literary device / literary analysis**

👉 Example: The course focused on literary analysis of nineteenth-century British novels and their social context.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The journal publishes **literary** criticism that examines how contemporary authors address themes of identity and cultural displacement.
   该期刊发表文学批评文章，探讨当代作家如何处理身份认同和文化流离的主题。

2. Her use of elaborate metaphor and symbolism revealed a distinctly **literary** style that distinguished her from other journalists.
   她对精致隐喻和象征手法的运用展现了一种鲜明的文学风格，使她有别于其他记者。

3. The professor assigned several **literary** essays that required students to analyze narrative techniques across multiple historical periods.
   教授布置了几篇文学论文，要求学生分析跨越多个历史时期的叙事技巧。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "literary" as "liter-ary" — pertaining to "literature," the world of written artistic expression and scholarly works.
把"literary"想成与"literature（文学）"相关——属于书面艺术表达和学术著作的世界。
