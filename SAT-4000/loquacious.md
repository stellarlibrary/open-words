# 🗣️ loquacious

🔊 /ləˈkweɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Tending to talk a great deal; very talkative; characterized by excessive or rambling speech
**中文：** adj. 饶舌的，多话的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- talkative（健谈的）
- garrulous（唠叨的）
- verbose（冗长的）
- voluble（滔滔不绝的）
- chatty（爱闲聊的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **loquacious speaker / loquacious personality / unusually loquacious / loquacious nature**

👉 Example: The loquacious guest dominated the dinner conversation, barely allowing anyone else to speak.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The normally reserved professor became surprisingly **loquacious** whenever the discussion turned to his favorite area of Renaissance art history.
   这位通常沉默寡言的教授每当讨论转向他最喜爱的文艺复兴艺术史领域时，就变得出奇地健谈。

2. Her **loquacious** tendencies during meetings often caused deliberations to extend well beyond their scheduled time and agenda.
   她在会议中多话的倾向常常导致讨论远远超出预定的时间和议程。

3. While some found his **loquacious** manner charming, others considered it exhausting and wished he would exercise more brevity.
   虽然有些人觉得他健谈的方式很有魅力，但另一些人觉得很累人，希望他能更简洁一些。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "loqu-acious" — "loqu" comes from Latin "loqui" meaning "to speak," so loquacious means someone who speaks a lot.
把"loquacious"想成"loqu（说话）+ acious（充满的）"——"loqu"来自拉丁语"loqui（说话）"，所以loquacious就是话多的人。
