# 🕳️ loophole

🔊 /ˈluːphəʊl/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** An ambiguity or inadequacy in a law or set of rules that allows one to avoid compliance; a narrow opening in a wall, especially for shooting through
**中文：** n. 漏洞；枪眼；观察孔

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- gap（缺口）
- flaw（缺陷）
- weakness（弱点）
- escape clause（免责条款）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **legal loophole / tax loophole / exploit a loophole / close a loophole / find a loophole**

👉 Example: The corporation exploited a tax loophole to avoid paying millions in federal taxes.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Legislative analysts discovered a critical **loophole** in the new regulation that could allow corporations to circumvent environmental protections.
   立法分析人员在新法规中发现了一个关键漏洞，可能允许企业规避环境保护规定。

2. The government pledged to close every **loophole** in the tax code that permitted wealthy individuals to shelter income offshore.
   政府承诺堵住税法中允许富人将收入转移到海外避税的每一个漏洞。

3. Defense attorneys identified a procedural **loophole** that ultimately led to the dismissal of all charges against the defendant.
   辩护律师发现了一个程序漏洞，最终导致对被告的所有指控被撤销。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "loop-hole" — a hole in a loop of rules that you can slip through to escape restrictions.
把"loophole"想成"loop（环）+ hole（洞）"——规则之环上的一个洞，你可以从中溜出去逃避限制。
