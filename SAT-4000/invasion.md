# ⚔️ invasion

🔊 /ɪnˈveɪʒn/<br>
🏷️ *noun | 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) An instance of invading a country or region with armed force; an unwelcome intrusion into another's domain or privacy
**中文：** n. 入侵；侵略；侵犯；侵袭

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- incursion（侵入）
- intrusion（侵犯）
- encroachment（侵占）
- onslaught（猛攻）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **military invasion / invasion of privacy / invasion of territory / launch an invasion / repel an invasion**

👉 Example: The country mobilized its armed forces to repel the sudden invasion from the north.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **invasion** of Normandy in 1944 marked a decisive turning point in the Allied campaign during the Second World War.
   1944年的诺曼底登陆标志着第二次世界大战中盟军战役的决定性转折点。

2. Many citizens protested that the new surveillance legislation constituted an unacceptable **invasion** of their fundamental privacy rights.
   许多公民抗议称，新的监控立法构成了对其基本隐私权不可接受的侵犯。

3. The **invasion** of non-native species has severely disrupted the ecological balance of several freshwater lake ecosystems in the region.
   外来物种的入侵严重破坏了该地区几个淡水湖泊生态系统的生态平衡。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-vasion" as "in + vade (to go)" — an invasion is when forces go into a territory uninvited.
把"invasion"想成"in（进入）+ vasion（走）"——invasion就是军队未经邀请进入一个领土。
