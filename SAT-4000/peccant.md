# ⚖️ peccant

🔊 /ˈpekənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Guilty of sin or moral offense; violating a rule or principle; corrupt or depraved
**中文：** adj. 犯罪的；有过失的；堕落的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- sinful（有罪的）
- transgressing（犯戒的）
- erring（犯错的）
- corrupt（堕落的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **peccant behavior / peccant individual / peccant nature / peccant act**

👉 Example: The tribunal condemned the official's peccant conduct and imposed severe disciplinary sanctions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The religious council denounced the **peccant** behavior of those who violated the sacred traditions of the community.
   宗教委员会谴责了那些违反社区神圣传统之人的犯罪行为。

2. Medieval theologians devoted extensive treatises to analyzing the **peccant** nature of humanity and the path to redemption.
   中世纪的神学家撰写了大量论著来分析人类堕落的本性以及救赎之路。

3. The judge reminded the court that even the most **peccant** offender deserves a fair trial under the principles of justice.
   法官提醒法庭，即使是最有过失的罪犯也有权在公正原则下接受公平审判。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "peccant" as related to "peccadillo" — both come from Latin "peccare" (to sin). A peccant person is one who sins.
把"peccant"和"peccadillo"联系起来——两者都源自拉丁语"peccare"（犯罪）。peccant的人就是犯了罪的人。
