# 🔭 telescope

🔊 /ˈtɛlɪskoʊp/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To compress or condense; to slide together in overlapping sections. (n.) An optical instrument designed for viewing distant objects
**中文：** v. 压缩；使套叠 n. 望远镜

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- compress（压缩）
- condense（浓缩）
- abbreviate（缩短）
- spyglass（小望远镜）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **telescope into / through a telescope / telescope a schedule / radio telescope**

👉 Example: The editor asked the writer to telescope three chapters into one concise summary.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Astronomers used a powerful **telescope** to observe the distant galaxy and document previously undetected stellar formations.
   天文学家使用强大的望远镜观察遥远的星系，并记录之前未被探测到的恒星结构。

2. Budget constraints forced the committee to **telescope** the five-year development plan into a much shorter three-year timeline.
   预算限制迫使委员会将五年发展计划压缩为更短的三年时间表。

3. The sections of the portable antenna **telescope** neatly into one another, making the device remarkably easy to transport.
   便携式天线的各部分整齐地套叠在一起，使该设备非常便于运输。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "tele-scope" as "tele" (far) + "scope" (to see) — an instrument to see far away; the verb sense comes from how telescope tubes slide into each other, compressing.
把"telescope"想成"tele"（远）+ "scope"（看）——一种看远处的仪器；动词含义来自望远镜管彼此滑入套叠、压缩的动作。
