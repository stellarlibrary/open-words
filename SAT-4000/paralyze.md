# 🧊 paralyze

🔊 /ˈpærəˌlaɪz/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To cause loss of movement or sensation in part or all of the body; to render powerless or unable to function
**中文：** v. 使麻痹；使瘫痪

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- immobilize（使不动）
- incapacitate（使丧失能力）
- disable（使无能力）
- cripple（使残废）
- stun（使震惊）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **paralyze with fear / paralyze the economy / paralyze decision-making / paralyzed by indecision**

👉 Example: The sudden blizzard paralyzed the entire transportation network of the metropolitan area.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The cyberattack managed to **paralyze** the nation's banking infrastructure, leaving millions of customers without access to their accounts.
   网络攻击成功瘫痪了国家银行基础设施，导致数百万客户无法访问其账户。

2. Fear of failure can **paralyze** even the most talented individuals, preventing them from pursuing ambitious goals and opportunities.
   对失败的恐惧甚至能使最有才华的人瘫痪，阻碍他们追求远大目标和机遇。

3. The severe winter storm **paralyzed** the city for three consecutive days, shutting down schools, businesses, and all public transit.
   严重的冬季暴风雪使这座城市瘫痪了连续三天，学校、企业和所有公共交通全部关闭。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "paralyze" as "para" (beside) + "lyze" (loosen) — something that loosens your control, making you unable to act or move.
把"paralyze"想成"para"（旁边）+ "lyze"（松弛）——使你失去控制，让你无法行动或移动。
