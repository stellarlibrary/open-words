# 😣 humiliate

🔊 /hjuːˈmɪlieɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To make someone feel ashamed, foolish, or disgraced, especially publicly; to injure someone's dignity or pride
**中文：** vt. 使屈辱；使丢脸；羞辱

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- embarrass（使尴尬）
- disgrace（使蒙羞）
- degrade（贬低）
- mortify（使羞愧）
- demean（贬损）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **humiliate someone publicly / humiliate an opponent / be humiliated by / feel humiliated**

👉 Example: The manager's decision to criticize her in front of the entire team was intended to humiliate her.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The ruling party sought to **humiliate** the opposition leader by releasing carefully edited recordings of private conversations.
   执政党试图通过发布经过精心剪辑的私人对话录音来羞辱反对派领袖。

2. History demonstrates that attempts to **humiliate** a defeated nation often sow the seeds for future conflict and resentment.
   历史表明，羞辱一个战败国家的企图往往会为未来的冲突和怨恨埋下种子。

3. The brilliant student refused to **humiliate** her classmates by boasting about her consistently superior academic performance.
   这位优秀的学生拒绝通过炫耀自己一贯优异的学业成绩来使同学们难堪。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "humiliate" as "humili + ate" — it "ate" away at someone's pride, leaving them feeling humbled and ashamed.
把"humiliate"想成"humili + ate"——它"吞噬"了某人的自尊，让他们感到卑微和羞愧。
