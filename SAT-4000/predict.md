# 🔮 predict

🔊 /prɪˈdɪkt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To declare or indicate in advance; to foretell a future event or outcome based on evidence or reasoning
**中文：** v. 预报；预言；预知；预测

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- forecast（预测）
- foretell（预言）
- anticipate（预期）
- prognosticate（预言）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **predict the outcome / predict that / difficult to predict / accurately predict**

👉 Example: Analysts attempted to predict the impact of the new policy on international trade relations.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Climate scientists use sophisticated computer models to **predict** long-term changes in global temperature and precipitation patterns.
   气候科学家使用复杂的计算机模型来预测全球温度和降水模式的长期变化。

2. No economist could accurately **predict** the severity of the financial crisis that would devastate markets worldwide.
   没有经济学家能够准确预测将摧毁全球市场的金融危机的严重程度。

3. The study aimed to **predict** which environmental factors most significantly influence species migration across temperate regions.
   这项研究旨在预测哪些环境因素对温带地区物种迁徙的影响最大。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "pre-dict" as "pre (before) + dict (say)" — to say something before it happens, like telling the future.
把"predict"想成"pre（之前）+ dict（说）"——在事情发生之前就说出来，就像预言未来。
