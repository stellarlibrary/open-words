# 🤝 indebted

🔊 /ɪnˈdɛtɪd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Owing money to someone; feeling grateful to someone for their help, kindness, or generosity
**中文：** adj. 负债的；感激的；受惠的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- grateful（感激的）
- obliged（感恩的）
- beholden（蒙恩的）
- thankful（感谢的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **deeply indebted / indebted to someone / forever indebted / heavily indebted**

👉 Example: The young scholar felt deeply indebted to her mentor for years of guidance and support.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The small nation remained heavily **indebted** to international financial institutions long after the economic crisis had officially ended.
   这个小国在经济危机正式结束后很长时间内仍然严重负债于国际金融机构。

2. The author acknowledged in her preface that she was profoundly **indebted** to the archivists who preserved those rare manuscripts.
   作者在序言中承认她深深感激那些保存了这些珍贵手稿的档案管理员。

3. Many developing countries find themselves perpetually **indebted**, creating a cycle of dependency that hinders sustainable economic growth.
   许多发展中国家发现自己永远处于负债状态，形成了阻碍可持续经济增长的依赖循环。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "in-debt-ed" — the word literally contains "debt," so someone indebted is either in financial debt or in an emotional debt of gratitude.
把"indebted"拆开——这个词本身就含有"debt"（债务），所以indebted的人要么有经济债务，要么有感恩之情的"情债"。
