# 🕯️ sacrifice

🔊 /ˈsækrɪfaɪs/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) The act of giving up something valued for the sake of something more important; an offering to a deity. (v.) To give up something precious; to offer as a religious offering; to sell at a loss
**中文：** n. 牺牲；供奉

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- offering（供奉）
- surrender（放弃）
- forfeiture（丧失）
- immolation（献祭）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **make a sacrifice / sacrifice for the greater good / sacrifice one's career / at the sacrifice of**

👉 Example: Many soldiers made the ultimate sacrifice to defend their nation's sovereignty during the prolonged conflict.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The dedicated scientist was willing to **sacrifice** years of personal comfort to pursue groundbreaking research in the laboratory.
   这位敬业的科学家愿意牺牲多年的个人舒适，在实验室中从事开创性的研究。

2. Ancient civilizations frequently offered elaborate **sacrifices** to appease their gods and ensure bountiful agricultural harvests.
   古代文明经常举行隆重的祭祀活动来安抚神灵，确保农业丰收。

3. The decision to **sacrifice** short-term profits for long-term sustainability ultimately proved to be a wise strategy.
   为长期可持续发展而牺牲短期利润的决定最终被证明是一项明智的策略。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "sacri-fice" — "sacr" means "sacred" and "fice" sounds like "offer," so sacrifice means to make a sacred offering by giving something up.
把"sacrifice"拆解为"sacr（神圣的）+fice（做）"——为了神圣的目的而做出奉献，即牺牲。
