# 😞 aggrieve

🔊 /əˈɡriːv/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** To cause grief or distress to; to offend or infringe upon someone's legal rights.
**中文：** 使悲痛；冒犯；侵害…的合法权利

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- wrong（冤枉）
- distress（使痛苦）
- afflict（折磨）
- oppress（压迫）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **aggrieved party, aggrieved by, feel aggrieved**
👉 Example: The aggrieved employees filed a formal complaint against the corporation.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The unfair policy deeply **aggrieved** the workers who had served the company for decades.
   不公平的政策深深伤害了那些为公司服务了数十年的员工。

2. Citizens who feel **aggrieved** by government decisions may seek redress through the courts.
   对政府决定感到不满的公民可以通过法院寻求补救。

3. The landlord's unlawful actions **aggrieved** the tenants, prompting them to consult an attorney.
   房东的违法行为侵害了租户的权利，促使他们咨询律师。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "ag-GRIEVE" — it contains "grieve" (悲伤), so to aggrieve someone is to cause them grief.
联想"ag-GRIEVE"——其中包含"grieve"（悲伤），所以aggrieve就是让人悲伤、受委屈。
