# ⚖️ biased

🔊 /ˈbaɪəst/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Showing an unfair tendency to favor one thing over another; prejudiced; producing skewed or unrepresentative results
**中文：** 有偏见的；结果偏倚的，有偏的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- prejudiced（有偏见的）
- partial（偏袒的）
- one-sided（片面的）
- skewed（偏斜的）
- slanted（有倾向性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **biased against / biased toward / biased sample / biased opinion / culturally biased**

👉 Example: The study's findings were questioned because the researchers used a biased sample of participants.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. Critics argued that the media coverage was deeply **biased** in favor of the incumbent candidate throughout the election.
   批评人士认为，在整个选举期间，媒体报道严重偏袒在任候选人。

2. A **biased** sample in a clinical trial can lead to fundamentally flawed conclusions and undermine scientific credibility.
   临床试验中有偏的样本可能导致根本性的错误结论，并损害科学可信度。

3. The committee acknowledged that the selection process had been **biased** against applicants from underrepresented communities.
   委员会承认遴选过程对来自代表性不足社区的申请者存在偏见。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "bias" as a weight on one side of a scale — when something is biased, the scale tips unfairly to one direction.
把"bias"想象成天平一侧的砝码——当某事有偏见时，天平会不公平地向一边倾斜。
