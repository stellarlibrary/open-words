# 🚫 fallacious

🔊 /fəˈleɪʃəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** Based on a mistaken belief or unsound reasoning; misleading or deceptive in appearance
**中文：** adj. 谬误的；虚伪的；使人误解的；靠不住的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- erroneous（错误的）
- misleading（误导的）
- specious（似是而非的）
- unfounded（无根据的）
- deceptive（欺骗性的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **fallacious argument / fallacious reasoning / fallacious assumption / fallacious logic**

👉 Example: The committee rejected the proposal because it was based on fallacious reasoning.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The professor exposed the **fallacious** reasoning in the student's thesis, demonstrating that the conclusion did not follow logically.
   教授揭示了学生论文中谬误的推理，证明其结论在逻辑上不成立。

2. Many consumers were deceived by the **fallacious** advertising claims that promised miraculous results without scientific evidence.
   许多消费者被虚假广告宣传所欺骗，这些广告在没有科学证据的情况下承诺奇迹般的效果。

3. It is **fallacious** to assume that correlation between two variables necessarily implies a direct causal relationship.
   认为两个变量之间的相关性必然意味着直接因果关系，这是谬误的。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "fallacious" containing "fallacy" — a fallacy is a false belief or flawed argument, so fallacious means full of fallacies.
把"fallacious"和"fallacy（谬论）"联系起来——谬论就是错误的信念或有缺陷的论证，所以fallacious就是充满谬误的。
