# 💪 onerous

🔊 /ˈɒnərəs/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Involving a great deal of effort, trouble, or difficulty; burdensome; carrying legal obligations
**中文：** adj. 繁重的；麻烦的；负有义务的；负有法律责任的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- burdensome（繁重的）
- arduous（艰巨的）
- taxing（费力的）
- demanding（要求高的）
- oppressive（沉重的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **onerous task / onerous burden / onerous duty / onerous regulations**

👉 Example: The new regulations imposed onerous requirements on small businesses struggling to remain profitable.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The committee members found the **onerous** reporting requirements extremely difficult to fulfill within the designated timeframe.
   委员会成员发现在规定时间内完成繁重的报告要求极为困难。

2. Many employees resigned because the **onerous** working conditions left them physically and mentally exhausted every single day.
   许多员工辞职是因为繁重的工作条件使他们每天都身心俱疲。

3. The contract included several **onerous** clauses that placed disproportionate financial liability on the smaller partner in the agreement.
   合同中包含几项苛刻的条款，将不成比例的财务责任加在了协议中较小的合作方身上。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "onerous" as "on-er-us" — something heavy placed ON US, like a burden pressing down on our shoulders.
把"onerous"想成"on-er-us"——某种沉重的东西压在我们（us）身上（on），就像负担压在肩膀上。
