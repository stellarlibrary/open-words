# 🚶 vagabond

🔊 /ˈvæɡəbɒnd/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having no settled home; wandering from place to place. (n.) A person who wanders from place to place without a fixed home or employment
**中文：** adj. 流浪的 n. 流浪者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- wanderer（流浪者）
- nomad（游牧民）
- drifter（漂泊者）
- vagrant（流浪汉）
- itinerant（巡回者）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **vagabond lifestyle / vagabond spirit / vagabond existence / a wandering vagabond**

👉 Example: The novel follows the adventures of a charming vagabond traveling across nineteenth-century Europe.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The romantic poet celebrated the **vagabond** lifestyle as a path to genuine freedom and authentic self-discovery.
   这位浪漫主义诗人颂扬流浪生活方式，视其为通往真正自由和真实自我发现的道路。

2. After losing his fortune, the once-wealthy merchant became a **vagabond** who wandered the countryside begging for sustenance.
   失去财富后，这位曾经富有的商人变成了一个流浪者，在乡间漂泊乞讨度日。

3. The historian described the medieval **vagabond** as both a figure of suspicion and a vital carrier of news between towns.
   历史学家将中世纪的流浪者描述为既是令人怀疑的人物，也是城镇之间重要的消息传递者。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "vagabond" as "vague-a-bond" — a person with only vague bonds to any place, always moving on.
把"vagabond"想成"vague-a-bond"——一个对任何地方只有模糊联系的人，总是在不断迁移。
