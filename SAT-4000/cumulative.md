# 📊 cumulative

🔊 /ˈkjuːmjələtɪv/<br>
🏷️ *adjective | 形容词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Increasing or growing by accumulation or successive additions over time
**中文：** adj. 累积的；累加的

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- accumulated（积累的）
- aggregate（总计的）
- collective（集体的）
- progressive（逐渐增长的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **cumulative effect / cumulative impact / cumulative total / cumulative evidence**

👉 Example: The cumulative effect of years of pollution has severely damaged the river ecosystem.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The **cumulative** impact of chronic sleep deprivation on cognitive performance has been well documented in medical research.
   长期睡眠不足对认知表现的累积影响已在医学研究中得到充分记录。

2. The professor explained that the final grade would reflect the **cumulative** score of all assignments submitted throughout the semester.
   教授解释说，期末成绩将反映整个学期提交的所有作业的累积分数。

3. Scientists warn that the **cumulative** effects of greenhouse gas emissions will intensify global climate disruptions in coming decades.
   科学家警告说，温室气体排放的累积效应将在未来几十年加剧全球气候紊乱。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "cumul-ative" as "accumulate" — things that are cumulative keep accumulating, growing larger over time.
把"cumulative"联想到"accumulate（积累）"——累积的事物不断积累，随着时间推移越来越多。
