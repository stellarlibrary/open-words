# 📉 relegate

🔊 /ˈrelɪɡeɪt/<br>
🏷️ *verb | 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To assign to a lower position or status; to banish or exile; to hand over for decision or action
**中文：** v. 把降低到；归入；驱逐，放逐；委托，移交

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- demote（降级）
- banish（放逐）
- downgrade（降低）
- exile（流放）
- consign（委托）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **relegate to / relegate someone to a lower position / relegate a task to others**

👉 Example: The manager decided to relegate the senior analyst to a minor supporting role in the department.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. After the restructuring, the board chose to **relegate** the experienced director to an advisory position with limited authority.
   重组后，董事会选择将这位经验丰富的主管降级为权力有限的顾问职位。

2. Critics argue that standardized testing tends to **relegate** creative subjects to the margins of academic curricula.
   批评者认为标准化考试往往将创造性学科边缘化。

3. The historian refused to **relegate** the contributions of minority communities to mere footnotes in the national narrative.
   这位历史学家拒绝将少数族裔社区的贡献降格为国家叙事中的脚注。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-leg-ate" as putting someone back on a lower leg of a ladder — pushing them down in rank or status.
把"relegate"想成"re-leg-ate"——把某人放回梯子的下一级——在等级或地位上把他们往下推。
