# 🤐 stifle

🔊 /ˈstaɪfəl/<br>
🏷️ *verb, noun | 动词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (v.) To suppress; to suffocate; to conceal. (n.) The joint of the hind leg in horses
**中文：** v. 使窒息；扼杀，抑制

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- suppress（压制）
- smother（使窒息）
- restrain（抑制）
- suffocate（使窒息）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **stifle creativity / stifle dissent / stifle a yawn / stifle growth**

👉 Example: Excessive regulations can stifle innovation and discourage entrepreneurial ventures.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The authoritarian regime sought to **stifle** all forms of political dissent through censorship and intimidation tactics.
   该独裁政权试图通过审查和恐吓手段扼杀一切形式的政治异见。

2. Critics argued that the new tax policy would **stifle** economic growth by discouraging foreign investment in emerging sectors.
   批评人士认为新的税收政策会通过阻碍外国对新兴行业的投资来抑制经济增长。

3. She struggled to **stifle** her laughter during the formal ceremony when the speaker mispronounced several distinguished names.
   当演讲者念错几位知名人士的名字时，她在正式仪式上努力忍住笑。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "stifle" as "still + life" — to stifle something is to make it still, to kill its life and energy.
把"stifle"想成"still（静止）+ life（生命）"——扼杀某物就是让它静止，消灭它的活力。
