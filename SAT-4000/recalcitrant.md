# 🦁 recalcitrant

🔊 /rɪˈkælsɪtrənt/<br>
🏷️ *adjective, noun | 形容词, 名词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (adj.) Having an obstinately uncooperative attitude toward authority or discipline. (n.) A person who is stubbornly resistant
**中文：** adj. 反抗的，顽强的；不顺从的 n. 顽抗者

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- obstinate（固执的）
- defiant（挑衅的）
- intractable（难驾驭的）
- unruly（不守规矩的）
- refractory（倔强的）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **recalcitrant student / recalcitrant attitude / recalcitrant debtor / recalcitrant nation**

👉 Example: The teacher devised new strategies to engage the recalcitrant students who refused to participate in class discussions.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. International sanctions were imposed on the **recalcitrant** nation that repeatedly violated the terms of the disarmament agreement.
   国际社会对这个屡次违反裁军协议条款的顽抗国家实施了制裁。

2. The supervisor found it increasingly difficult to manage the **recalcitrant** employee who openly challenged every directive given.
   主管发现越来越难管理这位公然挑战每项指令的不服从员工。

3. Despite extensive negotiations, the **recalcitrant** faction within the party refused to endorse the proposed legislative compromise.
   尽管经过广泛谈判，党内顽固派系仍拒绝支持提议的立法妥协方案。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "re-cal-ci-trant" as "re-kicking" — like a stubborn mule that keeps kicking back, a recalcitrant person resists all authority.
把"recalcitrant"想成"反复踢"——就像一头倔强的骡子不停往后踢，recalcitrant的人抵制一切权威。
