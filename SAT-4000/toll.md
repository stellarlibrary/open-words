# 🔔 toll

🔊 /toʊl/<br>
🏷️ *noun, verb | 名词, 动词*

---

## 📖 Meaning | 释义
**EN:** (n.) A charge for using a road or bridge; a harmful effect or cost; the sound of a bell rung slowly. (v.) To charge a fee; to ring a bell slowly and repeatedly
**中文：** n. 通行费；代价；伤亡人数；钟声 v. 征收通行费；敲钟

---

## 🔗 Synonyms | 近义词
- fee（费用）
- levy（征收）
- cost（代价）
- knell（丧钟声）
- tribute（贡品）

---

## 📌 Usage Tip | 用法提示
Use with: **take a toll on / death toll / toll road / toll booth / bell tolls**

👉 Example: Years of relentless stress took a heavy toll on her physical and mental health.

---

## 📝 Examples | 例句

1. The prolonged conflict has taken an enormous **toll** on the civilian population and the nation's critical infrastructure.
   持续的冲突对平民和国家关键基础设施造成了巨大的代价。

2. Church bells began to **toll** solemnly across the city as citizens gathered to commemorate the historic anniversary.
   当市民聚集纪念这一历史性周年纪念日时，教堂的钟声开始在城市上空庄严地响起。

3. The government proposed increasing the highway **toll** to fund much-needed repairs to the aging bridge network.
   政府提议提高高速公路通行费，以资助老化桥梁网络急需的维修工程。

---

## 🧩 Memory Trick | 记忆技巧
Think of "toll" as the price you pay — whether it is money at a toll booth or the cost that hardship takes on your life, like the tolling of a heavy bell.
把"toll"想成你付出的代价——无论是在收费站付的钱，还是困难对你生活的损耗，就像沉重的钟声一样。
